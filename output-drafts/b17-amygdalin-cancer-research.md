## Executive Summary

**B-17** is a promotional name given to **amygdalin**, a cyanogenic glycoside found naturally in apricot kernels, bitter almonds, peach pits, and apple seeds. Marketed as "vitamin B-17" by proponents (a designation NOT recognized by any scientific or regulatory body), it has been promoted for decades as a natural cancer cure.

**Key Finding**: The weight of scientific evidence indicates that amygdalin/laetrile has **not demonstrated efficacy in properly controlled clinical trials** and poses documented risks of cyanide toxicity.

## What is B-17/Amygdalin?

### Chemical Classification
| Property | Details |
|----------|--------|
| Type | Cyanogenic glycoside |
| Natural sources | Apricot kernels, bitter almonds, peach pits, apple seeds, lima beans, clover, sorghum |
| "Vitamin B-17" designation | Coined by E.T. Krebs Jr., **NOT an approved vitamin name** |
| Laetrile | Semi-synthetic derivative patented in 1950s (mandelonitrile-beta-glucuronide) |
| Mexican laetrile/amygdalin | Made from crushed apricot pits (mandelonitrile beta-D-gentiobioside) |

### Chemical Breakdown
```
Amygdalin + Hydrolysis → Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) + Benzaldehyde + Glucose
```

This breakdown occurs via:
- The enzyme **beta-glucosidase** (found in intestinal bacteria and certain plants)
- Non-enzymatic hydrolysis at physiological pH

## Proposed Mechanisms of Action (Proponent Claims)

### Theory 1: Enzyme Imbalance Selectivity
> Cancer cells have high beta-glucuronidase activity AND low rhodanese activity → laetrile is converted to cyanide preferentially in cancer cells only.

**Source**: Krebs et al., Med Rec 1950 (trophoblastic theory of cancer)

### Theory 2: Beta-Glucosidase Overexpression
> Cancer cells contain more beta-glucosidase than normal cells → selective cyanide release in tumors

### Theory 3: "Missing Vitamin" Deficiency Disease
> Cancer is caused by B-17 deficiency; supplementation restores health

**Source**: G. Edward Griffin, "World Without Cancer" (1974)

### Theory 4: Lysosomal Disruption
> Cyanide increases tumor acidity → lysosome destruction → cell death + immune stimulation

## CLINICAL TRIALS - HUMAN SUBJECTS

### Primary Clinical Trial Evidence

#### Study 1: Moertel et al. (NCI) - THE DEFINITIVE TRIAL

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Publication** | New England Journal of Medicine, 1982 |
| **Title** | "A clinical trial of amygdalin (Laetrile) in the treatment of human cancer" |
| **Sample Size** | 178 patients |
| **Design** | Clinical trial with metabolic therapy program |
| **Treatment** | IV laetrile followed by oral maintenance + diet, enzymes, vitamins |

**Results:**
> "There was no evidence of antitumor activity. No tumor regressed, no tumor remained static for more than 2 weeks, and no patient experienced a significant improvement in performance status or pain control." — Moertel et al., NEJM 1982

**Safety Findings:**
- Cyanide toxicity observed in several patients
- Blood cyanide levels near toxic range documented
- Toxicity amplified when taken orally (intestinal beta-glucosidase activation)

---

#### Study 2: Ellison, Byar & Newell (NCI Retrospective Analysis)

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Publication** | NEJM, 1978 |
| **Title** | "Special report on Laetrile: the NCI Laetrile Review" |
| **Methodology** | Retrospective analysis of multiple case series |

**Results:**
> No documented tumor regressions attributable to laetrile treatment.

---

#### Study 3: Milazzo, Lejeune & Ernst - Systematic Review

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Publication** | Supportive Care in Cancer, 2007 |
| **Title** | "Laetrile/Amygdalin for cancer treatment" |
| **Reports Analyzed** | 36 reports (case series and case reports only) |

**Results:**
> "None of these publications proved the effectiveness of laetrile. In fact, there was a lack of controlled trials... Therefore, the claim that laetrile has beneficial effects for cancer patients is not supported by sound clinical data." — Milazzo et al., Support Care Cancer 2007

**Note**: No randomized controlled trials were found in this systematic review.

## IN VITRO STUDIES (Cell Culture)

### Key Studies and Findings

#### Saleem et al. Review (2018)
- **Source**: Anticancer Agents in Medicinal Chemistry
- **Finding**: Amygdalin ↑ Bax, caspase-3; ↓ Bcl-2 → apoptosis induction
- **Interpretation**: Shows apoptotic pathway activation at cellular level

#### El-Desouky et al. (2020)
| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Source** | Anticancer Agents in Medicinal Chemistry |
| **Cell Line** | HepG2 (human hepatoma) |
| **IC50 Amygdalin alone** | 458.10 mg/mL |
| **IC50 with beta-D-glucosidase** | 3.2 mg/mL |
| **Mechanism** | G2/M cell cycle arrest; enhanced with zinc addition |

#### Liczbiński & Bukowska Review (2018)
- **Source**: Immunopharmacology and Immunotoxicology
- **Findings**:
  - Inhibited adhesion in breast, lung, bladder cancer cells
  - Decreased integrin expression
  - Inhibited Akt-mTOR pathway
- **Important Caveat**: 
> "Unfortunately, current studies have shown that HCN is also released in normal cells, therefore it may not be safe for human organism."

### Cell Culture Summary Table

| Study | Cell Type(s) | Concentration Range | Effect |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|--------|
| Multiple reviews | AML cell lines (KG-1, HL-60) | 3.5 mg/mL for 50% inhibition | No selective kill vs normal marrow |
| Saleem et al. | Various cancer types | Variable | Apoptosis pathway activation |
| El-Desouky | HepG2 liver cells | IC50 = 458.10 mg/mL | G2/M arrest |
| Liczbiński review | Breast, lung, bladder | Variable | Adhesion inhibition |

## IN VIVO STUDIES (Animal Models)

### NCI Animal Studies (1970s)

**Study Design:** Various rodent tumors transplanted into mice/rats

| Tumor Model | Dose Range Tested | Outcome |
|-------------|------------------|---------|
| L1210 lymphoid leukemia | 25–3,200 mg/kg | **No antitumor activity** |
| P388 lymphocytic leukemia | 25–3,200 mg/kg | **No response** |
| B16 melanoma | 25–3,200 mg/kg | **No effect** |
| Walker 256 carcinosarcoma | 25–3,200 mg/kg | **No activity** |
| Osteogenic sarcoma | Up to LD20 (20% of lethal dose) | **No antitumor activity** |
| Lewis lung carcinoma | Various doses | **No response** |

### Additional Animal Studies
- Human breast cancer cells implanted in mice: No response
- Human colon cancer cells implanted in mice: No response
- DMBA-induced rat mammary carcinoma: Not effective

**Key Finding**: Amygdalin showed "little anticancer activity" across multiple animal models, even at doses approaching toxic levels.

## SAFETY CONCERNS & TOXICITY DATA

### Documented Cyanide Poisoning Cases

#### Case 1: O'Brien et al. (2005)
| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Source** | European Journal of Emergency Medicine |
| **Patient** | Breast cancer with hepatic metastases |
| **Treatment** | Laetrile at Mexican clinic + oral apricot kernels |
| **Blood Cyanide Level** | Near lethal range |
| **Outcome** | Hospitalized, recovered with treatment |

#### Case 2: Sauer et al. (2015)
| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Source** | Wiener Medizinische Wochenschrift |
| **Patient** | 4-year-old child |
| **Exposure** | Apricot kernels (home remedy) |
| **Outcome** | Severe cyanide poisoning, required intensive treatment |

### Toxicity Symptoms (Laetrile/Amygdalin)
- Liver damage
- Difficulty walking (peripheral nerve damage)
- Fever
- Coma
- Death

### Risk Factors for Cyanide Poisoning
1. **Oral administration** — intestinal bacteria contain beta-glucosidases that activate cyanide release
2. **Combination with certain foods** — raw nuts, certain beans contain activating enzymes
3. **Megadoses** — far exceeding "therapeutic" levels
4. **Individual metabolic differences** — varying rhodanese activity (detoxification enzyme)

## COUNTER-ARGUMENTS (Scientific Consensus)

### Scientific Counterpoints with Citations

#### 1. NOT A VITAMIN
**Fact**: No essential physiological role identified for amygdalin.

> "There is no evidence that laetrile is needed for normal metabolism or that it can function as a vitamin in animals or humans." — NCI PDQ Summary

- Not required for metabolism or health
- Name "B-17" coined by proponents, not recognized by any scientific authority

#### 2. SELECTIVE TOXICITY THEORY DISPROVEN
**Fact**: Cyanide is released in normal cells too.

> "Unfortunately, current studies have shown that HCN is also released in normal cells, therefore it may not be safe for human organism." — Liczbiński & Bukowska, Immunopharmacol Immunotoxicol 2018

#### 3. NO EVIDENCE FROM PROPER TRIALS
**Fact**: Systematic reviews find no controlled trials demonstrating efficacy.

> "None of these publications proved the effectiveness of laetrile... Therefore, the claim that laetrile has beneficial effects for cancer patients is not supported by sound clinical data." — Milazzo et al., Support Care Cancer 2007

#### 4. CLINICAL TRIAL CONCLUSIONS
**Fact**: The only proper clinical trial found no benefit.

> "Amygdalin (Laetrile) is a toxic drug that is not effective as a cancer treatment." — Moertel et al., NEJM 1982

### Official Positions

#### NCI PDQ Summary (Health Professional Version)
- **Status**: Not updated since 2022; provided for reference only
- **Finding**: "Laetrile has shown little anticancer activity in animal studies and no anticancer activity in human clinical trials"
- **Approval Status**: NOT approved for use in the United States
- **Legal Status**: Interstate shipment banned since 1980 Supreme Court decision
