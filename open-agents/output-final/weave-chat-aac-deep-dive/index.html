Overview

The Mission: Communication for Everyone

★

"Weave Chat AAC is free and always will be because we firmly believe that everyone should be able to communicate." -- Weave Chat, LLC

In a market where AAC apps routinely cost $100 to $300, Weave Chat AAC is completely free -- no ads, no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, no hidden costs. Its developers have pledged it will stay that way forever. For the millions of individuals living with autism, ALS, apraxia, Down syndrome, stroke-related aphasia, and other conditions that affect speech, the financial barrier to digital communication tools has long been a painful reality. Weave Chat is working to eliminate that barrier entirely.

Weave Chat AAC is developed by Weave Chat, LLC, a small team founded by a speech-language pathologist (SLP) and a software engineer. This pairing is central to the app's identity: every feature is shaped by clinical understanding of how AAC users actually communicate, and every update is built on a foundation of solid engineering.

The team maintains an active relationship with their user community, responding quickly to feedback and feature requests. Volunteer translators from around the world have contributed to bringing the app to new languages. New vocabulary is added on a weekly basis, reflecting a development pace that matches or exceeds many commercial AAC products.

Technology

How Weave Chat Works

At its core, Weave Chat AAC is a category-based AAC system with a dynamic display. Users communicate by tapping picture-symbol buttons organized into vocabulary categories. When a button is pressed, the app speaks the word or phrase aloud using a synthetic voice. Users can construct sentences by selecting multiple buttons in sequence, building up messages in a sentence bar before speaking them.

This category-based approach differs from some competitors that use motor-planning or core-word approaches (like LAMP or Proloquo). The category model groups related words together -- all animals in one place, all foods in another -- making it intuitive for users who think in concrete categories.

Voice & Audio Output

The app leverages the device's built-in synthetic voices, meaning users benefit from whatever TTS (text-to-speech) engine their phone or tablet provides. Users can adjust vocal pitch and rate of speech to find a voice that feels right. Additionally, each button can hold up to 30 seconds of recorded audio -- useful for personal phrases, names, or situations where a human voice is preferred over synthetic speech.

The Fitzgerald Key Color System

One of Weave Chat's most clinically grounded design decisions is its use of the Fitzgerald Key color-coding system. Developed in the early 20th century and widely adopted in AAC practice, this system assigns colors to different parts of speech to help users visually distinguish word types at a glance and support language development by reinforcing grammatical categories.

Verbs
eat, go, play, want
Pronouns
I, you, he, she, they
Questions
who, what, where, why
Nouns
people, places, things
Adjectives
big, happy, cold, red
Conversational
hello, thanks, please
ⓘ

The Fitzgerald Key serves a dual purpose: it helps users visually distinguish word types at a glance, and it supports language development. For users learning to construct sentences, the colors provide implicit scaffolding -- "pick a yellow word, then a green word, then an orange word" can guide someone toward building "I want pizza."

Customization Deep Dive

Customization is where Weave Chat particularly shines for an app at the $0 price point. The level of configurability rivals apps costing hundreds of dollars.

🎨
Board & Button Customization
  • • Choose from 12 grid layouts plus the default scrolling board
  • • Adjust button size, border size, and text size
  • • Set custom behavior for backspace and clear-all buttons
  • • Add custom words, categories, and phrases (up to 2,000 custom buttons)
  • • Upload custom images for any button
  • • Set background colors and toggle between light and dark mode
  • • Enable text-only buttons when images are not needed
🔊
Voice Customization
  • • Select from all synthetic voices available on the device
  • • Adjust vocal pitch to personalize the voice
  • • Control rate of speech (slower for clarity, faster for fluency)
  • • Record up to 30 seconds of custom audio per button
ⓘ

Limits to know: The app has a default limit of 2,000 custom buttons. While this sounds generous, users who need to fill gaps in the pre-made vocabulary may use up a significant portion on essential words rather than personalized content. The development team will increase this limit upon request.

Assessment

Strengths & Weaknesses

▲ Strengths

  • Completely free forever -- no ads, no IAPs, no subscriptions
  • Cross-platform: iOS, Android, Kindle, Chromebook, Mac
  • Designed by a speech-language pathologist (SLP)
  • Fitzgerald Key color coding follows clinical best practices
  • 12+ board layouts with extensive customization options
  • Unique in-app picture messaging (no other AAC app has this)
  • Weekly vocabulary additions and regular updates
  • Multi-device sync with a single login
  • Institutional deployment support with privacy agreements
  • Does not share personal data with third parties

▼ Weaknesses

  • Vocabulary gaps: common words missing from pre-made categories
  • 2,000 custom button limit can be constraining
  • Cannot delete pre-made buttons, only hide them
  • Navigation learning curve for new users
  • Android sync issues reported by some users
  • VoiceOver/screen reader accessibility issues in settings
  • English only (other languages translated but pending release)
  • No switch access or scanning support
  • No Windows or web browser support

Conclusion

The Verdict

Weave Chat AAC occupies a unique position in the AAC landscape. It is not the most polished app available -- vocabulary gaps, navigation challenges, and sync issues are real concerns. But it is the most accessible app in the most fundamental sense of that word: it costs nothing and runs on almost everything.

For families who cannot afford a $300 app, for schools that need to deploy AAC across dozens of Chromebooks, for adults who need a quick solution on their existing Android phone -- Weave Chat is not just a good option, it may be the only option. And for many users, it is not merely adequate but genuinely excellent: its Fitzgerald Key organization, in-app messaging, and extensive customization rival apps costing hundreds of dollars.

The app's weaknesses are real but not fundamental. Vocabulary gaps can be addressed through continued weekly updates. The custom button limit can be raised on request. Navigation can be learned. And the development team's responsiveness and commitment to keeping the app free suggest that Weave Chat will only improve from here.

In a world where communication is a basic human right, Weave Chat AAC is making that right a little more attainable for everyone.
