Company Background

Who Makes Fluent AAC?

Fluent AAC Technology S.R.L. was registered in 2020 in Timisoara, Romania, with a mission its founders describe as "combining clinical expertise and technological innovation to redefine communication." While younger than industry veterans like AssistiveWare (Proloquo2Go) or PRC-Saltillo (TouchChat, LAMP Words for Life), the company's approach has quickly attracted attention in the AAC community.

The company takes a holistic approach, designing for the whole person rather than just their communication limitation. Every feature is developed through extensive collaboration between the R&D team, clinical experts, patient insight groups, and customers. The apps are designed by speech therapists and built in partnership with speech-language pathologists (SLPs).

Fluent AAC also produces a companion app called "Type and Speak", a text-based AAC solution for literate users with conditions including autism, apraxia, cerebral palsy, ALS, stroke, and traumatic brain injury. Together, the two apps address communication needs across the entire literacy spectrum.

Core Innovation

Grammar-Aware Word Prediction

Unlike traditional AAC apps with static icon grids, Fluent AAC's prediction engine dynamically conjugates words based on grammatical context -- turning telegraphic speech into complete, natural sentences.

How Traditional AAC Handles Grammar

Most competing apps show static word forms. To say "I walked," users must find the base word "walk" and then navigate to a separate conjugation page or folder to find "walked." Fluent AAC eliminates these extra steps by making conjugation automatic and contextual.

The ASHA Leader explored this trend in a March 2025 article, "Predictive Text: Who Controls the Conversation?" While some SLPs have expressed caution about predictive systems potentially guiding user choices, AAC users themselves have expressed excitement about capabilities that make their speech more natural and grammatically complete. The consensus is shifting toward viewing grammar prediction as an enhancement to communication, not a limitation of choice.

Progressive Learning

The "Hide Words" Feature

Start with one word. Add more gradually. The key insight: hidden words hold their positions, so motor patterns are never disrupted.

The Vocabulary Overwhelm Problem

A communication board with thousands of words is intimidating for a new user. The traditional solution -- simplified boards with fewer words -- creates a worse problem: when you add vocabulary later, the layout changes and the user must relearn where everything is. Weeks or months of motor learning, disrupted.

Fluent AAC's Solution

Hide Words lets therapists and caregivers hide untargeted vocabulary, showing only a manageable subset. The breakthrough: hiding words does not change the position of remaining words. Every word keeps its fixed location whether visible or not. When the user is ready, new words are simply revealed in place. No relearning. No disruption. Motor memory stays intact.

Design Principle

Motor Consistency: Words That Never Move

Like a pianist who knows where every key is without looking, Fluent AAC users develop muscle memory for word locations. This is possible because every word has a permanent, fixed position.

🧠

Motor Automaticity

Over time, motor plans for frequently used words become automatic. Users can "move their fingers the way their brain remembers," freeing cognitive energy to think about what to say rather than where to find words.

🎯

LAMP-Aligned Principles

This approach aligns with Language Acquisition through Motor Planning (LAMP), a well-researched therapeutic method. Consistent motor patterns leverage the same neural pathways that let touch typists type without looking.

⚡

Speed Over Scanning

Motor-based communication is dramatically faster than visual scanning. Instead of searching the board for each word, users navigate by feel -- reducing time per message and the cognitive effort required to communicate.

The Folder Navigation Problem

In folder-based AAC apps, reaching a word often requires a different sequence of taps depending on your current page. While core words may be consistent on the home screen, fringe vocabulary paths can vary. Fluent AAC eliminates this variability entirely -- every word, core or fringe, is always in the same location.

Assessment

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Grammar-aware prediction -- automatic conjugation unmatched by any major competitor
  • Hide Words -- progressive vocabulary without disrupting motor patterns
  • Motor consistency -- words never change position, supporting LAMP-like automaticity
  • Clinician-designed -- built in collaboration with SLPs and clinical experts
  • 7-day free trial -- rare among premium AAC apps, removes evaluation barrier
  • 25,000+ illustrations -- customizable with gender variants and personal photos
  • Flexible pricing -- monthly option makes AAC accessible at lower entry cost
  • Active development -- regular updates with new features and vocabulary (v7.1.0 Oct 2025)
  • Companion text app -- Type and Speak extends ecosystem to literate users
  • #1 user rating -- top-ranked by AAC users in community surveys

Weaknesses

  • Stability issues -- users report crashes when editing, sometimes requiring device restart
  • Customer support -- some users report weeks-long response times
  • iPad/iOS only -- no Android version excludes many potential users
  • Newer company -- registered 2020; less track record than established competitors
  • Smaller SLP community -- fewer training resources and peer support than Proloquo2Go/LAMP
  • Limited research base -- fewer independent clinical studies than LAMP or Proloquo2Go
  • Subscription model -- ongoing cost concerns for essential communication tool
  • No bilingual support -- unlike Proloquo2Go's true code-switching capability

Conclusion

The Verdict

Fluent AAC represents something genuinely new in the AAC landscape: an app that takes grammar seriously without making it complicated. By embedding grammatical intelligence into the prediction engine and pairing it with a progressive vocabulary system that respects motor learning, Fluent AAC has created a communication experience that feels more natural and linguistically complete than what most competing products offer.

The app is not without its challenges. Stability issues need addressing, the community is still growing, and the subscription model remains controversial for an essential communication tool. But the core innovations -- grammar prediction, Hide Words, and absolute motor consistency -- address real, longstanding needs in the AAC community that other products have not fully solved.

For families and SLPs evaluating AAC options, Fluent AAC deserves serious consideration -- particularly for users who will benefit from grammatical communication and structured vocabulary introduction. The 7-day free trial makes evaluation risk-free, and the monthly subscription option keeps the barrier to entry lower than most competitors.

In a field where communication is literally the product, Fluent AAC is making a compelling case that how you say something matters just as much as being able to say it at all.
