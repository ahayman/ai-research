The Nap Test: Informative for a Yes, Nearly Worthless for a No

He cannot nap, ever, even at maximum exhaustion, and he has not tried since starting duloxetine. That makes it a free test on a lifelong hard-zero channel. The asymmetry in what it can tell him is the whole verdict, and he needs to know it before he runs it.

The killer, stated plainly. Bonnet & Arand measured this directly: sleep deprivation drove nap latency from 13.4 minutes to 3.8, while a mere five-minute walk beforehand raised it from 6.9 to 11.9 [primary, verified]. A five-minute walk moves latency more than any plausible drug effect. Their conclusion is explicit: "measured sleep tendency is a combination of sleep drive and level of central nervous system arousal, where arousal has both state and trait components." Duloxetine improved his sleep. That reduces sleep drive. That makes napping harder. So the drug is pushing the outcome in both directions simultaneously, and a null result is compatible with the mechanism working exactly as hoped.
He has been running an aborted test his whole life. He reports lying there for 10 to 20 minutes before giving up. The formal nap test protocol allows a full 20 minutes and scores a no-sleep trial as 20. His lifelong "hard zero" may be a lifelong latency of 18 minutes that he has never let run [reasoning, not evidence]. A 20-minute latency is long, but it is a number, and a number can move. A "no" cannot. Running the full 20 minutes converts a lifelong binary into a measurement, and it is the single highest-value change available here. It is free, and the 20mg window closes at the dose change and does not reopen.

10Record: minutes to sleep (or 20), fell asleep Y/N, and separately "could I have?"The third is introspective and least trustworthy. Record it; do not let it outvote the first two.
Four more things that break the "lifelong hard-zero has no noise" reasoning, in descending severity after the sleep-drive confound. (2) The aborted test, above. (3) The effort paradox is his specific risk: trying to sleep prevents sleep, which is the defining mechanism of psychophysiological insomnia, the very phenotype he most resembles, and a deliberate test with a hypothesis attached maximises exactly the performance anxiety that raises latency. (4) The denominator is unknown: rare, self-selected attempts under uncontrolled conditions make a lifelong zero a weaker prior than it feels. (5) One nap is an event, not a rate. A single success does not establish nap ability. [reasoning, not evidence]
