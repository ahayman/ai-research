What Nobody Has Measured

These are not gaps in this page. They are the state of the field, and they are the most reliable claims here. Each one is an answer, not an absence of one.

On number 10, because it is the most uncomfortable one and it should not be smoothed over. Kluge 2007 is the study the wider literature relies on for duloxetine's sleep-architecture reputation. Its abstract reports stage 3 sleep, REM latency and REM duration, and nothing else. Total sleep time, sleep efficiency, wake after sleep onset, arousals, sleep onset latency and periodic limb movements are not in it. If those numbers exist, they are in the full text, and the full text defeated this project across six separate paywall attempts: ScienceDirect returned 403 to every route tried. Nobody in this chain has read it. This page is written without it, and that is stated here rather than quietly worked around.
What does exist, so that the inventory is not misread as "nothing is known." Two polysomnography studies (Kluge 2007, n=10, uncontrolled, one dose; Chalon 2005, n=6/arm, confounded). One fixed-dose trial with a 20mg arm and by-dose adverse events plus a validated insomnia scale, which is genuinely useful and genuinely limited. A 216-trial, 64,696-patient dose-effect network meta-analysis, which is adverse-event reporting rather than measured sleep. A dream literature that is small but clear about what it does not explain. The evidence is thin, not absent. The distinction that matters is between "we looked and found little" and "nobody looked," and this diagram is about the second.
Why this diagram matters more than it looks. He asked whether a dose increase will ruin his sleep. The honest floor under every other answer on this page is that the direct evidence he wants does not exist and was never collected. That is not a hedge and it is not modesty. It is the single most reliable finding here, and it means the confident answers he will encounter elsewhere are constructed rather than measured. The absences are the finding. Do not let anyone soften them, including this page.
