Sleep Adverse Events by Dose: The Only Trial That Ever Tested 20mg

All 135 duloxetine trials with posted results were scanned. NCT00408876 is the only one containing a fixed 20mg arm, and the only dose-stratified sleep outcome in the entire registry. This is what it found.

Placebo (n=117) 20mg (n=59) — his dose 60mg (n=116) 120mg (n=112)
Read the two middle bars in every group. 20mg and 60mg are indistinguishable on insomnia, on somnolence, on sleep disorder and on discontinuation. Insomnia actually goes slightly down. The inflection is at 120mg, where insomnia doubles and somnolence triples. The step he is considering sits on the flat part of every curve in this table. The one exception is fatigue, which is the only clean monotonic rise across the 20→60 step.

What this table cannot do, stated at full strength. These are adverse-event reports, not measured sleep. The 20mg arm is n=59, roughly half the size of the others. It is chronic low back pain, not fibromyalgia and not ME/CFS. It is Lilly-sponsored. And the precision available is poor: rough Wilson 95% intervals put insomnia at 20mg at about 4.8–20.5%, at 60mg at about 4.8–15.1%, and at 120mg at about 12.6–26.9%. The 20mg and 60mg intervals overlap almost entirely, and the 120mg interval barely clears 60mg's, so even the spike is suggestive rather than decisive within this single trial. The 120mg arm also lost 24.1% of its people to adverse events, which means its week-13 survivors are enriched for tolerators, so the real 120mg harm is plausibly worse than shown.
On what is missing from this table. Neither "abnormal dreams" nor "nightmare" nor any limb-movement term appears anywhere in it, across all 99 reported adverse-event terms. This is weak evidence of absence, not evidence of safety. The trial reported at a ≥1% threshold, and with n=112 a single case is 0.89% and would fall below it. This must not be read as "duloxetine caused no restless legs in 404 patients."
