The Whole Sleep Story, Sorted by What Kind of Claim It Is

Every load-bearing claim on this page, assigned to a provenance tier. The diagnosed failure of this whole project is caveats filed in appendices while headlines spend unearned certainty. This is the antidote: the grade travels with the claim.

The one distinction that does the work here. A claim killed by heterogeneity is NOT dead: a null average is entirely compatible with a large effect in one person, so those stay on the list as candidates. A claim killed by logic or measurement error IS dead: no amount of individual variation revives a mechanism that predicts the opposite of his report, or that rests on a cross-study artifact, or that was extrapolated from a different drug. The four in the black band are the second kind. They are not "unlikely for him." They are wrong.

How to read this page using this map. The top band is about him and is the most reliable thing here. The green band is real and it is the basis for the one defensible claim: there is no evidence that the 20 to 60mg step damages sleep, and the harm signal sits at 120mg. Everything in amber and below is either too small, too fragile, or too inferential to move a decision on its own. And the red band is the finding, not the gap. He asked for mechanism and candidates rather than verdicts, and this is what that looks like when the grades are made to travel with the claims instead of being filed at the bottom.
Provenance key: n=1, about him his own report · primary, verified the source was retrieved and the claim is in it · primary, but tiny retrieved, but underpowered or uncontrolled · model-based aggregate across-trial inference · reasoning / secondary an inference, or a review's assertion not traced to a primary · no evidence searched, found nothing: a finding, not a failure.
