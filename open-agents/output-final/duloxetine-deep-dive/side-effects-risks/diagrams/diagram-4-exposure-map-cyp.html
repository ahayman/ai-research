What Changes Your Drug Level

Duloxetine is a substrate of CYP1A2 (primary) and CYP2D6, and a moderate inhibitor of CYP2D6. Two things can move your exposure enough to matter. One of them is an ordinary antibiotic. And one item you were going to skip inverts into a calibration that applies to every population figure on every other page.

The inverse that does apply to you

The smoking figure is always published in the smoker's direction, because smokers are the exposure of interest. Read from where you stand, it says something different and more useful:

As publishedSmokers run 38.4% lower serum and 53.6% lower dose-adjusted duloxetine than non-smokers.
⇔
Read from where you standAs a non-smoker, you run 38–54% HIGHER exposure than a smoker on an identical prescription.

This is not a hazard and it requires no action. It is a calibration. Every population figure you will read for any given milligram dose is an average across a population that contains smokers, which means you sit systematically above that average before any other variable is considered. It is one more reason the population number does not describe you.

The corollary does not apply to you but is badly under-communicated generally: a person who quits smoking on a stable duloxetine dose receives a substantial spontaneous dose increase, with no prescription change and usually no warning.

Outbound — duloxetine changing other drugs

Duloxetine's moderate CYP2D6 inhibition matters for CYP2D6 substrates: tramadol (see Diagram 3), codeine (the same prodrug problem — duloxetine blunts its conversion to morphine), metoprolol, some antipsychotics, and TCAs. [secondary — unverified]

What this means practically

  • Tell any doctor prescribing an antibiotic that you take duloxetine, and name ciprofloxacin. That is the whole action, and it takes one sentence.
  • Do not take fluvoxamine with duloxetine without an explicit conversation about it.
  • Nothing needs doing about the non-smoker calibration. It just means you should read every "at 20mg, patients typically..." figure with the knowledge that you are not at the typical exposure for 20mg.

Nothing here directs a dose. It is what to understand and what to raise.
