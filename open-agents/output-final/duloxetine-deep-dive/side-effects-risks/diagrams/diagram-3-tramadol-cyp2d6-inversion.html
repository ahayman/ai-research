Tramadol: Less Benefit and More Risk, From the Same Tablet

Tramadol is commonly offered for fibromyalgia, which makes this live rather than theoretical: it is a plausible outcome of your next pain conversation. The usual warning mentions one hazard. There are three, and the second one inverts the normal logic of a drug interaction.

The pharmacokinetic mechanism — the part usually missed

Tramadol alone
Parent compoundTramadol — a serotonin-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor that also happens to be an opioid prodrug
↓
CYP2D6 — working normally
↓
Active metaboliteO-desmethyltramadol (M1) — this is what does the opioid pain relief
Analgesia happens. The serotonergic parent is converted onward and cleared at its normal rate.
Tramadol + duloxetine
Parent compoundTramadol — the serotonergic half, now lingering
↓
CYP2D6 — inhibited by duloxetine
↓
Active metaboliteO-desmethyltramadol (M1) — less of it is made
Less analgesia, more serotonin. Duloxetine is a moderate CYP2D6 inhibitor, and CYP2D6 is the exact enzyme tramadol needs in order to work.

This is worse than additive

↓ Benefit
The analgesic metabolite is blocked at the enzyme
↑ Risk
The serotonergic parent compound circulates longer

The usual mental model of a drug interaction is "more of both, so more of everything." This one is: less of the thing you want, more of the thing you don't.

And there is a trap folded inside it

A person whose tramadol "isn't working" will reasonably ask for a higher dose. That raises the serotonergic load while chasing an analgesic effect that is being blocked upstream. The escalation feels rational from inside and makes the situation strictly worse.
Tramadol underperforms→ "It's not working"→ Dose increased→ More serotonergic parent→ Still blocked at CYP2D6↶

The three hazards, stacked

(a) Serotonergic

Tramadol is an SNRI in its own right. Serotonin syndrome on duloxetine + tramadol is documented, including a 55-year-old with tachycardia, diaphoresis and psychomotor agitation whose duloxetine levels were unexpectedly elevated; both symptoms and levels fell only after tramadol was stopped. CYP2D6 poor-metaboliser genotype was excluded, so the raised levels were the interaction, not a metabolic quirk. [secondary — unverified]

(b) Pharmacokinetic

Duloxetine is a moderate CYP2D6 inhibitor. The inversion above. The same prodrug problem applies to codeine, which also needs CYP2D6 to become morphine. [primary, verified]

(c) Seizure threshold

Independent of serotonin. Tramadol lowers it on its own. A 2025 nursing-home cohort: seizure incidence 16.10 per 100 patient-years when an antidepressant was added to tramadol, 20.17 when tramadol was added to an antidepressant; CYP2D6-inhibiting antidepressants carried an adjusted IRR of 1.09 (95% CI 1.02–1.18). Elderly institutionalised population — the absolute figures do not transfer to a younger adult. The direction and the mechanism do. [secondary — unverified]
This is not a "never." Tramadol and duloxetine are co-prescribed routinely, and this page does not tell you to refuse it. It means that if tramadol is offered, this is the conversation to have, and the sentence worth saying is: "duloxetine inhibits CYP2D6, which is the enzyme tramadol needs in order to work." That single fact reframes the whole prescription, and a prescriber who hears it will usually think again about dose, about escalation, and about what else is available.

Nothing here directs a decision. It is what to understand and what to raise.
