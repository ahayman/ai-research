Blood Pressure and Heart Rate: The Mean Is Small, The Tail Is Real

Both halves of this are true, and holding them together is the whole skill. A population mean of +2.22 bpm is arithmetically compatible with most people sitting at zero and a minority sitting at +40.

The mean: genuinely small

42 placebo-controlled studies. 8,504 patients. This is a large, well-powered evidence base and it says the average effect is trivial.

Wernicke's conclusion, verbatim: "use of duloxetine does not appear to be associated with significant cardiovascular risks in patients with conditions for which the drug has been approved or studied." Even patients with elevated baseline BP showed no increased risk of sustained elevation. [primary, verified]

The tail: real, and at your exact dose

One case report, and it is uncomfortably on point. This is the strongest form of single-case causal evidence that exists.

Stevens DL, 2008 — "Duloxetine-associated tachycardia"

A 26-year-old man presenting with fatigue, tachycardia, diaphoresis and chest pain, about two months after starting duloxetine 20 mg/day. Cardiac workup including echocardiogram, exercise treadmill testing and Holter monitoring was negative except for tachycardia at 110–120 bpm.
On 20mg — HR 110–120 bpm
Duloxetine withheld — HR normalised in under a week (positive dechallenge)
Restarted at the same dose — tachycardia returned within 2 days (positive rechallenge)
Naranjo score: PROBABLE

The authors' own conclusion: duloxetine can cause clinically significant tachycardia "even in low doses." [primary, verified]

20 mg/day is your exact dose. This does not mean it will happen to you. It means the population mean does not rule it out, and it is the reason "monitor your blood pressure" is a reasonable instruction rather than an alarm.

Why these two facts are not in tension

A mean is a property of a population. It is not a property of any individual in it.

−10 bpm0+20+40 bpm
Most people: near zero. This is what makes the mean +2.22. A minority: +40. This is Stevens 2008.

Illustrative shape, not measured data. Nobody has published the individual distribution — which is itself the point. The published number is a mean, and a mean is compatible with many different distributions underneath it.

This is the same argument the 8.5-fold individual EC50 spread makes elsewhere in this deep dive, arriving from a different direction: the population curve does not describe any individual in it.

The label's own risk amplifiers, read precisely

"Orthostatic hypotension and syncope have been reported with therapeutic doses of duloxetine. Syncope and orthostatic hypotension tend to occur within the first week of therapy but can occur at any time during duloxetine treatment, particularly after dose increases. The risk of blood pressure decreases may be greater in patients taking concomitant medications that induce orthostatic hypotension (such as antihypertensives) or are potent CYP1A2 inhibitors… and in patients taking duloxetine at doses above 60 mg daily."

Four amplifiers, and a timing statement that is often misread as a fifth:

Antihypertensives Potent CYP1A2 inhibitors Doses above 60 mg daily Concomitant hypotensives "particularly after dose increases" — a timing statement, not an amplifier
The calibration that matters, and it runs against caution: the label's dose threshold is above 60mg. The label does not support treating a 20→30mg or a 20→60mg step as a high-risk event. That is worth having, because it is the kind of fact that gets lost when a warning is summarised rather than read.

Nothing here directs a dose. It is what to understand and what to raise.
