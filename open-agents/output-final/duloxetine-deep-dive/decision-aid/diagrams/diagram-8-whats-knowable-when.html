What Is Knowable, and When

The single most useful thing you can bring into the room is an accurate account of what you cannot yet know. Here is the shape of it.

Now — 2 weeks
Nothing. 13–37% chance of a pure fluke, depending on your true baseline rate. This is not a reason for pessimism. It is a reason not to treat two weeks as informative.
~4 weeks appointment
Still weak (2–14% fluke). Meaningful only if a real trigger has been survived — and unless chance supplies one, you will have no exposure-conditional data at all.
6–8 weeks
"Random fluctuation" becomes implausible. Placebo and regression are still fully live.
3 months
Chance is dead (<0.25%). Placebo decay should be starting if it is placebo — that is the point at which the two stories begin to separate.
3–6 months
+ 3 survived triggers
The strongest realistic evidence available to you. Drug vs placebo becomes genuinely separable, because the exposure test is the one regression to the mean cannot fake.
6–12 months
Matches where the trial evidence sits: staying on duloxetine → 13.7% relapse at 26 weeks (GAD, PMID 18559291), 14.4% at 52 weeks (MDD, PMID 19552867). That ~14% is an upper bound. Past 12 months, nobody has data on anyone — there is no placebo-controlled duloxetine data beyond a year. [primary, verified / no evidence exists]
The appointment arrives before the data does.
That is not a reason to delay anything, and it is not an argument for any particular dose. It is a reason to walk in knowing precisely what you do not yet know, and to say so. The strongest thing you can bring to that room is an accurate account of the size of your own uncertainty, because a doctor can work with a calibrated patient and cannot work with a confident one who is guessing.

✓ What the calendar kills

Random fluctuation. That's it. At six weeks, chance is under 5% even at the most conservative baseline. At three months it is under 1 in 400.

You do not need six months. You need about eight to twelve weeks.

✗ What survives the calendar entirely

Placebo. Regression to the mean. Natural course.

These are systematic, not random, so waiting does not touch them. They produce exactly the same zero-episode observation.

A three-month clean run proves the improvement is real. It says nothing about whether the drug caused it. Only the exposure test separates those.

Provenance. The decay curve and all percentages are [reasoning, not evidence] — a standard Poisson process for recurrent events, P(0) = e−λt, plotted at λ=3/month. The arithmetic is checkable; the input is his own retrospective estimate of "several times a month," which carries recall bias and which does not arithmetically reconcile with "episodes last 1–2 weeks" (see the exposure-denominator diagram). Relapse figures: GAD PMID 18559291, MDD PMID 19552867 [primary, verified] — and note these are the best available numbers, presented as an upper bound, because the famous ~25% "poop-out" figure is Solomon 2005, an observational NIMH cohort enrolled 1978–81 on TCAs and MAOIs whose definition cannot separate tachyphylaxis from natural course or non-adherence. The canonical range is 9–57%, which is a confession rather than an estimate. Duloxetine-specific tachyphylaxis has never been studied.
