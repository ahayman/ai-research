Interaction Card — For Any Other Prescriber

The point of this card is not that you would take these by choice. It is that they are given by clinicians who are not thinking about your antidepressant, in rooms where you may be the only person who knows.

🖨️ Designed to print and hand over. Keep a copy in your wallet or phone.
⚠ I take DULOXETINE (Cymbalta), 20mg daily
A serotonin-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor. Serotonergic. Please check before prescribing. Half-life ~12h (FDA range 8–17h). Substrate of CYP1A2 (primary) and CYP2D6; moderate CYP2D6 inhibitor.
⛔ Absolute — never co-administer
MAOIsphenelzine · tranylcypromine · isocarboxazid · selegiline

Contraindicated.

5 days off duloxetine before starting an MAOI. 14 days off an MAOI before starting duloxetine.

Linezolid→ infectious disease teams

A reversible MAOI. Avoid.

Typically given for resistant Gram-positive infection, by a team focused on the infection rather than the antidepressant.

Methylene blue (IV)→ anaesthetists, surgery

A potent reversible MAO-A inhibitor. Avoid. FDA safety communication, 2011.

Used intra-operatively. You may be unconscious when the decision is made.

If emergency use is unavoidable: stop duloxetine immediately; resume 24 hours after the last dose of the interacting drug.
⚠ Real — and the first one is likely to be offered
TRAMADOL→ routinely prescribed for fibromyalgia

Triple hazard, and only the first is usually mentioned.

1. Additive serotonergic load. Tramadol is itself an SNRI as well as an opioid. Documented duloxetine + tramadol serotonin syndrome cases exist, including one with tachycardia, diaphoresis and psychomotor agitation in whom duloxetine levels were unexpectedly elevated and fell only when tramadol was stopped — with CYP2D6 poor-metaboliser genotype excluded, so the raised levels were the interaction itself.

2. Worse than additive, and this is the part that gets missed. Duloxetine inhibits CYP2D6 — the enzyme that converts tramadol into M1 (O-desmethyltramadol), the metabolite that does the opioid analgesia. So duloxetine makes tramadol WORK LESS while the serotonergic parent drug lingers. Less of the benefit, more of the risk. And a patient whose tramadol "isn't working" may escalate the dose, escalating the serotonergic load with it.

3. Seizure threshold. Tramadol lowers it independently. CYP2D6-inhibiting antidepressants carried an adjusted IRR of 1.09 (95% CI 1.02–1.18) vs non-inhibiting ones.

Not "never" — it is co-prescribed routinely. But this is the one interaction where the diagnosis makes exposure likely and the risk is tripled up.

Fluvoxamine

Raises duloxetine AUC 6-fold. A potent CYP1A2 inhibitor.

Ciprofloxacin→ any antibiotic prescriber

Same CYP1A2 pathway. Tell whoever is prescribing the antibiotic.

Codeine

The same prodrug problem as tramadol: duloxetine blunts its conversion to morphine, so it works less.

Other antidepressantsesp. SSRIs · during cross-tapers

Genuine additive risk. The most common real-world cause of serotonin toxicity after MAOIs.

✗ Precisely wrong — the most likely bad advice
Alternate-day dosing

At a ~12h half-life, 48 hours between doses is four half-lives. Plasma falls to roughly 6% of peak before the next dose arrives. That is a small withdrawal every two days, indefinitely.

In-silico modelling covering duloxetine explicitly concludes that lengthening the dose interval at or below minimum therapeutic doses "likely increases the risk of withdrawal symptoms and cannot be recommended as a prudent strategy for tapering."

It is the most likely piece of bad advice to be offered, because it is intuitive, free, requires no compounding pharmacy, and is what a busy prescriber reaches for when there is no capsule below 20mg.

The counterintuitive better direction, from the same literature: dose MORE often, at lower doses.

✓ Over-warned — flagged by checkers, not supported by cases
Triptans

0 of 29 FDA case reports met the Hunter criteria (the accepted gold standard). 7/29 met the looser Sternbach. The authors: "The data do not support prohibiting the use of triptans with SSRIs or SNRIs."

A 14-year cohort of 19,017 co-prescribed patients / 30,928 person-years found an incidence of 0.6–2.3 per 10,000 person-years. The American Headache Society recommends against restricting.

5-HTP

Zero documented human cases of serotonin syndrome, alone or in combination, after decades of widespread over-the-counter use alongside antidepressants.

Mechanistically it is a direct serotonin precursor and bypasses tryptophan hydroxylase, so the concern is not silly. But the case count is zero. Grade it plausible-mechanism-with-no-cases, not danger.
