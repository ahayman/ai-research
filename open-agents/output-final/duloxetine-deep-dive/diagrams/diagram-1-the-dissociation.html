The absence was anticipated independently. Its lack was very stark, not just to me but to my whole family. We all braced for it, but it never came. Three or more people predicted a crash on prior grounds, and it did not arrive. That is not a memory reconstructed after the fact.
What this shape rules out
SedationSedation cannot sharpen the acute experience while removing the aftermath.
Emotional bluntingBlunting mutes globally. One thing got louder.
Any global muting storyA single dial cannot move two readings in opposite directions.
ExpectancyExpectancy predicts the acute experience would feel better. It felt worse. Wrong sign.
So the question is not “why did the episodes stop.” It is “why is the overwhelm decoupled from the crash.” Nobody has studied this decoupling in any human, for any drug. The human evidence explains the acute half. The animal evidence explains the aftermath half. Nothing has both.
Reported observations are n=1 and unblinded. What raises them above anecdote is the sign of the acute effect (wrong for expectancy) and the independent anticipation of the absence.
