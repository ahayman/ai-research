The Composite: Three Literatures Nobody Has Joined
No single candidate explains him. The best available account is a composite, and each column below comes from a body of research that has never been connected to the other two.
The acute half
Sensory overwhelm got worse
Candidate 2: serotonergic sensory-gating dose ladder
Human, dose-graded

Jensen 2008 (PMID 18000656): escitalopram 10mg, n=21, P50 gating intact. Oranje 2011 (PMID 20971512): 15mg, n=20, P50 gating disrupted. Same lab, same paradigm, same design.

A 1.5× dose step flipped the filter. Mismatch negativity is raised at both doses: detects more, filters less, stops responding more slowly.

It is the only one of seven candidates that predicts acute-worse, and the studies are escitalopram, so it also speaks to the Lexapro failure.

Limits: single lab (Copenhagen/Glostrup), all four studies are a single acute dose in drug-naive volunteers, so chronicity is unmeasured. Escitalopram, not duloxetine. n≈20 healthy males. The leap from a P50 waveform to a festival is enormous and unbridged.
The crash half
The 1–2 week crash never came
Candidate 3: Chattarji delayed amygdala + Musazzi glutamate decoupling
Animal only

Yasmin 2016 (PMID 27798355): a single 2-hour stressor produces amygdala spinogenesis and anxiety 10 days later, explicitly dissociable from the acute response.

Musazzi 2010 (PMID 20052403): chronic antidepressants including venlafaxine, at 14 days, completely prevent the stress glutamate surge while corticosterone still rises normally. The stressor lands; the sequela does not.

It is the only candidate whose native timescale is his timescale.

Limits: the join has never been tested. The regions do not match (Musazzi measured prefrontal cortex; Chattarji's effect is amygdala). Chattarji's vehicle gavage was also protective (Chakraborty 2019). Rats, footshock, not duloxetine. And it predicts acute-intact, when he reports acute-worse.
The sleep half
Sleep better, vivid dreams, less morning fatigue
Candidates 1 and 5: anxiolysis + sleep continuity; tonic LC-NE shift
One RCT at his exact dose

Candidate 1 is the only candidate with a positive trial at 20mg. Nicolini 2009 (PMID 18485261), fixed 20mg, n=84: HAM-A total and psychic separated. Somatic did not (p=0.07). Reduced night awakenings would explain the dreams and the morning fatigue with one mechanism.

Candidate 5 (lower tonic locus coeruleus firing) is the only candidate the nap test speaks to, which is why the nap test is worth running.

Limits: candidate 1 cannot explain acute-worse or the escitalopram failure, and needs candidate 7 to explain its own biggest hole. Candidate 5 is animal-only, its phasic limb is contradicted in duloxetine at 7 days (Alba-Delgado 2012), and SSRIs lower tonic firing too, so escitalopram should have worked and did not.

Nothing on this list explains all of him, and nothing on it has been tested in a human resembling him. That is not a failure of the search. It is the state of the field, and it sits directly on top of the evidence-absences. The composite is the honest answer, and presenting it as a unified theory would be the error that eight research passes kept making.
Ranking is by fit-to-him × evidence quality, not by elegance. The most elegant candidate ranks third. The dullest ranks first.
