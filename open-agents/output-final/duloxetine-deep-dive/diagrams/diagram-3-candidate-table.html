The Rated Candidate List
Seven live candidates, rated for fit to him rather than ranked toward a winner. Ranking is by fit × evidence quality, not elegance. The dullest candidate ranks first and the most elegant ranks third.
Fit scoring: ✓✓ predicts it natively · ✓ consistent with it · ○ silent · ✗ predicts the opposite. The one distinction that shortens the list: a candidate killed by heterogeneity (a null average) is not dead, because a null average is compatible with a large individual effect. A candidate killed by logic or measurement error is dead, and no amount of individual variation revives it.
1
Anxiolysis + sleep continuity (“the boring one”)
RCT-backed. The only candidate with a positive trial at his actual dose: Nicolini 2009, fixed 20mg GAD, n=84, HAM-A total and psychic separated. Somatic did not (p=0.07).
Fits
sleep ✓✓ · dreams ✓✓ · morning fatigue ✓✓ · pain-minimal ✓✓
Fails
acute-worse ✗ · escitalopram ✗
The hole: escitalopram gave him zero anxiolysis. If the benefit is just “an antidepressant reduced his anxiety,” why did the last one not? This candidate needs candidate 7 to explain its own biggest anomaly. Six passes were so busy hunting mechanism that nobody rated the obvious explanation.
2
Serotonergic sensory-gating dose ladder
Human, dose-graded, blind, placebo-controlled, crossover, and single-lab. Jensen 2008: 10mg, P50 intact. Oranje 2011: 15mg, P50 disrupted. A 1.5× step flipped the filter.
Fits
acute-worse ✓✓ the only candidate that predicts it · escitalopram ✓✓ · no-cognitive-content ✓
Fails
crash ○ · sleep ○
Limits: all four studies are a single acute dose, so chronicity is unmeasured and “the cost is transient” is unsupported. Cross-study, not one dose-response experiment. The only independent lab ran the opposite manipulation and got a mirror image, which argues for an inverted-U rather than one dial.
3
Chattarji delayed amygdala + Musazzi glutamate decoupling
Animal-only, and the join has never been tested. A 2-hour stressor produces amygdala changes 10 days later; chronic antidepressants at 14 days prevent the glutamate surge while corticosterone rises normally.
Fits
crash-absent ✓✓ the only matching timescale · no-cognitive-content ✓✓
Fails
acute-worse ✗ predicts acute-intact · escitalopram ✗
Why it ranks third despite the best timescale: it is the most elegant account in the chain and it has zero human evidence, an untested cross-region join (prefrontal vs amygdala), and a vehicle-gavage confound. Elegance ranks below a positive RCT.
4
Escitalopram-specific allosteric S2 mechanism
Mechanistic inference. The allosteric effect is real and characterised. Its relevance to his dissociation is entirely unevidenced.
Fits
escitalopram ✓✓ the only candidate that addresses it at all
Fails
everything else ○
Conflict of interest, disclosed because nobody applied it: the review is Zhong, Haddjeri & Sánchez 2012 (PMID 21901317), cited throughout this corpus as “Sanchez 2011.” Sánchez is at H. Lundbeck A/S, escitalopram's manufacturer, and the abstract opens by asserting escitalopram's superiority. The allosteric-site literature is substantially a manufacturer research programme. This project applies exactly that standard to a Viatris-funded pregabalin meta-analysis. Also: “escitalopram is the only SSRI with that site” is wrong. The site belongs to the transporter.
5
Tonic/phasic LC-NE shift (demoted from #1)
Animal-only for the tonic limb. Contradicted for the phasic limb. Alba-Delgado 2012, the only chronic-duloxetine locus coeruleus recording in existence: 7 days, osmotic minipump, and the sensory-evoked excitatory response decreased.
Fits
nap-failure ✓ this is where it earns its place · sleep ✓ · morning fatigue ✓
Fails
acute-worse ✗ predicts acute-better, twice over · crash ○ · escitalopram ✗
Why the orchestrator's favourite dropped from #1 to #5: the source was misread. Bari & Aston-Jones say the excitatory component was left intact; the ratio rose because tonic fell and the inhibitory component grew. A ratio rising because its denominator falls is not “acute reactivity up.” And in the theory it invokes, high tonic means distractible, not aroused, so lowering it predicts better filtering. Sanity check: atomoxetine is prescribed to reduce distractibility. It survives as a decent theory of his sleep, not a theory of everything.
6
His own CFS-aggravation hypothesis
Claim 1 established, claim 2 unstudied, claim 3 speculation. The Canadian Consensus Criteria describe the sequence using his own word: “Overload phenomena may trigger a 'crash'.” That crashes aggravate the illness has no evidence either way.
Fits
pain-minimal ✓✓ it explains why pain has not moved: the mechanism is slow by construction · crash-absent ✓
Fails
acute-worse ○ · everything else ○
It survives because it was killed by absence of research, not by logic. His exact intersection is the population these studies exclude or never assemble. But three adjacent findings lean against it, and honesty requires saying so: allostatic load in CFS correlates inversely with fatigue duration; prospectively the limb that predicts pain is metabolic, and the parasympathetic limb his model runs through predicted nothing; and the direct distress-to-pain path was null. The sharp free test: his model must run through fatigue. If pain improves while fatigue does not, it is dead.
7
Chronic 5-HT2C adaptation opposing acute action
Dead as an account of the dissociation, by logic. Alive but redundant for the crash half.
Fits
crash ✓ · escitalopram ✓
Fails
acute-worse ✗ Zohar 1988 predicts acute-better
It is a single-mechanism account, and a single mechanism cannot move two things in opposite directions. It has no state in which the acute is worse and the crash is absent. That is a logic argument, so it kills the candidate for the job it was hired for, and only for that job. It stays on the list, at the bottom, with the reason, because “supplement 6 killed it” is too coarse a verdict.
There is no winner, and the absence of one is the finding. Candidate 2 explains the acute half. Candidate 3 explains the crash half. Candidates 1 and 5 explain the sleep half. Candidates 4 and 7 gesture at the escitalopram failure. Nothing explains all of it, and nothing has been tested in a human resembling him.
Twelve further candidates are on a dead list, killed by logic or measurement rather than heterogeneity, and are not revivable by individual variation. See Category 4 for the full table, the graveyard, and the falsifiers attached to each live candidate.
