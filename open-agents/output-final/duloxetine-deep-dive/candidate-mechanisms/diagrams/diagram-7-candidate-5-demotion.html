The sentence at the centre of it

✗ What was read

"NET inhibition LOWERS locus coeruleus tonic firing via α2-autoreceptor feedback while RAISING terminal NE and RAISING the phasic:tonic ratio. Read what that predicts: baseline arousal DOWN, acute reactivity UP."

That is his entire report from a single mechanism. It is also not in the paper.

✓ What the source actually says

"ATM caused a significant decrease of the tonic activity of LC single-units, although leaving INTACT the sensory-evoked excitatory component of LC phasic response. Moreover, the magnitude of the INHIBITORY component of LC response to paw stimulation was INCREASED... and its duration was PROLONGED. Together, these effects produced an increase in the phasic-to-tonic ratio."

Bari A, Aston-Jones G. Neuropharmacology. 2013 Jan;64(1):53-64. PMID 22820275. [primary, verified]
The chain dated this "2012" and never named the authors. 2012 is the Epub date.
Read that three times. The excitatory phasic response was left INTACT, not increased. The ratio rose because (a) the denominator FELL and (b) the INHIBITORY component GREW. A ratio that rises because its denominator falls is not evidence that the numerator rose. And the second half is worse for the reading: the LC's inhibitory response to a sensory stimulus grew in magnitude and lasted longer, which describes a system that damps down harder and faster after each sensory event. That is close to the opposite of a system overwhelmed by a crowd. [reasoning, not evidence] on the last step; the measurements are primary.
Four independent kills, any one of which is sufficient
1

The theory says the opposite of what was claimed

Aston-Jones & Cohen 2005 (PMID 16022602), verbatim: high tonic firing is "disengagement from the current task and a search for alternative behaviors (exploration)". Tonic is not "baseline arousal." It is a distractible mode. So lowering it predicts better filtering. Sanity check: atomoxetine is prescribed to reduce distractibility. If raising the ratio made people more sensory-overwhelmed, atomoxetine would be a bad ADHD drug. [primary, verified]

2

The decisive study, found by nobody in eight passes

Alba-Delgado 2012 (PMID 22591831): duloxetine, 5 mg/kg/day, 7 days, osmotic minipump, in vivo LC recording. The only chronic-duloxetine LC recording in existence. Verbatim: antidepressants over 7 days "DECREASED the excitatory firing rate of the early response." The right drug, the right duration, recording the actual neurons, and the sensory-evoked excitatory response went DOWN. [primary, verified]

3

The counterexample inside his own history

The tonic limb is not NET-specific. Szabo 2000 (PMID 11343573): SSRIs lower LC tonic firing too, via serotonin's tonic inhibitory input, and Szabo proposes that as the anxiolytic mechanism of SSRIs. Escitalopram is an SSRI and it gave him ZERO anxiolysis. Heterogeneity cannot rescue this one, because he is not a population being generalised to. He is the single case being fitted. [primary, verified]

4

The source cannot bear the weight regardless

Atomoxetine, not duloxetine. A single acute IP injection, so it cannot speak to two-week dosing at all. Isoflurane-anaesthetised rats at 2–2.5%, and you cannot measure sensory overwhelm in an unconscious animal. The α2 mechanism is cited, not tested. And Alba-Delgado's inhibitory-period result runs opposite to Bari's: same claimed mechanism, two drugs, opposite signs on the same measurement. [primary, verified ×2]

What survives, reported against the line of attack. The tonic limb is now duloxetine-specific, not atomoxetine-extrapolated (Arai 2025: NA up around the LC, pERK down). The timescale limb holds and is fast: present at 2 days and it plateaus (Szabo), so at 14 days he is well past full expression. The persistence limb holds: the α2 autoreceptors controlling LC firing are not desensitised by chronic treatment (Mateo 2001), even though the ones controlling release are. [primary, verified ×3]
It is a good sleep and arousal candidate wearing a theory-of-everything costume. Stripped of the acute-worse claim it cannot support and the crash claim it never made, what remains is: duloxetine may have lowered his baseline arousal, which is consistent with his sleep improving and might be testable with a nap. That is worth having. It is not the spine of the document. And it is dose-silent: three doses in Bari produced three qualitatively different patterns, so anyone predicting the ratio at 60mg is extrapolating from an anaesthetised rat given one injection of a different drug.
