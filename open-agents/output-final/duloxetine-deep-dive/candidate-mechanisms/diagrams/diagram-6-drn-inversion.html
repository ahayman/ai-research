Rozeske 2011, verbatim: "These data suggest that uncontrollable stressors produce a desensitization of DRN 5-HT1A autoreceptors and that this desensitization is responsible for the behavioral consequences of uncontrollable stress." [primary, verified]
Zhang 2025, verbatim: chronic fluoxetine "downregulated the 5-HT activation of the inhibitory GirK current in 5-HT neurons." Hyperpolarisation fell from −26.03 mV to −8.7 mV; the neurons become resistant to autoinhibition and maintain their spiking. [primary, verified]
Rozeske's own authors flag it: "Indeed, acute fluoxetine is known to cause 5-HT1A-R internalization in the DRN." They raise it precisely because it threatens their causal claim. [primary, verified]
What it means for him. Read as it was proposed, this model predicts duloxetine INSTALLS his crash state rather than preventing it. So his Saturday is a counterexample to the model, not a confirmation of it. That is not a caveat to be filed at the bottom. It is a model running backwards, and no amount of individual variation makes a backwards model run forwards.
Two caveats against the tidiness of that table, stated because the standard demands it. [reasoning, not evidence] Rozeske and Laaris are rats; Zhang is mice, so the two halves are not the same species and a within-species head-to-head does not exist. And Rozeske's discussion concedes the sufficiency test has never been run: "it would also be desirable to determine whether manipulations that reduce DRN 5-HT1A-R inhibitory function, in the absence of IS treatment, also produce typical IS-induced behaviors." Nobody has checked whether desensitising these autoreceptors, on its own, produces helplessness.
This was the orchestrator's own proposed model. It was killed by the pass that was asked to build on it. That is what "killed by logic" looks like, and it is the reason the distinction between logic-death and heterogeneity-death is the engine of the whole page.
