What the spread licenses, and what it does not
✓

"The population curve does not describe any individual in it." That is a direct reading of the table, not an inference.

✓

"Try and see is correct clinical practice rather than laziness." There is no measurement a prescriber could take that would place him inside that 8.5-fold band. Titration is not sloppiness; it is the only instrument available.

✓

"His Lexapro / duloxetine divergence is expected, not anomalous." He does not need a special explanation for responding differently from a trial average, because responding differently from a trial average is the normal condition.

✓

"Forum variance is real, not noise." Reports of the same drug read like reports of different drugs because, pharmacokinetically, they nearly are.

✗

"Therefore any mechanism might be true for him." This is the move the page exists to refuse. Individual variation rescues a null average. It does not rescue a model that runs backwards, whose timescale is off by 1000×, or that rests on a misread source.

The distinction, once more, because it is what makes the list finite. An 8.5-fold spread is a fact about magnitude. It says an effect measured as zero on average can be large in one person. It says nothing about direction. A candidate that predicts the opposite of what he reports is not saved by variance, because it is not claiming an average.
He knew this before the project did. "Every study will have counter-evidence since for every measured effect, there will be some population that doesn't experience that effect, and even some population that may experience its opposite." Abanades is what that sentence looks like as a measurement.
