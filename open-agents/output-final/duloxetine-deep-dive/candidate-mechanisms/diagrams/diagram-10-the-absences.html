The six things nobody has studied

These are the highest-confidence claims on the page, because they assert the least. They are not hedging, and they must not be softened. An absence found by searching is a finding, not a failure.

1

Nobody has studied duloxetine in autistic adults

One three-patient case series exists. That is the entire literature. [no evidence exists]

2

Nobody has studied the overwhelm-to-crash cycle pharmacologically

The only pharmacological entry in the entire autistic-burnout literature is a single conference-abstract case report of a stimulant. The recovery literature is entirely environmental and behavioural: rest, self-understanding, reduced masking, changed expectations. There is no pharmacology in it at all. [no evidence exists]

3

Nobody has studied whether prior dissociation predicts recurrence

The question that would bear most directly on what his escitalopram reaction means for his duloxetine future. [no evidence exists]

4
THE ONE THAT SHAPES THE WHOLE LIST

Nobody has studied this decoupling in any human, for any drug

A drug that worsens acute stress reactivity while preventing the delayed sequela. Searched directly and from several angles: antidepressants and delayed stress sequelae; drugs decoupling acute reactivity from aftermath; prophylaxis of prolonged post-stress states. This is not "the evidence is weak." It is that the question has never been asked in a human. [no evidence exists]

5

Nobody has measured what duloxetine, or any SNRI, does to daytime sleep propensity

No MSLT study exists at any dose in any population. A PubMed search for duloxetine plus tonic plus phasic returns exactly one record, and it is about chin EMG. [no evidence exists]

6

Nobody has recorded a locus coeruleus neuron on chronic duloxetine in an awake animal

Let alone a human. The only chronic-duloxetine electrophysiology study (Rueter 1998) recorded the dorsal raphe and hippocampus and never touched the LC. The only chronic-duloxetine LC recording (Alba-Delgado 2012) is anaesthetised, in a neuropathic pain model, with noxious paw stimulation. [no evidence exists]

Why absence #4 explains the shape of the whole candidate list
What that means for his observation. A person who converted sensory overwhelm into a two-week collapse for decades, took a drug for two weeks, then absorbed five hours of two crowded festivals and two panic attacks with a heart rate above 140, and did not collapse, has produced an observation the literature has no entry for. That does not make the observation right. It makes it data, and it is a reason to record it carefully rather than a reason to believe it.
These absences are not a gap in the searching. They are a gap in the field. And note what absence #2 implies specifically: if his crash is an autistic shutdown or burnout phenomenon, then a drug appearing to prevent it is the only pharmacological observation of the phenomenon that exists, in a literature that contains none.
