The discriminators that remain available at 20mg
1

Order of effects — the strongest, and it is free

His model must run through the fatigue limb, because Okifuji found the direct distress→pain path was null and only distress→fatigue→pain survived. So: track fatigue and pain as separate lines. If pain improves while fatigue does not, his model is dead, whatever else is helping. [primary, verified]

2

Contingency on exposure — unique to his model

His benefit is purchased by crashes that did not happen, so it is contingent on exposure history and should be step-wise. The standard model's benefit is a pharmacological constant. The killer within-subject test: if a crash does break through, does pain step up and STAY up for weeks? If it returns to baseline in days, the aggravation limb is falsified even though crash-prevention would still be real.

3

Speed of onset

Direct analgesia is fast; allostatic unloading is slow by construction. Pain dropping within 2–4 weeks of a dose change means direct pharmacology. Pain drifting down over months at a fixed dose means his model, or noise. Bergenheim supplies the noise floor: 13% mean improvement over 10–12 years, only 16% achieving ≥50%. Spontaneous large improvement in fibromyalgia is rare, so a substantial drift at a fixed 20mg would be genuinely surprising and therefore informative. [primary, verified]

4

Pre-commit, or the test is worthless

Specify how long and how much, in advance. Without a pre-registered threshold, any outcome reads as confirmation ("it's working, just slowly"), which is exactly how an unfalsifiable belief forms.

The sensory-gating dose warning belongs next to the trap. He has already accepted worse acute sensitivity at 20mg. The Jensen/Oranje ladder says a 1.5× step flipped the filter from intact to disrupted. He is contemplating 3×. And the claim that the acute window would re-open only "transiently" is unsupported: every study in that literature is single-dose, so chronicity has never been measured. [no evidence exists]
The asymmetry is the actual decision content. Cost of waiting: months of unrelieved pain, which is a real cost, and pain matters to him. Benefit of waiting: a clean, unrepeatable answer. That is a question only he can answer. This page's job is to make sure he knows the window exists before it shuts. Nothing here directs a dose. [reasoning, not evidence]
Conversely, and this is the part that makes waiting cheap to reason about: if he holds 20mg and pain is unchanged while crashes stay prevented, his pain limb is cleanly falsified and the dose decision becomes trivial. The information value is asymmetric in time, and it is available for free by doing nothing.
