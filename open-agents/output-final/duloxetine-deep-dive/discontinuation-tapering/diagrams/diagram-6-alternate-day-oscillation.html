Alternate-Day Dosing: A Mini-Withdrawal Every 48 Hours

"Just take it every other day" is intuitive, free, needs no compounding pharmacy, and is what a busy prescriber reaches for when there is no smaller capsule. At duloxetine's half-life it is precisely wrong, and the reason is visible in the shape of the two curves.

"Prolonging the inter-dose interval led to a pronounced increase in receptor occupancy variation at standard doses of all antidepressants. Variation increased as dosage reduced until the ED50 for each drug, which is often far below doses used in current practice. Therefore, increasing the dose interval at minimum therapeutic doses, or even half of that dose, likely increases the risk of withdrawal symptoms and cannot be recommended as a prudent strategy for tapering." O'Neill JR, Sørensen A, Taylor D, Horowitz MA. J Affect Disord. 2026. PMID 40848773. Applies explicitly to duloxetine.
It is wrong for the same reason the drug is hard to taper. The hyperbolic curve that puts the cliff below 20mg is the curve that makes a plunge to 6% of peak biologically enormous rather than merely arithmetically small. So the intuition and the pharmacology point in opposite directions, and the pharmacology is right. The better move from the same literature runs the other way: dosing more often at lower doses may reduce withdrawal symptoms.
