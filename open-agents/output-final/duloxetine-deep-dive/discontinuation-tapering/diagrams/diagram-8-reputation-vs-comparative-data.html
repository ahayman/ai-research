Is Duloxetine Notably Severe? Reputation Versus Comparative Data

This project's own earlier draft asserted that duloxetine's discontinuation syndrome is "notably severe relative to other antidepressants." The comparative data do not support that, and the correction is worth making explicitly.

What builds the reputation

All real, and none of it is an incidence comparison
Half-life logic

At ~12h (FDA range 8–17h), duloxetine groups with paroxetine and venlafaxine. Short half-life genuinely is implicated in higher incidence and greater severity. [primary, verified]

WHO pharmacovigilance signal

Across 31,688 reports covering 28 antidepressants, the strongest disproportionate reporting is for "paroxetine, duloxetine, venlafaxine and desvenlafaxine." [primary, verified]

"250% more online reports"

From a 2009 FDA advisory committee count. This measures online report volume, which is confounded by marketing exposure and patient-community activity. It is not an incidence estimate. [secondary — unverified]

What the head-to-head data show

These run the other way
Comparative dizziness

The one symptom measured across drugs in the same meta-analysis. Venlafaxine 17.5%, duloxetine 5.1%, paroxetine 2.9%. Duloxetine sits between them, closer to the bottom. [primary, verified — Henssler 2024]

Which drugs reviews single out

Desvenlafaxine, venlafaxine, imipramine and paroxetine are named as warranting particular caution. Duloxetine is not top of that list. [primary, verified]

Reporting volume is not incidence

A pharmacovigilance signal counts reports, not patients. A heavily marketed drug generates more reports at the same true rate. The signal is evidence that duloxetine is in the conversation, not that it leads it. [reasoning, not evidence]

Discontinuation dizziness, measured across drugs in the same analysis

The clearest head-to-head comparison available. Henssler 2024, Lancet Psychiatry.

The honest verdict, in two parts

✓ Duloxetine belongs to the difficult group. The half-life logic is sound, the pharmacovigilance signal is real, and this is a drug that deserves a proper taper plan. That much of the reputation is earned.
✗ Duloxetine is not established as the worst. Where duloxetine and venlafaxine have been compared directly, venlafaxine looks worse. The claim that duloxetine is notably severe relative to other antidepressants is not supported by the comparative data.
Why this correction matters rather than being pedantry. A reputation built from half-life reasoning and report counts can feel like evidence of severity while containing no severity comparison at all. Getting this right does not make duloxetine easy to stop, because the taper-granularity problem is real and it is structural. It means the difficulty is mechanical, arising from a missing dose form below 20mg, rather than pharmacological, arising from duloxetine being uniquely nasty. Those two framings point at different solutions, and only one of them is true.

Provenance: comparative dizziness figures [primary, verified — Henssler 2024]. WHO pharmacovigilance [primary, verified — Drug Saf 2022, 31,688 reports]. The 250% figure [secondary — unverified]. Contested status: whether duloxetine's discontinuation syndrome is worse than other antidepressants' remains formally unresolved. It is in the difficult tier; it is not established as the worst.
