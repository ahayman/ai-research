Where the Taper Cliff Is, and the One You Have Already Been Down

Serotonin transporter occupancy modelled as occupancy = dose ÷ (dose + 7.9), using Takano's fitted ED50 of 7.9mg. The curve is steep at the bottom and flat at the top, and every capsule that exists sits on the flat part. You have made this descent once already, on a different drug, and you are standing at duloxetine's version of the step that hurt.

60mg → 20mg: the ramp

~16 points

40 milligrams of travel. A capsule for every step: 60, then 30, then 20. This is the stretch a dose increase would add.

20mg → 0: the cliff

~72 points

20 milligrams of travel. No capsule at any step. This stretch is identical whether the dose goes up or not.

The one you already did

60 points

Escitalopram 5mg → 0, a year ago. It cost you two weeks, and every step above it cost you nothing. Duloxetine's version is bigger, and escitalopram at least had a tablet you could have split.

Read it from the bottom up. Half the milligrams, more than four times the biological distance, and nothing to stand on. The entire steep region of the curve lies underneath the smallest dose form the manufacturer makes. Hyperbolic tapering says the last steps should be the smallest; duloxetine makes the last steps impossible with commercial products. That is the taper-granularity problem, and going up does not create it.

What is new is that you are not reading this cold. You have already made one of these descents, and the shape of what happened to you is the shape this curve predicts. That does not tell you how duloxetine's version will feel, because your own matched-occupancy history refutes that inference (see the read-across diagram). What it tells you is that the arithmetic on this page describes something real, and that you are standing on the part of the curve where it bites.

Provenance: curve shape [primary, verified] — Takano 2006, PMID 16506079, ED50 = 7.9mg. Plotted values [reasoning, not evidence] — an Emax model fitted to n≈3 per dose, single-dose, thalamus only. Modelled 20mg (72%) and 60mg (88%) track the measured 71.3% and 81.8%, but the model was fitted to those points. Occupancy below 20mg has never been measured in a human [no evidence exists].
