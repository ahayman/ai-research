The Taper Options, Graded — With One Crossed Out

Six things a prescriber might suggest when the time comes. One of them is precisely wrong, and it is the one most likely to be offered, because it is intuitive, free, and requires no compounding pharmacy.

✗Alternate-day dosing
Contraindicated
At a ~12h half-life, duloxetine is a worst case. Do not. See the box below and Diagram 6.

Why this one is crossed out rather than merely discouraged

48 hours between doses is about four half-lives, so plasma levels fall to roughly 6% of peak before the next dose arrives. That is a small withdrawal every two days, indefinitely. In-silico modelling covering duloxetine explicitly concludes that lengthening the dose interval at or below minimum therapeutic doses "likely increases the risk of withdrawal symptoms and cannot be recommended as a prudent strategy for tapering" (O'Neill, Sørensen, Taylor & Horowitz, PMID 40848773).

The counterintuitive better direction, from the same literature: "Dosing more often (twice- or thrice-daily) at lower doses may reduce withdrawal symptoms." That is the opposite of the intuition, and it follows from the same maths.

★Compounded liquid / custom-dose capsules
Best precision
no trials
The real answer for the stretch below 10mg. Needs a prescriber willing to write it and a compounding pharmacy willing to make it. Worth establishing availability before it is needed, which is the point of asking now.
✓Percentage-based taper
10% of current dose every 2–4 weeks
Guideline-endorsed
The mainstream recommendation, and the practical form of hyperbolic tapering. Slower for long-term users. Requires a dose form that can deliver the steps, which is exactly what duloxetine lacks below 20mg.
✓Bead counting
Documented
no trials
Not folk practice: it is in the Maudsley Deprescribing Guidelines and the manufacturer permits opening capsules. ~250 beads per 30mg capsule, roughly 8 per mg. Workable to about 5–10mg, and accuracy degrades exactly where it matters most, because bead counts and bead sizes both vary.
✓Gradual reduction over ≥2 weeks
Label-grade
Perahia's own recommendation and what the label supports. A minimum, not best practice, and it does not solve the sub-20mg problem at all.
?Switching to fluoxetine to taper
Case reports
no trials
A real technique, because fluoxetine's long half-life tapers itself. Three catches here. Fluoxetine is a potent CYP2D6 inhibitor, so it raises duloxetine levels during the crossover rather than lowering them. It is purely serotonergic, so it does not cover duloxetine's noradrenergic component. And it is an SSRI, which is the class that dissociated this reader. Probably the wrong tool for this person.
Notice what the grades have in common. Every option that actually solves the sub-20mg problem carries the words "no trials." Bead counting has never been tested in an RCT, compounded liquid tapers have never been tested, and the alternate-day contraindication is modelling rather than an observed outcome. This is not a field with a well-evidenced answer that prescribers are neglecting. It is a field where the pharmacology is clear, the practice is reasoned, and nobody has run the study.
