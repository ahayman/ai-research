The composite picture across the whole set: mismatch negativity is elevated at both doses, habituation is delayed at both doses, and P50 gating breaks only at the higher one. Put together, that reads: detects more, filters less, stops responding more slowly. Which is a reasonable description of what a crowded festival feels like from the inside — and that resemblance is a metaphor, not a measurement.
Why this is yours specifically. Of every candidate mechanism in this deep dive, this is the only one that predicts your single most distinctive observation: that the acute overwhelm got worse. And the studies are escitalopram studies, which is the drug that failed you primary, verified ×2. That is two hits from one small literature, and it is why the candidate ranks where it does. It is also why the dose implication belongs on this page rather than buried in a mechanism chapter: it is the strongest mechanistic argument against 60mg in the whole project, and it is about the thing you most fear. What would falsify it: the acute overwhelm improving over months at a fixed dose, or a 60mg increase leaving the acute experience unchanged.
What this diagram does not settle, and the limits are severe. (1) It is a cross-study comparison, two papers rather than one dose-response experiment — and cross-study comparison presented as measurement is this project's single most repeated error, so it is flagged here rather than smuggled reasoning, not evidence. The mitigation is real but partial: same lab, same paradigm, same population, same design, consecutive, explicitly framed by the authors as a dose extension. That is about as clean as cross-study gets, and it is still not within-study. (2) No independent replication. A PubMed author search returns exactly this group and no others primary, verified — by absence. (3) The other lab found the mirror image. There are three papers in total on serotonin and P50 suppression in healthy volunteers, from two labs. Mann 2008 (PMID 17895917) ran the opposite manipulation: "selective serotonin depletion significantly disrupted PPI, but not P50 suppression" primary, verified. So lowering serotonin breaks PPI and spares P50; raising serotonin breaks P50 and spares PPI. Those are mirror images and they do not describe a single dial. Mann's authors invoke "theories relating to optimal levels of monoaminergic neurotransmission," and an inverted-U with different measures on each limb is the more honest reading. (4) It is escitalopram, not duloxetine. (5) n≈20 healthy men, single acute doses, laboratory EEG. (6) The leap from a P50 waveform to five hours at a crowded festival is enormous and unbridged reasoning, not evidence. (7) Whether the cost persists at a steady dose has never been measured no evidence exists.
