"Beats Placebo" and "As Good as 60mg" Are Different Claims

Only one of them was ever asked. Your dose has a positive anxiety trial behind it. It has never been compared to the dose you are being offered, and the arithmetic showing that is exact.

The claimStatusWhy
"20mg beats placebo for anxiety" ASKED AND ANSWERED: yes HAM-A total and psychic factor, fixed 20mg arm, n=84. primary, verified
"20mg is as good as 60mg" NEVER ASKED The pooled non-inferiority analysis excluded the 20mg arm. No comparison exists. primary, verified
"20mg is worse than 60mg" ALSO NEVER ASKED The absence cuts both ways and this page will not pretend otherwise. reasoning, not evidence
"20mg failed on somatic anxiety" OVERSTATED Stated against this page's own convenience: the somatic null comes from an 84-patient arm against a 158-patient comparator, at p=0.07 — a near-miss in an arm roughly half its comparator's size. That ratio is worse than the one this project rejected as an excuse when it appeared in the pain trials. It is weak evidence of absence, not a demonstrated failure. primary, verified
The sharpest comparison in the material, with its caveats wired on. Response NNT for anxiety at 20mg works out to roughly 5.6 (60% against placebo's 42%) reasoning, not evidence, against NNTB 11 for fibromyalgia pain at 60mg primary, verified. Read naively, that says your current dose has better evidence for the thing you report benefiting from than the target dose has for the thing you were prescribed it for. Read carefully, it is a cross-trial, cross-indication, cross-endpoint comparison built partly on a secondary-source table with known internal inconsistencies — which is exactly the kind of elegant convergence this project has been fooled by several times. It is suggestive. It is not a measurement.
What this diagram does not settle. The HAM-A point estimates shown above come from a secondary review, not the primary paper secondary — unverified. A version of them circulates with percentages attached ("45% vs 55%"), and those percentages are internally inconsistent with the absolute changes they are supposed to describe: if −14.7 is a 45% decrease the baseline would be ≈32.7, and if −15.3 is a 55% decrease the baseline would be ≈27.8, and GAD trials enrol at ≈24–26. Both implied baselines are too high and they disagree with each other. Do not put those percentages in front of anyone as facts. Use the absolute changes and the response/remission rates. The significance findings are solid; the magnitudes are not.
A guardrail that must not be dropped. Li 2018 found duloxetine improves HAM-A psychic but not somatic anxiety across all doses, in 8 RCTs and 2,399 patients primary, verified. So the psychic/somatic split is a dose-independent property of the drug, not a 20mg-specific weakness. And critically: the HAM-A "somatic factor" is a list of physical symptom items (cardiovascular, gastrointestinal, respiratory, autonomic). It is NOT your "anxiety without negative cognitive content." Mapping you onto it would repeat a category error this project has already made once, with non-fearful panic disorder. Do not.
