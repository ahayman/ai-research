The Arguments That Died, and How

This page has the most corpses in the deep dive. Two of them were, at various points, this project's headline reasons to stay at 20mg. You are entitled to know that the arguments pointing your way collapsed too, because a caution built on an untested premise is still an untested premise.

ClaimStatusCause of deathWhy
"Emotional blunting is dose-dependent, so don't go up" DEAD Logic No dose-response study exists primary, verified — by absence. Ma 2021's dose sentence cites Price 2009, a qualitative interview study in which dose was never systematically measured; Ma 2021 and Masdrakis 2023 both root to the same 1990 Hoehn-Saric case report, so two reviews asserting it are one datum, not two. Aşçibaşi 2020 (n=98): doses systematically raised, blunting scores FELL, P<0.001. Blunting also correlates with depression severity, raising the live possibility it is substantially a symptom.
"30mg is the efficient dose" DEAD Direct RCT Arnold 2012 (PMID 22971669): 30mg, n=155/153, adequately powered, pain P=0.202 primary, verified. Derived from PET modelling; nobody checked whether it had been tested. The honest 30mg SERT band is ~63–92%, so a point estimate was false precision to begin with.
"NE rises therefore sleep worsens at 60mg" DEAD Measurement Outcome data contradicts it. In the only fixed-dose 20/60/120 trial, insomnia went down 20→60 and the validated insomnia scale got better at 60mg. Built on an n=2 occupancy point with a CI of −20% to +100%. primary, verified
"The 1.5% somnolence rate" WRONG COLUMN Measurement That is a discontinuation rate. The label's dose-dependent incidence is 10–11% vs 3%. primary, verified
"Murakami 2015 shows 60mg fails pain" FALSE Misreading Separated on ≥30% and ≥50% responder rates (the endpoints Cochrane's NNT is built on) and on 6 of 7 pain secondaries. An earlier pass filed those under "global/function." They are pain measures. primary, verified
"Chappell 2008 shows 60/120mg failed" FALSE Misreading Not a 60mg trial. 98 of 117 (83.8%) blindly escalated to 120mg at week 13; mean dose 113.4mg. Its design is a dose escalation, so it cannot be evidence against dose-response. Authors call it "supportive," not pivotal. primary, verified
"~66 patients ever received 20mg" REFUTED Arithmetic ~193 (Goldstein ≈114 + Russell 79), both verified per-arm. Sultan's "66" sits inside a ≥50%-pain-relief subsection and its meaning is unresolved. Do not try to explain it — the refutation does not depend on the explanation. primary, verified
"Morning dosing cuts insomnia risk ~60%" UNSOURCED No primary Appears on commercial health-content sites with no citation and no traceable primary. Treat as fabricated until someone produces the study. no evidence exists
"Going up makes coming down harder" WEAK, not dead — True at the margin: more drug does mean more to remove. But the cliff is below 20mg and you are already on it. 60→20 costs ~16 occupancy points with a capsule at every step; 20→0 costs ~72 with none. It is the softest available reason to stay, and this page does not offer it as one. reasoning, not evidence
"POTS risk" DOSE-QUALIFIED — Real, and in primary literature with a formal HRS Class III recommendation behind it — but every duloxetine-specific source says "high-dose" and "chronic" and traces to 80–240 mg/day, four to twelve times your dose. Not a 20mg claim. A 60mg conversation. primary, verified
"A dose increase may worsen the acute sensory cost" ALIVE, held loosely — The one anti-escalation argument that survives, and it is human and dose-graded: 10mg spared P50, 15mg broke it. Limits: single lab, three papers, no replication, escitalopram not duloxetine, single acute doses, and the other lab found a mirror image arguing for an inverted-U. The word "transiently" is unsupported. primary, verified ×2
Why this diagram exists at all. Eight passes over this material produced not a chain of increasing accuracy but alternating over-correction: each pass correctly diagnosed its predecessor's methodological sin, then committed the same sin in reverse. Recency was not reliability. The signature defect was caveats filed in appendices while headlines spent unearned certainty. This page is the one where that failure would cost you the most, because it is the page attached to a decision with a date on it. So the corpses are shown rather than quietly removed, including — especially — the ones that were arguing for the cautious option.
What this diagram does not settle. "Dead" here means dead as an argument, not "the opposite is true." Emotional blunting is real and common (~46%), dose reduction is the standard clinical management, and case reports consistently support that it reverses on reduction. What collapsed is only the claim that a dose-response has been demonstrated. The honest statement is: nobody has ever measured whether blunting is dose-dependent, which is a reason for monitoring rather than for confident prediction in either direction no evidence exists. And one finding here is worth carrying even though it is not comforting: Goodwin 2017 (n=669+150) found blunting at 46% with no difference according to ANY antidepressant agent primary, verified. If part of your model is that duloxetine is a different kind of drug from escitalopram and therefore safer on this axis, the survey data do not support that. Whatever made duloxetine different for you, it is not that duloxetine is a low-blunting drug.
