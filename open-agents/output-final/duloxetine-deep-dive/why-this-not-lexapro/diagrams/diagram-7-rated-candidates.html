The Rated Candidate List

For this page's question only: why did escitalopram dissociate him while duloxetine did not? Rated for fit, not ranked toward a winner.

Two axes, because they come apart, and the way they come apart is the finding. Fit = how well the candidate explains his specific divergence between the two drugs. Evidence = how good the underlying evidence is, graded independently of whether it flatters the story. A candidate can score high on one and low on the other, and the two at the top of this table do exactly that in opposite directions.

Sensory gating ladderJensen 2008 / Oranje 2011 HIGHbest fit on the page WEAK1 lab, 3 papers total, cross-study, all single-dose, no replication Escitalopram measurably degraded his sensory filter. 10mg spared P50; 15mg broke it. Read as a person: detects more, filters less, stops responding slower. His episodes are triggered by sensory overwhelm, so a drug that breaks the filter makes his problem worse rather than better. An independent lab reproducing the 10-vs-15mg flip would raise it. A within-study dose-response design would raise it a lot. Any chronic-dosing study showing the filter recovers would lower it. None of these exists in either direction.
Individual variation is its own answerAbanades 2011 HIGHbut it dissolves the question rather than answering it STRONGn=10, per-subject fits, primary Nothing special happened. An 8.5-fold spread in individual EC50 on one drug at one dose means two similar drugs diverging in one person is expected, not anomalous. This is his own argument, and it is why "try and see" is correct practice. Very hard to falsify, and that is a weakness rather than a strength. A hypothesis that cannot lose is not doing much work. Its value is practical: it licenses acting on his own data.

FAILS on the differenceexplains half the question		 GOOD on the acute limbthe rescue is reasoning, and Zohar predicts the opposite Acute serotonergic drive delays habituation, disrupts gating, and induces depersonalization in 18% under blind challenge. In the main animal model of stress collapse, excess serotonin causes the collapse. That is his escitalopram experience precisely. But duloxetine is also predominantly serotonergic at his dose, so it should have done the same. Rescued by evidence that the opposing chronic adaptation is fast enough to matter at two weeks. Finished by anything confirming Zohar's direction (chronic treatment blunts acute responses) in someone resembling him, since he reports the acute is worse.

Genuinely distinct, and the strongest claim on the page to being drug-specific, which is what the question needs. Needs any evidence at all linking that site to a dissociative or sensory outcome. It currently has none. A non-manufacturer replication of a clinical consequence would count for more than further test-tube work.
Wrong dose, or an inadequate trial MODERATE UNFALSIFIABLE NOWwhich is not the same as false He may have been at the wrong escitalopram dose. And he stopped because it made him non-functional, which was reasonable, and which means that if the benefit needs a weeks-long adaptation, he never reached it. The natural companion to the row above rather than its rival. Cannot be tested now. Uncomfortable, because it implies escitalopram might have worked had the acute phase been survivable. Included because leaving it out would be flattering rather than honest.
SSRI emotional bluntingGoodwin 2017 LOWand the reason is instructive SOLID on prevalenceabsent on everything the candidate needs The explanation most people reach for. His escitalopram experience superficially reads like textbook blunting. Rated low because it does not discriminate between the two drugs, which is the only thing this question requires. Goodwin 2017 (669 + 150): blunting runs ~46% and "no difference according to antidepressant agent." If blunting does not differ between agents, it cannot explain a difference between agents. Whatever made duloxetine different, it is not that duloxetine is a low-blunting drug.

Reading the pills: Fit is about him. Evidence is about the literature. They are graded separately on purpose.

The pattern in the top two rows is the honest finding of this page

The candidate that describes his experience most precisely rests on three papers from one laboratory, none of which dosed anyone twice. The candidate with the best evidence explains his divergence by saying that divergences of this size are ordinary.

Neither of those is a verdict, and no combination of them produces one. If that feels unsatisfying, the alternative on offer is a satisfying answer that this project has now constructed and demolished eight times.

There is no winner on this page, and that is the output rather than a failure to produce one.

He asked for mechanism rather than prediction and candidates rather than verdicts. Nothing here directs a dose, and nothing here could.
