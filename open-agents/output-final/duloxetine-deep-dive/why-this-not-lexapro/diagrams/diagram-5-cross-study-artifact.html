The Cross-Study Artifact, Laid Bare

The "he needed less serotonin" hypothesis rested on one 10-point gap between two numbers. Here is every axis on which those two numbers differ.

The claim: duloxetine 20mg delivers 71.3% SERT occupancy; escitalopram 10mg delivers 81.5%. Therefore the drug that hurt him gave more serotonergic drive than the drug that helped, and the difference is the dose of serotonin. The entire hypothesis is the 10.2-point gap.

How big is each confound? Each of these is measured, not asserted

Region effect

6–12 points
Measured within the same subjects, same drug, same scan (Abanades 2011 per-subject data). primary, verified

Subject 1: midbrain 0.73 vs thalamus 0.67. Subject 10: midbrain 0.84 vs thalamus 0.72. Kim 2017 (n=12) found a ~1.5-fold regional difference in the concentration needed for the same occupancy. primary, verified

⚠ A quotation error corrected here. The previous build attributed to Baldinger 2014, in quotation marks and tagged primary-verified: "midbrain is significantly higher... thalamus is significantly lower." The abstract says no such thing. It says: "When compared to the striatum, higher SERT occupancy in the midbrain and lower values in the thalamus were reported." The word "significantly" is not in the source, and the sentence is Baldinger's background summary of prior work rather than its own finding. The direction is right and holds on Abanades' within-subject data, so nothing here changes. But a paraphrase printed as a verbatim quote, with a significance claim added, is the exact failure class this project has caught twice elsewhere. primary, verified — abstract retrieved this pass

Modality effect

23–61 points
Same tracer, same 7 subjects, same drug. Only the scanner changed. primary, verified

Mean difference 23.1 across regions; 26.1 in thalamus and striatum; 61.4 in midbrain. And no head-to-head PET/DASB vs SPECT/ADAM cross-calibration exists at all no evidence exists.

Escitalopram's own 10mg span

29 points
10mg has been measured anywhere from 59% to 88% (Sørensen 2022, 17 studies). primary, verified

⚠ Every prior build called this "between-study noise." It is not. Sørensen's Table 2, read directly this pass, shows the span is largely regional: the bottom is the insula, hippocampus and putamen (59); the top is the amygdala (88). It is regional structure and measurement noise confounded, and they cannot be separated, because the extreme regions are also the worst-measured (hippocampus ±23, insula ±15). See diagram 6. The conclusion is unaffected: you still cannot compare a midbrain number to a thalamic one. The reason changes, and it matters, because "it's all noise" was used to dismiss the very regional structure his own candidate is built on.

A citation correction that must travel with this section

The escitalopram figure was cited throughout this project as "Klein 2006, PMID 17235610, Psychopharmacology 2006;188:263–272." That merges two different papers.

Klein 2006 = PMID 16955282, Psychopharmacology 188(3):263–72. SINGLE dose. Escitalopram 5/10/20mg, n=5 each → 60±6% / 64±6% / 75±5%.

Klein 2007 = PMID 17235610, Psychopharmacology 191(2):333–9. 10 DAYS. Escitalopram 10mg, n=6 → 81.5±5.4%.

The volume and pages belong to the first; the PMID and the 81.5% figure belong to the second. primary, verified

And the consequence is sharp. Vienna also measured escitalopram 10mg at single dose: 64%. Match on dosing paradigm alone and duloxetine's 71.3% is higher, despite escitalopram's number coming from the region that reads highest. The hypothesis inverts on its own data as soon as one axis is matched. That is a demonstration of instability, not a replacement finding: it is still cross-modality, cross-tracer, and cross-laboratory.

The verdict

Sørensen 2022's own methodological conclusion, verbatim: "comparison of occupancy data from different brain regions is potentially not meaningful, as SERT is not equally distributed throughout the brain." primary, verified

Retire the comparison. Not "hedge it," not "note the caveats." Remove the numbers, because no defensible number exists on either side.

What survives needs none of it: escitalopram 10mg's range entirely contains duloxetine's, so the two are indistinguishable given the noise, and SERT occupancy is therefore not the variable that explains his divergence.
