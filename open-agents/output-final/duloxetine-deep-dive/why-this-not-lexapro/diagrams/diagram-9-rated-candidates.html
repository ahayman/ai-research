The Rated Candidate List

For this page's question only: why did escitalopram dissociate him while duloxetine did not? Rated for fit, not ranked toward a winner.

Two axes, because they come apart, and the way they come apart is the finding. Fit = how well the candidate explains his specific divergence between the two drugs. Evidence = how good the underlying evidence is, graded independently of whether it flatters the story. A candidate can score high on one and low on the other, and the two at the top of this table do exactly that in opposite directions.

Sensory gating ladderJensen 2008 / Oranje 2011 HIGHbest fit on the page WEAK1 lab, 3 papers total, cross-study, all single-dose, no replication Escitalopram measurably degraded his sensory filter. 10mg spared P50; 15mg broke it. Read as a person: detects more, filters less, stops responding slower. His episodes are triggered by sensory overwhelm, so a drug that breaks the filter makes his problem worse rather than better. An independent lab reproducing the 10-vs-15mg flip would raise it. A within-study dose-response design would raise it a lot. Any chronic-dosing study showing the filter recovers would lower it. None of these exists in either direction.

This is his own argument, and it is why "try and see" is correct practice. Very hard to falsify, and that is a weakness rather than a strength. A hypothesis that cannot lose is not doing much work. Regional occupancy and distribution — his ownKim 2017 / Baldinger 2014 / Sørensen 2022 HIGH on the escitalopram failuresilent on everything else PREMISE ESTABLISHEDreplicated across two labs — but application to him: no data exists at all The drugs deliver engagement nobody can distinguish globally, and distribute it differently by region. The form has published precedent doing the identical work: Kim 2017 explains the SSRI onset delay by a regional gradient (DRN > putamen, p<0.001, different Hill coefficients). The amplifier is real and named: a gradient at the raphe modulates autoreceptor feedback and can invert net serotonin across the whole projection field. Baldinger states his premise almost verbatim as established preclinical fact: "regionally varying SERT activity and antidepressant drug concentration." Untestable, not refuted. Escitalopram has been imaged in 14 brain regions; duloxetine in 3. Every region the dissociation literature implicates (insula, ACC, prefrontal) has never been measured for duloxetine at any dose. The 3 overlapping regions show parity. Made specific it is falsifiable in one PET scan nobody has run. Against it: as stated it is a schema with 28 free parameters; Baldinger kills the intuitive version (escitalopram occupies the amygdala more than average, so "it never reached the emotional regions" is unavailable); Sørensen calls escitalopram's regional profile "relatively consistent", in tension with Kim; and it predicts nothing he can check.
Escitalopram-specific allosteric S2 bindingMurray 2016 / Jacobsen 2014 WOULD BE PERFECTthe only candidate that is specifically and only about escitalopram — the exact shape the question needs DISCONFIRMED IN VIVO ×2mouse-grade caveats, and one significant positive Escitalopram binds an allosteric site and slows its own dissociation from the main site. ⚠ This project called it "untested, surviving by never having been examined." That is false. It was tested twice, in vivo, with purpose-built knockin mice. Murray 2016 (all-Emory, no manufacturer): no effect on hippocampal serotonin (p=0.297), none in tail suspension, concluding the site "may not produce meaningful in vivo effects." Jacobsen 2014: escitalopram's effect "unaffected by loss of the allosteric site." Jacobsen's COI reads "ALP, MC and CS are employees of Lundbeck Research, USA" — and it concludes against the manufacturer's own differentiator, which cuts for its credibility. ⚠ The finding that cuts against this demotion, reported because omitting it would be the selective reading this project keeps committing. Murray reports three results and one is positive and significant: in marble burying, removing the site meant a 10-fold larger dose was needed for the same effect (p=0.023). That is not "essentially nothing." Murray's own conclusion has two halves, and the second is "there may be a regional specificity in the role of the allosteric site" — which points straight at the row above. Also: Jacobsen's allosteric test was a secondary objective, and Murray's knockin expressed at only 10–30% of normal. Mouse-grade objections do not kill. It is wounded, not dead, and it moves to the bottom of the live list. The COI disclosure still belongs on the positive allosteric literature, which is substantially a manufacturer research programme.
SSRI emotional bluntingGoodwin 2017 LOWand the reason is instructive SOLID on prevalenceabsent on everything the candidate needs The explanation most people reach for. His escitalopram experience superficially reads like textbook blunting. Rated low because it does not discriminate between the two drugs, which is the only thing this question requires. Goodwin 2017 (669 + 150): blunting runs ~46% and "no difference according to antidepressant agent." If blunting does not differ between agents, it cannot explain a difference between agents. Whatever made duloxetine different, it is not that duloxetine is a low-blunting drug.
Dead — killed by logic, measurement, or a fact about him, never by heterogeneity. Do not resurrect.

And the death runs the unusual way: it makes the escitalopram negative stronger, so every candidate that has to explain the failure now has a harder job.

Reading the pills: Fit is about him. Evidence is about the literature. They are graded separately on purpose.

The pattern across these rows is the honest finding of this page

The candidate that describes his experience most precisely rests on three papers from one laboratory, none of which dosed anyone twice. The candidate with the best evidence explains his divergence by saying that divergences of this size are ordinary. The candidate he proposed himself is the only one whose premise is established rather than assumed, and it predicts nothing he can observe. The candidate that would explain everything has been tested and mostly failed.

Two of the six are strong partly because they cannot lose, which is a weakness rather than a strength. Neither is a verdict, and no combination of them produces one. If that feels unsatisfying, the alternative on offer is a satisfying answer that this project has now constructed and demolished nine times.

There is no winner on this page, and that is the output rather than a failure to produce one.

He asked for mechanism rather than prediction and candidates rather than verdicts. Nothing here directs a dose, and nothing here could.
