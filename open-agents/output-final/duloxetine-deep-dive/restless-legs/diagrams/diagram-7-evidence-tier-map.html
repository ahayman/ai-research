The Whole RLS Story, Sorted by What Kind of Claim It Is

Not "what is true," but "what kind of thing is this." The strongest evidence on this page is a single observation from one person, and that person is you.

TierClaimWhy it sits there
n=1, about you His RLS got worse on 20mg. Observed, personal, and better evidence about him than any group study here. It is the one thing on this page that is definitely about the right person. It is also the thing that survives every correction below.
primary, verified Ferritin ≤75 → consider oral iron. TSAT >45% → no iron. IRLSSG task force guideline. Verified exactly. He was already inside the treatment zone at ~70.
primary, verified Antidepressant RLS/PLMS increases are "uncommon" and do not disrupt sleep. Kolla 2018, 18 prospective studies, abstract retrieved verbatim. Duloxetine is not mentioned in it.
primary, verified Melatonin evidence points both ways. Whittom (n=8, no placebo, PLM up) and Kunz (n=9, open-label, PLM down). Same drug, same 3mg dose, opposite result. Both verified. Both tiny.
primary, verified Pregabalin + duloxetine works, and it makes you drowsier. Gilron 2016. Both halves come from the same trial. Earlier passes reported the first half and called the trial nonexistent.
secondary — unverified Iron panels ordered 92.1%; iron given to only 50% of those with ferritin ≤75. Conference abstract, single health system, weak. It still corrects the framing: the failure is acting on the result, not ordering the test.
secondary — unverified Augmentation: dopamine agonists 6.0%, gabapentinoids 0.9%. From a meta-analysis, not from the AASM guideline it is usually attributed to. The confidence intervals overlap. The commonly-quoted "5.6%" is a pooled-across-all-drugs figure, not the dopamine-agonist rate.
reasoning, not evidence Serotonin inhibits dopamine, therefore duloxetine worsens RLS. The standard textbook account, and a clean deduction given duloxetine's negligible H1 affinity. But the step has never been measured in RLS patients on an SNRI. Coherence is not evidence.
no evidence exists Duloxetine and PLMS. Never measured. At any dose. Ever. The claim that it raises PLM is a review extrapolating from venlafaxine. This is why the posture is monitor, not predict.
no evidence exists Whether correcting iron reduces antidepressant-induced RLS. Searched directly and repeatedly. Nothing. It sounds obvious that it would. That is an assumption, not a finding.
no evidence exists PLMS dose-dependence for any SNRI. The only escalation study confounds dose with cumulative nights of exposure.
Why the pyramid is upside down here, and why that is fine. On most medical questions the strongest evidence is at the population level and the individual case is the weakest thing in the room. On this question it is reversed, because the population evidence for duloxetine and RLS does not exist, and the reader has a direct observation. That does not make his observation proof of a mechanism. It makes it the thing worth measuring carefully rather than explaining confidently.
The four "no evidence exists" rows are not gaps in this page's research. They are gaps in the literature, and they are among the most useful things here, because each one tells you which confident-sounding sentence to distrust when you meet it.
The failure mode this diagram is defending against. Eight research passes over this material each correctly diagnosed the previous pass's error and then committed the same error in reverse. The signature defect was caveats filed in appendices while headlines spent unearned certainty. So the provenance tags are printed in the sentences on this page rather than collected at the bottom, and the tier of a claim is meant to travel with the claim wherever it goes.
