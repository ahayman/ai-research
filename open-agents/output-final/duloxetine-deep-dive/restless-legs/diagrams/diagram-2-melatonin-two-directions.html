Melatonin and RLS: Two Studies, Two Directions

Three earlier passes over this material reached three confident conclusions, in different directions. All three were wrong. Here is the entire literature.

Supporting linkTierWhat it actually is
Mechanism
Zisapel 1983, PMID 6412963		 Rat, in vitro Melatonin inhibited calcium-dependent dopamine release in rat hypothalamus, in vitro, in 1983. The inferential distance to "3 mg oral melatonin suppresses human striatal dopamine tonight" is very large. primary, verified
Circadian correlation
Michaud 2004, Ann Neurol		 n=7, correlational Salivary melatonin acrophase runs ~2h before the acrophase of leg discomfort and PLMS. Real, and seven people. primary, verified
Case report
SLEEP 2024;47(Suppl 1):A508		 n=1, abstract RLS controlled within a week of stopping melatonin. Conference abstract. secondary — unverified
Absence of a listing
American Geriatrics Society		 Negative signal Melatonin does not appear on the AGS list of drug classes identified as worsening RLS. secondary — unverified
Bonus finding
Whittom 2010, same n=8		 Same caveats Bright light significantly DECREASED sensory leg discomfort, with no effect on movements. Melatonin did the reverse. A clean double dissociation in eight people. Morning bright light is free and does not interact with duloxetine. primary, verified
Grade: WEAK-TO-MODERATE. Hypothesis-generating, not established. One unreplicated n=8 within-subject experimental study with no placebo condition, supported by a chain that is low-tier at every link, and contradicted on the same endpoint by an n=9 open trial in an adjacent population. Enough to justify a cheap, reversible stop-and-observe trial. Not enough to state as fact that melatonin worsens RLS, and not enough to dismiss the question either.
Sources: Whittom S, Dumont M, Petit D, Desautels A, Adam B, Lavigne G, Montplaisir J. Sleep Med. 2010;11(4):351–5. PMID 20226733. • Kunz D, Bes F. Sleep. 2001;24(2):183–7. PMID 11247054.
Why a melatonin holiday now would not be clean. Melatonin is fast on and off (t½ ~40 min), free and reversible, which makes it the cheapest experiment available. But the reader is two weeks into a drug whose early side effects characteristically attenuate over weeks. So if he stops melatonin now and the RLS improves, that is equally consistent with melatonin removal and with duloxetine simply settling in. A negative result (no change) is therefore more informative than a positive one.
