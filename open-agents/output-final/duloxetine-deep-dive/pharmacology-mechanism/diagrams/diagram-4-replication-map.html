The entire human evidence base for duloxetine occupancy

Every claim about what duloxetine does to transporters at a given dose, anywhere in the world, traces back to these four studies. The two legs are not equally supported, and the asymmetry runs opposite to the way it is usually reported.

✓

SERT: 25 subjects, 2 laboratories, 2 continents, concordant

This is genuine independent replication, and in small-sample PET pharmacology it is rare enough to be worth stating plainly. The Sørensen 2022 systematic review lists both studies as its duloxetine sources, and reports 13 participants at the 20mg dose specifically (Takano's n≈3 plus Abanades' n=10). Trust the shape. The decimals are still n≈3 per dose in Takano.

✗

NET: 23 subjects, 1 laboratory, same first author, never replicated by anyone

The tracer itself is used in Vienna and at the Karolinska, so the method is not unvalidated. But no laboratory outside Chiba has ever measured duloxetine NET occupancy in a human being. Neither study reached Emax. Neither reports confidence intervals. Their two ED50 estimates disagree with each other by 20% (76.8 vs 92.4mg). This is where the "single institute" warning belongs, and it belongs only here.

Why the London study is drawn in a lighter shade of history: this project spent three research passes believing it did not exist. The researcher encountered "Abanades 2011," concluded it was a misattribution of the Chiba work, and discarded it. The validator then reviewed that call and confirmed it, stating in writing that there was "no independent replication of either curve by any other laboratory." Both were wrong. A real paper was killed as fake, and it happened to be the only independent replication in the evidence base. It is restored here. The point is not confession: it is that this is what a thin evidence base looks like from the inside, thin enough that one ten-person study changes the shape of the argument, and thin enough that three consecutive passes lost track of it.
The usual reporting has this backwards. It flags the NET number as small and treats the SERT curve as settled. The truth is a third thing: SERT is the well-corroborated leg and NET is the leg with no replication at all. [primary, verified]
