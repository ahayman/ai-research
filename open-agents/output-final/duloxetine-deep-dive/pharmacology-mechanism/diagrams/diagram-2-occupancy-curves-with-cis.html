The finding that matters more than any number on this chart: the 20mg interval is [58.1%, 84.5%] and the 60mg interval is [71.1%, 92.5%]. They overlap across the entire band from 71.1 to 84.5. At n≈3 per dose, this study cannot statistically distinguish 20mg from 40mg from 60mg on serotonin transporter occupancy. Every decimal place ever quoted from it, including repeatedly by this project, is false precision.
And the NET point at 60mg carries no information at all. It is n=2. Its 95% confidence interval runs from −20% to +100%, which is why the bar above runs off the chart in both directions. This is the single point that anchors the whole "norepinephrine engages at higher doses" story. Note also that the fitted model misses its own best-measured point: it predicts 20.7% at 20mg against a measured 29.7% (SD 1.3).
Trust the shape, never the decimals. The shape, a steep rise flattening into a plateau by roughly 20 to 40mg on serotonin, replicates across two laboratories on two continents with different subjects, sponsors and scanners. The individual numbers printed on it are one small study's arithmetic. [primary, verified]
