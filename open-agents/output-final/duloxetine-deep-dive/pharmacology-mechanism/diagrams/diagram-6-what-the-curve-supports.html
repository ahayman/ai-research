What the occupancy curve can and cannot support

The same graph gets used for both kinds of claim, and only one kind survives the sample sizes. Sorting them is most of the work of reading this literature honestly.

Trust the shape. Never the decimals.

The curve CAN support these

  • The shape: steep rise, plateau by roughly 20–40mg on SERT Replicated by two laboratories on two continents, with different subjects, ages, sponsors, scanners and analysis teams. They agree. This is about as good as small-sample PET pharmacology gets.
  • At 20mg, duloxetine is predominantly a serotonergic drug Consistent with the 9:1 binding ratio and with both occupancy legs. This survives every methodological objection on this page.
  • Occupancy plateaus at or near the minimum recommended dose Sørensen 2022, 17 studies, 10 drugs, 294 participants: "no substantial increase of SERT occupancy with SSRI and SNRI doses above the usual minimum recommended doses." A claim about shape, so it holds.
  • Individual variation is large, real, and measured 8.5-fold spread in individual EC50; 5.7-fold in plasma; 59–86% striatal occupancy across ten men on one dose.
  • The last step down is far bigger than any step up A hyperbola read right to left. The ratio survives the confidence intervals because it is a claim about curvature, not about any point.

The curve CANNOT support these

  • "You are at X% occupancy" Not knowable for any individual without scanning them. The population mean sits inside a distribution nearly 30 points wide at a single dose.
  • "20mg differs from 60mg on SERT" The confidence intervals overlap across 71.1–84.5%. At n≈3 per dose the study cannot tell the doses apart. Any decimal quoted from it is false precision.
  • Any ED50, on either transporter No study reached Emax, so every ED50 is an extrapolation from a model with Emax assumed at 100%. On NET the authors state a straight line fits their data better (RMSE 7.3 vs 11.8) than the hyperbola that produced ED50 = 76.8mg, and that the value "cannot be precisely estimated."
  • "20mg is sub-therapeutic on norepinephrine" There is no NET occupancy threshold for any clinical effect, for any drug, for any indication. This is not a false claim so much as an unfalsifiable one. There is no line to be below.
  • Comparisons against other drugs measured in other studies Region moves occupancy up to 12 points within the same scan; changing only the scanner moves it 23–61%; escitalopram 10mg's own between-study range is 59–88%. The differences between methods are larger than the differences between drugs.
  • Any prediction of who will improve Occupancy does not predict response. Sørensen 2022 reports studies "did not find significant correlations." In social anxiety at 78% mean occupancy: "no correlation between serotonin transporter occupancy… and social anxiety improvement." Never validated for pain at all.
  • Anything resting on the 60mg NET point n = 2. 95% CI from −20% to +100%. It carries no information.
The practical consequence. Almost every claim on the left is about the shape of the curve, and almost every claim on the right is about a point on it. That is the whole distinction, and it is a reliable test to apply to anything anyone tells you from this literature. One more rule follows from it: where occupancy and clinical outcomes disagree, outcomes win, because outcomes are what a patient's experience actually depends on. Category 9 is built that way.
