The Trap

Your hypothesis and the standard account make different predictions right now, at 20mg, and identical predictions at 60mg. That asymmetry is the single most decision-relevant fact on this page, and it has a deadline.

Model A — yours

The crash-aggravation route

20mg prevents crashes → cumulative load falls → fibromyalgia / ME-CFS eases → pain improves slowly over months

No threshold to cross. The dose you are already on is enough.

Model B — the standing analysis

The noradrenergic threshold

Pain relief requires noradrenergic reuptake inhibition at ~40–60mg → descending inhibition engages → pain improves

20mg does approximately nothing for pain, ever.

At 20mg — right now At 60mg
Model A predicts Pain drifts down slowly over months. Fatigue moves first or alongside. Improvement is contingent on exposure history. A breakthrough crash leaves a durable pain signature. Pain improves.
Model B predicts Nothing. Pain stays flat and is indifferent to exposure. Whatever moves, it is not pain. Pain improves.
Can you tell them apart? YES  —  the predictions diverge completely NO  —  both models are satisfied

Going up to 60mg does not test anything. It destroys the experiment.

At 60mg you cross the noradrenergic threshold and you carry on preventing crashes. Both mechanisms are running. So if your pain improves at 60mg, both models are satisfied and you will never know which one was right. reasoning, not evidence

The 20mg window is the only clean test of your own hypothesis that will ever exist for you. It is open right now, it costs nothing but time, and a dose increase closes it irreversibly.

The converse is also true and it is good news: if you hold 20mg for three to six months and pain is unchanged while crashes stay prevented, Model A's pain limb is cleanly falsified and the dose decision becomes trivial.

The separating observation, and it is free

Model A must run through the fatigue limb. In the highest-resolution within-person dataset that exists in fibromyalgia (Okifuji 2011, PMID 20591745: 81 women, 30 days, sampled 3×/day, lagged mixed-effects models), the direct route from distress to pain does not exist:

fatigue → pain+7% epoch, +3% day   p<.001
pain → emotional distress+7%   p<.001
emotional distress → fatigue+7%   p<.001
emotional distress → pain (the direct path)NULL

The authors, verbatim:

"The failure of emotional distress to predict subsequent pain may seem counterintuitive." primary, verified

So the only surviving route is indirect: distress → fatigue → pain. That is the best empirical scaffold your model has, and it is also what makes your model testable. reasoning, not evidence — the individual legs are primary, the chained inference is not

Track fatigue and pain as two separate lines, not one. If pain improves while fatigue does not, Model A is dead, and whatever is helping you is not the crash route. If fatigue improves and pain follows with a lag, Model A gains real support. This is the sharpest and cheapest test available to you, and it works whichever way the dose goes.

The real trade, stated honestly

Note first that "just wait and see if pain improves at 20mg" is not sufficient, because placebo, expectancy and regression to the mean produce slow improvement under either model. reasoning, not evidence The test needs the fatigue-versus-pain ordering, and it needs a threshold set in advance, because without one every outcome reads as confirmation ("it's working, just slowly"), which is exactly how an unfalsifiable belief forms.

Cost of holding at 20mg Months of unrelieved pain, against a real (if modest) 60mg upside of roughly 1 in 11 for at least half the pain gone.
Benefit of holding at 20mg A clean, unrepeatable answer to a question you raised and that nobody has ever studied in anybody.

The information value is asymmetric in time. The trade is not "which model is right." It is "how much is the answer worth, against months of pain?"

That question belongs to you and your doctor. Nothing here directs a dose. This diagram's only job is to make sure you know the window exists before it shuts, because eight research passes went by without anyone noticing it.
