Why the Threshold Sits Where It Sits

Duloxetine is 9 times more selective for the serotonin transporter than the norepinephrine transporter (Ki 0.8 nM vs 7.5 nM). The two occupancy curves therefore do very different things across the clinical dose range, and that difference predicts the threshold. It is also much thinner evidence than it looks.

The two curves across the clinical dose range

Measured human occupancy. SERT: Takano 2006, thalamus, [11C]DASB. NET: Moriguchi 2017, thalamus, [18F]FMeNER-D2.

SERT (serotonin transporter) NET (norepinephrine transporter)

The inference, and it is an inference

STEP 1 — MEASURED

SERT is near its ceiling at 20mg. Sørensen's cross-drug synthesis: "reached an apparent plateau at approximately 80%—at the usual minimum recommended dose."

STEP 2 — MEASURED

NET shows no plateau anywhere in the clinical range: 29.7% → 30.5% → 40.0%.

STEP 3 — INFERRED

So the extra 40mg cannot be buying much more serotonin. Whatever the 20→60mg step buys is mostly noradrenergic.

STEP 4 — ESTABLISHED

And descending inhibition is the noradrenergic limb. So a noradrenergic threshold is exactly where you would expect analgesia to appear.

Two independent evidence streams, the occupancy curve and the clinical trials, put the line in the same place. That convergence is the reason to take the threshold seriously as a mechanism rather than a coincidence. reasoning, not evidence

Steps 3 and 4 are reasoning, not measurement. Nobody has measured what the 20→60mg step does to descending inhibition in a human being.

The cleanest comparison available: milnacipran

Same tracer, same region, same institute, in humans. Milnacipran is FDA-approved for fibromyalgia, which makes its dose-occupancy relationship a usable yardstick.

Now break it, because it is elegant and that is the warning sign

This project's single most reliable finding is that every elegant synthesis in it has had a disconfirming detail sitting within reach. Here is this one's. The occupancy numbers underneath the whole story are thin to the point of fragility.

SERT 20mg95% CI 58.1 – 84.5%
SERT 60mg95% CI 71.1 – 92.5%
NET 20mg95% CI 26.5 – 32.9%
NET 40mg95% CI 0.9 – 60.1%
NET 60mg95% CI −20.2 – +100.2%
The SERT 20mg and 60mg intervals overlap across 71.1% to 84.5%. At n≈3 per dose, Takano's data cannot statistically distinguish 20mg from 40mg from 60mg on serotonin occupancy at all.
The 60mg NET point, which anchors the entire "norepinephrine engages at higher doses" claim, is n=2 with an interval running from −20% to over +100%. It carries essentially no information.

Provenance of the whole evidence base: 48 subjects, 4 studies, 2 institutions. SERT has 25 subjects across 2 independent labs (Takano at NIRS Chiba, and Abanades at the GSK Clinical Imaging Centre, Hammersmith, London), so it is genuinely replicated. NET has 23 subjects, one institute, the same first author on both papers, and no independent replication by anyone, ever.

So the mechanism is a coherent account of why a threshold exists. The trials are the reason to believe there is one. Trust the trials. The imaging is a story about the trials, not a substitute for them. reasoning, not evidence

Two occupancy figures this page refuses to use

"NET ED50 = 76.8mg, which is above the standard 60mg maintenance dose." This is an extrapolation 28% beyond the highest dose tested, from a hyperbola whose own authors report that a straight line fit their data better (RMSE 7.3 linear vs 11.8 nonlinear), with no confidence intervals, and the model misses its own best-measured point by 9 percentage points. The 2025 paper's ED50 of 92.4mg has the same problem and disagrees by 20%. Neither number should carry weight. Moriguchi's own words: "In our data, linear fitting was better than nonlinear fitting. However, linear fitting is not theoretical, because occupancy over 100% is not realistic."
"30mg gets you ~79% SERT, so ~97% of the serotonergic benefit of 60mg." False precision: the confidence intervals overlap, and the honest 30mg SERT band is ~63–92%. More decisively, 30mg was tested directly in fibromyalgia and failed the pain primary at P=0.202, which settles the question without any modelling at all. A dose can be pharmacologically elegant and clinically inert at the same time.

And the reason no occupancy number on this page describes you. Abanades fitted individual EC50 values across 10 people and found an 8.5-fold spread: 0.54 to 4.61 ng/mL. Same drug, same dose, same protocol, ten people, an 8.5-fold range in the concentration each needed for the same effect. primary, verified

The population curve does not describe any individual in it. This is why forums show wild variance, why "try it and see" is sound clinical practice rather than laziness, and why every number here is a statement about groups rather than about you.
