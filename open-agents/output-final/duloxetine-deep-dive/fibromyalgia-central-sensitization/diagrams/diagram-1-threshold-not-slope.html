The Threshold, Not a Slope

Every duloxetine pain trial with a fixed or identifiable dose, plotted by dose and by what happened to the pain primary endpoint. The effect is not a smooth curve and it is not a flat non-effect. It is a threshold with a fragile plateau.

"120 mg was no more effective than 60 mg." "No dose effect exists from 60 mg to 120 mg." But adverse events ARE dose-dependent. Past 60mg the curves diverge.
At 60mg the effect is small enough that well-powered trials miss the primary

The sentence that settles it, which no pass of this project quoted until late

Cochrane CD007115.pub3, Authors' conclusions, verbatim:

"There is adequate amounts of moderate quality evidence from eight studies performed by the manufacturers of duloxetine that doses of 60 mg and 120 mg daily are efficacious for treating pain in diabetic peripheral neuropathy but lower daily doses are not." primary, verified

That is not a hostile reading of Cochrane. That is the sentence Cochrane chose to conclude with.

One independent synthesis disagrees, and it belongs here rather than in a footnote. Sultan 2008 (PMID 18673529, Oxford Pain Research, no author stock holdings, one of very few non-conflicted sources in this literature) concluded: "The dose of duloxetine made little difference to the result." primary, verified Weigh it against Cochrane's formal conclusion. Note Sultan pools all doses including 120mg. Two independent syntheses disagree. That disagreement is real and unresolved.

What this means at 20mg

A reported pain improvement that is "noted but minimal", with the effect "mostly psychological and with my sleep", is not a disappointment and it is not a placebo confession. It is the sub-threshold signature, and it is what the trial record predicts from 20mg. reasoning, not evidence
