The Arithmetic of the Exclusion

Duloxetine ran two nearly identical anxiety trials, then pooled them by prespecified plan to run the formal non-inferiority comparison. Add the arms up. Three of them reconcile to the digit. One vanishes.

Asked, and answered
“Does 20mg beat placebo?”
Yes, on HAM-A total and the psychic factor, at p=0.01–0.001, in n=84. No, on the somatic factor, at p=0.07.
Never asked
“Is 20mg as good as 60mg?”
Unknown, and unknowable from this trial. With n=84 against n=158 and no comparison performed, the trial had neither the analysis nor the power to answer it. The 60% versus 65% response gap is a naive between-arm eyeball, not a test.
“Beat placebo” and “as good as 60mg” are different claims, and only the first was ever asked. An earlier pass of this project described 20mg as showing “near-parity” with 60–120mg. That is a gap in the record being mistaken for a finding in it. The distinction is worth carrying into the appointment intact, because it is exactly the kind of thing that flattens in a ten-minute conversation.

Provenance: primary, verified. The arm sizes come from the three trials' own abstracts, and the pooling plan is stated in Allgulander 2008's abstract. The reconciliation is exact.
