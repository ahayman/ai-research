Does It Keep Working? The Famous Number, Dissected

You asked about durability directly. Here is the figure everyone quotes, where it comes from, and why its own design cannot support the thing it is used to say.

25%
of people on an antidepressant experience “poop-out”
Solomon 2005
PMID 15766292

What Solomon 2005 actually is

DesignObservational, not randomised. No control condition of any kind.
CohortNIMH Collaborative Depression Study, enrolled 1978–81.
DrugsTricyclics and MAOIs, because that is what people took in 1980.
Median interval20 weeks of maintenance treatment.
Its definition“Recurrence of depression while on maintenance pharmacotherapy.”
The fatal problem, and it follows directly from the design: in an uncontrolled observational cohort, “recurrence during maintenance” cannot distinguish tachyphylaxis from the natural course of a recurring illness, from non-adherence, or from a drug that never worked in the first place. Solomon 2005 does not measure how often antidepressants stop working. It measures how often depression comes back in people who are taking one. Those are different quantities and the design cannot separate them.
The other figure in circulation
The canonical range for antidepressant tachyphylaxis: verbatim from the source review, “Rates of tachyphylaxis varied from 9% to 57% depending on the patient population and duration of follow-up”; “studies of antidepressant tachyphylaxis are largely heterogeneous”; “few established treatment strategies exist.”
9% 57%
0%25%50%75%100%
A quantity whose credible range spans six-fold is not an estimate. It is a confession that the field has no measurement instrument.

The single most informative result, and it inverts the usual story

Zimmerman & Thongy · PMID 17854252 · relapse during continuation treatment
24.1%
relapse in people who responded to PLACEBO
vs
7.4%
relapse in people who responded to the DRUG
If people who responded to a dummy pill lose their response more than three times as often as people who responded to the drug, then most of what gets called “poop-out” is placebo-response decay. The phenomenon is real as an experience and largely illusory as a pharmacology. And note the arithmetic: 24.1% sits remarkably close to Solomon's famous 25%. That may be coincidence. It may also be the whole story, which is that the famous 25% is the placebo-decay rate wearing a pharmacological costume. That cannot be established from here, and it is flagged as a pattern worth noticing rather than as a finding.

Why these survive the withdrawal critique: the critique attacks the placebo arms, because those people were taken off a drug, which inflates their relapse rate. So the contrast (41.8% vs 13.7%) is confounded. But the 13.7% itself is not confounded by withdrawal, because those people did not withdraw from anything.
What that ~14% is, precisely: an UPPER BOUND on tachyphylaxis, not a measure of it. It bundles together:
agenuine loss of drug effect
bnatural illness course breaking through a partially effective drug
cpeople who stopped taking their tablets
Tachyphylaxis is at most 14% and probably well under it, because that figure is everyone who relapsed for any reason at all.
No placebo-controlled duloxetine data exists past 12 months.
On anyone. For any indication. Past a year, nobody has data on anybody.
Duloxetine-specific tachyphylaxis has never been studied.
Not “studied and found reassuring.” Never measured. Targeted searches for duloxetine tachyphylaxis, duloxetine loss of efficacy, and SNRI poop-out returned no study measuring it for duloxetine in any indication.
One trap worth naming, because it is the shape of source that has damaged this project before. An AI-generated medical answer site asserts that “there is no documented evidence of tachyphylaxis with duloxetine,” citing an open-label compassionate-use study. That is not a source. “No documented evidence of tachyphylaxis” in an open-label uncontrolled study that was not measuring tachyphylaxis is not evidence of absence. It is absence of measurement. And one counter-pattern worth knowing: where recurrence has been examined closely, second recurrences were significantly less severe than the first (25.1 → 21.6, P<0.0001). That is not the pattern tachyphylaxis predicts, because pharmacological tolerance predicts escalating failure. Attenuating severity looks more like a drug still doing something partial against an illness running its own course.

Provenance: Solomon 2005, the 9–57% range, Zimmerman & Thongy, Davidson 2008 and Perahia 2009 are all primary, verified (abstracts retrieved; quotations verbatim). The critique of Solomon's definition, the reading of Zimmerman, and the upper-bound argument are reasoning, not evidence. The two absences are no evidence exists (negative search findings).
