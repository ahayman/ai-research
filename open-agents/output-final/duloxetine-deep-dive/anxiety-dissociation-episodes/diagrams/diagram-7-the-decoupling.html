Propranolol-style acute blunting
the obvious PTSD-prophylaxis analogy		An anti-parallel, and it is fatal to the analogy. Propranolol is a beta-blocker: it lowers heart rate and blunts acute physiological reactivity. Your heart rate hit >140 standing still. The analogy predicts the exact opposite of your single most striking observation. It also requires a memory with propositional content, which your episodes lack. Discarded.
Nobody has studied this decoupling in any human, for any drug.
A targeted search was run from several angles: antidepressants and delayed stress sequelae; drugs decoupling acute reactivity from aftermath; prophylaxis of prolonged post-stress states; dissociation of acute and delayed stress effects. The human literature does not contain this study.
The acute half is explained byHuman evidence. Escitalopram degrades sensory gating and habituation at 15mg. Real, and it points the wrong way for a benefit.
The aftermath half is explained byAnimal evidence. Chronic antidepressants at 14 days prevent a stress-induced glutamate surge while corticosterone rises normally (Musazzi 2010, PMID 20052403). A real decoupling, in rats, and it predicts acute-intact rather than acute-worse.
Nothing has both.
So what is your observation worth? A person who reliably converted sensory overwhelm into a two-week collapse for decades took a drug for two weeks, then took five hours of two crowded festivals and two panic attacks at over 140 bpm, and did not collapse. That is an observation the literature has no entry for. The absence is not a gap in the searching. It is a gap in the field. That does not make your observation right, and one exposure is one exposure. It makes it data.

Provenance: the observation and the family's independent anticipation are your own report. The ruling-out arguments are reasoning, not evidence. Musazzi 2010 and the propranolol meta-analysis are primary, verified. That nobody has studied this decoupling in humans: no evidence exists (negative search finding, and it is one of the four evidence-absences this deep dive treats as its most valuable claims).
