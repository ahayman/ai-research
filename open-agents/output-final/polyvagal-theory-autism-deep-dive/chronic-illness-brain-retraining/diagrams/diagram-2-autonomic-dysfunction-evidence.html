Autonomic Dysfunction in Chronic Illness — What's Actually Documented

Before evaluating polyvagal-branded interventions, establish the scientific floor. The autonomic abnormalities are real. They're measured by mainstream cardiology and neurology, independent of polyvagal theory's contested claims.

Long COVID

Long COVID

Autonomic findings
  • Reduced HRV (HF power, RMSSD)
  • Elevated resting HR
  • Sympathetic-vagal imbalance
  • Orthostatic intolerance / POTS
  • Delayed HRV recovery after exertion
Scale

Persistent autonomic dysfunction in 71.9% of long COVID participants up to 3.5y post-infection. POTS most common diagnosis.

Key research
Ståhlberg et al. (2021) Am J Med 134:1451–1456 — "Post-COVID-19 Tachycardia Syndrome." Carod-Artal (2021) Rev Neurol. RECOVER-AUTONOMIC platform trial (NCT06305806, n=380, IVIG & ivabradine; results pending 2026).
Evidence tier

Confirmed — multiple systematic reviews 2023–2024.

ME/CFS

ME/CFS

Autonomic findings
  • Reduced HRV predicts fatigue severity
  • Autonomic dysregulation is core diagnostic feature (ICC 2011, IOM/NAM 2015)
  • Post-exertional malaise has autonomic signature
  • Ventilatory threshold ~ PEM threshold
  • Orthostatic intolerance frequently comorbid
Scale

Disabling for ~50% of patients at any given time. CDC 1999: 31.4% cumulative recovery probability at 5y, 48.1% at 10y (criticized as optimistic by patient orgs).

Key research
Escorihuela et al. (2020) J Transl Med — HRV predicts fatigue severity. Workwell Foundation HR-pacing protocols. International Consensus Criteria (Carruthers 2011).
Evidence tier

Confirmed — autonomic involvement well-documented; specific mechanism remains active research.

POTS & Dysautonomia

POTS

Diagnostic criteria
  • HR ↑ ≥30 bpm within 10 min of standing
  • (≥40 bpm in adolescents)
  • Without orthostatic hypotension
Scale

Estimated 3–6 million Americans affected. 80–85% female, most commonly women of childbearing age (Dysautonomia International).

Subtypes

Neuropathic, hyperadrenergic, hypovolemic POTS. Post-viral POTS has expanded dramatically since 2020.

Key research
Dysautonomia International. Vernino et al. 2021 consensus. POTS as functional vs structural debate is settled — POTS is not a functional neurologic disorder (2024 PMC11614728).
Evidence tier

Confirmed — measurable, reproducible criteria.

The scientific floor. Autonomic dysfunction in these conditions is real, measurable, and documented in mainstream cardiology and neurology. This is the legitimate empirical ground that polyvagal-informed clinical adaptations build on — and that the brain-retraining industry uses to legitimate cure claims that the autonomic data itself doesn't support. Knowing where the floor actually is helps separate the reasonable adaptations from the overreach.
