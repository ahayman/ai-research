The Autism × Chronic Illness Phenotype Cluster

Autism, joint hypermobility, POTS, and ME/CFS-spectrum fatigue cluster together in well-documented ways. For an autistic adult, this is one of the most likely real-world encounters with polyvagal-branded chronic-illness claims.

Anchor diagram Why this chapter matters for you: if you're autistic and adult, you have meaningfully elevated odds of being somewhere in this cluster. Many autistic adults discover POTS, hEDS, or ME/CFS-spectrum fatigue mid-life.

Autism

Lifelong neurodevelopmental condition with sensory, social-communication, and information-processing differences. ~2% adult population estimate.

Frequently co-occurs with hypermobility & autonomic differences

hEDS / HSD (Hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos / Hypermobility Spectrum Disorder)

Connective-tissue conditions causing joint hypermobility, often pain, fatigue, and autonomic features.

51% of neurodivergent vs 20% general population (Csecs 2022, OR 4.51)

POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome)

HR ↑ ≥30 bpm within 10 min of standing (≥40 in adolescents) without orthostatic hypotension. Most common dysautonomia in young adults.

Est. 3–6M Americans, 80–85% female (Dysautonomia International)

ME/CFS-Spectrum Fatigue (and Long COVID)

Disabling fatigue with post-exertional malaise. Autonomic dysregulation is a core feature. Long COVID has expanded this population dramatically.

71.9% persistent autonomic dysfunction up to 3.5y post-COVID
What the overlap means. If you're an autistic adult struggling with fatigue, postural symptoms, or orthostatic intolerance, you're statistically not alone — and your symptoms are probably not "just anxiety." The cluster is recognized in mainstream autonomic medicine (Eccles 2014 in JNNP; Csecs et al. 2022 in Frontiers in Psychiatry; Sussex Neuroscience, UCL Autonomic Unit, Dysautonomia International). This is also exactly where the brain-retraining industry has found its largest market — because this population has been underserved by conventional medicine for decades.
Sources: Csecs et al. (2022) Frontiers in Psychiatry 12:786916. Eccles et al. (2014) JNNP. Dysautonomia International POTS facts. 2024 long-COVID follow-up study reporting 71.9% autonomic dysfunction at 3.5y. Casanova et al. 2020 systematic review of autism-EDS prevalence.
