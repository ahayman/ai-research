The Brain-Retraining Landscape

Four major programs marketed for ME/CFS, long COVID, fibromyalgia, MCAS, and related chronic illnesses. Side-by-side: founder, cost, mechanism claim, evidence quality, regulatory status.

Program Founder & credentials Cost (USD) Claimed mechanism Best evidence cited Methodological flags Regulatory / official status
Gupta Program
(AIR)		 Ashok Gupta. Self-described ex-ME patient. Not a clinician. ~$399–$599 course access Amygdala & insula "stuck" in maladaptive fight-flight; "retraining" via thought-stopping & visualization Toussaint & Bratty 2023 RCT (n=100 randomized; 40 completed) 60% dropout; no blinding; active control (not sham); subjective outcomes; COI: co-author Bratty is paid Gupta consultant (disclosed in paper) UK ASA upheld 3 complaints (April 2018) — must not claim treatment of ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, or electrical sensitivities
DNRS
(Dynamic Neural Retraining System)		 Annie Hopper. Self-described "limbic system rehabilitation specialist." Not a clinician. $300–$500 online + $1,500+ for retreats "Limbic system impairment"; behavioral & visualization "rewiring" McMaster University retrospective (2016): n=102 → 64 completed Unpublished in peer review as of 2025. Observational, uncontrolled, self-selected, high dropout, self-reported outcomes No regulatory action; David Tuller (Virology Blog 2020) detailed critique
Primal Trust
(Regulate / Integrate / Liberate)		 Dr. Cathleen King, DPT (physical therapy). Self-described chronic Lyme survivor. Former DNRS practitioner. ~$60–$100/month subscription + higher tiers Explicit polyvagal framing + "cell danger response" (Naviaux) + somatics + attachment repair No peer-reviewed RCTs (as of 2026). Relies on testimonials & reported 13,000+ member count. No published evidence base at all. Combines multiple contested frameworks. No regulatory action
Lightning Process
(LP)		 Phil Parker, UK osteopath. NLP + life coaching background. £700–£900+ for 3-day course NLP-derived cognitive interrupting + "rewiring" via behavioral & verbal techniques SMILE trial (Crawley et al. 2018, Arch Dis Child) Primary outcome switched mid-trial (school attendance → SF-36 self-report). Journal editorial correction issued (Brown 2019). Open-label, subjective outcomes. NICE 2021 (NG206): "do not offer the Lightning Process, or therapies based on it, to people with ME/CFS"
The pattern. Four programs, four mechanism phrases ("amygdala retraining," "limbic system rewiring," "polyvagal regulation," "NLP cognitive interruption"). None has a sham-controlled blinded RCT. The best of the four (Gupta AIR 2023) has 60% dropout, active rather than sham control, and an undisclosed-in-marketing COI between an author and the program. The worst (DNRS, Primal Trust) have no peer-reviewed RCT at all. The Lightning Process is the only one with an unambiguous NICE-NHS official "do not offer" recommendation, and that recommendation followed a major outcome-switching scandal in its primary trial.
