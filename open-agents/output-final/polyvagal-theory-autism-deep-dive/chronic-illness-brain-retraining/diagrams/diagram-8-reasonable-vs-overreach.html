Reasonable Polyvagal-Informed Adaptations vs Polyvagal-Cure Overreach

The chapter's central distinction. Knowing which side of this line a given claim sits on is the user's most useful filter.

The line in the sand Some practices in chronic-illness care happen to be framed with polyvagal language. They work (with caveats) regardless of which framework explains them. Other claims invoke the same polyvagal vocabulary to sell cure-promise programs that the data doesn't support. Same words. Very different evidence.

Reasonable adaptations

What's on this side
  • Pacing / energy envelope — Jason et al.; Workwell Foundation; NICE 2021 recommendation. Strong independent evidence.
  • Slow-paced breathing (~6 breaths/min, 0.1 Hz) — Lehrer/Gevirtz body of work. Moderate-strong evidence for hypertension, pain, PTSD; emerging for long COVID dysautonomia (HEARTLOC 2024).
  • HRV biofeedback — independent Lehrer-Vaschillo protocol; HeartMath / Inner Balance consumer-grade implementations.
  • Autonomic-aware activity pacing — HR ceiling, ventilatory threshold monitoring, Workwell HR-pacing protocols.
  • Trauma-aware / safety-first orientation — recognizing the psychological cost of years of medical dismissal; validating symptom reports.
  • Sleep optimization — strongly evidence-based for chronic illness recovery.
Why these qualify

Each has an evidence base that predates and does not depend on polyvagal theory. Calling them "polyvagal-informed" is scaffolding language, not mechanism claim. They work — within their evidence-tier ranges — even if PVT turns out to be wrong.

Cost to try most of these: low or free. Risk: low. Reversibility: high. These are the things to try first.

Polyvagal-cure overreach

What's on this side
  • "Rewire your nervous system in 8 weeks" — timeline claims unsupported by evidence about adult neural plasticity in chronic disease.
  • "Cure ME/CFS / long COVID via brain retraining" — cure claims that go beyond the symptom-management evidence the practices have.
  • "Polyvagal-informed practitioner certifications" sold without clinical scope, by trainers without medical credentials.
  • Condition-agnostic single protocol — same program for MCS, mold illness, ME/CFS, fibromyalgia, long COVID, MCAS, suggesting all are "limbic dysfunction" with one cause.
  • "Stuck in dorsal vagal" diagnoses applied without autonomic measurement.
  • "Polyvagal supplements / vagal tone tonics" — Cat 8 territory.
  • "Push through your PEM" framing in retraining programs, against ME/CFS clinical consensus.
Why these don't qualify

The claims invoke polyvagal vocabulary for credibility but are not supported by either polyvagal evidence (Register 1 is too narrow to support them) or by the broader autonomic medicine literature. The "mechanism" is metaphor, the "evidence" is testimonials, and the cost is often substantial.

Cost to try: $300–$1,500+. Risk: financial, time, PEM crash, sense of self-blame. Reversibility: financial loss not refundable; PEM crash potentially long-lasting.

The distinction in one sentence

If a polyvagal-framed practice has independent evidence from outside polyvagal theory and doesn't claim to cure your disease — it's probably reasonable. If a polyvagal-framed program claims to cure your disease based on its own internally-developed evidence and uses vague mechanism language — it's overreach. This single test is portable across the wellness landscape.
