The Concrete Evaluation Framework

10 questions to ask of any brain-retraining or "rewire your nervous system" claim. Portable beyond polyvagal-branded programs — applies to any commercial chronic-illness intervention.

User tool

Print this. Paste it into your notes app. When you encounter a new program, work through it before paying. A "no" or "vague" on multiple questions is a strong signal the evidence claim deserves skepticism, regardless of how many testimonials are featured in marketing.

1

Is the mechanism specified?

Does the program offer a specific, testable neural-circuit mechanism? Or vague "neuroplasticity," "limbic system," "vagal tone," "amygdala retraining" language?

Specific circuits, falsifiable predictions Vague metaphor language
2

Is there an RCT with a credible sham control?

Not just any RCT — a randomized, blinded trial with a sham control. An "active control" of general health education is weaker; a sham group that doesn't know they're getting the placebo is the proper standard.

Blinded sham control No RCT; or active control only; or no blinding
3

Was the primary endpoint pre-specified and unchanged?

Was the primary outcome registered (e.g., on clinicaltrials.gov, ISRCTN) before data collection began? Did it stay the same throughout the trial? Outcome-switching is the PACE/SMILE pattern.

Pre-registered, unchanged Switched mid-trial; or never registered
4

Are the outcomes objective?

Self-reported fatigue and "feeling better" questionnaires are vulnerable to expectancy and demand effects. Objective measures (HR/HRV, actigraphy, return to work, school attendance, hospitalization) are more robust.

Objective endpoints Self-report only
5

Are harms reported honestly?

Does the program/study systematically collect and report adverse events — crashes, PEM exacerbations, financial harm, psychological harm from feeling blamed? Or are harms invisible because nobody asked?

Structured adverse-event reporting No harm data; or harms only in advocacy literature
6

Does the study control for natural recovery?

ME/CFS has substantial natural fluctuation (CDC 1999: 31% cumulative recovery at 5y). Post-viral fatigue and long COVID often resolve over months to years. A program claiming to cure must distinguish its effect from this background recovery.

Wait-list / matched control comparison No control, or controls inadequate to natural recovery
7

Is selection bias addressed?

Self-paying, motivated, less-severely-affected patients self-select into expensive programs. The trial population may not represent the general patient population. Severe/housebound patients are often excluded entirely.

Severity range documented; representativeness analyzed Self-selected sample; severe cases excluded
8

Is the evaluation independent?

Has the program been evaluated by researchers without commercial ties to its sale? Or are all positive studies authored or co-authored by founders, paid consultants, or close collaborators?

Multiple independent replications All studies by program-linked researchers
9

Is the COI transparently disclosed — especially in consumer-facing materials?

Academic publications have COI disclosure norms. Marketing materials usually don't. Look for whether researcher-program financial relationships are clearly stated where patients encounter the claims.

COI disclosed in both academic & marketing channels COI only in journal small-print, not in marketing
10

Is the founder qualified for the medical claims being made?

Is the founder a clinician with relevant expertise (viral pathophysiology, immunology, autonomic medicine)? Or are they a coach, NLP practitioner, or self-described former patient making strong claims about complex diseases?

Relevant clinical/research credentials No relevant credentials; coach/practitioner positioning
How to use this. For each question, mark good signal, bad signal, or unclear. A program that scores "good" on 7+ questions is rare and worth serious consideration. A program scoring "bad" on 7+ questions deserves heavy skepticism regardless of testimonials. The Gupta Program currently scores roughly 2 partial / 8 poor. DNRS and Primal Trust score even lower because the studies don't exist. The Lightning Process has NICE-NHS official "do not offer" status. Use the framework before paying; not after.
