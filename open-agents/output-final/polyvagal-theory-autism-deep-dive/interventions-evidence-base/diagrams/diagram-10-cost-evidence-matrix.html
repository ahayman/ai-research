How to read this. The horizontal axis is direct out-of-pocket cost (time costs apply across the board; only money is plotted). The vertical axis is the strength of the published efficacy evidence for the specific use case relevant to an autistic adult with burnout. Tier classifications come from the framework in Diagram 1.
Risk overlay. Items not shown here that would fall on a third axis: Wim Hof + water (Tier E), holotropic breathwork unscreened (Tier E), brain-retraining cure-claims for ME/CFS (Tier E). These are kept off the matrix because cost/evidence misses the relevant variable for them.
The dominant strategy. Start in the top-left quadrant. Exhaust the free, high-evidence interventions before paying for anything in the bottom-right. If you do pay, prefer trauma-sensitive yoga with a certified instructor (top-right) over consumer wearables (bottom-right). For acute crisis interruption: cold face splash. For sustained autonomic flexibility: daily slow breathing + humming. For trauma context: TSY. The expensive devices add convenience and novelty but rarely add evidence-based benefit beyond what these basics provide.
