taVNS Current Evidence State

Tracking transcutaneous auricular vagus nerve stimulation specifically in autism, from feasibility studies through pre-registered RCTs in progress.

Anatomy first. The auricular branch of the vagus nerve (Arnold's nerve) innervates the cymba conchae of the outer ear. This is real, well-characterized anatomy. Electrical stimulation of this region produces measurable central vagal activation in healthy subjects. The mechanism is among the most anatomically grounded of any consumer-adjacent "vagal" intervention.
2023 · September
PMC10568329 — At-Home Feasibility Pilot in Autism
12 patients with ASD (ages 7–16). Daily 1-hour sessions, 2 weeks, remote supervision via specially designed at-home equipment platform. 88.5% completion across 132 sessions. Anxiety (CASI-R, PRAS-ASD, CGI) and sleepiness (CASQ) improved at 2 weeks. No sham control. Adverse events: mild headache, transient electrode-site pain, itchiness, stinging. Frontiers in Psychiatry.

2024 · Published protocol
PMC10875019 — Pre-Registered RCT Protocol (Pending Results)
~80 children with high-functioning ASD. 40 sham arm, 40 active arm. Double-blind. Pre-registered primary endpoint. This is the methodological design the user can trust. When results publish, they will be the most informative single piece of taVNS-in-autism evidence to date. Frontiers in Psychiatry.

2025–2026 · Expected
PMC10875019 Completed Results
Expected to publish results during 2026. If active arm shows significant effect on pre-specified primary endpoint with adequate effect size, taVNS in autism moves from Tier B/C → Tier B (clear) or A. If no significant effect, the autism-specific case shrinks substantially. Either outcome is high-information.
Pending
Verdict as of 2026-05-21. taVNS in autism currently sits at Tier B–C. The anatomical mechanism is genuinely grounded, the feasibility study showed encouraging signals on anxiety and sleepiness, and a methodologically appropriate RCT is in the publication pipeline. This is the most evidence-strong "vagal stimulation" pathway for autism that is not yet over-marketed. Note: consumer auricular stimulators are not equivalent to research-grade taVNS — the trials use specific stimulation parameters and supervised dosing that consumer devices typically do not match.
