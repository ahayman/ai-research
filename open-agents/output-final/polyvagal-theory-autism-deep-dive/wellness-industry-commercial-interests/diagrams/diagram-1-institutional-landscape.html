The two anchor institutions
Polyvagal Institute (PVI)
501(c)(3) non-profit · Denver, CO · EIN 84-5084757 · Founded 2020
Founders: Porges, Dana, Onderko, Redfield.
Revenue (2024): ~$807K. Activities: training (80%), research (10%), community (10%). Grants made (2024): $0.
Offerings: Certificate Course (35 CEs); "Polyvagal-Informed" credential; Organization Certification; Embodied Equity course; learning library.
Unyte Health (formerly Integrated Listening Systems)
For-profit private company · Commercial home of SSP since 2017
Primary product: Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP) — subscription-based, sold through trained professionals.
Porges: Chief Scientific Advisor + patent royalty recipient.
Dana: Advisor.
Other products: Rest and Restore Protocol, Focus System, PhysioCam.
Key individuals (linked to one or both)
Stephen W. Porges, PhD
Theory originator (1994). Co-founder PVI. Chief Scientific Advisor Unyte. Patent royalty recipient on SSP and PhysioCam tech. Author for W. W. Norton. Authored 2025 polyvagal-theory update (PMC12302812).
Deb Dana, LCSW
Co-founder PVI. Advisor to Unyte. Founder of Rhythm of Regulation® (independent commercial training). Most influential popularizer among therapists. Multiple Norton + Sounds True trade titles.

Trade publishers (the trade-publishing pipeline)
W. W. Norton Professional Books / Norton Series on Interpersonal Neurobiology publishes most of the canonical clinical/popular polyvagal titles (Porges, Dana, Kase, Inderbitzen, Badenoch). Viking / Penguin Random House for van der Kolk. Sounds True for Dana's Anchored and Badenoch's course material. Jessica Kingsley Publishers + LicenseToThink/Zebr for Bridges. Publisher concentration matters: Norton's series is the principal vehicle for the theory's transmission from clinical neuroscience to clinical-practitioner readership.

Key relationships across this map:
Porges → PVI (co-founder) → teaches paid certificate courses → students self-identify as "Polyvagal-Informed" → some launch independent coaching businesses
Porges → Unyte (Chief Scientific Advisor) → receives royalties on SSP → consumers / clinicians pay subscriptions
Popular authors → Norton (mostly) → trade royalties on book sales → readers enter the framework → some enroll in PVI training → downstream demand for the theory
Consumer-device manufacturers → direct retail → consumers (independent of PVI/Unyte, but borrowing the framework's vocabulary)
Five categories of actor sit in the polyvagal-theory ecosystem: one non-profit (PVI), one anchor for-profit (Unyte), key individuals with overlapping roles, trade publishers that amplify the theory, and a consumer-product layer that borrows the theory's vocabulary. The popular-author chain is concentrated in a small number of trade-publisher imprints, with W. W. Norton's Interpersonal Neurobiology Series carrying most clinical titles. Most consumer "vagus nerve" devices marketed in this ecosystem are not FDA-cleared as medical devices.
