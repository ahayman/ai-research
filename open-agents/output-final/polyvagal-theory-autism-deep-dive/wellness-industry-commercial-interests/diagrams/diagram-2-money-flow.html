The structural point. None of this is illegal or unusual. Conflict of interest in commercialized scientific frameworks is common. The point is: the people generating public claims about how well the framework works are often the same people who profit when the framework remains popular. Consumer-facing communications about evidence rarely include the conflict-of-interest disclosures that academic communications are required to include. Disclosure asymmetry is the structural pattern the user should expect.
Multiple revenue streams converge on the polyvagal-theory ecosystem: patent royalties (Porges ↔ Unyte / NeuralSolution), trade-book royalties (Norton, Viking, Sounds True, Jessica Kingsley), course tuition (PVI, Rhythm of Regulation, Kase & CO, PESI, Embody Lab), clinical-product subscriptions (SSP via Unyte), and direct-to-consumer device and supplement sales. Aggregate consumer "vagus nerve device" market is in the hundreds of millions of dollars annually. Most consumer products operate under the FDA general wellness device framework — which requires no clinical efficacy demonstration.
