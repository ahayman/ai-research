McEwen's Allostatic Load Curve — Applied to Autistic Experience

McEwen's allostatic load model is the framework Raymaker's autistic-burnout research actually uses. The curve maps cumulative wear-and-tear over time: as autistic-specific stressors (sensory mismatch, masking demand, social-communication mismatch, transition cost) keep loading the system without adequate recovery, the curve moves from healthy allostasis through four allostatic-load conditions into allostatic overload — the pathophysiological state experienced as autistic burnout.

1. Healthy allostasis

Stress responses scale to demand and recover when demand drops. Flexibility intact.

e.g., manageable sensory days, supportive environment, adequate recovery, well-paced demands

2. Allostatic load (4 conditions, McEwen)

(a) Repeated hits from multiple novel stressors. (b) Lack of adaptation — same stressor still costs full activation. (c) Prolonged response — system doesn't shut off. (d) Inadequate response — compensatory hyperactivity of other mediators.

e.g., autistic-specific: sensory mismatch, masking demand, social-communication mismatch, transition cost, double empathy load

3. Allostatic overload

Cumulative load exceeds capacity for recovery. System operates with chronic dysregulation across HPA-axis, autonomic, immune, and metabolic systems. Tolerance for new stressors drops sharply.

e.g., shutdowns now occur from "normal" demands, mounting fatigue, masking has higher cost, recovery time required after small events

4. Autistic burnout

Pathophysiological state: chronic exhaustion, skill loss, reduced stimulus tolerance. Duration 3+ months (often 6–18). Recovery requires systemic demand reduction, not acute interventions.

e.g., loss of previously-managed skills (phone calls, public transport, cooking), sensory hypersensitivity, weeks-to-months of demand reduction needed
Why this curve matters for the chapter's argument This is the empirical framework. Raymaker's research explicitly invokes "cumulative load." Mantzalas's Conceptual Model integrates Job Demands–Resources and Conservation of Resources. The mitochondrial-allostatic-load framework (PMC9732262) directly invokes McEwen. Treatment implications follow the curve, not the polyvagal ladder: demand reduction, recovery time, removal of chronic stressors, accommodation. Acute interventions (breathwork, cold exposure, "ventral vagal activation") cannot reverse a curve built over months — that takes systemic change.
