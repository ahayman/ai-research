Meltdown — The State With Some Empirical Anchoring

An autistic meltdown is an involuntary response to overwhelming input. Of the three states in this chapter, meltdown has the closest mapping to measured autonomic data. The polyvagal framing as "sympathetic mobilisation" is consistent with Cheng 2020's HRV reactivity findings — not a direct test, but a defensible reframing.

Phenomenology → Trigger Path

1

Cumulative load is high (often unnoticed)

Sensory mismatch (light, sound, texture, smell), demand load (decision-making, social interaction, change), accumulated chronic stress. Threshold is lower than usual.

2

Trigger event (often "small")

A minor sensory event, a small request, an unexpected change. The visible trigger is the last item over a threshold that was already nearly breached, not the cause.

3

Threshold breach → autonomic recruitment

Mobilisation surge. Cardiovascular activation. Sensory amplification. Cognitive narrowing.

4

Meltdown — outward dysregulation

Loss of behavioural control. Crying, shouting, motor agitation. Inability to "use words" or self-soothe in the moment. Not a tantrum (not goal-directed). Not chosen.

5

Recovery — sensory reduction + safety

Quiet space, low light, no demands, sometimes physical containment (own choice). Recovery takes hours, not minutes. Premature re-engagement re-triggers.

Empirical Anchor

Cheng, Huang & Huang (2020)

Neurosci Biobehav Rev 118:463–471. Meta-analysis of 34 HRV studies in autism.

Baseline parasympathetic HRV

Hedges' g = −0.5168, p < 0.0001. Lower than non-autistic comparison groups.

Baseline RSA

Hedges' g = −0.5860, p = 0.0010. Lower respiratory sinus arrhythmia at rest.

RSA reactivity to social stress

Hedges' g = −0.7246, p < 0.0001. The largest effect in the meta-analysis. Reduced parasympathetic withdrawal under social stress → relatively greater sympathetic dominance under stress.

What this evidence does and does not show Cheng 2020 measured HRV in laboratory paradigms — not meltdown directly. The reframing "meltdown = sympathetic over-arousal" is consistent with the data but not a direct test. The inferential chain is plausible but one step removed from naturalistic measurement.
Verdict: empirically defensible reframing. Of the three states in this chapter, meltdown is the one where polyvagal language sits closest to measured physiology. Use the framing with appropriate hedges ("consistent with," "the closest mechanism we have measured"), not as direct empirical fact.
