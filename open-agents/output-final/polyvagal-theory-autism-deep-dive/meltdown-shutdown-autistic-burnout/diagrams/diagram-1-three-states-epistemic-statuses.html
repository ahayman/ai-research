Three States, Three Epistemic Statuses — The Centre of This Chapter

Popular polyvagal writing bundles meltdown, shutdown, and autistic burnout under one framework. The empirical picture is uneven. Each state has a different evidence status, and rigorous reading must keep them separate. This grid is the structural argument of the chapter at a glance.

USER-FLAGGED CENTRE OF THE DEEP DIVE. Most polyvagal popularisations apply one framework to all three states. The actual evidence base is three different framings of three different strengths. Reading this grid first orients everything that follows.

The point of this grid. Reading from left to right within each row, the framing claim changes status from "reasonable mapping" (meltdown) to "useful description, wrong mechanism" (shutdown) to "wrong framework entirely" (burnout). The same word "polyvagal" carries different evidence weight across the three columns. Honest writing must keep these separate.
