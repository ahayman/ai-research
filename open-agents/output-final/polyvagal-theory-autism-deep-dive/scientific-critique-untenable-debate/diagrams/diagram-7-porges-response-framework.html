Porges's Response Framework

In "When a Critique Becomes Untenable" (Clinical Neuropsychiatry 23(1):113–128, 2026), Porges doesn't engage the critics' individual anatomical/phylogenetic points sequentially. He instead argues the critique misreads what kind of theory PVT is.

Source: Porges S. W. (2026). When a Critique Becomes Untenable: A Scholarly Response to Grossman et al.'s Evaluation of Polyvagal Theory. Clinical Neuropsychiatry, 23(1):113–128. PubMed 41768026.

Porges's core argument
"Critics evaluate a reconstructed proxy of the theory shaped by persistent category errors. The charge of scientific untenability does not apply to Polyvagal Theory as formulated."
Branch 1

Category error #1: conflating neuroanatomy with neurophysiology

Critics treat structural neuroanatomy claims (where vagal neurons are) as if they were physiological claims (what states they generate). These are different levels of analysis.

"Conflation of neuroanatomy with neurophysiology"
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: PVT itself moves between anatomical and physiological claims constantly. The architecture is the theory. You can't pin failed predictions on critics confusing levels when the theory operates at both levels.
Branch 2

Category error #2: reducing theory to measurement

Critics target RSA as if it were the theory itself. PVT is broader than any single measurement; specific RSA quantification methods Porges doesn't endorse don't refute the theory.

"Reduction of theory to measurement"
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: Grossman & Taylor 2007 already addressed RSA-as-vagal-tone broadly, not just specific methods. PVT clinical applications routinely use RSA as a vagal tone proxy without the caveats Porges retroactively introduces.
Branch 3

Theory framing: systems-level, pathway-specific

PVT is a systems-level framework of autonomic state regulation, not a simple anatomical claim. The architecture organizes states; individual anatomical premises are illustrative, not load-bearing.

"A systems-level, pathway-specific framework of autonomic state regulation"
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: If the anatomical claims are illustrative rather than load-bearing, what specifically is the systems-level content the theory uniquely contributes that other frameworks (Neurovisceral Integration, predictive processing, allostasis) don't? Vague systems-level claims risk becoming unfalsifiable in practice.
Branch 4

Falsifiability defense

PVT does generate "a range of specific, testable predictions." Falsifiability operates at the systems level; individual anatomical predictions are not the theory.

"Explicit conditions of falsifiability as a systems-level, pathway-specific framework"
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: Porges has previously stated PVT "was not proposed to be either 'proven' or 'falsified', but rather to be informed by research and modified." This is exactly the kind of formulation Popperian falsifiability is designed to identify as problematic.
Branch 5

Measurement preference, not refutation

Critics' findings reflect "differences in measurement preference, level of analysis, or theoretical framing rather than evidence against the theory's organizing principles."

"Differences in measurement preference, level of analysis, or theoretical framing rather than evidence against the theory"
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: Monteiro 2018's lungfish finding isn't a "measurement preference" — it's a direct empirical demonstration that contradicts a stated PVT prediction (uniquely mammalian myelinated vagus from a ventral source). That's exactly what falsifying evidence looks like.
Branch 6

"Reconstructed proxy" framing

What critics are attacking is a version of PVT they've reconstructed for ease of attack — a strawman of the actual peer-reviewed theory.

"The critique fails to engage the theory on its own terms ... lacks representational fidelity, appropriate alignment of levels of analysis, and responsiveness to theoretical and empirical clarification."
— Porges 2026 abstract
Critics' rejoinder: Many of the "reconstructed" claims attacked are direct quotes from Porges's own peer-reviewed publications (e.g., "only mammals have a myelinated vagus"). The "you're attacking a proxy" defense risks becoming unfalsifiable: any successful attack can be reclassified as targeting "the wrong version" of the theory.

Rhetorical strategy

Porges's response notably does not engage the lungfish data, the DMN/NA cardiac neuron counts, the Doody/Burghardt/Dinets reptile-sociality evidence, or the Neuhuber/Berthoud DMV-freezing critique on their specific terms. Instead it argues all of these findings either miss the theory's level of analysis or attack a "reconstructed proxy." The response is conceptual rather than empirical — which is itself a structural feature worth noticing in the falsifiability debate (Diagram 8).

The honest read of Porges's response: his rebuttal is intellectually serious and identifies real distinctions between anatomical and physiological claims. But it doesn't restore the falsified empirical content. Whether PVT survives as a systems-level framework once the falsified anatomical claims are removed — and whether the surviving framework still says anything beyond what Neurovisceral Integration or predictive processing already say — is the question the next few decades will resolve.
