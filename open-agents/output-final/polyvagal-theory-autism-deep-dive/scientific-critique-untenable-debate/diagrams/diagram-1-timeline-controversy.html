Timeline of the Polyvagal Critique (1994 → 2026)

The scientific critique didn't begin in 2026. It builds across two decades of comparative physiology, vagal anatomy, and reanalysis of RSA. The 2026 "Untenable" consensus is the consolidation point, not the origin.

1994

Porges introduces Polyvagal Theory

Society for Psychophysiological Research presidential address. Two functionally distinct vagal branches; mammalian "smart" myelinated vagus tied to social engagement.

2007

Grossman & Taylor — RSA critique

Biological Psychology 74(2):263–285. First major published critique. RSA is not a reliable direct measure of cardiac vagal tone; multiple confounds (respiration, baroreflex, sympathetic).

2011

Porges, The Polyvagal Theory (Norton)

Major book-length elaboration. Theory enters trauma-therapy mainstream over the next decade.

2018

Monteiro et al. — Lungfish paper

Science Advances 4(2), eaaq0800. Myelinated cardiorespiratory vagal fibers in South American lungfish (~10 m/s conduction), from a ventrolateral primordial-NA source. Directly contradicts "uniquely mammalian myelinated vagus."

2022

Neuhuber & Berthoud — Vagal anatomy critique

Biological Psychology 174, 108425 (PubMed 36100134). Evidence does NOT support DMV role in freezing. Mammalian freezing primarily mediated by nucleus ambiguus. DMV should not be linked to passive defensive behavior.

2022

Taylor, Wang & Leite — Phylogeny of cardiorespiratory control

Biological Psychology 172. Comparative review across vertebrate lineages. Myelinated cardiac vagal fibers present across taxa, not novel to mammals.

2023

Doody, Burghardt & Dinets — Reptile sociality

PubMed 37094735. Challenges "asocial reptile / social mammal" dichotomy. Reptiles show communal breeding, prolonged parental care, life-long monogamy — undermining PVT's evolutionary narrative.

2023 May

Grossman — Five Premises critique

Biological Psychology 180, 108589 (PubMed 37230290). Single-author comprehensive critique. "Broad consensus among experts that each basic physiological assumption of the polyvagal theory is untenable."

2023 Oct

Giroux et al. — "Scientifically questionable, useful in practice"

Journal of Psychiatry Reform. UC Davis-led commentary pre-empts the 2026 consensus with the same framing — accept the scientific critique; defend clinical utility.

2025

Porges — Status update paper

Clinical Neuropsychiatry 22(3):169–184. "Polyvagal theory: current status, clinical applications, and future directions." Sets up the direct response from critics.

2026 Feb

"Why the Polyvagal Theory is Untenable" — 39-author consensus

Clinical Neuropsychiatry 23(1):100–112 (PubMed 41768017, PMC12937499). Grossman + 38 invited experts. Conclusion: PVT is "untenable, because it is not defensible based on existing neurophysiological and evolutionary evidence."

2026 Feb

Porges — "When a Critique Becomes Untenable"

Clinical Neuropsychiatry 23(1):113–128 (PubMed 41768026). Same journal issue. Defends PVT as a "systems-level, pathway-specific framework" and argues critics attack a "reconstructed proxy" of the theory.

2026 Mar–Apr

Clinician wave

InSight+/MJA (Whitehead, March 9, 2026). Psychology Today (Chen Feb; McDonald April). Trauma Therapist Institute (Kase, "Significant, Not Sacred"). Substack (Auer). Centrist position: accept anatomical critique; defend clinical utility.

Read this timeline like: the 2026 "debunking" headlines are accurate about the existence of a 39-author consensus, but they hide a 20-year arc of cumulative critique that the consensus paper synthesises rather than initiates. The Grossman & Taylor 2007 RSA paper, Monteiro 2018 lungfish paper, and Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022 anatomy paper are each individually serious independent challenges. The 2026 consensus consolidates them and adds 36 more expert signatures.
