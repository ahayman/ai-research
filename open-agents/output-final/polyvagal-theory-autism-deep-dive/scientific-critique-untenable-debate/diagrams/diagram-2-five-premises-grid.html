The Five Premises Under Attack

Grossman 2023 and the 2026 Untenable consensus identify five basic premises that PVT rests on. Each row shows what the theory claims, what the comparative-physiology / neuroanatomy / phylogeny literature actually shows, and where the dispute sits today.

1 RSA as a direct measure of cardiac vagal tone

PVT's claim

Respiratory Sinus Arrhythmia directly indexes central vagal outflow to the heart. Higher RSA = higher "ventral vagal" tone = better social engagement / safety.

Counter-evidence

Grossman & Taylor 2007 + 20 years of subsequent psychophysiology: RSA is shaped by respiratory rate, tidal volume, baroreflex activity, blood pressure, and sympathetic tone — not just vagal traffic. Treating RSA as a clean vagal tone proxy is a category mistake.

Falsified

RSA is informative but not a direct vagal index. Foundational paper 2007; consistently supported since.

2 Functional duality of dorsal motor nucleus (DMV) vs. nucleus ambiguus (NA) in mammalian cardiac control

PVT's claim

Mammalian cardiac vagal control is functionally split between DMV (unmyelinated, slow, "primitive") and NA (myelinated, fast, "smart") — generating the dorsal-vs-ventral state model.

Counter-evidence

In mammals, cardiac vagal control is overwhelmingly NA-mediated. ~80% of cardiac vagal preganglionic neurons in cats are NA; ~60% in monkeys. The DMV contribution is real but minor — not a meaningful "split." Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022 specifically dispute PVT's anatomical framing.

Substantially weakened

NA dominates. PVT defenders cite qualitative differences (transcriptomics, embryology) — real but don't restore functional duality.

3 Uniquely mammalian myelinated cardioinhibitory vagus from a ventral source

PVT's claim

The "smart vagus" — myelinated cardiac vagal fibers from a ventral source — is the evolutionarily novel feature that distinguishes mammals from earlier vertebrates and enables the Social Engagement System.

Counter-evidence

Monteiro 2018 (lungfish): myelinated cardiorespiratory vagus with ~10 m/s conduction from a ventrolateral primordial-NA source. Sharks have ~45% myelinated cardiac preganglionics. Fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds all have myelinated cardiac vagal fibers. The "uniquely mammalian" claim is empirically contradicted.

Falsified

Direct empirical refutation from comparative physiology. The strongest single result against PVT.

4 Vagal-sociality coevolution: mammalian vagal architecture co-emerged with mammalian social behavior

PVT's claim

Reptiles are asocial; mammals are social. The myelinated vagus + Social Engagement System emerged together as a coupled mammalian evolutionary innovation enabling cooperative, affiliative behavior.

Counter-evidence

Doody, Burghardt & Dinets 2023: reptiles show communal breeding, prolonged/communal parental care, life-long monogamy, social learning, dominance hierarchies. "The labels 'social' and 'asocial' are too crude to have utility." The asocial-reptile premise is empirically wrong.

Falsified

Sociality is phylogenetically widespread. Mammalian-uniqueness story doesn't survive comparative ethology.

5 Historical physiological literature supports the polyvagal architecture

PVT's claim

The polyvagal architecture is consistent with — and supported by — earlier 20th-century physiological literature on vagal control.

Counter-evidence

Grossman 2023 and the 2026 Untenable consensus systematically re-read cited historical sources and argue they don't support PVT's reading. The 39-author paper specifically discusses "interpretations of earlier physiological research literature" as one of the contested premises.

Contested

This premise is a meta-claim about literature interpretation. Hard to "falsify" cleanly but the consensus is critics have the better reading.

The pattern: the most testable empirical claims (Premises 1, 3, 4) have been clearly falsified by direct evidence. The dual-architecture claim (Premise 2) is substantially weakened but defenders cite qualitative NA/DMN differences. The historical-literature claim (Premise 5) is a meta-claim where both sides cite the same sources differently.
