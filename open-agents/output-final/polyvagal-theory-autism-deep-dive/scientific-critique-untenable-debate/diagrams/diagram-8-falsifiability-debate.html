The Falsifiability Debate

A philosophical core of the dispute: is PVT a falsifiable scientific theory, or has it retreated to formulations that evade falsification? Both sides have legitimate claims here. Middle column shows where the dispute actually sits.

Critics' position

PVT either makes testable predictions that have been falsified, or evades falsification

Testable claim: "Only mammals have a myelinated vagus." Status: Falsified by Monteiro 2018 (lungfish) + comparative literature.
Testable claim: DMV mediates freeze. Status: Falsified by Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022 + direct anatomy.
Testable claim: Reptiles are asocial. Status: Falsified by Doody, Burghardt & Dinets 2023.
"The theory was not proposed to be either 'proven' or 'falsified', but rather to be informed by research and modified."
— Porges, earlier formulation cited by critics as Popperianly problematic
PVT's defenders "ignore the overwhelming scientific consensus."
— Grossman et al. 2026 (Untenable consensus paper)

Core point: When specific empirical predictions are falsified, the response shouldn't be to redescribe the theory as immune to that kind of attack. That's the textbook ad-hoc rescue pattern Popper warned about.

Where the dispute actually sits

The honest middle position

1. The most testable empirical predictions of PVT (mammalian myelinated-vagus uniqueness; DMV freeze mediation; asocial reptile) have not survived contact with the relevant comparative-physiology, neuroanatomy, and ethology literatures.
2. When PVT is reformulated as "a systems-level, pathway-specific framework" with the anatomical premises demoted to illustrative, the question becomes whether the surviving framework has unique empirical content or quietly becomes a different theory.
3. Falsifiability is not all-or-nothing. Theories can have falsifiable empirical content and unfalsifiable systems-level claims simultaneously. The empirical content of PVT has fared poorly; whether the systems-level claims add anything beyond Neurovisceral Integration, predictive processing, or allostasis is an open empirical/conceptual question.
4. Both sides have legitimate sub-arguments. Critics: the testable parts didn't survive testing. Porges: critics targeted illustrative anatomical claims while ignoring the systems-level architecture. The disagreement isn't whether falsifiability matters — it's about what counts as "the theory."
Porges's position

PVT generates falsifiable predictions; critics attack illustrative claims while missing the systems-level framework

Defense: PVT has "explicit conditions of falsifiability as a systems-level, pathway-specific framework of autonomic state regulation."
Defense: The theory generates "a range of specific, testable predictions." Anatomical claims are part of how the theory is articulated, but the framework's content is broader.
Defense: Critics make "category errors" — confusing measurement preference, level of analysis, and theoretical framing with evidence against the theory.
"Disagreements reflect differences in measurement preference, level of analysis, or theoretical framing rather than evidence against the theory's organizing principles."
— Porges 2026 (When a Critique Becomes Untenable)
"The charge of scientific untenability does not apply to Polyvagal Theory as formulated."
— Porges 2026

Core point: A theory operating at systems level can have specific anatomical claims that are illustrative without making the whole theory stand or fall with those claims. Critics need to engage the theory's actual organizing principles.

The unresolved question
If PVT's empirically tested predictions don't survive, but the "systems-level" reformulation is too abstract to test, what specifically is the theory now claiming that distinguishes it from competitors that don't carry the falsified baggage?
What an honest reader should conclude: the falsifiability debate isn't a tie. The most testable parts of PVT have been falsified by direct evidence. Porges's "systems-level" defense is conceptually serious but at risk of becoming unfalsifiable in practice. Whether that defense preserves enough content for PVT to count as a distinct theory rather than a renamed Neurovisceral Integration framework is the live question. Critics aren't claiming PVT is meaningless — they're claiming its specific empirical commitments don't hold and its retreat to systems-level abstraction needs to specify what predictions remain.
