Brainstem Anatomy — Where the Vagal Nuclei Live

Schematic cross-section of the medulla oblongata showing the four nuclei associated with the vagus nerve.

DMN (dorsal motor nucleus) Parasympathetic efferents to subdiaphragmatic viscera (stomach, GI, pancreas). Mostly unmyelinated C-fibers.
NA (nucleus ambiguus) Cardio-inhibitory parasympathetic to heart + branchial motor to larynx, pharynx, palate. Mostly myelinated B-fibers.
NTS (nucleus tractus solitarius) Primary central relay for visceral sensory afferents (~80% of vagal fibers). Projects to insula via thalamus.
Spinal trigeminal relay Receives somatic sensation from a small ear-skin patch via the auricular branch of the vagus. Basis of taVNS.
Figure 1. Schematic only. Real medullary anatomy is more elaborate; this view collapses several rostro-caudal levels into one cross-section for teaching clarity. Note that DMN and NA are both motor (efferent) nuclei but project to different targets via different fiber types — this functional split is one of the most important things to understand about vagal anatomy.
