The Two Motor Nuclei and Their Projection Targets

Nucleus ambiguus (green, myelinated) projects mostly above the diaphragm — to heart, larynx, pharynx. DMN (blue, unmyelinated) projects mostly below — to stomach, intestine, pancreas.

NA (green, solid) — myelinated, faster, supradiaphragmatic
DMN (blue, dashed) — unmyelinated, slower, subdiaphragmatic
Figure 2. The two efferent vagal nuclei have distinct projection profiles. NA carries the moment-to-moment cardiac brake and voluntary-style motor to larynx/pharynx; DMN carries the slower digestive parasympathetic output. The diaphragm is a useful approximate boundary, not an absolute rule. Below the splenic flexure of the colon, the vagus stops — distal colon and rectum get parasympathetic supply from sacral spinal nerves (S2–S4), not vagus.
