HRV Metrics Comparison

Heart rate variability is not one number. Different metrics index different things, and they don't always agree. Knowing which is which makes wearable readings less mysterious.

Metric Domain What it measures Primary interpretation Caveats / use case
RMSSD Time Root mean square of successive differences between adjacent RR intervals (ms) Vagal / parasympathetic. Highly correlated with RSA and HF. Best single short-term vagal index. Recommended for recordings of ≤5 min. Used by most "HRV" wearable apps.
SDNN Time Standard deviation of all NN intervals across the recording (ms) Overall variability — mixed sympathetic + parasympathetic contributions. Standard for 24-hour ambulatory recordings. Less specific to vagal control than RMSSD.
SDANN Time Standard deviation of 5-minute mean NN intervals across a 24-hour recording (ms) Long-period rhythms — circadian and ultradian. Captures variability across hour-to-hour shifts; not relevant for short recordings.
pNN50 Time % of successive RR intervals differing by >50 ms Vagal / parasympathetic. Similar to RMSSD. Older metric, mostly replaced by RMSSD. Still appears in some studies.
HF Frequency Spectral power in 0.15–0.40 Hz band (ms²) Cardiac vagal control / RSA. Highest-frequency band corresponds to typical adult breathing rates. Most direct frequency-domain analog of RSA. Affected by all the RSA confounds (breathing rate, position, etc.).
LF Frequency Spectral power in 0.04–0.15 Hz band (ms²) Mixed — baroreflex modulation, some sympathetic, some parasympathetic. Older literature interpreted as "sympathetic." This interpretation is now contested. Treat as a mixed band.
VLF Frequency Spectral power in 0.0033–0.04 Hz band (ms²) Slow rhythms — thermoregulation, hormonal, RAAS. Only meaningful in 24-hour recordings. Captures very slow autonomic shifts.
LF/HF ratio Frequency Ratio of LF to HF power Once interpreted as "sympathovagal balance" — this interpretation is largely abandoned. Caution. Heathers (2012) called it "an obituary." Modern HRV literature treats with skepticism. Still appears on consumer devices.

Key practical relationships:

• RMSSD ≈ HF — they capture almost the same physiological signal (cardiac vagal control). Correlate 0.85–0.95 in most recordings.
• SDNN includes contributions from multiple bands; useful as a global index but less specific.
• RSA (peak-to-trough or band-passed) is conceptually close to HF; one is computed in the time domain, the other in the frequency domain.
• LF/HF is best treated as a curiosity, not a meaningful biomarker. If a wearable foregrounds it, take it with skepticism.
The LF/HF ratio "obituary." When you see consumer apps or older clinical writeups talk about "your sympathetic-parasympathetic balance" using the LF/HF ratio, that interpretation has been substantially undermined in the contemporary HRV literature. Billman (2013) and Heathers (2012) — among others — demonstrated that the assumptions underlying the ratio (LF as pure sympathetic, HF as pure parasympathetic, linear interaction) don't hold. Use RMSSD or HF for vagal estimation, and avoid reading "balance" claims from LF/HF.
