Practitioner-Evaluation Checklist

Apply when considering any practitioner offering polyvagal-informed services. Portable / screenshotable.

Save this image. Bring the questions to an initial consultation. A practitioner who scores well will welcome them.
# Question What you want to hear Red flag
1 What is your clinical license? LCSW, LMFT, PsyD, PhD psychology, MD, RN, OT, PT — a license that authorizes mental-health treatment in your jurisdiction. "I'm a certified polyvagal practitioner" / "I'm a coach." Certificates are not licenses.
2 What's your scope of practice within that license? Specific, bounded scope they can articulate. Vague "I work with the whole person" answers that don't address scope.
3 Can you cite specific studies for the mechanism you're claiming? Names of authors, years, journals (approximate is fine). "I just see it work with clients." Testimonials are not evidence.
4 What's your stance on the scientific controversy around polyvagal theory? Knows Grossman 2023 / 2026 Untenable consensus; distinguishes framework from mechanism. Hasn't heard of it / dismisses it as "academic infighting."
5 Do you have financial conflicts of interest with products you recommend? Discloses (Polyvagal Institute training, Unyte certification, affiliate links, etc.). Doesn't disclose / gets defensive when asked.
6 Do you distinguish framework claims from mechanism claims? Yes — can explain the difference. Treats "polyvagal" as synonym for "scientifically grounded."
7 Would you update your beliefs if a definitive study tomorrow showed polyvagal theory is wrong? "Yes — the practices I use have independent evidence bases." "Polyvagal theory has helped so many people, it must be true."
8 What's your approach when something isn't working for a client? Discusses trying different modalities, referring out, integration. Doubles down on more polyvagal.

If they score well

The polyvagal framework is one tool among many. They use it as language and vocabulary, not as ideology. The underlying clinical license is doing the work.

If they score poorly

The framework is functioning as branding or as belief system. This is not the same as using it as a tool. Consider another practitioner.

A practitioner who scores well will welcome these questions. One who scores poorly will treat them as adversarial.
