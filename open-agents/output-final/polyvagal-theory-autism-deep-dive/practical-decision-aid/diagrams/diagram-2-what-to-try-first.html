What to Try First — Decision Tree for Autistic Adult with Burnout

An order of operations. Demand reduction and sensory accommodation come before nervous-system regulation techniques.

You feel overwhelmed, drained, or stuck. What now?

Run the checks below in order. Don't skip ahead.

↓

Are you sleeping ≥ 7 hours, mostly unbroken?

No: Fix sleep first. Sleep deficit alone produces burnout-pattern symptoms.
Yes: Continue.
↓

Have you done a sensory environment audit?

No: List your three biggest unmodified sensory inputs (noise, lighting, fabric, scent, clutter). Modify one this week.
Yes: Continue.
↓

Have you done a demand audit?

No: List demands; defer, decline, or renegotiate at least one. This is the Raymaker 2020 / AASPIRE framework: demand reduction is the autistic-burnout intervention.
Yes: Continue.
↓

Any medical red flags? (PEM, syncope, persistent fatigue >6 months, severe depression markers, postural symptoms)

No red flags: Continue.
Yes: See a clinician for workup. ME/CFS, POTS, depression, thyroid, sleep apnea, B12, anemia — each has a different intervention pathway.
↓

Are Tier A practices in place daily?

Paced breathing (5–6 bpm), humming, cold face splash as needed, social safety contact, sensory-quiet recovery time.

No: Start the two-week starter protocol (Diagram 6).
Yes: Continue.
↓

After 4–6 weeks of consistent Tier A, has anything shifted?

Yes, modest shift: Continue. Consider adding one Tier B practice with pre-specified endpoints.
No shift: The proximal cause is probably elsewhere — revisit steps 1–4. Don't escalate to Tier B until A is consistently in place.
↓

Default position

For an autistic adult with overwhelm and burnout, the highest-leverage interventions are demand reduction, sensory accommodation, sleep, and the Tier A practices. Most people skip these and go straight to expensive Tier B/C tools. Don't. Master the foundations first.

Note: This is a decision tree, not a hierarchy of personal worth. Some readers will move quickly through steps 1–5 because they've already done that work; others will spend months on step 3.
