The Two-Week Starter Protocol

You could start tonight. Zero cost beyond what you own. Built entirely from Tier A practices.

Week 1 — Foundations

Morning (5 min)

Paced breathing. Open a free app (Allos / Breathing App / Breath Ball). Set to 5–6 bpm (5s in / 5s out). Breathe with the pacer for 5 minutes.

As needed (acute over-arousal)

Cold face splash. Splash face with cold tap water for ~30 seconds. Triggers the diving reflex; fast, reliable reset.

Evening (5 min)

Repeat paced breathing. Optionally add humming on the exhale.

This week (one-time)

Sensory audit. List your three biggest unmodified sensory inputs. Modify one (noise-canceling headphones, lamp instead of overhead, fabric change).
Demand audit. List demands. Defer, decline, or renegotiate at least one.
Sleep priority. If sleep is short or broken, fix this first. Other practices won't compensate for sleep deficit.

Week 2 — Add layers

Continue all Week 1 practices.

New additions

Pre-emptive paced breathing. Before known stressors (meetings, social events, transitions), 2–3 minutes of paced breathing. Anticipatory parasympathetic preparation reduces stress-response magnitude.
Glimmers list. Write down five small experiences that move you toward feeling regulated. Pure self-mapping; not a mechanism claim.
One social safety contact. Identify a person whose presence reliably regulates you. Make plans to spend time with them this week. Co-regulation has decades of attachment-theory evidence.
Sensory recovery block. Schedule one explicit sensory-quiet ≥60-minute block. No screens, no others, reduced input. This is recovery, not avoidance.

After two weeks — assess honestly

  • Did you do the paced breathing most days? If not, what got in the way?
  • Did cold face splash help in acute moments? When did it work; when didn't?
  • Did sensory modifications change your baseline? How much?
  • Did you actually defer the demands you flagged? If not, why?
  • What single change had the largest effect?

Then: Consider whether to add Tier B practices. Avoid: Spending money on Tier C/D products until Tier A is consistently in place.

If after consistent Week 1+2 nothing has shifted, the proximal cause is probably outside the polyvagal layer — revisit medical workup, demand levels, sleep, accommodations.
