The Five-Tier Evidence Framework (A–E)

A portable decision tool. Apply it to any polyvagal-adjacent claim — or any wellness claim, full stop.

A Do these now
Criteria Multiple RCTs + meta-analyses; replicated across labs; free or near-free; minimal risk; mechanism plausible & consistent with established physiology; works regardless of whether PVT is correct.
Examples
  • Slow paced breathing (~6 bpm)
  • Humming / Bhramari pranayama
  • Cold face splash (diving reflex)
  • Adequate sleep (foundational)
  • Sensory accommodation
  • Demand reduction (Raymaker)
  • Social safety with attuned others
B Structured trial
Criteria At least one well-controlled RCT or multiple smaller controlled trials; replicated across ≥2 independent labs; mechanism plausible but not fully established; some COI exposure but manageable. Pre-specify endpoints; treat as n=1.
Examples
  • HRV biofeedback (HeartMath, Polar+Elite)
  • taVNS (via clinician)
  • Trauma-sensitive yoga (TCTSY)
  • Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP)
  • Polyvagal-informed therapy (licensed)
  • HRV monitoring (Oura/Whoop/Garmin)
C Be skeptical
Criteria Manufacturer-funded studies; no sham control; mechanism claim speculative; marketing outruns evidence; evidence base controlled by entity with financial stake.
Examples
  • Truvaga / Sensate / Pulsetto / Apollo Neuro
  • "Vagus nerve" supplements
  • Gua sha / "vagal massage" tools
  • Non-licensed "polyvagal" coaching
  • State-diagnosis without measurement
D Red flags
Criteria Cure-claims for stable conditions; pseudo-credentials standing for licenses; expensive courses with no outcomes data; diagnosis without measurement; undisclosed COIs with strong efficacy claims.
Examples
  • Autism "cure" via polyvagal interventions
  • "Rewire your nervous system in 5 days"
  • $2K–$5K courses, no published outcomes
  • "Polyvagal-informed" without clinical license
  • Refusal to disclose COIs
E Documented harm
Criteria Documented adverse events in vulnerable populations; risk profile not characterized; cure-claims where iatrogenic harm reported; combinations known to be unsafe; substitution for diagnosis of treatable conditions.
Examples
  • Brain-retraining for ME/CFS (DNRS, Gupta, Primal Trust, Lightning Process — UK NICE 2021 advises against)
  • Wim Hof + water combo (cardiac/drowning risk)
  • Holotropic breathwork without screening
  • Polyvagal language substituting for medical workup

The framework moves vertically from "strongest evidence, do it now" (A) through "be skeptical" (C) to "documented harm in specific populations" (E). Most of what's worth doing is in Tier A and costs nothing.
