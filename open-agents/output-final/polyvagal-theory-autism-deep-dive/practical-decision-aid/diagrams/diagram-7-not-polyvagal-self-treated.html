Symptoms That Should NOT Be Polyvagal-Self-Treated

Wellness self-care belongs in the maintenance layer, not the diagnostic-workup layer. When in doubt, get evaluated.

The cost of substitution is delayed treatment of treatable conditions. If any of the following are present, polyvagal practices should be a complement to — not a substitute for — medical or mental-health evaluation.
Symptom or pattern What to consider Why polyvagal framing is wrong here
Chronic fatigue >6 months with post-exertional malaise (PEM) ME/CFS, Long COVID, fibromyalgia spectrum Brain-retraining and aggressive activation can worsen PEM. Pacing-based management is the evidence-based approach (UK NICE 2021).
Syncope, near-syncope, postural lightheadedness, racing heart on standing POTS, dysautonomia, cardiac eval Cardiology workup needed. Aggressive vagal activation can worsen orthostatic intolerance.
Persistent anhedonia, hopelessness, worthlessness, suicidal ideation Major depressive disorder; treatment-resistant depression Evidence-based depression treatment (psychotherapy, possibly medication) is standard of care. PVT framing should not delay treatment.
Unexplained weight loss, bowel/bladder changes, blood in stool, lumps Medical workup Wellness self-care should never substitute for medical evaluation of unexplained physical symptoms.
New confusion, severe headache, vision changes, motor loss Emergency neurological evaluation Self-evident — call emergency services or go to ER.
Persistent severe sleep disturbance Sleep study, mental-health evaluation Sleep apnea, depression, anxiety, restless legs all need targeted treatment.
Suspected untreated ADHD with executive function collapse ADHD evaluation Specific evidence-based treatments (medication, behavioral interventions) exist; polyvagal framing may delay diagnosis.
Persistent panic attacks Panic disorder treatment CBT and (sometimes) medication have strong evidence; can co-occur with PTSD and need targeted treatment.
Symptoms suggestive of perimenopause, thyroid disorder, B12 deficiency, iron-deficiency anemia Primary care lab workup Each has specific treatment; symptoms can mimic burnout patterns. Lab workup is cheap and high-yield.
Hypermobility with multi-system symptoms hEDS / HSD evaluation Genetics, cardiology, GI evaluation may be needed.

The pattern: wellness self-care is a complement to, not a substitute for, medical and mental-health evaluation. Polyvagal-adjacent practices belong in the maintenance / regulation / foundation layer of your life. They do not belong in the diagnostic-workup layer. When in doubt, get evaluated.

This is the safety chapter within the synthesis. If a clinician dismisses your concerns and you suspect substitution is happening, get a second opinion.
