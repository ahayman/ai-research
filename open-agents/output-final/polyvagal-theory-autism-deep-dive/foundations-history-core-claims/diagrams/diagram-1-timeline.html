1992–1994Development

Porges integrates ANS literature at NIH

As visiting scientist at the National Institutes of Health and professor at University of Maryland, Porges immerses himself in vertebrate autonomic-nervous-system research. The polyvagal hypothesis takes shape.

8 October 1994Origin

SPR Presidential Address, Atlanta

Porges delivers his presidential address to the Society for Psychophysiological Research. The polyvagal theory is unveiled to the scientific community.

1995First publication

"Orienting in a Defensive World" — Psychophysiology

The address is formalized as the canonical first paper: Psychophysiology 32(4):301–318. The "vagal paradox" framing and the three-vagal-system proposal enter the formal literature.

2001Refinement

"Phylogenetic substrates of a social nervous system"

International Journal of Psychophysiology, 42(2):123–146. The "social engagement system" concept is articulated more fully, integrating cranial nerves V, VII, IX, X, and XI with cardiac vagal regulation.

2004Key concept

"Neuroception" term introduced

Zero to Three, 24(5):19–24. Porges coins "neuroception" to describe sub-conscious neural detection of safety, danger, and life-threat — explicitly distinct from conscious perception.

2009Synthesis

Cleveland Clinic review paper

"The polyvagal theory: New insights into adaptive reactions of the autonomic nervous system" (Cleveland Clinic Journal of Medicine, 76 Suppl 2). A consolidated, medical-audience statement of the theory.

2011Book

The Polyvagal Theory — Norton

Porges publishes The Polyvagal Theory: Neurophysiological Foundations of Emotions, Attachment, Communication, and Self-Regulation (W. W. Norton, 416 pages, ISBN 9780393707007). The book becomes the standard reference.

2014Research

Listening Project Protocol (LPP) findings published

Porges et al. publish preliminary findings on the LPP — an acoustic intervention designed to recruit the middle-ear muscles and the social engagement system (PMC4117928). The research predecessor to the commercial SSP.

2014Popularization

van der Kolk — The Body Keeps the Score

Bessel van der Kolk's book draws heavily on polyvagal theory in its trauma chapters. The book becomes a global bestseller and the dominant popularization vector for PVT in the trauma audience.

2016Product launch

Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP) commercialized

SSP is launched as a commercial product through Integrated Listening Systems (now Unyte Health). Porges holds patent rights and receives royalties; he serves as Chief Scientific Advisor at Unyte.

2017Distribution

SSP made available to mental-health professionals

Broader pro release expands SSP's reach into clinical practice across trauma, autism, and child development.

2018Clinical metaphor

Deb Dana — The Polyvagal Theory in Therapy

Dana's Norton book introduces the "polyvagal ladder" and "Rhythm of Regulation" clinical metaphor — the three states color-coded green (ventral), yellow (sympathetic), red (dorsal). This becomes PVT's most-circulated teaching tool.

2020Institution

Polyvagal Institute founded

Porges and Dana co-found the Polyvagal Institute (PVI), a 501(c)(3) non-profit offering paid courses, certifications, and educational content.

2022Article

"Polyvagal Theory: A Science of Safety" — Frontiers

Frontiers in Integrative Neuroscience, 16:871227 (PMC9131189). Porges reframes the theory as a "science of safety" with measurable physiological substrate.

2022First critique

Neuhuber & Berthoud — Biological Psychology

"Functional anatomy of the vagus system: How does the polyvagal theory comply?" (PubMed 36100134). Argues evidence does not support a dorsal-vagal role in mammalian freezing as proposed by PVT.

2023Major critique

Grossman — "Five basic premises refuted"

"Fundamental challenges and likely refutations of the five basic premises of the polyvagal theory" (Biological Psychology, PubMed 37230290). Argues all five premises are inconsistent with established comparative neuroanatomy.

2025Update

Porges — "Current Status, Clinical Applications, Future Directions"

Clinical Neuropsychiatry, 22(3) (PMC12302812). Porges's most recent restatement, with new biomarkers (vagal efficiency, weighted coherence) and explicit responses to critics.

Feb 2026Consensus

"Why the Polyvagal Theory is Untenable"

39 co-authors (38 invited international experts + Grossman) sign a consensus paper in Clinical Neuropsychiatry (PMC12937499). A direct rebuttal of Porges's 2025 update.

2026Response

Porges — "When a Critique Becomes Untenable"

Same issue of Clinical Neuropsychiatry (PubMed 41768026). Porges argues the critics attack a reconstructed version of the theory rather than the actual claims.
