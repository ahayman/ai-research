The Three-Tier Hierarchy: The Polyvagal "Ladder"

Per polyvagal theory, the autonomic nervous system is not a two-branch on/off system but a three-tier hierarchy. Each tier emerged at a different stage of vertebrate evolution. Under threat, the system disengages from newest to oldest (dissolution). Recovery reverses the sequence.

Ventral Vagal Complex (VVC) — Safe & Social
Newest · Claimed Mammalian
Anatomy (per PVT) Nucleus ambiguus (NA). Myelinated, fast-conducting. Targets heart, bronchi, larynx; integrates with cranial nerves V, VII, IX, X, XI (face, jaw, middle ear, neck).
Function Calm states. Social engagement. Sustained attention. Prosodic voice. Listening. Co-regulation. The "vagal brake" modulates heart rate via RSA.
Lived experience Calm-but-alert. Present. Available for connection. Open face. Eye contact accessible. Voice carries warmth. Body settled.
Color (Deb Dana ladder) Green — "I am here, I am okay, I can engage."
↓ Dissolution: threat detected, VVC disengages
Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS) — Fight or Flight
Intermediate
Anatomy Thoracolumbar outflow. Sympathetic chain ganglia. Adrenergic signaling. Engages adrenal glands (epinephrine, norepinephrine).
Function Mobilization. Cardiovascular activation, muscular preparation, narrowed attention, suppressed digestion. Survival behavior: fight or flight.
Lived experience Activated, hypervigilant, irritable, anxious. Heart racing. Voice flat or sharp. Restless body. Worried mind. Focused on threat.
Color (Deb Dana ladder) Yellow — "I am not safe, I need to do something."
↓ Further dissolution: SNS overwhelmed or escape impossible
Dorsal Vagal Complex (DVC) — Shutdown & Collapse
Oldest · Shared with reptiles (per PVT)
Anatomy (per PVT) Dorsal motor nucleus of the vagus (DMNX). Unmyelinated, slow-conducting. Targets subdiaphragmatic viscera (gut, liver); also some cardiac fibers for severe bradycardia.
Function Immobilization. Freeze, feigned death, vasovagal syncope, metabolic shutdown. Energy conservation under inescapable threat.
Lived experience Collapsed, numb, dissociated. Voice gone. Face blank. Heart may drop. Mind blank. Withdrawal. Disappearance.
Color (Deb Dana ladder) Red — "I cannot escape, I will disappear."
↑ Recovery passes through sympathetic activation back to ventral vagal availability
Note on the ladder. The "ladder" is Deb Dana's 2018 clinical teaching metaphor (The Polyvagal Theory in Therapy, Norton). The underlying technical theory specifies the brainstem nuclei, the myelinated/unmyelinated distinction, the phylogenetic ordering, and the specific cranial-nerve composition of the VVC. Popular polyvagal content typically circulates the ladder without the anatomy. This diagram shows both layers side-by-side.
