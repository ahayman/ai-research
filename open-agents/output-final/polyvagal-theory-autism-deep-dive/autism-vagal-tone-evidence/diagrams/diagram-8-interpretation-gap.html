The Interpretation Gap — Same Autonomic Data, Four Predictions

The single most important conceptual point in this chapter. The autonomic findings in autism are real and replicate. Four major theoretical frameworks predict the same correlations through different mechanisms.

The autonomic data (uncontested by any of the four frames below)

Lower baseline parasympathetic HRV (g = −0.52)
Lower RSA (g = −0.59) at rest
Lower RSA reactivity to social stress (g = −0.72) — largest effect
Cheng, Huang & Huang 2020, Neurosci Biobehav Rev 118:463–471

Polyvagal Theory (PVT)

Porges 1994, 2011 — contested by Grossman 2023 & "Untenable" consensus 2026 (see Cat 3)
Lower HRV reflects a deficit of the phylogenetically novel ventral vagal complex / social engagement system. The "vagal brake" doesn't release properly during social engagement. Mammalian-specific autonomic substrate.
Predicts: Lower baseline parasympathetic, reduced RSA reactivity to social demand. Matches the data.

Neurovisceral Integration (NVI)

Thayer & Lane 2000, multiple updates · J Affect Disord · well-established in autonomic neuroscience
Lower HRV reflects reduced top-down control from prefrontal cortex over amygdala / autonomic circuits. Higher resting HRV = better prefrontal-amygdala regulatory coupling. No phylogenetic claims required.
Predicts: Lower baseline HRV, reduced reactivity. Matches the data.

Predictive Processing / Interoception

Friston, Seth, Barrett · arguably the dominant contemporary framework
HRV is a peripheral marker of the brain's interoceptive prediction-error processing efficiency. Autistic interoceptive differences (Bird & Cook alexithymia work) predict different HRV patterns through prediction-error dynamics.
Predicts: HRV patterns vary with interoceptive prediction accuracy. Compatible with the data.

Allostatic Load

Sterling & Eyer; McEwen · the framework Raymaker's autistic-burnout work actually uses
Lower HRV reflects accumulated wear-and-tear from chronic stress exposure. For autistic adults with chronic over-demand and barrier exposure, allostatic load is the direct empirical explanation.
Predicts: Lower HRV proportional to chronic-stress accumulation. Highly compatible with the data.

The interpretation gap

All four frameworks predict the same autonomic correlations. The data alone does not discriminate between them. Citing Cheng 2020 as evidence for polyvagal theory is a category error — Cheng 2020 is evidence of an autonomic correlation, not of a polyvagal-specific mechanism. Choosing among the four frames depends on which theoretical assumptions you accept, not on which fits the autonomic numbers.

Where this lands for the reader: The reduced parasympathetic HRV and reduced RSA reactivity in autism are real. The framing matters: "the data fit polyvagal predictions" is true but radically incomplete — the same data fits three other major frameworks equally well, and two of them (NVI, allostatic load) do not carry the contested anatomical-evolutionary baggage PVT does. Category 9 (Alternative & Competing Models) walks through this landscape in full. The takeaway here: don't update toward "polyvagal theory is right" from autonomic-correlation data alone.
