The Sensory-Defensive-State Hypothesis — Porges's Original Clinical Claim

The mechanism story that motivated the Listening Project Protocol and the Safe and Sound Protocol. A specific, testable claim — with preliminary evidence, real methodological limits, and a sharp neurodiversity-paradigm pushback.

PVT claim

Autistic sensory hypersensitivities reflect a nervous system chronically stuck in a defensive (threat-detection) state
↓
Auditory hypersensitivitySound aversion, prosody decoding difficulty — the original Porges hypothesis: middle-ear muscle dysregulation
Food selectivity / gutRestrictive eating, sensory food aversion, GI symptoms framed as defensive-state expressions
Tactile defensivenessTouch aversion, clothing intolerance, sensory-seeking
Visual sensitivityLight, fluorescent, busy-pattern aversion
↓

Proposed clinical pathway

Acoustic stimulation (filtered music) → recruits middle-ear muscle neural regulation → reduces defensive state → releases social engagement system
↓

Listening Project Protocol (2014) → Safe and Sound Protocol (2017–present)

Commercial intervention sold through Unyte Health. Porges holds patent rights and receives royalties.

Evidence consistent with the hypothesis

  • LPP 2014 (PMC4117928): two small trials, ~146 children. Filtered-music group showed reduced auditory hypersensitivity
  • Bal & Porges 2010: within-autism, higher RSA correlated with faster emotion recognition
  • Cheng 2020: lower baseline RSA + lower reactivity to social stress, consistent with chronic autonomic engagement
  • Self-report from some SSP users describes felt reduction in sensory overwhelm

Methodological & interpretive concerns

  • LPP 2014 authors openly acknowledge "small sample sizes, issues defining control conditions, limited random assignment"
  • 2023 adult-ASD SSP pilot (PMC10049251) had n = 6, no sham control
  • Acoustic interventions are nearly impossible to blind — placebo / expectation effects highly plausible
  • Mechanism (middle-ear muscle → vagal regulation → defensive state) is theoretically embedded in contested PVT anatomical claims
  • No RCT vs. matched-active-control intervention has shown SSP-specific effect superiority

Neurodiversity-paradigm pushback

Framing autistic sensory features as a "defensive state to be exited" inherits a deficit-medicalization frame. Many autistic adults experience their sensory profile as a stable, partly-adaptive feature of their neurotype — not a chronic alarm to be silenced. Improved vagal tone does not eliminate the need for sensory-friendly environments, work-pace accommodation, and other disability-affirming changes. Trauma Geek, Neurodiverse Connection, and others have explicitly published this critique. The intervention may help some people. The framing is a separate question from whether the device works.
Bottom line: The defensive-state hypothesis is a specific testable clinical claim with preliminary support in small pilots and significant methodological concerns. It is also the original clinical claim that motivated Porges's autism intervention work — reading it well is reading the historical origin of the SSP industry. Treat it as: plausible, not yet established by rigorous RCT, theory-laden, and commercially captured. (See Category 6: Interventions for the detailed SSP evidence audit.)
