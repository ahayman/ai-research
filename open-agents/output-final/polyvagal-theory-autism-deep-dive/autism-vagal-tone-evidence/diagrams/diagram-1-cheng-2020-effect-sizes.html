The clinically important point: The reactivity effects are larger than the baseline effects, and the largest single effect is RSA reactivity to social stress (g = −0.72, a medium-to-large effect). The autonomic difference shows up most clearly when an autistic person is navigating a social demand, not at rest.
Interpretive caveat: An effect size says how reliably two groups differ on average. It does not say why they differ. The g = −0.72 reactivity effect is consistent with PVT, with Neurovisceral Integration (Thayer & Lane), with predictive processing, and with allostatic load — the data don't discriminate.
