Polyvagal-Informed EMDR: What's Added to Standard EMDR

Rebecca Kase (Norton 2023) is explicit that the polyvagal layer is orientation, not modification. The eight-phase EMDR Standard Protocol remains intact.

Source. Kase, R. (2023). Polyvagal-Informed EMDR: A Neuro-Informed Approach to Healing. W. W. Norton. ISBN 978-1-324-03031-7. Published 23 May 2023. Per the publisher: "maintains fidelity to the evidence-based practice of EMDR." Per EMDRIA: the integration offers polyvagal-informed orientation across the existing eight phases.
Left column · Standard EMDR (unchanged) Shapiro's Standard Protocol, 1989 onwards. Eight phases, evidence-base built before polyvagal-informed adaptations.
Right column · Polyvagal layer (added) Vocabulary, pacing sensitivity, and case-conceptualisation lens added without modifying the underlying protocol.
Phase
Standard EMDR (Shapiro 1989+)
Polyvagal Layer (Kase 2023)
1
History-Taking Detailed trauma history, target identification, client suitability assessment.
Polyvagal-informed history-taking. Map the client's autonomic patterns: which states they spend most time in, what triggers shifts, what glimmers exist. Add this to standard history without replacing it.
2
Preparation Client psychoeducation about EMDR, container exercises, safe-place visualisation.
Autonomic-state mapping & ventral anchoring. Add polyvagal psychoeducation. Build the polyvagal ladder vocabulary. Identify the client's ventral resources. Container exercises are reframed in autonomic-state terms.
3
Assessment Identify target memory, image, negative/positive cognition, SUDS rating, body sensation.
Add autonomic-state check. Note current location on the ladder; ensure ventral anchor is accessible before entering desensitisation.
4
Desensitisation Bilateral stimulation (eye movements, tones, taps). Iterative reprocessing of target memory until SUDS at 0 or 1.
Pacing sensitivity. Watch for sympathetic over-arousal or dorsal collapse during BLS. If client slides toward dorsal, pause to re-establish ventral. Bilateral stimulation itself is unchanged.
5
Installation Strengthen positive cognition; VOC rating to 7.
Anchor in ventral. Tie installation to felt-sense of regulated state. Note any autonomic shifts.
6
Body Scan Scan body for residual disturbance while holding target + positive cognition.
Track autonomic signature. The body scan is already body-oriented; polyvagal layer adds explicit attention to autonomic-state markers (breathing, posture, prosody readiness).
7
Closure Return to baseline before session ends; container exercise if unfinished.
Re-establish ventral state. Don't let client leave in sympathetic or dorsal. Use named navigate-practices from the polyvagal toolkit.
8
Re-evaluation Begin next session by checking target stability and integrating between-session experience.
Autonomic patterns since last session. Add review of state-tracking between sessions. Client's lived autonomic experience informs targeting.
How to read this. The polyvagal layer is consistently additive and consistently vocabulary-and-pacing oriented. The therapeutic mechanism — what EMDR is actually doing — remains the standard protocol's mechanism. This is exactly what makes the integration both clinically reasonable and analytically modest: nothing about the underlying intervention depends on PVT being biologically correct. The polyvagal language serves as a shared vocabulary for therapist and client, and as a pacing-sensitivity lens for the therapist.
