Which Therapy Practices Actually Require Polyvagal Theory?

The chapter's central evaluation. Left: practices whose evidence base is independent of PVT being biologically correct. Right: practices whose specific claims depend on PVT.

The analytical question: Polyvagal-informed therapy is widely practiced. If polyvagal theory turns out to be wrong about specific brainstem nuclei and evolutionary phylogeny (the 2026 Untenable consensus argues exactly this — see Category 3), which therapy practices still hold up? The answer determines what's worth pursuing.
Works regardless of PVT being correct 8 practices
Attuned therapeutic presence
Predates PVT by ~25 years
Grounded in Bowlby's attachment theory (1969), Ainsworth's Strange Situation (1969-1978), Rogers's client-centered therapy. The alliance/relationship variable is among the most robust effect findings in psychotherapy research.
Paced breathing in session (~6 bpm)
Cardiovascular physiology
Lehrer, Bernardi, Russo — from the 1990s onwards. FDA-cleared device-guided slow breathing exists (e.g., RESPeRATE). The baroreflex/resonance mechanism is well-characterised independent of PVT.
Titration and pendulation
Levine 1970s (predates Levine-Porges meeting)
Coined by Peter Levine in the 1970s as Somatic Experiencing was developing. Levine met Porges in 1978. SE's techniques were already taking shape independently.
Co-regulation as therapeutic stance
Attachment-theory grounded
Bowlby (1969), Ainsworth (1978), Sroufe (1996 — "dyadic regulation of emotion"), Schore (2000s). The relational quality predates and exceeds PVT's mechanistic explanation.
Body awareness / somatic tracking
Somatic tradition broadly
Hakomi (Kurtz 1970s), bioenergetics (Lowen 1958), Gestalt body-awareness, Reich (1930s). The somatic tradition is far older than PVT.
EMDR's eight standard phases
Shapiro 1989 onwards
EMDR's evidence base was built well before PVT-informed adaptations. Kase's 2023 book explicitly maintains protocol fidelity; the polyvagal layer is orientation, not modification.
IFS as parts-based therapy
Schwartz 1980s onwards
Self-led therapeutic stance, parts ontology, eight C's. Developed by Schwartz in eating-disorder work in Chicago. The IFS-PVT mapping (Self ↔ ventral) is vocabulary alignment, not mechanism.
Mindfulness-based interventions
MBSR 1979 onwards
Kabat-Zinn (MBSR 1979), Linehan (DBT mindfulness module 1993), Segal/Williams/Teasdale (MBCT 2001). All predate or are independent of PVT.
Specifically requires PVT being correct 4 claims
"We activate your ventral vagal complex"
SSP marketing, specific mechanism claims
The Safe and Sound Protocol's marketing rests specifically on the claim that filtered audio recruits middle-ear muscles → activates the ventral vagal complex. If PVT's specific anatomical predictions don't hold, this mechanism story is at risk.
"Dorsal vagal shutdown" as biological description
Mechanism-load-bearing
SE materials that describe collapse as specifically dorsal-vagal-mediated. The 2026 Untenable consensus argues that the bradycardic vagal response is overwhelmingly nucleus-ambiguus-mediated, not dorsal-motor-nucleus-mediated. As biological mechanism, this claim is at risk.
"Neuroception" as biological process
When claimed as distinct from perception
As metaphor for sub-conscious threat detection, this is fine. As a specific biological process distinct from established threat detection (amygdala-driven, vmPFC top-down), the claim is unsupported.
"Polyvagal training" of vagal tone
Causal mechanism claim
If marketed as specifically training the polyvagal-defined ventral pathway, the claim depends on PVT. The underlying interventions (breathing, exposure to safety cues) may still improve HRV via well-established cardiovascular routes.
The practical bottom line. The interventions and stances on the left side of this map — which include essentially everything a skilled trauma-informed therapist actually does in session — have evidence bases independent of PVT. The claims on the right side are precisely the ones the 2026 Untenable consensus disputes. If you're an autistic adult weighing whether to pursue polyvagal-informed therapy, the practices are very likely to help for reasons that don't depend on PVT being biologically correct. The contested theory is doing branding work, not therapeutic work.
