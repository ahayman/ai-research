Treatment-Selection Map for Autistic Adults with Comorbidities

Where polyvagal-informed therapy fits relative to the gold-standard treatments for the common autism comorbidities. Frame as adjunct/wraparound, not primary treatment.

Anxiety Disorders
~28-42% lifetime prevalence in autistic adults
Primary
CBT (autism-adapted)
Strong evidence; meta-analyses support; needs autism-aware delivery (concrete examples, less abstract emotional vocabulary).
Primary
Exposure therapy / ACT
For specific phobias and OCD-spectrum presentations.
Adjunct
Polyvagal-informed therapy
Adds autonomic-state mapping, paced breathing, co-regulation. Useful as wraparound or for clients who do not engage well with pure CBT.
Autism-specific
Sensory accommodation, demand reduction
Many autistic anxiety presentations are responses to sensory and demand environments. Environmental change often dominates therapy effects.
Depression
~23-37% lifetime prevalence in autistic adults
Primary
CBT / Behavioural Activation
Robust evidence; behavioural activation may translate especially well for autistic adults.
Primary
IPT or medication evaluation
Interpersonal therapy and pharmacotherapy as indicated.
Adjunct
Polyvagal-informed therapy
The "dorsal shutdown" framing maps onto how some autistic adults describe burnout-adjacent depression. Useful vocabulary; doesn't replace primary treatment.
Caution
Burnout vs depression
Autistic burnout and clinical depression overlap but are not identical. See Category 5 for the three-states epistemic distinction. Don't treat burnout as straightforward depression.
PTSD / Complex Trauma
Wide range (11-60% by population)
Primary
PE, CPT, EMDR
Prolonged Exposure, Cognitive Processing Therapy, EMDR. All have substantial evidence base.
Adjunct
Polyvagal-informed EMDR (Kase)
Adds pacing sensitivity and autonomic-state framing. Doesn't modify EMDR protocol.
Adjunct
SE, Sensorimotor, IFS
Independent evidence bases; useful especially for clients who don't tolerate top-down trauma processing.
Autism-specific
Screen for masking trauma
Lifetime masking-related stress can present as complex trauma. Standard PTSD screening may underdiagnose.
Autism Core Features
Sensory, social, executive function
Primary
Sensory accommodation
Environmental modification often more impactful than any therapy. Lighting, sound, texture, predictability.
Primary
Demand reduction
Reducing chronic over-demand is the empirical treatment for autistic burnout (Raymaker 2020). PVT-language may scaffold the conversation but the operative intervention is structural.
Primary
Occupational therapy
Practical scaffolding for daily life. Strong autism evidence base.
Caution
PVT as autism "treatment"
Polyvagal-informed therapy does not treat autism core features. Marketing that implies otherwise (most notably some SSP claims) outruns the evidence.
How to use this map. Polyvagal-informed therapy is best framed as adjunct or wraparound rather than primary treatment for any specific comorbidity. The gold-standard treatments for anxiety, depression, and PTSD have substantial evidence; the polyvagal layer can integrate them or provide a useful framework for clients who don't tolerate the standard modalities — but it doesn't replace them. For autism core features, the operative interventions are environmental and structural (sensory accommodation, demand reduction, OT), not polyvagal.
