Co-Regulation: Attachment Theory Roots vs Polyvagal Language

Co-regulation has a 25+ year intellectual history before polyvagal theory ever entered the conversation. The vocabulary changed; the practice didn't.

Attachment-theory lineage (1958-1996) The 38 years during which co-regulation was developed as a clinical and developmental concept. Independent evidence base. Substantial empirical literature.
Polyvagal-theory overlay (1994-2018) Vocabulary added to an already-established practice. Offers a neural-mechanism story that is now contested (Cat 3) for an already-supported clinical phenomenon.
1958
Bowlby begins drafting
Pre-PVT · -36 years
Manuscript work on Attachment and Loss begins. The theoretical groundwork for understanding caregiver-infant emotional regulation is laid in pure attachment-theory terms.
1969
Bowlby, Attachment and Loss Vol. 1
Pre-PVT · -25 years
Hogarth Press, January 1969. The foundational text of attachment theory. Describes the attachment behavioural system, secure base, safe haven — all proto-coregulation concepts.
1969-78
Ainsworth Strange Situation
Pre-PVT · -16 years
Laboratory paradigm for assessing infant-caregiver attachment quality. Establishes secure, anxious, avoidant patterns. Ainsworth et al. Patterns of Attachment (1978).
1979
Bowlby Vol. 2 (Separation) & Vol. 3 (Loss) sequence completes (1973, 1980)
Pre-PVT · -14 years
Bowlby's trilogy completes with separation and loss volumes; attachment theory is now a comprehensive psychological model.
1996
Sroufe, Emotional Development
Pre-PVT clinical adoption · +2 years after PVT introduction
Cambridge University Press. Sroufe formalises attachment as the dyadic regulation of emotion — the infant-caregiver pair is the regulatory unit. This is the canonical formulation of co-regulation in developmental psychology.
1994
Porges, polyvagal presidential address
PVT introduced
Society for Psychophysiology Research presidential address. Published 1995 in Psychophysiology. Polyvagal theory is introduced. Co-regulation already has 25+ years of theoretical and empirical development at this point.
2000s
Schore right-brain attachment neurobiology
PVT vocabulary spreads
Allan Schore publishes extensively on right-brain regulation of attachment. Begins to integrate polyvagal language into attachment-neurobiology frameworks. The marriage of attachment theory and PVT vocabulary takes shape in clinical writing.
2011
Porges, The Polyvagal Theory (book)
PVT formalised in book form
Norton publishes The Polyvagal Theory. Co-regulation now widely framed in polyvagal terms across the trauma-therapy world.
2018
Dana, Polyvagal Theory in Therapy
Clinical operationalisation
Dana's Rhythm of Regulation framework introduces the ladder, glimmers, autonomic mapping. By this point, co-regulation is broadly understood through polyvagal vocabulary in trauma therapy — despite its 25+ year prior history in attachment-theory terms.
The implication. When a clinician says "I co-regulate with my client," this is overwhelmingly a description of attachment-theory-grounded therapeutic presence. Whether the underlying biological substrate is best described by polyvagal theory, neurovisceral integration, or something else does not change the practice. The relational quality is what helps; the explanation of why it helps is what is contested.
