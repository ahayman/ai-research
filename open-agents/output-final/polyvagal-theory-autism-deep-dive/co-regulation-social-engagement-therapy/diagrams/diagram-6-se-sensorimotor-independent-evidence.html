Somatic Experiencing & Sensorimotor Psychotherapy: Independent Evidence Bases

Both modalities developed independently of polyvagal theory. PVT vocabulary was added to already-complete frameworks.

Somatic Experiencing (Peter Levine)
1970s
Levine develops SE foundations PhD in medical biophysics; ethological observations of how prey animals shake off survival activation. Titration and pendulation concepts coined.
1978
Levine meets Porges First meeting; the beginning of what SE-affiliated sources describe as "40 years of fraternal collaboration." Note: SE's core techniques already exist at this point.
1997
Waking the Tiger published North Atlantic Books. Levine's first major book. PVT is mentioned but is not the foundation of the method.
2010
In an Unspoken Voice published North Atlantic Books. Forewords by Gabor Maté. Polyvagal language increasingly integrated, but SE techniques remain the operational frame.
2017
Brom et al. SE RCT (Israel) Journal of Traumatic Stress 30(3). First SE-specific RCT.
Brom 2017 RCT details n = 63 (33 SE, 30 waitlist). 15 weekly SE sessions. Large effect sizes: d = 0.94-1.26 for PTSD symptoms; d = 0.70-1.08 for depression. 44.1% lost PTSD diagnosis post-treatment. Conducted in Israel during period of acute collective trauma.
Sensorimotor Psychotherapy (Pat Ogden)
Early 1970s
Ogden apprentices with Ron Kurtz Body-oriented psychotherapy lineage from Hakomi. Working in psychiatric hospital, notices client disconnection from body and links it to psychological symptoms.
1981
Co-founds Hakomi Institute With Ron Kurtz and a core group. The body-oriented therapy tradition is formalising in institutional form.
Mid 1980s
Hakomi Bodywork → Hakomi Integrative Somatics Ogden founds her own school, evolving away from pure Hakomi.
2000s
Sensorimotor Psychotherapy Institute formed Hakomi Integrative Somatics evolves into SPI. Training programs developed.
2002
First SP paper published Ogden's first formal SP publication.
2006
Trauma and the Body published W. W. Norton, Interpersonal Neurobiology Series (alongside Schore, Siegel, van der Kolk). Phase-oriented trauma treatment model. Integrates attachment theory, neuroscience, mindfulness, Hakomi.
2010s
PVT vocabulary integrated Later SP materials incorporate polyvagal language. The method's foundational structure remains the Hakomi/Gestalt/attachment lineage.
What this shows. Both SE and Sensorimotor Psychotherapy have substantively complete frameworks before polyvagal vocabulary was added to them. SE has its own RCT evidence base (Brom 2017, large effect sizes). Sensorimotor inherits the evidence base of the body-oriented therapy tradition (Hakomi, somatic, Gestalt) plus attachment theory. If polyvagal theory turns out to be biologically incorrect, neither of these modalities loses its core practices or its evidence base. They lose their currently-popular vocabulary for talking about what they do.
