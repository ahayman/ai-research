Dana's Rhythm of Regulation Framework

The four-verb curriculum (befriend, map, navigate, shape) and the polyvagal ladder that anchors it. The canonical clinical translation of polyvagal theory.

The Polyvagal Ladder
VENTRAL VAGAL
Safe & social · regulated · connected · curious · engaged
SYMPATHETIC
Mobilised · fight-or-flight · anxious · activated
DORSAL VAGAL
Immobilised · collapse · shutdown · numb · disconnected
BEFRIEND 1

What: Build a relationship with the autonomic nervous system as something one can come to know rather than something happening to one.

Anti-shame framing: "Your nervous system is trying to keep you safe."

Key in: Anchored (2021) · client-facing book.

MAP 2

What: Personal Profile Map · Triggers Map · Glimmers Map.

Tools: Worksheets to identify specific cues that move the client up or down the ladder.

Key in: Polyvagal Theory in Therapy (2018).

NAVIGATE 3

What: Real-time skills to shift state — paced breathing, posture, prosody, environment, social contact, sensory anchoring.

Note: These tools overlap heavily with any trauma-informed therapy. Polyvagal vocabulary adds the autonomic-state targeting language.

Key in: Polyvagal Exercises (2020) · 50 named practices.

SHAPE 4

What: Repeated practice over time to broaden the window of ventral access.

Mechanism story: Building "neural pathways of safety" — consistent with neuroplasticity-style reasoning even if specific polyvagal mechanism is contested.

Between sessions: Homework and integration.

Session arc (as Dana teaches it)
Beginning
Therapist establishes ventral-vagal connection (regulated presence, attuned prosody, calm). The client lands.
Middle
Titrated approach to sympathetic or dorsal material. State-tracking on the ladder throughout.
End
Re-establish ventral state before the client leaves the room. "Anchor" before transition.
Between
Client uses navigate-and-shape practices to maintain access; brings observations back next session.
