Low Resting RSA: A Transdiagnostic Biomarker

The same RSA signature shows up across many psychiatric conditions, autism among them. This is exactly what Beauchaine's 2015 paper called transdiagnostic.

LOW RESTING RSA

Reduced parasympathetic-vagal cardiac control at baseline. Observed across multiple psychopathologies in independent meta-analyses. Per Beauchaine (PMC4389219, 2015): a non-specific marker of emotion-regulation difficulty across psychiatric categories.

Anxiety Disorders
n=2086 patients vs n=2294 controls / 36 studies
Lower HRV (high-frequency component) across generalised anxiety, panic, social anxiety, OCD. Reduced parasympathetic activation in resting state.
Chalmers et al. 2014, Frontiers in Psychiatry
Depression
Multiple meta-analyses
Major depressive disorder shows reduced resting RSA. The depression-HRV link is among the most replicated findings in the literature.
Beauchaine 2015, multiple meta-analyses
PTSD
Meta-analytic: lower resting RSA vs controls
Resting RSA reliably lower in PTSD relative to controls. The transdiagnostic biomarker framing applies even within trauma-specific contexts.
Campbell et al. 2019 / PubMed 30779926
Autism Spectrum
Cheng 2020: g = -0.52 baseline / g = -0.72 RSA reactivity
Meta-analysis of autism HRV studies. Lower baseline RSA and lower RSA reactivity to social stress. See Category 4 for full evaluation.
Cheng et al. 2020, Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews
Conduct Disorder / Externalising
Attenuated RSA baseline + reduced RSA withdrawal to stressor
Beauchaine's 2007 work on developmental psychopathology found this signature in middle-school and adolescent males with ODD/CD.
Beauchaine et al. 2007, Biological Psychology
Nonsuicidal Self-Injury
Observed in Beauchaine 2015 transdiagnostic review
Adolescents and adults engaging in NSSI show low resting RSA, consistent with broader emotion-dysregulation signature.
Beauchaine 2015, PMC4389219
Schizophrenia Spectrum
Observed across disorders
Schizophrenia and related psychotic disorders also exhibit reduced resting RSA, adding to the transdiagnostic profile.
Beauchaine 2015 review
The implication for PVT-specificity claims. If low RSA is the signature across seven different psychiatric categories, then "low RSA" does not localise to any one polyvagal-defined state. The transdiagnostic finding is what makes RSA a useful general marker of emotion regulation difficulty — and exactly what undermines claims that PVT specifically explains, say, autistic dysregulation. Anything that improves RSA will likely help across these conditions; the improvement does not validate PVT specifically over competing frameworks (NVI, predictive processing, allostatic load).
What this means clinically. For an autistic adult considering polyvagal-informed therapy, the autonomic-state intervention is likely to do real work across whatever combination of comorbidities the client carries. That's a feature, not a bug. The framing — "we're working with your nervous system" — remains true at the level of physiology. What it doesn't license is specific mechanism claims tied to PVT alone.
