"Is Polyvagal The Right Framework?" — A Decision Aid

A practical tool. For each kind of question you might be using polyvagal language for, which framework is actually best-suited? When does PVT add value, and when does it just relabel what another framework predicts more cleanly?

Considering using polyvagal theory framing to understand a phenomenon?

If: HRV is low / emotion regulation is hard

Use: NVI (Neurovisceral Integration). Same HRV predictions as PVT without contested anatomy. Mainstream in autonomic neuroscience. PVT framing is acceptable as clinical shorthand but isn't doing distinct explanatory work.

If: Chronic exhaustion, burnout, "I have nothing left"

Use: Allostasis & allostatic load. The framework Raymaker 2020 / AASPIRE autistic burnout research uses. Predicts recovery through demand reduction, not through "vagal regulation." PVT's "dorsal vagal shutdown" framing is metaphor on top of allostasis substrate.

If: Trying to understand emotion construction / labelling

Use: Constructed Emotion (Barrett) or Predictive Processing (Seth). PVT's "states" framework is too coarse. The emotional granularity construct (Barrett 2004+) is more useful for understanding alexithymia and emotion regulation interventions.

If: Interoception (body awareness, sensing internal state)

Use: Craig 2002/2009 (insula) + Garfinkel 2015 (three dimensions). PVT doesn't represent interoception in the fine-grained way Garfinkel's accuracy/sensibility/awareness model does. For autism: Bird & Cook 2013 (alexithymia, not autism, drives interoception findings) is critical context.

If: Practitioner claims a specific brainstem mechanism

REJECT or hedge. If the claim is "the dorsal vagal nucleus mediates freezing" or "the ventral vagal complex evolved exclusively in mammals," the empirical consensus has rejected these (Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022; Grossman 2023; 2026 Untenable consensus). Anyone making these claims as established science is wrong.

If: PVT provides useful clinical vocabulary

Accept it as heuristic, not theory. If "I'm in dorsal vagal" helps you name what you're experiencing and access support, that's legitimate clinical use. The Journal of Psychiatry Reform 2023 captures this stance: "Polyvagal Approaches: scientifically questionable but useful in practice." Use the framing if it works; don't treat it as biological mechanism.

If: Buying an intervention sold on polyvagal claims

Apply commercial-skepticism filter. Cheap, low-risk interventions (paced breathing, humming, cold exposure) work via known vagal physiology regardless of PVT being right. Expensive commercial products (SSP, vagus-nerve supplements, branded "polyvagal" certifications) need independent evidence. Cf. Cat 6 (Interventions) and Cat 8 (Wellness Industry).

If: Defending PVT as scientifically supported

Carefully — PVT's core claims are contested. The 2026 Untenable consensus (39 co-authors) rejects all five basic premises. PVT survives as a clinical heuristic with cultural inertia, not as a confirmed scientific framework. Honest framing distinguishes "clinically used" from "scientifically established."
The meta-pattern. PVT adds value as a clinical/personal vocabulary for autistic people who find the "ladder" metaphor useful for naming their states — that's real benefit. PVT does NOT add value as the scientific framework for understanding autistic HRV, burnout, emotion regulation, or interoception — for those, mainstream alternatives (NVI, predictive processing, allostasis, constructed emotion) explain the data more parsimoniously without the contested anatomical baggage. Use PVT where it's genuinely useful as scaffolding; don't import its mechanistic claims as if they were established.
