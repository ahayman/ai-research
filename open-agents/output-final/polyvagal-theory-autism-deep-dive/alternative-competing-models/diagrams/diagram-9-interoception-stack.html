The Interoception Stack — How Body Awareness Actually Works

Three layers of interoception research. Craig (2002/2009) established the cortical substrate. Garfinkel (2015) showed the functional differentiation. Bird & Cook (2013) showed alexithymia drives the clinical findings often attributed to autism. None of these require PVT — and PVT's framework can't represent their fine-grained distinctions.

Layer 3 — Clinical Application: Alexithymia Hypothesis

Bird & Cook 2013 (Translational Psychiatry); Shah et al. 2016 (Cortex); Cook et al. 2013 (Psychological Science)
The interoceptive and emotional difficulties often attributed to autism are actually attributable to co-occurring alexithymia, which is present in ~50% of autistic adults but is conceptually distinct from autism.
Key finding. When alexithymia is controlled for, autistic-vs-non-autistic interoception differences largely disappear.
Implication. "Autism causes interoception deficits" → wrong target. Alexithymia does. PVT-for-autism narrative misses this.

Layer 2 — Functional Differentiation: Three Dimensions

Garfinkel, Seth, Barrett, Suzuki, Critchley 2015 (Biological Psychology)
Interoception isn't one thing — it has three dissociable dimensions. A person can have high subjective body awareness (sensibility) but low objective accuracy.
Accuracy. Objective performance on heartbeat detection. How well can you actually count your heartbeats?
Sensibility. Self-reported subjective awareness. How attuned do you believe you are?
Awareness. Metacognitive — does your sensibility match your accuracy?
Autism finding (Garfinkel et al. 2016): autistic adults showed higher sensibility but lower accuracy — an interoceptive awareness mismatch, not simple deficit. PVT cannot represent this nuance.

Layer 1 — Neuroanatomical Substrate: The Anterior Insula

Craig 2002 (Nat Rev Neurosci, 5,400+ citations); Craig 2009 (Nat Rev Neurosci, 6,200+ citations)
The insular cortex (particularly the anterior insula) is the cortical hub of interoception. Body signals are first represented in posterior insula, re-represented in mid-insula, and integrated in anterior insula — where activation corresponds to subjective feeling.
Pathway. Spinal lamina I + vagal afferents → thalamus → posterior insula → mid-insula → anterior insula → conscious feeling
Evidence base. Most-replicated finding in interoception neuroscience. One of the best-established frameworks in cognitive neuroscience overall.
Anterior insula damage > 15% volume = acquired alexithymia (Hogeveen 2016). The insula is causally involved.
How this stack relates to PVT. PVT focuses on brainstem vagal nuclei and peripheral autonomic output. Craig focuses on cortical representation of interoception. Garfinkel decomposes interoception into dissociable functional dimensions. Bird & Cook show alexithymia (not autism per se) drives the clinical findings. These are all explaining body-state awareness — and they do it with finer mechanistic granularity than PVT. PVT can coexist with the interoception stack in principle (the brainstem and cortex aren't competing), but PVT's framework doesn't represent the accuracy-vs-sensibility distinction, the insular pathway, or the alexithymia-vs-autism dissociation. For interoception specifically, the Craig–Garfinkel–Bird & Cook framework is what current research uses.
