The Five Competing Models — Landscape Map

Polyvagal Theory is one framework in a crowded landscape. Five mainstream alternatives explain the same autonomic, interoceptive, and emotion-regulation data without requiring PVT's contested anatomical and evolutionary claims. Plus three interoception-specific frameworks.

Polyvagal Theory (PVT)

Three-tier vagal hierarchy. Contested by the 39-author 2026 Untenable consensus. The framework whose status this chapter is testing.

1. Neurovisceral Integration (NVI)

Thayer & Lane 2000, 2009; Smith 2017

vmPFC tonically inhibits amygdala → vagus → HRV. Mainstream in autonomic neuroscience. Same HRV predictions as PVT without phylogenetic claims.

2. Predictive Processing / Active Inference

Friston, Seth, Barrett, Quattrocki & Friston 2014

Brain as prediction machine. Emotion = interoceptive inference. Dominant contemporary framework in computational neuroscience.

3. Allostasis & Allostatic Load

Sterling & Eyer 1988; McEwen 1998; Sterling 2012

Stability through change. Cumulative wear-and-tear from chronic over-demand. The framework Raymaker 2020 autistic-burnout research uses.

4. Theory of Constructed Emotion

Barrett 2017; Lindquist et al. 2012

Emotions are constructed from interoceptive prediction + concept + context. Not hardwired biological programs — directly contradicts PVT's state architecture.

5. Somatic Marker Hypothesis

Damasio 1994; Damasio & Carvalho 2013

Body-state markers guide decision via vmPFC. Older framework; overlaps PVT's "body feels it first" claim without requiring vagal-specific anatomy.

Interoception-specific frameworks (compatible with above)

Craig 2002, 2009: Anterior insula is the cortical hub of interoception. 11,000+ citations.
Garfinkel 2015: Three dimensions of interoception (accuracy, sensibility, awareness). Dissociable.
Bird & Cook 2013: Alexithymia, not autism, drives interoceptive deficits. ~50% co-occurrence.
What this landscape map shows. The user's "is polyvagal even the right framework?" question depends on whether competing models explain the same data more parsimoniously. Five mainstream frameworks predict autistic HRV findings, emotion regulation difficulties, and burnout-like exhaustion without invoking PVT's anatomically contested claims. The interoception cluster at the bottom further reframes autism-emotion findings as alexithymia-driven and decouples sensibility from accuracy — nuance PVT cannot represent.
