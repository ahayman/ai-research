Somatic Markers — Damasio's Body-Guided Decision-Making

Damasio (1994): body-state signals guide decision-making before conscious deliberation. Adjacent to PVT's "neuroception" concept but doesn't require any specific vagal-nuclei claims. Centered on the ventromedial prefrontal cortex and the broader interoceptive cortical network.

Descartes' Error (1994)
Where it overlaps PVT. Both Damasio and Porges emphasize body-to-brain signals as primary in emotion and decision. PVT's "neuroception" — non-conscious detection of safety/threat — is conceptually adjacent to Damasio's "somatic markers as pre-conscious decision-guidance." Both share the broad framing: the body is upstream of cognition.

Where it differs from PVT. No three-tier vagal hierarchy. No phylogenetic claims about vagal nuclei. No claim that "social engagement" has a dedicated brainstem substrate. Centered on vmPFC + interoceptive cortex (insula), not brainstem nuclei. Updated in Damasio & Carvalho 2013 to converge with predictive-processing / constructed-emotion frameworks. The "body feels it first" insight that makes PVT appealing is fully captured here without the contested phylogenetic baggage.
