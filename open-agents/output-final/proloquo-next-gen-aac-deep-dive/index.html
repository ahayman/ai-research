What happens when you take over a decade of real-world usage data from more than 10,000 AAC users and pour 100 person-years of development into building something entirely new? You get Proloquo -- AssistiveWare's bold, research-driven reimagining of augmentative and alternative communication that earned the first-ever App Store Cultural Impact Award given to an AAC app.

Launched in 2022, Proloquo is not an update to Proloquo2Go. It is a fundamentally different product built on a fundamentally different philosophy: that consistency, motor planning, and an integrated coaching ecosystem matter more than unlimited customization. With over 16,000 pre-planned words -- four times more than any competing AAC system -- and innovative features like Thinking Space and Focus Mode, Proloquo represents a generational leap in how nonspeaking individuals communicate.

"Over a hundred person-years of work went into creating Proloquo and Proloquo Coach." — AssistiveWare

But the app is not without its trade-offs. English-only language support, iPad-only availability, a subscription pricing model, and limited customization compared to its predecessor make Proloquo the right choice for some users and the wrong one for others. Understanding what it does differently -- and why -- is essential for families, clinicians, and educators making one of the most consequential technology decisions in a person's life.

📊 Vocabulary Deep Dive

Vocabulary Size Comparison: Proloquo vs. Competing AAC Apps

Crescendo Evolution: The Research Foundation

Crescendo Evolution was built by analyzing anonymous word usage data from over 10,000 AAC users across both Proloquo2Go (symbol-based) and Proloquo4Text (text-based). This real-world data was combined with extensive vocabulary research to identify the words needed for full participation in educational curriculum from early childhood through late elementary school and beyond.

The result: over 16,000 pre-planned words, including 4,500+ graphic symbols for general conversation and 5,000+ essential curriculum words. This is more than four times the vocabulary of any competing AAC system.

How the Grid Works

  • Home Screen: 48 symbol buttons + 12 text-only buttons
  • 9 Tabs: Organized by topic and function
  • Static Top Rows: Two rows of the most frequently used words remain visible across all views
  • No Back Button: Every word reached through the same consistent motor path
  • 90%+ Curriculum Words: Come preprogrammed out of the box

The Customization Philosophy

Users can add new words, symbols, and photos that are unique to the individual AAC user. Users cannot move or remove preprogrammed vocabulary. This deliberate restriction preserves motor planning integrity -- ensuring that every word is always in the same place, reached through the same path.

This is the central philosophical tension between Proloquo and Proloquo2Go. AssistiveWare argues that consistency enables fluency. Critics argue that it limits personalization for users with unique communication needs.

💰 Pricing Comparison

Cumulative Cost Over 5 Years: Proloquo Subscription vs. Proloquo2Go One-Time

The Subscription Debate

Proloquo2Go charges a one-time fee of $249.99 -- pay once, own it forever. Proloquo uses a subscription model: free to download with a generous 6-month trial, then $99.99 per year.

The math is straightforward: Proloquo becomes more expensive than Proloquo2Go after approximately 2.5 years of use. For a tool that many people need for life, the cumulative difference is significant.

Subscription advocates point out that the initial cost is much lower, that ongoing revenue supports continuous development and the free Proloquo Coach app, and that cloud infrastructure for unlimited team sharing requires ongoing funding.

Subscription critics argue that AAC is a lifelong need and that subscriptions create indefinite financial burden -- a particularly painful consideration for families already managing the considerable expenses associated with disability.

AssistiveWare periodically offers promotional pricing (such as 50% off the first year during AAC Awareness Month), and institutional/volume licensing is available for schools and organizations.

👥 Who Is Proloquo For?

✓ Ideal Candidates

  • Nonspeaking children (as young as 2) who can independently interact with an iPad
  • Adults needing symbol-based AAC communication
  • People with autism, stroke, physical trauma, or progressive conditions (ALS, Parkinson's)
  • Families wanting structured coaching support through Proloquo Coach
  • Educational teams wanting seamless, cloud-synced vocabulary collaboration
  • Users who benefit from strong motor planning and consistent word placement
  • AAC beginners who benefit from a massive pre-built vocabulary that works out of the box

  • Cross-platform support (Android, Windows, macOS)

📖 Deep Dive Topics

Thinking Space: Communication Beyond Sentences ▼

One of Proloquo's most innovative features is Thinking Space -- a flexible whiteboard area that breaks free from the linear sentence bar found in traditional AAC apps.

In Thinking Space, users can drag symbols from the vocabulary grid into an open canvas, then cluster, rearrange, and connect them freely. This enables forms of expression that go beyond constructing grammatical sentences: brainstorming, concept mapping, showing relationships between ideas, and exploratory communication.

For emergent communicators -- including very young children just beginning their AAC journey -- Thinking Space removes the pressure of building "correct" sentences. A child can place symbols for "dog," "park," and "happy" on the canvas to communicate a feeling or memory without needing to construct "I was happy at the park with the dog."

The contents of Thinking Space can be saved as photos, printed, or shared with communication partners, making it a powerful tool for both real-time interaction and reflective communication.

Focus Mode: Teaching Without Restricting ▼

Focus Mode addresses one of the thorniest challenges in AAC education: how to direct a learner's attention to target vocabulary without restricting their access to the full word set.

Traditional approaches to teaching AAC often involve shrinking the grid, hiding buttons, or removing words to reduce overwhelm. But these strategies limit communicative freedom and can create prompt dependency, where the user only communicates what they have been explicitly set up to say.

Proloquo's Focus Mode uses light and motion to gently highlight target words, creating visual pathways that invite exploration without building walls. The full vocabulary remains accessible at all times. If a student navigates away from the tab containing the focus words, the visual cues follow them, gently guiding attention back without forcing compliance.

This design reflects a broader philosophy in modern AAC practice: that access to robust vocabulary should never be sacrificed for instructional convenience.

Voice Technology: Acapela Neural + Personal Voice ▼

Proloquo was the first AAC app to incorporate Acapela Neural Voices -- speech synthesis powered by deep neural network technology that produces significantly more natural, smoother-sounding output compared to traditional concatenative synthesis.

The voices include multiple regional English accents, allowing users to select a voice that reflects their identity and geographic context. The app also supports Apple's system voices.

Most notably, Proloquo supports Apple's Personal Voice feature (iOS 17+). Personal Voice allows users to create a synthesized voice that sounds like their own natural speech by reading 150 phrases to train the model. For people with progressive conditions like ALS or Parkinson's who may lose their natural voice over time, this feature represents a profound form of identity preservation -- continuing to communicate in your own voice even after the ability to produce natural speech has diminished.

Some users have reported quality issues with recent voice updates, describing a "grating breathy quality" in certain voice packs. AssistiveWare continues to refine voice quality with each update.

Cloud Architecture & Team Sharing ▼

Proloquo's cloud-based architecture enables the most seamless team collaboration experience in the AAC market. When a family purchases a Proloquo subscription, every member of the AAC user's team can download both Proloquo and Proloquo Coach for free and sync automatically.

All vocabulary data is stored in the cloud and syncs instantly across devices. When a teacher adds a new word at school, it appears on the home iPad within moments. Role-based permissions allow the team to control who can use the vocabulary, who can edit it, and who can manage team membership.

This stands in sharp contrast to Proloquo2Go, where sharing vocabulary between devices required manual backup and restore procedures through iTunes, Dropbox, or WiFi -- a process that was technically challenging and often resulted in teams working with inconsistent vocabulary setups across settings.

The cloud sync also ensures that the AAC user always has the same words in the same places regardless of location -- home, school, therapy, or community.

The 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award ▼

In November 2023, Apple selected Proloquo for its App Store Award for Cultural Impact -- an honor recognizing apps that have made "a lasting impact on people's lives and communities." Proloquo was the first AAC app ever to receive this distinction.

The award brought unprecedented mainstream visibility to AAC technology. For a field that often operates in the margins of the tech industry, Apple's recognition signaled that communication access is not a niche concern but a cultural imperative.

This recognition came alongside apps like Duolingo and Headspace in previous years, placing AAC technology on the same stage as some of the most widely used applications in the world.
