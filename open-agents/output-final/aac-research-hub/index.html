Cross-Cutting Findings

Key insights across all three phases

Products cover only 2 of 7 domains
All 9 AAC apps focus on Expressive output and Physical access. Receptive, Pragmatic, Cognitive, Psychosocial, and Rate/Efficiency domains remain underserved.
The taxonomy reveals that communication challenges span far beyond speech output. Yet the entire product market is built around "type/select then speak" paradigms, ignoring comprehension, social cognition, memory, and the rate gap between 8-15 WPM (AAC) and 150-250 WPM (natural speech).
8 patent-free zones identified
Receptive support, cognitive-communication, fatigue management, conversational repair, LLM-powered prediction, and dementia AAC all have virtually zero patent barriers.
While Tobii holds 1,000+ eye tracking patents and PRC-Saltillo controls Minspeak through ~2033, the most promising opportunity areas are completely open. No AAC-specific LLM patents have been granted. Expired foundational patents (Baker/Minspeak, dynamic grid navigation, NLP symbol parsing) further expand the buildable space.
Market consolidating into 3 power centers
Tobii Dynavox (acquisitions 2022-2025), Smartbox Group (PE-backed by Verdane, Jan 2026), and PRC-Saltillo (Avaz + Therapy Box) are absorbing competitors.
PRC-Saltillo now controls 3 of 14 analyzed AAC apps (TouchChat, LAMP WFL, Avaz). Forbes AAC controls CoughDrop (and influenced the Sweet Suite fork). AssistiveWare maintains 3 products (Proloquo, Proloquo2Go, Proloquo4Text). Tobii Dynavox acquired Acapela (2022), Rehadapt (2023), and made 4 deals in 2024-2025. Smartbox/Verdane acquired TTMT (148 employees, Medicare in all 50 states). This consolidation reduces consumer choice and creates openings for independent entrants.
On-device LLMs enable breakthrough AAC
3B parameter models run on smartphones. SpeakFaster research shows 29-60% rate improvement. No commercial AAC product uses LLMs yet.
A quantized 3B model uses approximately 1.5GB RAM, achieves sub-50ms time-to-first-token, and can power prediction, text simplification, context-aware vocabulary, abbreviation expansion, and conversational repair suggestions -- all offline. The SpeakFaster project (Google Research + Team Gleason, Nature Communications) validated this in the field with ALS eye-gaze users.
30-50% abandonment signals product-market failure
Nearly half of AAC systems are abandoned. Top factors: lack of training, poor device-user fit, and caregiver buy-in. Products are not adapting to users.
No AAC app adapts to the user's fluctuating state (fatigue, cognitive decline, recovery). All apps assume stable abilities. For progressive conditions (ALS, dementia, MS), this means the product becomes unusable as the disease advances. For recovering conditions (TBI, stroke), the product doesn't grow with the user. This is a solvable engineering problem, not a research gap.
Dementia is the largest underserved population
57M people globally (projected 139M by 2050), 23% of all potential AAC beneficiaries. No major commercial AAC app is designed for dementia.
Communication difficulties are among the earliest symptoms. Over 75% of dementia cases are undiagnosed. All mainstream AAC apps assume stable or improving abilities, the exact opposite of dementia's trajectory. Niche research tools (CIRCA, My PATI) exist but lack mainstream distribution. This is the single largest diagnostic group that could benefit from AAC technology.
