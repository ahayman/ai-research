Executive Summary

Highest Risk Areas

  • Eye-gaze AAC: Tobii holds 1,000+ patents. Any eye-tracking AAC product must navigate their portfolio or license.
  • PRC-Saltillo enforces aggressively: The Speak for Yourself lawsuit led to app removal from Apple/Google stores WITHOUT a court injunction.
  • Microsoft holds 2 key AAC patents: SGD proxies (US9,679,497) and conversation throughput (US10,262,555) directly target AAC interaction patterns.
  • Apple's Personal Voice: Patent-protected voice synthesis built into every iOS device threatens voice-banking solutions.

Biggest Opportunities

  • LLM/GenAI-powered AAC: Nearly wide open patent territory for AAC-specific generative AI applications.
  • Foundational AAC patents EXPIRED: Minspeak (US4,661,916), dynamic keyboards (US5,748,177, US5,920,303) are all expired.
  • Symbol systems use copyright, not patents: You can create your own symbol set freely. Open alternatives (Mulberry, ARASAAC) exist.
  • LAMP is a methodology, not patented: Motor planning principles are freely usable; only the specific LAMP Words for Life product is copyrighted.
  • Multilingual/underserved language AAC: Virtually no patent protection exists.
  • Teletherapy-integrated AAC: Unpatented intersection of AAC and remote therapy.
  • Social media integration for AAC: No patent coverage for AAC designed for modern communication platforms.

Key Takeaways for New Market Entrant

Immediate Actions Required

  • Formal Freedom-to-Operate analysis: Hire patent counsel to review Tobii eye-tracking patents and Microsoft AAC patents before developing any eye-gaze or SGD features.
  • Review PRC-Saltillo patent portfolio: Understand which improvement patents are still active vs. expired (foundational ones are expired, newer ones active through 2027-2034).
  • File provisional patents: In identified white spaces (LLM-powered AAC, multimodal adaptive systems, multilingual AAC) to establish priority dates.
  • Monitor quarterly: Neuralink, Meta, Apple, and university patent filings for BCI and emerging tech developments.

Safe Design Approaches

  • Single-meaning symbols (not polysemous/Minspeak) with standard folder/category navigation
  • Standard NLP-based word prediction (not PRC's specific "word chunk" approach)
  • Apple's built-in eye tracking (iOS 18+) or licensed Tobii SDK rather than custom eye-tracking hardware
  • Own original symbol artwork or use Mulberry (CC BY-SA, commercially usable)
  • LLM-powered contextual suggestions (no existing AAC-specific patents)
  • Open Board Format (.obf) for interoperability

Patent Litigation History in AAC

PRC / Semantic Compaction Systems v. Speak for Yourself (2012-2013)

Landmark Case | Federal Court, USA | Patents: US5,748,177 & US5,920,303

The most important AAC patent litigation case in history. PRC and SCS sued Speak for Yourself LLC, a small AAC app developer, for infringement of two dynamic keyboard patents. Despite no court injunction being issued, Apple removed SfY from the App Store and Google removed it from Play Store, silencing actual AAC users who relied on the app.

Key lesson: Platform gatekeepers (Apple/Google) can amplify patent enforcement far beyond what courts order. A patent complaint alone may be enough to get a competing app removed.

Outcome: Settlement - SCS granted Speak for Yourself a non-exclusive license. Both asserted patents have since EXPIRED (2015 and 2017). Coverage

Tobii AB v. Pupil Labs GmbH (Germany, 2025)

Munich Regional Court I | Case 7 O 15033/24 | Patent: EP 3,332,285 B1 | Ruled Oct 9, 2025

Tobii sued Pupil Labs, maker of "Neon" and "Pupil Invisible" eye-tracking systems, for patent infringement in Germany. The court ruled partially in Tobii's favor, prohibiting Pupil Labs from offering/delivering certain products without Tobii's permission and ordering damages.

Outcome: Partial win for Tobii. Demonstrates Tobii's willingness and ability to enforce patents internationally. Subject to appeal. Pupil Labs Response

University of Virginia Patent Foundation v. DynaVox Systems (2015)

U.S. District Court, Western District of Virginia | Case 3:2015cv00015 | Filed March 27, 2015

UVA sued DynaVox for breach of contract - failure to pay royalties for the "ERICA" eye gaze system patents. DynaVox held an exclusive license to UVA-developed eye-gaze computer apparatus patents and paid royalties from 2005-2009 before stopping.

Outcome: Shows Tobii Dynavox's eye tracking AAC technology has roots in university-licensed IP. Coverage

Tobii Technology, Inc. v. Weinblatt (USA, 2020)

Declaratory Judgment Action | 2020

In April 2020, Weinblatt sent a letter to Tobii claiming infringement of his patents by Tobii products (Pro Lab, Pro Spectrum, Pro Fusion). Tobii responded by filing a declaratory judgment action seeking a noninfringement ruling.

Outcome: Demonstrates Tobii proactively litigates to protect freedom to operate. Analysis

Non-Patent IP Protections (Copyrights, Trademarks, Trade Secrets)

Symbol sets in AAC are primarily protected by copyright and trademark, not patents. These protections are often MORE important barriers than patents for entering the AAC space.

White Space Innovation Opportunities

1. LLM/GenAI-Powered AAC (Top Priority)

  • Context-aware message generation using large language models
  • Personalized vocabulary prediction with transformer models
  • Automated conversation scaffolding for AAC users
  • Smart phrase generation from minimal input (1-2 symbols to full sentences)
  • Patent landscape: Nearly wide open for AAC-specific applications
  • Research shows 57% more motor action savings than traditional predictive keyboards
  • Eye-gaze AAC users show 29-60% text-entry rate improvements with LLM assistance

2. Multimodal Unified AAC Platform

  • Combine touch, eye-gaze, voice, gesture, and BCI in single adaptive system
  • Real-time automatic switching between modalities based on user state and fatigue
  • IRISBOND is pursuing this but patent coverage appears limited
  • Could file first-mover patents on adaptive modality switching

3. AAC for Underserved Languages & Cultures

  • Multilingual AAC with culturally appropriate symbols
  • Tonal language support (Mandarin, Vietnamese, Thai, etc.)
  • Non-Latin script AAC (Arabic, Devanagari, CJK)
  • Virtually no patent protection in this space - huge market opportunity

4. Teletherapy-Integrated AAC

  • AAC platforms with built-in SLP consultation and remote monitoring
  • Data analytics for therapy outcome tracking
  • Remote assessment and device configuration tools
  • Patent white space at AAC-telehealth intersection

5. AI-Powered Symbol Generation

  • Generate personalized symbols from photos using generative AI
  • Create culturally relevant symbols on demand
  • Adaptive symbol complexity based on user cognitive level
  • No existing patents on AI-generated AAC symbols

6. Social Media & Modern Communication

  • AAC interfaces designed for TikTok, Instagram, Discord, etc.
  • Emoji and meme generation for AAC users
  • Real-time group chat participation tools
  • Essentially no patent coverage in this space

7. Edge AI / On-Device Intelligence

  • Running personalized language models entirely on-device for privacy and speed
  • Federated learning across AAC users for improved predictions
  • Limited patent coverage for AAC-specific edge AI
