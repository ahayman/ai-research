AAC Abandonment: Condition-Specific Drivers

Overall: 30-50% abandonment rate in children
Top success factors: caregiver buy-in, family involvement, ongoing support. Top failure factors: lack of training, poor device-user fit.
Autism
Stigma, stakeholder knowledge gaps, and poor device-user fit drive abandonment. Parents and teachers may lack training on aided language stimulation.
Aphasia
Clinician misconceptions about AAC "giving up on speech" lead to under-referral and late introduction, reducing effectiveness and adoption.
Progressive Conditions (ALS, MS, Parkinson's)
The "assess once" myth: failure to re-evaluate AAC as disease progresses leads to devices that no longer match abilities. Access methods may need to change multiple times.

Top 5 Apps: Ecosystem Radar

Key Ecosystem Pain Points

No Universal SLP Dashboard

SLPs managing 50-80+ student caseloads have no cross-app management tool. No automated IEP integration or standardized outcomes measurement exists in any product.

Home-School Generalization Gap

The #1 barrier to AAC success for autistic children. No single product integrates parent coaching, SLP analytics, school deployment, and teletherapy into a unified ecosystem.

Vendor Lock-in via 7 Mechanisms

Vocabulary customization, motor planning dependencies, symbol system familiarity, professional training investment, insurance/funding ties, analytics history, and limited interoperability all reinforce lock-in.

Subscription Controversy

TD Snap's May 2024 switch from one-time ($79.99-$310) to $9.99/month generated massive backlash: AT Act Programs cannot purchase subscriptions; managed iPads excluded; users described it as "holding children's voices hostage."

No GLP-Specific Mode

Gestalt Language Processing is prevalent in autism, yet no AAC app offers gestalt recording, stage-based progression, or phrase-level navigation. 96%+ of ASD children report sensory differences -- no app offers sensory-aware customization.

AI Capability Level by Product

Key Research Findings

SpeakFaster: 29-60% Rate Improvement

Google Research fine-tuned LaMDA (64B parameters) for AAC. Published in Nature Communications (Nov 2024). 57% more motor action savings vs. traditional prediction. Best LLM result: 30.4 WPM.

CHI 2024: 82% of Autistic Participants Want LLM

82% wanted to continue using LLM for communication assistance; 64% would consider it a primary resource. Expert raised concerns about questionable advice quality -- hallucination risk is critical (LLM literally puts wrong words in user's mouth).

On-Device LLM Now Practical

iPhone 17 Pro: 136 tok/s (Cactus framework), sub-50ms time-to-first-token, 20-word sentence in <200ms. Eliminates the infrastructure barrier. On-device processing also eliminates most HIPAA concerns.

Communication Rate Gap Persists

AAC users communicate at 8-10 WPM vs. 150-160 WPM for natural speech (less than 10% of natural speed). No commercial AAC product uses LLMs as a core communication engine -- the field remains wide open.

ASD-Specific LLM Capabilities

Figurative language detection/translation, social pragmatics support, context-aware prediction, conversation preview/rehearsal, and real-time speech simplification. GPT-4o shows high agreement with human experts in evaluating pragmatic reasoning.

Emotional Detection Technology: Accuracy by Modality

Research Findings & ASD Considerations

Zero Apps Detect Emotional State

All emotional features across all 15 apps are output-oriented (expression, not detection). Three apps offer manually-selected emotional expression. No app adapts its interface based on any emotional or physiological signal.

Touchscreen Patterns: Primary Detection Modality

Interaction-pattern-based fatigue/frustration detection (86-93% accuracy) is always available, requires no additional hardware, is least invasive, and privacy-respecting. "Autism Motor Signature" study (Anzulewicz et al. 2016, Scientific Reports) achieved 93% from touchscreen patterns alone.

Facial Recognition Unreliable for ASD

Only 37% of autistic individuals express emotions in neurotypical patterns. 40-65% have alexithymia. Standard FER models trained on neurotypical data will not work reliably for autistic users without custom training on autistic expression data.

EU AI Act: Fatigue Detection Is Exempt

EU AI Act (Feb 2025) bans emotion recognition in education but exempts medical/safety uses. Critically, it explicitly notes that "physical states, such as pain and fatigue, are not considered emotions" -- fatigue detection is NOT banned. COPPA 2025 requires robust compliance for biometric/behavioral data in children's apps.

Recommended Multi-Modal Approach

Primary: Touchscreen interaction patterns (~86-93%). Opt-in: HRV via Apple Watch (75-85%). Supplemental: Eye gaze patterns (~66%). Frame as "adaptive support system" not "emotion detection." Lead with fatigue/distress detection for regulatory safety and ethical clarity.

Voice Cloning Technologies: Minimum Audio Required

Lower is better. ElevenLabs and open-source tools need only seconds of audio vs. hours for legacy systems. Apple Personal Voice improved from 150 phrases (~15 min) to 10 phrases (~1 min) in iOS 26.

Key Findings & Identity Insights

Only 2 of 15 Apps Have Integrated Voice Cloning

Spoken uses ElevenLabs (10-second instant clone). Grid uses ElevenLabs (free for 18+) plus SpeakUnique. The other 13 apps offer no built-in cloning -- the best voice technology is locked behind 2 products.

Voice Banking Adoption Crisis

12% of MND patients bank their voice before losing speech. Primary barriers: late referral, lack of awareness, emotional difficulty confronting disease progression, and technical burden of recording 1,600 sentences.

On-Device Voice Cloning Now Viable

NeuTTS Air (748M params, 3s audio) and Chatterbox (350M params, 5s audio, MIT licensed) can run on phones/Raspberry Pi. Yet zero AAC apps have integrated any open-source voice cloning tool.

COPPA 2025: Voiceprints Are Biometric Data

Effective June 2025, voiceprints are classified alongside fingerprints and iris patterns. Voice cloning for children under 13 requires verifiable parental consent. No AAC-specific exemption exists in any deepfake legislation (169 laws since 2022).
