AAC Abandonment: Condition-Specific Drivers

Overall: 30-50% abandonment rate in children
Top success factors: caregiver buy-in, family involvement, ongoing support. Top failure factors: lack of training, poor device-user fit.
Autism
Stigma, stakeholder knowledge gaps, and poor device-user fit drive abandonment. Parents and teachers may lack training on aided language stimulation.
Aphasia
Clinician misconceptions about AAC "giving up on speech" lead to under-referral and late introduction, reducing effectiveness and adoption.
Progressive Conditions (ALS, MS, Parkinson's)
The "assess once" myth: failure to re-evaluate AAC as disease progresses leads to devices that no longer match abilities. Access methods may need to change multiple times.

Emerging Technology Pipeline

Technologies poised to transform AAC. Evidence from Nature, NEJM, and shipping products.

LLM-Powered AAC
29-60%
Rate improvement in lab/field tests (Nature Communications). SpeakFaster project (Google Research). No commercial AAC-specific deployment yet.
BCI ECoG (Metzger, Nature 2023)
78 WPM
Brainstem stroke patient. 75% accuracy. Lab/clinical trial; not commercially available.
BCI Intracortical (Card & Willett, NEJM 2024)
97.5%
Accuracy with 125K-word vocabulary, ~32 WPM. ALS patient. Lab/clinical trial; not commercially available.

Shipping Now

Apple Personal Voice
SHIPPING (iOS 17+)
On-device voice synthesis from 150 sentences (~15 min recording). Free, private, works as system voice in any iOS AAC app. Limited satisfaction data available.
ElevenLabs Voice Cloning
SHIPPING (via Spoken)
Minimum 10 seconds of audio needed (1-2 minutes recommended for quality). Integrated into Spoken app. Free tier limited to ~10 min/month; full access requires ElevenLabs subscription.

Research Stage

LLM-Powered AAC Prediction
EARLY RESEARCH
SpeakFaster (Google Research): 29-60% text-entry rate improvement. No AAC-specific LLM patents granted yet -- wide open innovation space.
AI Abbreviation Expansion
PROTOTYPE
SpeakFaster achieves 70%+ abbreviation expansion accuracy. Full sentences from abbreviated input. Not patented as AAC system.
Brain-Computer Interfaces
LAB / CLINICAL TRIAL
ECoG (78 WPM / 75% accuracy) and intracortical (32 WPM / 97.5% accuracy) approaches show transformative potential but remain years from commercial viability. Note: no single BCI achieves both top speed and top accuracy simultaneously.
