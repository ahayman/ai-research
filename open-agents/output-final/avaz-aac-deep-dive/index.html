Origin Story: From Chennai to the World

The story of Avaz begins in 2009 in the special education classrooms of Chennai, India. Ajit Narayanan, an electrical engineering graduate from the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras, had been working with schools serving children with autism, cerebral palsy, and other complex communication needs. What he found was a vast, unmet need.

In India, an estimated 2-3 million children have autism spectrum disorder, and millions more have conditions that affect speech. Yet the AAC devices available cost between $5,000 and $10,000, supported only English, and were designed entirely for Western users. For a country where the average annual household income was around $1,500, these devices existed in a parallel universe.

Narayanan's insight was both simple and radical: what if AAC technology could be built on affordable consumer hardware, using languages that Indian children actually spoke, with vocabulary that reflected Indian culture -- the foods, festivals, family structures, and daily routines of South Asian life? The result was Avaz -- meaning "Voice" in Hindi.

How Avaz Works

Avaz AAC operates through two complementary communication modes that allow users to grow with the app as their skills develop. A Quick Toggle feature enables seamless switching between both modes within the same session.

Picture Mode: Visual Communication

Avaz's symbol-based communication system, designed for users who communicate primarily through images. The app comes pre-loaded with over 15,000 SymbolStix pictures from a library of more than 40,000, covering an enormous range of words, concepts, and ideas.

Symbols are organized in a grid layout using the Fitzgerald Key color coding system. Users build sentences by tapping symbols in sequence. A sentence strip at the top of the screen shows the constructed message, which can be spoken aloud using high-quality text-to-speech voices.

The system uses consistent motor planning -- core words appear in the same position across all vocabulary levels, allowing users to develop muscle memory that accelerates communication speed over time.

Keyboard Mode: Text-Based Communication

For users who can read and type, Keyboard Mode provides a full QWERTY keyboard with predictive text. This mode is essential for older users, adults with acquired communication disorders (such as aphasia from stroke), and children who have progressed beyond symbol-based communication.

The app grows with the user -- a child might start with Picture Mode and gradually transition to Keyboard Mode as literacy skills develop, without needing to switch to a different app entirely.

Core Word Philosophy

Avaz's vocabulary is organized around a research-backed principle: core words -- the small set of words that make up approximately 80% of everything we say -- should be the foundation of AAC communication. Words like "want," "go," "more," "help," and "like" are used constantly across every conversation context.

Avaz organizes these into 3 graded vocabulary levels based on generative language development principles. The Frozen Row feature ensures the most critical core words remain visible at all times, regardless of which category folder the user is browsing.

🎨

Fitzgerald Key Color Coding

Avaz uses the Fitzgerald Key, a decades-old educational convention that color-codes words by their grammatical function. This system is consistent with materials used across special education classrooms worldwide, allowing children to transfer their understanding between the app and other learning environments.

YellowPeople & Pronouns
GreenVerbs & Actions
OrangeAdjectives & Descriptors
BlueNouns & Objects
PinkSocial Phrases
White/GrayFunction Words

This color coding allows quicker visual scanning and helps users rapidly identify parts of speech, building grammatical awareness naturally through repeated exposure.

What Makes Avaz Unique

Cross-Platform (iOS + Android)

The only major AAC app available on both iOS and Android. Competitors like Proloquo2Go, TouchChat, and LAMP Words for Life are iOS-only. In India, where Android commands 95%+ market share, this is critical.

AI-Powered Expressive Tones

Uses AI to infuse emotional nuance into synthesized speech: excitement, frustration, sarcasm, sadness, curiosity. Traditional AAC produces flat, monotone output -- Avaz lets users convey feelings, not just words.

40,000+ SymbolStix Pictures

Pre-loaded with 15,000+ symbols from a library of 40,000+. High-quality, professionally designed symbols covering an enormous range of words, concepts, and ideas. Customizable with personal photos.

Built-in Caregiver Training

The Avaz Dashboard provides hands-on AAC strategy guidance for 10+ real-world scenarios. No need for external training resources -- everything a caregiver needs is inside the app.

Avaz Dashboard: Built-in Training

One of the most common barriers to successful AAC use is not the technology itself but the lack of training for supporting adults. Avaz addresses this directly with its built-in Dashboard system.

Caregiver Training Scenarios

Hands-on guidance for 10+ real-world AAC scenarios: mealtimes, circle time, reading activities, playground interactions, bedtime routines, and more. Each scenario includes step-by-step strategies.

Progress Tracking

Comprehensive analytics with rich graphs and charts tracking communication development. Monitor word usage patterns, vocabulary growth, and session engagement. Email notifications keep stakeholders informed.

AAC Strategies

Elaborate tips and strategies for comprehensive caregiver training. Learn about modeling, prompting hierarchies, core word instruction, and communication temptations -- all without leaving the app.

Avaz Live

Remote therapy sessions from within the app. Screen sharing with therapists or parents for real-time collaboration. Stable, secure connections for seamless teletherapy.

PRC-Saltillo Acquisition (October 2025)

In October 2025, Avaz became a wholly owned subsidiary of PRC-Saltillo, the global leader in AAC technology based in Wooster, Ohio. PRC-Saltillo's portfolio includes TouchChat, LAMP Words for Life, Dialogue AAC, Unity AAC, and dedicated hardware devices.

A New Chapter for Global AAC Access

All Avaz employees retained. No pricing changes planned. A newly incorporated entity in India strengthens PRC-Saltillo's ability to serve AAC users across India and Southeast Asia.

"AAC is a right, not a privilege." -- Sarah Wilds, CEO of PRC-Saltillo

The acquisition positions Avaz to leverage PRC-Saltillo's research capabilities, clinical partnerships, and global distribution -- while continuing to serve the markets and languages that make it unique.

Strengths & Limitations

✅ Strengths
  • Cross-platform -- Only major AAC app on both iOS and Android
  • 8+ Indian languages -- Unmatched regional language coverage with culturally authentic vocabulary
  • Most affordable -- $199.99 lifetime vs $249-$299 competitors; $9.99/mo subscription option
  • Built-in training -- Avaz Dashboard provides caregiver guidance for 10+ scenarios
  • Research-based vocabulary -- Core word system with consistent motor planning
  • Media-rich -- YouTube, GIFs, and animation features unique among AAC apps
  • AI Expressive Tones -- Emotional voice output (excitement, sarcasm, sadness, etc.)
  • Progress tracking -- Graphs, charts, and email notifications for therapists/parents
  • Free trial -- 14-day no-credit-card trial lowers barrier to entry
  • PRC-Saltillo backing -- Resources of the world's leading AAC company
⚠ Limitations
  • Speech controls -- Voice speed and pitch adjustment range may not satisfy all users
  • US market presence -- Smaller user community and fewer SLP resources than Proloquo2Go in North America
  • Subscription concerns -- Some users frustrated that voice output requires paid subscription
  • Prediction engine -- Sentence/grammar prediction less sophisticated than some competitors
  • Navigation density -- Grid layout can overwhelm users with motor planning difficulties (apraxia)
  • Ecosystem size -- Fewer third-party resources, training videos, and community materials

Conclusion

Avaz AAC occupies a unique and important position in the AAC landscape. It is the only major communication app available on both iOS and Android, supports more languages than any competitor, and is priced to be accessible to families in developing countries where the need is greatest. Its combination of research-based vocabulary design, AI-powered expressive tones, media-rich communication, and built-in caregiver training makes it one of the most feature-complete AAC apps available at any price.

The 2025 acquisition by PRC-Saltillo brings global AAC leadership resources while preserving Avaz's identity and mission. For the millions of children and adults around the world who cannot speak, Avaz represents the fundamental promise that technology can be a great equalizer -- that a child in a village in Tamil Nadu deserves the same right to express themselves as a child in a suburb of Boston.

In Hindi, Avaz means "Voice." For its users, it is exactly that.
