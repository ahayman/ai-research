i Overview

Proloquo2Go is the world's most popular symbol-based Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) app, developed by AssistiveWare, a Netherlands-based company founded in 2000. Released in April 2009, it was the first full-featured AAC app for iOS and has since been trusted by hundreds of thousands of users worldwide.

The app enables nonspeaking children and adults with conditions including autism, Down syndrome, cerebral palsy, apraxia, and dysarthria to communicate through a symbol-based interface. Its research-based Crescendo vocabulary system, built from data gathered from over 10,000 users, provides a framework that grows with the user from simple single-word communication to complex multi-word sentences.

Before Proloquo2Go, dedicated AAC devices cost between $5,000 and $15,000. At $249.99 as a one-time purchase, it democratized access to communication technology and triggered what AssistiveWare calls "the consumer device-based AAC revolution."

📅 History & Timeline

The story of AssistiveWare begins with a personal tragedy. In 1996, Giesbert Nijhuis, a close friend of David Niemeijer, became paralyzed from the neck down in a car accident. Niemeijer responded by developing accessibility software, eventually founding AssistiveWare in Amsterdam in 2000. The company's name "Proloquo" derives from the Latin word for "speak out."

⚙ Complete Feature Set

Crescendo Vocabulary System

Built on analysis of data from over 10,000 users, Crescendo is grounded in a powerful linguistic insight: just 200-400 "core words" account for roughly 80% of everything we say. The system offers three progressive levels designed to grow with the user:

The folder-based organization places core words on the main page (accessible with a single tap) while fringe vocabulary lives in categorical folders. The system includes grammar support, color-coded parts of speech (Fitzgerald Key), and over 27,000 symbols with the ability to add custom photos from camera, photo library, or Dropbox.

Voice Technology

100+ free natural-sounding voices powered by Acapela Neural Voice technology. AssistiveWare pioneered children's TTS voices in 2012, and the latest versions feature AI-powered neural voices that are significantly smoother and more natural.

Voice updates are now decoupled from app updates (since v8.4), so users receive voice improvements without full reinstalls.

♿ Accessibility & Access Methods

Where Proloquo2Go truly distinguishes itself from competitors is in its comprehensive accessibility support. The app was built from the ground up to serve users with vastly different physical and cognitive abilities. No other AAC app offers this breadth of access methods.

Grid Flexibility (23 Layouts) ▼

From 9 buttons (for users with significant motor or vision challenges) to 144 buttons per page (for users with good fine motor skills). The recommended default is 77 buttons, though research reveals that 80% of users override this setting. Adjustable toolbar sizes also accommodate eye-tracking users who need larger targets.

Switch Scanning Methods (5 Modes) ▼
  • Automatic Scanning: System cycles through items; user activates switch to select
  • Step Scanning: User advances through items one at a time with switch presses
  • Inverse Scanning: Items advance automatically; user holds switch to stop
  • Automatic Step Scanning: Combines automatic advancement with step-based selection
  • Screen-as-Switch Mode: The device screen itself serves as a large switch

Supports both Bluetooth and wired external switches. Single-switch and two-switch configurations available.

Auditory Scanning ▼

Items are spoken aloud as they are highlighted during scanning -- essential for users with vision impairments who cannot rely on visual cues. This feature was introduced in version 4.0 (2014) and was a first in iOS AAC apps.

Apple Watch Integration ▼

The Apple Watch serves dual purposes: users can speak directly from their wrist (even in Silent Mode since v8.5), and the watch can function as a single switch for controlling Proloquo2Go on a paired iPhone. This provides a discreet access method that does not draw attention to the user's communication needs.

Phone Call Voice Support ▼

Version 8.7 (February 2025) introduced "Add audio in calls" -- allowing users to communicate with their Proloquo2Go voice during phone and FaceTime calls on iOS/iPadOS 18.2+. This represents a significant leap in communication independence, previously limited to dedicated AAC hardware.

Additional Accessibility Features ▼
  • VoiceOver Compatibility: Works with Apple's built-in screen reader
  • Hold Duration: Adjustable for users with fine motor challenges
  • Select on Release: Alternative selection method for motor control
  • Keyguard Support: Physical overlays for motor assistance
  • Adjustable Toolbar: Bigger targets for eye-tracking users

⚖ Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Industry pioneer -- First full-featured AAC app for iOS (2009), established trust and community
  • Research-backed vocabulary -- Crescendo built from 10,000+ user data, not guesswork
  • Unmatched accessibility -- 23 grid sizes, 5 scanning modes, auditory scanning, keyguard, Apple Watch
  • True bilingual communication -- Code-switching across languages mid-sentence
  • One-time purchase -- $249.99 with lifetime updates, no subscription
  • 100+ natural voices -- Including genuine children's voices and neural technology
  • Active development -- Regular meaningful updates (phone calls, neural voices)
  • Deep customization -- Nearly everything configurable for individual needs
  • Insurance-fundable -- One-time cost accepted by state programs
  • Apple ecosystem integration -- Watch, phone calls, iCloud, Family Sharing
  • Strong support ecosystem -- Extensive documentation, webinars, community

Weaknesses

  • Steep learning curve -- Navigation and customization difficult for new users
  • Complex folder navigation -- Multi-tap paths to fringe vocabulary frustrate some users
  • Motor planning concerns -- Buttons can be moved, undermining muscle memory
  • iOS only -- No Android, Windows, or Chromebook support
  • High cost -- $249.99 plus requires Apple device
  • Grid size paradox -- 80% ignore recommended 77-button setting; smaller grids = worse outcomes
  • No symbol-mode word prediction -- Available in some competing apps
  • Word duplication in folders -- Same words in multiple locations can confuse users
  • Future uncertainty -- Proloquo (2022) is "next gen" -- questions about long-term focus
  • Professional expertise required -- SLPs unfamiliar with the app struggle to guide users
  • Occasional voice quality issues -- Some report concerns after updates

Professional Community Perspective

The professional AAC community largely considers Proloquo2Go the "gold standard", but with important caveats. SLPs value the deep customization and research-backed vocabulary, but consistently note that professional expertise is essential for successful implementation.

Some SLPs report being unable to guide users past basic setup due to their own unfamiliarity with the software, highlighting a training gap in the professional community. This suggests that the app's complexity can be a barrier not just for families, but for the professionals supporting them.

Published research supports the app's effectiveness: a PubMed study titled "Proloquo2Go Enhances Classroom Performance in Children With Autism Spectrum Disorder" validates its clinical utility.

🎯 The Verdict

Proloquo2Go occupies a unique position in the AAC landscape: it is simultaneously the most powerful and the most complex symbol-based communication app available.

For users who need its accessibility depth, bilingual capabilities, or extensive customization, there is simply no equivalent. For first-time AAC users without professional guidance, the complexity can be a genuine barrier to success.

The app's 15-year track record, 4.77-star rating, and ongoing development demonstrate that AssistiveWare takes its commitment to this product seriously. The recent addition of phone call support and neural voices shows that Proloquo2Go is not just being maintained -- it is being actively improved.

Whether Proloquo2Go is the right choice depends on the individual user's needs, their support team's expertise, and their device preferences. But for the hundreds of thousands of people who rely on it daily, Proloquo2Go is not just an app -- it is their voice.
