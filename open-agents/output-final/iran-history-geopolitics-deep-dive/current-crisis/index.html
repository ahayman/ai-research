I

The Spark: October 7, 2023

October 7, 2023
Hamas Attacks Israel
Hamas launched the deadliest attack on Israeli soil in history. Beginning at 6:30 AM, at least 4,300 rockets were fired while approximately 6,000 militants breached the Gaza-Israel barrier at 119 locations. Fighters attacked military bases and massacred civilians in 21 communities, including the Nova music festival. More than 1,200 Israelis and foreign nationals were killed; 251 people were taken hostage.

◆ Pivotal Decision: Iran's Role

The U.S. ODNI assessed in February 2024 that "Iranian leaders did not orchestrate nor had foreknowledge of" the attack. Iran publicly praised the assault but intelligence consensus is that Hamas planned independently, though using capabilities partly developed with Iranian support over decades.

Who Decided
Hamas leadership (Sinwar, Deif)
Why
Break the status quo; derail Israel-Saudi normalization; force the Palestinian issue back to center stage
What Changed
Set off a chain of escalation that would ultimately destroy Iran's entire proxy architecture
Unverified Claims
Iran's exact foreknowledge remains debated; some analysts believe Iran had general awareness without operational details

Israel's Response: The Gaza Campaign

The IDF launched one of the most destructive urban military operations in modern history. By late 2025: 70,000-75,000+ Palestinians killed (academic estimates suggest 100,000+ including those under rubble), 170,000+ injured, 93% of school buildings (526 of 564) damaged, and over 500,000 Gazans facing starvation — described by the IPC as the "highest number facing catastrophic hunger" ever recorded.

Sources: Gaza Health Ministry; Brown University Costs of War Project; IPC
II

The Axis of Resistance Under Siege (2023-2024)

Israel systematically dismantled Iran's proxy network across the region, eliminating leaders, destroying infrastructure, and severing supply lines. Each blow left Iran more exposed and more desperate.

Hezbollah and the Northern Front

From October 8, 2023, Hezbollah launched rockets into northern Israel in solidarity with Hamas, displacing tens of thousands of Israeli civilians.

  • Pager Attacks (Sep 17-18, 2024): Israel's Mossad detonated thousands of booby-trapped pagers and walkie-talkies sold to Hezbollah via a shell company. Wave 1 killed 12+ and wounded 2,750+; Wave 2 killed 30+ and injured 750+. Devastated Hezbollah's communications.
  • Nasrallah Assassinated (Sep 27, 2024): IAF dropped 80+ bombs on an underground HQ in Beirut's Dahieh suburb, killing Secretary-General Hassan Nasrallah, Hezbollah's leader since 1992, and 32+ others.
  • Ground Invasion (Oct 2024): Israel invaded southern Lebanon. A US-brokered ceasefire came in November 2024 but proved fragile. 1.2 million Lebanese displaced (over a quarter of the population).
Houthi Red Sea Campaign

Beginning October 19, 2023, Yemen's Houthis launched over 520 attacks, targeting 176+ ships and conducting 155 strikes on Israeli territory. They seized the Galaxy Leader cargo ship on November 19. Over 2,000 ships diverted from the Red Sea by March 2024.

The US responded with Operation Prosperity Guardian (December 2023) and Operation Poseidon Archer (January 2024, 150+ precision munitions on 60+ Houthi targets). The Houthis paused attacks after the October 2025 Gaza peace plan but threatened resumption in late February 2026.

Israeli Strikes on Iranian Assets in Syria
  • Dec 25, 2023: Assassination of IRGC General Razi Mousavi in Damascus
  • Jan 20, 2024: IRGC Quds Force intelligence officer Omidzadeh killed
  • Apr 1, 2024: Israel struck the Iranian consulate in Damascus, killing IRGC Quds Force commander Zahedi and 15 others. This attack on a diplomatic facility crossed a major red line for Tehran and triggered Iran's first direct retaliation.

◆ Pivotal Decision: The Fall of Assad (December 8, 2024)

The Assad regime collapsed after opposition forces led by HTS swept the country. Assad fled to Russia.

Who Decided
Turkish-backed rebels seized the moment; Assad failed to hold
Why It Happened
Hezbollah & Iran severely weakened by Israeli strikes; Russia preoccupied with Ukraine
What Changed
Iran lost its key Mediterranean base and primary supply route to Hezbollah. An estimated $50B in Iranian investment evaporated.
Consequence
Iran's proxy shield was now shattered — stripped of Hamas leadership, Hezbollah decapitated, Syria lost, Houthis under pressure
III

Iran & Israel: Direct Confrontation (2024)

With its proxies under siege, Iran crossed a historic threshold: direct military strikes on Israel. Each exchange demonstrated the effectiveness of allied defense and exposed Iran's vulnerability, ultimately emboldening further Israeli action.

Operation True Promise I — April 13, 2024

Iran's first-ever direct attack on Israel. The IRGC, with Hezbollah, Iraqi militias, and Houthis, launched 170 drones, 30 cruise missiles, and 120 ballistic missiles — the largest drone attack ever conducted by any country. A defense coalition (US, UK, France, Jordan, Israel) destroyed approximately 99% of incoming weapons. Minimal damage, no fatalities.

Consequence: Demonstrated Iran's willingness to strike directly but also proved the effectiveness of allied missile defense, inadvertently emboldening Israel.

◆ Pivotal Decision: Assassinating Haniyeh in Tehran

What Happened
July 31, 2024: Israel killed Hamas political leader Ismail Haniyeh in an IRGC guesthouse in Tehran, hours after he attended President Pezeshkian's inauguration
Who Decided
Israeli government / Mossad; publicly acknowledged December 2024
Why
Eliminate Hamas leadership; demonstrate ability to reach Iran's heartland; humiliate Tehran's security apparatus
What Changed
A profound humiliation for Iran. Triggered True Promise II and accelerated the cycle of direct confrontation.

Operation True Promise II — October 1, 2024

Iran launched approximately 200 ballistic missiles at Israel in two waves, citing the assassinations of Haniyeh, Nasrallah, and IRGC General Nilforoushan. This was a more aggressive strike than April, relying on faster ballistic missiles rather than drones. Most were intercepted, though some struck targets.

◆ Pivotal Decision: Israel's October 26 Retaliation

What Happened
Israel struck Iranian missile factories (Khojir, Parchin), Shahrud Space Center, and critically, nearly all of Iran's Russian-supplied S-300 air defense systems
Who Decided
Netanyahu / Israeli security cabinet; Biden urged "proportional" response and opposed nuclear/oil targets
Why
Degrade Iran's missile capability and, critically, strip its air defenses for future operations
What Changed
Destruction of S-300 batteries left Iran largely defenseless against aerial attack. This proved decisive when the 2025 and 2026 wars came.
IV

The Diplomatic Interlude (January - October 2025)

Gaza Ceasefire — January 15, 2025

Qatar mediated a ceasefire between Israel and Hamas. The first phase included release of 33 of 98 remaining hostages. A total of 168 hostages were eventually returned alive to Israel across all releases.

The Gaza Peace Plan — October 2025

Announced by Trump and Netanyahu on September 29, signed October 9. Key provisions: immediate ceasefire, return of all hostages (final 20 released October 13), demilitarization, international stabilization force, transitional governance, reconstruction, and a conditional pathway to Palestinian statehood. Endorsed by UN Security Council on November 17.

While the Gaza peace plan brought a measure of stability to one front, it freed Israel and the US to focus squarely on the Iranian nuclear threat.
V

The Twelve-Day War (June 13-24, 2025)

◆ Pivotal Decision: Israel Strikes Iran

On June 12, the IAEA declared Iran non-compliant with NPT safeguards for the first time since 2005, confirming enough 60% enriched uranium for nine nuclear warheads. The next morning, Israel struck.

Who Decided
Israeli government, with at least tacit US coordination
Why
IAEA non-compliance finding provided legal/political cover; closing nuclear window; Iran's air defenses already destroyed
Scale
200+ fighter jets, 5 waves, 330+ munitions, ~100 targets across 27 provinces
What Changed
Severely damaged Natanz, Fordow, Isfahan. US entered combat June 22 with bunker-busters. Established the precedent of direct US strikes on Iran.

Iranian Retaliation

Iran and the Houthis launched simultaneous ballistic missile barrages at Israeli cities. Missiles struck buildings in Bat Yam (61 buildings damaged, 9 killed, ~200 injured), Rehovot, Kiryat Ekron (shopping mall hit), and Tel Aviv. Iran fired hundreds of missiles at Israeli population centers, military, energy, and government sites, including at least one hospital.

Ceasefire — June 24, 2025

Mediated by US and Qatar. Iran: at least 610 killed. Israel: 28 killed. The Economist assessed Israel achieved military dominance potentially surpassing the aftermath of the 1967 Six-Day War. IAEA confirmed in March 2026 that Natanz was rendered inaccessible though not fully destroyed.

VI

The Road to War (July 2025 - February 2026)

The Twelve-Day War's aftermath set up the conditions for the current conflict: Iran accelerated nuclear development, sanctions tightened, the economy cratered, protests erupted, and diplomacy failed.

Nuclear Escalation

In October 2025, Khamenei reportedly authorized development of compact nuclear warheads for ballistic missiles — reversing years of restraint. While enrichment beyond 60% was reportedly not authorized, rumors persisted of covert enrichment at undeclared sites. US intelligence assessed breakout time at "almost zero."

Sources: ISPI; Euronews; Iran International

Economic Collapse & Sanctions

Snapback sanctions reimposed September-October 2025, layered atop Trump's "maximum pressure" campaign (February 4, 2025). The rial fell to ~145,000 tomans/USD. Inflation hit 42%. GDP contracted from ~$600B (2010) to ~$356B (2025). Oil exports sustained only by Chinese purchases (~1.38M bpd).

The December Protests

December 28, 2025: the largest protests since the 1979 Revolution erupted across 100+ cities. The government's crackdown killed thousands in what became the largest massacres in modern Iranian history. Protest slogans increasingly reflected monarchist sentiments; exiled Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi intensified political efforts.

◆ Pivotal Decision: Diplomacy's Final Failure

Four rounds of US-Iran nuclear negotiations over ten months, mediated primarily by Oman.

Round 1 (Apr 12, 2025)
Oman; US envoy Witkoff, Iranian FM Araghchi
Round 2 (Apr 19, 2025)
Rome; Omani-mediated
Round 3 (Feb 6, 2026)
Muscat, Oman; indirect talks
Round 4 (Feb 26, 2026)
Geneva; no agreement. The gap was unbridgeable: US demanded halt to all enrichment; Iran insisted enrichment was a sovereign right.
Consequence: Less than 48 hours later, the US and Israel launched the joint strike. Diplomacy's failure removed the last barrier to military action.

Iran-Russia Strategic Partnership

Signed January 17, 2025; entered into force October 2, 2025. A 20-year agreement committing to defense cooperation and intelligence sharing. Critically, it stated that if either side was subjected to aggression, the other would "not provide assistance to the aggressor." This fell short of a mutual defense pact — and as events would show, Russia provided neither military assistance nor meaningful civilian support when war came.

VII

The 2026 Iran War (February 28 - Present)

◆ Pivotal Decision: Operations Epic Fury & Roaring Lion

On February 28, 2026, less than 48 hours after nuclear talks collapsed, the US and Israel launched a massive joint operation. In the first 12 hours, nearly 900 strikes hit over 30 sites across Iran.

Who Decided
Trump administration and Netanyahu government; launched without congressional authorization under War Powers Resolution notification
Why (US Stated)
"Protect US forces, protect the homeland, ensure free flow of maritime commerce through Hormuz, and collective self-defense of allies including Israel"
Targets
Ballistic missile sites, air defense, C2 centers, government buildings, maritime mining capabilities, military leadership including Khamenei
What Changed
Khamenei assassinated (confirmed March 1) — first assassination of a sitting head of state by foreign military strike in modern history. Iran's military severely degraded.

Iran's Response: Operation True Promise IV

Iran's most extensive retaliatory campaign. By March 5: over 500 ballistic and naval missiles and nearly 2,000 drones launched. Approximately 40% targeted Israel, 60% targeted US positions across the region including:

  • US Fifth Fleet HQ in Bahrain
  • American bases in Qatar, UAE, Kuwait, and Iraq
  • Israeli military and civilian targets
  • Azerbaijan (accused of enabling Israeli operations)

By March 8, the IRGC announced a 27th wave of True Promise IV strikes. However, US military assessments noted missile launches dropped 86% and drone strikes 73% since Day 1 — indicating severe degradation of Iran's offensive capabilities.

Regional Escalation: Hezbollah Re-enters the War

On March 2, Hezbollah broke the November 2024 ceasefire, launching projectiles at a missile defense site south of Haifa. Israel responded by bombing Beirut at 3 AM and issuing evacuation orders to 50 villages. By March 8: at least 217 killed in Lebanon, 2 Israeli soldiers killed. Over 64,000 remained internally displaced from the previous round.

Regional Escalation: Iraqi Militias Attack US Forces

Iranian-aligned Popular Mobilisation Forces and the Islamic Resistance in Iraq launched dozens of attacks on US bases, including Erbil. The US embassy in Baghdad was attacked on March 7. Iraq's government opposed being drawn into the conflict but was powerless to stop Iran-backed groups.

Regional Escalation: Gulf States Under Fire

Iranian missiles and drones struck targets in Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, the UAE, and Oman — all hosting US military facilities. At least 2 killed in Saudi Arabia. On March 7, President Pezeshkian apologized to neighboring countries and pledged to halt attacks on regional states, signaling recognition that attacking Gulf states was strategically counterproductive.

The Strait of Hormuz Crisis

Iran targeted the Strait of Hormuz (through which ~20% of global oil transits). Tanker traffic dropped ~70% and soon neared zero. Iran threatened to fire on passing ships. Economic impacts:

  • Oil prices surged 25%+; Brent crude jumped from ~$70 to $80+
  • War-risk insurance premiums: ~$250,000 per large tanker transit
  • US gasoline rose $0.43/week to $3.41/gallon national average
Congressional and Domestic Debate

The strikes were launched without congressional authorization and proved deeply divisive:

  • Republican support: Senate Majority Leader Thune commended Trump; most Republicans rallied behind the president
  • Democratic opposition: Senate Minority Leader Schumer called for Congress to reassert war powers. Constitutional scholars called the strikes unconstitutional.
  • Bipartisan dissent: Rep. Massie (R) and Rep. Khanna (D) jointly introduced a resolution to restrict presidential war-making. Sen. Kaine (D) and Sen. Paul (R) introduced a Senate counterpart.
  • Both votes failed: Senate 47-53 on procedural vote; House rejected 219-212

Trump indicated the campaign may last "four to six weeks."

Iran's Leadership Crisis

The Succession Question

Following Khamenei's assassination on February 28, an Interim Leadership Council was established under Article 111 of the constitution:

  • Alireza Arafi — Guardian Council
  • Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i — Chief Justice
  • Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf — Speaker of Parliament
  • Masoud Pezeshkian — President

Potential successors for Supreme Leader: Mojtaba Khamenei (the late leader's son, considered most likely), Hassan Khomeini (grandson of the Revolution's founder), and several senior clerics. Pezeshkian has emerged as the primary public voice, rejecting Trump's demand for "unconditional surrender."

How Each Event Led to the Next

The path from October 7 to the present war was neither inevitable nor easily preventable. Five interlocking dynamics drove escalation at each stage.

1. The Destruction of Iran's Proxy Architecture

Israel systematically dismantled the "Axis of Resistance": killing Hamas and Hezbollah leaders, devastating their military capabilities, contributing to Assad's fall, and degrading Houthi operations. This left Iran exposed, stripped of its ability to project power through proxies, and forced into increasingly direct confrontation with Israel and the US.

2. The Failure of Deterrence

Iran's April and October 2024 direct attacks on Israel were intended to re-establish deterrence. They achieved the opposite: demonstrating the effectiveness of allied missile defense (99% interception) and exposing the limitations of Iran's offensive capabilities. Each Iranian strike emboldened Israel to escalate further, culminating in the destruction of Iran's S-300 air defenses in October 2024 — which made the Twelve-Day War and the current conflict possible.

3. The Nuclear Threshold

Iran's steady march toward weapons capability — enriching to 60%, accumulating material for nine warheads, and reportedly authorizing warhead development — created a closing window that both Israel and the US perceived as requiring military action before it shut entirely. The IAEA's non-compliance finding in June 2025 provided the trigger for the Twelve-Day War; the reported October 2025 warhead authorization escalated urgency further.

4. Diplomatic Collapse

Four rounds of nuclear negotiations over ten months failed to bridge an unbridgeable gap: the US demanded Iran halt enrichment entirely, while Tehran insisted enrichment was its sovereign right under international law. The failure of the final round on February 26, 2026 removed the last remaining barrier to military action. Forty-eight hours later, the bombs fell.

5. Iran's Internal Fragility

The regime's simultaneous crises — economic collapse (42% inflation, GDP halved since 2010), the largest protests since 1979, military defeats, and loss of allies — created a perceived vulnerability that Israel and the US moved to exploit. The December 2025 protests and brutal crackdown suggested the regime might be near a breaking point. Ironically, the war may rally nationalist sentiment even among Iranians who oppose the regime.

Open Questions

  • Duration: Will the "four to six week" timeline hold, or does this become an extended campaign?
  • Nuclear status: Have Iran's nuclear capabilities been fully neutralized, or do undeclared covert facilities exist?
  • Succession: Who becomes the next Supreme Leader? Will the leadership vacuum lead to factional infighting or unity?
  • Regime stability: Does the war rally nationalist sentiment around the regime, or does it accelerate collapse alongside ongoing protests?
  • Regional spread: Will further Hezbollah, Iraqi militia, or Houthi escalation draw additional parties into the conflict?
