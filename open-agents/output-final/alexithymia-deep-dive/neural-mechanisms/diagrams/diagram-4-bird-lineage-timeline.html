The Bird/Murphy/Brewer Interoception Lineage

The asymmetric walk-back. The Bird group’s alexithymia interoception claim has been STRENGTHENED across multiple papers. The autism-specific claim was WALKED BACK. The Nicholson 2018 paper is from an INDEPENDENT group at City University London / UCL, not from the Bird lab. The Aaron 2020 quadratic-relationship paper is by Aaron, Blain, Snodgress & Park — NOT Trevisan, Mehling & McPartland.

2015

Architectural framework: three-dimensional model

Garfinkel, Seth, Barrett, Suzuki & Critchley

"Knowing your own heart." Three dissociable dimensions of interoception: accuracy, sensibility, awareness. Independent of the Bird group. Becomes the architectural standard for distinguishing detection-deficit from parsing-deficit profiles.

Biological Psychology 104:65–74
2016a

Foundation: Alexithymia as general interoception deficit

Brewer, Cook & Bird

"Alexithymia: a general deficit of interoception." N=208 participants. Self-reported alexithymia associated with poor non-affective interoception. Interpretation: alexithymia involves a general interoception failure, not just an emotional one. Methodological caveat: self-report measures only.

R Soc Open Sci · PMC5098957
2016b

The autism-specific walk-back

Shah, Hall, Catmur & Bird

"Alexithymia, NOT autism, is associated with impaired interoception." Two heartbeat-detection experiments across autistic and non-autistic samples. Interoceptive-accuracy variance loads on alexithymia, not autism diagnosis. This is the actual walk-back — and it is autism-specific.

Cortex 81:215–220 · PubMed 27253723
2018a

Strengthening the multidomain claim

Murphy, Brewer, Catmur & Bird

"Multidomain, multidimensional failure of interoception." Three experiments: respiration, muscular effort, taste sensitivity. High-alexithymia individuals show reduced interoceptive accuracy AND reduced use of cues across all three domains, after controlling for autism, depression, anxiety. This is a strengthening, not a walk-back.

J Exp Psychol Gen 147(3):398–408 · PMC5824617
2018b

HCT methodological clarification

Murphy, Brewer, Hobson, Catmur & Bird

HCT-with-confound-control paper. N=287. Bivariate analyses showed no relationship; after controlling for ten covariates (age, gender, BMI, knowledge of HR, HRV, depression, etc.) the predicted negative alexithymia–HCT relationship emerged. Methodological clarification, not substantive retreat.

Biol Psychol 136:189–197 · PubMed 29803614
2018c

INDEPENDENT challenge — NOT a Bird-lab paper

Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino & Gaigg

"Interoceptive impairments do not lie at the heart of autism or alexithymia." From City University London / UCL, NOT the Bird lab. Multiple experiments using cardiac HCT with Bayesian analyses supporting the null hypothesis. This is a third-party challenge, not a Bird-internal walk-back.

J Abnormal Psychol 127(6) · PMC6089261
2019

Multiroute synthesis

Hobson, Brewer, Catmur & Bird

"The Role of Language in Alexithymia: Moving Towards a Multiroute Model." Most current Bird-group synthesis. Alexithymia = common downstream phenotype of multiple upstream impairments. Interoception is ONE route. Language is another. Conceptualisation possibly a third. Different alexithymics may sit at different upstream loci.

Emotion Review 11(3):247–261 · SAGE 10.1177/1754073919838528
2020

The parsing-deficit substrate (separate group)

Aaron, Blain, Snodgress & Park

"Quadratic relationship between alexithymia and interoceptive accuracy." Frontiers in Psychiatry 11:132. PMC7076086. NOT Trevisan, Mehling & McPartland — common misattribution. U-shaped curve: both LOW and HIGH IA can co-occur with high alexithymia. Architectural support for the parsing-deficit reading: deficit may be at the labelling/interpretation layer rather than detection.

Front Psychiatry 11:132 · PMC7076086
2021

Authoritative current synthesis

Brewer, Murphy & Bird

"Atypical interoception as a common risk factor for psychopathology: A review." Direct quote: “many individuals with alexithymia experience a generalised interoceptive impairment, rather than one specific to emotional processing.” Impairment hypothesised at multiple representational levels — conscious detection, recognition, labelling — with individuals differing in which level is affected.

Neurosci Biobehav Rev 130:470–508 · PMC8522807
Bottom line. The Bird group’s 2025 position is that alexithymia involves a multidomain, multidimensional interoception failure (affirmed; not walked back). The cardiac heartbeat-counting task is methodologically problematic. Autism per se is not associated with interoception deficits — variance loads on co-occurring alexithymia (this autism-specific claim IS walked back). The deficit may sit at multiple representational levels and different alexithymics may sit at different levels. Language and conceptualisation is a parallel route. The Nicholson 2018 paper is a live third-party challenge, not a Bird-internal retraction. The Aaron 2020 quadratic finding is the parsing-deficit substrate — from a separate group, with corrected attribution.
