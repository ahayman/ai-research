The Strongest Single Causal Claim — Hogeveen 2016

Hogeveen, Bird, Chau, Krueger & Grafman (2016). Acquired alexithymia following damage to the anterior insula. Neuropsychologia 82:142–148. PMC4752907.

Sample

200 brain-injured veterans + 51 controls

Vietnam War veterans with focal penetrating brain injuries from the Phase III Vietnam Head Injury Study (VHIS). 51 non-injured veteran controls matched on demographics. Pre-injury TAS-20 not directly available, but the controlled comparison establishes that the alexithymia is post-injury.

Method

Voxel-based lesion-symptom mapping

Quantified lesion location and volume via structural MRI; regressed post-injury TAS-20 scores against lesion location, volume, and identity of damaged region. Specificity contrasts: covaried for adjacent ACC damage and for total lesion volume.

Results

Anterior-insula damage produces dose-dependent acquired alexithymia

Result 1
Anterior insula damage predicts post-injury TAS-20 elevation. Effect specific to AI region within the lesion-symptom map.
Result 2
Approximately 15% of brain-injury patients in the sample meet alexithymia criteria post-injury — an elevated rate over the ~10% general-population baseline.
Result 3 — The dose-response
Loss of more than 15% of anterior-insula volume reliably produces acquired alexithymia in adults previously non-alexithymic.
Result 4 — The specificity claim
The dose-response holds after controlling for ACC damage and total lesion volume. Effect is specific to AI rather than to a generic damaged-brain pattern.

Interpretation

Strongest single causal claim in alexithymia neuroscience. Lesion-symptom mapping bypasses the correlation/causation problem of fMRI; the specificity contrast rules out the generic damaged-brain confound. This is the cleanest evidence the field has that anterior insula is causally involved in producing alexithymia.

Caveats

  • Speaks to acquired/organic alexithymia (Messina 2014's third category)
  • Demonstrates AI is sufficient to produce the phenotype
  • Does NOT prove primary developmental alexithymia originates from the same locus
  • The "15%" threshold is a discretisation of a continuous dose-response, not a magic cutoff
  • Single high-quality study; not yet directly replicated

For the user

This is the cleanest evidence that anterior insula is causally involved in producing alexithymia. It does NOT mean YOUR alexithymia is anterior-insula-driven. The Hobson 2019 multiroute model acknowledges other upstream causes — language, conceptualisation, attention, predictive coding, symbolic elaboration. The lesion finding establishes that one route exists, not that it's the only route or even the route most active in any given individual.
