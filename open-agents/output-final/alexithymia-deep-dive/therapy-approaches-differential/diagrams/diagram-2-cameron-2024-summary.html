Approximate proportions illustrating CBT dominance. The exact taxonomy depends on which third-wave protocols are categorized as "CBT-derivatives." Any reading of this evidence base is a reading of largely-CBT data.
Effect-size range: 0.41 to 13.25.
Important caveat: the upper end of the range is one trial outlier with very small SD. The median effect size is much more modest. Reading the range without reading the distribution gives a misleading impression of treatment magnitude.

The caveats that matter

1. Subtype-blind: Almost no trial stratifies outcomes by TAS-20 facet pattern, by Bermond Type, by parsing/detection/hypervigilance subtype, or by interoceptive accuracy quartile.
2. CBT dominance: ~67% of trial arms are CBT-derived. Reading the meta-evidence is reading mostly CBT data, with limited coverage of ACT, EFT, Schema therapy, MBT, somatic modalities for alexithymia outcomes specifically.
3. Alexithymia outcome focus: Many alexithymia-touching trials measure other primary outcomes (BPD symptoms, chronic pain). Cameron 2024 specifically captures trials where alexithymia is a primary or named secondary outcome.
4. Heterogeneity: Populations, doses, comparators, and follow-up windows vary widely. Even "CBT for alexithymia" is not a single thing.
Implication for this page: "Subtype-matched therapy" recommendations (ACT for parsing-deficit, DBT for granularity-building, somatic for trauma-derived) are theoretical extrapolations from related literatures, not findings from head-to-head subtype-stratified RCTs. This caveat must be honored throughout.

Source: Cameron K, Ogrodniczuk JS, Hadjipavlou G. (2024) Psychological Treatments for Alexithymia: A Systematic Review. PMC11673933. Modality proportions are illustrative.
