Bornemann ReSource Project — Submodule × Alexithymia Signal

Within a single 9-month multimodal mindfulness training, three sequential modules produced different effects on alexithymia. The closest published within-mindfulness submodule × alexithymia interaction signal.

Module 1 — Months 1–3
Presence
Body-scan, breathing meditation, attention training, present-moment focus.
Effect on TAS-20:
Reduced alexithymia ↓
REDUCED TAS-20
Module 2 — Months 4–6
Affect
Loving-kindness, dyadic affect-sharing, emotion-related training.
Effect on TAS-20:
Reduced alexithymia ↓
REDUCED TAS-20
Module 3 — Months 7–9
Perspective
Metacognition, perspective-taking on self and others, observing self.
Effect on TAS-20:
No reduction
NO TAS-20 REDUCTION
Additional finding: Greater TAS-20 reductions occurred among participants with higher baseline alexithymia (TAS-20 above 51) than among those with low baseline. This means the modules that worked, worked more for the people who needed them most.
Hedge / what this is and isn't: Single multi-component trial (n < 200 across three time-staggered cohorts). No head-to-head replication. Not stratified by interoceptive subtype. Not stratified by parsing/detection/hypervigilance phenotype. Frame as suggestive supporting signal for the within-mindfulness submodule heterogeneity story — not as definitive evidence for any specific subtype-matched claim.

Source: Bornemann & Singer ReSource project, analyzed in Norman E, Marzano L, Coulson M, Oskis A. (2019) Effects of mindfulness-based interventions on alexithymia: A systematic review. PMC10270453.
