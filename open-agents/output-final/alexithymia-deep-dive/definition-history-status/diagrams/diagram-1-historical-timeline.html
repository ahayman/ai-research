A Half-Century Timeline of Alexithymia

From a clinical observation in a Boston psychosomatic clinic to five competing theoretical frames in 2024.

Foundational Original lineage (Sifneos / Taylor / Bagby) Attention-appraisal (Preece) Constructed emotion (Barrett) Interoception deficit (Bird group) Multiroute synthesis (Hobson)
Etymology.  a- + lexis + thymos  = "no words for feeling" — from Greek ἀ- (not), λέξις (word), θῡμός (disposition / feeling).
1960sSifneos & Nemiah at Beth Israel

Greek-born psychiatrist Peter Sifneos and US psychiatrist John Nemiah work with psychosomatic patients in Boston. They notice patients who can describe events but not feelings — concrete, externally oriented, low fantasy.

1972Term coined

Sifneos publishes Short-Term Psychotherapy and Emotional Crisis (Harvard UP). The term "alexithymia" appears in print for the first time.

1973First instrument: BIQ

Sifneos publishes the Beth Israel Hospital Psychosomatic Questionnaire — observer-rated, 17 items. First operationalised measure of the construct.

1976Heidelberg Conference

11th European Conference on Psychosomatic Research, Sept 14–17, 1976. Nemiah, Freyberger & Sifneos publish "Alexithymia: A View of the Psychosomatic Process" — the first systematic theoretical statement.

1985TAS-26 (first Toronto scale)

Taylor, Ryan & Bagby publish the original 26-item Toronto Alexithymia Scale — the first widely-used self-report instrument. Includes a fantasy/imagination subscale.

1994TAS-20 — fantasy dropped

Bagby, Parker & Taylor publish the revised 20-item TAS. DIF, DDF, EOT only. Fantasy/imagination items removed for psychometric reasons. Becomes the field's dominant measure.

2001BVAQ — recovers affective dimension

Vorst & Bermond publish the Bermond–Vorst Alexithymia Questionnaire. Five-factor structure including emotionalising and fantasizing — explicitly recovers what TAS-20 lost.

2013Alexithymia hypothesis of autism

Bird & Cook in Translational Psychiatry: emotional symptoms attributed to autism are largely driven by co-occurring alexithymia. Establishes alexithymia as a dimensional, trans-diagnostic trait.

2017Preece attention-appraisal model

Preece, Becerra et al. propose a CBT-style reformulation. EOT = attention deficit; DIF/DDF = appraisal deficit. Drops imaginal component on theoretical grounds.

2017Barrett constructed emotion

Lisa Feldman Barrett's theory of constructed emotion provides a frame in which alexithymia = impoverished emotion-concept granularity. Implies vocabulary-building intervention.

2018Multidomain interoception failure

Murphy, Brewer, Catmur & Bird in JEP:General: alexithymia tracks interoceptive failures across respiration, muscular effort, taste — not just cardiac, not just emotion-specific.

2018Independent challenge (Nicholson et al.)

An independent group (Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino & Gaigg) reports Bayesian-null cardiac findings — neither autism nor alexithymia tracks heartbeat-detection. Not the Bird group; a third-party challenge.

2019Multiroute model (synthesis)

Hobson, Brewer, Catmur & Bird propose: alexithymia is the common downstream phenotype of multiple upstream impairments — interoception, language, conceptualisation. Different routes, same surface.

2021Bird group review affirms multidomain

Brewer, Murphy & Bird in Neurosci Biobehav Rev: multidomain interoception failure with deficits at multiple representational levels (detection / recognition / labelling).

2024Taylor & Bagby — Defense paper

"Alexithymia: A Defense of the Original Conceptualization" — explicit critique of Preece's CBT reformulation. The two main definitional camps now publicly engaged.
