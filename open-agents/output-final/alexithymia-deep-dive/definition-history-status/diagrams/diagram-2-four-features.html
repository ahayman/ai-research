The Four (or Five) Features of Alexithymia — and What Each Instrument Captures

The original Sifneos/Nemiah construct had four to five elements. The dominant measure (TAS-20) drops one. Newer instruments restore some of what was lost.

DIF

Difficulty Identifying Feelings

Confusion between bodily arousal and discrete emotion. Cannot map "something's off" onto a specific feeling label.

"I am often confused about what emotion I'm feeling."

DDF

Difficulty Describing Feelings

Even when an emotion is identified, words for it are unavailable, vague, or generic ("bad", "off"). Conversation about feeling stalls.

"It is difficult for me to find the right words for my feelings."

EOT

Externally Oriented Thinking

Cognitive style that prefers concrete external detail (events, schedules, technical content) over inner reflection.

"I prefer talking to people about their daily activities rather than their feelings."

FANTASY

Restricted Imaginative / Fantasy Life

Thin daydreams, sparse symbolic content, low spontaneous fantasy. The most psychoanalytically-flavoured of the original criteria. Dropped from the TAS-20.

"I have very few daydreams. I'd rather just look at what's actually in front of me."

BVAQ (Bermond-Vorst) — 5-factor 2001 Yes Yes Yes Yes No
PAQ (Perth) — attention-appraisal 2018 Yes Yes Yes No Yes
Why this matters. The TAS-20 is the field's most-used instrument, but it measures only three of the four original features — and only against negative emotions. A person rich in fantasy life with normal positive-emotion fluency but high DIF/DDF will still score "alexithymic" on the TAS-20. A person with the original five-feature constellation but rich positive-emotion fluency may look mixed on PAQ. The instrument silently selects the construct.
