Alexithymia is a Dimension, Not a Category

Approximate population distribution of TAS-20 scores. The "alexithymic" label rests on a pragmatic cutoff — there is no natural break in the data.

~70-75%Non-alexithymic TAS-20 score below 52. Typical population mean ~45-50.
~15-20%Borderline / moderate TAS-20 score 52-60. Some difficulty with emotion identification or description.
~10%"Alexithymic" TAS-20 score ≥61. The standard pragmatic cutoff. Range across studies: 5-13%.
The pragmatic-cutoff caveat. The TAS-20 ≥61 threshold is a useful clinical and research convention, not a natural break in the population. Recent latent-profile and factor-mixture-modelling work (Schaeuffele 2021; Hogrefe FMM 2025; Frontiers Psychology 2025) supports a severity continuum rather than discrete "alexithymic" vs "non-alexithymic" classes. The more useful self-orientation is "where am I on each axis?" — DIF, DDF, EOT, fantasy/affective — rather than "which side of the cutoff?".
