Externalisation — Proxies for Internal States

When the internal channel is unreliable (parsing-deficit alexithymia), external signals carry information about the emotional state that does not depend on the labelling step. Working with these signals gives the user information that bypasses the limits of their parsing.

The structural argument

For a parsing-deficit profile, the internal signal arrives but the labelling step fails. Some external signals — heart rate, behaviour, posture, avoidance pattern — carry information about the emotional state that is not dependent on the labelling step. Working with these signals lets the user read themselves from outside in, when reading from inside out is unreliable.

This is the cleanest example of the external-to-internal scaffold. Don't try to read the inside; produce an outside that carries the inside's information; read the outside.

1. Heart rate

OBJECTIVE
  • Smartwatch tracking
  • Resting HR vs. context-elevated HR
  • Patterns over weeks reveal triggers
  • HRV / parasympathetic tone (some watches)

2. Sleep patterns

OBJECTIVE
  • Sleep onset / continuity
  • Wake count / total time
  • REM proportion (some devices)
  • Often reflects what the conscious mind cannot

3. Behaviour

OBSERVABLE
  • What did I do today?
  • Did I cancel the social plan?
  • Did I work past midnight?
  • Did I avoid the call?
  • Behaviour is observable when feelings are not.

4. Body posture & tension

OBSERVABLE
  • Shoulders, jaw, breathing depth
  • More easily noticed by mirror, partner, photo
  • Than felt directly from inside
  • Cumulative tension over a day

5. Avoidance patterns

OBSERVABLE
  • What tasks have I been avoiding?
  • Which people?
  • Avoidance is one of the most reliable markers of unprocessed affective load.

6. Reactivity patterns

OBSERVABLE
  • What triggered an outsized response?
  • In retrospect, what was that response about?
  • Disproportion as data: where the affect that wasn't named earlier finally surfaced.

Putting it together — the four-column journal entry

  • Situation: what happened
  • Behaviour: what I did, what I avoided
  • Body-state: what was tight, heavy, hot, restless, shaky
  • Best-guess label: an approximate emotion-word, or "I don't know"

All four columns serve as proxy data for internal emotional state. Over weeks, the patterns become a map of the emotional life the user could not see from inside.
