The Somatization Pathway — How Trauma Lands in the Body

When affect cannot be cognitively encoded, where does it go? The original Sifneos / Nemiah framing: alexithymia routes affect into autonomic and somatic channels. Van der Kolk's "the body keeps the score" — operationalised as a default routing pathway when the cognitive-emotional encoding pathway fails.

Trauma exposure / chronic developmental adversity

Acute trauma or sustained developmental adversity. The system is loaded with affective material that needs encoding.

Cognitive-emotional encoding pathway fails (alexithymia)

The conceptual/labelling/symbolic-elaboration system that would normally convert bodily afferents into nameable affect is impaired. The signal is generated but cannot be metabolized into language or emotion concept.

Default routing — autonomic / somatic / motor channels

Limited emotional awareness leads to prolonged amplified physiological arousal and neurovegetative reactivity, with autonomic dysregulation as the proximal substrate. Affect routes through the body when it cannot route through language.

PMC3563604 alexithymic brain review; PLOS One DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0203212 (alexithymia → more intense somatic activation perception)

4

Misattribution of autonomic symptoms to physical illness

Patients misattribute autonomic symptoms of anxiety (tremor, paresthesias, paralysis, gastric distress, cardiovascular sensations) to physical illness because the cognitive-emotional layer of identification is missing. The body's signal becomes a medical complaint rather than an affective experience.

Catastrophic appraisal + loud signal without label

Higher arousal-emotion endorsement, higher catastrophic appraisal. The body's signal is loud (high interoceptive sensibility) but cannot be metabolized into nameable affect, so it gets read as catastrophic medical or threat content.

Where the unmetabolized affect lands clinically

FND / PNES — Functional Neurological Disorder, Psychogenic Non-Epileptic Seizures. High alexithymia comorbidity; develops on grounds of co-occurring trauma, autism, BPD, PTSD vulnerabilities (PMC11608540).
Trauma-subtype FND — Patients with FND + probable PTSD show higher symptom severity and lower physical health; alexithymia partially accounts for the relationship (PMC9706184).
Somatic symptom disorder — Distress expressed primarily through bodily complaints. Foundational alexithymia association from psychiatric outpatients onward.
Chronic pain — Alexithymia → somatization with sequential mediators (Frontiers 2020 mediation model). Chronic pain populations show ~37%+ alexithymia comorbidity.
Combat veteran somatic morbidity — Somatization pathway operative in chronic war-trauma populations (PMC3610587). Connection between alexithymia and somatic morbidity in combat PTSD.
Cardiovascular / immune / inflammatory — 20-year cohort: CVD death risk increased 1.2% per 1-point TAS-26 increase (Finnish men). Decreased IL-2/IL-4 production longitudinally in alexithymic women.

Van der Kolk operationalised

Van der Kolk's "the body keeps the score" framing — that trauma is encoded in the body when it cannot be encoded in language — is, on this reading, exactly the predicted output of an alexithymic trauma-processing system. The somatic encoding is not mystical; it is the default routing pathway when the cognitive-emotional encoding pathway fails. Alexithymia is the operational substrate for "the body keeps the score."

This reading provides a mechanistic account for why trauma + alexithymia disproportionately produces the somatic-symptom / FND / chronic-pain phenotype. The cognitive-emotional encoding pathway has failed; the affect must go somewhere; the body is the next channel.

Honest evidence label. Foundational + replicated cross-sectional FND/SSD/CPS findings. The pathway specificity claim ("alexithymia as the bridge between trauma and somatization") is theoretically compelling and partially supported by mediation analyses, but the directional claim "alexithymia causes the somatic landing" is not as cleanly tested as "alexithymia is associated with both."

Clinically actionable. Identifying alexithymic trauma-survivor patients early may allow somatically-focused interventions (with appropriate trauma-sensitive scaffolding) to short-circuit the FND/SSD/CPS pathway before it consolidates. This is one of the most actionable parts of the directional claim — flagged for the differential-diagnosis literature.
