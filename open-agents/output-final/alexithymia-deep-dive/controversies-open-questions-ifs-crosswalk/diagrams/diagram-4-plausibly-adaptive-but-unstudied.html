"Plausibly Adaptive but Not Yet Studied" Niches

Domains where alexithymia might confer selective fit, but where peer-reviewed empirical evidence is essentially absent. These should be held as plausible-but-speculative, NOT as established findings.

All four niches below are theoretical extrapolations from adjacent literatures.
None has been directly tested in published peer-reviewed work.
Speculative

Engineering, CS, STEM, Mathematics careers

Theoretical basis

High autistic-trait prevalence in these fields is documented. High autistic-trait → high alexithymia rate is documented (~50% co-occurrence). The two-step inference is plausible but is a chain of two correlations rather than direct evidence.

What's missing

No direct evidence that alexithymia specifically predicts STEM career fit, performance, or satisfaction. Medical-students literature shows elevated alexithymia (~26.9%) but this is partly stress consequence and partly possibly self-selection — direction has not been established empirically.

Speculative

Programming, scientific research, structured analytical work

Theoretical basis

Alexithymia's documented decision-making profile (preserved under explicit risk; emotional-attention bypass; reduced framing bias) has surface fit to structured analytical work where emotional intuition would be noise rather than signal.

What's missing

No direct alexithymia-task-fit data published. No occupational-cohort studies. No performance-on-the-job correlations.

Speculative

Long-term meditation/contemplative practice

Theoretical basis

Theravada and Zen traditions explicitly cultivate decoupling from automatic emotional reactivity. Functional output overlaps with alexithymia in some respects (low reactivity to affective stimuli, sustained equanimity, reduced craving/aversion).

What's missing

Trained equanimity and trait alexithymia are NOT the same thing — equanimity involves chosen non-attachment to fluently-experienced affect, while alexithymia involves limited fluency in the first place. The functional similarity is real; the underlying mechanism is different.

Speculative

Strategic / competitive contexts under emotional pressure

Theoretical basis

Poker, negotiation, courtroom advocacy, certain leadership contexts — all reward emotional bypass under pressure. Alexithymia's emotional-Stroop bypass and attenuated anticipatory affect may map onto demands here.

What's missing

No direct studies in these populations. No performance-or-outcome data correlating alexithymia with strategic-competitive fit. The mapping is at the level of plausible mechanism, not empirical demonstration.

How to hold these niches. They are plausible. Their plausibility comes from real mechanisms documented in the more-validated adaptive-function findings (decision-making asymmetry, emotional bypass, framing-effect resistance, attenuated anticipatory affect). But plausibility from mechanism is not evidence of selection. The user should hold these as "possibly true, not yet shown" — they may inform self-understanding without licensing strong claims.
