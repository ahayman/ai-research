The Five Major Theoretical Frames for Alexithymia

Each frame carves the construct at a different joint and recommends a different therapy. The 2019 Hobson multiroute synthesis is the cleanest accommodation — alexithymia as a common downstream phenotype of multiple upstream impairments.

Lineage 1 (1972–present)

Sifneos / Nemiah / Taylor / Bagby — Original conceptualisation

Sifneos 1972; Nemiah & Sifneos 1976; Taylor & Bagby 2024 (PMC11555664)

Core claim

Personality-level deficit in the capacity to symbolise emotional experience. Includes poverty of subjective emotional life itself.

Operationalisation

TAS-20 (DIF / DDF / EOT, three of the original four features). Imaginative life dropped because it failed to factor-load.

Therapy implication

Psychodynamic, mentalisation-based, emotion-focused — slow depth-symbolic work targeting symbolic capacity itself.

Lineage 2 (2017+)

Preece — Attention-appraisal model

Preece, Becerra, Robinson, Dandy & Allan 2017

Core claim

Two-stage failure of emotion regulation. Either the person fails to attend to emotional state, or attends but fails to appraise (interpret/label) it.

Operationalisation

PAQ (Perth Alexithymia Questionnaire). Covers both positive and negative emotions.

Therapy implication

CBT-tractable. Attention-redirection training, granularity-building, labelling-skill practice.

Lineage 3 (2016–present)

Bird / Murphy / Brewer — Multidomain interoception failure

Brewer, Cook & Bird 2016; Murphy et al. 2018; Brewer, Murphy & Bird 2021 (PMC8522807)

Core claim

Multidomain interoception failure. Affects cardiac, respiratory, muscular, taste — not just affective. Deficit may sit at detection, recognition, or labelling level (varies by individual).

Independent challenge

Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino & Gaigg 2018 (PMC6089261) — Bayesian null result on cardiac HCT. Bird response: HCT is methodologically problematic.

Therapy implication

Theoretically motivates interoception training — but practical relevance varies by which level the deficit sits at.

Lineage 4 (2017+)

Barrett — Theory of constructed emotion

Barrett 2017 (PMC5390700)

Core claim

Emotions are constructed by the brain on the fly using sensory input + concept knowledge + context. Alexithymia = impoverished emotion-concept system.

Operationalisation

Emotional granularity measures (precision of emotional differentiation in language).

Therapy implication

Granularity-building. Build concept inventory; the brain's emotion-construction process improves with richer vocabulary.

Synthesis (2019)

Hobson, Brewer, Catmur & Bird — Multiroute model

Hobson et al. 2019 (Emotion Review SAGE 10.1177/1754073919838528)

Core claim

Alexithymia is a common downstream phenotype of multiple possible upstream impairments. Interoception failure is one route. Language/labelling impairment is another. Emotion-concept failure is a third. Different alexithymics may arrive at the same surface phenotype via different upstream paths.

Why this matters

Theoretically generous — accommodates Bird's interoception evidence, Barrett's constructed-emotion evidence, Preece's attention-appraisal evidence, and Garfinkel's three-dimensional model, all as routes rather than as competing claims to "the truth about alexithymia."

Therapy implication

Match the intervention to which route is impaired in the individual: interoception-route → body-aware work (carefully); language-route → granularity / labelling; concept-route → Barrett-style scaffolding.

The user's takeaway. No single frame is "right." The Sifneos/Bagby tradition treats alexithymia as a deeper personality-level deficit; Preece treats it as a CBT-tractable two-stage failure; Bird treats it as multidomain interoceptive failure; Barrett treats it as impoverished emotion-concept system. The Hobson multiroute model is the cleanest current synthesis: alexithymia is the common surface phenotype, and different upstream impairments converge there. Different individuals likely sit on different routes — which is what makes treatment-matching the central unresolved question.
