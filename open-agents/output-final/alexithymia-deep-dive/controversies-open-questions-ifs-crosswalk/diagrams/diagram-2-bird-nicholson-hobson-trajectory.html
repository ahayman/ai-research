The Bird / Nicholson / Hobson Interoception Trajectory

Corrected attribution. The Bird group did NOT walk back the multidomain interoception failure account. The Nicholson 2018 challenge is from an INDEPENDENT group. Hobson 2019 is the multiroute synthesis.

Bird group (continuing position) Independent challenge Synthesis
2013
Bird & Cook — "Mixed Emotions" (PMC3731793)
The "alexithymia hypothesis of autism" articulated. ~50% of autistic adults are alexithymic; emotional symptoms attributed to autism may be alexithymia-driven.
Translational Psychiatry. Sets up the autism-vs-alexithymia distinction that becomes important downstream.
2016
Brewer, Cook & Bird — General deficit of interoception (PMC5098957)
208 participants. Self-reported alexithymia → poor non-affective interoception + increased perceived similarity between affective and non-affective body states. Foundational claim: alexithymia = general failure of interoception.
Royal Society Open Science. Self-reported interoception (becomes important for the 2018 fork).
2016
Shah, Hall, Catmur & Bird — Alexithymia, not autism (PubMed 27253723)
Behavioural heartbeat-detection. Variance in interoceptive accuracy is captured by alexithymia level, not by autism diagnosis. Interoceptive deficits are a feature of alexithymia, not of autism per se.
Cortex. The autism walk-back, not an alexithymia walk-back.
2018
Murphy, Brewer, Catmur & Bird — Multidomain failure (PMC5824617)
Three experiments using novel non-cardiac tasks (respiratory output, muscular effort, taste sensitivity). High-alexithymia individuals: reduced interoceptive accuracy AND reduced use of interoceptive cues across all three domains. Controls for autism, depression, anxiety.
J Exp Psychol Gen. STRENGTHENS the multidomain account.
2018
Murphy, Brewer, Hobson, Catmur & Bird — HCT control variables (PubMed 29803614)
N=287. Initial bivariate: no relationship between alexithymia and HCT. After controlling for age, gender, time-estimation ability, BMI, knowledge of HR, HRV, depression, anxiety, BP, resting HR — predicted negative relationship emerges. Two findings: (a) association is real once confounds are controlled; (b) HCT is methodologically problematic.
Biological Psychology. Methodological clarification, NOT substantive walk-back.
2018
Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino & Gaigg — Independent challenge (PMC6089261)
Cardiac HCT, Bayesian analyses. Result: neither autism nor alexithymia associated with HCT performance. Bayesian evidence supports the null hypothesis. This paper is from an INDEPENDENT group at City University London — NOT from the Bird lab.
J Abnormal Psychology. The actual third-party challenge to the Bird thesis. Often misattributed to Bird group.
2019
Hobson, Brewer, Catmur & Bird — Multiroute model (Emotion Review)
Alexithymia as common downstream phenotype of multiple upstream impairments. Interoception is ONE route. Language/labelling is another. Emotion-concept failure may be a third. The closest the lineage comes to qualifying its position: interoception is one route (still affirmed), not the route.
SAGE 10.1177/1754073919838528. Most current synthesis.
2020
Aaron, Blain, Snodgress & Park — Quadratic relationship (PMC7076086)
U-shaped relationship: BOTH low and high interoceptive accuracy can co-occur with high alexithymia. Alexithymia best understood as problem of interpretation/labelling that can occur with either weak or hypervigilant body-signal detection.
Frontiers in Psychiatry. Independent group; supports Garfinkel three-dimensional architecture and parsing-deficit reading. Often misattributed to Trevisan.
2021
Brewer, Murphy & Bird — Atypical interoception review (PMC8522807)
Authoritative current synthesis. Direct quote: "many individuals with alexithymia experience a generalised interoceptive impairment, rather than one specific to emotional processing." Impairment hypothesised at multiple representational levels — conscious detection, recognition/labelling, or both — with individuals differing in which level is affected.
Neurosci Biobehav Rev. The current Bird-group position. NOT a retraction.
What was actually walked back. The autism-specific claim. Shah et al. 2016 had argued interoception deficits in autism are mediated by co-occurring alexithymia. Subsequent Bird-group work continues to support this asymmetric position: alexithymia tracks interoceptive deficit; autism per se does not. The multidomain-failure-in-alexithymia claim has been strengthened, not retreated.

Critical attribution corrections

  • PMC6089261 = Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino & Gaigg (2018) — INDEPENDENT group at City University London, NOT Bird lab.
  • PMC7076086 = Aaron, Blain, Snodgress & Park (2020) — quadratic relationship paper. Often misattributed to Trevisan.
  • The Bird group's published position in 2026 still affirms multidomain interoception failure. Brewer/Murphy/Bird 2021 is the authoritative current synthesis.
  • Hobson 2019 multiroute model qualifies but does not abandon the interoception-route account.
