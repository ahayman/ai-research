How IFS fits

IFS treats the psyche as composed of "parts" (distinct sub-personalities with their own perspectives, feelings, and protective functions) that can be related to from a core "Self." Therapy proceeds by Self-led dialogue with parts.

The fit case for alexithymia: externalising parts as distinct entities provides scaffolding when introspective access is poor — the user can think about a part rather than from inside an unintegrated affective state. Parts can be identified by behaviour, role, or context as well as by felt sense, providing access channels that don't require interoceptive labelling.

The friction case: IFS standard protocol (the "6 Fs": Find, Focus, Flesh out, Feel toward, befriend, Fears) leans on the interoceptive/emotional-recognition channel that alexithymia disrupts. The "Flesh out" and "Feel toward" steps assume the client can detect feelings about parts. For parsing-deficit alexithymics the standard protocol can stall at exactly these steps. Adaptations exist (Sarah Bergenfield, Sanni Lehtinen, IFS Telehealth Collective).

Where IFS does NOT cover the alexithymia landscape

  • Acquired alexithymia (lesion-based). Post-stroke, post-TBI, post-neurodegeneration alexithymia. Hogeveen 2016 lesion data. IFS has nothing direct to say.
  • Normative male alexithymia (gender-socialisation-driven). Levant's framing of alexithymia as a gender-role outcome; Levant's ART psychoeducational group intervention. IFS does not directly target this.
  • The pharmacological angle. Intranasal oxytocin and other adjuncts. Not in IFS scope.
  • Most non-IFS therapies. CBT, DBT, ACT, EAET, MBT, EFT, somatic experiencing, schema therapy. Each has its own evidence and indications. IFS is one option among many.
  • Specific comorbidities. Eating disorders, FND, schizophrenia spectrum — IFS exists in these contexts but does not own them; comorbidity-specific protocols have their own evidence bases.
  • The general meditation-related-adverse-effects literature. Britton 2021, Goldberg 2022, Lindahl, Treleaven. Relevant to alexithymia therapy choice broadly, not specifically to IFS.
  • Measurement instruments other than TAS-20. PAQ, BVAQ, TSIA, LEAS — most not used in IFS contexts but real for alexithymia assessment.
