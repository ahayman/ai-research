Treatment-matching evidence

1.Treatment-matching evidence by subtype is thin. No published RCT directly tests interoception-first vs external/cognitive-first therapy stratified by alexithymia parsing-deficit subtype.Cameron 2024 systematic review explicitly flags this. Bornemann's submodule analysis and Lumley's symptom-severity moderation are the closest signals; neither is interoception-stratified.
2.Iatrogenic harm literature for alexithymia specifically is thin. Norman 2019 review of mindfulness for alexithymia reports no adverse-event data from any of the four reviewed trials.Direct alexithymia-specific harm RCTs essentially absent. Mechanistic case is built from somatosensory-amplification literature plus general MRAE literature plus Treleaven's trauma-sensitive mindfulness framework.
3.Long-term post-treatment follow-up data short. Most studies stop at 6–12 months.Durable change vs reversion not well-characterised. 19% remission in personality-disorder samples is from intensive treatment in highly-comorbid populations and may not generalise.

Subtype and measurement

4.LPA cluster subtype replication is incomplete. Chen four-cluster scheme has not consistently replicated outside Chinese-student samples.Recent LPA work (Schaeuffele 2021, 2025 nursing students, 2025 elderly, 2025 Hogrefe FMM) supports severity continua over discrete classes.
5.Type II / psychosis prospective UHR data missing. Van der Velde 2015 is parametric-cross-sectional.No prospective transition data published. Polish replication exists (Cedro 2001) but is older. Independent (non-Polish, non-Dutch) replications using BVAQ specifically are limited.
6.TAS-20 vs interview-based measure adequacy is unresolved. Self-report constraint not solved.Triangulation (TAS-20 + LEAS or TSIA + interoception measure) is the field's current best-practice direction but most published research still uses TAS-20 alone.

Mechanism / route question

7.Whether the multiroute model admits a third route — autistic perceptual/interoceptive architecture distinct from alexithymia-as-language-deficit and alexithymia-as-interoception-deficit — is not yet articulated cleanly in the peer-reviewed literature.Hobson 2019 articulates two routes; an autism-specific architecture as a third candidate route has been informally proposed but not formally articulated in the peer-reviewed literature.
8.Whether the parsing-deficit framework actually carves alexithymia at a useful joint, vs. the more useful joint is the multiroute model's (interoception-route vs language-route).Open theoretical-empirical question. The parsing-deficit framework is a clinically intuitive framing; the multiroute model is a theoretically grounded framing. Whether both can be reconciled in a single architecture has not been articulated.

Trauma and overwhelm cascade

9.Cumulative-overwhelm cascade not tested as single longitudinal mechanism. The chain alexithymia → late-state-recognition → cumulative dysregulation → meltdown/burnout exists in clinical writing and qualitative work but has no longitudinal empirical study.Frankel/Paulsen, Neff, Trauma Geek lineage articulate it. Quattrocki & Friston provide formal mechanism. But no primary empirical study tracks the chain in autistic adults longitudinally.
10.Repeated-meltdown-as-traumagenic dose-response untested. Clinical-writing consensus exists; longitudinal data does not.Inferential evidence is moderate (Rumball 2020 includes mental-health events as Criterion-A-non-meeting traumas; polyvagal framings treat meltdowns as high-cost autonomic events). No published longitudinal study tracks meltdown frequency and subsequent PTSS development.

Cultural validity

11.Cross-cultural construct validity unresolved. Mean differences between East Asian and Western samples may reflect construct non-equivalence; clean answers not available.The TAS-20 was developed in 1980s North America. Whether elevated East-Asian scores reflect higher prevalence, construct-non-equivalence, or cultural-fit is genuinely unsettled. The colonialist-validity question remains open.

Adaptive function and pharmacology

12.Adaptive function literature is thin. Eight individual papers across separate domains; no coherent published research program uniting them.Hsu 2021 (N=42), Zhang 2017, Bagnoli 2010, Lee & Lee 2024, Pisani 2020, Soto 2011, Schouten 2020, Häusser 2013, Starita & Cellini, Mottron 2011 — collectively suggestive but each domain is a separate paper without an integrating theoretical-empirical synthesis.
13.Pharmacology beyond oxytocin almost nothing. Oxytocin shows promise but is not approved.Luminet 2011, Quirin & Carter 2014 review. Beyond oxytocin, pharmacological adjuncts for alexithymia are essentially unstudied.

Pre-trauma vulnerability

14.Pre-trauma interoception-as-vulnerability prospectively tested only in narrow contexts. Military samples (French military, Fort Campbell 101st Airborne); not in general population.McCaslin 2006 NYC police is the field's anchor study. Heinrichs 2005 firefighter study found alexithymia INCREASED with PTSD across follow-up rather than predicting it. No large-N general-population prospective design has been published.
