The Mediation Chain — Adverse Childhood Experiences → Alexithymia → Adult Psychopathology

Childhood maltreatment isn't just associated with adult mental-health problems. One of the routes by which it gets there is by failing to build the emotional self-regulation infrastructure that secure caregiving would have built.

Adverse childhood experiences

Maltreatment in the developmental window:
  • Emotional neglect — strongest predictor
  • Physical maltreatment
  • Sexual maltreatment
  • Caregiver dismissiveness, inconsistency, hostility
→
Disrupts emotion-coaching, mirroring, vocabulary-building

Alexithymia (secondary phenotype)

Failure to develop fluent emotional vocabulary and self-regulation:
  • High DIF — confusion about which feeling
  • High DDF — can't put into words
  • Variable EOT
  • Often co-occurs with insecure (especially dismissive) attachment
→
Mediates emotional processing, regulation, recovery

Adult psychopathology

Significant indirect effects through alexithymia:
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • PTSD / complex trauma
  • Somatisation, somatic symptom disorder
  • Substance use
  • Eating disorders

Why emotional neglect specifically. Of the maltreatment subtypes, emotional neglect appears as the strongest meta-analytic predictor of adult alexithymia. The proposed mechanism is direct: emotional neglect is the absence of the co-regulation, mirroring, and emotion-coaching that build emotional vocabulary in children. Physical and sexual abuse remain significant predictors but plausibly operate through additional pathways (dissociation, structural traumatic adaptations).

What this means for the adult. If your alexithymia traces partly to emotional neglect or maltreatment in childhood, that fraction is the developmental "secondary" phenotype. It is theoretically more responsive to relational, trauma-informed, and attachment-focused therapies than the constitutional fraction. The two often co-occur and need to be held simultaneously rather than chosen between.
