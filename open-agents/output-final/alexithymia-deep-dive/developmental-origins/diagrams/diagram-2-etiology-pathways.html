Etiology Pathways — Three Routes to Alexithymia

Where the alexithymia came from conditions which therapies are likely to help. Most alexithymic adults are mixed — usually primary + secondary, occasionally with an organic overlay.

Primary ~30–42% heritable

Constitutional, lifelong, dispositional. Genetic + early-cognitive-architectural. Often co-occurs with autism, ADHD.
↓
Twin evidence: Jorgensen 2007 (Danish, ~30%), Picardi 2011 (Italian, ~42%). No single-gene story; polygenic, trait-level.
↓
Therapy implications Externalised / cognitive-first approaches (granularity-building, affect-labeling, structured journaling) tend to fit better than insistence on internal-introspection. Closer to accommodation than reversal — and not necessarily a deficit; interacts with the adaptive-function frame.

Secondary developmental

Emerges from trauma, attachment disruption, parental modeling, gender socialisation, cultural shaping. The largest variance share at the population level.
↓
Pathways:
  • Childhood maltreatment (Aaron 2023)
  • Insecure attachment, especially dismissive (Lyvers 2019)
  • Parental alexithymia (modeling)
  • Gender socialisation (Levant NMAS)
  • Cultural shaping (Ryder 2018)
↓
Therapy implications More state-changeable than primary. Trauma-informed work, relational/attachment-focused therapy, mentalisation-based therapy, psychoeducation. Cameron 2024: medium TAS-20 effects across 21 RCT arms.

Organic ~15% of TBI

Acquired post-lesion: stroke, traumatic brain injury, neurodegeneration. Reliably localizes to anterior insula. Covered fully in Cat 4 (Neural).
↓
Lesion evidence: Hogeveen 2016 (PMC4752907) — >15% anterior insula damage produces dose-dependent acquired alexithymia.
↓
Therapy implications Neurorehabilitation framework. Compensatory strategies, environmental scaffolding. Insight-oriented therapy generally not first line. Rarely fully modifiable.
The clinically common case is mixed. An autistic adult with childhood emotional neglect is not cleanly "primary" or "secondary" — they are both. The proportional split varies by individual; estimating it precisely is impossible from current science. The honest framing: some constitutional contribution + some developmental contribution + very rarely a lesion contribution. Therapy plans should address both the constitutional fraction (accommodation) and the developmental fraction (relational/trauma work) rather than picking one.

Sources: Messina, Beadle, Paradiso (2014); Jorgensen et al. (2007) PubMed 17917473; Picardi et al. (2011) PubMed 21911104; Aaron et al. (2023); Lyvers et al. (2019) PubMed 30848836; Levant NMAS; Ryder et al. (2018); Hogeveen et al. (2016) PMC4752907; Cameron et al. (2024) PMC11673933.
