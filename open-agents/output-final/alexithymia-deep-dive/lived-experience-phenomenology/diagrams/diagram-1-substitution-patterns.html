The Substitution Patterns

When emotion-words aren't available, what arrives in their place — and what each substitution actually carries.

The core problem

"How do you feel?"

→ no confident emotion-label arrives

The system doesn't return nothing. It returns a different kind of answer. Four common patterns:

Somatic substitution

The body says it instead

"My stomach hurts"
≈
"I'm anxious"
"My head is pounding"
≈
"I'm frustrated"
"Tightness in my chest"
≈
"I'm grieving"
What this carries: real body-state data. Drives the somatic-symptom-disorder overlap with alexithymia. The body-state is doing the work an emotion-label would normally do.
Behavioural substitution

The action-impulse gets named

"I want to punch the wall"
≈
"I'm angry"
"I want to hide"
≈
"I'm ashamed"
"I want to leave"
≈
"I'm overwhelmed"
What this carries: a felt sense of what is wanted, even when the underlying affect is illegible. Particularly common in male alexithymic clients (Levant lineage).
Cognitive substitution

The thinking gets named, not the feeling

"I'm thinking a lot about X"
≈
"I'm worried about X"
"Going over the conversation"
≈
"Hurt by what was said"
"I'm analysing the situation"
≈
"I'm afraid of it"
What this carries: the cognitive activity is real and often overwhelming. Maps closely onto the EOT (Externally-Oriented Thinking) TAS-20 facet.
Numerical / situational

A scale or schema instead of a category

"My mood is 4/10 today"
≈
(but what kind of 4?)
"It's a yellow day"
≈
(yellow over what?)
"About a 3 on the bad scale"
≈
(3 of which bad?)
What this carries: the most accurate report the person can give. Quantitative or schematic frame substitutes for the categorical-emotional one. Honest, but doesn't carry what an emotion-label would.
The substitutions are not failures of communication. They are accurate reports from a system in which the standard-emotion-label channel is faulty. They carry real data about what's going on — they just need translation work the alexithymic person often cannot do alone. Treating substitutions as information rather than as evasion is the first move that opens up a useful conversation.
