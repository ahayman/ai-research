Empirical: CONTESTED
What the ledger shows The mental-health cost of chronic masking in autistic adults is one of the best-established findings in this literature. Multiple measurement instruments, multiple study designs (cross-sectional, EMA, co-twin), and one rigorous systematic review converge on the same picture: masking is associated with depression, anxiety, suicidality, identity erosion, interpersonal-trauma exposure, and authenticity loss. The co-twin and EMA designs provide some of the strongest causal inference currently available. The longitudinal prospective study is missing — but the overall direction of the evidence is unambiguous: chronic masking has a measurable mental-health cost, and that cost is part of the substrate from which the cumulative-overwhelm cascade is built.
