Autistic Burnout vs PTSD vs Depression — Distinguishing Features

All three frequently co-occur in autistic adults; they are distinct constructs with different recovery pathways. Higgins/Arnold 2025 found r = 0.70 between autistic burnout and PTSS — substantial overlap, but NOT identity.

Autistic Burnout

EtiologyCumulative load: chronic sensory + social mismatch + masking demands + barriers to support relief.
Core triadChronic exhaustion + loss of skills + reduced tolerance to stimulus.
DurationTypically 3+ months; chronic/recurrent pattern.
Emotional qualityExhaustion / frustration / sensory-overload-driven.
Withdrawal"I cannot tolerate the input."
Recovery responds toEnvironmental change, accommodation, unmasking, rest.
MeasurementABSI (Arnold/Higgins et al. 2023). AASPIRE Autistic Burnout Scale Inventory.
AnchorRaymaker et al. 2020 (PMC7313636); Mantzalas 2022 CMAB; Arnold/Higgins 2023 ABSI.

PTSD / CPTSD

EtiologyDiscrete trauma exposure (DSM-5 Criterion A) OR cumulative non-Criterion-A events (Rumball 2020).
Core clustersRe-experiencing + avoidance + hyperarousal + (CPTSD adds) emotional dysregulation, negative self-concept, interpersonal difficulties.
Duration>1 month for diagnosis; chronic without intervention.
Emotional qualityFear / hyperarousal / numbing / shame.
Withdrawal"This place / this trigger is unsafe."
Recovery responds toTrauma processing (TF-CBT, EMDR, somatic, IFS); rest alone insufficient.
MeasurementPCL-5 (PTSD); ITQ (ICD-11 CPTSD).
AnchorRumball 2020 (DOI 10.1002/aur.2306); Cloitre ICD-11 CPTSD development; ICD-11 working group.

Depression (MDD)

EtiologyMultifactorial — genetic, neurochemical, life-stress, biological. Not primarily load-driven.
Core triadPersistent sadness / anhedonia / cognitive-behavioural slowing. Worthlessness, hopelessness.
Duration≥2 weeks for diagnosis; episodes typically months long.
Emotional qualitySadness / worthlessness / anhedonia / hopelessness.
Withdrawal"I see no point."
Recovery responds toAntidepressants, CBT, behavioural activation; environmental change less reliably effective alone.
MeasurementPHQ-9, BDI-II.
AnchorDSM-5 / ICD-11 MDD criteria.

Why the distinction matters clinically Burnout responds to environment / rest / unmasking; PTSD requires trauma processing; depression has its own treatment landscape. Many autistic adults present with all three simultaneously, and the recovery pathway requires addressing each on its own terms. The Higgins/Arnold r = 0.70 finding tells us burnout and PTSS share substantial variance — particularly self-image and memory factors — but does not collapse the constructs into one. "Burnout = PTSD" is a stronger claim than the data support; "burnout overlaps substantially with PTSS in autistic adults" is what the data say.
