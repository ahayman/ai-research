The Multiroute Model — Alexithymia as Common Phenotype, Not Single Mechanism

Hobson, Brewer, Catmur & Bird (2019) proposed the most defensible current synthesis: alexithymia is the common downstream phenotype of multiple upstream impairments. Theorists differ on which upstream route they emphasize; the multiroute view accommodates all of them.

Three different upstream impairments, all converging on the same observable pattern: difficulty identifying / describing / focusing on internal emotional states. Different alexithymics can be on different routes. Some have multiple routes contributing. The same TAS-20 score can mean three quite different things.

Interoception Route

Brewer, Cook, Bird (2016); Murphy et al. (2018) PMC5824617; Brewer, Murphy, Bird (2021) PMC8522807
Multidomain failure of interoception — across cardiac, respiratory, muscular, taste, thermal modalities. The body's signal channel itself doesn't deliver adequate information to the rest of the system. Affirmed and strengthened by the Bird group through 2021.
TherapyInteroception-focused work — MABT, scaffolded body-scan, ADIE-style accuracy training. Built carefully because this same channel is over-attended in the hypervigilance subtype.

Language Route

Hobson, Brewer, Catmur, Bird (2019) Emotion Review
Body signals are detected adequately but the emotion-concept vocabulary is impoverished or poorly mapped. The label-mapping fails, not the detection. The person feels something but can't put it into language because the language for it doesn't exist for them in usable form.
TherapyGranularity-building (Barrett), affect-labeling (Lieberman 2007), structured journaling, vocabulary scaffolding. External-to-internal: build the concept first, then map back.

Conceptualization Route

Barrett (2017) Theory of Constructed Emotion PMC5390700
A possible third route. Emotions are constructed from interoceptive input + conceptual categories + situational context. If the construction process itself is impaired — e.g., the brain's predictive-processing pipeline doesn't reliably produce emotion-categories from sensory input — alexithymia results even with intact detection and intact vocabulary.
TherapyLess developed clinically. Theoretical fit with constructed-emotion frame; emerging predictive-processing-informed approaches. Most speculative of the three routes.
Why the multiroute frame matters. It dissolves a long-running theoretical dispute. Rather than choosing between "alexithymia is fundamentally an interoception problem" (Bird/Brewer) vs "alexithymia is fundamentally a language/concept problem" (Hobson, Barrett), the multiroute model lets both be true for different individuals. This makes therapy-matching tractable: the route conditions which intervention class is theoretically motivated. It also explains why subtype-blind intervention claims so often fail to replicate — different routes respond to different therapies, and the same trial enrolls all routes without distinction.

Sources: Hobson, Brewer, Catmur, Bird (2019) Emotion Review 11(3):247-261; Brewer, Cook, Bird (2016); Murphy et al. (2018) PMC5824617; Brewer, Murphy, Bird (2021) PMC8522807; Barrett (2017) PMC5390700.
