The Interoceptive Split — Detection vs Parsing vs Hypervigilance

The most consequential axis for therapy choice. The same intervention (body-focused mindfulness) goes opposite directions across these three positions. Even-handed framing with corrected attribution.

Position A

Detection-Deficit

Body signals are not adequately registered. Emotions therefore have nothing to "be made of." The deficit is at the input stage of the perception pipeline.
"When I check in with my body, there's just nothing there. Heart rate, breath, muscle tension — I don't really register any of it."
Lineage Brewer, Cook, Bird (2016) "Alexithymia: a general deficit of interoception" — foundational paper, Royal Society Open Science (PubMed 27853532).
Murphy, Brewer, Catmur, Bird (2018) PMC5824617 — multidomain failure across respiration, muscular effort, taste.
Brewer, Murphy, Bird (2021) PMC8522807 — current authoritative review affirming multidomain failure.
Therapy implications

Interoception-focused interventions (MABT, scaffolded body-scan, ADIE-style accuracy training) are most theoretically motivated. Built carefully, with safety scaffolding.

Position B

Parsing / Labeling-Deficit

Body signals ARE detected. The deficit is in interpreting them — the person feels something but cannot parse it as "anxiety" vs "anger" vs "hunger" vs "tired." The label-mapping fails, not the detection.
"My heart's racing and my stomach is tight. I don't know if I'm anxious, hungry, angry, or coming down with something."
Lineage Garfinkel, Seth, Barrett, Suzuki, Critchley (2015) "Knowing your own heart" — three-dimensional model: accuracy / sensibility / awareness can dissociate.
Aaron, Blain, Snodgress, Park (2020) PMC7076086 — quadratic relationship: both low AND high IA can co-occur with high alexithymia.
Hobson, Brewer, Catmur, Bird (2019) Emotion Review — multiroute: language is one route to alexithymia.
Therapy implications

Granularity-building (Barrett), affect-labeling (Lieberman 2007), structured journaling, vocabulary scaffolding. External-to-internal: build the concept first, then map back to inner state.

Position C

Hypervigilance with Mislabeling

Body signals are over-attended but interpreted somatically/catastrophically rather than as emotions. The signal is over-detected and mis-categorized. Overlaps heavily with somatic symptom disorder, anxiety, chronic pain.
"My chest hurts all the time. I keep checking it. I think there's something physically wrong with me."
Lineage Somatosensory amplification literature (Barsky framework, Perez et al.).
Garfinkel-3D: high sensibility + low accuracy = poor metacognitive awareness of body signals' meaning.
Comorbidity with FND, chronic pain, fibromyalgia, IBS, somatic symptom disorder is a marker.
Therapy implications

Interoception-first work specifically contraindicated — the body channel is over-attended already. Defusion-based approaches (ACT) and reattribution work (CBT, somatic-symptom-focused therapy) are more theoretically motivated.

The multiroute synthesis (Hobson, Brewer, Catmur, Bird 2019)

The most defensible current framework. Alexithymia is the common downstream phenotype of multiple possible upstream impairments. The Bird group's 2019 model formalized this: different alexithymics can be on different routes, and the deepest deficit in some sub-profiles may include detection while in others it is only labeling.

Critical attribution corrections.
  • PMC6089261 ("Interoceptive impairments do not lie at the heart of autism or alexithymia") — authors are Nicholson, Williams, Grainger, Christensen, Calvo-Merino, Gaigg (2018), an INDEPENDENT group from City University London / UCL — NOT Murphy/Catmur/Bird. This makes the field debate more contested than an internal-walk-back framing would suggest.
  • PMC7076086 (quadratic relationship paper) — authors are Aaron, Blain, Snodgress, Park (2020) — NOT Trevisan, Mehling, McPartland.
  • The Bird/Murphy/Brewer lineage has NOT walked back the multidomain interoception failure thesis — they have strengthened it. What was walked back is autism-specific (the variance loads on alexithymia, not autism per se).
