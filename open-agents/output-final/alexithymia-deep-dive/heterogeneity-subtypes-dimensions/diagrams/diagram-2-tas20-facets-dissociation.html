TAS-20 Facets — What They Capture and How They Dissociate

The dominant self-report measure has three subscales. They are not equally well-measured, and they don't always move together.

DIF

Difficulty Identifying Feelings (7 items)
"I am often confused about what emotion I am feeling."
"When I am upset, I don't know if I am sad, frightened or angry."
Captures: uncertainty about which emotion is occurring. The central phenomenological complaint of cognitive alexithymia.

DDF

Difficulty Describing Feelings (5 items)
"I find it hard to describe how I feel about people."
"It is difficult for me to find the right words for my feelings."
Captures: translation from inner state to language. Even when a feeling is identified, putting it into words fails.

EOT

Externally-Oriented Thinking (8 items)
"I prefer to analyze problems rather than just describe them."
"I prefer to just let things happen rather than to understand why they turned out that way."
Captures: cognitive style — preference for surface, practical, external content over internal-emotional content. The most contested factor.

How the facets dissociate — three common patterns

Pattern DIF DDF EOT Phenomenology
Comprehensive (general-high) High High High The most clearly alexithymic profile.
High-cognitive (introvert-high) High High Normal Feelings present but illegible. Confusion, not emptiness.
High-EOT (extrovert-high) Normal Normal High Task-focused, externally-oriented; intact emotion access. Often a cognitive-style choice rather than a deficit.

These cluster labels remain useful as descriptive shorthand, but recent latent-profile-analysis work (Schaeuffele 2021; Hogrefe 2025; 2025 nursing-students LPA; 2025 elderly LPA) finds that solutions distinguished by severity fit better than the four-class profile differentiation. Treat these as descriptive, not as validated discrete entities.

What TAS-20 does not capture: positive emotion (items focus on negative valence); imaginative/fantasy life (Sifneos's original fifth criterion, dropped in the TAS-20 development); the affective dimension of low arousability (captured by BVAQ but not TAS-20). For triangulation, pair TAS-20 with BVAQ or the Perth Alexithymia Questionnaire (Preece 2018).

Sources: Bagby, Parker, Taylor (1994); Schroeders, Kubera, Gnambs (2022) PMC9597132; Preece et al. (2018) PAQ.
