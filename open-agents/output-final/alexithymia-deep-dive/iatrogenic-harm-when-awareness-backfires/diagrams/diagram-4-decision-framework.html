Decision Framework — Profile-Stratified Caution Levels

A clinical-decision aid for evaluation, not a definitive contraindication list. Identify the profile first, then make the protocol decision. The default "everyone should try mindfulness" is supplanted by a profile-stratified recommendation.

What this framework is and isn't. This is an evaluation aid, drawing on Tier 1 + Tier 2 evidence (the meditation-related-adverse-effects literature, the somatosensory-amplification literature, the trauma-sensitive mindfulness framework, the parsing-vs-detection deficit distinction). It is not a definitive contraindication list. No subtype-stratified RCT exists to validate caution-level assignments precisely. The framework codifies clinical reasoning that is well-supported in adjacent literatures but extrapolated to the alexithymia case.
Profile Caution level Reasoning
Hypervigilance with mislabelling
Somatic-symptom disorder; anxiety with somatic focus; chronic pain with somatic vigilance		 High Body-attention amplifies already-loud signals; somatosensory amplification mechanism; risk of symptom intensification. Consider external/cognitive-first alternatives (Diagram 6) before any body-focused work.
Dissociative-subtype PTSD
PTSD with marked depersonalisation/derealisation		 High Body-focus can deepen depersonalisation (Lindahl PMC9043097); Lanius choice-points framework (PMC11257171) applies. Stabilisation and felt-sense anchoring before any open-ended body-focus.
High trauma load with low integration capacity
PTSD/CPTSD with poor affect-tolerance, no felt-sense anchor, limited support		 High Body-focus can trigger trauma re-experiencing without containment. Treleaven's 36 modifications apply. Phase-based protocol consensus: stabilise → build interoceptive parsing skills → trauma processing → integrate. Direct exposure or unmodified body-focus before stabilisation = iatrogenic-harm pathway.
Parsing-deficit alexithymia
Signals detected (often loud), can't interpret as nameable affect		 Moderate Tier 2 mechanism prediction: not directly tested in subtype-stratified RCTs but plausible. Consider external-vocabulary scaffolding (granularity-building, affect-labelling, structured journaling) before internal-introspection-first protocols. Monitor markers of deterioration (Diagram 5).
Detection-deficit alexithymia
Signals not detected; low interoceptive sensibility		 Low — may be appropriate Standard recommendation applies: interoceptive-awareness training builds genuinely missing detection capacity. Quadt et al. 2021 ADIE trial in autistic adults showed positive outcomes for interoceptive training in this profile. Protocols of record: MABT (Price & Hooven 2018), MBSR adapted for low-interoception, structured heartbeat-detection training.
No trauma load, no somatic-symptom complications, mid-range interoception Standard Standard mindfulness caution applies. Tier 1 MRAE risk is non-trivial in any population (~32-50% reporting some adverse effect); routine practice-monitoring is reasonable. No specific contraindication.
How to use this with a clinician. If a therapist recommends body-scan / interoceptive-awareness / open-ended mindfulness without first asking which profile you sit in, that's a signal of a one-size-fits-all protocol, not a profile-matched one. A reasonable conversation: "I have [profile]. Mechanism research suggests this profile may not be the best match for body-focused practice. Can we talk about external-vocabulary alternatives like granularity-building or affect-labelling first?" The alternatives in Diagram 6 give you concrete redirection options to suggest.
The honest framing. This framework is the user's actionable tool for evaluation. It does not say "you must not do X." It says "for this profile, the mechanism case for caution is plausible enough that a profile-matched alternative deserves consideration first." The user retains decision authority. The clinician retains protocol authority. The framework's job is to make the profile-matching question explicit rather than letting it default to a protocol that may not fit.
