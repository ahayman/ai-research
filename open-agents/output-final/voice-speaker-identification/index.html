Voiceprint Storage & Comparison

What is a Voiceprint?

A compact mathematical vector (not raw audio) that captures unique vocal characteristics. Cannot be reverse-engineered to speech.

Typical Dimensions

192-dim (x-vector) to 2048-dim (Qwen3). Larger embeddings capture more detail but require more storage.

Enrollment

3-30 seconds of speech needed to create a voiceprint. Modern systems (Qwen3) achieve biometric precision with just 3 seconds.

Comparison Methods

Cosine similarity (simple), PLDA scoring (statistical), or neural scoring. Typical threshold: 0.25-0.75 cosine similarity.

Security

Must be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit. Comply with FIPS 140-2. Pair with anti-spoofing for production use.

Storage Size

512-dim float32 = 2KB per voiceprint. A database of 1M speakers needs only ~2GB of embedding storage.
