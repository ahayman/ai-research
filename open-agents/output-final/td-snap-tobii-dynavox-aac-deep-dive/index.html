Overview

For someone who cannot speak, selecting the right communication software is not a matter of convenience -- it is a matter of voice. TD Snap, developed by Tobii Dynavox, has emerged as one of the most comprehensive augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) platforms on the market, offering an unprecedented range of communication strategies within a single application.

With eight distinct page sets, native eye gaze tracking integration, bilingual code-switching, and smart home control, TD Snap aims to serve virtually every AAC user profile -- from a toddler learning their first words to an adult recovering from a stroke.

What makes TD Snap unique among AAC apps is the sheer breadth of its page set library. Where most competitors offer one or two vocabulary systems, TD Snap provides six first-party page sets and two third-party options, all within the same application. This means a speech-language pathologist (SLP) can evaluate multiple communication strategies for a client without purchasing separate apps.

Three Access Methods

TD Snap supports touch (direct screen tapping), eye gaze (using Tobii Dynavox eye tracking hardware), and switch scanning (single or dual switch for users with severe motor impairments) across all page sets.

The Company Behind the Software

The story of Tobii Dynavox begins with two separate innovations on two continents, ultimately converging into the world's leading assistive communication company.

1983
The EyeTyper
Gary Kiliany, a Carnegie Mellon University graduate student, created the "EyeTyper" for a woman with cerebral palsy at the Rehabilitation Institute of Pittsburgh. This invention became the seed of DynaVox Systems, one of the first companies dedicated to AAC technology.
2001
Tobii Technology Founded
Tobii Technology was founded in Sweden, initially selling eye tracking technology for gaze analysis in psychology, athletics, and market research.
2005
First Eye-Controlled Computer
Tobii released the world's first eye-controlled computer, enabling people with disabilities to interact with technology using only their gaze.
2014
Tobii Acquires DynaVox
After DynaVox filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy, Tobii acquired them on May 22, 2014. The combined entity -- Tobii Dynavox LLC -- became the world's leading assistive communication provider, now employing 1,000+ people across 65+ countries.
2024
Subscription Transition
TD Snap AAC was unpublished from the App Store and replaced with a subscription-based version at $9.99/month, sparking significant controversy in the AAC community.
2025-26
Version 1.38 & Beyond
Latest releases added improved symbol diversity, bilingual Motor Plan page sets, YouTube Player integration, and Apple Shortcuts support.

Tobii Dynavox further expanded through acquisitions of Acapela (Belgian speech synthesis) and Rehadapt (German medical-grade mounting solutions).

Eye Gaze Technology

The feature that most distinguishes Tobii Dynavox from its competitors is native eye gaze tracking. Tobii's eye tracking is built on pupil-center corneal reflection (PCCR) -- a technique refined over 15+ years of R&D.

How Pupil-Center Corneal Reflection Works
1

Near-Infrared Illumination

The eye tracker emits near-infrared (NIR) light toward the user's eyes. NIR is invisible and causes no discomfort, with minimal interference from ambient lighting.

2

Corneal Reflection

NIR light creates reflection patterns on two eye surfaces -- the cornea (outer layer) and the pupil -- producing detectable light signatures.

3

Glint Detection

Sensors detect the corneal reflection (called the "glint") -- a bright spot created by light bouncing off the cornea's curved surface.

4

Pupil Center Calculation

Image processing algorithms simultaneously identify the exact center of the pupil using contrast detection between the dark pupil and surrounding iris.

5

Gaze Point Estimation

The relative positions of the glint and pupil center are compared. As the eye moves, the pupil shifts relative to the corneal reflection, enabling precise, continuous gaze tracking.

Why Tobii's Implementation Stands Out

◎

Dynamic Pupil Switching

Automatically alternates between bright and dark pupil detection, optimizing accuracy across eye colors and lighting conditions.

⚙

Universal Compatibility

Works across all ethnicities, accommodates glasses and contact lenses, and handles eye conditions common in AAC populations.

↺

Fast Recovery

When a user looks away and returns, Tobii's tracker recovers gaze lock faster than any competing system on the market.

✦

15+ Years of R&D

The technology has been refined through more than a decade of research specifically for assistive communication applications.

Ecosystem Lock-In Note

Tobii Dynavox explicitly states they cannot guarantee compatibility with non-approved hardware and will not provide technical support for TD Snap on third-party devices. This means eye gaze functionality is exclusively available through Tobii Dynavox hardware.

Bilingual Code-Switching: Why It Matters

Code-switching is a natural communication behavior where bilingual speakers mix languages within a single conversation or sentence. For AAC users, being forced to choose one language for their device -- when their family and community operate in two -- constitutes an inequitable reduction in communicative capacity.

"Bilingual AAC users have the right to code-switch whenever they deem necessary. ALL bilingual AAC users should ALWAYS have access to both languages on their AAC systems." -- AAC community consensus (OMazing Kids AAC Consulting)

TD Snap's true code-switching support allows users to compose messages that naturally blend languages without navigating to a separate vocabulary -- a feature that remains rare in the AAC market.

Why the Backlash Matters

  • Lifetime cost: AAC users who need the app for life face indefinite costs. Five years = ~$600 vs. the previous $100 one-time payment.
  • Service disruption: Some families reported forced updates during the transition temporarily disabled communication on their devices.
  • Institutional barriers: AT Act programs and MDM systems (JAMF) reported incompatibilities with subscription models.
  • Ethical dimension: Critics argue communication is a fundamental human right, and subscription-gating access to one's voice is morally untenable.

Strengths & Weaknesses

✔ Strengths
  • Unmatched page set variety -- 8 page sets covering virtually every AAC user profile in a single app
  • World-leading eye gaze -- 15+ years of R&D with universal compatibility and fast recovery
  • Growth pathway -- Users can start with a 1x1 grid and advance to Motor Plan 66 or Text without switching apps
  • Bilingual code-switching -- True in-sentence language switching, a rare AAC feature
  • Data tracking -- Exportable .xlsx analytics for SLP clinical analysis
  • Google Assistant -- Smart home control extends the platform beyond communication
  • All access methods -- Touch, eye gaze, and switch scanning supported universally
✘ Weaknesses
  • Subscription model -- $9.99/month creates ongoing costs; significant community backlash
  • Ecosystem lock-in -- Eye gaze requires Tobii hardware ($10,500+ for TD Pilot)
  • No Android -- Only available on iPad and Windows
  • Add-on costs -- PODD and Gateway require purchases beyond base subscription
  • Complexity -- 8 page sets, 12+ grid sizes, and multiple access methods can overwhelm new users
  • Hardware pricing -- Dedicated devices represent $10K+ investments (may be covered by insurance)

Verdict

TD Snap occupies a unique position in the AAC landscape. No other single application offers the combination of eight distinct page sets, world-leading eye gaze technology, bilingual code-switching, smart home control, and a clear growth pathway from first communication to literate independence.

For families, SLPs, and institutions willing to invest in the Tobii Dynavox ecosystem, the clinical capabilities are exceptional. The variety of page sets alone makes TD Snap invaluable for evaluations -- an SLP can test Core First, Motor Plan, Express, PODD, and Gateway with a single client in a single session, without purchasing or installing separate apps.

The subscription pricing model, however, has introduced a persistent tension. For a community that views communication as a fundamental right, any barrier to access draws scrutiny. Tobii Dynavox's challenge going forward is to sustain the rapid innovation that justifies recurring costs while ensuring that no one loses their voice because of a billing cycle.

"TD Snap is by far the best. Better than the most popular one out there that is being used by the schools." -- Speech-Language Pathologist, App Store Review (2025)
Bottom Line

TD Snap is the most comprehensive AAC platform available, with unmatched versatility in page sets and access methods. It is the clear choice when eye gaze access is needed. The subscription model is its most significant drawback. Evaluate using the combined 60-day + 1-month free trials before committing.
