Imagine waking up from surgery and being unable to speak. Your thoughts are clear, your needs are urgent, but the words simply will not come. For millions of adults living with conditions like stroke, ALS, Parkinson's disease, and traumatic brain injury, this is not a hypothetical scenario -- it is daily reality.

While the AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) market has long been dominated by symbol-based systems designed with children in mind, one speech-language pathologist decided that adults deserved something better: a communication tool built around real photographs, personal memories, and the dignity of grown-up life. APP2Speak is that tool.

✎ The Story Behind APP2Speak

Gina Baldwin, M.S., CCC-SLP, did not set out to build an app. For over 40 years, she worked in the trenches of speech-language pathology -- hospitals, rehabilitation centers, long-term care facilities -- helping adults who had lost their ability to speak. She created countless speech boards, tried dozens of devices and apps, and watched her patients struggle with tools that were not designed for them.

"After decades of working with adults who had lost their speech, the pattern was impossible to ignore. The tools were often too childish, too complex, or too focused on therapy or school instead of daily life."

-- Gina Baldwin, M.S., CCC-SLP, Creator of APP2Speak

The problems were consistent across the industry: AAC devices cost hundreds or thousands of dollars. Symbol-based systems used cartoon-like icons that felt infantilizing to adult users. Interfaces were cluttered with therapy-oriented features rather than tools for everyday conversation. And perhaps most critically, adult communication needs were treated as an afterthought in a market oriented primarily toward children.

Baldwin's solution was straightforward but revolutionary in its simplicity: build an AAC app around real photographs. After confirming that nothing like it existed on the market, she founded Cabson Consulting LLC and began developing APP2Speak. The app launched for iPhone and Android in 2020, bringing decades of clinical insight into a single, photo-driven communication platform.

⚙ How APP2Speak Works

APP2Speak provides three distinct communication modes, each designed for different situations and skill levels. Together, they form a comprehensive system that grows with the user.

Mode 1

Preset Communication Pages

Out of the box, APP2Speak comes preprogrammed with 80 real-life photographs organized across 4 themed pages covering essential daily needs -- food, hygiene, emotions, and common requests. Users simply touch a photo to hear the associated word or phrase spoken aloud.

80 preset photos 4 themed pages Zero setup time 2 size options
Mode 2

Custom Communication Pages

This is where APP2Speak truly distinguishes itself. Users can create an unlimited number of custom pages populated with their own photographs.

  • Personal photo library from the device
  • Internet images saved to the device
  • In-app camera for taking photos directly within APP2Speak
  • Each photo can have one or more custom phrases associated with it
  • Custom pages can be shared via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi with other APP2Speak users
Unlimited pages Personal photos No training required
Mode 3

Text-to-Speech

A freeform typing interface where users type any message and the app speaks it aloud. Frequently used phrases are saved to history for quick access. Features include:

  • Male or female text-to-speech voice options
  • Adjustable speech rate for natural pacing
  • Voice recording -- family members can record their own voice
  • Phrase history for quick access to common messages

📸 Photo-Based vs Symbol-Based AAC

Traditional AAC systems overwhelmingly rely on symbol sets like PCS (Picture Communication Symbols) -- stylized line drawings that represent words and concepts. While these work well for many children, they present significant challenges for adults with acquired communication disorders.

📷 Photo-Based (APP2Speak)

+ Immediate recognition -- no learning curve, no abstraction to decode
+ Deep personalization -- your family, your home, your world
+ Adult dignity -- nothing childish about real photographs
+ Emotional connection -- personal photos create meaningful bonds
+ Research-backed -- VSD studies show photos work better for aphasia (PMC6436972)

✎ Symbol-Based (Traditional)

+ Abstract concepts -- can represent "maybe," "tomorrow," "because"
+ Linguistic structure -- LAMP/Unity support sentence building
+ Language development -- designed to build skills over time
- Cognitive load -- abstract symbols may confuse adults with brain injuries
- Dignity concerns -- cartoon icons feel infantilizing for adults

The trade-off is clear: photo-based AAC excels at immediate, personal, dignified communication for adults, while symbol-based systems are stronger for linguistic development and abstract expression. APP2Speak addresses the abstract-concept gap partly through its text-to-speech mode, but users who cannot type may find their expressive range limited.

♿ Accessibility: From Bluetooth Switches to Eye Tracking

Many conditions that cause speech loss also affect motor function. APP2Speak integrates deeply with iOS accessibility technologies to ensure users with severe physical limitations can still communicate.

🎧

Bluetooth Switches

External switch access for users with severe motor limitations, including advanced ALS and quadriplegia.

✋

Touch Accommodations

iOS hold duration, ignore repeat, and tap assistance for users with tremors or limited motor control.

👁

Low Vision

Two photo display sizes and compatibility with iOS low-vision settings for visually impaired users.

👀

Apple Eye Tracking

iOS 18+ eye tracking for completely hands-free navigation and photo selection using only eye movements.

🔒

Page Lock

Prevents accidental edits or navigation during active use -- essential for motor-impaired users.

  • Photo-based personalization -- unlimited custom pages with personal photos
  • Affordable -- $199.99 one-time, no subscriptions
  • Created by an SLP -- 40+ years of clinical experience
  • ASHA recognized -- listed on ASHA Solution Center

✗ Weaknesses

  • Smaller ecosystem -- fewer users, tutorials, and community resources
  • Limited clinical research -- no independent outcome studies
  • No linguistic framework -- no LAMP/Unity for complex sentence building
  • English only -- not available in other languages
  • Single-developer risk -- Cabson Consulting LLC is a small operation
  • No cloud backup -- custom content at risk if device is lost
  • Abstract concept gap -- photos struggle with "maybe," "tomorrow," etc.
  • Fewer person-centered features -- scored lower than some competitors in LTC study

🔭 Recent Developments & Future Outlook

APP2Speak continues to evolve. The integration with Apple's iOS 18 Eye Tracking in 2024 was a significant milestone, opening the app to users who had previously been unable to use touchscreen-based AAC. The Control Bionics partnership extends accessibility further into the progressive disease community.

The app maintains an active presence at ATIA conferences and publishes regular blog content covering user stories, AAC best practices, and feature updates. Version 1.50, the most recent release, was published in June 2024.

Looking ahead, the AAC landscape is being reshaped by advances in AI-powered speech generation, personalized voice banking, and brain-computer interfaces. APP2Speak's photo-based approach positions it well to incorporate these technologies -- imagining a future where AI can automatically suggest photos, generate personalized visual scenes, or integrate with voice banking to produce speech that sounds like the user did before their condition.

For now, APP2Speak fills an important and underserved niche: affordable, dignified, deeply personal communication for adults who have lost their voice. In Gina Baldwin's words, the mission is simple -- "helping to provide a voice to those in need."
