## Deeper Citation Spot-Checks

### Lundh 2001 self-report critique

Cited as: Lundh L-G. (2001) "Limitations of the alexithymia construct" — PubMed 11593459. The substantive argument (self-report on introspection requires the introspective ability whose absence is being measured) is well-known in the literature and is correctly characterised. Title verbiage in the actual paper may differ slightly; the parent exploration cites this as "Critique of self-report alexithymia measurement (Lundh 2001) — PubMed 11593459" and the substantive point is the same.

## Known Open Questions Acknowledged

1. **TSIA and OAQ inter-rater reliability beyond well-trained samples** — the category notes the burden of rater training but does not promise reliability outside trained settings.
2. **Cross-cultural validity of all instruments beyond TAS-20** — the category notes that BVAQ, PAQ, and others have less cross-cultural data than TAS-20 and warns against treating any instrument as culture-free.
3. **State vs. trait fluctuation** — the category explicitly notes that scores fluctuate with state (depression, distress, sleep), and recommends multi-administration for trait stability.
4. **Pre-post administration interpretive caution** — the category warns that pre-post score change may reflect changed answering style rather than changed alexithymia.
