# Pragmatic Manipulation Sub-Hub: Build Plan

## The Single Source of Truth for Sub-Hub Work

This document defines the build order, content scope, and safety commitments for the Pragmatic Manipulation and Self-Protection sub-hub. It exists so the work can proceed systematically without per-page decision overhead.

---

## Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

These apply to every page in the sub-hub:

1. **Every strategy traces to a source.** No recommendation appears on any page unless it is traceable to (a) peer-reviewed research, (b) established safety frameworks from authoritative organizations (NDVH, Duluth, SAMHSA, Campbell's Danger Assessment, WHO, CDC, Workplace Bullying Institute), or (c) autistic community knowledge clearly labeled as experiential rather than empirical.

2. **Evidence quality is labeled.** The five-tier system from the foundational autism research document applies throughout: [VALIDATED], [PRELIMINARY], [EXTRAPOLATED], [COMMUNITY KNOWLEDGE], [CONTESTED]. Never present community knowledge as empirical; never present extrapolation as validated.

3. **No page repeats the forbidden claims.** Section 12 of the trauma framework document lists claims that are NOT well-supported (triune brain, polyvagal literalism, "trauma stored in tissues," recovered memory, universal PTG, "time heals," forgiveness requirements, pop-gaslighting overreach, "narcissistic abuse" as clinical category, boundaries-as-slogan). These must never appear on a hub page.

4. **Safety before engagement.** Every pattern page and context page must direct the reader to the Safety Assessment page before they apply any strategy. If lethality indicators are present, the correct response is advocacy contact, not hub strategies.

5. **Cross-link to pragmatics.** Every page links back to the relevant pragmatics categories so readers can learn the underlying mechanism without having to read the entire curriculum first.

6. **Template compliance.** Every page follows the 10-part template established in the Safety Assessment page:
   1. Opening reframe
   2. The concept explained
   3. How it looks in practice
   4. How to distinguish from legitimate behavior / miscommunication
   5. Autism-specific considerations
   6. Strategies (with conditionals)
   7. What this page cannot do
   8. Key takeaways
   9. Citation register
   10. Cross-links

7. **Downstream-use checklist.** Before finalizing each page, verify:
   - Every strategy traces to the strategy-level evidence map in the DV frameworks doc
   - Every mention of leaving pairs with safety planning + advocate referral
   - Every strangulation mention carries the lethality marker
   - Every "work it out" framing is screened against Bograd & Mederos
   - No Section 12 forbidden claims
   - Cross-links to relevant pragmatics categories

### Tier 3: Pattern Pages (Build Third)

Each page describes a specific manipulation tactic, how to recognize it, how to distinguish it from legitimate behavior, and which strategies apply.

Built in priority order by (a) prevalence/consequence and (b) strength of research base.

| # | Page | Priority | Primary Sources |
|---|------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Gaslighting** | Highest | Sweet (2019) sociology; Abramson (2014) philosophy; Stern (2007) popular; Duluth Wheel category 5. Most consequential and most researched pattern. |
| 2 | **DARVO** | Highest | Freyd (1997); Harsey & Freyd (2020); compound pattern with strong documentation. |
| 3 | **Tone Policing** | High | Specific vulnerability for autistic people; tone policing + direct communication = perpetual target. Documented in autistic community research (NeuroClastic; Douglas & Sedgewick 2024). |
| 4 | **Diagnostic Weaponization** | High | "That's your autism talking." Pearson et al. (2022); Douglas & Sedgewick (2024) weaponized features framework. |
| 5 | **Sensory Gaslighting** | High | Foundational vulnerability starting in childhood. Reardon (Autism Wellbeing UK); community sources; extrapolation from sensory processing research. |
| 6 | **The Compliance Trap** | High | "If you'd just mask better." Hull et al. masking research; extrapolation from ABA criticism literature (careful — Kupferstein is under Expression of Concern). |
| 7 | **Post-Meltdown Shaming** | High | Douglas & Sedgewick (2024) weaponized features — abusers deliberately triggering meltdowns then framing the autistic person as unstable. |
| 8 | **Pragmatic Packaging Invalidation** | High | "You said it wrong so it doesn't count." The core weaponization of pragmatic asymmetry. Cross-references register, hedging, politeness pragmatics categories. |
| 9 | **Weaponized Accommodations** | Moderate | "I do so much for you." Debt dynamic created around basic accommodations. Extrapolation from coercive control literature. |
| 10 | **Memory Gaslighting** | High | "That never happened." Connects to episodic memory research; documentation strategy is the mechanical compensation. |
| 11 | **Bandwagon Isolation** | Moderate | "Everyone agrees with me." Duluth Wheel isolation category; Sweet's social scaffolding framework. |
| 12 | **Concern Trolling** | Moderate | "I'm just worried about you." Paternalistic dismissal framed as care. |
| 13 | **Information Asymmetry Exploitation** | Moderate | Withholding information to create dependence; using hierarchy to control access. |
| 14 | **Emotional Invalidation Double Bind** | Moderate | The three-way trap: flat affect = "you don't feel it," intense expression = "you're overreacting," masking = unsustainable. Documented in autism-prosody research. |
| 15 | **Performative Allyship** | Lower | "I'm one of the good ones" as a manipulation tactic. Community knowledge more than empirical. |

**Breakpoint after Tier 3:** All recognition patterns have dedicated pages with strategies linked.

| 8 | **Social Services** | Moderate | Assessments where masking hides support needs; then support is denied because "you don't seem like you need it." |
| 9 | **Online/Digital Settings** | Moderate | Digital communication advantages (time to compose, written records) but also new risks (harassment, doxxing, pile-ons). |
| 10 | **Religious and Community Settings** | Moderate | Authority structures, doctrine-based compliance pressure, community scaffolding for gaslighting. |

## Scope Notes

**What this sub-hub is:**
- Research-grounded recognition and strategy content for autistic readers experiencing pragmatic manipulation
- Structured to be navigable (pattern → strategy → safety; or context → patterns present → strategies available)
- Explicit about evidence quality and honest about limits

**What this sub-hub is not:**
- A clinical treatment manual
- A substitute for trained advocates or therapists
- Legal advice
- A guarantee of safety in any specific situation
- Tested on every autistic population (non-speaking, intellectual disability, Black autistic women, etc. remain research gaps)

**Every page will carry these limits as a standing caveat.**
