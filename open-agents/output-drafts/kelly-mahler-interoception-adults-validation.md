# Research Validation Report: Kelly Mahler's Interoception Approach (Adults)

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: HIGH

- **Confirmed**: 13 claims (72%)
- **Uncertain**: 4 claims (22%)
- **Invalid**: 1 claim (6%)

**Key Findings**:
The factual core of the research holds up under independent verification. Mahler's credentials (OTD from Misericordia, OTR/L, Hershey PA, adjunct at Elizabethtown and Misericordia) check out. The published-research-is-pediatric finding is robust — no adult RCT, no adult feasibility study exists in PubMed under her name. The catalog's adult/pediatric split is real, with two genuinely adult-targeted products (Workbook 2019; Lens-of-an-Autistic-Adult course) anchoring a much larger pediatric-rooted catalog. One material correction: Mahler *does* publicly engage harm risk on her site, but specifically about Interoceptive Exposure Therapy (a different modality) — *not* the Aaron 2020 quadratic finding or body-scan trauma harms. The research overstated her engagement with the Aaron 2020 risk.

**Systemic Issues**:
- Mahler's clinical credibility (OT field) and her research credibility (peer-reviewed body in adults) are easy to conflate. The page must keep them distinct.
- "Adapted for adults" language in marketing materials is ambiguous; the research correctly flags it.
- The CAIA adult version exists as a product but does not appear to have published peer-reviewed psychometric validation — distinct from the well-validated MAIA-2 (Mehling et al.). The research should clarify this difference.

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### Claim 1: Kelly Mahler is an OT based in Hershey PA, holds OTD, and has been practising ~20 years
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple independent sources (LinkedIn, OT Potential directory, Theramoves, AEP Connections, kelly-mahler.com about page) confirm OTD from Misericordia University, OTR/L licensure, Hershey PA, ~20 years OT experience starting ~2002.
- **Sources**: kelly-mahler.com/about-kelly/, otpotential.com directory, theramoves.com presenter page
- **Notes**: The OTD vs. MS apparent variance is publication-period: she completed the doctorate after publishing earlier work as MS-OTR/L. She is also adjunct faculty at Elizabethtown College and Misericordia University.

### Claim 2: *Interoception: The Eighth Sensory System* (2015, AAPC Publishing) is K-12 / autism-framed and is not adult-targeted
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: ResearchGate, Springer review (J. Autism Dev. Disord. 2017), Goodreads, multiple booksellers confirm: published 2015 (not 2017 — minor correction), AAPC Publishing, ISBN 978-1-942197-14-0, 184–186 pp, $29.95 paperback. Foreword by A. D. (Bud) Craig. Subtitle is *Practical Solutions for Improving Self-Regulation, Self-Awareness and Social Understanding of Individuals with Autism Spectrum and Related Disorders*. Springer review treats it as a clinician/educator book with autism focus.
- **Notes**: Research draft said "2015/2017" — the original publication is 2015. The book is not exclusively K-12 but is autism-framed and clinician-targeted; "adult application" is not a structural feature of the book. A 90%-of-autistic-adults-under/unemployed statistic appears in the introduction, raising an adult relevance question, but the book's exercises and case studies are predominantly pediatric-flavoured. Confirmed as predominantly clinician-targeted, autism-focused, not adult-native.

### Claim 3: *My Interoception Workbook* (2019) is co-authored with Chloe Rothschild and Jarvis Alma and is designed for adolescents/teens/adults
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Google Books listing, kelly-mahler.com product page, Autism Awareness Centre listing, OCALI library catalog, Toledo Parent column all confirm: co-authors Kelly Mahler, Chloe Rothschild, Jarvis Alma. Published 2019 (some listings reference subsequent updates). Three sections, 18 chapters. $39 USD.
- **Notes**: Confirmed self-advocate co-authorship is a structural feature of this product. The workbook explicitly states it is *not* a curriculum for building interoceptive awareness — confirmed. The recommendation to use it as a precursor to *The Interoception Curriculum* is on the kelly-mahler.com product page. Confirmed.

### Claim 4: *The Interoception Curriculum* claims to be adaptable preschool-to-adult; was not designed in adult populations; has no published adult research
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Product FAQ states "can be adapted for preschoolers through adults." Published research (Hample/Mahler/Amspacher 2020 pilot; Mahler 2022; Mahler et al. 2024) all use children/adolescent populations in special-education school settings. PubMed search yields no adult-population intervention study under Mahler's authorship.
- **Notes**: One published adult-self-learner testimonial appears on the product page (an autistic adult describing the curriculum as "a game changer"). No peer-reviewed adult intervention data.

### Claim 5: Mahler's published research is exclusively with autistic children in school settings
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED with one nuance
- **Evidence**: PubMed search confirms the Hample/Mahler/Amspacher 2020 pilot, the Mahler 2022 *OTI* paper (one-group pre-post, N=14, ages 9–19), and the Mahler 2024 *OT in Health Care* feasibility paper are all pediatric. The Mahler/McLaughlin/Anson 2020 *AJOT* paper "Interoception Across Varying Degrees of Mental Wellness" is a cross-sectional descriptive study, not an intervention.
- **Nuance**: The *AJOT* 2020 paper may include adult participants given its "varying degrees of mental wellness" framing, but it is descriptive and not an intervention trial of the Curriculum. The intervention research is pediatric-only.
- **Notes**: This was stated correctly in the concept research.

### Claim 6: There is no published RCT of Mahler's Interoception Curriculum
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED (as of 2026-04-29)
- **Evidence**: PubMed and journal database searches do not surface a published RCT of the curriculum. Mahler's research page lists "Randomized control trial using The Interoception Curriculum with school aged children" as in development, suggesting one is planned/underway but not yet reported.
- **Notes**: A scoping review (PMC11650107, "Interoception and its application to paediatric occupational therapy") classified existing Mahler studies as moderate risk of bias in outcome measurement.

### Claim 7: The 2022 Mahler study used a one-group pre-post design with N=14, not an RCT
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: PubMed abstract and full Wiley OTI paper confirm one-group pre-post design, N=14 (11 male, 3 female), ages 9–19, self-contained special-education classroom.
- **Notes**: This was stated correctly.

### Claim 8: Garfinkel et al. 2015 *Biological Psychology* established the three-component model (accuracy, sensibility, awareness)
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple independent sources confirm "Knowing your own heart: distinguishing interoceptive accuracy from interoceptive awareness," *Biological Psychology* 104, 65–74, by Garfinkel, Seth, Barrett, Suzuki, Critchley. The 2016 follow-up "Making sense of what you sense" (Garfinkel & Critchley) extends to sensibility.
- **Notes**: Foundational citation, well-established.

### Claim 9: Bud Craig's anterior insula work (2002 *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*; 2009 *NRN*) established the AIC as substrate for interoception and integrated awareness
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Both papers are foundational and widely cited. Craig's 2009 *NRN* paper *How do you feel — now? The anterior insula and human awareness* is the canonical citation for the AIC-awareness link.
- **Notes**: Mahler's book has Craig as a foreword author, which is a publicly acknowledged endorsement / collaboration.

### Claim 10: Aaron 2020 found a quadratic relationship between alexithymia and interoceptive accuracy, with the mindfulness pilot showing no group difference
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Aaron, Snodgress, Blain, Park (2020) *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, N=76 young adults, heartbeat detection task, TAS-20, brief mindfulness body-scan vs. control. Quadratic relationship significant (p=0.002); linear relationship between sensibility and alexithymia (p=0.040). The pilot intervention did not produce a group difference favouring mindfulness over control on outcome measures.
- **Notes**: The author order in the Frontiers article is Aaron, Snodgress, Blain, Park (the IFS Cat 6 page lists them as "Aaron, Snodgress, Blain, Park" — verified). PMC ID PMC7076086.

### Claim 11: Shah, Hall, Catmur, Bird (2016) *Cortex* established that alexithymia, not autism, drives interoceptive impairment
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: PubMed ID 27253723. Title is "Alexithymia, not autism, is associated with impaired interoception." *Cortex* 81, 215–220.
- **Notes**: Same finding load-bearing for IFS Cat 6 page; consistent across both pages.

### Claim 12: The CAIA-3 (Comprehensive Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness, 3rd ed.) is Mahler's assessment tool with an adult version
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (6/10) — exists but published psychometric validation is weak/unclear
- **Evidence for**: kelly-mahler.com product page describes the CAIA as having three sub-assessments (Interoceptive Awareness Interview self-report, Assessment of Self-Regulation picture-card self-report, Caregiver Questionnaire). Adult version is sold.
- **Evidence against**: Independent search did not surface peer-reviewed psychometric validation papers for the CAIA in mainstream databases. By contrast, the MAIA-2 (Mehling et al. — a *different* instrument) is well-validated with multiple peer-reviewed studies, including the Brief MAIA-2 in *Scientific Reports*. The CAIA appears to be a clinical/practitioner-distributed instrument rather than a research-validated psychometric scale.
- **Why uncertain**: The product exists; its peer-reviewed psychometric validation does not appear to be published. The page should distinguish "Mahler's CAIA" from "the research-validated MAIA-2" to avoid misleading the user.
- **Sources**: kelly-mahler.com/product/the-comprehensive-assessment-of-interoceptive-awareness-3rd-edition/, MAIA-2 publications (Mehling 2018)

### Claim 13: Mahler's framing is neurodiversity-affirming and not deficit-focused
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED with caveats
- **Evidence**: Independent reading of her interoception-and-neurodiversity page confirms explicit anti-deficit framing: "there is no wrong way to feel," critique of teaching that anxiety/hunger feels a certain way, "undoing before doing," reference to autistic clients having had "a lifetime of having their inner experience being misunderstood, mislabeled, or gaslighted." She centres autistic self-advocates (Rothschild, Alma).
- **Caveat**: The framing is affirming; the empirical research base is still pediatric and evaluates "improvement" using caregiver-report measures (CQIA-2) and executive-function measures (BRIEF-2), which carry their own framing assumptions. The framing is genuine but the research instruments are conventional.

### Claim 14: Mahler's curriculum operates on a "more awareness is better" model and does not engage Aaron 2020's quadratic finding
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Independent searches of Mahler's site, articles, and interview transcripts show no discussion of Aaron 2020 specifically. Her adverse-effects page (Adverse Aspects of Interoceptive Exposure) addresses a different therapy modality (Interoceptive Exposure Therapy, the panic-disorder treatment) — not body-scan or mindfulness training, and not the Aaron 2020 quadratic risk.
- **Notes**: This is a real gap in her materials. The page should preserve this finding clearly.

### Claim 15: Mahler engages harm risk publicly via her "Adverse Aspects of Interoceptive Exposure" page
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED, but the original concept research understated this
- **Evidence**: kelly-mahler.com/what-is-interoception/interoception-and-mindfulness/adverse-aspects-of-interoceptive-exposure/ identifies four risks of Interoceptive Exposure Therapy: desensitization, physical risks (seizures, cardiac, asthma/lung), temporary effects (research suggesting exposure therapy generally lacks long-lasting effect), and limited transfer to real-world conditions.
- **What this DOES address**: Interoceptive Exposure Therapy (the panic-disorder treatment that intentionally provokes distressing body sensations).
- **What this does NOT address**: Body-scan harms in trauma populations, Aaron 2020 quadratic risk, the Lindahl/Britton meditation-related-difficulties literature.
- **Correction**: The concept research said Mahler's materials "do not formally report adverse-event rates for body-scan-style attention practices in trauma populations." This is correct, but the research understated her engagement with adverse-effects discussion generally. The explanation should clarify that she does engage harm publicly *but* about a different modality (IE) — and that this nonetheless leaves the body-scan / mindfulness / interoception-training risks untreated.

### Claim 16: Roughly 25–30% of Mahler's catalog is genuinely adult-targeted; 70–75% is pediatric being recommended for adults
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (7/10) — directionally right, exact split estimable but imprecise
- **Evidence for**: Independent inventory confirms only 2 products (My Interoception Workbook, Lens-of-an-Autistic-Adult course) are designed-for-adults from inception. The CAIA-3 has an adult version. The Non-Speaking Client course is adult-applicable. The Curriculum, Activity Cards, Yoga/Exercise Cards, Big Book of Games, children's books, and most courses are pediatric-rooted.
- **Why uncertain**: The exact percentage depends on how products are counted (course bundles vs. individual courses; major products vs. minor) and on how "adult-targeted" is defined (designed-for-adults vs. adult-applicable). The directional claim — "minority adult-targeted, majority pediatric being recommended for adults" — is robust. The exact percentage is not.
- **Recommendation**: Use language like "a minority of her catalog is adult-targeted; the majority is pediatric content recommended for adult use" rather than committing to a specific percentage.

### Claim 17: Mahler is co-PI on research projects
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED (as her self-description) but not independently verifiable in detail
- **Evidence**: kelly-mahler.com/about-kelly/ describes her as "a co-principal investigator in several research projects pertaining to topics such as interoception, self-regulation, trauma & autism."
- **Notes**: Her published peer-reviewed papers list her as co-author with Hample, Amspacher, McLaughlin, Anson. Whether she holds formal CO-PI status on funded grants is not independently verifiable in the search results. The claim is reasonable; the page can present it without overstating her academic role.

### Claim 18: The Interoception Activity Cards have no separate adult version — they are one deck described as "preschool through adulthood"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Product page describes 170 cards with no age differentiation. Separate adult deck does not exist.
- **Notes**: User asked specifically — this is a clean "no, there is no separate adult version of the Activity Cards."

## Research Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: Peer-reviewed psychometric validation of CAIA-3
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: Claim 12 (UNCERTAIN 6/10)
- **Why**: The CAIA is sold as an assessment but appears not to have peer-reviewed psychometric validation. The research-validated alternative (MAIA-2 by Mehling) should be named for the user.
- **Suggested queries**: "Mahler CAIA psychometric validation reliability," "Comprehensive Assessment Interoceptive Awareness reliability validity"
- **Expected impact**: Allow clean recommendation language about which assessment to actually use.

### Opportunity 2: Adult-population research on the Interoception Curriculum
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Driven by**: Claims 4, 5, 6 (all CONFIRMED that no adult research exists)
- **Why**: The user's profile (autistic adult, alexithymia) is precisely the population not yet researched. Confirming this gap is final, not merely "not yet found," strengthens the page's honesty.
- **Suggested queries**: "Interoception Curriculum adult RCT," "Mahler Curriculum adult feasibility 2025 2026"
- **Expected impact**: Page can definitively state "no adult evidence exists as of [date]."

### Opportunity 3: Explicit Mahler engagement with Aaron 2020 / mindfulness harms
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: Claim 14 (CONFIRMED gap), Claim 15 (clarification)
- **Why**: Confirming the gap definitively (no Mahler material engages Aaron 2020 or body-scan trauma harms) lets the page state this without hedging.
- **Suggested queries**: "Mahler Aaron 2020 quadratic," "Mahler body scan harm trauma"
- **Expected impact**: Strengthens the honest-fit-assessment section.

### Opportunity 4: Adult-specific clinical training in Mahler's certification
- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: Coverage gap — research did not specify whether her clinician trainings include adult-clinical-specific modules
- **Why**: User asked whether her certification includes adult clinical work; current research only confirms her workshops include adult content but does not detail the certification structure.
- **Suggested queries**: "Mahler interoception certification adult clinician module"
- **Expected impact**: Allow more concrete user-action recommendation.

### Opportunity 5: User reports / community feedback from autistic adults
- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: Coverage gap — limited testimonial data from autistic adults specifically
- **Why**: Autism-affirming framing is one thing; lived experience of autistic adults using the curriculum is another data layer.
- **Suggested queries**: "autistic adult Mahler Interoception Curriculum review experience"
- **Expected impact**: Adds qualitative ground-truth layer.

## Recommendations for Downstream Agents

The explanation should:
- State Mahler's credentials (OTD Misericordia, OTR/L, Hershey PA, ~20 years OT, adjunct at Elizabethtown and Misericordia) without overstating them.
- Clearly distinguish her clinician-educator status from research-scientist status.
- Inventory her catalog honestly with the adult-vs-pediatric split front and centre.
- Name the two genuinely adult-targeted core products (Workbook 2019; Lens-of-an-Autistic-Adult course) and the two adult-applicable products (CAIA-3 adult; Non-Speaking Client course).
- Be precise that the Interoception Curriculum is K-12-rooted and "adapted" to adults via buyer modification, with one published adult testimonial and zero adult research.
- Engage the Aaron 2020 quadratic finding directly and note that Mahler's materials do not address it.
- Acknowledge Mahler's adverse-effects-of-IE page but clarify it addresses a different modality (Interoceptive Exposure Therapy, not body scan / mindfulness training).
- Clearly distinguish the CAIA from the research-validated MAIA-2 — mention both, recommend the MAIA-2 when an adult wants a research-validated self-report measure.
- Use language like "a minority of her catalog is adult-targeted" rather than committing to a specific percentage.
- Not present the framing as "validated for adults" — it isn't. Present it as "clinically used with adults; not researched in adults."
