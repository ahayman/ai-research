## Major Commercial Products

### 1. Proloquo2Go (AssistiveWare)
- **Platform**: iOS only (iPhone, iPad, Mac)
- **Price**: $249.99 (one-time); Mac $124.99; 50% school bulk discount
- **Type**: Symbol-based AAC
- **Target**: Children and adults, beginning to advanced communicators
- **Key Features**:
  - 100+ natural-sounding voices
  - 23 pre-programmed grid layouts (9-144 buttons per page)
  - True bilingual code-switching (English/Spanish mid-sentence)
  - English, Spanish, French, Dutch
  - Switch scanning, direct selection
  - Folder-based vocabulary with core words on main page
- **Strengths**: Industry gold standard, strong SLP community, excellent customization, free for SLP evaluators
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, expensive, complex setup for families, no Android
- **Rating**: ~4.5/5 App Store

### 2. Proloquo (AssistiveWare) - Next Generation
- **Platform**: iOS (iPhone, iPad)
- **Price**: $249.99
- **Type**: Symbol-based AAC (next-gen design)
- **Target**: Nonspeaking people, families, educators
- **Key Features**: Research-based design, paired with Proloquo Coach companion app
- **Strengths**: Won 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award (first AAC app), modern design
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, still establishing adoption alongside Proloquo2Go
- **Note**: Coexists with Proloquo2Go (no plans to discontinue P2G)

### 3. TouchChat HD with WordPower (PRC-Saltillo)
- **Platform**: iOS only
- **Price**: $299.99 with WordPower / $149.99 without
- **Type**: Symbol-based AAC
- **Target**: Autism, Down Syndrome, ALS, apraxia, stroke
- **Key Features**:
  - WordPower vocabulary by SLP Nancy Inman
  - 40,000+ SymbolStix symbols
  - 45+ button actions
  - Head tracking (Face ID devices)
  - Built-in switch scanning
  - English, Spanish, French, Mandarin
- **Strengths**: Budget-friendlier than Proloquo2Go, simpler interface, comprehensive vocabulary
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, no true bilingual code-switching, complex customization

### 4. LAMP Words for Life (PRC-Saltillo)
- **Platform**: iOS only
- **Price**: $299.99
- **Type**: Motor planning-based AAC (LAMP approach + Minspeak/Unity)
- **Target**: Users who benefit from consistent motor patterns
- **Key Features**:
  - Language Acquisition through Motor Planning (LAMP™)
  - Unity® vocabulary system
  - Standard 84-location grid
  - Direct select, AeroSwitch scanning, head tracking
  - Word Finder and Vocabulary Builder
- **Strengths**: Unique motor planning approach, consistent button locations, strong for motor learners
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, expensive, reported glitches/iPad slowdown, steep learning curve

### 5. TD Snap (Tobii Dynavox)
- **Platform**: iOS (iPad), Windows, Tobii Dynavox devices
- **Price**: Subscription-based (after 1-month trial); Enterprise $149.99 one-time
- **Type**: Symbol-based with multiple page sets
- **Target**: Emerging to advanced communicators, including aphasia
- **Key Features**:
  - Page sets: Core First, Motor Plan, Express, Text, Scanning, Aphasia
  - PODD and Gateway via in-app purchase
  - Eye gaze, touch, and switch access
  - Google Assistant integration
  - True bilingual code-switching support
- **Strengths**: Versatile page sets, eye gaze native support, integration with Tobii hardware
- **Weaknesses**: Subscription model controversial, Tobii ecosystem lock-in

### 6. Grid (Smartbox/Thinksmartbox)
- **Platform**: iOS, Windows
- **Price**: £9.99/mo or £299.99 lifetime (iPad); 30-day free trial
- **Type**: Symbol + text-based
- **Target**: Wide range of communicators
- **Key Features**:
  - Eye gaze support
  - Environment control
  - Computer access features
  - Multiple access methods (touch, eye gaze, switches, pointer)
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive environment control, eye gaze native, computer access integration
- **Weaknesses**: British English default confuses US users, audio bugs reported after updates, complex setup
- **Rating**: 3.7/5 App Store

### 7. Avaz AAC (Avaz Inc, India)
- **Platform**: iOS AND Android
- **Price**: Free 14-day trial → $9.99/mo, $99.99/yr, or $199.99 lifetime
- **Type**: Symbol-based
- **Target**: Children and adults, especially Indian market
- **Key Features**:
  - 6+ Indian languages (Hindi, Tamil, Kannada, Malayalam, Telugu, Marathi + Gujarati, Bengali)
  - 40,000+ SymbolStix pictures
  - Picture mode + Keyboard mode with prediction
  - YouTube integration, GIFs
  - Built-in Avaz Dashboard tutorial
  - Fitzgerald key color coding
- **Strengths**: Cross-platform, affordable, multilingual Indian support, built-in training, MIT Top 35 Innovation
- **Weaknesses**: Speech speed/pitch controls limited, less established in US market

### 8. Spoken
- **Platform**: iOS AND Android AND Mac
- **Price**: Free basic → $12.99/mo, $99.99/yr, or $249.99 lifetime (iOS); $6.99/mo, $59.99/yr, or $99.99 lifetime (Android)
- **Type**: Text-based with AI prediction
- **Target**: Adults with acquired conditions (ALS, aphasia, stroke)
- **Key Features**:
  - AI-powered predictive text that learns from user
  - Natural-sounding text-to-speech
  - Adapts to individual communication patterns
  - Not limited to preset vocabulary
- **Strengths**: AI-powered, cross-platform, learns from user, good for literate adults
- **Weaknesses**: Text-based only (not suitable for pre-literate users), smaller symbol vocabulary

### 9. Fluent AAC
- **Platform**: iOS only
- **Price**: Not publicly listed (likely subscription)
- **Type**: Symbol-based with grammar prediction
- **Target**: Beginning to advanced communicators
- **Key Features**:
  - Word prediction with grammar conjugation
  - 3,000+ fringe word vocabulary
  - "Hide Words" feature (start with one word, grow gradually)
  - Words never change position (motor consistency)
  - Customizable vocabulary levels
- **Strengths**: #1 rated by AAC users in surveys, innovative grammar features, motor consistency
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, newer/smaller company, limited public pricing info

### 10. APP2Speak
- **Platform**: iOS only
- **Price**: $199.99 (one-time)
- **Type**: Photo-based AAC
- **Target**: Adults with speech impairments (ALS, stroke)
- **Key Features**:
  - Customization with user's own photos
  - Text-to-speech
  - Unlimited custom vocabulary
  - Compatible with iOS accessibility (Bluetooth switches, touch accommodations)
- **Strengths**: Highly personalized, affordable for adults, praised by SLPs
- **Weaknesses**: iOS only, less clinical research backing, smaller user base

## Open Source / Free Options

### 11. Cboard (UNICEF-backed)
- **Platform**: Web, Android, iOS
- **Price**: Free
- **Type**: Symbol-based
- **Key Features**:
  - 40-45+ languages
  - 20,000+ symbols from Global Symbols database
  - Browser-based Speech Synthesis API
  - Fully customizable boards
- **Strengths**: Free, massive language coverage, UNICEF backing, cross-platform web app, Microsoft AI for Accessibility grant
- **Weaknesses**: Less polished than commercial options, limited offline capability, no AI prediction

### 12. CoughDrop (now Forbes AAC/MavWare)
- **Platform**: Web, iOS, Android
- **Price**: $9/mo or $295 lifetime
- **Key Features**: Web-based, customizable boards, cloud sync
- **Strengths**: Was fully open source, web-based cross-platform
- **Weaknesses**: Open source license changes after Forbes AAC acquisition, community trust concerns
- **Note**: Previously open-sourced code still available; new versions proprietary

### 13. OpenAAC Sweet Suite
- **Platform**: Web
- **Price**: Free / open source
- **GitHub**: github.com/open-aac/sweet-suite-aac
- **Strengths**: Fully open source, community-driven
- **Weaknesses**: Less mature, smaller user base

### 14. Weave Chat AAC
- **Platform**: Android, iOS
- **Price**: Free
- **Strengths**: Totally free, accessible
- **Weaknesses**: Gaps in pre-made vocabulary, limitations on custom button additions

## Market Opportunity Analysis

### 1. Android-First/Cross-Platform (HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Most major apps are iOS-only. Android has ~72% global market share. Only Avaz, Spoken, and free apps serve Android well.

### 2. Adult/Aging Population (HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Most apps designed for children. Aging population with acquired conditions (stroke, ALS, Parkinson's, dementia) is growing rapidly. Aphasia alone affects ~2M Americans.

### 3. AI-Native Communication (HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Current AI integration is minimal. LLM-powered prediction, context-aware suggestions, conversational AI, personality modeling are all frontier opportunities.

### 4. Affordable Pricing (MEDIUM-HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
29% face affordability issues. Freemium models with progressive features could capture underserved market. Web-first approach reduces distribution costs.

### 5. Multilingual/Global Markets (HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Only Avaz serves Indian languages. Most apps limited to English + few European languages. Asia-Pacific and Latin America are fastest-growing regions.

### 6. Simplified Onboarding (MEDIUM OPPORTUNITY)
Complex setup is universal complaint. AI-powered onboarding, progressive disclosure, guided setup wizards could differentiate.

### 7. Education Integration (HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Education got F grade in OpenAAC GPA. Classroom tools, IEP integration, progress tracking for educators are underserved.

### 8. Interoperability & Data Portability (MEDIUM OPPORTUNITY)
No standards exist. Users locked into ecosystems. Open data formats could attract users frustrated with lock-in.

### 9. Wearable/Smartwatch AAC (EMERGING OPPORTUNITY)
No current AAC solutions for wearables. Quick-access communication on smartwatch could complement phone/tablet.

### 10. Communication Partner Training (MEDIUM OPPORTUNITY)
33% of caregivers lack training. Digital training platforms, AI coaching for partners could fill gap.

### 11. Web-First Architecture (MEDIUM-HIGH OPPORTUNITY)
Web apps work across all platforms, lower development cost, no app store fees. Cboard proves model works but lacks polish.
