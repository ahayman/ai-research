# Complete Guide to US Presidential Terms: 1789-Present

From George Washington's precedent-setting first inauguration on April 30, 1789, to Donald Trump's historic return to office in 2025, the American presidency has evolved through 69 distinct terms. This comprehensive guide explores every presidential term in United States history, documenting the triumphs, tragedies, controversies, and transformations that have shaped the nation's highest office.

The presidency has been held by 45 individuals across these 69 terms, with Grover Cleveland and Donald Trump each serving non-consecutive terms. Eight presidents died in office, four by assassination and four by natural causes, while Richard Nixon remains the only president to resign. Through wars and peace, prosperity and depression, these leaders have guided America through its most defining moments.

## Term 1: George Washington (1789-1793)

**President:** George Washington
**Term Dates:** April 30, 1789 - March 4, 1793
**Term Length:** 3 years, 10 months, 4 days
**Political Party:** Unaffiliated (Independent)
**Vice President:** John Adams

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** February 22, 1732 (Westmoreland County, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** December 14, 1799 (age 67)
- **Age During Term:** 57-60 years old

**Summary:**
George Washington's first term established virtually every precedent for the American presidency. As the unanimous choice of the Electoral College, Washington faced the daunting task of defining an entirely new office while leading a fragile young nation. He created the Cabinet system, established executive branch protocols, and navigated the young republic through its formative years.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Established the Cabinet system with departments of State, Treasury, and War
- Signed the Judiciary Act of 1789, creating the federal court system
- Oversaw ratification of the Bill of Rights (1791)
- Created the First Bank of the United States
- Established precedent of presidential neutrality in foreign affairs

**Historical Events:**
- First presidential inauguration in New York City
- Bill of Rights ratified (December 15, 1791)
- Whiskey Rebellion began (1791)
- Capital moved from New York to Philadelphia

**Controversies:**
- Critics accused him of monarchical tendencies and excessive formality
- The Whiskey Rebellion tested federal authority over states
- Partisan newspapers began attacking his administration

**Popularity:**
Washington remained universally revered throughout his first term. His decision to serve was seen as essential to the republic's survival, and his careful approach to executive power earned widespread respect.

## Term 2: George Washington (1793-1797)

**President:** George Washington
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1793 - March 4, 1797
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Unaffiliated (Independent)
**Vice President:** John Adams

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** February 22, 1732
- **Death Date:** December 14, 1799
- **Age During Term:** 61-64 years old

**Summary:**
Washington's second term proved more challenging as partisan divisions deepened between Federalists and Democratic-Republicans. He successfully navigated neutrality during the French Revolutionary Wars and suppressed the Whiskey Rebellion, demonstrating federal authority while avoiding tyranny.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Neutrality Proclamation (1793) kept US out of European wars
- Jay Treaty (1795) normalized relations with Britain
- Pinckney's Treaty (1795) secured navigation rights on the Mississippi
- Whiskey Rebellion suppressed without bloodshed (1794)
- Established two-term precedent by voluntarily retiring

**Historical Events:**
- French Revolutionary Wars created pressure to take sides
- Whiskey Rebellion suppressed (1794)
- Farewell Address warned against political parties and foreign entanglements

**Controversies:**
- Jay Treaty criticized as too favorable to Britain
- Partisan press attacks intensified, with some calling him a tyrant
- Growing tension between Hamilton and Jefferson factions

**Popularity:**
Despite increasing partisan attacks, Washington remained the most respected figure in America. His voluntary retirement after two terms established the precedent that lasted until FDR and became constitutional with the 22nd Amendment.

## Term 3: John Adams (1797-1801)

**President:** John Adams
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1797 - March 4, 1801
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Federalist
**Vice President:** Thomas Jefferson (Democratic-Republican)

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** October 30, 1735 (Braintree, Massachusetts)
- **Death Date:** July 4, 1826 (age 90)
- **Age During Term:** 61-65 years old

**Summary:**
John Adams' single term was dominated by foreign policy crises and growing partisan warfare. As the only time in history that a president and vice president were from opposing parties, his administration was marked by tension with Jefferson and his own party. Adams' greatest achievement was avoiding a full-scale war with France.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Avoided war with France through skilled diplomacy
- Convention of 1800 ended the Quasi-War
- Established the US Navy Department (1798)
- Appointed John Marshall as Chief Justice

**Historical Events:**
- XYZ Affair inflamed anti-French sentiment (1798)
- Quasi-War with France (1798-1800)
- Capital moved to Washington, D.C. (1800)
- First president to live in the White House

**Controversies:**
- Alien and Sedition Acts severely restricted civil liberties
- Deeply unpopular with Democratic-Republicans
- Conflicts within his own Federalist Party

**Popularity:**
Adams was a polarizing figure who lost his re-election bid to Jefferson. His principled stance for peace over war cost him politically but preserved the young nation from a potentially disastrous conflict.

**Notable:** Adams and Jefferson died on the same day, July 4, 1826, the 50th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence. Adams' last words were reportedly "Thomas Jefferson survives," not knowing Jefferson had died hours earlier.

## Term 4: Thomas Jefferson (1801-1805)

**President:** Thomas Jefferson
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1801 - March 4, 1805
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** Aaron Burr

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** April 13, 1743 (Shadwell, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** July 4, 1826 (age 83)
- **Age During Term:** 57-61 years old

**Summary:**
Jefferson's first term is defined by the Louisiana Purchase, which doubled the nation's size for just $15 million. His "Revolution of 1800" marked the first peaceful transfer of power between opposing parties, proving American democracy could survive partisan transitions.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Louisiana Purchase (1803) acquired 828,000 square miles for $15 million
- Authorized Lewis and Clark Expedition
- Reduced national debt and military spending
- Repealed Alien and Sedition Acts

**Historical Events:**
- Marbury v. Madison (1803) established judicial review
- Louisiana Purchase Treaty signed
- Lewis and Clark departed St. Louis (1804)

**Controversies:**
- Vice President Aaron Burr killed Alexander Hamilton in a duel (1804)
- Louisiana Purchase arguably exceeded constitutional authority
- Maintained slavery while proclaiming liberty

**Popularity:**
Jefferson was immensely popular, winning re-election in a landslide. His vision of an agrarian republic and his reduction of federal power resonated with most Americans.

**Notable:** Jefferson was the principal author of the Declaration of Independence and died on its 50th anniversary.

## Term 5: Thomas Jefferson (1805-1809)

**President:** Thomas Jefferson
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1805 - March 4, 1809
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** George Clinton

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** April 13, 1743
- **Death Date:** July 4, 1826
- **Age During Term:** 61-65 years old

**Summary:**
Jefferson's second term was marred by foreign policy challenges, particularly British impressment of American sailors. His Embargo Act of 1807, intended as economic warfare, proved disastrous for the American economy and deeply unpopular.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Banned the international slave trade (effective January 1, 1808)
- Lewis and Clark returned with valuable western knowledge
- Maintained peace despite British and French provocations

**Historical Events:**
- Embargo Act of 1807 attempted economic coercion
- Burr Conspiracy and trial (1807)
- Chesapeake-Leopard Affair (1807)

**Controversies:**
- Embargo Act economically devastating, especially to New England
- Perceived as executive overreach by critics
- Failed to resolve impressment issue

**Popularity:**
Jefferson's popularity declined significantly in his second term due to the embargo's economic pain. He followed Washington's two-term precedent, declining to seek a third term.

## Term 6: James Madison (1809-1813)

**President:** James Madison
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1809 - March 4, 1813
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** George Clinton (died April 1812)

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** March 16, 1751 (Port Conway, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** June 28, 1836 (age 85)
- **Age During Term:** 57-61 years old

**Summary:**
Madison inherited the foreign policy crises that plagued Jefferson's second term. Unable to resolve disputes with Britain through diplomacy, he led the nation into the War of 1812, the first declared war in American history.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Led nation into War of 1812 to defend national honor
- Continued development of western territories

**Historical Events:**
- War of 1812 declared (June 1812)
- Vice President Clinton died in office
- Early naval victories boosted morale

**Controversies:**
- Nation poorly prepared for war
- Military setbacks in early campaigns
- Federalist opposition to "Mr. Madison's War"

**Popularity:**
Despite early military failures, Madison won re-election in 1812 as Americans rallied around the flag.

**Notable:** Madison is known as the "Father of the Constitution" and was the shortest president at 5'4".

## Term 7: James Madison (1813-1817)

**President:** James Madison
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1813 - March 4, 1817
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** Elbridge Gerry (died November 1814)

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** March 16, 1751
- **Death Date:** June 28, 1836
- **Age During Term:** 61-65 years old

**Summary:**
Madison's second term saw the dramatic conclusion of the War of 1812, including the burning of Washington and Andrew Jackson's triumph at New Orleans. The war's end ushered in the "Era of Good Feelings" and national unity.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Successfully concluded War of 1812
- Treaty of Ghent (1814) restored pre-war boundaries
- Presided over beginning of "Era of Good Feelings"
- Second Bank of the United States chartered (1816)

**Historical Events:**
- British burned Washington, D.C. (August 1814)
- Star-Spangled Banner written during Baltimore bombardment
- Battle of New Orleans (January 1815)

**Controversies:**
- Fled Washington during British invasion
- Second VP died in office
- Questions about war's necessity given status quo ante peace

**Popularity:**
Madison emerged from the war with enhanced prestige as national pride surged. The Federalist Party collapsed due to its opposition to the popular war.

**Notable:** Madison was the last surviving signer of the Constitution, dying in 1836.

## Term 8: James Monroe (1817-1821)

**President:** James Monroe
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1817 - March 4, 1821
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** Daniel D. Tompkins

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** April 28, 1758 (Westmoreland County, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** July 4, 1831 (age 73)
- **Age During Term:** 58-62 years old

**Summary:**
Monroe's first term epitomized the "Era of Good Feelings," a period of relative political harmony following the collapse of the Federalist Party. He presided over significant territorial expansion and the contentious Missouri Compromise.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Acquisition of Florida from Spain (Adams-Onis Treaty, 1819)
- Missouri Compromise (1820) temporarily resolved slavery expansion debate
- First Seminole War concluded
- Began national "goodwill tour"

**Historical Events:**
- Panic of 1819 caused economic hardship
- Missouri Compromise debate
- Florida Purchase

**Controversies:**
- Missouri Compromise controversial in both North and South
- Panic of 1819 brought first major depression

**Popularity:**
Monroe was extremely popular, embodying national unity during the Era of Good Feelings.

**Notable:** Monroe was the last "Founding Father President" and the third president to die on July 4.

## Term 9: James Monroe (1821-1825)

**President:** James Monroe
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1821 - March 4, 1825
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican
**Vice President:** Daniel D. Tompkins

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** April 28, 1758
- **Death Date:** July 4, 1831
- **Age During Term:** 62-66 years old

**Summary:**
Monroe's second term is best remembered for the Monroe Doctrine, which declared the Western Hemisphere closed to future European colonization. This became a cornerstone of American foreign policy for over a century.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Monroe Doctrine proclaimed (December 1823)
- Recognized Latin American independence movements
- Maintained national unity despite sectional tensions

**Historical Events:**
- Monroe Doctrine announced
- Lafayette's triumphal American tour (1824)
- Growing sectional tensions over slavery

**Controversies:**
- Little significant opposition; ran virtually unopposed for re-election
- Monroe Doctrine had limited immediate impact but lasting legacy

**Popularity:**
Monroe won re-election in 1820 with only one electoral vote against him (and that was reportedly cast to preserve Washington's unanimous honor).

## Term 10: John Quincy Adams (1825-1829)

**President:** John Quincy Adams
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1825 - March 4, 1829
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic-Republican / National Republican
**Vice President:** John C. Calhoun

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** July 11, 1767 (Braintree, Massachusetts)
- **Death Date:** February 23, 1848 (age 80)
- **Age During Term:** 57-61 years old

**Summary:**
John Quincy Adams, son of John Adams, won the contested 1824 election when no candidate received an Electoral College majority and the House chose him. Accusations of a "corrupt bargain" with Henry Clay haunted his entire presidency.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Promoted internal improvements (roads, canals)
- Erie Canal completed during his term (1825)
- Supported the arts and sciences

**Historical Events:**
- Erie Canal opened, transforming commerce
- Contested 1824 election decided by House
- Birth of Jacksonian Democracy in opposition

**Controversies:**
- "Corrupt Bargain" accusations (alleged deal with Henry Clay for Secretary of State position)
- Opposition blocked most initiatives
- Seen as elitist by Jacksonians

**Popularity:**
Adams was very unpopular, losing decisively to Jackson in 1828. However, he later served 17 distinguished years in the House of Representatives.

**Notable:** Adams is one of only two presidents whose father was also president (the other being George W. Bush).

## Term 11: Andrew Jackson (1829-1833)

**President:** Andrew Jackson
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1829 - March 4, 1833
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** John C. Calhoun (resigned December 1832)

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** March 15, 1767 (Waxhaws, Carolina border)
- **Death Date:** June 8, 1845 (age 78)
- **Age During Term:** 61-65 years old

**Summary:**
Jackson's first term brought "Jacksonian Democracy" to Washington, expanding suffrage and challenging established elites. His war against the Second Bank of the United States and the Indian Removal Act defined his presidency's lasting impact.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Indian Removal Act (1830)
- Vetoed Bank recharter, beginning "Bank War"
- Rotation in office ("spoils system")
- Faced down Nullification Crisis

**Historical Events:**
- Petticoat Affair disrupted cabinet
- Indian Removal Act passed
- Nullification Crisis with South Carolina began

**Controversies:**
- Indian Removal Act led to Trail of Tears (humanitarian catastrophe)
- Petticoat Affair caused cabinet resignations
- Spoils system criticized as corruption

**Popularity:**
Jackson was enormously popular with common people, representing a new democratic era. He easily won re-election despite elite opposition.

**Notable:** Jackson was the first president from west of the Appalachians and survived an assassination attempt in 1835.

## Term 12: Andrew Jackson (1833-1837)

**President:** Andrew Jackson
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1833 - March 4, 1837
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** Martin Van Buren

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** March 15, 1767
- **Death Date:** June 8, 1845
- **Age During Term:** 65-69 years old

**Summary:**
Jackson's second term completed his destruction of the Second Bank of the United States and achieved something no other president has done: completely paying off the national debt. His policies, however, contributed to the Panic of 1837.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Paid off the entire national debt (only time in US history)
- Distributed surplus to states
- Defeated the Second Bank of the United States
- Issued Specie Circular requiring gold/silver for land purchases

**Historical Events:**
- Bank War concluded
- National debt eliminated (January 1835)
- Texas independence from Mexico (1836)

**Controversies:**
- Removal of federal deposits from Bank controversial
- Senate censured Jackson (later expunged)
- Specie Circular contributed to economic crisis

**Popularity:**
Jackson remained immensely popular and successfully chose his successor, Martin Van Buren.

**Notable:** Jackson is the only president to have completely paid off the national debt.

## Term 13: Martin Van Buren (1837-1841)

**President:** Martin Van Buren
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1837 - March 4, 1841
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** Richard M. Johnson

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** December 5, 1782 (Kinderhook, New York)
- **Death Date:** July 24, 1862 (age 79)
- **Age During Term:** 54-58 years old

**Summary:**
Van Buren inherited an economic catastrophe when the Panic of 1837 struck just weeks after his inauguration. Despite his political skills, he could not overcome public blame for the depression that lasted throughout his term.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Proposed Independent Treasury System
- Maintained neutrality in Canadian rebellion
- Established 10-hour workday for federal employees

**Historical Events:**
- Panic of 1837 began (major depression)
- Trail of Tears continued
- "Aroostook War" border dispute with Britain

**Controversies:**
- Blamed for depression ("Martin Van Ruin")
- Continued Jackson's Indian removal policies
- Failed to address slavery issue

**Popularity:**
Van Buren was deeply unpopular due to the economic crisis and lost his re-election bid to William Henry Harrison.

**Notable:** Van Buren was the first president born as a US citizen rather than a British subject.

## Term 14: William Henry Harrison (1841)

**President:** William Henry Harrison
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1841 - April 4, 1841
**Term Length:** 31 days (shortest in history)
**Political Party:** Whig
**Vice President:** John Tyler

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** February 9, 1773 (Berkeley, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** April 4, 1841 (age 68)
- **Age During Term:** 68 years old

**Summary:**
Harrison's presidency was the shortest in American history. He delivered the longest inaugural address in history (nearly two hours) on a cold, wet day without a coat, developed pneumonia, and died 31 days later.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- None (died before implementing any agenda)

**Historical Events:**
- First president to die in office
- Constitutional crisis over succession resolved by Tyler

**Controversies:**
- Questions about VP succession (Tyler established precedent)
- Medical care questioned by historians

**Popularity:**
Harrison was elected on the popular slogan "Tippecanoe and Tyler Too," referring to his military victory over Native Americans in 1811.

**Notable:** Harrison was the oldest president elected until Reagan and had the shortest presidency. His inaugural address remains the longest ever delivered.

## Term 15: John Tyler (1841-1845)

**President:** John Tyler
**Term Dates:** April 4, 1841 - March 4, 1845
**Term Length:** 3 years, 11 months
**Political Party:** Whig (expelled 1841); later Independent
**Vice President:** None

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** March 29, 1790 (Charles City County, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** January 18, 1862 (age 71)
- **Age During Term:** 51-54 years old

**Summary:**
Tyler's presidency was defined by his assertion that he was the full president, not merely an "acting president." This precedent shaped all subsequent successions. Expelled from his own party, Tyler governed without partisan support.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Established precedent that VP becomes full president upon death
- Webster-Ashburton Treaty resolved border disputes with Britain
- Texas annexation treaty signed (completed under Polk)

**Historical Events:**
- First VP to assume presidency
- Expulsion from Whig Party
- Texas annexation debates

**Controversies:**
- Entire cabinet (except Webster) resigned
- "His Accidency" nickname showed contempt
- Expelled from own party after vetoing Whig bills

**Popularity:**
Tyler was deeply unpopular with both parties and did not seek re-election.

**Notable:** Tyler later served in the Confederate Congress, the only president to hold office in a hostile foreign government.

## Term 16: James K. Polk (1845-1849)

**President:** James K. Polk
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1845 - March 4, 1849
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** George M. Dallas

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** November 2, 1795 (Pineville, North Carolina)
- **Death Date:** June 15, 1849 (age 53)
- **Age During Term:** 49-53 years old

**Summary:**
Polk is considered one of the most effective presidents, achieving all four of his major goals in a single term: acquiring California, settling the Oregon boundary, reducing tariffs, and establishing an independent treasury. He expanded US territory by 1.2 million square miles.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Mexican-American War victory
- Acquired California and the Southwest (Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo)
- Oregon Treaty established 49th parallel boundary with Britain
- Walker Tariff reduced duties
- Independent Treasury established

**Historical Events:**
- Mexican-American War (1846-1848)
- California Gold Rush began (1848)
- Oregon boundary dispute resolved

**Controversies:**
- Mexican-American War criticized as imperialist aggression
- Opponents called it "Mr. Polk's War"
- Expanded slavery territory

**Popularity:**
Polk kept his promise to serve only one term and achieved remarkable success despite controversy over the war.

**Notable:** Polk died just 103 days after leaving office, the shortest post-presidential life until that time.

## Term 17: Zachary Taylor (1849-1850)

**President:** Zachary Taylor
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1849 - July 9, 1850
**Term Length:** 1 year, 4 months, 5 days
**Political Party:** Whig
**Vice President:** Millard Fillmore

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** November 24, 1784 (Barboursville, Virginia)
- **Death Date:** July 9, 1850 (age 65)
- **Age During Term:** 64-65 years old

**Summary:**
Taylor, a hero of the Mexican-American War, brought an unexpected perspective to the slavery debate. Despite being a Southern slaveholder, he opposed the expansion of slavery into new territories and threatened to veto the Compromise of 1850.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Opposed expansion of slavery despite being a slaveholder
- Threatened to personally lead troops against secession
- Clayton-Bulwer Treaty with Britain on Central America

**Historical Events:**
- Compromise of 1850 debates began
- California Gold Rush brought statehood question
- Death sparked succession to Fillmore

**Controversies:**
- Unexpected death led to conspiracy theories
- Sudden shift in policy under Fillmore
- Never voted before being elected president

**Popularity:**
Taylor was a popular military hero known as "Old Rough and Ready."

**Notable:** Taylor never voted in an election before winning one. His remains were exhumed in 1991 to test poisoning theories (natural causes confirmed).

## Term 18: Millard Fillmore (1850-1853)

**President:** Millard Fillmore
**Term Dates:** July 9, 1850 - March 4, 1853
**Term Length:** 2 years, 7 months, 26 days
**Political Party:** Whig
**Vice President:** None

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** January 7, 1800 (Moravia, New York)
- **Death Date:** March 8, 1874 (age 74)
- **Age During Term:** 50-53 years old

**Summary:**
Fillmore reversed Taylor's opposition to compromise and signed the Compromise of 1850, including the controversial Fugitive Slave Act. This temporarily preserved the Union but earned him Northern enmity.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Signed Compromise of 1850
- Authorized Commodore Perry's expedition to Japan
- Opened trade negotiations with Japan

**Historical Events:**
- Compromise of 1850 passed
- Fugitive Slave Act enforced
- Perry expedition authorized

**Controversies:**
- Fugitive Slave Act deeply unpopular in North
- Lost Whig nomination for full term
- Later ran on Know-Nothing ticket (1856)

**Popularity:**
Fillmore was the last Whig president and was denied his party's nomination for a full term.

**Notable:** Fillmore is often ranked among the worst presidents for his support of the Fugitive Slave Act.

## Term 19: Franklin Pierce (1853-1857)

**President:** Franklin Pierce
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1853 - March 4, 1857
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** William R. King (died April 1853)

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** November 23, 1804 (Hillsborough, New Hampshire)
- **Death Date:** October 8, 1869 (age 64)
- **Age During Term:** 48-52 years old

**Summary:**
Pierce's presidency is considered one of the most disastrous. The Kansas-Nebraska Act, which he supported, repealed the Missouri Compromise and led to "Bleeding Kansas," bringing the nation closer to civil war.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Gadsden Purchase (1853) acquired Arizona territory
- Expanded western territories

**Historical Events:**
- Kansas-Nebraska Act (1854)
- "Bleeding Kansas" violence began
- Republican Party formed in opposition

**Controversies:**
- Kansas-Nebraska Act deepened sectional crisis
- Pro-slavery stance alienated North
- Only elected president denied renomination by own party

**Popularity:**
Pierce was deeply unpopular by the end of his term, abandoned by his own party.

**Notable:** Pierce is consistently ranked among the worst presidents for accelerating the path to Civil War.

## Term 20: James Buchanan (1857-1861)

**President:** James Buchanan
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1857 - March 4, 1861
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Democratic
**Vice President:** John C. Breckinridge

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** April 23, 1791 (Cove Gap, Pennsylvania)
- **Death Date:** June 1, 1868 (age 77)
- **Age During Term:** 65-69 years old

**Summary:**
Buchanan presided over the final collapse toward civil war. His attempts at compromise and his belief that he lacked constitutional authority to prevent secession left him powerless as Southern states left the Union after Lincoln's election.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Few; attempted to preserve Union through compromise

**Historical Events:**
- Dred Scott decision (1857)
- John Brown's Raid on Harper's Ferry (1859)
- Lincoln elected; secession began (1860-1861)

**Controversies:**
- Failed to prevent secession
- Considered too passive during crisis
- Consistently ranked as worst or near-worst president

**Popularity:**
Buchanan was extremely unpopular and is consistently rated as one of the worst presidents.

**Notable:** Buchanan remains the only president who never married.

## Term 21: Abraham Lincoln (1861-1865)

**President:** Abraham Lincoln
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1861 - March 4, 1865
**Term Length:** 4 years
**Political Party:** Republican
**Vice President:** Hannibal Hamlin

**Personal Details:**
- **Birth Date:** February 12, 1809 (Hodgenville, Kentucky)
- **Death Date:** April 15, 1865 (age 56)
- **Age During Term:** 52-56 years old

**Summary:**
Lincoln's first term was consumed by the Civil War, the bloodiest conflict in American history. His leadership preserved the Union, and the Emancipation Proclamation transformed the war into a crusade against slavery.

**Notable Accomplishments:**
- Preserved the Union through Civil War
- Emancipation Proclamation (1863)
- Gettysburg Address (1863)
- Homestead Act and transcontinental railroad authorized
- Created National Banking System

**Historical Events:**
- Civil War (1861-1865)
- Battle of Gettysburg (1863)
- Gettysburg Address

**Controversies:**
- Suspended habeas corpus
- Expanded executive power dramatically
- Polarizing during war; now revered

**Popularity:**
Lincoln was deeply controversial during the war but is now consistently ranked as the greatest or one of the greatest presidents.

**Notable:** Lincoln was the first Republican president and the tallest at 6'4".

## Term 22: Abraham Lincoln (1865)

**President:** Abraham Lincoln
**Term Dates:** March 4, 1865 - April 15, 1865
**Term Length:** 42 days
**Political Party:** Republican (National Union)
**Vice President:** Andrew Johnson

## Key Takeaways

- **45 individuals** have served as president across **69 terms**
- Only **Grover Cleveland** and **Donald Trump** served non-consecutive terms
- **8 presidents** died in office: 4 assassinated (Lincoln, Garfield, McKinley, Kennedy), 4 natural causes (Harrison, Taylor, Harding, FDR)
- **Only Richard Nixon** resigned from office
- **FDR** served longest (12+ years, 4 terms); **Harrison** served shortest (31 days)
- **Theodore Roosevelt** was youngest at 42; **Trump** was oldest to begin at 78
- **3 presidents** died on July 4: Adams, Jefferson (both 1826), Monroe (1831)
