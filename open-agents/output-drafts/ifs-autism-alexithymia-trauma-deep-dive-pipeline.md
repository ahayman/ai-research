## Required Coverage (must shape the taxonomy)

The exploration must produce categories that cover all five blocks below. Block 3 (intersections) is load-bearing — alexithymia must get its own page, not be folded into a generic "neurodivergence" category.

1. **The model itself** — parts taxonomy (managers / firefighters / exiles / Self), 8 Cs of Self, key mechanics (unblending, witnessing, unburdening, in-sight vs. direct access), Schwartz's origin story, theoretical claims about multiplicity-of-mind as default vs. pathology, ontological status of "parts" (subpersonalities? metaphor? literal entities?)
2. **Evidence base and critiques** — RCTs and effect sizes, conditions studied, what's contested or under-evidenced, mainstream clinical psychology reception, where the model overreaches ("all parts welcome," "no bad parts," universality), Schwartz / IFS Institute commercial dynamics, certification pipeline criticism
3. **Intersection questions** — load-bearing block:
   - **ALEXITHYMIA**: IFS depends on noticing internal sensations — alexithymia disrupts exactly that. Poor fit, training modality, or structural adaptation needed? What does the literature say? What do clinicians actually do?
   - **AUTISM**: Literal/concrete thinking, sensory profile, metaphor-as-literal discomfort, masking complications, special-interest pattern recognition as asset. Adaptations? Autistic IFS practitioners?
   - **COMPLEX TRAUMA**: Standard fit case; cover dissociation, structural-dissociation theory comparisons (Van der Hart et al.), and relationship to EMDR / SE / parts-work generally
4. **Failure modes and contraindications** — spiritual bypass, premature unburdening, therapist over-reliance on Self-energy framing, "parts" language as avoidance, populations poorly served, retraumatization risks, what happens when a client cannot access Self
5. **Practical mechanics** — the 6 Fs (Find, Focus, Flesh out, Feel toward, beFriend, Fears), session structure, between-session work, written/journaling adaptations (specifically relevant for alexithymia), IFS-informed vs. full IFS, self-led / self-help honest assessment
