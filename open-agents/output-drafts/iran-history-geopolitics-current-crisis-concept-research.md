# The Current Crisis: Israel, Iran & US Military Action (2023-2026)

## Overview

The period from October 2023 to March 2026 represents the most dramatic escalation in Middle Eastern geopolitics since the 1979 Iranian Revolution. What began as a devastating Hamas attack on Israel on October 7, 2023 cascaded into a regional conflagration that systematically dismantled Iran's "Axis of Resistance," collapsed the Assad regime in Syria, and ultimately led to open warfare between the United States, Israel, and Iran. As of March 8, 2026, a joint US-Israeli military campaign against Iran is in its ninth day, the Supreme Leader of Iran has been assassinated, and the Strait of Hormuz has been effectively shut down. The trajectory from the October 7 attacks to the current war traces a path of escalation that was neither inevitable nor easily preventable, shaped by the decisions, miscalculations, and ambitions of leaders in Tehran, Jerusalem, Washington, and beyond.

## I. October 7, 2023: The Spark

### The Hamas Attack

On October 7, 2023, the Palestinian militant group Hamas launched the deadliest attack on Israeli soil in the nation's history. Beginning at approximately 6:30 AM local time, Hamas fired a barrage of at least 4,300 rockets into Israel while approximately 6,000 militants breached the Gaza-Israel barrier at 119 locations. Fighters entered by vehicle, on foot, and via powered paragliders, attacking military bases and massacring civilians in 21 communities, including the Nova music festival. More than 1,200 Israelis and foreign nationals were killed, and 251 people were taken hostage into Gaza. The scale and ferocity of the attack stunned Israel's intelligence and military establishment, which had failed to detect the operation despite years of surveillance.

Sources: [October 7 attacks - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/October_7_attacks); [Congress.gov CRS Report](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R47828)

### Iran's Connection

Iran has long served as Hamas's primary state sponsor, providing funding, weapons, and training. However, the U.S. Office of the Director of National Intelligence assessed in February 2024 that "Iranian leaders did not orchestrate nor had foreknowledge of" the October 7 attack. While Iran publicly praised the assault, the intelligence consensus is that Hamas planned and executed it independently, though using capabilities partly developed with Iranian support over decades. Iran's lack of foreknowledge may have been a deliberate Hamas decision to ensure operational security and to prevent Iran from potentially vetoing an operation with such unpredictable consequences.

### Israel's Response: The Gaza Campaign

Israel's military response was immediate and devastating. The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) launched a comprehensive air and ground campaign in Gaza that would become one of the most destructive urban military operations in modern history. By late 2025, at least 70,000-75,000 Palestinians had been killed according to the Gaza Health Ministry, with academic estimates suggesting the true toll exceeded 100,000 when accounting for those buried under rubble and unreported deaths. Over 170,000 were injured. Approximately 93% of school buildings (526 of 564) in Gaza were directly hit or damaged. By March 2024, more than 500,000 Gazans faced starvation -- described by the IPC as the "highest number of people facing catastrophic hunger" ever recorded on its scale, and widely expected to be the most intense man-made famine since World War II.

Sources: [Casualties of the Gaza war - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casualties_of_the_Gaza_war); [Brown University Costs of War](https://costsofwar.watson.brown.edu/sites/default/files/2025-10/Human-Toll-in-Gaza_Costs-of-War_Crawford_7-October-2025.pdf)

## II. The Axis of Resistance Under Siege (2023-2024)

### Hezbollah and the Northern Front

Beginning October 8, 2023, Hezbollah opened a second front by launching rockets and missiles into northern Israel in solidarity with Hamas, displacing tens of thousands of Israeli civilians from border communities. This lower-intensity conflict simmered for nearly a year before Israel escalated dramatically.

**The Pager Attacks (September 17-18, 2024):** In a stunning intelligence operation nicknamed "Operation Grim Beeper," Israel's Mossad detonated thousands of pagers and walkie-talkies that had been covertly manufactured with embedded PETN explosive and sold to Hezbollah through a shell company. The first wave on September 17 killed at least 12 people and wounded over 2,750, including Iran's ambassador to Lebanon. The second wave on September 18 targeted walkie-talkies, killing at least 30 and injuring over 750. The operation dealt a devastating blow to Hezbollah's communications infrastructure and morale.

**Assassination of Hassan Nasrallah (September 27, 2024):** On September 27, 2024, the Israeli Air Force killed Hezbollah Secretary-General Hassan Nasrallah in a massive airstrike in Beirut's Dahieh suburb. F-15I fighters dropped more than 80 bombs on an underground headquarters located 60 feet below residential buildings, killing Nasrallah and at least 32 others. Nasrallah had led Hezbollah since 1992 and was one of the most powerful figures in the Middle East.

**Israeli Invasion of Southern Lebanon (October 2024):** Israel launched a ground invasion of southern Lebanon in October 2024. A U.S.-brokered ceasefire was reached at the end of November 2024, though it proved fragile, with Israel launching near-daily incursions into Lebanon throughout 2025. An estimated 1.2 million Lebanese -- more than a quarter of the population -- were displaced during the fighting, with over 64,000 still internally displaced as of late 2025.

Sources: [2024 Lebanon electronic device attacks - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Lebanon_electronic_device_attacks); [NPR: From pager blasts to Nasrallah's killing](https://www.npr.org/2024/09/29/g-s1-25348/israel-hezbollah-lebanon-hassan-nasrallah-timeline); [2024 Israel-Lebanon ceasefire agreement - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Israel%E2%80%93Lebanon_ceasefire_agreement)

### Houthi Red Sea Campaign

On October 19, 2023, the USS Carney intercepted four Houthi land-attack cruise missiles and 15 drones launched toward Israel over the Red Sea, marking the start of the Houthis' participation in the regional conflict. On November 19, the Houthis captured the Galaxy Leader, a Japanese-operated cargo ship with links to an Israeli businessman. Over the following year and a half, the Houthis launched more than 520 attacks, targeting at least 176 ships and conducting 155 strikes on Israeli territory.

The U.S. announced Operation Prosperity Guardian in December 2023, a 24-nation coalition to protect merchant shipping. On January 12, 2024, the U.S. and UK launched Operation Poseidon Archer, striking over 60 Houthi targets with more than 150 precision-guided munitions. By March 2024, over 2,000 ships had diverted routes away from the Red Sea.

The Houthis paused attacks following the Gaza peace plan in October 2025 but threatened to resume in late February 2026 in response to the US-Israeli strikes on Iran.

Sources: [Red Sea crisis - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea_crisis); [Wilson Center: Timeline Houthi Attacks](https://www.wilsoncenter.org/article/timeline-houthi-attacks)

### Israeli Strikes on Iranian Assets in Syria

Following October 7, Israel dramatically escalated its campaign against Iranian military personnel in Syria:

- **December 25, 2023:** Israel assassinated Razi Mousavi, a senior Iranian general, in Damascus.
- **January 20, 2024:** Brigadier General Sadegh Omidzadeh, an IRGC Quds Force intelligence officer, was killed in an Israeli strike.
- **April 1, 2024:** Israel struck the Iranian consulate building in Damascus, killing 16 people including IRGC Quds Force commander Mohammad Reza Zahedi and seven other IRGC officers. This attack on a diplomatic facility crossed a major red line for Tehran.

Source: [Israeli airstrike on Iranian consulate in Damascus - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_airstrike_on_the_Iranian_consulate_in_Damascus)

### The Fall of Assad (December 2024)

On December 8, 2024, the Assad regime collapsed after a rapid offensive by opposition forces led by Hay'at Tahrir al-Sham (HTS) and the Turkish-backed Syrian National Army. Bashar al-Assad fled to Russia. The collapse deprived Iran of a key Mediterranean base and its primary supply route to Hezbollah. Iran had spent an estimated $50 billion supporting Assad over more than a decade. The fall was enabled by Turkish support for the rebels, the severe weakening of Hezbollah and Iran by Israeli strikes, and Russia's preoccupation with the Ukraine war.

Source: [Fall of the Assad regime - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Assad_regime); [Al Jazeera: Analysis](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/12/10/analysis-al-assads-downfall-has-upended-the-regional-balance-of-power)

## III. Iran and Israel: Direct Confrontation (2024-2025)

### Operation True Promise I (April 13, 2024)

In retaliation for the April 1 Damascus consulate strike that killed General Zahedi, Iran launched its first-ever direct military attack on Israel. On the night of April 13, the IRGC, in coordination with Hezbollah, Iraqi militias, and the Houthis, launched 170 drones, 30 cruise missiles, and 120 ballistic missiles at Israel and the Israeli-occupied Golan Heights. It was the largest drone attack ever conducted by any country.

The defense coalition -- involving American, British, French, and Jordanian air forces alongside Israel's missile defense systems (codenamed Operation Iron Shield) -- destroyed approximately 99% of incoming weapons, most before they reached Israeli airspace. Damage was minimal, with no fatalities but several injuries. The operation demonstrated Iran's willingness to strike directly but also the effectiveness of the allied defense architecture.

Source: [Al Jazeera: True Promise](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/14/true-promise-why-and-how-did-iran-launch-a-historic-attack-on-israel); [April 2024 Iranian strikes on Israel - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/April_2024_Iranian_strikes_on_Israel)

### Assassination of Ismail Haniyeh (July 31, 2024)

On July 31, 2024, Israel assassinated Hamas political leader Ismail Haniyeh in Tehran itself -- he was killed in his accommodation at an IRGC-run guesthouse after attending the inauguration ceremony for President Pezeshkian. The IRGC stated Haniyeh was killed by a "short-range projectile with a warhead of approximately 7 kilograms." Israel publicly acknowledged responsibility in December 2024. The killing on Iranian soil was a profound humiliation for Tehran's security apparatus.

Source: [Assassination of Ismail Haniyeh - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Ismail_Haniyeh)

### Operation True Promise II (October 1, 2024)

Iran responded on October 1, 2024, launching approximately 200 ballistic missiles at Israel in two waves, citing the assassinations of Haniyeh, Nasrallah, and IRGC General Abbas Nilforoushan. This was a significantly more aggressive strike than April, relying heavily on ballistic missiles rather than the slower drones and cruise missiles of the first attack. Most were intercepted, though some struck targets.

### Israel's October 26 Retaliatory Strikes

Israel struck back on October 26, targeting Iranian missile and drone manufacturing sites (Khojir and Parchin), the Shahrud Space Center, and critically, nearly all of Iran's Russian-supplied S-300 air defense systems. Four Iranian soldiers and one civilian were killed, but the strategic damage was severe: the destruction of Iran's S-300 batteries left the country largely defenseless against future aerial attacks. President Biden had urged a "proportional" response and opposed strikes on nuclear facilities or oil infrastructure.

Source: [October 2024 Israeli strikes on Iran - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/October_2024_Israeli_strikes_on_Iran); [Congress.gov: Escalation](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IN12347)

## IV. Gaza Ceasefire and the Diplomatic Interlude (January-October 2025)

### January 2025 Ceasefire

On January 15, 2025, Qatar mediated a ceasefire agreement between Israel and Hamas. The deal followed a three-phase approach: the first phase included a ceasefire and the release of 33 of the 98 remaining hostages (infants, children, women, elderly, and injured men) in exchange for Palestinian prisoners. The ceasefire went into effect on January 19, 2025. Thirty hostages were released during this phase. A total of 168 hostages were eventually returned alive to Israel across all releases.

### The Gaza Peace Plan (October 2025)

On September 29, 2025, President Trump announced a comprehensive peace plan alongside Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu. It was signed on October 9 and went into effect the following day. Key provisions included:

- Immediate ceasefire and return of all hostages (the remaining 20 living hostages were released on October 13)
- Demilitarization of Gaza
- Deployment of an International Stabilization Force
- Transitional governance by Palestinian technocrats under international supervision
- Large-scale reconstruction
- A conditional pathway toward Palestinian self-determination and statehood recognition

The plan received wide international support and was endorsed by the UN Security Council on November 17, 2025.

Sources: [January 2025 Gaza war ceasefire - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/January_2025_Gaza_war_ceasefire); [Gaza peace plan - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_peace_plan)

## V. The Twelve-Day War (June 13-24, 2025)

### Trigger and Israeli First Strike

On June 12, 2025, the IAEA Board of Governors declared Iran non-compliant with its NPT safeguards agreement for the first time since 2005, after confirming that Iran had enough uranium enriched to 60% for nine nuclear warheads. The following morning, June 13, Israel launched a surprise aerial assault.

More than 200 fighter jets conducted five waves of airstrikes, dropping over 330 munitions on approximately 100 targets across 27 Iranian provinces, with a third of strikes concentrated on Tehran. Targets included the Natanz Nuclear Facility, Fordow enrichment site, Isfahan nuclear technology center, military bases, infrastructure installations, and key military and political leaders. Israeli forces assassinated prominent military commanders and nuclear scientists.

### Iranian Retaliation

On June 15, Iran and the Houthis launched simultaneous ballistic missile barrages at Israeli cities. Missiles struck buildings in Bat Yam and Rehovot, a shopping mall in Kiryat Ekron, and locations in Tel Aviv. In Bat Yam alone, 61 buildings were damaged, 9 people were killed, and about 200 were injured. Over the 12-day conflict, Iran fired hundreds of ballistic missiles at Israeli population centers, military, energy, and government sites, including at least one hospital.

### U.S. Entry into Combat

The U.S. deployed five Arleigh Burke-class destroyers from the 6th Fleet to the Eastern Mediterranean to assist with Israeli missile defense. On June 22, the United States directly struck Iran's nuclear facilities at Natanz, Fordow, and Isfahan with bunker-buster bombs, significantly more powerful than the Israeli munitions used on the same targets.

### Ceasefire

On June 24, 2025, President Trump announced a ceasefire mediated by the U.S. and Qatar. The terms included a 12-hour cessation of hostilities by Iran, followed by a further 12-hour mutual cessation. Despite violations in the initial hours that killed 16 people in Iran and 4 in Israel, the ceasefire held under U.S. pressure.

### Casualties and Consequences

Iran reported at least 610 citizens killed; Israel claimed 28 killed. The war demonstrated Israel's overwhelming air superiority and the vulnerability of Iran's conventional military. According to The Economist, Israel achieved a level of military dominance potentially surpassing the aftermath of the 1967 Six-Day War.

The IAEA confirmed in March 2026 that while the Natanz facility was not destroyed, significant damage to its entrance buildings made it inaccessible.

Sources: [Twelve-Day War - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelve-Day_War); [Britannica: 12-Day War](https://www.britannica.com/event/12-Day-War); [FPRI: Humiliation and Transformation](https://www.fpri.org/article/2025/10/humiliation-and-transformation-the-islamic-republic-after-the-12-day-war/)

## VI. The Road to the 2026 War (July 2025 - February 2026)

### Iran's Nuclear Escalation

The Twelve-Day War fundamentally altered Iran's nuclear calculus. In October 2025, according to the Italian Institute for International Political Studies (ISPI), Supreme Leader Khamenei authorized the development of compact nuclear warheads for ballistic missiles -- reversing years of restraint. While Khamenei reportedly did not authorize enrichment beyond 60%, rumors persisted of an undisclosed enrichment effort at a covert site not declared to the IAEA. U.S. intelligence continued to assess publicly that Iran had not yet commenced a formal weaponization program, but breakout time was assessed at "almost zero" for producing enough fissile material.

Sources: [Euronews: Has Iran authorized nuclear weapons?](https://www.euronews.com/2025/12/24/has-irans-khamenei-authorised-small-nuclear-weapons-what-we-know-so-far); [Iran International: Khamenei green light](https://www.iranintl.com/en/202512247199)

### Snapback Sanctions

In September-October 2025, the UK, France, and Germany triggered the UN "snapback" mechanism under the 2015 JCPOA, reimposing international sanctions that froze Iranian assets abroad, halted arms transactions, and imposed ballistic missile penalties. This added to the already crushing U.S. maximum pressure campaign reimposed by Trump on February 4, 2025, which aimed to drive Iranian oil exports to zero.

### Iran's Economic and Social Crisis

Iran's economy was in freefall. The rial depreciated to approximately 145,000 tomans per U.S. dollar, with inflation reaching 42% by December 2025. GDP had contracted sharply, falling from approximately $600 billion in 2010 to an estimated $356 billion in 2025. Despite sanctions, Iran maintained approximately 1.38 million barrels per day in oil exports, almost entirely to China.

On December 28, 2025, massive protests erupted across Iran -- the largest since the 1979 Revolution, spreading to over 100 cities. The government responded with a brutal crackdown that left thousands dead in what became the largest massacres in modern Iranian history. Protest slogans increasingly reflected monarchist sentiments, with exiled Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi intensifying political efforts.

Sources: [2025-2026 Iranian protests - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests); [House of Commons Library: Iran challenges in 2026](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10456/)

### The Failed Nuclear Negotiations (April 2025 - February 2026)

On April 12, 2025, following a letter from Trump to Khamenei with a 60-day deadline, Iran and the U.S. began negotiations:

- **Round 1 (April 12, 2025):** Oman, led by U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff and Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi.
- **Round 2 (April 19, 2025):** Rome, Omani-mediated.
- **Round 3 (February 6, 2026):** Muscat, Oman, indirect talks.
- **Round 4 (February 26, 2026):** Geneva, Switzerland, at the residence of Oman's ambassador to the UN.

The fundamental gap was unbridgeable: the U.S. demanded Iran halt uranium enrichment entirely, while Tehran insisted enrichment was its sovereign right under international law. After the February 26 round produced no agreement, events moved rapidly toward war.

Sources: [2025-2026 Iran-United States negotiations - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iran%E2%80%93United_States_negotiations); [NPR: Trump not happy with talks](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/28/nx-s1-5730151/trump-iran-nuclear-talks); [CNBC: Most intense talks](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/27/us-iran-nuclear-talks-oil-middle-east.html)

### Iran-Russia Strategic Partnership

On January 17, 2025, Presidents Putin and Pezeshkian signed the Comprehensive Strategic Partnership Treaty, entering into force on October 2, 2025. The 20-year agreement committed both nations to deepen security and defense cooperation, exchange intelligence, and -- critically -- stated that if either side was subjected to aggression, the other would "not provide assistance to the aggressor." This fell short of a mutual defense pact but signaled Moscow's alignment with Tehran.

Source: [Iranian-Russian Treaty on Comprehensive Strategic Partnership - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian%E2%80%93Russian_Treaty_on_Comprehensive_Strategic_Partnership)

## VII. The 2026 Iran War (February 28 - Present)

### The Joint US-Israeli Strike: Operations Roaring Lion and Epic Fury

On February 28, 2026, less than 48 hours after the final round of nuclear talks concluded without agreement, the United States and Israel launched a massive joint military operation against Iran. The U.S. codenamed it Operation Epic Fury; Israel called it Operation Roaring Lion. In the first 12 hours, nearly 900 strikes hit over 30 sites across Iran, targeting:

- Ballistic missile sites and manufacturing facilities
- Air defense systems
- Command and control centers
- Government buildings in Tehran
- Maritime mining capabilities
- Military leadership targets, including suspected locations of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei

**The Assassination of Khamenei:** Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, Iran's Supreme Leader since 1989, was killed in an Israeli airstrike targeting high-ranking officials around Tehran. His death was confirmed by the Iranian government on March 1. This marked the first assassination of a sitting head of state by a foreign military strike in modern history.

The strikes were launched without congressional authorization. Trump sent a War Powers Resolution notification to Congress, citing the need to "protect United States forces in the region, protect the United States homeland, and advance vital United States national interests including ensuring the free flow of maritime commerce through the Strait of Hormuz and in the collective self-defense of allies, including Israel."

Sources: [2026 Iran war - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Iran_war); [CNN Live Updates](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/iran-war-us-israel-trump-03-08-26); [NPR: Congress reaction](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/28/nx-s1-5730203/iran-israel-trump-congress-strikes-reaction)

### Iran's Response: Operation True Promise IV

Iran's IRGC launched Operation True Promise IV, its most extensive retaliatory campaign to date. By March 5, 2026, Iran had fired over 500 ballistic and naval missiles and almost 2,000 drones since February 28. Approximately 40% targeted Israel and 60% targeted U.S. military positions across the region, including:

- The U.S. Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain
- American bases in Qatar, the UAE, Kuwait, and Iraq
- Israeli military and civilian targets
- Azerbaijan (which Iran accused of enabling Israeli operations)

By March 8, the IRGC had announced a 27th wave of True Promise IV strikes. However, U.S. military assessments noted that the volume of Iran's missile launches and drone strikes had dropped by 86% and 73%, respectively, since the first day of the war, indicating severe degradation of Iran's offensive capabilities.

Sources: [WION: Operation True Promise 4](https://www.wionews.com/world/-operation-true-promise-4-meaning-behind-iran-s-military-response-to-us-israel-strikes-1772283467249); [Press TV: 27th wave](https://www.presstv.ir/Detail/2026/03/08/765096/Iran-Israel-United-States-IRGC)

### Regional Escalation

**Hezbollah Re-enters the War:** On March 2, Hezbollah broke the November 2024 ceasefire, launching projectiles at northern Israel for the first time since then, targeting a missile defense site south of Haifa. Israel responded by bombing Beirut at 3 AM local time and issuing evacuation orders to civilians in 50 villages across southern Lebanon and the Beqaa Valley. By March 8, at least 217 people had been killed in Lebanon and two Israeli soldiers had died in combat there.

**Iraqi Militias Attack US Forces:** Iranian-aligned Popular Mobilisation Forces and the Islamic Resistance in Iraq launched dozens of attacks on U.S. bases in Iraq, including Erbil. The U.S. embassy in Baghdad came under attack on March 7. Iraq's government, which did not want to be drawn into the conflict, found itself powerless to stop Iran-backed groups operating on its territory.

**Gulf States Under Fire:** In a dramatic escalation, Iranian missiles and drones struck targets in Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, the UAE, and Oman -- all of which host U.S. military facilities. On March 7, President Pezeshkian apologized to neighboring countries and pledged to halt attacks on regional states as long as their territories were not used as launchpads against Iran, signaling recognition that attacking Gulf states was strategically counterproductive.

Sources: [Al Jazeera: Day 9](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/8/iran-war-what-is-happening-on-day-nine-of-us-israel-attacks); [Al Jazeera: US embassy Baghdad](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/7/us-embassy-in-baghdad-attacked-as-us-israel-war-on-iran-escalates)

### Casualties (as of March 8, 2026)

- **Iran:** At least 1,332 killed (and rising), with Israel having dropped approximately 4,000 bombs in the first days alone.
- **Lebanon:** At least 217 killed since March 2.
- **United States:** Seven service members killed, primarily from Iranian attacks on bases in Saudi Arabia and Iraq.
- **Israel:** At least two soldiers killed in Lebanon, with civilian injuries from missile attacks.
- **Gulf states:** Two killed in Saudi Arabia from Iranian missile strikes; casualties reported but figures incomplete for other states.

Sources: [Al Jazeera: Death toll tracker](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/1/us-israel-attacks-on-iran-death-toll-and-injuries-live-tracker)

### The Strait of Hormuz Crisis

Iran's retaliation targeted the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately 20% of global oil supply transits. Tanker traffic dropped approximately 70% in the first days and soon ground to near zero. Iran threatened to fire on ships passing through the strait. Oil prices surged by over 25%, with Brent crude jumping from approximately $70 to over $80 per barrel. War-risk insurance premiums for the strait increased dramatically, adding approximately $250,000 per transit for large tankers. The national average U.S. gasoline price rose $0.43 in one week to $3.41 per gallon.

Sources: [Al Jazeera: Shutdown of Hormuz](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/3/3/shutdown-of-hormuz-strait-raises-fears-of-soaring-oil-prices); [2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis); [Washington Post: Oil prices](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/03/02/oil-prices-strait-hormuz/)

### Congressional and Domestic Debate

The strikes were deeply divisive domestically:

- **Republican support:** Senate Majority Leader John Thune commended Trump; most Republicans rallied behind the president.
- **Democratic opposition:** Senate Minority Leader Schumer called for Congress to reassert its war powers role. Constitutional scholars called the strikes unconstitutional.
- **Bipartisan dissent:** Rep. Thomas Massie (R) and Rep. Ro Khanna (D) jointly introduced a House resolution to restrict presidential war-making without congressional approval. Sen. Tim Kaine (D) and Sen. Rand Paul (R) introduced a Senate counterpart.
- **Votes failed:** The Senate failed 47-52 on a procedural vote; the House rejected a resolution 219-212.

Trump indicated the campaign may last "four to six weeks."

Sources: [NPR: Congress](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/28/nx-s1-5730203/iran-israel-trump-congress-strikes-reaction); [Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/trumps-iran-strikes-are-unconstitutional); [Al Jazeera: Senate vote](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/4/us-senate-fails-to-advance-war-powers-measure-to-rein-trumps-war-with-iran)

## VIII. Iran's Leadership Crisis

### The Succession Question

Following Khamenei's assassination on February 28, 2026, an Interim Leadership Council was established under Article 111 of Iran's constitution, consisting of:

- **Alireza Arafi** (Guardian Council)
- **Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i** (Chief Justice)
- **Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf** (Speaker of Parliament)
- **Masoud Pezeshkian** (President)

Potential successors to the Supreme Leader position include Mojtaba Khamenei (the late leader's son, considered most likely), Hassan Khomeini (grandson of the Revolution's founder), and several senior clerics and politicians. President Pezeshkian has emerged as the primary public voice, rejecting Trump's demand for "unconditional surrender" and vowing Iran would never capitulate.

Sources: [Assassination of Ali Khamenei - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Ali_Khamenei); [CNN: Who's running Iran](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/01/middleeast/iran-new-supreme-leader-khamenei-dead-intl-latam); [Washington Post: Succession](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/01/iran-succession-supreme-leader-khamenei/)

## IX. All Actors' Current Positions (as of March 8, 2026)

### Iran

- **Government:** Operating under an Interim Leadership Council; no new Supreme Leader yet selected. President Pezeshkian has rejected unconditional surrender and vowed continued resistance.
- **Military posture:** Degraded but still capable of missile and drone launches. True Promise IV strikes continue at reduced volume. Iran's IRGC has fired over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones since February 28 but capacity is declining rapidly.
- **Nuclear status:** Natanz facility inaccessible due to damage. Reports of covert enrichment at undeclared sites. Authorization for warhead development reportedly given in October 2025 but status unknown given current military operations.
- **Internal situation:** Massive protests ongoing since December 2025. The economy is in severe crisis with 42%+ inflation. The assassination of Khamenei has created a power vacuum.

### Israel

- **Stated objectives:** Destruction of Iran's nuclear and missile capabilities, regime change in Tehran.
- **Military operations:** Conducting "wide-scale strikes" on targets in Tehran and throughout Iran, including oil depots and refineries for the first time on March 8. Also conducting renewed operations in Lebanon.
- **Political situation:** Netanyahu faces elections in autumn 2026 if the budget passes by March 31; otherwise the government dissolves automatically. The Haredi draft exemption remains a contentious domestic issue. The multi-front war has exhausted Israeli society after years of reserve duty.

Sources: [Al Jazeera: Regime change aim](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/3/as-bombing-continues-israels-war-aim-in-iran-becomes-clear-regime-change); [Chatham House: Israel 2026](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2026/01/israel-2026-elections-will-be-referendum-legacy-7-october-and-future-social-contract)

### United States

- **Policy stance:** "Maximum pressure" combined with active military operations. Trump has framed the war as necessary to prevent Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons and to achieve regime change.
- **Military deployments:** Massive buildup in the Middle East, including carrier strike groups, air wings, and ground-based missile defense. Seven U.S. service members killed as of March 8.
- **Diplomatic position:** Trump demands Iran's "unconditional surrender." No ceasefire negotiations underway. White House projects campaign lasting four to six weeks.

### Saudi Arabia

## XI. Analysis: How We Got Here

The path from October 7, 2023 to March 2026 can be understood through several interlocking dynamics:

**The destruction of Iran's proxy architecture.** Israel systematically dismantled Iran's "Axis of Resistance" -- killing Hamas and Hezbollah leaders, devastating their military capabilities, contributing to Assad's fall, and degrading Houthi operations. This left Iran exposed, unable to project power through proxies and forced into increasingly direct confrontation.

**The failure of deterrence.** Iran's April and October 2024 direct attacks on Israel, intended to re-establish deterrence, achieved the opposite: they demonstrated the effectiveness of allied missile defense and exposed the limitations of Iran's offensive capabilities, emboldening Israel to strike Iran's homeland directly.

**The nuclear threshold.** Iran's steady march toward nuclear weapons capability -- enriching to 60%, accumulating material for nine warheads, and reportedly authorizing warhead development -- created a closing window that both Israel and the U.S. perceived as requiring military action.

**Diplomatic collapse.** Four rounds of nuclear negotiations between April 2025 and February 2026 failed to bridge the fundamental gap between U.S. demands (halt enrichment) and Iranian insistence (enrichment as sovereign right). The failure of diplomacy removed the last barrier to military action.

**Iran's internal fragility.** The regime's simultaneous crises -- economic collapse, massive protests, military defeats, and loss of allies -- created a perceived vulnerability that Israel and the U.S. moved to exploit. Ironically, the war may rally nationalist sentiment even among Iranians who oppose the regime.

**U.S. political dynamics.** The Trump administration's "maximum pressure" doctrine, combined with close alignment with Netanyahu, created policy conditions favorable to military action. The absence of congressional authorization reflects the long erosion of legislative war powers since 2001.

The conflict remains ongoing as of this writing, with no ceasefire negotiations underway. Iran has not requested a ceasefire and has vowed not to surrender. The White House has projected a four-to-six-week campaign. The regional consequences -- including Hormuz disruption, Gulf state attacks, Lebanon's resumed conflict, and potential nuclear escalation -- make this the most dangerous moment in Middle Eastern geopolitics since at least 1973, and arguably since 1945.
