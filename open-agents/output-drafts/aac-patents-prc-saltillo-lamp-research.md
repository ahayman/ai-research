## Executive Summary

PRC-Saltillo (formerly Prentke Romich Company + Saltillo Corporation, merged 2019) holds one of the most significant patent portfolios in the AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) industry. In 2020, following the death of founder Bruce Baker, PRC-Saltillo acquired ALL intellectual property (patents, trademarks, copyrights) from Semantic Compaction Systems, Inc. (SCS), consolidating what had previously been a licensor-licensee relationship into unified ownership.

**Critical Findings:**

- **20+ US patents** identified directly related to AAC technology, owned by PRC-Saltillo / Semantic Compaction Systems
- **Foundational Minspeak patents (US4661916, US5317671)** are EXPIRED but established the conceptual foundation
- **Dynamic keyboard patents (US5748177, US5920303)** were actively enforced in litigation against "Speak for Yourself" app -- these are likely expired or nearing expiration (filed 1995/1997)
- **Newer patents (US9208594, US9229925, US9183655, US9286884, US8234589, US8893044, US8577279)** covering polysemous symbol navigation, visual scenes, theme previewing, and tactile symbols are likely still ACTIVE
- **LAMP is NOT directly patented** -- it is a therapeutic approach/methodology, not a patented technology. However, LAMP is implemented on PRC's patented devices and uses their patented language systems
- **Unity and Minspeak are TRADEMARKED** (registered trademarks of PRC-Saltillo), separate from patent protection
- **PRC has demonstrated willingness to aggressively enforce patents**, including getting competitor apps removed from Apple App Store and Google Play

## Core Minspeak / Semantic Compaction Patents (Foundational)

### Patent 1: US4661916A -- The Original Minspeak Patent

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Patent Number** | US4661916A |
| **Title** | System for method for producing synthetic plural word messages |
| **Inventors** | Bruce R. Baker, Richard D. Creech, Kenneth W. Smith |
| **Original Assignee** | Semantic Compaction Systems |
| **Filing Date** | October 15, 1984 (priority from Jan 18, 1982) |
| **Grant Date** | April 28, 1987 |
| **Status** | **EXPIRED** |
| **Google Patents** | https://patents.google.com/patent/US4661916A/en |
| **Related Products** | All Minspeak-based AAC systems, Unity, LAMP WFL |
| **Relevance** | **HIGH** -- Foundational patent for semantic compaction |

**Description:** A linguistic coding system and keyboard for use by people unable to use their own voices. Based on the sentence rather than the word, phoneme, or letter. A keyboard coupled to a computer stores plural word messages or sentences in memory for selective retrieval. Sentences are fed to a voice synthesizer for audible spoken messages. The keyboard utilizes polysemic (many-meaning) symbols on keys, where selected combinations of symbols retrieve recorded plural messages from computer memory.

**Key Claims:** Multi-meaning icon sequences to encode vocabulary; polysemous symbol combinations on a keyboard for message retrieval; voice synthesizer output.

## Patent Enforcement History

### Semantic Compaction Systems, Inc. v. Speak for Yourself LLC (2012-2013)

**This is the single most important patent enforcement action in AAC history and is critical business intelligence for any company entering the space.**

#### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~2011 | Speak for Yourself (SFY) AAC app launched on iPad |
| March 2012 | SCS and PRC file patent infringement lawsuit against SFY |
| April 2012 | Media coverage: "Patents Threaten To Silence A Little Girl" (Techdirt, TIME, PCWorld) |
| June 2012 | Apple removes SFY from App Store (no injunction had been issued) |
| August 2012 | Google removes SFY from Play Store |
| 2012 | Three child users attempt to intervene in case, arguing irreparable harm |
| 2013 | Case settled; confidential terms; SCS/PRC drop all claims and takedown notices |

#### Patents Asserted

- **US5,920,303** -- Dynamic keyboard and method for dynamically redefining keys
- **US5,748,177** -- Dynamic keyboard and method for dynamically redefining keys

#### Key Allegations

SCS/PRC alleged that Speak for Yourself infringed on "patented technology for dynamic keyboards and methods for dynamically redefining keys on a keyboard in the context of Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) systems," based on a patent issued in 1995.

#### Strategic Implications

1. **PRC/SCS demonstrated aggressive enforcement** -- They didn't just file suit; they got the competing app removed from both major app stores WITHOUT a court injunction
2. **The patents used are now EXPIRED** -- US5748177 expired ~2015; US5920303 expired ~2017
3. **Newer patents may be enforced similarly** -- The active patents (US9208594, US9229925, US9183655, etc.) cover more specific aspects of polysemous symbol navigation that could be asserted against new entrants
4. **Public backlash was significant** -- Major media coverage portrayed PRC/SCS negatively; this may moderate future enforcement
5. **Settlement terms are confidential** -- Unknown if SFY obtained a license or simply agreed to modify its approach

## Key IP Risks & Strategic Considerations

### HIGH RISK Areas (Patents Likely Still Active)

1. **Polysemous Symbol Navigation** (US9208594, US9229925) -- If your system uses multi-meaning symbols with visual indicators that link to sub-pages, you may infringe
2. **Visual Scene Displays** (US8577279) -- If your system embeds vocabulary within photographic scenes, this patent is relevant
3. **Theme/Category Preview** (US8234589, US8893044) -- If your system shows category or theme feedback while users pre-select symbols in a sequence
4. **Symbol Teaching Visual Scenes** (US9183655) -- If your system includes visual teaching aids for symbol sequences
5. **Tactile Symbol Sequences** (US9286884) -- If your system uses physical/tactile overlays with multi-meaning symbols
6. **Matrix Keyboard Layout** (US7318019) -- Color-coded, grammatically organized keyboard layouts

### MEDIUM RISK Areas

7. **Word Chunk Prediction** (US7177797, US7194404) -- Word prediction using word chunks (may be expired or expiring)

### LOW RISK Areas (Patents Expired)

## LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) IP Analysis

### Key Finding: LAMP is NOT Patented

LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) is a **therapeutic approach/methodology**, not a patented technology. It was developed by:
- John Halloran, MS, CCC-SLP
- Cindy Halloran, OTR/L
- Mia Emerson, MS, CCC-SLP

### What LAMP Actually Is

LAMP is based on neurological and motor learning principles. Its core principle: the motor plan to say a word on an AAC device should be **consistent across time and unique from other words**. This enables the brain to develop automatic motor plans for communication, similar to typing or handwriting.

### How LAMP Relates to PRC Patents

LAMP is **implemented on** PRC's patented devices and language systems:
- LAMP Words for Life is an AAC app that runs on PRC Accent devices and iOS
- LAMP uses Unity vocabulary organization (which IS covered by Minspeak/SCS patents)
- The consistent motor planning principle requires a **stable keyboard layout** -- which relates to the dynamic keyboard patents (ensuring key locations remain consistent)

### IP Risk Assessment for LAMP

- **The therapeutic APPROACH of motor planning is NOT patentable** -- Any company can build an AAC system designed around motor planning principles
- **LAMP Words for Life is a TRADEMARK** -- Cannot use this name
- **The specific vocabulary layout used in LAMP WFL is copyrighted** -- Cannot copy the exact icon arrangements
- **The underlying symbol encoding technology IS patented** -- If implementing LAMP-like principles using polysemous/multi-meaning symbols with dynamic keyboard navigation, the SCS/PRC patents may apply

### Safe Approach for Motor Planning

A new AAC product CAN incorporate motor planning principles by:
1. Using consistent symbol locations across vocabulary levels
2. NOT using polysemous/multi-meaning icons (use single-meaning symbols instead)
3. NOT using the specific Minspeak encoding sequences
4. NOT using Unity vocabulary organization
5. Creating an independent vocabulary layout and symbol set
6. Calling it something other than "LAMP"

## Recommendations for a New AAC Company

### What You CAN Do Safely

1. **Use single-meaning symbols** (one symbol = one word) -- The core Minspeak patents on polysemous encoding are expired, but newer patents on polysemous navigation are active
2. **Build grid-based displays** -- Basic grid layouts are not patented
3. **Implement word prediction** -- General word prediction is widely used; avoid "word chunk" specific methods
4. **Use motor planning principles** -- Consistent button locations across vocabulary levels
5. **Use open-source symbol sets** (ARASAAC, Mulberry, OpenMoji) or create your own
6. **Build on standard platforms** (iPad, Android tablets)
7. **Implement text-to-speech** -- TTS technology is widely available
8. **Create visual scene displays** -- CAREFULLY: US8577279 covers embedding vocabulary in scenes; design around this specifically

### What You Should AVOID Without Legal Clearance

1. **Multi-meaning icon sequences** where the same icon means different things in different combinations (core Minspeak concept -- older patents expired but newer improvement patents active)
2. **Visual indicators on symbols** showing links to sub-pages in polysemous systems (US9208594)
3. **Category/theme preview feedback** during symbol sequence navigation (US8234589, US8893044)
4. **Interactive dictionaries** for looking up polysemous symbol meanings (US9229925)
5. **Teaching visual scenes** that explain why symbol combinations mean what they do (US9183655)
6. **Specific vocabulary from Unity, LAMP WFL, or any PRC language system** (copyright protected)
7. **Any PRC/SCS trademarked names** (Unity, Minspeak, LAMP Words for Life, Semantic Compaction, etc.)

### Recommended Legal Steps

1. **Obtain a formal freedom-to-operate (FTO) opinion** from a patent attorney specializing in AAC/assistive technology
2. **Monitor the PRC-Saltillo patent portfolio** -- They continue to file new patents
3. **Design around active patents** -- Specifically US9208594, US9229925, US9183655, US8577279, US8234589, US8893044, US9286884
4. **Consider licensing** -- If you want to use polysemous symbol technology, contact PRC-Saltillo about licensing
5. **Build independent IP** -- Develop and patent your own novel approaches to AAC
6. **Use different symbol sets** -- Avoid PCS (Tobii Dynavox) and Minspeak icons
