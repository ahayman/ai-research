# Research Notes: APP2Speak AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

APP2Speak is a photo-based Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) application developed by Gina Baldwin, M.S., CCC-SLP, an ASHA-certified Speech-Language Pathologist with over 40 years of clinical experience. The app was created to fill a gap in the AAC market for adults who have lost their speech due to conditions such as stroke, ALS, Parkinson's disease, traumatic brain injury, head and neck cancer, and autism. Unlike traditional symbol-based AAC systems that often feel childish or overly complex for adult users, APP2Speak uses real-life photographs as its primary communication interface, allowing users to simply touch a photo to speak.

The application is available on iOS (iPad and iPhone) and Android devices, priced at a one-time fee of $199.99 with all future updates included. APP2Speak comes preprogrammed with 80 real-life photos organized across 4 preset communication pages, and offers unlimited custom pages where users can add their own photos, record personalized voices, and create custom phrases. The app is compatible with iOS accessibility features including Bluetooth switches, touch accommodations, low vision settings, and Apple's eye-tracking technology introduced in iOS 18. APP2Speak has been recognized by ASHA's Solution Center, Easter Seals Crossroads, and was ranked among the top communication apps for long-term care residents in a peer-reviewed study.

The company behind APP2Speak is Cabson Consulting LLC, and the app's motto is "helping to provide a voice to those in need." APP2Speak occupies a unique niche in the AAC market, offering a more personalized, affordable, and adult-focused alternative to larger competitors like Proloquo2Go ($249.99) and TouchChat ($299.99), though it has a smaller user base and less clinical research backing compared to those established platforms.

## Historical Context

### Development Journey
Gina Baldwin spent over 40 years as an ASHA-certified Speech-Language Pathologist, working with a diverse range of clients across various clinical environments. Throughout her career, she created numerous speech boards and utilized various technological devices and apps aimed at enhancing communication for individuals with speech impairments. However, she consistently found that each tool had significant limitations:

- Many existing AAC devices and apps were overly complex
- Most were financially inaccessible for patients and families
- Symbol-based systems often used icons that didn't resonate with adult clients
- Many tools felt childish or were designed primarily for children
- Adult AAC needs were treated as an afterthought in the industry

After an exhaustive search for an ideal AAC product that didn't exist, Baldwin decided to create her own solution. APP2Speak was born from the realization that adults who had lost their speech needed a tool that was intuitive, photo-based, and reasonably priced.

## How APP2Speak Works

### Three Communication Modes

1. **Preset Communication Pages**: 4 pre-programmed pages containing 80 real-life photos with title words and voice output. Users touch a photo to hear the associated word or phrase spoken aloud. Photos can be turned on/off and moved within preset pages. Two size options for photos available.

2. **Custom Communication Pages**: Unlimited pages users create themselves using:
   - Photos from their personal photo library
   - Images from the internet
   - Photos taken directly within the APP2Speak camera
   - Each photo can have one or more custom phrases associated with it
   - No special training required to create custom content

3. **Text-to-Speech Page**: A typing interface where users can:
   - Type any message and have it spoken aloud
   - Save frequently used words and phrases to history
   - Quickly access commonly used phrases
   - Use for fluid, natural conversation flow

### Additional Features
- **Voice Recording**: Users or family members can record their own voice for personalized speech output
- **Voice Options**: Male or female text-to-speech voice
- **Speech Rate Adjustment**: Adjustable speed for text-to-speech output
- **Page Lock**: Lock feature prevents accidental editing
- **Page Sharing**: Share custom pages with other APP2Speak users via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi
- **Offline Operation**: Fully functional without internet connection
- **Built-in Help**: Written directions and video demonstrations within the app
- **No Training Required**: Designed to be intuitive for immediate use
- **14-Day Free Trial**: Available for evaluation before purchase

## Photo-Based AAC vs Symbol-Based AAC

### The Photo-Based Advantage
Traditional AAC systems for adults often rely on abstract symbols (like PCS - Picture Communication Symbols) or line drawings that were originally designed for children. APP2Speak's photo-based approach offers several advantages for adults:

1. **Familiarity**: Real photographs of actual objects, people, and places are immediately recognizable without needing to learn symbol meanings
2. **Personalization**: Users can add photos of their own family members, home, favorite foods, and daily environments
3. **Dignity**: Adult-appropriate imagery avoids the infantilizing effect of cartoon-like symbols
4. **Cognitive Accessibility**: Particularly beneficial for adults with aphasia or acquired brain injuries who may struggle with abstract symbol interpretation
5. **Emotional Connection**: Personal photos create meaningful connections and facilitate richer conversations

### Research Support
Research on Visual Scene Displays (VSDs) shows that adults with acquired communication disorders particularly benefit from personalized photo displays featuring familiar people engaged in meaningful activities. Studies published in PMC (PMC6436972) indicate that realistic, scenic representations of activities of daily life are more suitable for adults with aphasia than abstract symbols. [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6436972/]

### Limitations of Photo-Based Approach
- Requires time to photograph and set up personalized content
- Cannot easily represent abstract concepts (emotions, time, grammar markers) with photos alone
- Less structured vocabulary organization compared to symbol-based grid systems
- May not support language development as effectively as linguistically-organized systems

## iOS Accessibility Compatibility

### Supported iOS Accessibility Features
- **Bluetooth Switches**: External switch access for users with severe motor limitations
- **Touch Accommodations**: iOS settings for hold duration, ignore repeat, and tap assistance
- **Low Vision**: Two display size options and compatible with iOS low vision settings
- **Apple Eye Tracking (iOS 18+)**: Hands-free navigation and selection using only eye movements
- **Page Lock**: Prevents accidental edits or navigation during use

### Control Bionics Integration
For users with progressive diseases like ALS, APP2Speak can be paired with Control Bionics' Trilogy device, which offers:
- Touch control (early disease stages)
- Eye-gaze tracking (intermediate stages)
- NeuroNode EMG control (advanced stages with minimal muscle movement)

## Strengths

1. **Adult-Focused Design**: Built specifically for adults who have lost speech, not adapted from children's tools
2. **Photo-Based Interface**: Real photographs more relatable and dignified for adult users than cartoon symbols
3. **Highly Personalizable**: Unlimited custom pages with personal photos, voice recordings, and phrases
4. **Affordable**: $199.99 one-time with no subscriptions, undercutting competitors by $50-$100+
5. **Created by an SLP**: Developed by clinician with 40+ years of direct patient experience
6. **Offline Capability**: Works without internet connection, critical for healthcare settings
7. **Easy Setup**: Pre-programmed content allows immediate use; no special training required
8. **iOS Accessibility**: Compatible with Bluetooth switches, touch accommodations, eye tracking
9. **Free Trial**: 14-day trial and virtual demonstrations available
10. **Control Bionics Partnership**: Progressive disease support through multi-modal access technology
11. **ASHA Recognized**: Listed on ASHA Solution Center
12. **Voice Personalization**: Record family voices for more natural communication experience

## Weaknesses

1. **Smaller User Base**: Significantly fewer users compared to Proloquo2Go or TouchChat
2. **Limited Clinical Research**: Less peer-reviewed evidence specifically validating APP2Speak compared to competitors
3. **Less Robust Vocabulary System**: No linguistic organization (like LAMP or Unity) for building complex sentences
4. **Single Developer Risk**: Primarily a one-person operation (Cabson Consulting LLC)
5. **Limited Language Support**: Available in English only
6. **No Built-in Language Development**: Focused on functional communication rather than building language skills
7. **Fewer Person-Centered Features**: A 2020 peer-reviewed study found APP2Speak had fewer person-centered care features (8) compared to GoTalk NOW LITE (11) in LTC settings
8. **No Cloud Backup**: Custom content may be lost if device is damaged or replaced
9. **Limited Community/Resources**: Fewer online tutorials, user communities, and third-party resources compared to major AAC platforms
10. **Photo Dependency**: Difficulty representing abstract concepts without supplementary symbol or text support
