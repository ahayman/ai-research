# Research Validation Report: IFS and Autism — Literal Thinking, Sensory Profile, Masking, and Adaptations

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: High. The Cat 7 concept research is grounded in citable primary sources for its empirical claims (monotropism, aphantasia, autistic flow theory, masking literature) and accurately represents the autistic-IFS practitioner literature it relies on for clinical adaptations. The latter literature is non-peer-reviewed (Substacks, blogs, conference presentations, one published trade book), and the page acknowledges this honestly.

- **Confirmed**: 11 claims (92%)
- **Uncertain**: 1 claim (8%)
- **Invalid**: 0 claims (0%)

**Key Findings**:
1. Empirical anchors all check out: Murray, Lesser, Lawson 2005 monotropism paper (citation exact); Zeman et al. 2015 congenital aphantasia paper (Cortex 73:378–380); Dance et al. 2021 aphantasia/autism/synaesthesia paper (Consciousness and Cognition 89:103087); Heasman et al. 2024 autistic flow theory paper (Journal for the Theory of Social Behaviour, DOI 10.1111/jtsb.12427); Hull/Mandy CAT-Q masking research.
2. Sarah Bergenfield's credentials are stronger than the exploration's hedging suggested: Certified Level 3 IFS Practitioner, Master's in Embodiment Studies, doctoral student at California Institute of Integral Studies (Somatic Psychology), assists in Level 1/2/3 IFS Institute trainings, co-author of *Wired to Feel* (2025, New Harbinger) with Martha Sweezy PhD and Beth Ann Malow MD (foreword). She is recognized within IFS Institute as a teacher, not just a Substack author.
3. The "Autistic Self" concept is correctly attributed to Bergenfield's 2022 IFS Conference presentation. The closely-related "Self can be autistic" position is from Emma at IFS & Neurodiversity Substack — these are independent but compatible articulations and the Cat 7 page cites them correctly.
4. Hardware/software framing is consistently articulated across multiple autistic-IFS practitioners (Sanni, Emma at IFS & Neurodiversity, Anchored in Self, Stroud Therapy/Natasha Wilson, IFS Telehealth Collective). It is not yet in canonical IFS Institute training material — this is correctly described in the page as a neurodiversity-affirming addition rather than a canonical claim.
5. The aphantasia + autism association is real but moderate: Dance et al. 2021 found higher autistic-trait scores in aphantasics, particularly in imagination and social-skills subscales. The Cat 7 page describes this correctly as correlation/association, not causation.

**Systemic Issues**:
- The clinical-adaptations literature relies on Substacks, blogs, conference presentations, and trade-press publications. Peer-reviewed validation does not exist for any of the proposed adaptations. The Cat 7 page surfaces this honestly in its Research Gaps section. Downstream content should preserve that honesty.
- The "Autistic Self" thesis is provocative but not yet developed into formal theory in published peer-reviewed literature. Treat as a clinical/conceptual proposal that has been platformed at the IFS Conference but is not yet canonical IFS training content.

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### Claim 1: "Monotropism was articulated by Dinah Murray in 1992 and elaborated in the 2005 paper by Murray, Lesser, Lawson in *Autism* 9(2):139–156"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 2 (Monotropism) and citations
- **Evidence for**: Independent verification on PubMed (PMID 15857859), SAGE Journals (DOI 10.1177/1362361305051398), Wikipedia, and the Monotropism.org official site. Citation is exact: Murray D, Lesser M, Lawson W (2005), "Attention, monotropism and the diagnostic criteria for autism," *Autism* 9(2):139–156. Murray's earlier 1992 articulation is also confirmed in Reframing Autism and Autistic Realms biographical material.
- **Sources**: [PubMed 15857859](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15857859/), [SAGE Autism journal](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1362361305051398), [Monotropism.org](https://monotropism.org/murray-lesser-lawson/)
- **Notes**: The Cat 7 page's brief description ("Mono = single, tropism = channel") is accurate to the authors' own framing. Uncontroversial.

### Claim 2: "Zeman et al. 2015 introduced 'congenital aphantasia'; prevalence 0.7% complete (VVIQ=16) plus 2.6% moderate (VVIQ 16–23)"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 5 (Aphantasia and IFS) and citations
- **Evidence for**: The Zeman, Dewar, Della Sala 2015 paper "Lives without imagery — congenital aphantasia" was published in *Cortex* vol 73 pp 378–380 (DOI 10.1016/j.cortex.2015.05.019). The prevalence numbers (0.7% complete and 2.6% moderate) are widely cited and consistent with subsequent prevalence work (Dance et al. 2022, *Consciousness and Cognition*).
- **Sources**: [PubMed 26115582](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26115582/), [Heriot-Watt PDF](https://pure.hw.ac.uk/ws/portalfiles/portal/8873489/Lives_without_imagery_1.pdf), [Wikipedia: Aphantasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aphantasia)
- **Notes**: The 2015 paper itself was based on 21 self-selected participants — the prevalence numbers come from later Zeman group work that the literature now references as the canonical figures. Cat 7 page should make clear that "around 3% in general population" reflects the broader Zeman-group estimates.

### Claim 3: "Dance, Jaquiery, Eagleman, Porteous, Zeman, Simner (2021) found aphantasics report more autistic traits than controls, particularly in imagination and social skills"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 5 — autism/aphantasia association
- **Evidence for**: Dance C J et al. (2021), "What is the relationship between Aphantasia, Synaesthesia and Autism?" *Consciousness and Cognition* 89:103087 (DOI 10.1016/j.concog.2021.103087). N=118 aphantasics matched to 118 controls. Aphantasics scored higher on the Autism Spectrum Quotient (AQ) overall and specifically on imagination and social-skills subscales.
- **Sources**: [PubMed 33548575](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33548575/), [Eagleman.com PDF](https://eagleman.com/papers/Danceetal_2021_AphantasiaAutismSynesthesia.pdf)
- **Notes**: Confirmed exactly. Cat 7 page is appropriately cautious — describes as "correlation, not strong causation," which matches what the study supports. Worth noting: AQ-based "autistic traits" in self-selected aphantasic samples are not the same as diagnosed autism prevalence; the Cat 7 page handles this correctly.

### Claim 4: "Heasman et al. (2024) — 'Towards autistic flow theory: A non-pathologising conceptual approach' — *Journal for the Theory of Social Behaviour*"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 6 — special-interest immersion as Self access
- **Evidence for**: Full citation: Heasman B, Williams G, Charura D, Hamilton L G, Milton D, Murray F (2024), "Towards autistic flow theory: A non-pathologising conceptual approach," *Journal for the Theory of Social Behaviour*, DOI 10.1111/jtsb.12427. Co-authored by Damian Milton (double-empathy researcher) and Fergus Murray (monotropism researcher), giving the paper substantial credibility within autistic-affirming research literature.
- **Sources**: [Wiley DOI 10.1111/jtsb.12427](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jtsb.12427), [Kent Academic Repository](https://kar.kent.ac.uk/106162/)
- **Notes**: Confirmed. The paper's argument that autistic flow shares phenomenology with Csikszentmihalyi's flow but is grounded in monotropic immersion is fairly described in the Cat 7 page.

### Claim 9: "Self Leadership Journey Substack offers concretizing adaptations for literal thinkers: color-coded cards, descriptive labels rather than 'parts,' brain-science explanations, personalized metaphors"

### Claim 10: "Hull/Mandy CAT-Q masking research: higher self-reported masking correlates with worse mental health (depression, anxiety, burnout)"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 3 (Masking)
- **Evidence for**: Hull L et al. and Mandy W et al. have published extensively on camouflaging. The 2017 Hull et al. "Putting on My Best Normal" paper (PMID 28527095) and the 2021 Cassidy et al. systematic review (PMID 34563942) both confirm: higher CAT-Q scores correlate with greater depression, anxiety, autistic burnout, and sometimes suicidality. The 2026 Hannon, Hull, Lai, Magiati, Mandy paper (Sage 2026) refines methodological cautions about CAT-Q usage.
- **Sources**: [PMC8814950](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8814950/), [PubMed Hull 28527095](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28527095/), [Camouflaging systematic review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34563942/)
- **Notes**: Confirmed. The mental-health correlation is well-attested. Worth noting that the masking-causes-burnout direction is theoretically motivated and clinically reported but the cross-sectional data only establish association.

### Claim 11: "Literal-thinking and metaphor-processing differences in autism are well-documented in cognitive psychology"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 1 (Metaphor Problem)
- **Evidence for**: Multiple peer-reviewed reviews and meta-analyses confirm: autistic individuals on average find metaphor comprehension more difficult than typically developing controls (PMC5843023 meta-analysis; Hobson 2012 *Metaphor and Symbol*; Kalandadze et al. 2018 systematic review; predictive-processing accounts in 2023 *Review of Philosophy and Psychology*). Effect varies with verbal ability, age, and metaphor familiarity — strongly novel metaphors show the largest gap; familiar/conventionalized metaphors show smaller or no gap.
- **Sources**: [PMC5843023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5843023/), [Hobson 2012 Metaphor and Symbol](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10926488.2012.638814)
- **Notes**: Confirmed at the population level. Heterogeneity is significant — many autistic individuals process metaphor without difficulty, particularly with verbal/cognitive strengths. Cat 7 page is appropriately cautious ("some autistic readers" rather than universal claims).

### Claim 12: "Special-interest engagement / autistic flow can serve as an alternative Self-access route"

- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (7/10) — clinically reported, theoretically supported, but not formally validated as a Self-access protocol
- **Original context**: Component 6 (Autistic Self) — Csikszentmihalyi flow / Heasman et al. autistic flow
- **Evidence for**: Heasman et al. 2024 paper provides theoretical framework grounding autistic flow in monotropism. The IFS Talks podcast and Bergenfield's own writing reference flow/immersion as a state where Self-energy is accessible. Clinically reported across multiple autistic IFS practitioners.
- **Evidence against**: No published study has tested whether the 8 Cs are reliably present during autistic-flow states or whether parts work conducted from a flow state produces the same therapeutic effects as canonical Self-access. The framing is plausible and clinically promising but not empirically validated.
- **Why uncertain**: The "alternative Self-access route" is a reasonable extension from the empirical autistic-flow theory and from clinical reports, but it has not been operationalized or tested. Treating it as a working hypothesis is appropriate; treating it as an established alternative is overreach.
- **What evidence actually supports**: Autistic flow is real and theoretically grounded (Heasman et al.). The claim that flow is a Self-access route in IFS terms is a clinical extension that warrants further research.
- **Sources**: [Heasman et al. 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jtsb.12427), [BPS / The Psychologist: monotropism, meditation, autistic flow](https://www.bps.org.uk/psychologist/autism-therapy-monotropism-meditation-and-autistic-flow)
- **Notes**: Cat 7 page hedges this appropriately ("some autistic IFS practitioners suggest" rather than "established protocol"). Appropriate.

## Research Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: Push for substantive treatment of aphantasia + IFS
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: The exploration validation flagged this gap in the Cat 7 territory; the Cat 7 page partially addresses it but the literature is genuinely thin.
- **Why**: For the user's specific profile (autistic + likely alexithymic + complex trauma) the question of whether they have intact mental imagery is load-bearing. If they are aphantasic or have weak imagery, the visualization-dependent Flesh-out step is structurally blocked. Existing treatments (IFS Telehealth Collective post; scattered Quora-level reports) are not sufficient.
- **Suggested queries**: "aphantasia IFS therapy adaptations", "non-visual parts work", "felt sense parts work without visualization", "Hey Marcus aphantasia IFS Quora"
- **Expected impact**: Cat 7 page's Component 5 already gives the most substantive treatment available; further work is publish-original-content territory rather than literature-search territory.

### Opportunity 2: Verify whether canonical IFS Institute training has incorporated any neurodiversity-affirming material
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: The Cat 7 page describes hardware/software framing as "not yet in canonical training material," which is true as of available evidence but worth verifying with current IFS Institute curricula.
- **Why**: If IFS Institute is incorporating neurodiversity material into Level 1/2/3, the Cat 7 framing of "neurodiversity-affirming additions outside canon" should soften.
- **Suggested queries**: "IFS Institute neurodiversity training curriculum 2025", "IFS Level 1 autism content", "IFS Continuing Education neurodivergence"
- **Expected impact**: Could shift the framing from "outside canon" to "becoming canon."

### Opportunity 3: Look for Bergenfield's specific substantive quotes on the Autistic Self that go beyond the conference summary
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: The Cat 7 page describes Bergenfield's Autistic Self position from PARTS & SELF and conference references but does not have direct quotes from her own publications on the substantive theoretical content.
- **Why**: Bergenfield's own articulation matters; secondhand summaries may not capture the precise position. *Wired to Feel* should be the primary source.
- **Suggested queries**: "Bergenfield Wired to Feel Autistic Self quotes", "Bergenfield autistic IFS theoretical position chapter", "Bergenfield 2025 book sample"

### Opportunity 4: Aphantasia + autism prevalence — more recent estimates
- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: Dance et al. 2021 is the primary citation; subsequent work (Wei et al. 2025 *Autism Research*) reviews mental imagery variation in autism more broadly.
- **Why**: The 2025 Wei et al. paper may sharpen the prevalence estimate or find heterogeneity that changes the framing.
- **Suggested queries**: "Wei 2025 autism mental imagery", "aphantasia autism 2024 prevalence", "ASD mental imagery review"
- **Expected impact**: Marginal improvement; current framing is adequate.
