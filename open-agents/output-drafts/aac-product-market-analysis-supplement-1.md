# AAC Product Market Analysis — Supplement 1: Five Additional Products

## Purpose

This supplement extends the original 9-product market analysis with 5 additional AAC products that were covered in the competitive analysis but omitted from the initial market evaluation. These range from a next-generation product from a major vendor (Proloquo) to open-source alternatives (Sweet Suite, Weave Chat) and niche products (APP2Speak, CoughDrop). Each is evaluated against the same 7-domain Communication Struggles Taxonomy framework.

## Per-Product Analysis

### 10. Proloquo (AssistiveWare) — $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr, iOS only

**Overview:** Proloquo is AssistiveWare's next-generation symbol-based AAC app, coexisting with (not replacing) Proloquo2Go. Released in 2022, it uses a subscription model and features a fixed 60-button grid with the Crescendo Evolution vocabulary (16,500 words). Won the 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award — the first AAC app to receive this recognition. Includes free access to the Proloquo Coach companion app for parent/educator training.

**Struggles Addressed Well:**
- **Motor speech bypass (2.1):** Symbol-based output with fixed 60-button grid optimized for motor planning consistency. Words appear only once, and locations never change. Average 1.6 taps per word — extremely efficient.
- **Language formulation (2.2):** Crescendo Evolution vocabulary with 16,000+ words (claimed 4x larger than competitors). 9-tab organization by word kind with semantic clustering. Comprehensive core word foundation.
- **Emotional expression (2.5):** First AAC app to use Acapela Neural Voices (60+ free voices). Genuine children's voices (Josh, Ella). Apple Personal Voice support for voice preservation. My-Own-Voice cloning ($99/yr or $999 one-time via Acapela Group; confirmed compatible with Proloquo2Go and Proloquo4Text — Proloquo compatibility unconfirmed). Note: ExpressivePower (emotive expression control) is a Proloquo2Go feature and has not been confirmed in the newer Proloquo app.
- **Cognitive support (5):** Focus Mode uses light and motion to guide attention without hiding vocabulary — philosophy of "guide attention, don't restrict access." Thinking Space allows non-linear symbol exploration for beginning communicators. 60-button grid simpler than many competitors' default grids.
- **Psychosocial (6):** Proloquo Coach teaches communication partners best practices, building a support team. Free for SLPs. Regional English variants (6) respect cultural identity. Subscription model ($9.99/mo) more accessible than $250+ one-time purchases.
- **Rate/efficiency (7):** 1.6 taps per word average. Motor planning consistency enables automaticity. Semantic clustering reduces search time. No algorithmic prediction — efficiency comes from vocabulary architecture.

**Struggles Addressed Poorly:**
- **Physical access (4):** No built-in switch scanning (relies on iOS Switch Control, which has fewer modes than Proloquo2Go's 5 dedicated modes). Compatible with iOS 18 eye gaze, Hiru, TD Pilot, VersaEye, HeadMouse Nano. Fixed 60-button grid excludes users who need smaller/larger targets.
- **Receptive support (1):** Thinking Space and Focus Mode provide some receptive scaffolding, but no dedicated comprehension tools. Better than most competitors but still primarily expressive.
- **Pragmatic/social (3):** Social vocabulary in Crescendo Evolution covers greetings, turn-taking, etc. Proloquo Coach teaches communication functions. But no PODD, no social stories, no conversation scripts.

**Clinical Evidence:**
- 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award
- Published research primarily on Proloquo2Go (PubMed 30255726: enhanced classroom performance in ASD; ERIC ED577783: reduced echolalia; PubMed 33756090: aphasia navigation)
- Proloquo-specific studies limited due to recency; AssistiveWare cites internal data (60-button vs 77-button grid produced same language outcomes across 10,000+ users)

**Pricing:** $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr subscription; free 1-month trial; free for SLPs/clinicians; school licenses available. Proloquo Coach included free.

**Platform:** iOS only (iPad, iPhone). Requires iOS 17.5+ (may vary by region/update). Cross-device sync.

**Patent Position:** Same as Proloquo2Go — uses Crescendo vocabulary system (not Minspeak), avoiding PRC-Saltillo patents. AssistiveWare holds no known offensive AAC patents.

**Key Strengths:** Largest AAC vocabulary (16,500 words), exceptional motor planning efficiency (1.6 taps/word), innovative Focus Mode and Thinking Space features, first-of-kind companion training app (Coach), genuine children's voices (Acapela Neural), affordable subscription model, Apple Personal Voice support.

**Key Weaknesses:** English only (no bilingual, no Spanish/French/Dutch), fixed grid excludes some users, iOS only, relies on iOS Switch Control rather than built-in scanning, most clinical evidence is on the older Proloquo2Go product.

**Relationship to Other Products:** Coexists with Proloquo2Go. Proloquo is recommended for the majority of English-language iPad users. Proloquo2Go remains right for: users needing smaller grids, non-English languages, bilingual support, or dedicated built-in scanning.

Sources: AssistiveWare product pages, App Store, PubMed, ASHA, ATIA, Closing the Gap, PrAACtical AAC, multiple SLP review guides.

### 11. APP2Speak (Cabson Consulting) — $199.99 one-time, iOS and Android

**Overview:** APP2Speak is a photo-based AAC app designed by ASHA-certified SLP Gina Baldwin (40+ years clinical experience) specifically for adults who have lost speech. Unlike symbol-based competitors that use cartoon-style icons designed for language development, APP2Speak uses real-life photographs — reflecting the philosophy that adults "already know how language works" and need access to communication, not language teaching. Available on both iOS and Android.

**Struggles Addressed Well:**
- **Motor speech bypass (2.1):** Photo-to-speech output. Touch a photo to trigger speech. 80 pre-loaded photos across 4 pages plus unlimited custom pages.
- **Psychosocial (6):** Strongest differentiator. Photo-based approach preserves adult identity and dignity, avoiding juvenile cartoon symbols. Voice recording allows familiar voice instead of robotic TTS. Runs on mainstream consumer devices (not stigmatizing dedicated hardware). At $199.99, dramatically more affordable than $5K-$15K dedicated devices.
- **Cognitive support (5):** Reduced cognitive load by design — "taking one element of communicating (having to talk) out of the equation." Real-life photos are more concrete and recognizable than abstract symbols. No special training required. Easy-to-read instructions with built-in video demonstrations.

**Struggles Addressed Poorly:**
- **Language formulation (2.2):** Cannot build novel sentences through symbol combination. No robust linguistic frameworks (core word grids, semantic-syntactic organization, morphology). Limited to pre-stored photo+phrase pairs and typed text.
- **Rate/efficiency (7):** Basic word prediction in text mode only. No abbreviation expansion, sentence prediction, or recency-based suggestions. No core word layouts for motor planning efficiency.
- **Physical access (4):** Supports touch, Bluetooth switches (via iOS accessibility), Apple eye tracking (iOS 18+), and head tracking. Partnership with Control Bionics for progressive disease transition. But no dedicated eye-tracking features (dwell time, gaze patterns) and less sophisticated scanning than dedicated AAC apps.
- **Receptive support (1):** Primarily expressive. Photos + speech output may provide incidental comprehension support. No dedicated receptive tools.
- **Pragmatic/social (3):** Save phrases per photo for convenient conversation. TTS history for quick access. But no conversation scaffolding, social scripts, or pragmatic supports.
- **Emotional expression (2.5):** Male/female TTS voices. Can record familiar voice (significant for identity). But no prosody controls, emotional tone markers, or voice banking integration.

**Clinical Evidence:**
- JMIR Aging (PMC7177427): One of only 7 AAC apps rated "highly" in a systematic evaluation (607 apps screened for eligibility, 23 evaluated in detail)
- One of 4 apps in the JMIR study whose design was informed by an SLP (alongside Chatable, CommunicAide Free, CommunicoTool Adult)
- Listed on ASHA Solution Center, Closing the Gap, ATIA exhibitor
- SLP testimonials positive; but no RCTs or specific outcome studies
- App Store rating: 4/5 stars

**Pricing:** $199.99 full version (one-time, includes all future updates). APP2Speak Lite: $19.99. No subscription. 14-day free trial available.

**Platform:** iOS (iPad, iPhone) and Android. Offline capable.

**Patent Position:** No known patents. Solo SLP developer. No patent risk identified.

**Key Strengths:** Purpose-built for adults, photo-based (dignified), cross-platform, SLP-designed, affordable one-time purchase, Control Bionics partnership for progressive diseases, no subscription required.

**Key Weaknesses:** Limited sentence construction capability, small developer (sustainability risk), basic word prediction only, limited multilingual support, small user base (5+ Android installs), limited clinical evidence, no robust alternative access.

**Target Population:** Adults with acquired speech loss — stroke/aphasia, ALS, Parkinson's, head/neck cancer, TBI, apraxia. Not designed for language development in children.

Sources: app2speak.com, App Store, ASHA Solution Center, JMIR Aging (PMC7177427), ATIA, Accessibility.com, Easter Seals Crossroads.

### 12. CoughDrop (Forbes AAC/MavWare) — $9/mo or $295 lifetime, Web/iOS/Android/Windows

**Overview:** CoughDrop is a web-first, cloud-synced AAC application originally created by Brian Whitmer (co-founder of Instructure/Canvas LMS) whose daughter has Rett Syndrome. Acquired by Forbes AAC in early 2023 (via subsidiary MavWare, LLC). CoughDrop's defining innovation is its web-based architecture — it runs on virtually any device with a browser while maintaining cloud sync across all platforms. The open-source codebase (AGPLv3) was transitioned to OpenAAC after the acquisition; future updates are closed-source.

**Struggles Addressed Well:**
- **Motor speech bypass (2.1):** Symbol-based communication boards with customizable grid layouts. Quick Core Pro series available in 12, 24, 40, 60, 84, and 112 button layouts.
- **Language formulation (2.2):** Quick Core vocabulary sets with core word foundation. CoreWord Language System (via Forbes AAC) provides dynamic layouts that automatically show likely next words. Fitzgerald Key color coding for parts of speech.
- **Physical access (4):** Touch, 1-switch and 2-switch scanning (built-in), camera-based eye gaze and head tracking (open-source, no expensive hardware needed), dedicated hardware eye tracking via gazelinger library. Built-in evaluation tool adjusts button size, image type, spacing. Forbes ProSlate/WinSlate dedicated SGDs.
- **Cognitive support (5):** Multiple vocabulary levels (12-112 buttons). Built-in evaluation tool for assessment. Vocabulary progression tools. Consistent motor plans. Color coding (Fitzgerald Key). Activity-based boards for routines.

**Struggles Addressed Poorly:**
- **Emotional expression (2.5):** Device TTS only with 2 premium voices per account. No emotional prosody, no voice banking, no voice cloning.
- **Receptive support (1):** Modeling mode for supporters (aided language stimulation). Remote modeling. Highlighted button search. But no dedicated comprehension tools or receptive modes.
- **Pragmatic/social (3):** Social media posting (Facebook/Twitter). Built-in messaging between users/supervisors. Video recording. But no dedicated conversation scaffolding or pragmatic teaching tools.
- **Rate/efficiency (7):** Word prediction in keyboard mode. Quick-use phrases. Core vocabulary approach. CoreWord dynamic layouts. But no grammatical prediction, sentence completion, or abbreviation expansion.

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Research-based design with input from 30+ SLPs, OTs, and AT specialists
- Featured in UNICEF App Catalogue
- University of South Carolina 2025 workshop on CoughDrop for AAC evaluation
- SLP reviews positive (PrAACtical AAC: "most intuitive and cost-effective AAC application")
- No peer-reviewed clinical trials specific to CoughDrop found

**Pricing:** $9/mo per communicator or $295 lifetime (includes 5 years Cloud Extras). Supporter account: $45. Modeling account: free. Premium symbols (PCS/SymbolStix): $45 add-on (increased from $25 in 2023).

**Platform:** Web browser, iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, Chromebook, Amazon Kindle/Fire, Forbes ProSlate/WinSlate dedicated SGDs. True cross-platform.

**Patent Position:** No known offensive patents. Open-source heritage (AGPLv3). Uses Open Board Format (OBF) for interoperability. Open Symbols for free symbol access.

**Key Strengths:** True cross-platform web-based architecture, cloud sync across all devices, camera-based eye gaze/head tracking without expensive hardware, remote modeling/management, built-in evaluation tool, Open Board Format interoperability, organization/school dashboard, activation heat map, AAC Now! program (immediate access while waiting for insurance-funded device), affordable pricing.

**Key Weaknesses:** Open source → closed source transition after Forbes acquisition raised community trust concerns. No emotional expression features. Basic TTS only. Dependence on Forbes AAC for ongoing development. Camera-based eye tracking less precise than dedicated hardware. No advanced prediction or grammar tools.

**Unique Features:**
- Camera-based eye gaze/head tracking (no additional hardware)
- Remote modeling (supervisor models on communicator's screen remotely)
- Open Board Format (OBF) pioneered by CoughDrop
- Activation heat map for AT assessment
- Location-based usage reporting (GPS)
- AAC Now! program

**Acquisition History:** Founded 2014 by Brian Whitmer → Acquired March 2023 by Forbes AAC (via MavWare, LLC) → Open source codebase transferred to OpenAAC as "Sweet Suite AAC" → Future CoughDrop updates closed-source → Price increase July 2023 (first in 9 years).

Sources: mycoughdrop.com, forbesaac.com, OpenAAC, GitHub, UNICEF, PrAACtical AAC, SLP Now, BridgingApps, Closing the Gap, CoughDrop Zendesk.

### 13. OpenAAC Sweet Suite — Free/Open Source, Web-based

**Overview:** Sweet Suite AAC is the open-source continuation of the CoughDrop codebase, maintained by the OpenAAC non-profit organization. After Forbes AAC acquired CoughDrop in 2023, the previously open-sourced code (AGPLv3) was transferred to the OpenAAC GitHub account and renamed. It represents the last open-source version of CoughDrop. OpenAAC, founded by Brian Whitmer and Scot Wahlquist, also maintains the Open Board Format standard, OpenSymbols library, and hosts the annual AAC in the Cloud conference.

**Struggles Addressed Well:**
- **Motor speech bypass (2.1):** Symbol-based communication boards identical to CoughDrop's architecture. Quick Core vocabulary sets (24, 60, 112 buttons).
- **Physical access (4):** Touch, 1-switch and 2-switch scanning, camera-based eye gaze/head tracking (inherited from CoughDrop codebase). Keyboard/mouse for web access.
- **Language formulation (2.2):** Core vocabulary approach. Customizable boards. Open Board Format import/export.

**Struggles Addressed Poorly:**
- **All domains:** Sweet Suite inherits CoughDrop's capabilities but lacks commercial-grade updates, support, and maintenance. No dedicated support team, training resources, or regular feature development.
- **Emotional expression (2.5):** Device TTS only. No voice banking or emotional prosody.
- **Receptive (1), Pragmatic (3), Cognitive (5), Psychosocial (6), Rate (7):** Same limitations as CoughDrop, with the additional constraint that features frozen at the last open-source release.

**Clinical Evidence:** None specific to Sweet Suite. CoughDrop's evidence base applies to the shared codebase.

**Pricing:** Free (AGPLv3 open-source license). Self-hosting required (Ruby on Rails + PostgreSQL + Redis).

**Platform:** Web-based. Runs in any modern browser. Can be packaged for mobile/desktop.

**Patent Position:** No patents. Open-source. Uses Open Board Format and OpenSymbols.

**Key Strengths:** Completely free, open-source, vendor-independent, interoperable (OBF), cross-platform, cloud-syncable, proven codebase (based on CoughDrop).

**Key Weaknesses:** Requires self-hosting (significant technical barrier), no dedicated support, limited ongoing development (small non-profit), no commercial-grade reliability guarantees, undocumented API, no dedicated hardware partnerships, no insurance funding pathway.

**OpenAAC Ecosystem:**
- **Open Board Format (OBF):** Open standard for AAC board interchange (.obf/.obz files). Supported by CoughDrop, Cboard, AsTeRICS Grid, BoardBuilder.
- **OpenSymbols:** Aggregates ~57,000+ open-licensed symbols from ARASAAC (~12,909), Sclera (~11,443), Mulberry (~3,116), Tawasol (~950). Documented Open API.
- **AAC in the Cloud:** Free annual online conference (2026: June 24-25).
- **knAAC Awards:** Annual community awards for AAC products and individuals.
- **AAC Certifications:** Baseline quality certifications for AAC apps.
- **State of AAC Report:** Annual ecosystem assessment (2024 GPA: 1.9/4.0).
- **aac_shim:** JavaScript library for embedding web apps inside AAC systems.
- **aac-metrics:** Tool for analyzing AAC board set efficiency.

Sources: github.com/open-aac/sweet-suite-aac, openaac.org, openboardformat.org, opensymbols.org, aacconference.com.

### 14. Weave Chat AAC (Weave Chat, LLC) — Free, iOS/Android/Kindle/Chromebook

**Overview:** Weave Chat AAC is a completely free, symbol-and-text AAC app created during the COVID-19 pandemic by Kyanne (a pediatric SLP with 7+ years experience) and an engineer co-founder. The app uses Fitzgerald Key color coding, offers 3,000+ words with weekly additions, and is available on more platforms than almost any competitor. Revenue comes from optional Weave Chat Connect subscriptions for clinicians (remote board management), keeping the core app permanently free.

**Struggles Addressed Well:**
- **Motor speech bypass (2.1):** Symbol-based dynamic display boards with Fitzgerald Key color coding. 12 grid layouts (1x2 to 7x15). Text keyboard also available.
- **Pragmatic/social (3):** Unique in-app AAC texting/messaging — users can text other Weave Chat users using picture-symbol messages. Copy/paste AAC messages to other apps. Conversational words category (pink-coded). Audio recording (30 sec per button) for personalized greetings.
- **Psychosocial (6):** Completely free removes financial barrier entirely. Runs on mainstream devices. Created by practicing SLP. Community-driven development.

**Struggles Addressed Poorly:**
- **Physical access (4):** Touch and dwell time (added July 2025) only. No built-in switch scanning, eye gaze, or head tracking. Relies on OS-level accessibility features (iOS Switch Control, Android Switch Access). Significant limitation compared to dedicated AAC apps.
- **Rate/efficiency (7):** No word prediction, no frequency-based sorting, no abbreviation expansion, no recents/history. Custom phrases and scrolling boards are the only efficiency tools.
- **Emotional expression (2.5):** Device TTS only (no own voices). Users report sync issues with downloaded TTS voices. No prosody controls or emotional markers.
- **Receptive support (1):** Fitzgerald Key color coding provides visual language categorization. No dedicated comprehension tools.
- **Language formulation (2.2):** 3,000+ words with category-based organization. Custom buttons and phrases. But 2,000 custom button limit (expandable on request). Notable vocabulary gaps (e.g., missing basic pet vocabulary). No core word approach.
- **Cognitive support (5):** Fitzgerald Key color coding. Adjustable grid sizes (1x2 to 7x15). PIN lock for caregiver control. Light/dark mode. But no specific cognitive scaffolding features.

**Clinical Evidence:** No published peer-reviewed studies specific to Weave Chat AAC. Website references general AAC research. Featured on The Take AACtion Podcast. Listed in Closing the Gap, OMazing Kids Feature Matching Charts, Easter Seals K-12 ATLAS.

**Pricing:** Completely free. No ads. No in-app purchases. Optional Weave Chat Connect subscription for clinicians (remote board editing).

**Platform:** iOS, Android, Amazon Kindle/Fire, Chromebook, M1+ Macs. Most cross-platform coverage of any AAC app.

**Patent Position:** No known patents. Small startup.

**Language Support:** English (full support with grammar). Spanish (beta, verb conjugation added Oct 2025). 17+ languages in alpha/translation phase including Arabic, French, German, Vietnamese, Tagalog. True multilingual code switching (iOS and Amazon Fire; Android forthcoming).

**Key Strengths:** Completely free (mission-driven), broadest platform support, SLP-designed, actively maintained (monthly updates), growing multilingual support with true code switching, unique in-app AAC messaging, 12 grid layout options, audio recording on buttons, community-driven.

**Key Weaknesses:** No switch scanning/eye gaze/head tracking (native), no word prediction, 2,000 custom button limit, vocabulary gaps, device TTS only, no published clinical evidence, modest adoption (~1,300/month Android downloads), some users report navigation difficulties.

**App Store Ratings:** Google Play: 4.37/5 (110 ratings). Teachers Pay Teachers: 4.87/5 (15 reviews).

Sources: weavechat.com, App Store, Google Play, Closing the Gap, OMazing Kids, Easter Seals K-12 ATLAS, The Take AACtion Podcast, NWACS.

## Impact on Original Analysis

### Updated Product-Struggle Coverage Matrix (14 Products)

The addition of these 5 products reinforces the original finding that **all AAC products focus on Expressive output (D2) and Physical access (D4)**, while Receptive (D1), Pragmatic (D3), Cognitive (D5), Psychosocial (D6), and Rate/Efficiency (D7) remain underserved.

**New observations:**
1. **Proloquo's Thinking Space and Focus Mode** are the most innovative approaches to cognitive support and receptive scaffolding in the market, but still don't constitute dedicated receptive tools.
2. **CoughDrop's camera-based eye tracking** is a unique accessibility democratization — no other AAC app offers hardware-free eye tracking.
3. **APP2Speak's photo-based approach** uniquely addresses adult psychosocial needs (dignity, identity) that symbol-based apps overlook.
4. **Weave Chat's in-app AAC messaging** is a novel social/pragmatic feature no other app offers.
5. **The free/open-source options (Sweet Suite, Weave Chat, Cboard)** collectively demonstrate that the technology barrier to basic AAC is essentially zero — the real gap is in advanced features (prediction, emotional expression, adaptive interfaces, receptive support).

### Market Consolidation Update
- PRC-Saltillo now controls 4 of 14 products analyzed (TouchChat, LAMP WFL, Avaz, plus APP2Speak's competitive space)
- Forbes AAC controls CoughDrop (and by extension influenced the Sweet Suite fork)
- AssistiveWare maintains 3 products (Proloquo, Proloquo2Go, Proloquo4Text)
- Only Weave Chat and Fluent AAC are truly independent small developers

## Research Gaps

1. **Sweet Suite deployment data:** No data found on how many independent instances are running. Self-hosting requirement makes adoption tracking difficult.
2. **CoughDrop post-acquisition user satisfaction:** No systematic survey of user experience changes after Forbes AAC acquisition.
3. **Weave Chat clinical outcomes:** No published studies despite growing adoption. The free model makes it harder to fund clinical research.
4. **APP2Speak long-term outcomes:** No longitudinal data on adult users maintaining APP2Speak vs. abandoning it.
5. **Proloquo vs. Proloquo2Go switching data:** How many P2G users have migrated to Proloquo? What drives the decision?
