### 1. Oracle Bone Script Dating (~1250 BCE)
**Claim**: The earliest attested Chinese writing — oracle bone inscriptions — dates to approximately 1250 BCE.
**Verdict**: CONFIRMED (with nuance)
**Evidence**: Cambridge Core radiocarbon dating study confirms oracle bone inscriptions corresponding to Wu Ding's reign have been dated to "1254-1197 BC (±10 years)." The research's "approximately 1250 BCE" is within this range. The "~1250 BCE" qualifier is appropriately imprecise.

### 10. 1956 Simplification — "515 Simplified Characters"
**Claim**: 1956: First list of 515 simplified characters introduced.
**Verdict**: INVALID (minor factual imprecision)
**Evidence**: The 1956 Chinese Character Simplification Scheme actually consisted of three parts: the first list contained 230 simplified characters, the second contained 285, and the third contained 54 simplified components. The combined total of lists 1 and 2 was 515 simplified characters (reducing 544 complex characters to 515). However, the 230 in the first list were implemented immediately in 1956, while the 285 in the second list were implemented in batches between 1956-1959. Calling the initial 1956 release "515" conflates the immediate and phased implementation.
**Correction**: More precisely: "1956: Chinese Character Simplification Scheme published with 230 immediately implemented simplified characters (first list), 285 additional characters implemented in batches through 1959 (second list), and 54 simplified components (third list) — totaling 515 simplified characters across the first two lists."
**Sources**:
- [Chinese Character Simplification Scheme — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Character_Simplification_Scheme)

## Corrections and Clarifications

### Corrections Needed

1. **1956 "515 simplified characters"**: Should be described more precisely as three lists (230 + 285 + 54 components) totaling 515 simplified characters across the first two lists, with the first list implemented immediately and the second in batches through 1959.

2. **Oracle bone characters deciphered**: The "~1,700" figure is at the high end of the range. Most sources cite ~1,200-1,500. Consider using "approximately 1,200-1,700" or the more conservative "about 1,500."

### Minor Clarifications

1. **Oracle bone dating**: The research correctly uses "approximately 1250 BCE" but the body text also says "approximately 1250-1050 BCE" for the oracle bone period, which is a good range. The earlier date of 1250 is confirmed by radiocarbon dating (1254-1197 BCE for Wu Ding's reign).

2. **Kangxi Dictionary date**: The research correctly states 1716 CE. The Zihui dictionary (1615) first used a reduced radical set, which the Kangxi Dictionary adopted and made authoritative.
