## Claim-by-Claim Verification

### 1. Dual Face System (面子/脸)
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- Hu Hsien-chin (1944) "The Chinese Concepts of 'Face'" in American Anthropologist is the foundational source — verified as published in Vol. 46, No. 1, Part 1, January–March 1944.
- The miànzi (social prestige) vs liǎn (moral integrity) distinction is well-established in the literature and confirmed across multiple academic sources.
- The characterization of miànzi as transactional and liǎn as moral is accurate per Hu's original analysis and subsequent scholarship.
- Goffman (1955) treating face as a unitary concept divorced from Chinese specificity — confirmed.
- Brown & Levinson (1987) positive/negative face framework as individual-centered — confirmed.

### 2. Address Terms Evolution
**Status: CONFIRMED with nuances**
- 同志 (tóngzhì) as universal Mao-era address term — confirmed. China Daily and multiple sources verify its rise and decline.
- 小姐 (xiǎojiě) acquiring sex-work connotation in mainland China — **confirmed**. Multiple sources including Language Log, Wiktionary, and Chinese language learning platforms verify the shift began in the 1980s-90s reform era, particularly in southern China. The term remains safe when prefixed with a surname (e.g., 王小姐).
- 小姐 remaining standard in Taiwan — **confirmed**. No pejorative connotation in Taiwan usage.
- 同志 acquiring LGBT meaning — **confirmed**. Proposed by Edward Lam at the 1989 Hong Kong Lesbian and Gay Film Festival. Now primary meaning in Taiwan, Hong Kong, and among mainland youth.
- 美女/帅哥 as casual flattering address terms — confirmed via China Daily and Quora sources.
- 老板 (lǎobǎn) as flattering address in post-reform era — confirmed.
- **Nuance**: The 师傅 (shīfu) timeline could be more precise — it was used before 1949 for skilled craftsmen, but its expanded use as a general respectful address term was a Mao-era development.

### 3. Modesty and Self-Deprecation
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- Gu Yueguo (1990) framework with four notions (respectfulness, modesty, warmth, refinement) — confirmed across multiple academic sources including Scribd, ResearchGate, and ERIC.
- The four maxims (Self-denigration, Address, Tact, Generosity) — confirmed.
- Classical humble/honorific pairs (拙作/大作, 寒舍/贵府, 犬子/令郎) — these are well-documented in Chinese honorifics literature.
- Contemporary modesty deflections (哪里哪里, 过奖了) — standard knowledge, confirmed.
- Generational shift toward accepting compliments more directly — this is noted in contemporary pragmatics research but not as formally quantified.

### 4. Indirect Speech Acts and Refusal Rituals
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- Three-round gift refusal pattern — **confirmed** by recent research (ScienceDirect 2024, De Gruyter 2024). The pattern of offer → decline → insist → decline → insist → accept is well-documented.
- Edward Hall's high-context/low-context framework (1976) and Chinese as high-context — confirmed.
- Bill-splitting ritual (抢着买单) — well-known cultural pattern, confirmed in pragmatics literature.
- Beebe, Takahashi & Uliss-Weltz (1990) refusal taxonomy — confirmed as a standard reference.

### 5. Guanxi System
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- Fei Xiaotong's "differential mode of association" (差序格局 chāxù géjú) — **confirmed**. Published in his 1948 work "From the Soil" (乡土中国). The concentric-circles model is accurately described.
- The etymology: cha (difference) + xu (order/sequence) + geju (pattern/framework) — confirmed.
- GRX model (ganqing, renqing, xinren) — confirmed via multiple academic sources.
- Kinship extension as guanxi-building strategy — confirmed.
- 走后门 (zǒu hòumén) as back-door connections — standard terminology, confirmed.

### 6. Sentence-Final Particles
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- The core particle inventory (吗, 吧, 呢, 啊, 了, 嘛, 哦, 啦) — confirmed.
- Maximum three-particle clustering in hierarchical four-layered structure — confirmed by Fang (2022) in Studia Linguistica and Paul (2015).
- Functional descriptions of individual particles — confirmed with the note that descriptions are simplified; each particle has multiple nuanced functions depending on context.
- English lacking structural equivalent — confirmed; English uses intonation, tag questions, and discourse markers instead.

### 7. Kèqi (客气)
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- Literal meaning "guest-energy" — confirmed.
- The paradox of 别客气 as itself a politeness move — confirmed in pragmatics literature.
- Research by scholars on kèqi's distinct properties vs Western "politeness" — confirmed (ResearchGate, Gale Academic sources).

### 8. Brown & Levinson Critique
**Status: CONFIRMED**
- The critique that B&L's framework maps imperfectly onto Chinese — well-established in the literature.
- Gu Yueguo (1990), Mao (1994), Spencer-Oatey (2000) as alternative/adapted frameworks — confirmed.
- Jia & Yang (2021) "Emancipating Chinese (im)politeness research" — confirmed, published in Lingua.
- Chinese politeness prioritizing involvement/warmth over non-imposition — confirmed across multiple sources.

## Minor Corrections / Additions Needed

1. **Fei Xiaotong dating**: The research says 1948 but "From the Soil" (乡土中国) was published in 1947 (serialized) / 1948 (book form). The English translation by Gary Hamilton and Wang Zheng was published in 1992. Either 1947 or 1948 is acceptable.

2. **师傅 (shīfu) history**: Should note that shīfu predates the PRC — it originally meant "master craftsman/teacher." The Mao era expanded it to a general respectful term for workers, and it remains widely used today for taxi drivers, repairmen, etc.

3. **Taiwan pragmatic characteristics**: Could add that Taiwan Mandarin's softer pragmatic style is partly influenced by Japanese colonial-era politeness norms and the Minnan (Hokkien) substrate language.

4. **Contemporary digital address evolution**: The 亲 (qīn) phenomenon from Taobao is well-documented and could be enriched with the note that it's from the abbreviation of 亲爱的 (qīn'ài de, "dear/beloved").
