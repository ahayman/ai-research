# Research Notes: Autism and Suicide Rates

## Executive Summary

Research consistently demonstrates that autistic individuals face a significantly elevated risk of suicide compared to the general population. A 2024 meta-analysis of 10 studies comprising 10.4 million persons found that autistic people have a relative risk of 2.85 for suicide mortality compared to non-autistic individuals. However, some studies report even higher figures—up to 7-9 times greater risk depending on factors such as intellectual disability status, gender, and co-occurring conditions.

The elevated suicide risk in autism represents a major public health concern. In 2021, an estimated 13,400 excess suicide deaths occurred globally among autistic persons, representing 1.8% of all suicide deaths worldwide and 621,000 excess years of life lost. Notably, autistic individuals constitute approximately 1% of the population but account for a disproportionate share of suicides—one UK study found that up to 11% of people who died by suicide showed elevated autistic traits.

Key findings reveal that autistic individuals without intellectual disability face the highest risk (OR: 9.40), autistic women have substantially elevated risk compared to non-autistic women (unlike general population patterns where men predominate), and late diagnosis, camouflaging/masking behaviors, and co-occurring ADHD significantly amplify suicide risk.

## Key Facts & Statistics

### Overall Suicide Risk
- Autistic individuals have a **2.85x higher relative risk** of dying by suicide compared to the general population (2024 meta-analysis) [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39197224/]
- Studies report risk ranging from **3-9x higher** depending on methodology and population studied [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- Autistic people without intellectual disability are **over 9 times more likely** to die by suicide (OR: 9.40; 95% CI: 7.43–11.9) [Source: https://news.ki.se/people-with-autism-run-a-higher-risk-of-premature-death]
- Autistic people make up approximately **1% of the population but up to 11% of suicides** [Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suicide_among_people_with_autism]

### Prevalence of Suicidal Ideation and Attempts
- **34.2%** of autistic individuals without intellectual disability report lifetime suicidal ideation [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]
- **24.3%** report suicide attempts or behaviors [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]
- **21.9%** report having made suicide plans [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]
- **66%** of adults with Asperger's syndrome (autism without intellectual disability) report lifetime suicidal ideation vs. 17% in general population [Source: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpsy/article/PIIS2215-0366(14)70248-2/fulltext]
- **35%** of adults with Asperger's syndrome report suicide plans or attempts [Source: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpsy/article/PIIS2215-0366(14)70248-2/fulltext]

### Children and Youth
- **20%** of autistic children and teens report suicidal ideation in the past year vs. 14.2% non-autistic [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- **10%** of autistic youth report suicide attempts vs. 4.5% non-autistic (more than double the rate) [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- Suicidal ideation can occur as early as **age 7** in autistic children [Source: https://afsp.org/autism-and-suicide/]
- Young autistic people (ages 5-30) have **2.38x higher risk** of suicide death [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6457664/]

### Adults
- **42%** of autistic adults report suicidal ideation in the past year vs. 4.8% non-autistic [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- **18%** report suicide attempts vs. 0.7% non-autistic [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- Autistic adults are **25 times more likely** to make a suicide attempt than non-autistic adults [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
- **Over 60%** of adults diagnosed with autism later in life report lifetime suicidal ideation [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]

### Global Burden (2021 Data)
- **13,400 excess suicide deaths** among autistic persons globally in 2021 [Source: https://www.healthdata.org/research-analysis/library/global-burden-suicide-mortality-among-people-autism-spectrum]
- **1.8%** of all global suicide deaths were among autistic individuals [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39197224/]
- **621,000 excess years of life lost (YLLs)** due to suicide in autism [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39197224/]
- Suicide burden among autistic persons exceeds that of cocaine use disorders, rabies, or testicular cancer in the general population [Source: https://www.healthdata.org/research-analysis/library/global-burden-suicide-mortality-among-people-autism-spectrum]

### General Population Comparison (2023 US Data)
- Overall US suicide rate: **14.12 per 100,000** population [Source: https://www.cdc.gov/suicide/facts/data.html]
- Over **49,000** people died by suicide in the US in 2023 (one death every 11 minutes) [Source: https://afsp.org/suicide-statistics/]
- Men die by suicide **3.8x more** than women in general population [Source: https://afsp.org/suicide-statistics/]
- Firearms account for **55.36%** of all suicide deaths in general population [Source: https://www.cdc.gov/suicide/facts/data.html]

## Risk by Autism Support Level / Intellectual Disability Status

### Level 1 Autism (Without Intellectual Disability) - HIGHEST RISK
- **9.40x higher** suicide mortality rate compared to general population (OR: 9.40; 95% CI: 7.43–11.9) [Source: https://news.ki.se/people-with-autism-run-a-higher-risk-of-premature-death]
- **34.2%** prevalence of suicidal ideation [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]
- **66%** lifetime suicidal ideation in clinical samples (Asperger's syndrome) [Source: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpsy/article/PIIS2215-0366(14)70248-2/fulltext]
- Higher risk attributed to:
  - Greater awareness of social difficulties and exclusion
  - More engagement in camouflaging/masking behaviors
  - Higher rates of late diagnosis
  - More likely to experience workplace/social challenges

### Level 2-3 Autism (With Intellectual Disability) - ELEVATED BUT LOWER RISK
- **2.41x higher** suicide mortality rate compared to general population (OR: 2.41; 95% CI: 1.14–5.11) [Source: https://news.ki.se/people-with-autism-run-a-higher-risk-of-premature-death]
- Still significantly elevated compared to general population but lower than autism without ID
- Research limitations:
  - Fewer studies focused on this population
  - Methodological challenges with self-report measures
  - Communication barriers may lead to underreporting

### Impact of Co-occurring ADHD
- Autism + ADHD (no ID): **7.25x higher** risk of suicide attempts [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31238998/]
- Autism + ADHD: **13.09x higher** risk of completed suicide [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31238998/]
- Females with autism + ADHD: **1 in 5** have attempted suicide [Source: https://news.ki.se/autism-linked-to-greatly-increased-risk-of-suicidal-behaviour]

## Gender Differences

### Key Finding: Reversed Gender Pattern
Unlike the general population where men die by suicide at 3-4x higher rates than women, autistic males and females have **similar rates of suicide death**. Some studies suggest autistic females may actually have higher rates.

### Female-Specific Statistics
- Autistic females: **3.42x higher** suicide risk than non-autistic females (Utah study 2013-2017) [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6457664/]
- Young autistic females (5-30 years): **5.07x higher** risk than non-autistic female peers [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6457664/]
- Females with autism + ADHD: **OR 10.27** for suicide attempts [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31238998/]
- Autistic women are more likely to receive late diagnosis, increasing risk
- Higher rates of camouflaging reported in autistic women

### Male-Specific Statistics
- Males with autism + ADHD: **OR 5.55** for suicide attempts [Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31238998/]
- While absolute numbers of male suicide deaths remain higher, the relative risk increase is more pronounced for females

### LGBTQ+ Considerations
- Autistic individuals are **overrepresented in LGBTQ+ populations** [Source: https://afsp.org/autism-and-suicide/]
- Being both autistic and transgender/gender nonconforming carries **elevated suicide risk** compared to either identity alone [Source: https://afsp.org/autism-and-suicide/]
- Transgender/gender non-conforming autistic samples: **63.8%** suicidal ideation vs. 30.8% in non-TGNC autistic samples [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]

## Historical Context

### Timeline of Research
- **Pre-2014**: Limited research; suicide risk in autism largely unrecognized
- **2014**: Landmark Cassidy et al. Lancet Psychiatry study on Asperger's syndrome establishes 66% suicidal ideation rate
- **2016**: Hirvikoski et al. Swedish study identifies 9.4x mortality risk in autism without ID
- **2018**: First systematic review identifies 13 relevant studies
- **2019**: Hirvikoski Swedish population study (54,168 individuals) confirms familial and individual risk
- **2021**: Suicidal Behaviours Questionnaire—Autism Spectrum Conditions (SBQ-ASC) developed—first validated autism-specific screening tool
- **2022**: Cambridge study reveals 10-41% of suicide deaths involve undiagnosed autism
- **2024**: Updated systematic review identifies 80 studies (6x increase from 2018), confirming elevated risk across populations
- **2024**: Global Burden of Disease extension quantifies 13,400 excess deaths globally

## Major Research Studies

### 1. Utah 20-Year Population Study (Kirby et al., 2019)
**Methodology**: Retrospective cohort study using Utah Registry of Autism and Developmental Disabilities (URADD), Office of Medical Examiner data, and Utah Population Database. 1998-2017.
- **Sample**: 16,904 individuals with ASD; 8,827 total suicide deaths in state
- **Key Findings**:
  - 49 individuals with ASD died by suicide
  - RR 1.57 overall (2013-2017)
  - RR 3.42 for females with ASD
  - RR 5.07 for young females (5-30 years)
  - ASD suicide deaths averaged younger (32.4 vs 41.8 years)
- **Limitations**: Limited intellectual disability data
[Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6457664/]

### 2. Swedish National Registry Study (Hirvikoski et al., 2016)
**Methodology**: Population-based study using Swedish National Patient Registry and cause of death data. Over 27,000 autism cases and 2.7 million controls.
- **Sample**: 27,122 individuals with ASD; 2,672,185 controls
- **Key Findings**:
  - Overall mortality 2.6% ASD vs 1% controls
  - 83 individuals with ASD died by suicide (0.31%) vs 0.04% general population
  - OR 9.40 for autism without ID
  - OR 2.41 for autism with ID
  - Suicide was top 3 cause of premature death in autism without ID
[Source: https://news.ki.se/people-with-autism-run-a-higher-risk-of-premature-death]

### 3. Swedish Familial Liability Study (Hirvikoski et al., 2019)
**Methodology**: Matched case-cohort study using Swedish national registers 1987-2013, examining individual and familial risk.
- **Sample**: 54,168 individuals with ASD (43,570 without ID, 10,598 with ID); 270,840 controls; 347,155 relatives
- **Key Findings**:
  - Highest risk in ASD without ID + ADHD
  - OR 7.25 for suicide attempts in ASD+ADHD
  - OR 13.09 for completed suicide in ASD+ADHD
  - 1 in 5 women with ASD+ADHD attempted suicide
  - Familial aggregation of suicide risk confirmed
[Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31238998/]

### 4. Lancet Psychiatry Asperger's Study (Cassidy et al., 2014)
**Methodology**: Clinical cohort study; retrospective analysis of adults newly diagnosed at specialist diagnostic clinic in England, 2004-2013.
- **Sample**: 374 adults diagnosed with Asperger's syndrome
- **Key Findings**:
  - 66% reported lifetime suicidal ideation (vs 17% general population)
  - 35% reported suicide plans or attempts
  - OR 9.6 for suicidal ideation vs general population
  - Mean age at diagnosis: 31 years (late diagnosis pattern)
  - More suicidal ideation (66%) than depression (31%)—unique pattern
[Source: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpsy/article/PIIS2215-0366(14)70248-2/fulltext]

### 5. Meta-Analysis Without ID (Newell et al., 2023)
**Methodology**: Systematic review and meta-analysis; 36 primary studies; quality assessment via Newcastle-Ottawa Scale.
- **Sample**: 48,186 autistic/possibly autistic participants without ID
- **Key Findings**:
  - Suicidal ideation: 34.2% (95% CI 27.9–40.5%)
  - Suicide plans: 21.9% (95% CI 13.4–30.4%)
  - Suicide attempts: 24.3% (95% CI 18.9–29.6%)
  - No significant difference between diagnosed and undiagnosed autistic individuals
  - TGNC samples: 63.8% suicidal ideation
[Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10018918/]

### 6. Global Burden of Disease Extension (2024)
**Methodology**: Systematic review and meta-analysis extending GBD 2021; searched PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO; meta-regression analysis.
- **Sample**: 10 studies; 10.4 million persons
- **Key Findings**:
  - Pooled RR 2.85 (95% UI: 2.05–4.03)
  - 13,400 excess suicide deaths globally (2021)
  - 1.8% of all global suicide deaths
  - 621,000 excess years of life lost
  - Higher RR for females than males
[Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39197224/]

### 7. Undiagnosed Autism in Suicide Deaths (Cassidy et al., 2022)
**Methodology**: Analysis of Coroners' inquest records plus family interviews in two English regions.
- **Sample**: 372 people who died by suicide; 29 family interviews
- **Key Findings**:
  - 10% of coroner records showed elevated autistic traits (11x UK autism rate)
  - 41% of family-reported cases showed elevated autistic traits (19x UK rate)
  - Suggests significant underdiagnosis among suicide decedents
[Source: https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/study-reveals-high-rate-of-possible-undiagnosed-autism-in-people-who-died-by-suicide]

## Risk Factors

### Autism-Specific Risk Factors
1. **Camouflaging/Masking**: Hiding autistic traits to fit in; associated with exhaustion, burnout, and elevated suicidality [Source: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/sltb.12965]
2. **Autistic Burnout**: Chronic exhaustion from navigating non-autistic world; often first experienced in adolescence; lasts months to years [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8992925/]
3. **Late/Missed Diagnosis**: Adults diagnosed later in life report 60%+ suicidal ideation [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11042491/]
4. **Unmet Support Needs**: Lack of appropriate accommodations and services
5. **Sensory Overload**: Chronic stress from sensory sensitivities

### Shared Risk Factors (Elevated in Autism)
1. **Depression**: 4x more common in autistic individuals [Source: https://afsp.org/autism-and-suicide/]
2. **Anxiety**: Affects ~40% of autistic children, ~60% of autistic adults [Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suicide_among_people_with_autism]
3. **Social Isolation/Loneliness**: Common due to social communication differences
4. **Unemployment**: Higher rates in autism even with education
5. **Trauma/Abuse**: Higher exposure rates in autistic populations
6. **Rumination**: Getting "stuck" on negative thoughts
7. **Problem-Solving Difficulties**: Challenges with flexible thinking
8. **Impulsivity**: Especially with co-occurring ADHD

### Systemic/Environmental Risk Factors
1. **Diagnostic delays**: Mean age of diagnosis 31+ years in some studies
2. **Healthcare barriers**: Difficulty accessing appropriate mental health care
3. **Stigma and discrimination**: Negative societal attitudes
4. **Lack of autism-informed crisis services**

## Protective Factors

### Identified Protective Factors
1. **Social Support and Connectedness**: Strong relationships reduce risk [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11579173/]
2. **Sense of Belonging**: Feeling accepted and included
3. **Positive Autistic Identity**: Neurodiversity-affirming perspective
4. **Self-Esteem**: Recognizing personal strengths and value
5. **Access to Appropriate Services**: Autism-informed mental health care
6. **Employment**: When appropriate accommodations provided
7. **Community Involvement**: Autistic peer connections

### Notable Finding
Traditional protective factors in general population (older age, higher education, employment, cohabiting) do **not** show same protective effects in autistic individuals [Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11579173/]

## Research Gaps

1. **Limited data on autism with intellectual disability**: Most research focuses on autism without ID; suicide risk in more significantly affected individuals may be underestimated due to communication and methodological barriers.

2. **Geographic limitations**: Most studies conducted in Nordic countries, UK, US, and Canada; global representation is lacking.

3. **Lack of longitudinal data**: Most studies are cross-sectional; need for studies tracking suicidality across the lifespan.

4. **Limited intervention research**: Few autism-specific suicide prevention interventions have been developed or tested.

5. **Underdiagnosis confound**: Suicide statistics may undercount autistic individuals due to high rates of undiagnosed autism.

6. **Support level granularity**: DSM-5 support levels (1, 2, 3) not typically used in research; most studies use ID as proxy.

7. **Validated assessment tools**: Only one autism-specific suicide screening tool (SBQ-ASC) validated as of 2023.

8. **Intersectionality**: Limited research on how race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status interact with autism and suicide risk.
