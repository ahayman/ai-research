# Research Notes: Proloquo2Go AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

Proloquo2Go is the world's most popular symbol-based Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) app, developed by AssistiveWare, a Netherlands-based company founded in 2000. Released in April 2009, it was the first full-featured AAC app for iOS and has since been trusted by hundreds of thousands of users worldwide. The app enables nonspeaking children and adults with conditions including autism, Down syndrome, cerebral palsy, apraxia, and dysarthria to communicate through a symbol-based interface with over 27,000 symbols and more than 100 natural-sounding voices.

Proloquo2Go is positioned as the "gold standard" in AAC solutions, offering unparalleled customization with 23 pre-programmed grid layouts (from 9 to 144 buttons per page), comprehensive switch scanning support, bilingual code-switching, and its research-based Crescendo vocabulary system. Available as a one-time purchase for $249.99 on iOS, it stands in contrast to subscription-based competitors. The app maintains a 4.77 out of 5-star rating on the App Store based on 12,000+ ratings.

However, the app faces criticism for its steep learning curve, complex navigation in its folder-based vocabulary system, and potential motor planning limitations when buttons are rearranged. AssistiveWare has also released Proloquo (2022) as a next-generation AAC app, though Proloquo2Go continues to receive active development with version 8.7 being the most recent release as of early 2025.

## Historical Context

### Company Origins
AssistiveWare was founded in 2000 by David Niemeijer, PhD, in Amsterdam, Netherlands. The company origin story begins in 1996 when Giesbert Nijhuis, a friend of Niemeijer, became paralyzed from the neck down in a car accident. Niemeijer developed an on-screen keyboard called KeyStrokes, which became AssistiveWare's first product and the first universal access solution for Mac OS X.

### Timeline of Key Events
- **1996**: Giesbert Nijhuis accident sparks the founding vision
- **2000**: AssistiveWare officially founded by David Niemeijer
- **2002-2008**: Development of the first full suite of universal access solutions for Mac OS X
- **2005**: Proloquo name chosen for a Mac OS X AAC solution
- **2009 (April)**: Proloquo2Go released as first full-featured AAC app for iOS, democratizing access to AAC
- **2010**: Pictello visual storytelling app for iOS released
- **2012**: Co-created first genuine text-to-speech children's voices with Acapela Group
- **2014**: Proloquo2Go 4.0 introduced major updates including auditory scanning, Crescendo vocabulary
- **2015**: Created first truly bilingual AAC with bilingual code-switching in Proloquo2Go
- **2022**: Released Proloquo as next-generation AAC app based on 5+ years of research
- **2023**: Proloquo (the newer app) won App Store Award for Cultural Impact
- **2024-2025**: Proloquo2Go versions 8.4-8.7 with neural voices, Apple Watch improvements, call support

### Impact on the AAC Landscape
Proloquo2Go fundamentally changed the AAC industry by bringing communication tools to consumer devices (iPhone/iPad). Before 2009, dedicated AAC devices cost $5,000-$15,000. Proloquo2Go made AAC accessible at a fraction of the cost, triggering what AssistiveWare calls "the consumer device-based AAC revolution."

AssistiveWare has stated there are no plans to discontinue Proloquo2Go. Proloquo (released 2022) is designed for users with sufficient vision and fine motor skills to operate an iPad independently. Proloquo2Go remains the better choice for users needing extensive accessibility accommodations (switch scanning, adjustable grid sizes), bilingual/multilingual communication, fine motor/vision disability support, and deep customization capabilities.

## Product Features Deep Dive

### Vocabulary System: Crescendo
- Research-based: Built on analysis of data from 10,000+ users
- Core word foundation: 200-400 core words that make up 80% of daily speech
- Three pre-programmed levels: Basic Communication, Intermediate Core, Advanced Core
- 10,000+ fringe word vocabulary with customizable levels
- Folder-based organization: Core words on main page, fringe vocabulary in categorical folders
- Grammar support: Helps users construct grammatically correct sentences
- Vocabulary growth path: Designed to support progression from single words to complex sentences

### Symbol Library
- Over 27,000 symbols (some sources cite 14,000+ base symbols plus variations)
- SymbolStix-style symbols included
- Ability to add custom photos from camera or photo library
- Dropbox integration for custom images
- Thousands of new symbols added in version 8.4

### Voice Technology
- 100+ free voices across multiple accents
- Acapela Neural Voices: Latest natural-sounding voice technology
- Languages: English, Spanish, French, Dutch
- Children's voices: First genuine children TTS voices (pioneered by AssistiveWare in 2012)
- Apple Personal Voice: Supported on compatible devices
- Bilingual code-switching: Switch languages mid-sentence
- Multiple accents: US, UK, Australian English and more

### Accessibility & Access Methods
- Direct selection: Finger touch on screen
- Switch scanning: Single and two-switch support (automatic, inverse, step, automatic step)
- Auditory scanning: Items spoken aloud as highlighted (for vision-impaired users)
- Screen-as-switch mode: Use the device screen itself as a switch
- External switch support: Via Bluetooth or wired switches
- Apple Watch: Use as single switch for iPhone
- Hold Duration & Select on Release: For fine motor control support
- VoiceOver compatibility: Apple's built-in screen reader
- 23 grid sizes: From 9 to 144 buttons per page
- Adjustable toolbar size: Bigger targets for eye-tracking users
- Keyguard support: Physical overlays for motor assistance

### Communication Features
- Message bar: Compose multi-word messages
- Recents view: See recently activated buttons (15 min, 30 min, day, etc.)
- Social media integration: Email, Facebook, Twitter, copy/paste
- Text-to-speech keyboard: Full keyboard with word prediction
- Quick phrases: Pre-stored common phrases
- Apple Watch app: Speak from wrist, use in Silent Mode
- Phone call support: Use AAC voice in calls (v8.7+, iOS 18.2+)

### Customization
- Grid layouts: 23 pre-programmed sizes
- Button appearance: Color, size, symbols, text
- Folder organization: Fully reorganizable vocabulary structure
- Custom vocabulary: Add/remove/modify buttons and pages
- Color coding: Parts of speech color coding (Fitzgerald Key)
- User profiles: Multiple user setups on one device
- Backup/restore: iCloud backup support

### Platform & Compatibility
- Devices: iPad, iPhone, iPod touch, Apple Watch
- iOS: Requires iOS/iPadOS 12.4+ (latest features require iOS 18.2+)
- Apple Watch: Requires watchOS 3+, paired with iPhone
- Mac: Available on macOS via Mac App Store
- No Android version: iOS exclusive

## Pricing & Funding

### Direct Purchase
- App price: $249.99 (one-time purchase, includes lifetime updates)
- No subscription: Unlike competitor Proloquo which uses subscription model
- Family Sharing: Share across family members' devices via Apple Family Sharing

### Comparison with Competitors
- TouchChat HD with WordPower: $299.99
- LAMP Words for Life: $299.00
- TD Snap: Varies (subscription options available)
- Proloquo (newer AssistiveWare app): Subscription model

### Funding & Insurance Options
- Insurance/Medicaid: Can be covered through medical insurance with SLP recommendation
- IEP funding: School districts may cover cost for students with IEPs
- Early Intervention: County services for younger children
- AbleNet partnership: AssistiveWare partners with AbleNet to guide insurance process
- Dedicated devices: Available as pre-loaded on dedicated AAC hardware (e.g., ACCI Choice pro, ProSlate)
- Note: Some state programs will not fund subscription-based apps, making Proloquo2Go's one-time purchase an advantage

## Strengths

1. **Industry pioneer**: First full-featured AAC app for iOS (2009), established trust
2. **Comprehensive vocabulary**: Research-based Crescendo with 27,000+ symbols
3. **Unmatched accessibility**: 23 grid sizes, multiple scanning modes, VoiceOver, keyguard support
4. **Bilingual support**: True code-switching across languages mid-sentence
5. **One-time purchase**: No subscription, lifetime updates included
6. **Voices**: 100+ natural-sounding voices including children's voices
7. **Active development**: Regular updates with meaningful new features
8. **Deep customization**: Nearly everything can be tailored to individual needs
9. **Insurance-fundable**: One-time cost model accepted by state programs
10. **Apple Watch integration**: Wrist-based communication and switch use
11. **Phone call support**: Use AAC voice in actual phone/FaceTime calls
12. **Strong support ecosystem**: Extensive documentation, webinars, community

## Weaknesses

1. **Steep learning curve**: Navigation and customization can be very difficult for new users
2. **Complex folder navigation**: Users can become overwhelmed finding words in different folders
3. **Motor planning concerns**: Button placement can be changed, undermining muscle memory
4. **iOS only**: No Android support limits device choices
5. **Expensive**: $249.99 is significant cost, plus requires Apple device
6. **Recommended settings ignored**: 80% of users don't follow recommended 77-button grid setting
7. **Smaller grids = poorer outcomes**: Research shows grids under 77 buttons lead to worse language outcomes
8. **Word duplication**: Words appearing in multiple folders can confuse users
9. **No built-in word prediction in symbol mode**: Available in competitors
10. **Being superseded**: Proloquo (2022) represents the "next generation," raising questions about long-term investment
11. **Voice quality complaints**: Some users report "grating breathy quality" after voice updates
12. **Professional expertise needed**: SLPs unfamiliar with the app may struggle to guide users effectively
