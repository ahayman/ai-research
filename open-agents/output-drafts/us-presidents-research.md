# Research Notes: Complete Guide to US Presidential Terms

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive research covers all 47 presidential terms in United States history, from George Washington's inauguration in 1789 to Donald Trump's second term beginning in 2025. The research includes detailed information about each presidential term including dates, political parties, vice presidents, major accomplishments, historical events, controversies, popularity assessments, and personal details about each president.

The United States has had 45 individuals serve as president across 47 presidencies (Grover Cleveland and Donald Trump each served non-consecutive terms). Eight presidents died in office (four by assassination, four by natural causes), and one resigned. The presidency has evolved from George Washington's precedent-setting administration to the modern executive office we know today.

## Complete Presidential Terms Data

### Term 1: George Washington (1789-1793)
- **President**: George Washington
- **Term Dates**: April 30, 1789 - March 4, 1793
- **Term Length**: 3 years, 10 months, 4 days
- **Political Party**: Unaffiliated (Independent)
- **Vice President**: John Adams
- **Birth Date**: February 22, 1732
- **Death Date**: December 14, 1799
- **Age During Term**: 57-60 years old
- **Major Accomplishments**: Established executive branch precedents, created Cabinet system, Judiciary Act of 1789, Bank of the United States
- **Historical Events**: Inauguration of the republic, Bill of Rights ratified (1791)
- **Controversies**: Whiskey Rebellion (1791-1794), criticized for monarchical tendencies
- **Popularity**: Universally revered, set two-term precedent voluntarily
- **Notes**: Only president never affiliated with political party; established peaceful transfer of power traditions

### Term 2: George Washington (1793-1797)
- **President**: George Washington
- **Term Dates**: March 4, 1793 - March 4, 1797
- **Term Length**: 4 years
- **Political Party**: Unaffiliated (Independent)
- **Vice President**: John Adams
- **Birth Date**: February 22, 1732
- **Death Date**: December 14, 1799
- **Age During Term**: 61-64 years old
- **Major Accomplishments**: Jay Treaty with Britain, Pinckney Treaty with Spain, maintained neutrality in European wars
- **Historical Events**: French Revolution's impact on US politics, Whiskey Rebellion suppression
- **Controversies**: Jay Treaty criticized as too favorable to Britain, partisan press attacks
- **Popularity**: Remained highly popular despite partisan criticism
- **Notes**: Voluntarily stepped down after two terms, establishing precedent lasting until FDR

### Term 3: John Adams (1797-1801)
- **President**: John Adams
- **Term Dates**: March 4, 1797 - March 4, 1801
- **Term Length**: 4 years
- **Political Party**: Federalist
- **Vice President**: Thomas Jefferson (Democratic-Republican)
- **Birth Date**: October 30, 1735
- **Death Date**: July 4, 1826
- **Age During Term**: 61-65 years old
- **Major Accomplishments**: Avoided full-scale war with France through diplomacy, Convention of 1800, established US Navy Department
- **Historical Events**: Quasi-War with France (1798-1800), XYZ Affair
- **Controversies**: Alien and Sedition Acts restricted civil liberties, deeply unpopular
- **Popularity**: Lost re-election; polarizing figure during partisan era
- **Notes**: Only time president and VP were from opposing parties; died on July 4, 1826 (same day as Jefferson)

### Term 4: Thomas Jefferson (1801-1805)
- **President**: Thomas Jefferson
- **Term Dates**: March 4, 1801 - March 4, 1805
- **Term Length**: 4 years
- **Political Party**: Democratic-Republican
- **Vice President**: Aaron Burr
- **Birth Date**: April 13, 1743
- **Death Date**: July 4, 1826
- **Age During Term**: 57-61 years old
- **Major Accomplishments**: Louisiana Purchase (1803) - doubled nation's size for $15 million, Lewis and Clark Expedition authorized
- **Historical Events**: Marbury v. Madison (1803) established judicial review
- **Controversies**: VP Aaron Burr killed Alexander Hamilton in duel (1804)
- **Popularity**: Very popular; easily won re-election
- **Notes**: Principal author of Declaration of Independence; died on July 4, 1826, same day as John Adams

### Term 5: Thomas Jefferson (1805-1809)
- **President**: Thomas Jefferson
- **Term Dates**: March 4, 1805 - March 4, 1809

- **Popularity**: Declined in second term due to embargo policy

### Term 12: Andrew Jackson (1833-1837)
