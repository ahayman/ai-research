# Research Validation Report: IFS Evidence Base

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: High. The concept research is precise, hedges where it should hedge, and resists overclaiming in either direction. Independent re-verification of each study's design, sample size, effect sizes, and funding source confirmed the concept research's framing in nearly every detail. Three claims sit at "Uncertain" not because they are wrong but because primary text access is gated and one or two figures rest on secondary reporting that is consistent across multiple sources but cannot be triple-verified from the primary article.

- **Confirmed**: 13 claims (81%)
- **Uncertain**: 3 claims (19%)
- **Invalid**: 0 claims (0%)

**Key Findings**:

1. The Shadick 2013 RA RCT details (n=79, 9-month intervention, education control, Beck/STAI/SF-12 outcomes, 1-year sustained benefits in pain/depression/self-compassion, no objective disease activity change) are confirmed across independent secondary sources. The "proof-of-concept" framing is the authors' own language.
2. The Haddock 2016/2017 depression pilot RCT is confirmed as small (n≈32), as showing **no significant between-group difference**, and as carrying methodological caveats (therapist inexperience, asymmetric medication use). The "preliminary evidence for efficacy" language is the authors' own; the concept research's framing as equivalence-suggestive-but-underpowered is independently supportable.
3. The Hodgdon 2021 PTSD pilot details are confirmed: n=17, uncontrolled, within-subject design, **CAPS d = −4.46**, DTS d = −3.05, 92% no-longer-meets-PTSD-criteria at 1-month follow-up, Foundation for Self Leadership funded. The authors themselves explicitly acknowledge the design "likely inflated the effect size to some degree" — independent verification of the limitations language is solid.
4. The 2025 Ally et al. PARTS-SUD pilot is confirmed: n=10, single-arm, 12 weekly group + 6 individual sessions, 70% retention, Foundation-funded, Cambridge Health Alliance / Harvard. PCL-5 reduction of 1.7 points/week with 95% CI is verified.
5. The 2025 Buys scoping review counts (27 studies, 2 RCTs, 5 quasi-experimental, 3 qualitative, 17 case studies) are confirmed. Buys flags lack of adverse-event monitoring, which the concept research surfaces.
6. SAMHSA NREPP framing — concept research is correct on every material point: 2015 listing, 2017 contract termination, 2018 phaseout, 2018 launch of "Evidence-Based Practices Resource Center" that explicitly does NOT replace NREPP, "samhsa-nrepp.foundationifs.org" is a private IFS-Foundation mirror not a federal site, and the 2015 rating was specifically "effective for general functioning and well-being" + "promising for depression/anxiety/physical-health/self-concept" based on the single Shadick 2013 study.
7. **One important addition surfaced during validation**: There is a **second PTSD pilot** — Comeau et al. 2024 (Psychological Trauma) — describing a single-arm online group IFS study (n=15, 13 responders) for PTSD called "PARTS." This is distinct from the Ally 2025 PARTS-SUD study and was not in the concept research. CAPS-5 d = −0.7 at week 16 and d = −0.9 at week 24, with 53% achieving clinically meaningful response (≥10-point CAPS-5 reduction). Foundation-funded. **This is a more credible-sounding effect size than the Hodgdon d = −4.46**, in part because it uses an online group format and a longer follow-up.

**Systemic Issues**:
- The IFS-Foundation funding pattern across the published evidence base is even more pronounced than the concept research conveys. *Every* IFS RCT or pilot since the Shadick 2013 RA trial appears to be Foundation for Self Leadership funded or has Foundation-affiliated authors. Independent (non-IFS-Foundation) replication remains effectively zero as of April 2026.
- The "headline number cycle" pattern is consistent: study reports a striking within-subject finding (d = −4.46, 92% remission) → IFS marketing strips the methodological caveats → number circulates as a marketing fact. The concept research correctly anticipates this cycle.
- The Foundation's own self-description claims arm's-length operation and that it "will not attempt in any way to influence the results of the research." This is the standard COI mitigation language; it is not the same as independent replication.

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### Claim 1: "Shadick 2013 RA RCT had n=79 (39 IFS, 40 education control)"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: PubMed abstract (23950186), J Rheumatol full-text Table 1, multiple secondary sources all cite identical numbers.
- **Sources**: [PubMed 23950186](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23950186/); [J Rheumatol 2013;40:1831–1841](https://www.jrheum.org/content/40/11/1831)

### Claim 2: "Shadick 2013 was a proof-of-concept RCT, not a confirmatory trial"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: The authors' own title: "...A Proof-of-Concept Study." This framing is consistent across the IFS Institute's summary, the J Rheumatol record, and PubMed.
- **Sources**: [J Rheumatol full record](https://www.jrheum.org/content/40/11/1831)

### Claim 3: "Shadick 2013 found sustained 1-year benefits in pain, depression, self-compassion"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: At 1-year follow-up, self-assessed joint pain (mean change −0.6, SD 1.1, p=0.04), self-compassion (+1.8, SD 2.8, p=0.01), depressive symptoms (−3.2, SD 5.0, p=0.01). Posttreatment also favored IFS for overall pain (−14.9, SD 29.1, p=0.04) and physical function (+14.6, SD 25.3, p=0.04). No sustained improvements in anxiety, self-efficacy, or objective disease activity.
- **Sources**: [J Rheumatol abstract](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23950186/)

### Claim 4: "Haddock 2016/2017 was a pilot RCT comparing IFS to TAU (CBT or IPT)"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Journal of Marital and Family Therapy, 2017 (online 2016). Female college students randomized to IFS or TAU (CBT or IPT). 16 sessions planned.
- **Sources**: [PubMed 27500908](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27500908/); [JMFT abstract](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jmft.12184)

### Claim 5: "Haddock 2016/2017 found no significant between-group difference"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Authors: "Results demonstrated a decline in depressive symptoms for both conditions and no significant differences in the magnitude or rate of change."
- **Sources**: [PubMed abstract](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27500908/)

### Claim 6: "Haddock sample size was small (n≈32)"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED with minor variance
- **Evidence**: Most secondary sources cite n=32 (IFS=17, TAU=15) in the analytic sample; some cite enrollment of 37. Concept research says n=32 with note about 37 enrolled — this matches.
- **Sources**: [IFS Institute summary](https://ifs-institute.com/resources/research/efficacy-internal-family-systems-therapy-treatment-depression-among-female)

### Claim 7: "Haddock had IFS therapist inexperience and asymmetric medication use"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: 4 of 5 IFS therapists had less than 1 year of experience; >50% of TAU participants on antidepressants vs. none in IFS arm. These are documented in the IFS Institute's own published summary and in critical secondary commentary.
- **Sources**: [IFS Institute summary](https://ifs-institute.com/resources/research/efficacy-internal-family-systems-therapy-treatment-depression-among-female)

### Claim 8: "Haddock equivalence framing is overreach — design was underpowered superiority that failed to find a difference"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED (this is a methodological argument, but it's correct as stated)
- **Evidence**: With n=32, statistical power to detect a meaningful between-arm difference is low; with no pre-specified equivalence margin, the trial cannot affirm equivalence. Standard methodological reasoning. Independent commentary in the Society for the Advancement of Psychotherapy critique notes the same overreach pattern.
- **Sources**: [Society for the Advancement of Psychotherapy](https://societyforpsychotherapy.org/internal-family-systems-exploring-its-problematic-popularity/)

### Claim 9: "Hodgdon 2021 was uncontrolled, n=17, IFS-Foundation funded"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Tandfonline full text describes design as uncontrolled effectiveness pilot; n=17 enrolled; Foundation for Self Leadership funded; IFS Institute summary corroborates.
- **Sources**: [Tandfonline article](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10926771.2021.2013375); [IFS Institute summary](https://ifs-institute.com/resources/research/internal-family-systems-ifs-therapy-posttraumatic-stress-disorder-ptsd-among); [Foundation IFS press release](https://foundationifs.org/news/208-ifs-shown-to-reduce-ptsd-symptoms-for-a-group-of-patients-with-multiple-childhood-trauma-new-journal-research-article-published)

### Claim 10: "Hodgdon 2021 effect size for CAPS PTSD was d = −4.46 and authors acknowledge this 'likely inflated' due to design"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Direct quotation surfaced in independent secondary reporting — the paper's own limitations section reads "this was an open, uncontrolled trial using a within subjects design, which likely inflated the effect size to some degree" and the authors note that "these effect sizes are larger than what has been observed in the PTSD treatment literature." Companion DTS effect size d = −3.05.
- **Sources**: Independent reporting matches concept research framing; d values verified across at least three independent secondary sources.

### Claim 11: "92% no-longer-meets-PTSD-criteria at 1-month follow-up"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED with framing caveat
- **Evidence**: 12 of 13 completers (92% of completers, ~71% of n=17 intent-to-treat) no longer met PTSD criteria at 1-month follow-up. Concept research correctly distinguishes completer vs. ITT framing.
- **Sources**: [Tandfonline article abstract](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10926771.2021.2013375)

### Claim 12: "Ally 2025 PARTS-SUD pilot details: n=10, single-arm, Foundation-funded"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Frontiers in Psychiatry full text — n=10, 70% retention, single-arm pilot, 12 weekly group + 6 individual sessions, Cambridge Health Alliance / Harvard Medical School authors, funded by Richard C. Schwartz Research Fellowship and Foundation for Self Leadership. PCL-5 −1.7 points/week (CI -2.45, -0.93, p=0.002); craving −0.25 points/week (CI -0.45, -0.06, p=0.014).
- **Sources**: [Frontiers in Psychiatry full text](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2025.1544435/full); [PMC11983591](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11983591/)

### Claim 13: "Buys 2025 scoping review found 27 studies: 2 RCTs, 5 quasi-experimental, 3 qualitative, 17 case studies"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Tandfonline article — exact counts match. Buys explicitly notes adverse-event monitoring gap.
- **Sources**: [Tandfonline scoping review](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13284207.2025.2533127)

### Claim 14: "NREPP terminated in 2018; IFS listed November 2015 for general functioning/well-being only"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple independent sources confirm NREPP contract terminated December 28, 2017, registry phased out early 2018; SAMHSA Assistant Secretary McCance-Katz characterized the standards as "skewed" and inadequate; April 2018 launch of Evidence-Based Practices Resource Center that "does not replace NREPP." The IFS NREPP rating was "effective for general functioning and well-being" + "promising" for depression/anxiety/physical-health/self-concept, based on the single Shadick 2013 study. Application submitted April 2014; listing November 23, 2015.
- **Sources**: [Wikipedia: NREPP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Registry_of_Evidence-Based_Programs_and_Practices); [Washington Post 2018](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/health-science/trump-administration-freezes-database-of-addiction-and-mental-health-programs/2018/01/10/ed421654-f577-11e7-beb6-c8d48830c54d_story.html); [STAT News 2018](https://www.statnews.com/2018/01/10/trump-behavioral-health-evidence-based/); [Counseling Today](https://ct.counseling.org/2018/01/samhsa-shutters-database-evidence-based-practices/); [Foundation IFS NREPP archive](https://samhsa-nrepp.foundationifs.org/); [Green-Hennessy 2018 commentary](https://e1.nmcdn.io/assets/cmhn/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/Green-Hennessy-NREPP.pdf)

### Claim 15: "samhsa-nrepp.foundationifs.org is a private IFS-Foundation archive, not a federal site"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Domain registration is under the IFS Foundation (foundationifs.org subdomain). Page footer notes "Powered by Grantuoso.org," not SAMHSA. Content is a frozen mirror of the 2015 NREPP entry. Federal NREPP site is offline since 2018.
- **Sources**: [Archive site](https://samhsa-nrepp.foundationifs.org/)

### Claim 16: "Foundation for Self Leadership funds most cited IFS research"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Hodgdon 2021, Comeau 2024 (PARTS PTSD pilot), Ally 2025 PARTS-SUD all explicitly Foundation-funded. Shadick 2013 senior-author Schwartz is the IFS founder and study includes IFS Institute–affiliated authors. Haddock 2016/2017 funding is less clear from public summaries but did receive Foundation support according to Foundation press materials.
- **Sources**: [Foundation IFS Research page](https://www.foundationifs.org/research); [Foundation IFS press releases](https://foundationifs.org/news/247-parts-pilot-study-published)

## Uncertain Claims (with reasoning)

### Uncertain 1: Exact Haddock enrollment vs. analytic sample (n=32 vs. n=37)
- **Validity score**: 8/10
- **Why uncertain**: Some sources cite enrollment of 37; analytic sample appears to be 32 (IFS=17, TAU=15). The concept research notes both numbers. Without the gated full-text, the discrepancy can't be triple-verified.
- **Resolution**: Concept research's footnote ("some sources cite 37 enrolled; 32 in the analytic sample") is the right move.

### Uncertain 2: Whether Hodgdon 2021's 92% figure is completer-only or ITT
- **Validity score**: 8/10
- **Why uncertain**: Multiple secondary sources cite "92% of completers (12/13)" and others cite simply "92% no longer met criteria." The completer-only framing is most defensible given attrition (n=17 → 13 completers). Concept research's "12 of 13 completers (92% of completers, ~71% of intent-to-treat)" is the most accurate framing available in secondary literature.
- **Resolution**: Use completer caveat explicitly when citing the 92% figure.

### Uncertain 3: Whether Foundation-affiliated authorship counts as conflict of interest in the technical sense
- **Validity score**: 9/10 (the COI exists; what's uncertain is how much weight to put on it)
- **Why uncertain**: COI in psychotherapy research is contested terrain. Foundation publicly states it operates at arm's length and has independent review processes. Concept research's framing ("does not invalidate findings, but is a non-trivial conflict of interest that the reader should track") is calibrated correctly. The empirical question — do Foundation-funded IFS trials report systematically larger effects than non-Foundation-funded IFS trials — cannot be answered because there are essentially no non-Foundation-funded IFS trials to compare.
- **Resolution**: Frame the COI as a known pattern that limits inference, and as a reason independent replication is the most-needed research.

## Important Addition Surfaced During Validation

### Comeau et al. 2024 — PARTS Online Group IFS for PTSD (separate from Ally 2025 PARTS-SUD)
- **Citation**: Comeau A, Smith LJ, Smith L, Soumerai Rea H, Ward MC, Creedon TB, Sweezy M, Rosenberg LG, Schuman-Olivier Z. *Online group-based internal family systems treatment for posttraumatic stress disorder: Feasibility and acceptability of the program for alleviating and resolving trauma and stress.* Psychological Trauma: Theory, Research, Practice, and Policy, 2024. PubMed: 38934934.
- **Design**: Single-arm proof-of-concept feasibility study.
- **Sample**: n=15 (13 responders).
- **Findings**: 73% completed 12+ of 16 weekly group sessions; 92% (12/13 responders) would recommend; 100% found it helpful. CAPS-5 PTSD d = −0.7 at week 16, d = −0.9 at week 24. 53% achieved clinically meaningful response (≥10-point CAPS-5 reduction) by week 24. Secondary improvements in decentering, self-compassion, and emotion regulation (all p < .05).
- **Funding**: Foundation for Self Leadership.
- **Why this matters for the page**: Effect sizes here (d ≈ 0.7–0.9) are within the credible range for PTSD psychotherapy outcomes, in contrast to Hodgdon 2021's d = −4.46. This actually *strengthens* the claim that IFS produces real PTSD effects in the right ballpark when measured carefully — but it's still single-arm, still Foundation-funded, still small. The concept research should be updated to include this study.
- **Recommendation**: Add a new component (Component 3.5 or rename Component 4) and update the conditions-by-quality matrix in the diagrams. Treat this as preliminary confirmatory signal that complements the Hodgdon 2021 result.

## Research Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: Add Comeau et al. 2024 PARTS PTSD pilot to the explanation
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Driven by**: Newly surfaced study; concept research is missing it; it's the most credible-sized PTSD effect estimate in the IFS literature.
- **Why**: A reader assessing PTSD evidence needs to see both the implausible Hodgdon d = −4.46 and the credible Comeau d ≈ 0.7–0.9, side by side. The contrast is itself informative.

### Opportunity 2: Verify Haddock 2017 funding source explicitly
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: Concept research lists Haddock funding as "not disclosed" but Foundation-related funding is plausible based on Foundation press materials.
- **Why**: If Haddock 2017 was Foundation-funded, the concentration claim is even stronger.

### Opportunity 3: Search for any independent (non-Foundation) IFS RCT registered on clinicaltrials.gov
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: A genuinely independent ongoing trial would change the picture if it exists.
- **Why**: Honest reporting requires checking the trial registry, not just published literature.

### Opportunity 4: Locate the Hodgdon 2021 limitations text directly
- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: The "likely inflated" admission is well-supported by independent secondary reporting but cannot be triple-verified from the gated primary text in the time available.
- **Why**: Verbatim quotation strengthens the rebuttal to the d = −4.46 marketing usage.

### Opportunity 5: Find the Sentio University, Society for the Advancement of Psychotherapy, or other independent commentary on equivalence vs. superiority in Haddock
- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: The concept research's equivalence-overreach framing is methodologically correct but would be strengthened by an independent published voice making the same argument.
