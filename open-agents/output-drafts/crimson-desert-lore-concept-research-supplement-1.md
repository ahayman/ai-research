# Supplement 1: The Time-Loop / Avatar Framing

## Why this supplement exists

The original concept-research pass missed a major piece of the cosmology. A user reports a streamer who finished the game claiming, on the basis of late-game / post-game readable books, that:

1. Kliff is an avatar of the four Guardians.
2. The Guardians have been rewinding time across many iterations to find a configuration in which Kliff defeats Umbra.
3. Umbra does not remember individual timelines but gains power across them.
4. The current playthrough is the last possible iteration.
5. Some creatures retain memory across timelines, which is why NPCs greet Kliff as if they know him.

This supplement evaluates each claim against post-launch community sources. **The headline finding: claims 1, 2, and 4 are confirmed in-game lore (located in Alustin's Axiom Archive, a late-game / post-game accessible body of books). Claim 3 is partially supported but with a different mechanism than the streamer described. Claim 5 is mostly fan inference, not confirmed in-fiction text — the strongest specific case (Goyen) is a different mechanic entirely.**

This means the original research's Research Gap #2 ("the fourth guardian") and Gap #3 ("the post-game library framing is an inference") were **wrong** — there *is* a body of post-game-accessible lore in Alustin's archive that fundamentally reframes the entire story, and the streamer's description was substantively accurate, not speculation.

## Verdict on each user-supplied claim

### Claim 1 — Kliff is an avatar of the Guardians: CONFIRMED

Multiple independent community sources, citing the in-game Alustin's Archive books, agree: the Guardians cannot directly intervene against Umbra (an entity of comparable cosmic order), so they require an **avatar** to act in their behalf. Kliff is that avatar. Crucially, the Kliff the player controls is not Kliff's "natural" personality — community summaries report the books explicitly state the Guardians **manipulated this iteration's Kliff to be low-emotion / high-altruism**, on the theory that previous, more-emotional Kliffs failed because attachment compromised the mission. This is the in-fiction explanation for the persistent player complaint that "Kliff has no personality."

This is a stronger claim than my original research's "Kliff is being silently auditioned by the four guardians from the first hour" (Example 3 of the original doc). The original framing was correct in direction but understated: he is not being auditioned in *this* timeline; he was selected across many prior timelines, and this timeline's Kliff is a deliberately engineered version optimized from prior failures.

### Claim 2 — Guardians rewind time / run iterations: CONFIRMED

Sources converge on a specific number: the player's playthrough is the **108th loop**. The number is widely interpreted as a deliberate Buddhist allusion (the 108 defilements one must overcome to attain enlightenment), which is a plausibly-deliberate authorial choice given the game's other South/East Asian iconographic borrowings.

The mechanism, per community summaries: when Kliff fails to defeat Umbra, the Guardians use Abyss power to **rewind the timeline** and try again with adjusted variables — Kliff's personality, the timing/configuration of allies, which factions are aligned how. Each failed loop becomes data; the 108th loop is described as the "answer sheet" assembled from those failures. This explains in-fiction why so many events and NPC introductions feel arbitrary or convenient: they are the Guardians' optimized stage-management.

Specific failed iterations attributed to community write-ups (treat with mild skepticism — these may be summarized rather than verbatim from the books):

- **Cycle 17** — Guardians attempted to make Kearush an ally of Kliff; the attempt failed when humans imprisoned Kearush in a zoo / cage before he could be reached. (This re-frames the chapter-5 Kearush boss fight: in the prior cycle he was supposed to be a friend; in this cycle the Guardians let the imprisonment play out because the resulting fight produces a useful outcome.)
- **Cycle 26** — Kliff became too emotionally attached to the Greymanes and Pailune, and failed his mission against Umbra as a result. (This is the failure that caused the Guardians to engineer the current low-emotion Kliff.)

These specific cycle numbers should be treated as **community-reported but not directly verified by me from primary in-game text**. The 108th-loop top-line claim is corroborated across at least three independent community sources and is treated as confirmed.

### Claim 3 — Umbra accumulates power across cycles: PARTIAL / DIFFERENT MECHANISM

The streamer's claim that "Umbra doesn't remember individual timelines but gains power with each cycle" is **not** the framing community sources use. What community sources actually say: in the 108th loop **specifically**, Umbra has gained a new capacity — it can now manipulate mortals as the Guardians do, via its vessel **Caliburn**. This is the structural change that makes the 108th loop different from all prior loops, and it is why this is the *last* loop the Guardians can attempt (see Claim 4).

So the corrected version of the streamer's claim is:

- **Confirmed**: Something Umbra-side has changed across cycles such that the Guardians cannot attempt another loop after this one.
- **Confirmed (more narrowly)**: Specifically, Umbra acquired its own avatar-mechanism (Caliburn) — a structural escalation, not a continuous power-accumulation curve.
- **Not confirmed**: A general "Umbra gets stronger every loop" mechanic. The escalation is treated as a single threshold event (acquiring Caliburn as a vessel-and-strategist), not gradual.
- **Not confirmed and not contradicted**: Whether Umbra retains memory across loops. Community sources do not address this directly. The streamer's version ("does not remember") is plausible but unsupported.

This is the claim where the streamer's paraphrase is the loosest. Treat as "directionally correct, mechanically different."

### Claim 4 — This is the last cycle: CONFIRMED

Multiple community sources state directly that the 108th loop is the **last loop the Guardians can attempt**, because Umbra-via-Caliburn has gained the same kind of mortal-manipulation capacity the Guardians have. The framing implies — though I have not located the verbatim in-game line — that further rewinds would no longer be reliably engineerable from the Guardian side, because Umbra would now also be optimizing across cycles. This makes the player's 108th-loop run a "last shot."

This is the single highest-stakes piece of cosmology in the game, and it is **invisible to players who don't access Alustin's archive**.

### Claim 5 — Some creatures retain memory across timelines: MOSTLY FAN INFERENCE

The streamer's strongest specific case is Goyen (chapter 9 boss). Community sources confirm that **Goyen is canonically a previous-cycle Kliff who remained in the Abyss** rather than reincarnating into a fresh loop. The Database character entry for Goyen uses the internal asset ID `boss_old_kliff_swordshield` (literally "old Kliff"), which is essentially developer-side confirmation that the boss is mechanically a previous Kliff. The Greymane Howling fighting style and "mirror match" boss design are also consistent.

However, "Goyen is a previous Kliff stuck in the Abyss" is a **different mechanism** from "creatures retain memory across timelines." Goyen is not a normal Pywel resident who remembers prior iterations. He is a previous Kliff-iteration who, instead of resetting with the world, was preserved in the Abyss (the substrate above the timeline) and so is still there in the current cycle. The Abyss-as-supra-temporal substrate is what preserves him, not a memory-bleed mechanic in normal creatures.

I could not find any community source confirming a general mechanic in which **NPCs in Pywel retain memory across loops**. This claim appears to be either:

- A streamer over-extrapolating from the Goyen case (the most likely reading), or
- A reference to a specific late-game scene I could not surface through this research pass, or
- Conflation with the Visione memory-fragment system (which lets Kliff *view* preserved memories but does not give NPCs persistent across-loop awareness), or
- Conflation with NPCs reacting to Kliff's Greymane reputation and his post-Pailune-fall fame, which can plausibly read on a streamer's first watch as "they know him from somewhere."

**Verdict on Claim 5**: The "Goyen is a previous Kliff" piece is confirmed, but the broader "creatures retain memory across timelines" mechanic is **fan inference, not confirmed lore**. The original research's intuition that there is no NPC-memory-bleed mechanic was correct.

### Claim 6 — NPCs greet Kliff as if they know him: UNABLE TO VERIFY AS A SYSTEMATIC PATTERN

I was unable to find community write-ups identifying a systematic pattern of NPCs greeting Kliff as if from a prior life. What community sources *do* describe:

- **NPCs reacting to Kliff's reputation** (Greymane leader, post-Pailune fame) — confirmed, this is a normal in-fiction recognition mechanic and is exactly the sort of thing that could read as "memory" on a streamer's first watch.
- **The Visione memory-fragment scenes** (chapter 2 onward) — confirmed; Kliff views *other people's* preserved memories, which can include moments he was never present for.
- **Sebastian's "as though fate had a hand in it" framing for re-meeting Kliff in Varnia** — phrased fatalistically in community summaries, but not as cross-loop recognition.

If the streamer cited a specific scene (e.g., "this NPC in Hernand greets Kliff and Kliff has never met them"), the most defensible reading is that the NPC is **recognizing the Greymane / Pailune-survivor reputation**, not Kliff personally across loops. **Treat the streamer's NPC-recognition claim as unverified pending a specific scene citation.**

## Where the in-game text actually lives

This is the structurally important finding for the original research's Gap #3. The post-game / late-game-accessible body of lore the streamer described is real, and it is located in:

- **Alustin's Axiom Archive** — accessible during the late-game story chapters as a physical location inside the Abyss; community sources describe the cycle/avatar lore as concentrated in books readable here.
- **Archive Records: Vol. I and subsequent volumes** — in-game items / readable books; the database entry for Vol. I describes it as "writings from an unknown author, from which an unfathomable sense of desperation and agony seems to bleed from its pages." This is consistent with the contents being a Guardian's record of cycle failures.
- **The Words of Alustin (chapter 53 main mission)** — narrative trigger for Alustin-archive content.

The original research correctly identified the Library of Providence / Axiom Archive as the place where the cosmology lives. What the original research **missed** was that the cosmology there is not "merely" the four-guardians / Abyss-balance / Umbra story — it is also the time-loop / Kliff-as-engineered-avatar story. These two layers stack: the four-guardians cosmology is the surface read of the archive; the cycle-mechanics is the deep read.

## Implications for the existing webpage

**Recommendation: minor-to-moderate revision, not a full rewrite.** Specifically:

1. **Add a new spoiler-blurred subsection to Component 5 (the Four Guardians)** explaining the cycle/avatar mechanic, anchored on Alustin's Axiom Archive as the in-game source. Frame it as "what the late-game books reveal that the cutscenes do not."
2. **Update Component 4 (the Abyss)** to add: the Abyss is also a substrate **across timelines**, not just across worlds. This is the rule that lets the Guardians rewind time and that lets Goyen persist as a previous-Kliff.
3. **Update the Goyen reference in Component 6 / Diagram 5** with the "previous-Kliff trapped in the Abyss" framing. This is a clean spoiler-blurred reveal that lands hard.
4. **Update the chapter-by-chapter arc** to flag that chapter 5 (Kearush) and chapter 9 (Goyen, Master Du) acquire much heavier weight under the cycle reading. Don't restructure the arc; annotate it.
5. **Update the Common Misconceptions list** to retire the misconception "there is no hidden post-credits / post-game library reveal." The original research had this *as a misconception correction* and the correction was wrong. There *is* a body of late-game lore that the streamer was correctly excited about.
6. **Update the glossary** with: 108th Loop, Cycle (as a cosmological term), Avatar (Kliff's role), Goyen (= previous-cycle Kliff in the Abyss), Caliburn-as-Umbra-vessel-and-cycle-manipulator.
7. **Add a clearly-labeled Community Theory footnote** for the Cycle 17 / Cycle 26 specifics, the "creatures retain memory across timelines" claim, and the NPC-recognition-as-cross-loop reading. These are the parts of the streamer's account that are **directionally suggestive but not source-verifiable** as direct in-game text.
8. **Diagram 6 (Cosmological Hierarchy)** should add a horizontal time-axis showing prior loops collapsed into the current one. This is the single most powerful diagram update.
9. **Diagram 7 (Main Story Shows vs. Library Reveals)** gains a strong row: "Kliff is a quiet protagonist with no personality" → "[SPOILER] Kliff was deliberately engineered to be low-emotion / high-altruism by the Guardians across 108 cycles."

## What the streamer probably saw

Best inference: the streamer encountered the Alustin Axiom Archive late-game or in post-game cleanup, read a sequence of books that lay out the cycle mechanic in the Guardians' voice, and accurately summarized the headline claims to the user. Where the streamer's account diverged from confirmed text was in the soft edges: Umbra's exact power-accumulation mechanic, and the generality of the cross-timeline memory claim. The core (avatar, 108th cycle, last cycle, Goyen-as-previous-Kliff) is well-supported.
