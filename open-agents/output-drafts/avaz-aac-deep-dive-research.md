# Research Notes: Avaz AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

Avaz AAC is an augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) app developed by Avaz Inc., originally headquartered in Chennai, India with offices in Silicon Valley. Created by Ajit Narayanan, an IIT Madras-educated engineer, Avaz was India's first AAC intervention and has grown into a globally recognized communication solution for individuals with speech disabilities including autism, cerebral palsy, intellectual disability, aphasia, and learning disabilities. The app uniquely supports both iOS and Android platforms and offers communication in 8+ Indian languages, making it one of the most linguistically diverse AAC tools available.

In October 2025, Avaz became a wholly owned subsidiary of PRC-Saltillo, a leading global AAC company based in Wooster, Ohio. This acquisition expanded PRC-Saltillo's reach into India and Southeast Asia while maintaining Avaz's existing product lineup and pricing. The app features over 40,000 SymbolStix pictures, Fitzgerald key color coding, expressive voice tones powered by AI, YouTube integration, GIF support, and a built-in Dashboard tutorial system for caregiver training.

Avaz has received numerous accolades including the MIT Technology Review TR35 "Innovator of the Year" award (2011) and the National Award for Empowerment of People with Disabilities from the President of India (2010). With pricing starting at $9.99/month and a lifetime option at $199.99, Avaz positions itself as one of the most affordable full-featured AAC apps on the market.

## Historical Context

### Timeline

- **2009**: Ajit Narayanan begins working with special needs schools in India
- **2010**: Avaz hardware device launched; wins National Award for Empowerment of People with Disabilities from the President of India
- **2011**: Ajit Narayanan wins MIT Technology Review TR35 "Innovator of the Year" - first disability product to receive this
- **2012**: Android and iPad versions launched
- **2013**: Ajit Narayanan delivers TED talk in Long Beach, California
- **2015**: FreeSpeech app invented
- **2022**: Major app redesign with expressive tones, media-rich icons
- **October 2025**: Acquired by PRC-Saltillo

### Founding Story

Ajit Narayanan, an IIT Madras graduate, worked with special needs schools in India before building a tablet called "Avaz" - meaning "Voice" in Hindi. It was India's first AAC device. By partnering with Asian hardware manufacturers, the original device cost ~$800 compared to $5,000-$10,000 for US equivalents.

## Core Features

### Communication Modes
- **Picture Mode**: SymbolStix symbols with Fitzgerald key color coding
- **Keyboard Mode**: Text-based with predictive text
- **Quick Toggle**: Seamless switching between modes

### SymbolStix Library
- 40,000+ total pictures, 15,000+ pre-loaded
- Customizable with personal photos

### Fitzgerald Key Color Coding
- Words color-coded by part of speech
- Consistent with classroom materials

### Core Word Vocabulary
- Research-based word order
- 3 graded vocabulary levels
- "Frozen Row" keeps core words accessible
- Consistent motor patterns across levels

### Expressive Tones (AI-Powered)
- Tones: excitement, frustration, sarcasm, sadness, curiosity

### Media-Rich Communication
- YouTube integration (built-in search, custom start/stop)
- GIFs with adjustable loop count
- Animation reinforcement

### Visual Themes

### Avaz Dashboard (Caregiver Training)

### Progress Tracking
- Graphs and charts for development

## Strengths

1. Cross-platform (iOS + Android)
2. Affordable pricing ($9.99/mo, $199.99 lifetime)
3. 8+ Indian languages
4. Built-in caregiver training

## Weaknesses

1. Speech speed/pitch controls limited
2. Less established in US market
3. Subscription model frustrates some users
4. Sentence prediction less robust than competitors
5. Navigation can overwhelm users with apraxia
6. Smaller third-party ecosystem
