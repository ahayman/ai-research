## 2. How Proloquo Differs from Proloquo2Go

### Key Architectural Differences

| Feature | Proloquo (new) | Proloquo2Go (legacy) |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Grid Size** | Fixed 60 buttons per page | 23 adjustable grid sizes (9 to 144 buttons) |
| **Vocabulary** | Crescendo Evolution (16,000+ words) | Crescendo (4,750 unique + 7,250 in storage) |
| **Languages** | English only (6 regional variants) | English, Spanish, French, Dutch + bilingual |
| **Pricing** | Subscription ($9.99/mo or $99.99/yr) | One-time purchase ($249.99) |
| **Companion app** | Proloquo Coach (included) | No companion app |
| **Thinking Space** | Yes (unique feature) | No |
| **Focus Mode** | Yes (unique feature) | No |
| **Progressive Language** | Via Focus Mode approach | Via Progressive Language hiding/revealing buttons |
| **Switch Scanning** | Via iOS Switch Control only | Built-in dedicated scanning (5 modes) |
| **Motor Planning** | Optimized for fixed grid consistency | Varies by grid size selection |

### Coexistence, Not Replacement
Proloquo is NOT replacing Proloquo2Go. Both products coexist and serve different user needs:
- **Proloquo** is recommended for the vast majority of English-language iPad users who need symbol-supported AAC and can physically access the 60-button grid.
- **Proloquo2Go** remains the right solution for users who:
  - Need smaller grid sizes (physical access limitations preventing use of 60-button grid)
  - Need languages other than English (Spanish, French, Dutch)
  - Need bilingual support
  - Need dedicated built-in switch scanning (beyond iOS Switch Control)
  - Prefer a one-time purchase over subscription

### The 60-Button Grid Decision
AssistiveWare chose the fixed 60-button grid based on data from over 10,000 Proloquo2Go users (with anonymous consent-based data collection):
- The recommended Proloquo2Go grid was 7x11 (77 buttons), but almost 80% of users were using smaller grids
- Research showed users with Proloquo (60 buttons) and Proloquo2Go (77 buttons) demonstrated the same language outcomes
- 60 buttons provides sufficient efficiency while being less overwhelming to parents and educators

## 3. Proloquo Coach (Companion App)

### Overview
- **Separate app** (App Store ID: 1488458662), free with active Proloquo subscription
- **Available on:** iPhone and iPad
- **Target audience:** Parents, families, educators, and communication partners (not the AAC user directly)

### What It Does
Proloquo Coach is a guided learning experience that teaches communication partners how to support AAC users. It provides:

1. **Structured learning "books"** covering major areas of AAC best practice:
   - Start with "What is AAC?" basics
   - Progress through individual communication functions (making choices, refusing/protesting, etc.)
   - Each book has two components: **Learn** chapters (knowledge) and **Practice** chapters (hands-on)

2. **Step-by-step modeling guidance:** Teaches parents how to model AAC with beginning users

3. **Personalized feedback and insights:** Practice at a guided pace with personalized feedback

4. **Team building:** Guidance on including teachers, family members, and private therapists to create a support team around the child

5. **AAC expert support:** In-app access to AssistiveWare's AAC experts (available 24/7)

### Guiding Principles
- Parents learn to **add something new to something they are already doing** (building habits into existing routines)
- AAC is integrated into **existing multimodal communication** occurring naturally within the family
- Focus remains on the **communication connection and relationship** between parent and child

## 4. Vocabulary System

### Crescendo Evolution Vocabulary
Proloquo uses **Crescendo Evolution**, a next-generation version of the Crescendo vocabulary system originally developed for Proloquo2Go:

- **16,000+ words** -- four times more than any other AAC system (per AssistiveWare's claims)
- **Average 1.6 taps per word** to build phrases (extremely efficient navigation)
- Research-based: informed by frequency data, simulation runs, clinical insights, and anonymous user data from thousands of Proloquo2Go/Proloquo4Text users

### Organization Structure
- **9 tabs** allow users to move between different kinds of words
- **First 7 tabs** are organized by word kinds (parts of speech) and contain 1-2 pages of core words representing general concepts
- **Semantic clustering:** Words are grouped by related meanings rather than alphabetically
  - Adjectives: horizontal pairs of opposites (e.g., wet/dry) clustered conceptually
  - Verbs: clustered by related meanings
  - Nouns: organized by fringe categories
- **Each word appears as a button only once** (except words with multiple distinct meanings), to minimize the number of motor plans needed

### Core Words Philosophy
- Core words (I, you, go, do, want, etc.) make up ~80% of daily communication
- Core words are the foundation, always accessible from the Home Page
- Fringe words (topic-specific vocabulary) are organized in tabs and folders

### Vocabulary Customization
- Users **cannot change the base vocabulary** (intentional design decision to maintain consistency)
- Users **can add custom buttons** and personalize fringe vocabulary
- The base vocabulary is locked to preserve motor planning patterns and ensure consistent word locations across all users/environments

### Contrast with Proloquo2Go's Crescendo
- Proloquo2Go Crescendo: 4,750 unique words + 7,250 pre-categorized in storage = ~12,000 total
- Proloquo Crescendo Evolution: 16,000+ words, fundamentally redesigned organization

## 5. Access Methods

### Touch (Primary)
- Designed primarily for direct touch access on iPad/iPhone
- Fixed 60-button grid with buttons larger than standard iPad UI elements
- Optimized for motor planning with consistent button placement

### Switch Scanning
- **Proloquo (new app):** Compatible with **iOS Switch Control** -- single or two switch scanning
- **Proloquo2Go:** Has **built-in dedicated scanning** with 5 scanning modes:
  - Automatic scanning
  - Automatic selecting
  - Inverse scanning
  - Step scanning
  - Automatic step scanning
  - Supports external switches or screen-as-switch
- **Important limitation:** Proloquo (the new app) relies on iOS-level Switch Control rather than having its own built-in scanning engine. This is one reason Proloquo2Go remains recommended for some switch users.

### Eye Gaze
- Compatible with **iOS 18+ native eye-tracking support**
- Compatible with Made for iPad certified eye tracking cameras:
  - **Hiru** (by Irisbond)
  - **TD Pilot** (by Tobii Dynavox)
  - **VersaEye**
- Large, adjustable toolbar and button targets suitable for eye tracking

### Head Tracking
- Fully compatible with **HeadMouse Nano** and similar head tracking devices
- Described as one of the most effective access methods for users who cannot touch the screen, especially combined with a switch to click

### VoiceOver
- Compatible with Apple's **VoiceOver** screen reader for users with vision impairments

### General iOS Accessibility
- Compatible with all standard iOS accessibility features (AssistiveTouch, Dwell Control, etc.)

## 6. Physical Access Features

### Grid Size
- **Proloquo:** Fixed at 60 buttons per page (designed to be optimal balance of efficiency and accessibility)
- **Proloquo2Go:** 23 pre-programmed grid sizes ranging from 9 to 144 buttons per page

### Motor Planning Support
- Consistent button locations across all pages and folders
- Core words from Home Page appear in the exact same position on first page of each tab
- Words appear only once to minimize the number of different motor plans needed
- Fixed grid means motor plans are never disrupted by grid size changes

### Button Design
- Buttons in Proloquo are larger than regular iPad icons and UI elements
- Symbol + text label on each button
- Adjustable toolbar size

### Customization in Proloquo
- Cannot change grid size or base vocabulary layout (intentional)
- Can add custom buttons
- Can personalize fringe vocabulary
- Focus Mode can highlight specific buttons

### Customization in Proloquo2Go (for comparison)
- 23 grid sizes
- Full vocabulary editing
- Progressive Language to hide/reveal buttons
- Extensive appearance customization

   - Published in *ASHA Perspectives on Augmentative and Alternative Communication*
   - DOI: 10.1044/aac18.4.137
   - Foundational paper on using Proloquo2Go with autistic individuals

2. **"Proloquo2Go Enhances Classroom Performance in Children with Autism Spectrum Disorder"** (Collette, Brix, Brennan, DeRoma, Muir, 2019)
   - Published in *OTJR: Occupation, Therapy Journal of Research* (SAGE)
   - PubMed ID: 30255726
   - Found that children using Proloquo2Go required less support during classroom activities than when using no technology

3. **"The Proloquo2Go App in Reducing Echolalia in Autism"**
   - Published via ERIC (ED577783)
   - Found significant reduction in echolalic utterances when using AAC intervention

4. **"AAC apps for aphasia: a pilot study on the role of intuition and learning"** (2021)
   - PubMed ID: 33756090
   - Participants with aphasia performed significantly better using Proloquo2Go for average search time and rate of cell selection

5. **Core Vocabulary AAC Interventions Review** (2023)
   - Published in *Review Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders* (Springer)
   - Found SGD-based systems (including Proloquo2Go) were used in 40% of reviewed core vocabulary studies

## 10. Receptive Support Features

### Thinking Space
- Proloquo's **Thinking Space** is a flexible area where users can arrange and explore symbols
- Designed for both expressive and receptive purposes: users can visualize and explore thoughts and meanings
- Even the earliest communicators can use it without needing to form full linear sentences
- Contents can be saved as photos, printed, or shared

### Visual Symbol Support
- Over 10,000+ symbols (Proloquo2Go has 27,000+)
- Symbols paired with text labels to support literacy development and comprehension
- Semantic organization helps users understand word relationships and categories

### Scaffolding
- Proloquo provides scaffolding for learning categories, concepts, and sentence structure
- Designed because many children learning AAC struggle to learn these essential aspects of language
- Consistent page set and logical fringe word categories support concept development

### Focus Mode (Receptive Guidance)
- Uses light and motion to **attract attention to specific buttons** in the grid
- Non-selected words appear dimmed but remain accessible
- Helps beginning users or users with attention challenges understand what buttons are available and relevant

### Limitations
- Proloquo is primarily designed as an **expressive** communication tool
- No specific "receptive language mode" or dedicated comprehension exercises were found in the research
- The receptive benefits come from the symbol-based interface, semantic organization, and modeling support rather than explicit receptive language training features

## 11. Cognitive/Simplification Features

### Focus Mode
- The primary cognitive simplification feature in Proloquo
- Uses **light and motion** to gently guide attention to key words without hiding or removing other vocabulary
- Non-target buttons appear dimmed but remain accessible (not locked out)
- Focus Mode persists across navigation -- if the student leaves the tab with target words, the path back uses light/motion to invite return

### Progressive Language (Proloquo2Go)
- Proloquo2Go offers **Progressive Language**, which hides/masks some buttons to start
- Words are revealed in a logical order based on language development and frequency
- As the user develops language, new words can be gradually introduced
- Buttons stay in the same location throughout, so motor plans are never disrupted

### Simplification Philosophy Difference
- **Proloquo:** Does NOT hide vocabulary. Instead uses Focus Mode to attract attention while keeping full vocabulary available. Philosophy: "Don't limit access; guide attention."
- **Proloquo2Go:** Can hide/reveal vocabulary progressively. More traditional simplification approach.

### For Beginning Communicators
- Thinking Space allows beginning communicators to express ideas without forming linear sentences
- Semantic clustering helps with concept learning
- Large, clear buttons (60-button fixed grid) are easier to scan than dense grids
- Proloquo Coach teaches communication partners how to support users at their level

### Note
No specific features were found for users with intellectual disability-specific cognitive support (e.g., simplified modes for adults with cognitive impairments, visual schedules, or task-based communication boards). The simplification approach is primarily through Focus Mode and Thinking Space.

## 12. Social/Pragmatic Features

### Social Communication in Vocabulary
- The Crescendo Evolution vocabulary includes social/pragmatic vocabulary as part of the core word set
- Communication functions like greeting, refusing/protesting, requesting, commenting, and asking questions are built into the vocabulary structure
- Proloquo Coach specifically teaches communication functions (making choices, refusing/protesting, etc.)

### PODD/SCERTS Compatibility
- Proloquo does **NOT** use the PODD (Pragmatic Organisation Dynamic Display) system
- PODD is implemented in a separate app called **simPODD** (by Assistiveware's competitors)
- No specific SCERTS (Social Communication, Emotional Regulation, and Transactional Support) integration was found
- Proloquo uses its own Crescendo Evolution vocabulary system, which is distinct from PODD, LAMP, Unity, or other AAC vocabulary systems

### Conversation Tools
- **Thinking Space:** Can be used for turn-taking and conversation exploration
- **ExpressivePower:** Emotional expression through voice and sounds
- **Quick phrases/pre-stored messages:** Available in Proloquo4Text; research did not confirm equivalent feature in Proloquo itself (Proloquo is symbol-based, not text-based)

### Limitations
- No evidence of dedicated social stories features
- No evidence of conversation partner prompts or scripts
- No visual scene displays (VSD) for social contexts
- The social/pragmatic support is embedded in the vocabulary organization and Proloquo Coach's teaching rather than dedicated pragmatic features

## 13. Rate Enhancement / Efficiency Features

### Motor Planning Efficiency
- **1.6 taps per word average** to build phrases in Proloquo -- this is the primary rate enhancement mechanism
- Consistent button locations mean users develop automaticity (muscle memory for word locations)
- Words appear only once to minimize motor plans

### Vocabulary Efficiency Design
- Most frequently used words placed on first page of each tab
- Home Page words appear in exact same spot on first page of opened tabs
- 9-tab organization minimizes navigation depth
- Semantic clustering means related words are nearby

### Prediction Features
- **Proloquo (symbol-based):** No specific word/sentence prediction features were found -- the efficiency comes from vocabulary organization and motor planning rather than predictive algorithms
- **Proloquo4Text (text-based):** Features **PolyPredix** self-learning word and sentence prediction, abbreviation expansion, and stored phrases
- **Proloquo2Go:** Added advanced word prediction in later versions

### Abbreviation Expansion
- **Not found in Proloquo** (symbol-based app)
- **Available in Proloquo4Text:** Abbreviations (minimum 2 characters) expand to full text. Configurable expansion triggers (after space, after return, or always)

### Quick Phrases / Pre-stored Messages
- **Proloquo4Text:** Has Phrases and Quick Talk "Quick Blocks" for storing common phrases, sentences, and paragraphs. Categories include contact details, medical information, frequently used content
- **Proloquo/Proloquo2Go:** The efficient vocabulary organization and Thinking Space serve a similar function for symbol-based communication

### Key Insight
Proloquo's rate enhancement strategy is fundamentally different from text-based AAC apps. Instead of prediction algorithms, it relies on:
1. Massive pre-organized vocabulary (16,000+ words)
2. Minimal taps per word (1.6 average)
3. Consistent motor plans (words never move)
4. Semantic organization (related words clustered together)

## 14. Target Population

### Primary Users
- **Nonspeaking individuals** who need symbol-supported AAC
- People with autism, Down syndrome, cerebral palsy, Angelman syndrome, apraxia of speech, and other conditions affecting speech
- Anyone who can physically access an iPad independently (tap buttons in a 60-button grid)

### Age Range
- **Toddlers to adults** -- designed to grow with the user over their lifespan
- Children's voices available for younger users
- Vocabulary is comprehensive enough for sophisticated adult communication

### Specific Conditions Served
- Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) -- most commonly referenced
- Cerebral palsy
- Down syndrome
- Angelman syndrome
- Apraxia of speech
- Aphasia (with limitations)
- ALS/MND (voice banking becomes relevant)

## Summary of Key Differentiators

**What makes Proloquo unique in the AAC market:**

1. **Subscription model with Coach included** -- shifts from expensive one-time purchase to affordable monthly access with built-in parent training
2. **Largest AAC vocabulary** -- 16,000+ words in Crescendo Evolution, 4-7x larger than competitors
3. **1.6 taps per word** -- extremely efficient despite massive vocabulary
4. **Fixed 60-button grid** -- controversial but data-driven decision that prioritizes motor planning consistency
5. **Focus Mode** -- novel approach to scaffolding that guides without restricting
6. **Thinking Space** -- unique non-linear communication area for exploring thoughts
7. **Proloquo Coach** -- no other major AAC app includes a structured parent/educator training companion
8. **Acapela Neural Voices** -- first AAC app to use this technology; genuine children's voices
9. **Regional English variants** -- 6 regional vocabularies reflecting cultural and linguistic differences
10. **2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award** -- first AAC app to win this recognition

**Key limitations:**
- English only (Proloquo2Go covers Spanish, French, Dutch)
- No bilingual support
- Fixed grid size may exclude users needing smaller/larger grids
- Relies on iOS Switch Control rather than built-in scanning
- Apple ecosystem only (no Android)
- Subscription model may be barrier for some families (though cheaper than P2G's $250 one-time cost over short term)
- Most published clinical research is on Proloquo2Go, not the newer Proloquo app specifically
