# Research Notes: Fluent AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

Fluent AAC is a symbol-based augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) app for iPad, developed by Fluent AAC Technology S.R.L., a company registered in Timisoara, Romania (CUI 43282436, J35/3266/2020). The app is designed for children and adults with Autism, Down Syndrome, ALS, apraxia, stroke, or other conditions that affect a person's ability to use natural speech. Fluent AAC markets itself as the "#1 Rated AAC App for iPad" based on user feedback surveys, and combines clinical expertise with technological innovation to deliver a communication solution that grows with its users.

The app distinguishes itself through several innovative features: a grammar-aware word prediction system that conjugates icons to match sentence grammar, a "Hide Words" feature that supports progressive vocabulary learning while maintaining motor consistency, over 25,000 customizable illustrations, and a 3,000-5,000+ fringe word vocabulary with customizable vocabulary levels. Fluent AAC is designed by speech therapists and developed through collaboration with clinical experts, patient insight groups, and customers.

As of October 2025, the app is at version 7.1.0 and uses a subscription model with a 7-day free trial. The company also produces "Type and Speak," a companion text-based AAC app. While praised for its innovative approach and user-friendly design, some users have reported stability issues and the app has mixed App Store ratings.

## Core Features

### 1. Grammar-Aware Word Prediction
- Word prediction that changes and conjugates icons to match sentence grammar
- When forming sentences, the system predicts the next word based on grammatical context
- Icons are dynamically adjusted (e.g., verb tenses, plurals) to match the sentence being constructed
- "Plurals using the keyboard" explicitly mentioned in update notes
- This represents a significant innovation in symbol-based AAC, where most competing apps present static icon grids
- Addresses a key limitation of traditional AAC: grammatical expression

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/, https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fluent-aac-communication-app/id1489807378]

### 2. "Hide Words" Feature
- Users/therapists can hide words that haven't been targeted yet
- Allows starting with a small set of words and gradually expanding
- New words are revealed progressively as the user's communication expands
- Critically, hiding/unhiding words does NOT change the position of other words
- Supports motor automaticity: learned motor patterns remain intact
- Enables systematic vocabulary introduction without disrupting established motor plans
- Allows important exploration time for new vocabulary
- Therapists and caregivers can control the vocabulary exposure level

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/]

### 3. Motor Consistency
- Words never change position on the communication board
- Once an icon has a location, its placement remains consistent
- Users develop muscle memory for word locations
- Enables faster, more natural communication over time
- Motor plans become faster than visual scanning, reducing cognitive load
- Users can "move their fingers the way their brain remembers"
- Consistent with LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) principles
- Frees cognitive energy to think about what to say rather than where to find words

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/, https://aaccommunity.net/ccc/motor-planning/]

### 4. Vocabulary System
- 3,000-5,000+ fringe word vocabulary (sources vary; growth across versions)
- Customizable vocabulary levels
- Core words accessible on main display
- Fringe vocabulary organized for easy access
- Search feature to find any vocabulary word quickly
- Users can add custom buttons and their own photos
- Supports both beginning and advanced communicators
- No limit on language development claimed

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/fringe-words, https://www.fluentaac.com/core-words]

### 5. Illustrations & Customization
- 25,000+ illustrations available
- Illustrations can be made male or female
- Users can choose a character that suits them
- Icons described as "nicer than others and easy to understand"
- Users can add custom icons, change illustration styles
- Icon border colors are customizable
- Users can add their own photos as icons
- Fully customizable interface and vocabulary

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/, https://www.speechandlanguagekids.com/aac-apps-review/]

### 6. Text-to-Speech
- Built-in text-to-speech functionality
- Words pressed appear at top of screen to form sentences
- Sentences are spoken aloud when completed
- Multiple voice options available

### 7. Learning Resources
- Step-by-step tutorials built into the app
- Tutorials made by industry leaders
- Lessons and FAQ pages for AAC newcomers
- AAC implementation guidance on website
- Modeling activities and teaching resources
- Printable communication boards (free)

[Sources: https://www.fluentaac.com/learn-aac, https://www.fluentaac.com/communication-boards]

## Strengths

1. **#1 User Rating**: Placed first in AAC user feedback surveys
2. **Grammar Innovation**: Grammar-aware prediction/conjugation is relatively unique among symbol-based AAC apps
3. **Hide Words Feature**: Innovative approach to progressive vocabulary learning with motor consistency
4. **Motor Consistency**: Words never change position, supporting LAMP-like motor planning principles
5. **Large Illustration Library**: 25,000+ customizable illustrations with gender variants
6. **Designed by SLPs**: Developed in collaboration with speech therapists and clinical experts
7. **Flexible Pricing**: Multiple pricing tiers including affordable monthly option
8. **Free Trial**: 7-day free trial lets users evaluate before committing
9. **Comprehensive Learning Resources**: Built-in tutorials, online lessons, printable communication boards
10. **Holistic Approach**: Centers on the whole person, not just communication limitations
11. **Active Development**: Regular updates (version 7.1.0 as of Oct 2025)
12. **Companion App**: Type and Speak provides text-based AAC option for literate users

## Weaknesses

1. **Platform Limitation**: Primarily iPad/iOS only (no Android version)
2. **Stability Issues**: Users report crashing when editing, requiring device restart
3. **Mixed App Store Ratings**: Some reviews cite low ratings; stability complaints
4. **Customer Support**: Users report waiting weeks for support responses
5. **Subscription Model**: Ongoing cost vs. one-time purchase of competitors
6. **Newer/Smaller Company**: Registered 2020; less established than AssistiveWare or PRC-Saltillo
7. **Limited Pricing Transparency**: Pricing details not prominently displayed on website
8. **Smaller Community**: Less SLP community support/resources compared to Proloquo2Go or LAMP
9. **Less Research Base**: Fewer published studies compared to LAMP or Proloquo2Go

## User & SLP Community Reception

### Positive Feedback
- Rated #1 by AAC users in expert-reviewed surveys
- Illustrations praised as "nicer than others and easy to understand"
- Hide Words feature valued for progressive vocabulary learning
- Grammar prediction seen as innovative
- Free trial appreciated by families evaluating AAC options
- SLPs using Fluent AAC in clinics, hospitals, and schools worldwide

### Concerns
- App stability issues (crashing during editing)
- Slow customer support response times
- Subscription fatigue (ongoing cost for essential communication tool)
- Less established track record compared to competitors
- Limited independent research validation

### ASHA Perspective on Predictive Text in AAC (2025)
- ASHA Leader article (March 2025): "Predictive Text: Who Controls the Conversation?"
- Some SLPs concerned that predictive text may compromise user autonomy
- AAC users themselves expressed excitement about predictive text capabilities
- Growing acceptance of AI-driven prediction in AAC
