# Research Validation: AAC Technology — Speech-Generating Devices, Apps, and Access Methods

## Validation Summary

The AAC Technology concept research is well-sourced and highly accurate. All major factual claims were independently verified. The document provides a responsible, evidence-based overview of the AAC landscape with appropriate caveats. No significant errors were found.

**Overall Confidence Rating: 9/10** — High confidence. All major claims confirmed; minor details about pricing may shift over time.

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### Claim 1: Proloquo2Go uses the Crescendo vocabulary system (AssistiveWare)
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: AssistiveWare's official website confirms Proloquo2Go uses the research-based Crescendo vocabulary system with three pre-programmed vocabulary levels (Basic Communication, Intermediate Core, Advanced Core). Crescendo provides access to 4,750 unique words with another 7,250 pre-categorized in storage. The Progressive Language feature allows gradual revealing of buttons.
- **Sources**: [AssistiveWare Crescendo](https://www.assistiveware.com/crescendo), [Apple App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/proloquo2go-aac/id308368164)

### Claim 2: TouchChat (PRC-Saltillo) offers multiple vocabulary sets including WordPower
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: PRC-Saltillo's official site confirms TouchChat offers WordPower vocabulary and is available in English, Spanish, Canadian French, and Mandarin/Simplified Chinese. Regular price confirmed at $299.99.
- **Note**: The research document states TouchChat is "approximately $100 less than Proloquo2Go" — this needs checking as both appear to be in the ~$300 range. The language list in the research (English, Spanish, Hebrew, Arabic, Canadian French) may be partially inaccurate — Mandarin/Chinese appears in verified sources but Hebrew and Arabic were not confirmed in this search. **UNCERTAIN (4)** on the specific language list.
- **Sources**: [PRC-Saltillo TouchChat](https://prc-saltillo.com/apps/touchchat), [TouchChat App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/touchchat-hd-aac/id398860728)

### Claim 3: LAMP Words for Life uses Language Acquisition through Motor Planning principles with consistent icon placement
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple sources confirm LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) is a PRC-Saltillo product designed specifically for autism, emphasizing consistent icon placement to support motor learning.
- **Sources**: [PRC-Saltillo](https://prc-saltillo.com/)

### Claim 4: AAC abandonment rates estimated at 30-50%
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: The 30-50% abandonment rate figure appears in peer-reviewed literature, specifically referenced in a UK study on AAC professional decision-making (PMC6924798) and in the Taylor & Francis article "Rethinking device abandonment" (2023). Multiple sources corroborate this range.
- **Sources**: [PMC6924798](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6924798/), [Taylor & Francis AAC 2023](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/07434618.2023.2199859)

### Claim 5: AAC does not inhibit speech development
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple systematic reviews confirm this. A key review found that across 27/27 studies, AAC interventions showed an increase in speech production or no change — no studies reported a decrease. A systematic review specifically for children with autism found AAC "does not hinder speech production" and "may increase speech production." This is one of the most robust findings in the AAC literature.
- **Sources**: [ASHA Evidence Map](https://apps.asha.org/EvidenceMaps/Articles/ArticleSummary/48f75b29-1b15-4e0b-8e36-f65092d0d145), [PMC4017351](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4017351/), [Communication Community](https://www.communicationcommunity.com/does-aac-prevent-speech-development/)

### Claim 6: Jordyn Zimmerman — did not receive AAC until age 18, earned Bachelor's from Ohio University, Master's from Boston College, named to Forbes Accessibility 100 (2025), Board Chair of CommunicationFIRST
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: All details independently verified. Zimmerman received Proloquo2Go at 18, earned Bachelor's in Education Policy from Ohio University, Master's of Education from Boston College (currently pursuing MBA from Quantic). Named to inaugural Forbes Accessibility 100 in 2025. Board Chair of CommunicationFIRST. Apple Distinguished Educator and two-time Presidential appointee.
- **Note**: The research document does not mention her current MBA pursuit or Apple Distinguished Educator status — these are additions, not errors.
- **Sources**: [Jordyn Zimmerman official site](https://www.jordynzimmerman.com), [19th News](https://19thnews.org/2023/04/jordyn-zimmerman-nonspeaking-autistic-advocate-communication/), [Boston College Magazine](https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/sites/bc-magazine/fall-2024-issue/linden-lane/discovering-her-voice.html)

### Claim 7: Core vocabulary (~200-400 words) accounts for ~80% of utterances
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: This is a well-established principle in AAC literature. Multiple sources confirm the core-fringe distinction where approximately 200-400 high-frequency words constitute roughly 80% of daily communication.
- **Sources**: [AAC Institute](https://aacinstitute.org/core-vocabulary-and-the-aac-performance-report/), [The Autism Helper](https://theautismhelper.com/core-and-fringe-vocabulary-what-it-is-how-to-use-it/)

### Claim 8: SGD costs $5,000-$15,000+ for dedicated devices, $500-$1,500+ for consumer tablets
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED (general range)
- **Evidence**: Tobii Dynavox dedicated devices are well-documented as costing in the $5,000-$15,000+ range. Consumer tablet + app combinations are significantly less. These are standard industry price points.
- **Sources**: [Tobii Dynavox](https://us.tobiidynavox.com/pages/td-i-series)

### Claim 9: BCI for ALS — UC Davis demonstrated real-time speech synthesis from brain activity (2025)
- **Rating**: CONFIRMED
- **Evidence**: Multiple news sources report brain-computer interface advances in 2025 for speech synthesis from neural signals, including work from UC Davis/Berkeley. The research document correctly notes this is not yet clinically available for autism populations.
- **Sources**: [ScienceDaily 2025](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/06/250612081317.htm), [Berkeley Engineering](https://engineering.berkeley.edu/news/2025/03/brain-to-voice-neuroprosthesis-restores-naturalistic-speech/)

## Issues Found

### Minor Issues
1. **TouchChat language availability**: The research lists Hebrew and Arabic as available languages. Verified sources show English, Spanish, Canadian French, and Mandarin/Simplified Chinese. Hebrew and Arabic may be available through specific vocabulary files or page sets but were not confirmed as primary supported languages. **Recommend verifying.**
2. **TouchChat price comparison**: The claim that TouchChat is "approximately $100 less than Proloquo2Go" could not be confirmed — TouchChat appears to be $299.99 and Proloquo2Go pricing varies. This detail may be outdated or context-dependent.

### No Major Issues Found
The document is factually sound, well-balanced, and appropriately cautious in its claims.
