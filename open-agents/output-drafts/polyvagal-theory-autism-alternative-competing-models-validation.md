# Research Validation Report: Alternative & Competing Models for Polyvagal Theory

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: HIGH

- **Confirmed**: 17 claims (94%)
- **Uncertain**: 1 claim (6%)
- **Invalid**: 0 claims (0%)

**Key Findings**: The concept research's citation accuracy is high. Foundational citations for each of the five competing models (NVI, predictive processing, allostasis, constructed emotion, somatic markers) all check out against independent sources. The convergence/divergence map's claim that PVT and competitors make overlapping predictions on autism HRV data is well-supported. The one area of genuine uncertainty is the specific dating of the Sterling & Eyer 1988 chapter, which is correctly placed in 1988 in *Handbook of Life Stress* but appears in some sources as a separate working paper version.

**Systemic Issues**: None significant. The research is appropriately hedged where genuine uncertainty exists (e.g., the somatic marker hypothesis evidence base is correctly described as "mixed for specific operationalizations"). The chapter's central analytical claim — that PVT has no unique explanatory advantage on the data the user cares about — is well-supported by the independent literature.
