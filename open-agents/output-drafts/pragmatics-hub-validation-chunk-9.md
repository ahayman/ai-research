### Critical-attention findings (NOT INVALID, but require correction)

1. **Campbell 2003 sample-size attribution error (factual inaccuracy, not safety-critical).**
   Two files cite Campbell 2003 AJPH as having "310 femicide cases and 324 abused controls" (`manipulation-hub-safety-assessment.md:98` and `manipulation-foundation-research-dv-frameworks.md:206`). **This is incorrect.** Campbell 2003 AJPH actually used **220 femicide cases and 343 abused control women**. The 310/324 figures belong to **Campbell, Webster & Glass (2009)**, the *Journal of Interpersonal Violence* 24(4) Danger Assessment validation paper — which is also cited in the files. The DA's empirical grounding is real, the substantive claim is correct, but the citation is attached to the wrong Campbell paper. **Recommendation:** re-attribute the 310/324 figures to Campbell, Webster & Glass 2009, or revise the text to use the correct Campbell 2003 figures (220/343).

2. **"44% of femicide victims were separating" attribution to Campbell 2003 — the figure is off and the attribution may be wrong.**
   Campbell 2003 AJPH actually reports that **55.2% of intimate partner femicide victims were estranged from their partners when killed.** The "44%" number does not appear in the Campbell 2003 paper in the way the hub describes. A 43% figure exists in the UK Femicide Census (2018–2019), and "44%" may be a mis-remembered version of that or of Campbell's 55% figure. **This appears in at least 9 locations across the sub-hub.** The direction of the claim (separation period is the highest-risk window for femicide) is robustly supported — Campbell 2003 reports adjusted OR 3.64 for separation after cohabitation and adjusted OR 8.98 for high-control × separation. **Recommendation:** either revise the statistic to "approximately half (55.2%) of intimate partner femicide victims were estranged or in the process of leaving (Campbell et al., 2003)" or cite the Femicide Census 43% and attribute correctly. **The direction of the claim is intact; the specific number and citation need correction.**

3. **Lim & Magiati (2022) citation is mis-attributed.**
   The paper "Autistic Adults May Be Erroneously Perceived as Deceptive and Lacking Credibility" (PMC8813809) is actually by **Lim, Young & Brewer (2022)**, published in the *Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders*. It is NOT by Lim & Magiati. The foundation research file correctly identifies "Lim, Young & Brewer (2022)" but the sub-hub pattern pages (`manipulation-hub-pattern-performative-allyship.md`, `manipulation-hub-pattern-weaponized-accommodations.md`) cite it as "Lim & Magiati (2022)." The PMC ID is correct; the author attribution is wrong. **Recommendation:** replace "Lim & Magiati (2022)" with "Lim, Young & Brewer (2022)" wherever it appears.

**None of these errors undermine the life-safety guidance of the sub-hub.** All three are citation-accuracy problems. The substantive claims survive correction.
