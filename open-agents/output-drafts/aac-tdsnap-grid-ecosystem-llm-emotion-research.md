## TD Snap (Tobii Dynavox) Ecosystem

### 1.1 myTobiiDynavox Portal

**What it is:** A free, cloud-based platform (mytobiidynavox.com) serving as the central hub for the entire Tobii Dynavox ecosystem.

**Key Features:**
- **Backup & Sync:** Users can back up personalized vocabulary to the cloud. If a device is lost or replaced, content is preserved and can be restored.
- **Sharing:** Users can share synced page sets by entering a username or email address, enabling therapists, teachers, and family members to collaborate on vocabulary customization.
- **Team Access:** Any member of a communication team (SLPs, relatives, teachers) can be granted access to connect and share seamlessly online.
- **Community:** Provides access to a community of clinicians, technical experts, and families dedicated to communication success.
- **Software Access:** Free access to TD Snap, Communicator 5, and Gaze Viewer software for use during AAC evaluations and therapy sessions.
- **Data Visualization:** Users can log into myTobiiDynavox to access data visualizations related to usage tracking.

**Sources:**
- [myTobiiDynavox Portal](https://us.tobiidynavox.com/pages/mytobiidynavox)
- [TD Snap Saving, Sharing, and Syncing](https://us.tobiidynavox.com/blogs/product-discovery/td-snap-saving-sharing-and-syncing)

### 1.3 Boardmaker Integration / Pathways for Core First

#### Boardmaker Integration
**What it is:** Interactive Boardmaker Activities can be played within Snap Core First, enabling accessible games, books, calendars, and calculators created in Boardmaker to function inside the AAC app.

**Limitations:**
- Cannot play print activities within Snap
- Boardmaker Online (required to transfer activities to Snap Core First) is not localized
- Cannot edit Boardmaker activities within Snap -- editing must happen in Boardmaker itself
- Requires Boardmaker Online subscription for full activity transfer capability

#### Boardmaker 7 Platform
- Over 85,000 PCS (Picture Communication Symbols)
- Online management of account, activity assignment, and student progress monitoring
- Community of 800,000+ members (educators, parents, SLPs)
- Available as Boardmaker 7 Standard with various subscription tiers

#### Pathways for Core First
**What it is:** A free companion learning guide/app for TD Snap Core First.

**Key Features:**
- Intuitive video examples demonstrating implementation strategies
- Lesson plans for structured learning
- Interactive goals grid for tracking progress and addressing behavioral challenges
- Suite of supporting digital and printable materials
- Designed for caregivers, educators, therapists, and communication partners
- Separate Pathways app available for Snap Scene

## Grid (Smartbox) Ecosystem

### 2.1 Grid for iPad vs Grid 3 (Windows) -- Ecosystem Differences

**Grid 3 (Windows) -- Full Feature Set:**
- Complete AAC software for Windows
- Symbol and text communication with multiple page sets
- Environment control (IR, radio)
- Computer control (mouse/keyboard replacement)
- Remote editing capabilities (can edit Grid for iPad remotely)
- Voice activation ("Hey Grid 3")
- Full compatibility with all grid sets
- WordPower 60 + 100 page sets (Windows only)
- Fast Talker 3 (Windows only for full version)

**Grid for iPad -- Reduced Feature Set:**
- AAC app for iPad (touch, switch, mouse access; eye gaze via external tracker)
- Instant access to symbol and text communication resources
- Auto-sync with Grid 3 via Smartbox Account (cloud)
- Predictive text and sentence completion
- Variety of natural-sounding voices
- Can be remotely edited FROM Grid 3 (but cannot initiate remote editing)
- Cannot do Environment Control or Computer Control
- Cannot use voice activation ("Hey Grid 3")
- Some grid set commands do not function (compatibility percentage shown in settings)
- No iPhone or Apple Watch version (noted user complaint)

**Cross-Platform Sync:**
- Smartbox Account enables auto-sync between Grid for iPad and Grid 3
- Content stored in cloud, changes updated on both platforms
- Grid sets backed up automatically

## User Complaints & Pain Points

### TD Snap (Tobii Dynavox) -- Complaints

**CRITICAL: Subscription Model Controversy (May 2024)**
- Transition from one-time purchase ($79.99 / $310 with PODD) to $9.99/month subscription
- Users who previously purchased app report speech being disabled with updates, subscription pushed
- Parents in support groups warn against doing business with Tobii Dynavox
- AT3 (representing AT Act Programs) raised concerns: programs often cannot obtain subscriptions; subscription model doesn't work for managed iPads
- Advocacy groups requested annual subscription terms; Tobii Dynavox confirmed "no intention to provide annual subscriptions" (global policy)
- Users call it "holding children's voices hostage" for monthly fees
- Multiple user reports describe speech functionality being disabled after updates for users without active subscriptions, particularly concerning given dedicated AAC devices cost $5,000-$15,000+

**Device/Update Issues:**
- Multiple devices disabled simultaneously during forced updates
- Compatibility issues between app versions and hardware
- Parents report spending "small car equivalent" amounts on devices that stop working

**Pricing Concerns:**
- Questions about why product is offered as "free download" when speech requires paid subscription
- Users suggest at least one voice should be free
- No free tier with speech output

**What Works Well:**
- SLPs report TD Snap is "by far the best" AAC app
- Comprehensive page set ecosystem
- Strong professional/clinical tooling

## 3.6 Relevant Academic Research on LLM for AAC

**SpeakFaster (Google Research / Team Gleason, 2024):**
- Published in Nature Communications, November 2024
- LLM-powered interface for eye-gaze typing users with ALS
- Fine-tuned LLMs expand highly-abbreviated text (word initials + supplemental letters) into full phrases
- 57% fewer motor actions than traditional predictive keyboards (offline simulation)
- 29-60% faster text-entry rates than traditional baselines in user studies
- Uses conversational context to improve accuracy
- **NOT integrated into any commercial AAC product** -- research prototype only
- Collaboration between Google Research and Team Gleason

**Vocable AAC (WillowTree, 2024):**
- Open-source AAC app with ChatGPT integration
- Retains conversational context
- Enhances predictive pattern detection
- Bolsters semantic understanding of caregiver speech
- Faster, smoother, more natural conversational experience
- **Only known commercial AAC app with LLM integration** (not TD Snap or Grid)

**"The Less I Type, The Better" (CHI 2023):**
- Research paper exploring how AI language models can enhance or impede communication for AAC users
- Found both benefits and risks of LLM integration
- Concerns about clinical responsibility, data security, ethical implications

# SUMMARY: KEY GAPS AND OPPORTUNITIES

## For a $4M Government Grant AAC Project

### Gap 1: No LLM Integration in Either Platform
Neither TD Snap nor Grid uses large language models. Both rely on SwiftKey's pre-LLM neural network prediction (circa 2015-2016 technology). Google's SpeakFaster research showed LLMs can deliver 29-60% faster communication for ALS users with eye gaze. Vocable AAC has demonstrated ChatGPT integration is viable in production. This represents the single largest technology gap in both platforms.

### Gap 2: No Emotional/Affective Computing
Neither platform detects or responds to user emotional state, despite Tobii (parent company) having extensive eye tracking research on cognitive load, fatigue, and attention detection. This technology exists within Tobii's automotive and enterprise divisions but has never been applied to AAC.

### Gap 3: No Automatic Fatigue Adaptation
Alpha Core (Grid) addresses progressive conditions with manual layout switching, but neither platform automatically detects fatigue or adapts the UI. Eye tracking metrics (PERCLOS, blink rate, fixation accuracy) are already measured by both platforms' eye trackers but not used for adaptive purposes.

### Gap 4: No Health Data Integration
Neither platform integrates with Apple HealthKit, Google Health Connect, or any health monitoring system. For users with ALS, cerebral palsy, and other conditions, health data could inform UI adaptation.

### Gap 5: No Caregiver Real-Time Monitoring
Neither platform provides real-time caregiver dashboards, push notifications for milestones, or alerts for unusual usage patterns. The "communication team" concept exists in both platforms but is limited to content sharing.

### Gap 6: Limited Cross-Platform Access
TD Snap: iPad and Tobii hardware only (no iPhone, no Android, no Apple Watch)
Grid: Windows and iPad only (no iPhone, no Apple Watch, no Android)
Users report inability to communicate in emergencies when primary device is unavailable.

### Gap 7: Subscription Model Barriers
TD Snap's subscription transition has created significant controversy and barriers for school programs, AT Act programs, and families. Grid's pricing structure also draws complaints. Both create equity concerns for a government-funded project.
