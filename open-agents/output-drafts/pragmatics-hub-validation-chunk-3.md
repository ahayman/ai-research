## Systemic Issues (Applies Across Chunk)

### 1. "Rozenkrantz et al. (2021)" — NOT the false citation, but cite format is inconsistent (MEDIUM)

The brief warned about a false Rozenkrantz 2021 citation. The files in this chunk use the **real** Rozenkrantz, D'Mello & Gabrieli (2021) "Enhanced Rationality in Autism Spectrum Disorder" from *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 25(8):685-696 — verified via direct WebSearch hit to the Cell Press full-text URL and PubMed ID 34226128. This is the correct anchor paper.

However:
- **Cat 09 hub file** (`category-09-presupposition.md` line 286) says only "Rozenkrantz et al. (2021) demonstrated..." with no journal or title attribution. Reader cannot tell which paper this is.
- **Cat 09 autism file** correctly identifies it as "Trends in Cognitive Sciences" and gives the full author list.
- **No file confuses this with any paper on priors and autism perception** — so the brief's specific warning about the Pellicano & Burr substitution does not apply here.

**Recommendation**: Add explicit title/journal in the hub file when citing this paper, to prevent the ambiguity that made the brief flag it in the first place. The Pellicano & Burr (2012) "When the world becomes 'too real'" paper is **not cited in this chunk at all**, even though it would be a natural anchor for the Cat 10 predictive-processing discussion (which instead cites Van de Cruys 2014 and Vicente/Michel/Petrolini 2023 — both legitimate).

**Verdict**: CONFIRMED (citations are real) but RECOMMEND clarification for disambiguation.

### 2. Williams, Wharton & Jagoe 2021 — cited to two different journals (MEDIUM)

The Cat 10 hub file (line 348) cites the paper as *J Pragmatics*, 181, 112-127. The Cat 10 autism file (line 80) cites it correctly as *Frontiers in Psychology* 12:616664 (April 2021). The *Journal of Pragmatics* entry (vol 180, 121-130, July 2021) is actually the **companion piece** Jagoe & Wharton (2021) "Theory of autistic mind," not Williams, Wharton & Jagoe.

**The hub file has conflated two different papers into one citation.** Both papers are real; the hub just attributes the wrong one to the WWJ authorship.

**Verdict**: INVALID attribution (but both underlying papers exist). Minor but specific fix needed.

### 3. The "Breckinridge" fluidity-to-inelasticity citation is misattributed (MEDIUM)

Cat 08 autism file Section 6.2 (lines 347, 480) attributes the fluidity-to-inelasticity hypothesis to "Joshua Breckinridge" and "Breckinridge (2014)."

**Actual authorship**: The 2014 *Cognitive Semiotics* paper "Deixis and personhood: Autism, intersubjectivity, and the fluidity-to-inelasticity hypothesis" (doi:10.1515/cogsem-2014-0013) is by **Todd Oakley and Đorđe Vidanović**, volume 7(2), pages 191-225. Verified via DeGruyterBrill and ResearchGate.

"Breckinridge" does not appear to be an author on this paper. The origin of this misattribution is unclear — possibly a crossed-wire from another reference or a hallucinated name in the original research pass.

**Verdict**: INVALID author attribution. The paper and hypothesis are real; the named author is wrong. Fix: replace "Breckinridge" with "Oakley & Vidanović" throughout Cat 08 autism file (occurs at approximately lines 347, 354, 480).
