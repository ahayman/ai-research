# TouchChat HD with WordPower: The Complete AAC Deep Dive

For the estimated 2 million Americans who cannot rely on natural speech to communicate, augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) technology is not a luxury -- it is a lifeline. Among the growing ecosystem of AAC applications, TouchChat HD with WordPower has carved out a distinctive position: a clinically grounded, deeply customizable communication tool built on six decades of assistive technology expertise. Priced at $299.99, it bundles PRC-Saltillo's powerful TouchChat platform with Nancy Inman's celebrated WordPower vocabulary system -- and as of 2026, it is the first major AAC app to ship AI-powered features.

This deep dive examines everything a speech-language pathologist, parent, educator, or prospective user needs to know: the product's origins, how WordPower actually works, the complete feature set, strengths and weaknesses, real user experiences, and how it stacks up against competitors like Proloquo2Go and TD Snap.

## The Company Behind the App: PRC-Saltillo's 60-Year Legacy

The story of TouchChat HD with WordPower begins not in Silicon Valley, but in a Cleveland, Ohio hospital in 1966. Barry Romich, an engineering student at Case Western Reserve University, and Ed Prentke, an engineer at Highland View Hospital, shared a conviction that technology could give voice to those who had none. They founded the **Prentke Romich Company (PRC)** -- and in doing so, helped launch the entire field of augmentative communication technology.

Three years later, in 1969, PRC produced its first communication device: a typing system cobbled together from a discarded Teletype machine. It was crude by modern standards, but revolutionary for its time. By 1984 the company had created the **Touch Talker**, the first truly portable communication device, and in 1988 the **IntroTalker** brought digitally recorded speech into the AAC world.

In 1995 PRC launched **Unity**, the vocabulary system that would become the backbone of their product line. Meanwhile, Dave Hershberger -- then PRC's Director of Research and Development -- saw an opportunity to create a sister company focused on a different approach to AAC. Between 1996 and 1998 he founded **Saltillo**, which would develop the TouchChat line of products. In 2019, Hershberger was named CEO and oversaw the merger of both companies into **PRC-Saltillo**, uniting decades of expertise under one roof.

Today PRC-Saltillo is a worldwide developer of speech-generating devices (SGDs), market-leading iOS apps, and multiple AAC language systems. Their product line includes dedicated hardware like the Accent and NovaChat devices as well as the software-only TouchChat apps that run on standard iPads and iPhones.

## How WordPower Works: The Vocabulary System at the Heart of TouchChat

The defining feature that separates "TouchChat HD with WordPower" from the base "TouchChat HD" app is the **WordPower vocabulary system** -- and understanding how it works is essential to evaluating this product.

WordPower was created by **Nancy Inman, M.A.T., CCC-SLP**, a speech-language pathologist working at the Kennedy Krieger Institute. The origin story is both practical and moving: Inman was evaluating a young man with cerebral palsy who used a head stick to point to words arranged on a low-tech communication board. She recognized that he needed the same word-based approach on the dynamic-display AAC devices that were just emerging -- but nothing like that existed. So she built it herself.

The result was WordPower: a **word-based** vocabulary system (as opposed to symbol-based or icon-sequence systems) designed around several core principles:

### Core Design Principles

1. **High-Frequency Core Words First**: The main page of every WordPower layout features the words people use most often -- pronouns, common verbs, key adjectives. Research consistently shows that a small set of core words accounts for the vast majority of everyday communication.

2. **Color Coding by Part of Speech**: Words are organized by grammatical category using consistent colors (e.g., verbs in one color, nouns in another, pronouns in a third). This visual structure helps users internalize language patterns.

3. **Consistent Motor Planning**: Core words remain in the same physical position across different vocabulary sizes. A user who learns that "want" is in a specific location on WordPower 42 will find it in the same spot on WordPower 60. This consistency builds automaticity -- the ability to communicate without searching for words.

4. **Logical Next-Word Prediction**: After selecting a word, the system surfaces contextually likely follow-up words, reducing the number of button presses needed to build sentences.

5. **Word Completion**: Users can begin typing a word and have it completed, extending vocabulary reach far beyond the visible buttons.

6. **Core-Fringe Bridge**: Category pages and activity-specific pages embed core vocabulary alongside fringe (topic-specific) words, so users always have access to functional communication building blocks.

## The Complete Feature Set

### Communication Engine

At its core, TouchChat HD with WordPower is a **text-to-speech communication app**. Users select symbols, words, or type text, which appears in a message bar at the top of the screen and is spoken aloud through the device's speakers. The app supports three voice engines -- **Acapela**, **Almagu**, and **Apple** -- and as of version 2026.1, users can **code-switch** between voice engines within a single session.

The app includes over **45 button actions** beyond simple speech output: navigating between pages, adjusting volume, clearing the display, adding to message history, and more. With nearly **40,000 SymbolStix symbols** included at no extra cost (and PCS symbols available as a paid add-on), the visual vocabulary is substantial.

### Access Methods

TouchChat distinguishes itself with **three built-in access methods**, making it usable by individuals with a wide range of physical abilities:

- **Direct Touch**: Standard touch-based interaction with adjustable activation timing, dwell settings, and an activate-on-release option for users who need extra precision
- **Head Tracking**: Built directly into the app with no additional hardware or calibration required. Uses the TrueDepth camera on iPad Pro (2018 and later) and iPhone X and later. Trigger actions include hover, blink, mouth open, eyebrows up, tongue out, and frown.
- **Switch Scanning**: Configurable scanning with adjustable timing, scan patterns, and switch inputs for users who cannot use touch or head tracking

The built-in head tracking is a notable competitive advantage -- Proloquo2Go, for example, does not offer native head tracking support.

### Data Logging and Analytics

TouchChat integrates with **Realize Language**, PRC-Saltillo's subscription-based online analytics platform. Automated Data Logging (ADL) records every button press, timing data, and spoken output. The data flows to Realize Language, which generates visual reports including:

- Word clouds showing most-used vocabulary
- Bar charts tracking communication frequency over time
- Word lists with frequency counts
- Calendar views of daily usage patterns

As of version 2025.2, users can also manually export data logs directly from the app -- a frequently requested feature.

### AI Features (New in 2026)

The 2026.1 update made TouchChat one of the first major AAC apps to integrate artificial intelligence:

- **AI Message Edit**: Corrects grammar and spelling in composed messages, with options for additional enhancements like formality adjustment. This is particularly valuable for users who communicate through word-by-word selection and may produce grammatically incomplete sentences.
- **AI Image Generation**: Creates custom button images from text prompts. Users can describe an image and receive an AI-generated symbol, with built-in safety guardrails. This addresses a long-standing limitation of fixed symbol libraries -- when the perfect symbol for a user's needs does not exist, they can now create one.

PRC-Saltillo has stated that their AI implementation prioritizes authenticity, privacy, and user control.

## Platform and Technical Details

TouchChat HD with WordPower is an **iOS-only** application. It requires:

- **iOS 15.0** or later (iPadOS 15.0 for iPad)
- Compatible with iPhone 6 and later, iPad Air and later, iPad mini 4 and later, iPod touch 6th generation
- Newest device support: iPhone 16/16 Pro/16 Pro Max, iPad Pro M4, iPad Air M3
- **Mac**: macOS 12.5+ with Apple M1 chip or later (runs the iOS app)
- Universal app: a single purchase works on iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch

The app is available exclusively through the Apple App Store. There is no Android version, no web version, and no Windows version.

## Strengths: Where TouchChat Excels

1. **Unmatched Vocabulary Flexibility**: Eleven WordPower configurations spanning 20 to 140 cells means there is a vocabulary option for virtually every user profile, from individuals with severe motor limitations to power communicators.

2. **Clinically Grounded Design**: WordPower was designed by a practicing SLP and is built on research into core vocabulary and motor planning. It is not a tech product with clinical features bolted on -- it is a clinical tool implemented in technology.

3. **Built-in Head Tracking**: No other major AAC app offers native head tracking without additional hardware. For users with limited hand/arm mobility, this is transformative.

4. **Price Advantage**: At $299.99, it undercuts Proloquo2Go by roughly $100 while offering comparable (and in some cases superior) features.

5. **Deep Customization**: Every element is editable -- pages, grids, buttons, symbols, colors, fonts, borders. Power users and SLPs can tailor the experience to individual needs.

6. **Expanding AI Capabilities**: The 2026.1 AI features (message editing and image generation) show PRC-Saltillo investing in the future of AAC technology.

7. **Hardware Ecosystem**: Users can start with the iPad app and later transition to a dedicated PRC-Saltillo device (Accent, NovaChat) without relearning their vocabulary.

8. **Data-Driven Therapy**: Realize Language integration provides SLPs with actionable data on communication patterns, frequency, and growth over time.

## Weaknesses: Where TouchChat Falls Short

1. **iOS Lock-in**: With no Android, web, or Windows version, families are required to own Apple hardware. This excludes a significant portion of potential users and adds hardware cost.

2. **Layout Inconsistencies Across Levels**: When users progress from one WordPower size to another (e.g., WordPower 42 to WordPower 60), some elements change position -- including the home button and clear button. This undermines the promise of consistent motor planning and can be disorienting.

3. **Software Stability Issues**: Multiple users report bugs after iOS updates, including the app becoming unresponsive to touch. For a communication tool that is someone's only way to speak, crashes are not merely inconvenient -- they are isolating.

4. **Realize Language Requires Subscription**: The analytics platform that unlocks data logging insights requires an ongoing paid subscription beyond the app purchase price.

5. **Camera Roll Bug**: The app reportedly saves its logo image to the device's camera roll daily -- a minor but persistent annoyance.

6. **Complex Customization Curve**: The same depth of customization that is a strength for experienced SLPs can be overwhelming for parents and caregivers new to AAC.

## What Users Are Saying

### Speech-Language Pathologists

SLPs generally regard WordPower as one of the best word-based vocabulary systems available. The consistent core word placement, research-informed design, and Nancy Inman's ongoing involvement lend clinical credibility. The range of grid sizes is frequently cited as a standout feature that allows therapists to meet users where they are and grow with them.

### Parents and Caregivers

Parents describe the app as "extremely helpful" for nonverbal children and express relief at the lower price compared to Proloquo2Go. However, frustration with software bugs -- particularly after iOS updates -- is a recurring theme. Several parents note that when the app stops working, their child is effectively unable to communicate until the issue is resolved, sometimes requiring a full reinstallation.

### AAC Users

Users who have found the right WordPower configuration report efficient, fluid communication. The word-based approach with prediction is praised for supporting natural language generation. However, users transitioning between vocabulary sizes have noted the layout inconsistencies as a genuine barrier.

Proloquo2Go by AssistiveWare remains TouchChat's most direct competitor. Key differences:

- **Price**: TouchChat is ~$100 cheaper ($299.99 vs $399.99)
- **Vocabulary Philosophy**: WordPower is word-based with prediction; Proloquo2Go's Crescendo is a hybrid symbol-and-word system with stronger emphasis on developmental progression
- **Interface Complexity**: TouchChat is simpler and more approachable; Proloquo2Go is more feature-dense
- **Head Tracking**: TouchChat has it built in; Proloquo2Go does not
- **Symbol Libraries**: Both include SymbolStix; Proloquo2Go also bundles PCS symbols
- **Motor Planning**: Proloquo2Go places slightly stronger emphasis on consistent motor planning across levels

### Other Competitors

- **TD Snap** (Tobii Dynavox): Strongest for users who need eye tracking; integrates with Tobii hardware; available on both iOS and Windows
- **LAMP Words for Life** (PRC-Saltillo): Sister app using the LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) methodology -- a different approach to AAC within the same company
- **Proloquo** (AssistiveWare): Newer app with a more modern design and research-based vocabulary
- **GoTalk NOW** (Attainment Company): Simpler, scene-based AAC for users who benefit from photo/visual scenes

## Who Should Consider TouchChat HD with WordPower?

TouchChat HD with WordPower is best suited for:

- **Individuals with Autism** who benefit from word-based communication with symbol support
- **People with Down Syndrome** who are developing literacy alongside AAC
- **ALS and stroke patients** who need head tracking access and word prediction
- **Children with apraxia of speech** who benefit from consistent motor planning
- **Users with cerebral palsy** who need flexible access methods (touch, head tracking, scanning)
- **Families on Apple devices** who want a powerful AAC solution at a lower price than Proloquo2Go
- **Bilingual families** who need multilingual vocabulary support

It may not be the best fit for:
- Users who need Android or Windows compatibility
- Those who prefer a heavily symbol-based (rather than word-based) approach
- Users who need native eye tracking integration (TD Snap is stronger here)
- Families who are entirely new to AAC and want the simplest possible onboarding

## Key Takeaways

- TouchChat HD with WordPower is a clinically grounded, deeply customizable AAC app backed by PRC-Saltillo's six decades of assistive technology expertise
- WordPower, designed by SLP Nancy Inman, offers 11 grid configurations from 20 to 140 cells -- one of the widest ranges in the AAC market
- Built-in head tracking, 40,000 SymbolStix symbols, and 2026 AI features make it technically competitive
- At $299.99, it is roughly $100 cheaper than Proloquo2Go
- iOS-only availability and reported software stability issues are the most significant drawbacks
- Insurance and Medicaid funding options can offset the cost for qualifying individuals

## Conclusion

TouchChat HD with WordPower occupies a compelling position in the AAC landscape. It combines clinical credibility (WordPower's SLP-designed vocabulary), hardware flexibility (built-in head tracking and switch scanning), and forward-looking technology (AI message editing and image generation) at a price point that undercuts its primary competitor. The 2026.1 update signals that PRC-Saltillo is actively investing in the platform's future rather than resting on its legacy.

The app is not without rough edges. iOS-only availability, layout inconsistencies between vocabulary levels, and software stability concerns after system updates are legitimate drawbacks that users and clinicians should weigh. But for individuals and families who live within the Apple ecosystem and need a powerful, customizable, word-based AAC tool -- particularly one that supports head tracking out of the box -- TouchChat HD with WordPower remains one of the strongest options available.

For a $299.99 investment that can literally give someone their voice, that is a proposition worth serious consideration.
