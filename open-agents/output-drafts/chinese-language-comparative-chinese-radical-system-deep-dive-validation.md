**Notes**: The research should ideally cite the specific study these percentages come from. The general trend is right — phonetic components provide useful but imperfect pronunciation hints.

The research should note these are approximate and study-dependent. The general pattern (low exact match, better without tone, highest for rhyme) is correct regardless.

1. **"50 radicals cover most everyday characters"**: While this is a reasonable pedagogical claim and consistent with the Pareto distribution, a more precise statement might be "50 radicals provide meaningful decomposition cues for the majority of the ~3,000-4,000 characters needed for literacy."
