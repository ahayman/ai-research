## Honest framing check — Pennebaker mixed effects

**Required framing**: Pennebaker expressive-writing benefits in alexithymia samples are INCONSISTENT — some studies show high-alexithymia subgroups benefit MORE, others show LESS.

**Applied as**:
> "Pennebaker expressive writing in alexithymic samples shows mixed effects.
> - Some studies find that high-alexithymia subgroups benefit *more* than low-alexithymia controls...
> - Some studies find that high-alexithymia subgroups benefit *less*...
> - Some studies find no moderating effect of alexithymia at all."

This is the honest framing required. **Confirmed applied.**

## External-to-internal scaffold theme — consistency check

The unifying frame is the external-to-internal scaffold (build vocabulary first, build labels second, map to internal states third — opposite of body-focused interoceptive training which goes signal-detection-first). The frame is articulated explicitly in Section 1 and threads through:

- Section 2 (Granularity): "build conceptual vocabulary first... gradually map to internal states"
- Section 3 (Affect-Labelling): "even APPROXIMATE labels... may have some effect"
- Section 5 (Structured Journaling): "structured prompts... rather than free-form"
- Section 6 (Smartphone Apps): "alternatives to emotion-label-only inputs... avoid forcing identification when uncertainty is appropriate"
- Section 8 (Externalisation): "bypasses the unreliable internal channel by working with reliably-detectable external signals"
- Section 11 (Autistic + alexithymic specific): "build emotion-language faster through systematic categorical learning than through intuitive pickup"
- Section 12 (Trade-off framing): "build skill where it costs; accept where it serves"

**Confirmed: theme is consistently applied throughout.**

## Contested-claim flags — for the HTML build

Claims in this category that warrant honest hedging in the final HTML:

1. **Lieberman 2007 → clinical scale-up.** The Lieberman 2007 affect-labelling effect is a real, replicated lab finding. The scale-up to clinically meaningful symptom reduction at the population level is more uncertain. The HTML should reflect this honestly.

2. **MT-ALEX RCT replication status.** The MT-ALEX RCT is one trial. The "RCT-grade evidence" framing is honest only if accompanied by "with limited replication." HTML should hedge.

3. **Animi and AlexiLearn empirical evidence.** Limited published efficacy data. HTML should not over-promise; the framing "designed by people who understand the construct... limited published efficacy data" is the honest position.

4. **Pennebaker mixed-effects in alexithymia.** Already covered. HTML must keep the honest framing.

5. **Granularity ↔ alexithymia inverse correlation strength.** The conceptual relationship is robust; the empirical correlation strengths vary across samples. HTML should describe this as conceptually strong but empirically variable rather than as a tight quantitative relationship.

6. **"Build skill where it costs / accept where it serves" framing.** This is the user's preferred trade-off frame, well-supported by the supplement Topic 3 work. HTML should attribute this implicitly to the constructed-emotion / multiroute / adaptive-function literatures rather than to a single source — because this is a synthesis, not a direct citation.

7. **Special-interest as scaffolding for autistic + alexithymic emotion-language work.** Practitioner-level claim from autistic-affirming clinical writing (Neff; Trauma Geek lineage); not a peer-reviewed RCT-grade finding. HTML should frame as practitioner-level synthesis rather than empirical fact.

8. **Pattern-recognition-as-strength for systematic emotion-vocabulary acquisition.** Same — practitioner-level / autistic-affirming-clinical claim with theoretical resonance to the Mottron strength-based tradition (Mottron 2011 Nature) but not a directly tested mechanism in alexithymia. HTML should frame accordingly.

## What this category does NOT claim

To be clear about scope:

- This category does NOT claim self-directed approaches will fix alexithymia for everyone.
- This category does NOT claim self-directed approaches are a substitute for therapy in clinical-severity cases.
- This category does NOT claim "alexithymia is good actually."
- This category does NOT claim a unified mechanism for what is actually a heterogeneous set of techniques.
- This category does NOT claim Animi/AlexiLearn have RCT-grade evidence.
- This category does NOT claim affect-labelling alone is sufficient to remediate severe alexithymia.

## What it does claim, with appropriate weights

- Granularity-building has theoretical and moderate empirical support as a skill that can be developed.
- Affect-labelling has a real underlying mechanism (Lieberman 2007) and is a useful regulatory technique.
- Pennebaker expressive writing has mixed effects in alexithymia samples; modifications appear to help.
- Structured journaling templates appear to help alexithymic users more than free-form journaling.
- Smartphone apps designed for alexithymia have one RCT-grade trial (MT-ALEX); other apps are early-deployment.
- Psychoeducation has documented value as a stand-alone tool for many users.
- Externalisation strategies provide a structurally-correct workaround for parsing-deficit profiles.
- The autistic + alexithymic profile has specific adaptation needs beyond the general alexithymia population.
- The trade-off framing — build skill where it costs, accept where it serves — is well-supported by the adaptive-function literature reviewed in supplement Topic 3.
