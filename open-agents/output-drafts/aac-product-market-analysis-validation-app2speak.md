## 1. APP2Speak costs $199.99 one-time purchase

**UNCERTAIN (7/10)**

Evidence supports $199.99 for the iOS version. The APP2Speak FAQ and App Store listing both reference a "one-time charge of $199.99." ## 9. JMIR Aging (PMC7177427): One of only 7 AAC apps rated "highly" in systematic evaluation of 600+ apps

**UNCERTAIN (6/10)**

The PMC7177427 article is confirmed as real: "Mobile Apps to Support Caregiver-Resident Communication in Long-Term Care: Systematic Search and Content Analysis" (JMIR Aging, 2020). APP2Speak's own blog post confirms it was "one of only seven AAC apps to be 'highly rated'" in this study. However, the "600+ apps" figure is problematic. The study's 2017 review identified 607 apps for initial eligibility screening (a 205% increase from 199), but only 23 AAC apps were actually evaluated in detail. The "600+" figure refers to the initial search pool, not the number of apps that were systematically evaluated. APP2Speak also did NOT end up as one of the top-recommended apps -- the final recommendations were CommunicoTool Adult, GoTalk NOW, Google Translate, and TableTop Translator. Calling it "highly rated" is supported by the initial quality assessment, but claiming evaluation of "600+ apps" is misleading -- that was the screening funnel, not the evaluation set.

- Sources: [PMC7177427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7177427/), [JMIR Aging](https://aging.jmir.org/2020/1/e17136/), [APP2Speak blog](https://app2speak.com/app2speak-scores-highly-in-recent-ranking-of-speech-assistance-apps-for-patients-in-long-term-residential-care/)

## 10. One of only 2 apps whose design was informed by an SLP (from the JMIR study)

**INVALID**

The JMIR study (PMC7177427) states that **4 out of 23** AAC apps indicated inclusion of an SLP in development: App2Speak, Chatable, CommunicAide Free, and CommunicoTool Adult. The claim of "only 2" is incorrect -- it was 4. APP2Speak was indeed one of them, but the denominator is wrong. Additionally, 2 apps indicated research-informed content (Compass and Proloquo2Go) and 3 included end users.

- Sources: [PMC7177427](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7177427/), [JMIR Aging](https://aging.jmir.org/2020/1/e17136/)

## 13. Supports touch, Bluetooth switches (via iOS accessibility), Apple eye tracking (iOS 17+), and head tracking

**UNCERTAIN (7/10)**

Touch, Bluetooth switches, and eye tracking are well-documented. The app is described as "compatible with most iOS accessibility features such as low vision, touch accommodations, Bluetooth switches." Apple eye tracking (introduced in iOS 18, not iOS 17 as sometimes claimed) is confirmed to work with APP2Speak. However, **head tracking is not explicitly mentioned** in APP2Speak's own documentation or feature lists. Head tracking is an iOS accessibility feature (available since iOS 13) that theoretically works with any app, but APP2Speak does not specifically claim head tracking compatibility. The claim about "iOS 17+" for eye tracking may also be slightly off -- Apple announced eye tracking as part of accessibility features that shipped with iOS 18 (2024), not iOS 17. Minor factual error on the iOS version.

- Sources: [APP2Speak eye tracking blog](https://app2speak.com/breaking-barriers-how-app2speak-and-apples-eye-tracking-technology-empower-nonverbal-adults-with-degenerative-diseases/), [app2speak.com/faq](https://app2speak.com/faq/), [Apple Newsroom](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2024/05/apple-announces-new-accessibility-features-including-eye-tracking/)
