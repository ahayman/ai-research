Overall assessment: **HIGH CONFIDENCE**. The core claims are well-supported by multiple independent sources including academic publications, government statistics, and empirical studies. A few numerical claims require qualification.

## Potential Issues Identified

1. **"80% comprehension" ambiguity**: The PRC government's 80.72% figure measures people who "can use" (能用) Putonghua. This likely includes people with accented Putonghua and partial competence. It does not mean 80% speak "standard" Putonghua fluently.

2. **Speaker number precision**: All speaker numbers for Chinese varieties are estimates. China's census does not directly ask about language variety spoken, making precise counts impossible.

3. **"Dialect" terminology**: The research correctly flags the problematic nature of calling Chinese varieties "dialects," but should emphasize that this is a politically charged choice, not merely a terminological one.

4. **Endangerment vs. shift**: Some linguists distinguish between "language endangerment" (risk of complete extinction) and "language shift" (declining use without extinction risk). Most Chinese regional languages face shift rather than true endangerment — they still have tens of millions of speakers but are losing intergenerational transmission.

## Research Gaps

1. **Quantitative code-switching data**: While qualitative descriptions are rich, large-scale quantitative studies of code-switching frequencies across domains are still limited.
2. **Regional Putonghua variation**: The "middle tier" of regional Putonghua is under-studied relative to its importance as the actual daily language of most Chinese speakers.
3. **Digital-age dialect revival**: The extent to which internet dialect culture is slowing or reversing language shift is not yet quantified.
