## Potential weaknesses I have identified in the concept research

1. **The Lumley severity-moderation paradox is presented as a "real complication" for the parsing-deficit caution, but I haven't gone deep enough on the specific Yarns 2024 findings.** The 2024 RCT showed EAET produced larger pain reductions than CBT in older veterans; whether the severity-moderation finding holds in that specific RCT or is from earlier Lumley work needs to be verified before publication. *Mitigation*: attributed to Lumley group generally with appropriate hedging; not over-specifying the trial in which the moderation appeared.

2. **The "third-wave CBT including DBT" framing** could collapse the distinction between DBT and ACT. *Mitigation*: separate sections for DBT (Section 2) and ACT (Section 3) with distinct mechanism claims.

3. **The treatment-matching matrix risks being read as a clinical recommendation tool.** *Mitigation*: explicit framing that "any reader who uses this matrix to make a decision is making a theoretically-informed choice, not a choice grounded in subtype-stratified RCT evidence."

4. **The IFS section is brief.** This is intentional — the user has a separate companion deep dive on IFS. *Mitigation*: prominent cross-link to `../../ifs-autism-alexithymia-trauma-deep-dive/index.html`.

5. **Pharmacological adjuncts coverage is light.** Oxytocin is the only agent with an alexithymia-specific signal. *Mitigation*: explicitly noted that nothing else is established.
