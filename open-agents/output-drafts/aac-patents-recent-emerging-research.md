## Executive Summary

The AAC patent landscape is complex and multi-layered, spanning traditional speech-generating device (SGD) patents, eye-tracking technology, brain-computer interfaces (BCI), AI-powered speech recognition, and emerging neural interface technologies. Key findings for a new market entrant:

### Critical Takeaways

1. **Tobii AB dominates eye-tracking IP** with over 1,000 patents/applications worldwide and 200+ granted US patents, creating significant barriers for eye-gaze-based AAC products.

2. **Microsoft holds strategic AAC-specific patents** covering SGD proxies, conversation throughput, and social cue systems -- directly targeting AAC communication enhancement.

3. **PRC-Saltillo (Prentke Romich)** holds numerous device patents and the foundational Minspeak/semantic compaction patents that underpin their LAMP language system.

4. **Apple's Personal Voice and Live Speech** technologies are backed by patent filings covering personalized voice synthesis, potentially constraining voice-banking AAC approaches on iOS.

5. **Brain-computer interface patents are rapidly expanding**, with Neuralink (87 documents, 31 families), Neurable, and university research institutions filing aggressively.

6. **Silent speech / EMG technology** is an emerging patent hotspot, with Meta (via CTRL-Labs acquisition), Cornell University, and others filing patents on wearable muscle-signal-based communication.

7. **Google's Project Euphonia** has patent protection for atypical speech recognition technology using multiple speech recognizers, directly relevant to AAC users.

8. **Open-source AAC projects** (Cboard, formerly CoughDrop) generally lack patent protections but may have freedom-to-operate implications.

9. **The WIPO Technology Trends report** identifies assistive communication technology as a growing patent area, with significant IP activity in brain-machine interfaces.

10. **Generative AI and LLMs** applied to AAC represent the newest frontier, with limited direct patent coverage currently but expected rapid growth.

## Major Patent Holders and Portfolios

### 1. Tobii AB / Tobii Dynavox

**Portfolio Size:** 1,000+ patents and applications worldwide; 200+ granted US patents
**Headquarters:** Danderyd, Sweden
**AAC Relevance:** HIGH -- Direct AAC manufacturer and eye-tracking technology leader

Tobii holds the world's largest eye-tracking patent portfolio, covering:
- Sensor technology and illumination methods
- Eye-tracking algorithms
- Data transfer mechanisms
- Eye-control interaction techniques
- Gaze-controlled user interfaces
- Calibration systems for eye-tracking devices

**Key Recent Patent:**
- **US 12,293,017** -- "Method and system for guiding a user in calibrating an eye tracking device" (Granted May 6, 2025, Assignee: Tobii AB)

**IP Licensing:** Tobii actively licenses its eye-tracking IP to third parties and has a dedicated IP licensing business unit. Any company building eye-gaze-controlled AAC devices must evaluate Tobii's patent landscape carefully.

**Historical Context:** Tobii acquired DynaVox in May 2014, combining two leading assistive technology companies. DynaVox's earlier eye-gaze technology patents were developed in partnership with the US Navy.

## Emerging Technology Patent Areas

### 1. AI/LLM-Powered AAC Communication

**Status:** Rapidly growing but relatively underpopulated with AAC-specific patents
**Key Developments:**
- PrAACT (Predictive AAC with Transformers) uses BERT-based models for communication card prediction
- LLM-powered interfaces demonstrate 57% more motor action savings than traditional predictive keyboards
- Eye-gaze AAC users with ALS show text-entry rates 29-60% above baselines with LLM assistance
- GenAI patent families grew from 733 in 2014 to 14,000+ in 2023

**Opportunity Assessment:** This is the most significant white-space opportunity for a new entrant. While general LLM/GenAI patents exist, AAC-specific applications of generative AI are largely unpatented territory.

### 2. Brain-Computer Interfaces for AAC

**Status:** Active patent filing, early commercial deployment
**Key Players:**
- **Neuralink** (invasive implant): 87 documents, 49 active patents
- **Neurable** (non-invasive EEG/eye-tracking hybrid): 2+ granted US patents
- **Stanford University:** Intellectual property related to speech decoding from neural signals
- **UC Davis:** Neuroprosthetics lab achieving 97% accuracy in brain-to-speech translation
- **BrainGate consortium:** Ongoing research with patent filings

**Key Milestone:** Stanford researchers demonstrated inner-speech BCI decoding from a 125,000-word vocabulary in 2025.

### 3. Silent Speech / Surface EMG

**Status:** Emerging patent area with significant commercial interest
**Key Players:**
- **Meta (CTRL-Labs):** Acquired for ~$500M-$1B in 2019; neural wristband shipped September 2025
- **Cornell University:** Patent on wearable sEMG for silent speech (laryngectomy patients)
- **Control Bionics:** NeuroNode EMG device with patent-pending NeuroStrip
- **AlterEgo (MIT spin-off):** Silent speech wearable with patent portfolio

**Patent US 12,482,449** (granted November 2025) covers wearable EMG sensor for silent speech with AI speech model -- directly relevant to AAC.

### 4. Personalized Voice Synthesis / Voice Banking

**Status:** Active patent area dominated by big tech
**Key Players:**
- **Apple:** Personal Voice (iOS 17+) -- patent-protected
- **Google:** On-device speech synthesis personalization
- **Respeecher:** Voice cloning for laryngectomy and ALS patients
- **Acapela Group / ModelTalker:** Traditional voice banking services

### 5. Multimodal AAC Systems

**Status:** Growing research area, limited patent protection
**Key Concept:** Combining eye-gaze, BCI, EMG, gesture, and speech input into unified AAC platforms
**Notable Work:** IRISBOND is building the first AAC platform converging multiple assistive technologies -- eye tracking, tongue, ear, wheelchair, and avatar controls.

### 6. Wearable AAC Devices (AR/VR/MR)

**Status:** Early patent filings, mostly from big tech
**Key Research:** MR-based wearable AAC device achieving 93.30% accuracy with eye-gaze and 8.55 selections/minute information transfer rate.

## Patent Risk Assessment for New Entrants

### HIGH RISK Areas (Existing strong patent protection)

1. **Eye-gaze controlled AAC devices:** Tobii holds 1,000+ patents. Any eye-tracking AAC product must navigate Tobii's portfolio. Licensing may be required.

2. **Minspeak/Semantic Compaction language systems:** Exclusively patented. Cannot replicate this specific multi-meaning icon encoding approach.

3. **SGD proxy and conversation awareness systems:** Microsoft's US 9,679,497 and US 10,262,555 cover core AAC interaction patterns including emotional proxies, conversation throughput enhancement, and real-time suggestions.

4. **Personal Voice synthesis on iOS:** Apple's patent protection around Personal Voice could limit competing voice-banking solutions on Apple platforms.

5. **PRC-Saltillo device-specific technology:** Seven US patents covering Accent and PRiO device lines.

### MEDIUM RISK Areas (Growing patent activity, some coverage gaps)

6. **Atypical speech recognition:** Google's 2023 patent covers multi-recognizer approach for atypical speech. Voiceitt has proprietary (possibly patented) ASR technology.

7. **EMG/silent speech wearables:** Growing patent filings from Meta (CTRL-Labs), Cornell, and others. Control Bionics has specific AAC-focused EMG patent.

8. **Brain-computer interfaces for communication:** Active filing by Neuralink, Neurable, and universities. Early-stage but will create future constraints.

9. **Cloud-based speech generation:** Multiple patents from Google, Amazon, and others on distributed speech synthesis.

10. **Gesture recognition for communication:** Wearable Devices Ltd. and others have filed, but AAC-specific gesture patents are limited.

### LOW RISK Areas (Opportunities for innovation without major patent conflicts)

11. **LLM/GenAI-enhanced AAC communication:** Minimal direct patent coverage for AAC-specific generative AI applications.

12. **Symbol-based communication boards (using open symbols):** Mulberry and other open-licensed symbol sets available. Custom symbol development has no patent barriers.

13. **Cross-platform AAC web applications:** Open Board Format (.obf) is freely available. Web-based approaches avoid device-specific patent issues.

14. **Multilingual/multicultural AAC systems:** Limited patent activity for non-English AAC systems.

15. **Teletherapy-integrated AAC:** Relatively unpatented intersection of AAC and remote therapy delivery.

## White Space Opportunities

Based on the patent landscape analysis, these areas represent the strongest opportunities for a new entrant:

### 1. Generative AI / LLM-Powered AAC (TOP PRIORITY)
- Context-aware message generation using LLMs
- Personalized vocabulary prediction using transformer models
- Automated conversation scaffolding for AAC users
- LLM-driven smart phrase generation from minimal input
- **Patent landscape:** Nearly wide open for AAC-specific applications

### 2. Multimodal Unified AAC Platform
- Combining multiple input methods (touch, eye-gaze, voice, gesture, BCI) in a single adaptive system
- Real-time switching between modalities based on user state
- **IRISBOND is pursuing this but patent coverage appears limited**

### 3. AAC for Underserved Languages and Cultures
- Multilingual AAC with culturally appropriate symbols and vocabulary
- AAC systems for tonal languages, sign languages, and non-Latin scripts
- **Virtually no patent protection in this space**

### 4. Teletherapy-Integrated AAC
- AAC platforms with built-in SLP consultation and monitoring
- Remote assessment and device configuration tools
- Data analytics for therapy outcome tracking
- **Patent white space exists at the AAC-telehealth intersection**

### 5. Edge AI / On-Device AAC Intelligence
- Running personalized language models entirely on-device for privacy and speed
- Federated learning across AAC users for improved predictions
- **Limited patent coverage for AAC-specific edge AI**

### 6. Social Media and Modern Communication Integration
- AAC interfaces designed for social media platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Discord)
- Emoji and meme generation for AAC users
- Real-time group chat participation tools
- **Essentially no patent coverage**

### 7. Wearable Discreet AAC Devices
- Smartwatch-based or earpiece-based AAC systems
- Discreet communication aids that don't draw attention
- **Limited patent coverage beyond general wearable patents**

## Recommendations for New Entrant

### Immediate Actions
1. **Conduct a formal Freedom-to-Operate (FTO) analysis** with a patent attorney focusing on Tobii eye-tracking patents and Microsoft AAC patents before developing any product involving eye-gaze control or SGD communication proxies.

2. **Engage patent counsel to review PRC-Saltillo's Minspeak patents** to understand expiration dates and scope -- some early Baker patents from the 1980s may have expired but continuations could still be active.

3. **File provisional patent applications** in identified white-space areas (LLM-powered AAC, multimodal adaptive systems, AAC for underserved languages) to establish priority dates.

4. **Monitor Neuralink, Meta, and university patent filings** quarterly for BCI and EMG developments that could affect long-term AAC strategy.

5. **Leverage open-source resources** (Cboard, Open Board Format, Mulberry symbols) where possible to reduce IP exposure while building proprietary value on top.

### Strategic IP Positioning
- Build patent portfolio around AI-enhanced AAC communication (predictive text with context awareness, generative message suggestions, adaptive vocabulary learning)
- Consider design patents for novel AAC interface designs
- Explore licensing arrangements with Tobii for eye-tracking integration if pursuing gaze-based products
- Monitor Apple's accessibility patent filings to avoid conflicts with Personal Voice/Live Speech technology
