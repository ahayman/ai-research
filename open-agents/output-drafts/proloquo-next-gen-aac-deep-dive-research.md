# Research Notes: Proloquo - Next-Generation AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

Proloquo is AssistiveWare's next-generation Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) app, released in 2022 as a from-scratch reimagining of AAC technology built on over a decade of research insights and anonymous usage data from more than 10,000 AAC users. Unlike its predecessor Proloquo2Go (which remains available and actively maintained), Proloquo takes a fundamentally different design approach -- emphasizing consistency, motor planning, pre-planned vocabulary, and a companion coaching app to support the entire AAC team.

The app earned the prestigious 2023 Apple App Store Award for Cultural Impact, making it the first AAC app ever to receive this recognition. Proloquo ships with over 16,000 words via its Crescendo Evolution vocabulary -- more than four times what any competing AAC system offers -- while maintaining a compact, efficient grid layout. Combined with Proloquo Coach (a free companion app for parents and educators), unlimited cloud-synced team sharing, Acapela Neural Voices, and innovative features like Thinking Space and Focus Mode, Proloquo represents a significant evolution in AAC technology.

However, the app is not without limitations: it is English-only, iPad-only for the main app (Coach works on iPhone too), uses a subscription pricing model ($99.99/year after a 6-month trial), offers limited customization compared to Proloquo2Go, and has a steeper learning curve for users transitioning from other AAC systems. Its 4.1/5 App Store rating reflects both strong appreciation and some user frustrations.

## Historical Context

### AssistiveWare Company History
- **2000**: AssistiveWare founded by David Niemeijer in Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **Early 2000s**: Developed Mac OS X accessibility software (KeyStrokes, SwitchXS, VisioVoice, GhostReader, and the original desktop Proloquo)
- **2009**: Released Proloquo2Go -- the first full-featured AAC app for iOS
- **2013**: Released Proloquo4Text for text-based AAC users
- **2017-2022**: Over 100 person-years of development work on next-generation Proloquo
- **2022**: Officially launched Proloquo and Proloquo Coach
- **November 2023**: Won Apple App Store Award for Cultural Impact
- **2024**: Proloquo 4.0 released with major vocabulary and feature enhancements
- **March 2025**: Latest update fixing bugs and improving Focus Mode

### Why a New App?
AssistiveWare created Proloquo as a separate app (rather than updating Proloquo2Go) because the design philosophy was fundamentally different. Proloquo2Go was built around maximum customization and flexibility, while Proloquo was designed around consistency, motor planning, and an integrated ecosystem with Coach. The two apps serve different user needs and coexist.

## Current State

### Proloquo (Main AAC App)

**Crescendo Evolution Vocabulary**
- 16,000+ pre-planned words organized across 9 tabs
- Built from anonymous data from 10,000+ AAC users
- Over 90% of curriculum words come preprogrammed
- Words never move or get removed from their positions
- One-tap access to the most frequently used words via two static top rows
- No back button -- words always reached through the same motor plan path

**Thinking Space**
- A flexible whiteboard area where users can drag, cluster, and freely rearrange symbols
- Supports complex thought expression beyond linear sentence building
- Contents can be saved as photos, printed, or shared

**Focus Mode**
- Uses light and motion to gently guide attention to key words
- Does NOT restrict or hide vocabulary
- Reduces prompt dependency and encourages independent communication
- Visual cues invite the user back to relevant vocabulary

**Voice Technology**
- First AAC app to use Acapela Neural Voices (DNN/ML-based)
- Also supports Apple system voices
- Compatible with Apple Personal Voice (iOS 17+)
- Multiple regional English accents available

**Team Sharing & Cloud Sync**
- Unlimited team sharing at no additional cost
- Cloud-based data storage with instant sync
- Role-based permissions (use, edit, or manage)

### Proloquo Coach (Companion App)
- Free companion app included with Proloquo subscription
- Designed for parents, educators, and SLPs
- Available on both iPhone and iPad
- Guides families through the AAC learning journey
- Shows weekly usage graphs and tracks progress
- Direct AssistiveWare support messaging

## User Reviews & Feedback

### Positive
- Easy to use and less overwhelming than other apps
- Motor planning consistency praised by SLPs and families
- Personal Voice compatibility praised
- History and typing functions appreciated
- Proloquo Coach helps parents feel supported

### Negative
- Big learning curve in navigation for users switching from other systems
- Customization is very difficult and frustrating for some
- No way to change to larger font or make the icons smaller
- Voice quality issues in some recent updates
- Subscription model frustrates users who preferred one-time purchase
- English-only limitation excludes multilingual users

## Strengths

1. Research-based design from 10,000+ AAC users
2. 16,000+ words - 4x more than competitors
3. Strong motor planning consistency
4. Thinking Space for non-linear communication
5. Focus Mode guides without restricting
6. Free Proloquo Coach companion app
7. Unlimited team sharing with cloud sync
8. Acapela Neural Voices
9. Personal Voice support (iOS 17+)
10. Grows with the user (age 2 to adult)
11. 5,000+ curriculum words preprogrammed
12. 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award

## Weaknesses

1. English only
2. iPad only (main app)
3. Limited customization
4. Subscription pricing model ($99.99/year)
5. Single grid size (48+12)
6. Steeper learning curve for switchers
7. Limited accessibility options vs. Proloquo2Go
8. Voice quality issues in some updates
9. No macOS/Android support
10. Font/contrast limitations
