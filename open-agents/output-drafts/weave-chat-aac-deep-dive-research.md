# Research Notes: Weave Chat AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

Weave Chat AAC is a free, robust, category-based Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) application developed by Weave Chat, LLC. Created and maintained by an SLP (speech-language pathologist) and an engineer, the app is built on the core belief that everyone should be able to communicate, which is why it has been and always will be completely free with no ads, in-app purchases, or subscriptions.

Available on iOS, Android, Amazon Kindle, Chromebooks, and Apple computers with M1 chips or newer, Weave Chat AAC is one of the most broadly available free AAC apps on the market. The app uses a category-based approach with dynamic display, synthetic voices, and custom boards. Its color-coding system follows the Fitzgerald Key, a well-established clinical framework for organizing vocabulary by grammatical function.

As of early 2026, the app has been downloaded approximately 1,300 times per month on Android alone, carries a 4.37/5 rating on Google Play from 110 ratings, and continues to receive regular updates. Its most recent version (001.015.0010) was released in September 2025, with ongoing development including multilingual support (Castilian Spanish, Macedonian, and Vietnamese translations completed). The app has gained recognition for its unique in-app picture messaging feature, comprehensive customization options, and the fact that it is completely free while competing with apps costing $10-$300+.

## Developer & Company Background

Weave Chat AAC was created and continues to be maintained by Weave Chat, LLC. The founding team consists of an SLP (speech-language pathologist) and an engineer. This combination of clinical AAC expertise and software engineering has shaped the app's design philosophy, which prioritizes both communicative function and technical stability.

The company actively engages with the AAC community through social media (@weavechataac on Instagram) and accepts feedback from AAC users and professionals. Volunteer translators have contributed to making the app available in multiple languages. The company also provides institutional support, helping school districts, hospitals/clinics, and private practices implement the app, including privacy agreements (typically returned within 48 hours) and server whitelisting assistance.

New vocabulary is added weekly, demonstrating an ongoing commitment to expanding the app's communicative capabilities. The company also provides free materials and tutorials on Teachers Pay Teachers and their website.

[Source: https://www.weavechat.com, https://nwacs.info/company-page-weave-chat-aac]

## How Weave Chat Works

### Core Architecture
Weave Chat AAC is a **category-based AAC system** with dynamic display. Users communicate by selecting picture-symbol buttons organized into vocabulary categories. When a button is pressed, the app speaks the word or phrase aloud using a synthetic voice. Users can also construct sentences by selecting multiple buttons in sequence.

### Vocabulary Organization (Fitzgerald Key)
The app uses the Fitzgerald Key color-coding system to organize vocabulary:
- **Green**: Verbs (action words)
- **Yellow**: Pronouns (I, you, he, she, etc.)
- **Purple**: Questions (who, what, where, when, why, how)
- **Tan/Orange**: Nouns (people, places, things)
- **Blue**: Adjectives (describing words)
- **Pink**: Conversational words (greetings, social phrases)

### Board Options
- **Default Scrolling Board**: Buttons scroll within categories
- **12 Grid Board Layouts**: Fixed grid positions ranging from 1x2 to 7x15
- **Board Builder**: Create boards from scratch
- Grid board buttons can remain square with scrollable overflow, configurable per board

### Voice Output
- Synthetic voice selection dependent on device settings
- Adjustable vocal pitch and rate of speech
- Record up to 30 seconds of custom audio per button
- Category text can optionally be read aloud

### Unique Features
- **In-app Picture Messaging**: Users can text friends using picture symbols -- a feature not found in other AAC apps
- **Whiteboard**: Built-in drawing tool that persists drawings until cleared
- **Next Word Recommendations**: Improved prediction handling prefixes and suffixes
- **Custom Phrases**: Quick-access custom phrases from the board
- **Multi-device Sync**: Same login keeps profiles synced across devices

## Strengths

1. **Completely Free**: No cost, no ads, no in-app purchases, no subscriptions -- ever.
2. **Cross-Platform**: Available on iOS, Android, Kindle, Chromebook, and Mac.
3. **SLP-Designed**: Created by a speech-language pathologist, ensuring clinical validity.
4. **Fitzgerald Key Color Coding**: Follows established clinical color-coding framework.
5. **Extensive Customization**: 12 grid layouts, custom images, custom words/categories, voice settings, visual settings.
6. **In-App Messaging**: Unique picture-messaging feature not found in other AAC apps.
7. **Regular Updates**: Weekly vocabulary additions and frequent app updates.
8. **Multi-Device Sync**: Same login syncs across all devices.
9. **Active Community**: Developer responds to feedback and feature requests.
10. **Institutional Support**: Designed for school district deployment with privacy agreement support.
11. **No Data Sharing**: Does not share personal data with third parties.
12. **Whiteboard Feature**: Built-in drawing tool for additional communication.

## Weaknesses

1. **Vocabulary Gaps**: Missing common pre-made vocabulary (e.g., "cat" or "dog" under Pets, no abuse/trauma vocabulary).
2. **Custom Button Limit**: 2,000 button cap means users filling vocabulary gaps have less room for personalized content.
3. **Cannot Delete Buttons**: Users can only hide buttons, not delete them.
4. **Folder Image Cropping**: Folder images are cropped awkwardly.
5. **Android Reliability Issues**: Some users report sync failures.
6. **Navigation Learning Curve**: Some users find the initial navigation and custom board creation difficult.
7. **English Only (Currently)**: Only English vocabulary and images available as of early 2025.
8. **VoiceOver Accessibility**: Screen reader reads settings as if they were one sentence.
9. **No Switch Access**: No apparent native switch access/scanning support.
10. **No Windows/Web Support**: Not available on Windows computers or in web browsers.
