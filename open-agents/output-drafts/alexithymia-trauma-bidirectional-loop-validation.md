## Contested claims audit

### Claim 1: Direction of causation in first-responder alexithymia

**Status**: contested. Three published framings (protective adaptation, trauma sequela, pre-existing risk factor). All three appear in published literature; none has decisively won.

**How this category handles it**: Section 13 honestly presents all three framings, names the contested status, and doesn't push the user toward one reading. The "adaptive trade-off" framing is held alongside the "maintenance factor" framing without contradiction.

### Claim 2: Whether alexithymia is core feature or comorbidity in CPTSD

**Status**: ICD-11 says comorbidity (not a formal symptom). Clinical literature (Cloitre, Frewen, Schimmenti) says operationally near-core. Diagnostic vs. operational status diverges.

**How this category handles it**: Section 9 explicitly distinguishes diagnostic (formal, comorbidity) from operational (clinical, near-core) status. Honest about the divergence.

### Claim 3: "Desensitization without integration" as mechanism for treatment moderation

**Status**: theoretical model with face validity, partial empirical scaffolding (the physiological-subjective response disconcordance findings), strong clinical-pragmatic implications. Not a tested causal mechanism in alexithymic PTSD specifically.

**How this category handles it**: Section 6 labels this as a hypothesis/mechanism extrapolation, not as established fact. Cites the disconcordance literature as scaffolding.

### Claim 4: Body-focused trauma therapy as iatrogenic risk in alexithymia

**Status**: theoretically robust (parsing-deficit subgroup is real; Britton adverse-effects literature is real); alexithymia-specific empirical test is thin. Treleaven's framework is trauma-sensitive, not alexithymia-sensitive — extrapolation.

**How this category handles it**: Section 11 explicitly flags this as theoretical extrapolation rather than established. Forward-points to Cat 10 for full coverage.

### Claim 5: HPA-axis findings

**Status**: mixed direction in published literature. Lower CAR vs. higher tonic baseline sympathetic — both reported. Häusser 2013 cited as one cortisol-awakening-response source.

**How this category handles it**: Section 8 acknowledges mixed findings on direction. Doesn't claim one HPA story.

## Open questions / honest gaps to flag in the explanation

1. **No large-N prospective with pre-trauma TAS-20 + post-trauma PTSD outcome**. The McCaslin design exists at small scale; the field needs replication.
2. **No peritraumatic-dissociation prospective with pre-trauma alexithymia measurement**. Cross-sectional + SEM is what's available.
3. **No alexithymia-specific phase-based RCT**. STAIR-NT is operationalised consensus but specific evidence on whether phase-based protocols outperform standard for high-alexithymia subgroup is sparse.
4. **No formally tested mechanism for "desensitization without integration"** in alexithymic PTSD specifically. Plausible model, scaffolded by disconcordance findings, not directly tested.
5. **Treleaven's framework** is trauma-sensitive, not alexithymia-sensitive. Theoretical extrapolation.
6. **First-responder direction of causation** remains contested.
