### Issue 2: De Martino year confusion (single file)

`category-fallacies.md` line 244 lists:
> De Martino, B. et al. (2008). "Frames, Biases, and Rational Decision-Making in the Human Brain." *Science*.

This title belongs to De Martino, Kumaran, Seymour & Dolan **2006** in *Science* 313(5787):684-687, which is a general (not autism-specific) study of framing effects in neurotypical decision-making. The autism-specific framing-effect finding (7.66% vs. 14.24% susceptibility) is in:

> De Martino, B., Harrison, N. A., Knafo, S., Bird, G., & Dolan, R. J. (2008). Explaining Enhanced Logical Consistency During Decision Making in Autism. *Journal of Neuroscience*, 28(42), 10746-10750. PMC2682781.

The research file `category-fallacies-research-autism.md` has this correct (lines 19, 576). Only the summary hub file has the conflation. Correction action: Either split into two citations or replace the *Science* entry with the *J Neuroscience* 2008 paper.

### Issue 3: None other — framework accuracy is high

The pragma-dialectical ten-rule framework described in `category-fallacies-research-theory.md` Part 2 is accurate to Van Eemeren & Grootendorst 1992/2004. The rule names, formulations, and example-fallacy mappings all match published summaries of the pragma-dialectical approach. Walton's six dialogue types are accurately named and described.

### Issue 4: No deficit-model slippage detected

Across all twelve files, the neurodiversity-affirming framing holds consistently. Over-specification is framed as a rational strategy. Enhanced rationality is foregrounded as a strength in fallacies. Double empathy is cited in every `*-research-autism.md` file. "Deficit" appears only in quoted/critical contexts.

### Issue 5: No Section-12 forbidden claims detected

Extensive grep of all three manipulation files and all three fallacies files for: triune brain, polyvagal, body keeps the score, van der Kolk, recovered memory, narcissistic abuse, time heals, forgiveness requirement, post-traumatic growth, trauma stored in body.
