## TOPIC 1: ECOSYSTEM & INFRASTRUCTURE

### 1.1 Product Overview

AssistiveWare (headquartered in Amsterdam, Netherlands) offers two primary AAC apps:

**Proloquo2Go** (Legacy App)
- One-time purchase: **$249.99** (iOS), $124.99 (Mac)
- Available on iPad, iPhone, Apple Watch, and Mac (as separate app)
- 27,000+ symbols, Crescendo vocabulary, 100+ voices
- No active subscription required; however, no longer the recommended product for new users
- Schools receive 50% discount when purchasing 20+ copies through Apple School Manager

**Proloquo** (Next-Generation App)
- Subscription-based model with free 1-month trial
- Monthly and annual subscription plans via App Store
- Longer-term licenses (1-5 years) available directly from AssistiveWare for insurance claims and school districts
- Crescendo Evolution vocabulary with 16,000+ words (4x more than original Crescendo)
- Fixed 60-button grid layout (research shows comparable outcomes to Proloquo2Go's recommended 77-button grid)
- Won 2023 Apple App Store Award for Cultural Impact — first AAC app to receive this recognition

**Proloquo4Text** (Text-Based AAC)
- For literate users who need text-based communication
- Includes PolyPredix word prediction engine
- Supports Apple Personal Voice

### 1.2 Proloquo Coach — Companion App

**What it does:**
Proloquo Coach is a free companion app bundled with every Proloquo subscription. It serves as an educational and coaching platform for the entire support team around an AAC user.

**Key Features:**
- **Learning chapters:** Step-by-step, bite-sized lessons on AAC best practices (e.g., "Starting Proloquo With Your Child," "Learn About AAC")
- **Practice exercises:** Quizzes and interactive activities that reinforce AAC knowledge
- **Personalized feedback:** Custom insights and recommendations from AssistiveWare's team
- **Motivational notifications:** Reminders and encouragement to maintain consistent AAC modeling
- **Learning streams:** Users can choose content relevant to home or classroom contexts
- **Usage tab:** Weekly graphs showing how often the Proloquo app was used — basic usage frequency tracking
- **Support tab:** Direct messaging to AssistiveWare's in-app support team
- **Dual audience:** Designed for both parents/families and educators/teachers

**Who it's for:**
- Parents and family members learning to support AAC communication
- Teachers and classroom staff
- SLPs and clinicians (though primarily designed for non-clinical audiences)
- Grandparents and extended family

**Integration with Proloquo:**
- Automatically included with any Proloquo subscription at no additional cost
- All team members invited to a Proloquo team get access to Proloquo Coach
- Syncs with Proloquo to display usage statistics
- Available on iPhone and iPad

**Limitations:**
- Does NOT exist for Proloquo2Go — only works with the newer Proloquo app
- Usage tracking is limited to frequency metrics (times used per week), not vocabulary usage details, communication content, or language growth metrics
- No formal progress reporting for SLPs or IEP tracking
- No web-based version — iOS only

### 1.3 Parent/Caregiver Accounts & Team System

**Proloquo's Team Model (newer app):**
- **AAC Team Admin:** Primary account holder with full control over app settings, team membership, and data sharing preferences. Protected by a personal PIN.
- **Team members:** Can be assigned roles with varying permissions:
  - Vocabulary use permissions
  - Vocabulary editing permissions
  - Team member invitation permissions
- **Free team access:** Teachers, SLPs, grandparents, and other supporters can download Proloquo and join a team at no cost — only one paid subscription is needed per AAC user
- **Invitation system:** Team admin invites members via email; invitees receive setup instructions for both Proloquo and Proloquo Coach

**Proloquo2Go's User System (legacy app):**
- Multiple user profiles can be created within a single app installation
- No cloud-based team/account system
- Sharing is file-based (backups shared via AirDrop, Dropbox, Google Drive, or iTunes File Sharing)

**What's Missing:**
- No web-based parent/caregiver portal — everything is iOS app-based
- No SLP-specific dashboard or clinical reporting tools
- No progress tracking beyond basic usage frequency in Proloquo Coach
- No IEP goal tracking integration
- No communication logs or vocabulary growth analytics accessible to parents or professionals

### 1.6 Cloud Sync, Backup & Data Sharing

**Proloquo (newer app):**
- **Automatic cloud sync:** Vocabulary changes automatically synced across all team members' devices
- **Cross-device consistency:** "The user always has the same words in the same place, whether they are at home, school, or out in the community"
- **Backup/export:** Customization can be exported and backed up

**Proloquo2Go (legacy app):**
- **Automatic iCloud backup:** Enabled by default since version 5.0; stores one backup per user per device
- **Automatic Dropbox/Google Drive backup:** Stores the 3 most recent automatic backups; manual backups are retained indefinitely
- **AirDrop:** Direct device-to-device sharing without internet
- **Connect to Computer:** WiFi-based transfer to computer (same network required)
- **iTunes File Sharing:** Traditional wired backup method
- **Share buttons/folders:** Individual buttons and folders can be shared between Proloquo2Go users via AirDrop, Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive

### 1.7 Server-Side Infrastructure & Data

**Privacy-First Architecture:**
- "Communication messages are created on the hardware device itself and do not use any connection with AssistiveWare or other parties in the generation process" — all speech generation is fully on-device
- AssistiveWare will never collect conversation transcripts

**Anonymous Data Collection (opt-in only):**
- Requires AAC Team Admin consent
- Collects: number of different words spoken per week, total words spoken per week, frequency of 120 core words
- Data is fully anonymized — cannot identify individual users
- Used for product improvement and research publications
- AssistiveWare has consent-based anonymous data from tens of thousands of AAC users (10,000+ Proloquo2Go users, 1,000+ Proloquo4Text users)

**Account Infrastructure:**
- AssistiveWare account required for Proloquo (stores name, email, country)
- Stores AAC user settings, pronunciation exceptions, personalization (user-specific words, uploaded photos)
- Stores user activity metrics (number of times app is used to speak)
- In-app support messaging stored for support continuity

**No Web-Based Tools:**
- No web portal for any function
- No analytics dashboard accessible via browser
- No admin console for managing teams outside the iOS app
- All management is done within the iOS apps themselves

## TOPIC 2: LLM USAGE & AI TECHNOLOGY

### 2.1 Current Word Prediction: PolyPredix Engine

**Technology Type: Statistical prediction system (NOT neural/LLM-based)**

PolyPredix is AssistiveWare's proprietary word prediction engine, present in Proloquo2Go, Proloquo4Text, simPODD, Keeble, and Keedogo Plus.

**How PolyPredix Works:**
- **Statistical approach:** Uses frequency analysis and recency weighting rather than neural networks or language models
- **Prediction dictionary:** 90,000 common words and word combinations in highly optimized dictionaries
- **Multi-word prediction:** Goes beyond single-word completion — predicts multi-word sequences (e.g., typing "I wa" suggests "want to")
- **Context awareness:** Takes into account preceding words, leveraging common sentence patterns
- **Self-learning:** Adjusts word frequencies to individual user's patterns; learns new words and word combinations as user types
- **Language adaptive:** Can learn a new language entirely from user input
- **Effectiveness:** Reduces typing effort by 50%+ out-of-the-box with as few as 3 suggestions displayed

**Important distinction:** AssistiveWare explicitly describes PolyPredix as a "statistical prediction system" which "differs from linguistic rule-based systems." Statistical systems allow more freedom in writing style but do not guarantee grammatically correct sentences. This is clearly NOT an LLM or neural language model.

### 2.2 LLM/Generative AI Integration: NOT PRESENT

**Finding: Neither Proloquo2Go nor Proloquo currently use any LLM, large language model, or generative AI technology.**

Evidence:
- No announcements, blog posts, or press releases from AssistiveWare mentioning LLM, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any generative AI integration
- No App Store descriptions mention AI/ML beyond word prediction and neural voices
- PolyPredix is explicitly described as statistical, not neural
- Communication messages are generated entirely on-device with no cloud AI connection
- AssistiveWare's privacy architecture actively avoids cloud-based processing of communication data

### 2.3 Neural Technology: Limited to Voices Only

The only "neural" technology in AssistiveWare's products is:
- **Acapela Neural Voices:** Third-party neural text-to-speech voices providing more natural-sounding speech output
- **Apple Personal Voice:** Apple's on-device ML that creates a custom voice from 150 recorded phrases
- These are voice synthesis technologies, NOT language understanding or prediction models

### 2.4 AI/LLM in AAC: Industry Context

Research from PMC (2021) identifies several areas where AI could enhance AAC:
- **Context-aware prediction:** AAC systems adapted by location, time, communication partner, conversation content
- **Natural language processing:** Word/message prediction, word completion, icon-based prediction, automated part-of-speech tagging
- **Generative text:** LLMs generating contextually relevant communication suggestions

Recent research (2025) on GenAI in AAC found:
- SLPs became more efficient and confident with AI-generated AAC content
- **But:** Over-reliance risk and homogenization of communication options
- AAC users appreciated AI-generated phrases for reducing effort BUT noted "lack of personalization limited the acceptability"

**Key gap:** AssistiveWare has not publicly engaged with LLM integration while the broader assistive technology industry is exploring it. No public roadmap, no announced plans, no research partnerships on LLM technology.

### 2.5 Implications for Grant Project

This represents a significant opportunity:
1. **No LLM competitor in the AAC space** has shipped a production-quality integration as of early 2026
2. AssistiveWare's statistical PolyPredix, while effective at keystroke reduction, cannot provide contextual understanding, semantic prediction, or generative communication support
3. The privacy-first architecture (all on-device processing) both constrains and creates opportunity — on-device LLMs (Apple Intelligence, etc.) could be leveraged without compromising privacy
4. Research validates demand for context-aware, personalized prediction that goes beyond statistical frequency analysis

## TOPIC 3: EMOTIONAL TONE DETECTION & ADAPTATION

### 3.1 ExpressivePower Feature (Proloquo2Go Only)

**What it is:** ExpressivePower is AssistiveWare's trademark feature that enables AAC users to convey emotional tone through their synthesized speech. It is an **output expression tool** — NOT an emotion detection/sensing system.

**How it works:**
- Users can assign per-button voice settings: specific voice, volume, speech rate, pitch, and relative volume
- Pre-recorded emotional variants for common words: "dad/daddy/mom/mommy" in calling, angry, questioning, whining, and wheedling tones (children's voices)
- Pre-recorded play sounds: barking dog, fire truck siren, etc.
- Users can create buttons with custom ExpressivePower expressions without learning special codes
- Enables impersonations, dialog, mood-appropriate voice selection
- Provides "natural expressions such as excitement, frustration, boredom through change of intonation and pitch"

**Key limitation:** This is entirely user-initiated. The user must manually select the emotional expression they want to convey. There is no automatic detection of the user's emotional state.

### 3.2 Emotion DETECTION: NOT PRESENT

**Finding: Neither Proloquo2Go nor Proloquo includes any automatic emotion detection, affective computing, or emotional state recognition features.**

Specifically NOT present:
- No camera-based facial expression analysis
- No AR (augmented reality) features for emotion detection
- No physiological signal monitoring
- No behavioral pattern analysis for emotional state
- No frustration detection algorithms
- No fatigue detection or adaptation
- No HealthKit or Apple Health integration
- No heart rate, stress, or biometric data usage
- No adaptive UI that changes based on detected emotional state

### 3.3 Emotional EXPRESSION Support (Indirect)

While the apps don't detect emotions, they do support emotional communication:

**Emotional Vocabulary:**
- Both apps include vocabulary for emotional identification and expression
- Proloquo2Go helps users with "emotional identification and expression" including labeling feelings
- Example use case: child can tap "I need a break" instead of screaming when overwhelmed
- Research confirms that "increasing a student's ability to communicate helped decrease behaviour affected by distress, anxiety and frustration"

**ExpressivePower (Proloquo2Go):**
- Allows adding emotional tone to speech output
- User-selected, not automatic

**Functional Communication for Emotional Regulation:**
- The apps support functional communication replacement strategies (e.g., expressing frustration verbally instead of through challenging behavior)
- This is a fundamental AAC benefit, not a specific "emotion detection" feature

### 3.4 HealthKit / Health Data Integration: NOT PRESENT

No evidence of any HealthKit integration, health data access, or biometric data usage in any AssistiveWare product. This is consistent with their privacy-first approach and the fact that AAC apps operate in the communication domain rather than health monitoring.

### 3.5 Fatigue Detection / Adaptive UI: NOT PRESENT

No evidence of:
- Fatigue detection based on interaction patterns (slower responses, increased errors)
- Adaptive UI that simplifies based on detected fatigue
- Time-of-day adaptations
- Any system that modifies the interface based on inferred user state

The grid layout and vocabulary are static once configured. Proloquo uses a fixed 60-button grid. Proloquo2Go allows manual grid customization (23 pre-programmed sizes) but does not auto-adapt.

### 3.6 Research/Patents on Affective Features: NONE FOUND

No evidence of:
- Published research from AssistiveWare on emotional or affective computing
- Patents from AssistiveWare on emotion detection in AAC
- Academic partnerships focused on emotional sensing
- Public roadmap items related to affective computing

AssistiveWare's trademark portfolio includes: Crescendo, VocaPriority, ExpressivePower, and PolyPredix — all communication-focused, none affect-detection-focused.

### 3.7 Implications for Grant Project

This represents the largest unaddressed gap in current AAC technology:

1. **No AAC app on the market** currently provides automatic emotional state detection or adaptive UI
2. ExpressivePower is the closest feature to emotional computing in any AAC app, but it requires manual user selection — many AAC users (especially those with complex communication needs) cannot independently select appropriate emotional tone
3. Opportunity areas:
   - Camera-based expression recognition to auto-suggest emotional tone
   - Interaction pattern analysis (tap speed, error rate, pause patterns) for fatigue detection
   - HealthKit integration for physiological state awareness (heart rate via Apple Watch)
   - Adaptive UI that simplifies during detected stress/fatigue periods
   - Caregiver alerts when emotional distress patterns are detected
   - Contextual emotional vocabulary surfacing based on detected state

## USER SATISFACTION & PAIN POINTS

### Overall Ratings
- Proloquo2Go: Highly rated on App Store; described as "the best app" by many parents
- Proloquo: Won 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award
- Proloquo Coach: Praised for responsive support team and step-by-step guidance

### What Users Praise

**Parents:**
- "The best app you can get" for communication needs
- Child went from one word to 4-word sentences
- Responsive in-app support team ("responses within minutes")
- High customizability — personal photos, voices, layouts
- Proloquo Coach provides "step-by-step guidance"
- App "grows with the user's development"
- "Game changer" for families

**SLPs/Clinicians:**
- "Pretty impressive" Proloquo vocabulary system (NWACS review)
- Static core words and column feature praised
- Related words feature (activating 'friend' shows 'buddy', 'BFF', 'pal')
- Proloquo Coach makes app "more user-friendly for families"
- Free professional evaluation licenses valued
- Core Word Classroom is a well-regarded free resource

**Teachers:**
- Free team access appreciated (only one subscription needed)
- Cross-device sync means consistent vocabulary at home and school

### Pain Points and Complaints

**Cost/Pricing:**
- Proloquo2Go at $249.99 is "the most expensive AAC application on the market"
- "The price is steep, and it was a hard pill to swallow"
- Proloquo's subscription model frustrates some users who prefer one-time purchase
- Subscription model described as "problematic for state funding programs"
- Insurance process for AAC funding is complex

**Learning Curve:**
- "Big learning curve in navigation and learning how to customize things was very difficult and frustrating"
- "Even having prior experience with picture-based apps"
- Overwhelming for first-time AAC users, "especially if you aren't full-time"
- 80% of Proloquo2Go users do NOT follow recommended settings
- Many well-intentioned people make the app "easier" by reducing grid size, leading to poorer language outcomes

**Navigation/Vocabulary Organization:**
- "Users could become overwhelmed by vocabulary being in different places"
- "If language takes multiple button hits to get to, users may become frustrated"
- "Words can be found in multiple places, becoming confusing"
- "Vocabulary in the folder-based system can be difficult to navigate if a person doesn't understand what categories objects should be in"

**Motor Planning:**
- Proloquo2Go "does not really use motor planning" — buttons can be moved around, undermining muscle memory
- "May not be fully suited for individuals with severe motor planning difficulties"
- Proloquo addresses this with fixed core word positions, but has less flexibility as a result

**Voice Quality (Recent):**
- After a voice update: "grating breathy quality" that made some voices "less clear sounding"
- "Weird blip sounds on some words/noises that are startling"
- Subsequent update improved quality: "voices are now of higher quality sounding smoother and clearer"

**Accessibility Limitations (Proloquo):**
- Concerns about "accessibility for the visually impaired" in newer Proloquo app
- Fixed 60-button grid in Proloquo limits accommodation for visual/motor needs (Proloquo2Go offers 23 grid sizes)
- Limited language options compared to Proloquo2Go

**Data & Progress Tracking:**
- No formal progress reporting for SLPs or parents
- No data export capabilities
- AssistiveWare has explicitly stated they "do not want to provide data to parents or professionals until they find a way that aligns with their privacy principles"
- Recents/History features "were not designed for data collection"
- No vocabulary growth analytics
- No communication frequency trending beyond basic weekly graph in Proloquo Coach

**Platform Limitations:**
- iOS/Apple only — no Android version
- No web-based interface for any function
- School deployment complexity due to subscription model + Apple ecosystem requirements

### Critical Gap Summary for Grant Project
