## Replication status — flags by finding

### Anterior-insula structural reduction
**Status**: Well-replicated (multi-study meta-analytic synthesis). **Confidence**: High. **Watch**: The voxel-based-morphometry literature in psychiatry generally has replication concerns; even well-replicated regions have heterogeneous effect sizes. The qualitative direction (reduction in AI) is robust; the magnitude is variable.

### AI hypoactivation during emotional processing
**Status**: Replicated. **Confidence**: High for negative-valence stimuli; moderate-to-low for positive-valence (less studied). **Watch**: Most paradigms use negative material; the alexithymia-positive-emotion mechanistic story is much thinner.

### Hogeveen 2016 lesion dose-response (>15% AI threshold)
**Status**: Single high-quality study. **Confidence**: Strong design (200+ veterans, lesion-symptom mapping, controls for ACC and total volume). **Watch**: Not yet directly replicated. The "15% threshold" is a discretisation of a continuous dose-response and should not be over-reified. The 15% figure is also for *anterior insula* specifically — a different lesion fraction in a different region might have a different threshold. The user should hold this as the strongest single causal claim in the literature, with the appropriate caveat.

### Supragenual ACC hyperactivation
**Status**: Replicated across several studies. **Confidence**: Moderate. **Watch**: The interpretation as *compensatory* is theoretically motivated but not directly tested. Alternative interpretations (alternative attention recruitment, generic salience-network recalibration) are possible. The behavioural / mechanistic linkage is weaker than the AI evidence.

### ACC GABA findings
**Status**: Sparse literature. **Confidence**: Low. **Flag**: Treat as suggestive only. The downstream content should not lead with this finding.

### Reduced amygdala activation
**Status**: Mixed. **Confidence**: Low-to-moderate. **Watch**: Replication failures are notable. Some studies find no difference; some find increased rather than decreased activation. Effect-size heterogeneity is high. The downstream content correctly hedges this.

### Pohl 2018 amygdala-ACC reduced coupling
**Status**: Single resting-state fMRI study. **Confidence**: Low. **Watch**: Not yet replicated.

### OFC and mPFC findings
**Status**: Suggestive, smaller literatures. **Confidence**: Low-to-moderate. **Watch**: These findings are theoretically interesting (OFC for somatic-marker, mPFC for self-referential and conceptual processing) but the evidence base is much smaller than for AI/ACC. Frame as *plausible-but-not-yet-replicated* rather than *established*.

### Bird 2016 self-reported general interoception failure (PMC5098957)
**Status**: Foundational paper, methodologically self-report-only. **Confidence**: Moderate. **Watch**: Self-reported interoception measures are themselves susceptible to alexithymia-related self-report problems (Lundh paradox). The downstream content should note that the foundational paper used self-report measures.

### Murphy 2018 multidomain (respiration/muscle/taste) (PMC5824617)
**Status**: Single high-quality study. **Confidence**: Strong design with three different non-cardiac modalities. **Watch**: Not yet independently replicated outside the Bird group. The replication base is internal-to-Bird-lab, with the partial exception of subsequent autism-sample work using related paradigms.

### Murphy 2018b control-variable HCT effect (PubMed 29803614)
**Status**: Real, but methodologically hedged. **Confidence**: Moderate as published. **Watch**: The "the relationship emerges only after controlling for ten covariates" finding is statistically defensible but should be presented as a methodological clarification, not a clean substantive effect. The HCT itself is contested; clean signal requires confound correction.

### Nicholson 2018 Bayesian-null (PMC6089261)
**Status**: Real, real third-party group. **Confidence**: Methodologically robust within the cardiac-HCT paradigm. **Watch**: The Bird-group response is that cardiac HCT is a poor measurement tool. This is a genuine empirical disagreement, not a misattribution. The downstream content should present this as an active live debate, not as resolved.

### Garfinkel 2015 three-dimensional model (PMC paper)
**Status**: Architectural standard now widely adopted. **Confidence**: High. **Watch**: The model is more architectural than empirical-mechanism. The dissociation among the three dimensions is well-supported; the *causal* relationships among them are less well-mapped.

### Aaron 2020 quadratic relationship (PMC7076086)
**Status**: Single pilot study (N=76). **Confidence**: Moderate. **Watch**: Not yet directly replicated. The U-shaped relationship is consistent with broader heterogeneity but the specific quadratic curve has not been confirmed in larger samples. The pilot mindfulness-intervention finding (effect-modification by baseline IA) is suggestive but very small and should be presented as preliminary.

### Quattrocki & Friston 2014 predictive-processing
**Status**: Theoretical framework. **Confidence**: Low for direct empirical test in alexithymia; moderate as a generative theoretical synthesis. **Watch**: This is a theoretical proposal, not an empirically established mechanism. The downstream content correctly presents it as theoretical. Some convergent computational-modeling work exists but no direct alexithymia-precision-weighting RCT has been published.

### Barrett constructed emotion
**Status**: Broad theoretical frame; some empirical components well-supported (granularity-alexithymia inverse relationship). **Confidence**: High for the granularity finding; moderate for the broader frame as applied to alexithymia. **Watch**: Barrett's frame is contested in affective neuroscience generally; some critics argue it is unfalsifiable. The downstream content does not need to relitigate this debate but should present Barrett as one frame among several rather than as established.

### Preece attention-appraisal
**Status**: Operationalised via PAQ. **Confidence**: Moderate. **Watch**: The *behaviour-level* model is supported by PAQ psychometrics; the *mechanistic* claim that alexithymia is fundamentally an attention-or-appraisal failure is a theoretical reinterpretation not yet directly tested at the neural level. Taylor & Bagby (2024) explicitly contest this reformulation.

### Sifneos psychoanalytic frame (Taylor & Bagby 2024 defense)
**Status**: Active in 2024 literature; defended explicitly. **Confidence**: As a frame, well-articulated; as a mechanism, descriptive rather than mechanistic. **Watch**: The downstream content should present this as a real, defended frame rather than a historical artifact.

## Theoretical frame relationships — complementary vs competing

The downstream content should be careful about how the frames relate:

- **Bird/Murphy/Brewer (interoception route)** vs **Barrett (constructed emotion route)**. These are *different upstream loci* on the Hobson 2019 multiroute model. Not competing in the strong sense — they could both be true for different alexithymics or for the same alexithymic at different layers.
- **Quattrocki & Friston (predictive processing)** is a *computational frame* that can be applied to either of the above. Not strictly competing with Bird or Barrett; it offers a different abstraction layer.
- **Preece (attention-appraisal)** vs **Taylor & Bagby (Sifneos defense)**. These *are* in active dispute (Taylor & Bagby 2024 explicitly critiques Preece).
- **Sifneos psychoanalytic** maps onto the Hobson "language/symbolic-elaboration" route reasonably well, even though Taylor & Bagby would not phrase it that way.

The downstream content should present these as a layered architecture rather than as a single horserace. The Hobson multiroute model is the most useful integrative frame.

## Where the user-framing risk is high

- **"Where does my alexithymia live in the brain?"** This is the user's natural question. The honest answer is multiroute. The content must NOT collapse to a single neuroanatomical answer. Watch for unintentional drift toward "the answer is the anterior insula" — true that AI is the most replicated single locus, but it is *not* the answer for the user as a whole.
- **Replication caveats must be preserved**. The concept research's Replication-Status appendix must survive into the explanation and webpage; it is the honest hedge that prevents over-claiming.
- **Universal corrections must survive into the page**. Mistakes in attribution in the page text or sources list would propagate downstream.
- **The Hogeveen 15% threshold should not be over-reified**. It is a discretisation of a dose-response, not a magic cutoff.
