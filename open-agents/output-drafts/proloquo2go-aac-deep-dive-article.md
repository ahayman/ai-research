# Proloquo2Go: The Gold Standard in AAC Communication

When a parent set up an iPad loaded with Proloquo2Go for her nonverbal daughter, something remarkable happened: the child gained eight new words in a single hour. This kind of breakthrough moment is what has made Proloquo2Go the most widely used symbol-based AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) app in the world, trusted by hundreds of thousands of users across the globe. But behind the transformative success stories lies a complex tool with both remarkable strengths and notable limitations that every prospective user, family member, and speech-language pathologist should understand.

Since its launch in April 2009, Proloquo2Go has fundamentally reshaped how nonspeaking individuals communicate. Developed by the Dutch company AssistiveWare, it was the first full-featured AAC app for iOS, and it single-handedly triggered a revolution in consumer device-based augmentative communication. Before Proloquo2Go, dedicated AAC devices cost between $5,000 and $15,000. At $249.99 as a one-time purchase, it democratized access to communication technology in a way the field had never seen.

## The Origins: A Personal Mission Becomes a Global Solution

The story of AssistiveWare begins not in a corporate boardroom, but with a personal tragedy. In 1996, Giesbert Nijhuis, a close friend of David Niemeijer, became paralyzed from the neck down in a car accident. Niemeijer, who would go on to earn his PhD, responded by developing an on-screen keyboard called KeyStrokes -- the first universal access solution for Mac OS X. That project became the seed for AssistiveWare, officially founded in Amsterdam in 2000.

For nearly a decade, the company focused on Mac-based accessibility tools. Then, in 2009, the iPhone and iPad presented an unprecedented opportunity. The company released Proloquo2Go as the first full-featured AAC application for iOS, and the impact was immediate. For the first time, families could walk into an Apple Store, purchase a mainstream consumer device, and have a powerful communication system up and running for a fraction of the cost of traditional dedicated AAC hardware.

The name itself carries meaning: "Proloquo" derives from the Latin word for "speak out," and the "2Go" suffix signals its portable, on-the-go nature -- a radical departure from the bulky, expensive devices that had defined the AAC landscape for decades.

## How It Works: The Crescendo Vocabulary System

At the heart of Proloquo2Go is the **Crescendo** vocabulary system, a research-based framework built on data from over 10,000 users. Crescendo is grounded in a simple but powerful linguistic insight: just 200 to 400 "core words" account for roughly 80% of everything we say in daily conversation. Words like "I," "want," "go," "more," and "help" form the backbone of communication regardless of age, context, or language.

The system organizes vocabulary into a folder-based hierarchy. Core words sit on the main page, immediately accessible with a single tap. Fringe vocabulary -- more specific terms like "dinosaur," "playground," or "chocolate" -- lives in categorical folders that users navigate into as needed. The system offers three pre-programmed levels:

- **Basic Communication**: Simplified vocabulary for beginning communicators
- **Intermediate Core**: Expanded word set for developing language skills
- **Advanced Core**: Full vocabulary with 10,000+ fringe words for complex expression

This progressive structure is designed to grow with the user. A child who starts with Basic Communication can graduate to Intermediate and then Advanced as their language skills develop, without needing to learn an entirely new system.

## Symbol Library and Voice Technology

Proloquo2Go's communication power rests on two pillars: its visual symbol system and its voice output.

### 27,000+ Symbols

The app includes over 27,000 symbols that represent words, phrases, and concepts through easily recognizable images. Users can also add custom photos from their device's camera, photo library, or Dropbox account -- a critical feature for personalizing communication. Want to add a symbol for your family's pet, your child's favorite teacher, or the specific brand of snack they prefer? Custom photos make it possible.

The symbol library was expanded significantly in version 8.4 (June 2024), adding thousands of new symbols and improving visual representation across categories. Symbols follow a color-coding system based on the Fitzgerald Key, which assigns colors to different parts of speech (nouns in yellow, verbs in green, adjectives in blue, and so on), helping users develop grammar awareness alongside vocabulary.

### Natural-Sounding Voices

The app offers over 100 free natural-sounding voices across multiple languages and accents. This is not an afterthought feature -- AssistiveWare has been a pioneer in voice technology for AAC. In 2012, they partnered with Acapela Group to create the first genuine text-to-speech children's voices, addressing a long-standing gap where young users were forced to communicate through adult-sounding synthetic speech.

The latest versions (8.4+) feature **Acapela Neural Voices**, which leverage modern neural network technology for significantly smoother, more natural-sounding output. Voice updates are now separated from main app updates, meaning users can receive voice improvements without needing to reinstall or update the entire application.

**Language support** spans English (with regional variants for the US, UK, Canada, and Australia), Spanish, French, and Dutch. Notably, Proloquo2Go pioneered **bilingual code-switching** in 2015 -- users can switch languages mid-sentence, a feature that reflects how many bilingual families actually communicate.

## Accessibility: Designed for Every Body

Where Proloquo2Go truly distinguishes itself from competitors is in its comprehensive accessibility support. The app was built from the ground up to serve users with vastly different physical and cognitive abilities.

### Grid Flexibility

The app offers **23 pre-programmed grid layouts** ranging from 9 buttons per page (for users with significant motor or vision challenges) to 144 buttons per page (for users with good fine motor skills who want maximum vocabulary density). The recommended default is 77 buttons, though research reveals that 80% of users override this setting -- often choosing smaller grids that, unfortunately, research suggests lead to poorer language outcomes.

### Switch Scanning

For users who cannot directly touch the screen, Proloquo2Go supports multiple switch scanning methods:

- **Automatic scanning**: The system cycles through items automatically; user activates switch to select
- **Step scanning**: User advances through items one at a time with switch presses
- **Inverse scanning**: Items advance automatically; user holds switch to stop
- **Automatic step scanning**: Combines automatic advancement with step-based selection
- **Screen-as-switch mode**: The device's own screen serves as a large switch
- **External switches**: Via Bluetooth or wired connections

The app also supports **auditory scanning**, where items are spoken aloud as they are highlighted -- essential for users with vision impairments who cannot rely on visual cues alone.

### Apple Watch Integration

Users can speak from their Apple Watch, even in Silent Mode (as of v8.5). The Apple Watch can also function as a single switch for controlling Proloquo2Go on a paired iPhone, providing a discreet access method that does not draw attention to the user's communication needs.

### Phone Call Support

Perhaps the most exciting recent addition: **version 8.7** (February 2025) introduced the ability to use Proloquo2Go's voice during phone calls and FaceTime calls on iOS/iPadOS 18.2+. This represents a meaningful step toward full communication independence, allowing AAC users to participate in phone conversations using their chosen voice.

**Autism Spectrum Disorder (35%)** represents the largest user group. Published research in PubMed has demonstrated that "Proloquo2Go Enhances Classroom Performance in Children With Autism Spectrum Disorder," validating its effectiveness for this population. The visual symbol system aligns well with the visual learning strengths common in autism.

**Down Syndrome (15%)** users benefit from the simplified grid options and progressive vocabulary levels that match developmental progression.

**Cerebral Palsy (10%)** users leverage the extensive switch scanning options, adjustable grid sizes, and keyguard support to overcome motor challenges.

**Other conditions (40%)** include apraxia of speech, dysarthria, traumatic brain injury, stroke/aphasia, Angelman syndrome, and a wide range of genetic and neurological conditions affecting speech.

## Pricing, Funding, and the Economics of Communication

### The Cost Equation

Proloquo2Go costs **$249.99** as a one-time purchase from the Apple App Store. This includes all future updates at no additional charge and access to all 100+ voices. The app supports Apple Family Sharing, allowing it to be installed on multiple family devices.

While $249.99 is not trivial, context matters enormously. Dedicated AAC devices still cost between $3,000 and $15,000. Even among competing iPad apps, Proloquo2Go is competitively priced:

### Funding Pathways

Families rarely need to pay the full cost out of pocket:

- **Health insurance/Medicaid**: With a speech-language pathologist's recommendation, many insurance plans cover AAC app costs
- **IEP funding**: School districts are required to provide communication tools for students with Individualized Education Programs
- **Early Intervention programs**: County-level services for children under 3
- **AbleNet partnership**: AssistiveWare partners with AbleNet to help families navigate the insurance funding process
- **Dedicated device option**: Proloquo2Go can be purchased pre-loaded on dedicated AAC hardware (ACCI Choice Pro, ProSlate), which may have better insurance coverage than consumer iPad purchases

A significant advantage of Proloquo2Go's one-time purchase model: **some state programs refuse to fund subscription-based apps**, making Proloquo2Go eligible for funding where competitors with subscription models are not.

## Strengths: What Makes Proloquo2Go Stand Out

### 1. The Pioneer Advantage
Being the first full-featured AAC app for iOS (2009) gave Proloquo2Go a massive head start. Hundreds of thousands of users, SLPs, and educators have built their AAC expertise around this app. Training materials, support communities, and clinical knowledge are deeply established.

### 2. Research-Backed Vocabulary
The Crescendo system is not based on guesswork -- it was built from data gathered from over 10,000 actual users. This evidence base gives clinicians confidence in the vocabulary organization and progression levels.

### 3. Accessibility Depth
No competitor matches Proloquo2Go's range of access methods. With 23 grid sizes, five switch scanning modes, auditory scanning, Apple Watch control, and keyguard support, it can be configured for nearly any physical or cognitive profile.

### 4. True Bilingual Communication
The ability to switch languages mid-sentence is not a gimmick -- it reflects how bilingual families actually communicate. For families where Spanish and English (or French and English) are both spoken at home, this feature is transformative.

### 5. Lifetime Value
One payment, lifetime updates. In an era of software subscriptions, this is increasingly rare and especially meaningful for families managing the expenses of disability.

### 6. Ecosystem Integration
From Apple Watch support to phone call integration (v8.7) to iCloud backup, Proloquo2Go takes full advantage of the Apple ecosystem in ways that enhance daily communication.

## Weaknesses: Where Proloquo2Go Falls Short

### 1. The Learning Curve Problem
The most consistent criticism from users and professionals alike: Proloquo2Go is hard to learn. The depth of customization that makes it powerful also makes initial setup and configuration daunting. As one user put it: "There is a big learning curve in navigation and learning how to customize things was very difficult and frustrating."

### 2. Folder Navigation Complexity
The folder-based vocabulary system means users must sometimes navigate through multiple layers to find specific words. A user wanting to say "I want to go to the playground" might need to tap through several folders to locate "playground" in the fringe vocabulary. This multi-tap requirement can slow communication and frustrate users, especially children.

### 3. Motor Planning Vulnerability
Unlike LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning), Proloquo2Go does not prioritize consistent motor patterns. Buttons can be moved, added, or removed, which means the physical path to a word can change -- undermining the muscle memory that experienced users develop over time.

### 4. iOS Exclusivity
Proloquo2Go is available only on Apple devices. For families who prefer or can only afford Android devices, this is a dealbreaker. It also means the app cannot be used on Windows-based school computers or Chromebooks, which are common in educational settings.

### 5. The Grid Size Paradox
Research shows that larger grids (77+ buttons) lead to better language outcomes, yet 80% of users choose smaller grids. The app allows this, but some critics argue it should do more to guide users toward optimal settings.

### 6. No Symbol-Based Word Prediction
Some competing apps offer word prediction within the symbol interface, suggesting likely next words based on context. Proloquo2Go lacks this feature in symbol mode (though it has text-based word prediction in its keyboard).

### 7. The Proloquo Question
AssistiveWare released Proloquo in 2022 as a "next-generation" AAC app. While the company has stated there are no plans to discontinue Proloquo2Go, the existence of a newer product naturally raises questions about where future development resources will be concentrated.

### When to Choose Proloquo2Go Over Alternatives

**Choose Proloquo2Go if:**
- The user needs extensive accessibility accommodations (switch scanning, variable grid sizes)
- The family is bilingual and needs code-switching
- You want a one-time purchase with no recurring costs
- The user needs Apple Watch integration or phone call support
- Deep customization is a priority

**Consider alternatives if:**
- Motor planning consistency is the primary clinical goal (consider LAMP)
- Cross-platform support is essential (consider TD Snap)
- The user is a first-time AAC user who may be overwhelmed by complexity (consider Proloquo or simpler apps)
- The family uses Android devices (no iOS option available)

## Recent Updates and the Road Ahead

Proloquo2Go continues to evolve at a steady pace. The 2024-2025 release cycle brought genuinely meaningful improvements:

- **Neural voices** (v8.4): AI-powered voices that sound significantly more natural
- **Voice independence** (v8.4): Voice updates decoupled from app updates
- **New children's voices** (v8.4): Four additional child voice options from Acapela
- **Silent Mode Apple Watch** (v8.5): Speak from wrist even when device is silent
- **Phone call integration** (v8.7): Use AAC voice in phone and FaceTime calls

The phone call feature in particular represents a significant leap in communication independence. For the first time, AAC users can participate in phone conversations using their chosen synthetic voice -- a capability that was previously limited to dedicated AAC hardware.

While AssistiveWare has not published a formal public roadmap, the company's track record of consistent updates and its stated commitment to continuing Proloquo2Go development alongside the newer Proloquo app suggest that the product will continue to receive meaningful improvements.

## Key Takeaways

- **Proloquo2Go is the most established and widely-used symbol-based AAC app**, with 15+ years of development and hundreds of thousands of users worldwide
- **The Crescendo vocabulary is research-backed**, built from real usage data from 10,000+ users, and designed to support language growth from beginner to advanced
- **Accessibility support is unmatched**, with 23 grid sizes, comprehensive switch scanning, auditory scanning, Apple Watch control, and keyguard support
- **The $249.99 one-time price** is competitive and insurance-fundable, with no recurring subscription costs
- **The learning curve is real** -- families and professionals should expect a significant investment of time in setup and training
- **iOS exclusivity limits device options** but enables deep Apple ecosystem integration
- **The app continues active development** despite the release of the newer Proloquo app

## Conclusion

Proloquo2Go occupies a unique position in the AAC landscape: it is simultaneously the most powerful and the most complex symbol-based communication app available. For users who need its accessibility depth, bilingual capabilities, or extensive customization, there is simply no equivalent. For first-time AAC users without professional guidance, the complexity can be a genuine barrier to success.

The app's 15-year track record, 4.77-star rating, and ongoing development demonstrate that AssistiveWare takes its commitment to this product seriously. The recent addition of phone call support and neural voices shows that Proloquo2Go is not just being maintained -- it is being actively improved.

Whether Proloquo2Go is the right choice depends on the individual user's needs, their support team's expertise, and their device preferences. But for the hundreds of thousands of people who rely on it daily, Proloquo2Go is not just an app -- it is their voice.
