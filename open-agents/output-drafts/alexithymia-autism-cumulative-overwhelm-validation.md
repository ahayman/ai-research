# Self-Validation Notes — Category 8

## Validation approach

This category is built primarily from Supplement 3, which itself was a literature audit honoring the user's request for "weight proportional to evidence." Self-validation here focuses on:

1. Whether each empirical claim in the concept research has been faithfully transmitted from Supplement 3 with appropriate evidence-weight flagging.
2. Whether counter-evidence has been honoured rather than papered over.
3. Whether the cascade is presented as a synthesis of empirically anchored components rather than as a single empirically-validated mechanism.
4. Whether the user-specific framing (option C) has been consistently applied: each link presented at its evidence weight, the unified cascade flagged as plausible-but-untested.

## Claim-by-claim audit

### CONFIRMED

**Claim**: PTSD-pattern symptoms occur in autistic adults at 32–60% lifetime rates.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, replicated empirical finding.
- Source weight: Multiple primary studies and reviews cited in Supplement 3.

**Claim**: Rumball et al. 2020 found >40% of trauma-exposed autistic adults met PTSD threshold from non-Criterion-A events.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, primary empirical anchor for the category.
- Citation: PMID 32319731, DOI 10.1002/aur.2306.

**Claim**: Higgins/Arnold 2025 found r = 0.70 between autistic burnout (ABSI) and PTSS (PCL-5).
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, n=91, p < .001.
- Hedge required: r=.70 is substantial overlap, NOT identity. Burnout and PTSD remain distinct constructs with different recovery pathways.
- This hedge has been applied throughout the concept research and will be applied throughout the explanation.

**Claim**: Raymaker et al. 2020 defines autistic burnout as cumulative-load model.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, foundational community-based participatory research.
- PMC7313636. Three primary characteristics (chronic exhaustion, loss of skills, reduced tolerance to stimulus); cumulative-load etiology.

**Claim**: Murphy/Brewer/Bird 2018 demonstrate multidomain interoceptive failure in alexithymia.
- Status: **CONFIRMED, STILL CLAIMED** (across cardiac, respiratory, muscular, taste). PMC5824617.
- Universal correction: NOT walked back. What was walked back is the autism-specific interoception claim. Shah/Bird 2016 show it's "alexithymia, not autism."
- Framing applied throughout: *co-occurring alexithymia produces interoceptive failure in many autistic adults; autism per se does not produce interoceptive failure.*

**Claim**: CAT-Q (Hull 2019) scores correlate with depression, anxiety; total/Assimilation negatively correlate with well-being.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**.
- PMC6394586.

**Claim**: Cassidy et al. (2018, 2020, 2023) establish camouflaging as risk factor for suicidality, mediated through thwarted belongingness; extended via IMV model to defeat, entrapment, lifetime suicidal thoughts.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, replicated across multiple studies.

**Claim**: Co-twin control study (PMC12659362, 2025) finds camouflaging associated with psychological and physiological stress with stronger causal inference.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**.

**Claim**: Botha & Frost 2020 minority stress framework predicts poor mental health in autistic adults.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, replicated. DOI 10.1177/2156869318804297.

**Claim**: Milton 2012, 2022 double empathy problem with empirical support from Crompton 2020 telephone-game studies.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**.

**Claim**: Mahler 2022 interoception training improves emotion regulation in autistic children.
- Status: **CONFIRMED**, but flagged as treatment study, not mechanism study. Inferential support for the late-recognition hypothesis.
- PMC9045986.

**Claim**: Quattrocki & Friston 2014 propose autism as failure of interoceptive predictive coding centered on aberrant oxytocin precision-weighting.
- Status: **CONFIRMED as a published model**. Bird 2015 commentary reframes as alexithymia-not-autism.
- Flagged: theoretical, not empirically validated as breakdown mechanism.

### CONTESTED — flagged appropriately

**Claim**: Kupferstein 2018 reported PTSS in 46% of ABA-exposed respondents.
- Status: **METHODOLOGICALLY CONTESTED**.
- Counter-evidence: PMC9114057 critical response.
- Framing applied: "Some autistic adults report PTSS-pattern symptoms following ABA exposure (Kupferstein 2018, contested); the broader observation that instructed masking can be traumatizing has clinical writing support but lacks rigorous quantitative confirmation."

**Claim**: Universal ANS dysfunction in autism (polyvagal-informed framing).
- Status: **CHALLENGED by PMC8964964**.
- Framing applied: "Polyvagal-informed framing has substantial reduced-HRV empirical support, but the *specific* claims about chronic dorsal-vagal-shutdown-as-trauma-equivalent in autistic adults without discrete trauma exposure are clinical-writing extensions of the theory, not directly tested. PMC8964964 should be presented as legitimate counter-evidence."

**Claim**: Pathological dissociation in autistic adults tracks autistic traits per se.
- Status: **NOT SUPPORTED**.
- Counter-evidence: 2026 study (S305065652600012X) shows dissociation tracks bullying victimization, not autism traits per se.
- Framing applied: "Pathological dissociation in autistic adults was associated with bullying victimization rather than with autistic traits per se — supporting the model that dissociation tracks chronic trauma exposure rather than being an autism-intrinsic feature."

### THEORETICAL EXTENSIONS — flagged as such

**Claim**: Schimmenti's developmental-trauma-alexithymia framework applied to autism.
- Status: **THEORETICAL EXTENSION**, not Schimmenti's own focus.
- Framing applied: "The conceptual extension is plausible because [reasons]. But this remains theoretical synthesis, not Schimmenti's primary research focus."

**Claim**: The cumulative-overwhelm cascade as a unified causal chain.
- Status: **PLAUSIBLE SYNTHESIS WITH EMPIRICALLY VALIDATED COMPONENTS AND NO VALIDATED UNIFIED TEST**.
- Framing applied: "Each link has empirical support; the whole chain has not been validated as a single longitudinal mechanism. The substrate is empirical; the unification is a plausible synthesis."

**Claim**: Specific chain "failure-to-detect → cascade-to-breakdown" causal pathway.
- Status: **THEORETICALLY ARTICULATED, EMPIRICALLY UNTESTED**.
- Framing applied throughout.

### CLINICAL CONSENSUS WITHOUT EMPIRICAL TEST — flagged

**Claim**: Repeated meltdowns are themselves traumagenic events.
- Status: **CLINICAL CONSENSUS + INFERENTIAL FROM RUMBALL; DIRECT LONGITUDINAL TEST ABSENT**.
- Framing applied: "No published longitudinal study tracks autistic individuals' meltdown frequency and subsequent PTSS development. Inferential evidence is moderate."

**Claim**: Burnout cascade (chronic masking → exhaustion → burnout → depression/suicidality).
- Status: **PROBABLE BUT NOT EMPIRICALLY VALIDATED CAUSAL CHAIN**.
- Framing applied.
