# Validation: Image Schemas as Visual Building Blocks

## Validation Summary

This document is strongly researched with accurate core claims. The DISL publication exists and is correctly described. Johnson's image schema inventory, Mandler's developmental theory, and Grady's Primary Metaphor Hypothesis are all well-sourced and accurately presented. The one area requiring attention is the precise publication dating of DISL (published online 2024, assigned to volume 25, issue 2, which is the 2025 issue of the journal).

## Claim-by-Claim Verification

### Johnson's Image Schema Inventory (1987)

| Claim | Rating | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Mark Johnson introduced "image schema" in *The Body in the Mind* (1987) | CONFIRMED | Published by University of Chicago Press. Johnson defined image schema as "a recurring, dynamic pattern of our perceptual interactions and motor programs that gives coherence and structure to our experience" (p. xiv). |
| Core schemas: CONTAINER, PATH/SOURCE-PATH-GOAL, FORCE, BALANCE, LINK, CENTER-PERIPHERY, CYCLE, PART-WHOLE, UP-DOWN/VERTICALITY, NEAR-FAR/PROXIMITY, SCALE, CONTACT, SURFACE, FULL-EMPTY, ITERATION, SPLITTING, MERGING, OBJECT/COLLECTION | CONFIRMED | Johnson provided an initial list on p. 126 of *The Body in the Mind*. The specific schemas listed in the document are all recognized in the image schema literature. |
| Schemas are pre-linguistic, embodied, spatial, schematic, productive | CONFIRMED | These five properties are well-established characterizations of image schemas in the cognitive linguistics literature. |
| Johnson used simple diagrams (lines, arrows, circles) in original work | CONFIRMED | Johnson's diagrams are scattered throughout the book, though he only diagrammed a portion of the schemas he listed. |
| Image schema inventory is open-ended (no closed set) | CONFIRMED | The Wikipedia article on image schemas notes this: "the image schema list has never constituted a closed set." Different researchers include different schemas. |

**Section Confidence: 10/10**

### Mandler's Developmental Theory

| Claim | Rating | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Jean Mandler proposed Perceptual Meaning Analysis (PMA) | CONFIRMED | Mandler described PMA as an innate, domain-general mechanism that generates concepts from attended perceptual data. Published in Oxford Academic and multiple other venues. |
| PMA is a "data summarization device" | CONFIRMED | Mandler characterizes PMA as redescribing rich perceptual information into sparser, more abstract image-schematic format. |
| Earliest concepts are global categories composed of simplified spatial information | CONFIRMED | "The earliest concepts infants form tend to be global categories" — this is a core Mandler claim, well-documented. |
| Innately salient information: paths of motion, in/out containment, contingencies, self-motion vs. caused-motion | CONFIRMED | These specific categories of innately salient spatial information are documented in Mandler's publications. |
| Developmental progression: pre-linguistic spatial analysis → schema formation → conceptual subdivision → language interface → metaphorical extension | CONFIRMED | This accurately summarizes Mandler's developmental model. |

**Section Confidence: 10/10**

### DISL — Diagrammatic Image Schema Language (2024/2025)

| Claim | Rating | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| DISL exists as a published system | CONFIRMED | The article "The Diagrammatic Image Schema Language (DISL)" by Hedblom, Neuhaus, and Mossakowski is published and available via Taylor & Francis Online. |
| Authors: Maria M. Hedblom, Fabian Neuhaus, Till Mossakowski | CONFIRMED | All three authors confirmed in the publication record. |
| Published in *Spatial Cognition and Computation* | CONFIRMED | Journal title confirmed. |
| Publication year: "2024" per research document | UNCERTAIN (7/10) | The document states "published 2024, volume 25, 2025 issue." The article was published online in 2024 (DOI assigned 2024) but appears in Volume 25, Issue 2, pages 138-175, which is the 2025 issue. Hedblom's blog post from July 3, 2024 says "to be published by Spatial Cognition and Computation." The ADS abstract lists it as 2025. The most accurate statement is: "published online 2024, in the 2025 volume." |
| DISL draws on Mandler and Pagan Canovas's conceptual primitives | CONFIRMED | The article is described as systematizing "visual combination rules for different conceptual primitives from the cognitive science literature." |
| Uses comic-strip-like sequences of panels for temporal narratives | CONFIRMED | The strip format is a documented feature of DISL. |
| Includes a machine-readable logical exchange format | CONFIRMED | "DISL contains a logical exchange format in which the diagrams may be made machine-readable." |
| DISL is descriptive/analytic, not a productive communication system | CONFIRMED | This is an accurate characterization — DISL is a formal notation for researchers. |

**Section Confidence: 9/10** — The publication year should be clarified as "published online 2024, in volume 25 (2025)."

| AFFECTION IS WARMTH grounded in caregiver holding | CONFIRMED | Standard example in the primary metaphor literature. |
| DIFFICULTY IS HEAVINESS grounded in FORCE schema | CONFIRMED | Standard primary metaphor example. |

### Scaling Challenges

| Claim | Rating | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Image schemas handle spatial relations and physical actions well | CONFIRMED | Uncontroversial — this is their primary domain. |
| Image schemas handle emotions, goals, knowledge through metaphor | CONFIRMED | This is the core claim of CMT, well-documented. |
| Image schemas struggle with logical operations (NOT, IF, MAYBE) | CONFIRMED (as analysis) | This is a widely acknowledged limitation. Some researchers have proposed metaphorical groundings (NEGATION as BLOCKAGE, CONDITIONALITY as BRANCHING) but these are speculative and less well-established than core metaphors. |
| Cultural/institutional concepts resist schema grounding | CONFIRMED (as analysis) | "Democracy," "copyright," "mortgage" genuinely depend on social convention rather than embodied experience. |

**Section Confidence: 9/10**

## Corrections Required

1. **DISL publication date**: Clarify the dating. The most accurate formulation would be: "Published online in *Spatial Cognition and Computation* in 2024, appearing in Volume 25, Issue 2 (2025), pages 138-175." The document currently says "published 2024, volume 25, 2025 issue" which is essentially correct but could be clearer.

2. **DISL preliminary work**: The document could note that a preliminary version ("Visualising Image Schemas: A Preliminary Look at DISL") was published in 2022 as a ResearchGate working paper, establishing the timeline more clearly.
