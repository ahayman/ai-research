# Proloquo (AssistiveWare) - Independent Claim Validation

**Validation Date:** 2026-03-05
**Methodology:** Adversarial web search verification. Each claim was independently researched using multiple search queries across AssistiveWare official sources, Apple App Store listings, academic databases, and third-party reviews. Claims were only marked CONFIRMED if strong, independent evidence was found.

---

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### 1. Proloquo uses a subscription model: $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare's official Proloquo product page confirms: Monthly $9.99 USD, Yearly $99.99 USD. Also lists regional pricing (EU, AU, UK, CA). Both plans include a 1-month free trial. Proloquo2Go users get a discount.

Sources:
- [Proloquo and Proloquo Coach - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/products/proloquo)
- [Proloquo Licenses - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/products/proloquo-licenses)

### 2. Released ~2023, it is AssistiveWare's next-generation symbol-based AAC app
**INVALID (partially)**

Proloquo was released in **2022**, not 2023. The Apple UK Newsroom article from May 2023 describes it as already existing, and multiple sources confirm "In 2022, AssistiveWare released Proloquo." The App Store metadata shows a date around March 2022. It IS correctly described as the next-generation symbol-based AAC app.

Sources:
- [AssistiveWare ushers in next generation of AAC technology - Apple (UK)](https://www.apple.com/uk/newsroom/2023/05/assistiveware-ushers-in-next-generation-of-aac-technology/)
- [Comparing Proloquo and Proloquo2Go - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/proloquo-or-proloquo2go)
- [Introducing Proloquo and Proloquo Coach - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/introducing-proloquo-proloquo-coach)

### 3. Proloquo coexists with (not replaces) Proloquo2Go
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare explicitly states: "There are no plans to discontinue Proloquo2Go." Both apps remain available on the App Store. They can coexist on the same device, and AssistiveWare offers a discount on Proloquo for existing P2G users.

Sources:
- [Comparing Proloquo and Proloquo2Go - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/proloquo-or-proloquo2go)

### 4. Uses a fixed 60-button grid with Crescendo Evolution vocabulary containing 16,000+ words
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare's efficiency blog confirms: "48 buttons with symbols and 12 text-only buttons" = 60 total. The vocabulary contains "16,500 unique words" across symbol buttons (4,400), text-only buttons (400), Related Words, and phrases. The system is called Crescendo Evolution.

Sources:

### 12. ExpressivePower for emotive expression
**UNCERTAIN (4/10)**

ExpressivePower is a well-documented feature, BUT all references found link it to **Proloquo2Go**, not the newer Proloquo app. The Proloquo product page does not mention ExpressivePower. The feature allows users to say words with inflection, emotions, and sounds. It may not be available in the newer Proloquo app, which relies instead on Acapela Neural Voices for more natural intonation.

### 13. My-Own-Voice cloning costs $99/mo or $999 one-time
**INVALID**

My-Own-Voice is a product from **Acapela Group** (not AssistiveWare). Its pricing is: **$99/year** (or EUR 99/year) annual subscription OR **$999** (or EUR 999) one-time license. The claim says "$99/mo" (per month) which is WRONG -- it is $99 per YEAR. Additionally, My-Own-Voice is confirmed compatible with Proloquo2Go and Proloquo4Text, but I found no evidence it is supported in the newer Proloquo app specifically.

Sources:

### 26. PubMed 33756090: aphasia navigation (about P2G)
**CONFIRMED**

The study exists: "AAC apps for aphasia: a pilot study on the role of intuition and learning" (PMID: 33756090). Published in Disability and Rehabilitation: Assistive Technology, 2021. Nine individuals with aphasia searched for symbols on Proloquo2Go and Speak for Yourself. Participants performed significantly better using Proloquo2Go for average search time (p < .001) and rate of cell selection (p < .001).

Sources:
- [PubMed 33756090](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33756090/)
- [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17483107.2021.1900932)

### 30. Uses Crescendo vocabulary system (not Minspeak), avoiding PRC-Saltillo patents
**CONFIRMED**

Proloquo uses the Crescendo Evolution vocabulary system, which is AssistiveWare's own creation. Minspeak is a patented system owned by PRC-Saltillo (via Semantic Compaction Systems). Crescendo uses a fundamentally different approach (semantic clustering, static core rows, Related Words) versus Minspeak's multi-meaning icon sequences. The two systems are architecturally distinct.

Sources:
- [Crescendo - AssistiveWare](https://www.assistiveware.com/crescendo)
- [Semantic Compaction Systems And PRC-Saltillo](https://prc-saltillo.com/articles/semantic-compaction-systems-and-prc-saltillo?mode=view)
- [Patents, Trademarks, and Copyrights | Minspeak Academy](https://minspeak.com/patents-trademarks-and-copyrights/)
