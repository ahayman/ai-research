# Research Notes: The Peptide Therapy Landscape (the category pineal peptides sit inside)

## Executive Summary

"Peptides" is not one thing. There are **two almost entirely separate worlds** that share a chemistry word, and the wellness industry profits from blurring them.

**World 1 — legitimate peptide pharmaceuticals.** Peptides are short chains of amino acids (conventionally <~50; longer chains are "proteins"). This is a large, rigorous, evidence-based field. As of March 2024 the FDA had approved roughly **80–119 peptide drugs** (counts vary by definition), including insulin (1922), the GLP-1 agonists semaglutide and tirzepatide, GnRH analogs (leuprolide), desmopressin, octreotide, calcitonin, and tesamorelin [S1][S2][S3]. These went through full clinical trials, are manufactured under pharmaceutical quality control, and have defined indications. The global peptide-therapeutics market was ~$42.5B in 2023 [S2]. None of this is in dispute.

**World 2 — the "wellness/biohacking peptide" gray market.** A parallel ecosystem sells BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu, sermorelin/CJC-1295/ipamorelin, MK-677, Melanotan II, Selank/Semax, and the "Khavinson bioregulator" family (Epitalon and the other **pineal peptides** that prompted the user's question) — almost always as injectables or nasal sprays, almost always labeled **"research use only / not for human consumption,"** and almost always with marketing claims that vastly exceed the human evidence. For most of these, the entire clinical literature is **animal and in-vitro work plus anecdote**; several have *zero* completed published randomized human trials [S6][S8][S22].

**The regulatory picture is genuinely in flux right now (2026) and the prompt's framing is partly out of date.** The 2023 FDA action did happen: on **29 September 2023** the FDA placed **19 peptides into "Category 2"** of its interim 503A bulk-substances list (significant-safety-risk; effectively un-compoundable), citing immunogenicity, impurities, and absent human safety data [S5][S9]. **But that action has since been partially reversed.** In **February 2026** HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. announced ~14 of the 19 would move back toward Category 1, and on **15 April 2026** the FDA formally removed **12 peptides** (including BPC-157, GHK-Cu injectable, Melanotan II, TB-500, Epitalon, Semax) from Category 2, effective ~22 April 2026 [S10][S11][S12]. Crucially, **removal from Category 2 is NOT FDA approval** and does NOT by itself make these legal to compound — they remain unapproved investigational drugs pending Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) review (next meeting 23–24 July 2026) [S11][S12]. ProPublica and former FDA officials (incl. former acting commissioner Janet Woodcock) have publicly criticized the reversal as a safety risk, noting **no significant new clinical evidence has emerged since 2023** to justify it [S13].

**Bottom line for a skeptic:** Of the entire "wellness peptide" menu, the ones with real, replicated, Western-standard human RCT evidence are essentially the ones that *crossed over into being actual approved drugs* (tesamorelin for HIV lipodystrophy; afamelanotide/Melanotan-1 as Scenesse for EPP; thymosin alpha-1 approved abroad as Zadaxin). The famous "biohacker" peptides — BPC-157, TB-500, Epitalon and the pineal family — are sold on the strength of rodent data and testimonials, with safety data that is, bluntly, mostly absent, and a documented record of contaminated, mis-dosed gray-market product [S6][S22][S23][S31].

## What Peptides Are (legitimate pharma vs the wellness gray market)

**Definition.** A peptide is a short polymer of amino acids joined by peptide bonds. The boundary with "protein" is fuzzy but conventionally peptides are <~50 amino acids. Insulin (51 aa) sits right at the boundary and is usually counted as the founding therapeutic peptide [S1][S3].

**The legitimate FDA-approved peptide-drug class is large and real.** Representative approved peptide therapeutics, all of which went through standard drug review:

- **Insulin** — first therapeutic peptide, approved 1922; cornerstone of diabetes care [S1]. `[FDA-APPROVED for diabetes]`
- **GLP-1 / dual agonists — semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy/Rybelsus), tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound), liraglutide, dulaglutide, exenatide** — type 2 diabetes, chronic weight management, CV risk reduction; semaglutide added a CKD risk-reduction indication in 2024. The single most commercially successful peptide class [S1][S2]. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **GnRH analogs — leuprolide (Lupron), goserelin** — prostate cancer, endometriosis, central precocious puberty [S1]. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **Vasopressin analogs — desmopressin (DDAVP)** — diabetes insipidus, nocturnal enuresis, some bleeding disorders. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **Octreotide (Sandostatin)** — somatostatin analog; acromegaly, neuroendocrine tumors. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **Calcitonin (salmon)** — osteoporosis, hypercalcemia. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **Pramlintide (Symlin)** — amylin analog, adjunct to insulin [S1]. `[FDA-APPROVED]`
- **Tesamorelin (Egrifta SV / Egrifta WR)** — the only FDA-approved GHRH analog for adults; reduces visceral adipose tissue in HIV-associated lipodystrophy; approved on two Phase 3 double-blind placebo-controlled trials (n≈816); newer weekly-reconstitution F8 formulation approved 2024–2025 [S2][S15][S16]. `[FDA-APPROVED for HIV lipodystrophy]` — *this matters because it is one of the GH-secretagogue family that crossed the evidence bar.*
- **Afamelanotide (Scenesse)** — i.e. pharmaceutical Melanotan-1; FDA-approved Oct 2019 as a 16 mg implant to prevent phototoxicity in erythropoietic protoporphyria (EPP); 10+ yr safety data, 1,000+ patients [S20]. `[FDA-APPROVED for EPP]`

Scale: ~80–119 approved peptide drugs depending on source/definition; injectables dominate (~78% of approved delivery methods) [S2][S3].

**Why "it's a peptide" tells you nothing about safety or efficacy.** Insulin and Epitalon are both peptides in exactly the way that water and hydrofluoric acid are both liquids. The category is defined by chemistry, not by evidence. Marketing that leans on "peptides are natural signaling molecules your body already makes" is technically true and epistemically empty.

## The Popular Wellness Peptides (claim vs evidence)

### BPC-157 ("Body Protection Compound") — claimed: gut/tendon/ligament healing, "wolverine peptide"
- **Claim:** Accelerates healing of tendons, ligaments, muscle, gut lining; treats leaky gut, injuries, IBD.
- **Evidence:** A 2025 systematic review (Vasireddi et al., *HSS Journal*, DOI 10.1177/15563316251355551; PMC12313605) screened **544 articles, included 36 studies — 35 preclinical (animal/cell), and exactly ONE human study**, a small uncontrolled retrospective case series of intra-articular injection for chronic knee pain [S6][S7]. There is **no published, peer-reviewed, randomized, controlled human trial** demonstrating efficacy or safety for any indication; a Phase 2 placebo-controlled hamstring-strain RCT is registered (NCT07437547) with no results yet [S6]. Animal data are genuinely consistent and mechanistically interesting (angiogenesis, growth-factor upregulation) — but that is rodent data.
- **Tags:** `[ANIMAL/IN VITRO ONLY]` for humans · `[SAFETY UNKNOWN]` (no human safety data) · `[BANNED IN SPORT]` (WADA/USADA non-approved substance) [S6][S30] · DoD/OPSS: *"an unapproved drug … little to no reliable scientific evidence to support the safety or effectiveness of BPC-157 in humans"* [S30].

### TB-500 / Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4) — claimed: tissue repair, recovery, flexibility
- **Claim:** Healing/recovery accelerant; often stacked with BPC-157.
- **Evidence:** "TB-500" is a synthetic actin-binding fragment (LKKTETQ) of full thymosin beta-4. **No completed/published Phase 2–3 human trials of injectable TB-500 for musculoskeletal use exist.** Full-length Tβ4 *has* entered human trials (ophthalmic RGN-259 program; some cardiac work) but **none has yielded FDA approval**, and those results cannot be extrapolated to the marketed fragment [S8].
- **Tags:** `[ANIMAL/IN VITRO ONLY]` (for TB-500 itself) · `[HUMAN RCT — limited]` (only for parent Tβ4, no approvals) · `[SAFETY UNKNOWN]` · `[BANNED IN SPORT]` — on WADA S2 since 2011; ~30–45 day detection window [S8].

### Thymosin alpha-1 (Tα1, Zadaxin / thymalfasin) — claimed: immune modulation
- **Claim:** Immune support; infections, sepsis, cancer adjuvant, "long COVID."
- **Evidence:** This is the **strongest-evidenced** of the "thymic"/wellness peptides because it is a *real approved drug abroad*. Approved as **Zadaxin in 35+ countries** for chronic hepatitis B (and HCV w/ interferon); controlled trials 1995–2010 show HBeAg seroconversion ~25–40%, comparable to interferon with better tolerability; safety evaluated in 11,000+ patients across 30+ trials [S4][S21]. A large sepsis RCT (TESTS, reported ~2025) is the strongest controlled evidence to date [S4]. **Not FDA-approved in the US** (commercial/orphan-economics reasons, not a safety signal) — held 503A Category 2 status until the 2026 reclassification [S4][S21].
- **Tags:** `[FDA-APPROVED for X]` = NO (US) but **approved abroad for hepatitis B** · `[HUMAN RCT — limited→moderate]` (hepatitis B has real RCTs; many other marketed uses do not).

### GHK-Cu (copper tripeptide Glycyl-Histidyl-Lysine-Cu) — claimed: skin, hair, wound, anti-aging
- **Claim:** Collagen/elastin stimulation, wrinkle/scar reduction, wound healing, hair growth, systemic "anti-aging."
- **Evidence — split this carefully:** As a **topical cosmetic**, GHK-Cu has the most plausible support of the group: a naturally occurring plasma peptide (discovered 1973), with small human topical studies (e.g. ~70% of volunteers showed collagen increase vs vitamin C / retinoic acid comparators) plus some placebo-controlled topical serum work [S17]. Wound-healing/regeneration data are strong **in animal models** [S17]. BUT the systemic/injected "longevity" claims that drive the *peptide* (as opposed to skincare) market are **not supported by human trials** — most human data is dermatological and topical, not injected anti-aging. Note many sources here are cosmetics-industry marketing.
- **Tags:** `[HUMAN RCT — limited]` (topical skin, small studies) · `[ANIMAL/IN VITRO ONLY]` (systemic/anti-aging) · injectable GHK-Cu was on the FDA Category 2 list [S5][S11].

### Growth-hormone secretagogues (sermorelin, CJC-1295, ipamorelin, tesamorelin, MK-677)
- **Claim:** Boost endogenous growth hormone / IGF-1 → fat loss, muscle, recovery, "anti-aging," sleep.
- **Sermorelin** — GHRH analog; **was FDA-approved (pediatric GH deficiency)** historically; adult anti-aging use is off-label/compounded [S14]. `[FDA-APPROVED for pediatric GHD; off-label otherwise]`
- **CJC-1295 / ipamorelin** — **not FDA-approved**; almost always sold as a stack. Human data is thin: the most-cited is a single 2006 *JCEM* pharmacokinetic study showing CJC-1295 raised GH/IGF-1 dose-dependently in healthy adults — i.e. it *moves the biomarker*, not that it delivers clinical outcomes safely long-term [S14]. `[HUMAN RCT — very limited]` · `[BANNED IN SPORT]` (ipamorelin = GH secretagogue, WADA S2) [S19].
- **Tesamorelin** — the family member that **did** the trials → **FDA-approved for HIV lipodystrophy** (see above) [S15][S16]. `[FDA-APPROVED for HIV lipodystrophy]` · still WADA-prohibited.
- **MK-677 / ibutamoren** — orally active ghrelin-mimetic GH secretagogue. **Not approved for human use**, not a legal supplement ingredient; raised GH/IGF-1 in trials but **did not meaningfully improve functional outcomes** in older adults and **did not reduce visceral fat** [S18]. `[HUMAN RCT — limited, mostly negative/biomarker-only]` · `[BANNED IN SPORT]` (WADA S2) [S18][S19].

### Melanotan I vs Melanotan II — claimed: tanning (± libido for MT-II)
- **Melanotan-1 (afamelanotide / Scenesse)** — MC1R-selective; the *approved* one (FDA Oct 2019, EPP) [S20]. `[FDA-APPROVED for EPP]`
- **Melanotan-II** — non-selective melanocortin agonist (MC1R/3R/4R/5R); the gray-market "tan jab." **Never approved anywhere**; FDA has issued warning letters against tanning injections [S24]. Documented harms: nausea/vomiting, priapism, rhabdomyolysis, exogenous hypercortisolism, acute renal failure; **≥4 published case reports of melanoma temporally associated** with use (causation unproven); **no long-term safety studies** [S24]. Australia's TGA scheduled it alongside MDMA/heroin (possession without a research licence is criminal as of 2026) [S24].
- **Tags (MT-II):** `[ANECDOTE/MARKETING ONLY]` for benefit · `[SAFETY UNKNOWN→known-harmful]` · regulator-warned worldwide.

### Selank & Semax (Russian "nootropic" peptides — direct cousins of the Khavinson/pineal world)
- **Claim:** Anxiolysis (Selank), cognition/neuroprotection/stroke recovery (Semax).
- **Evidence:** Both are **approved medicines in Russia** (Selank registered 2009; Semax a fragment of ACTH(4-10)) and have a *better* evidence tier than most of this list — Selank completed Russian Phase III for anxiety; Semax has Russian controlled-trial data in stroke recovery and BDNF effects [S22]. **But** the literature is overwhelmingly Russian-language, small-n, single-country, and **not replicated in large Western trials**; neither is FDA/EMA-approved [S22]. Both were on the FDA Category 2 list (Semax removed April 2026) [S5][S11].
- **Tags:** `[HUMAN RCT — limited, Russia-only, unreplicated]` · not Western-approved. *These are the structural and methodological "cousins" of the pineal peptides: same research lineage (Russian institutional gerontology), same replication problem.*

### Pineal peptides (Epitalon / Epithalamin and the Khavinson "bioregulator" family) — the user's actual question, in context
- **Claim:** A 4-amino-acid pineal peptide (Epitalon = Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly, derived from the pineal extract epithalamin) that switches on telomerase, lengthens telomeres, restores melatonin rhythms, and extends lifespan / "reverses aging."
- **Evidence:** This is the **most ambitious claim with one of the weakest independent evidence bases.** Cell-culture work shows telomerase upregulation / telomere effects in human fibroblast lines [S25]; rodent studies report lifespan effects; Khavinson's elderly-cohort studies report reduced mortality. **But almost the entire human/animal body of work comes from one group — the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology under Vladimir Khavinson — and has not been replicated in large independent Western RCTs** [S25][S26]. The mechanism (telomerase/circadian) is biologically plausible; the leap to "lifespan extension / age reversal in healthy adults" is **unproven**. Epitalon was on the FDA Category 2 list and was among the 12 removed in April 2026 — still an unapproved investigational compound [S11][S26].
- **Tags:** `[ANIMAL/IN VITRO ONLY]` for the headline telomerase/longevity claims · `[HUMAN RCT — single-group, unreplicated]` · `[SAFETY UNKNOWN]` (no Western safety dataset) · `[ANECDOTE/MARKETING ONLY]` for "age reversal."

## Regulatory Status (current and precise)

### FDA compounding categories (the mechanism the fight is over)
Compounding pharmacies operate under FD&C Act **§503A** (patient-specific) and **§503B** (outsourcing facilities). To compound from a bulk substance lacking a USP monograph or FDA approval, the substance must be on (or under review for) the **interim 503A bulks list**. The FDA sorts nominated substances into **Category 1** (eligible for compounding under enforcement discretion while reviewed) and **Category 2** (significant safety risk — effectively *do not compound*) [S9][S11].

### The 2023 action (real, and the prompt is correct here)
On **29 September 2023**, the FDA moved **19 peptides into Category 2**, citing **immunogenicity, manufacturing impurities, and lack of large-scale human safety data** [S5][S9]. The 19: **BPC-157, TB-500, CJC-1295, ipamorelin, AOD-9604, thymosin alpha-1, GHK-Cu, Semax, Selank, KPV, MOTS-c, Epitalon, LL-37, Melanotan II, kisspeptin-10, GHRP-2, GHRP-6, PEG-MGF, DSIP** [S5]. Effect: licensed compounders had to stop; demand shifted to the unregulated gray market [S5].

### The 2026 partial reversal (this UPDATES the prompt — get this right)
- **27 Feb 2026** — HHS Secretary **RFK Jr. announced** ~14 of the 19 would move back toward Category 1; he has publicly called the 2023 action "illegal" / "no safety signal," and describes himself as a peptide user/"big fan" [S10][S13].
- **15 April 2026** — FDA formally **removed 12 peptides from Category 2**, effective ~22 April 2026 (Federal Register 2026-07361): **BPC-157, LL-37 (cathelicidin), Dihexa acetate, DSIP (emideltide), Epitalon, GHK-Cu (injectable), KPV, PEG-MGF, Melanotan II, MOTS-c, Semax (heptapeptide), TB-500 (Tβ4 fragment LKKTETQ)** [S11][S12].
- **CRITICAL caveat:** Per the FDA notice and legal analyses, *"Removal from Category 2 does not render these bulk drug substances eligible for compounding under section 503A."* They are **not** approved, and **not** automatically added to the bulks list — they move to an evaluative limbo pending **PCAC review (meeting scheduled 23–24 July 2026)**, which will weigh whether BPC-157 etc. belong on the 503A bulks list [S11][S12].
- **Criticism / counterweight:** **ProPublica** (and former FDA officials) report the original concerns were science-based — immune reactions up to anaphylaxis, bacterial/heavy-metal contamination, temperature sensitivity, and **adverse events including deaths in human data for six of the peptides** (causation not proven) — and that **no significant new clinical research has emerged since 2023** to justify reversal. Former acting commissioner **Janet Woodcock**: reversing it *"would be a disruption of the societal pact we have had since 1962 that drugs will be studied to see if they work before marketed in the U.S."* [S13].

### The "research chemical / not for human consumption" loophole
Sellers label vials **"research use only — not for human consumption"** to dodge the FD&C Act. **The FDA does not recognize a 'research chemical' exemption that permits human use of unapproved drugs.** Under the FD&C Act, anything *intended* to diagnose/treat/prevent disease is a drug requiring approval; the disclaimer is described by regulatory attorneys as "transparent and legally ineffective." FDA and DOJ have pursued sellers; independent testing of such vials has repeatedly found wrong dosing, contamination, and even the wrong compound [S27][S31].

### Anti-doping (WADA / USADA)
- WADA's **2026 Prohibited List** (in force 1 Jan 2026; approved by ExCo 11 Sep 2025) bans the relevant peptides under **S0 (non-approved substances), S2 (peptide hormones, growth factors, mimetics), and S4** [S19].
- **S2** explicitly covers GH secretagogues/GHRPs: ibutamoren (MK-677), ipamorelin, anamorelin, macimorelin, GHRP-1/2/3/4/5/6, hexarelin, ghrelin, etc. — **banned at all times, in- and out-of-competition, "non-specified" (strictest penalties)** [S19].
- **TB-500/Tβ4** on S2 since 2011 [S8]. **BPC-157** = WADA/USADA non-approved substance, prohibited at all times [S30].

### Legality of buying/possessing (US/UK)
- **US:** These are **unapproved drugs**; cannot be legally prescribed or sold as drugs/supplements. Sellers operate in a legally precarious "research chemical" gray market and are the usual enforcement targets; **individual end-users rarely face direct FDA action**, but there is no lawful human-use channel [S27][S28].
- **UK:** Possession of e.g. BPC-157 is **not itself a crime** (not scheduled under the Misuse of Drugs Act, not a "psychoactive substance"), **but** it has **no MHRA approval for human use**; selling for human consumption without MHRA authorization is illegal, and MHRA advises against importing unapproved substances for self-medication (customs seizure risk) [S28].

## Safety & Unknowns

- **The core problem is absence of data, not reassurance.** For BPC-157, TB-500, Epitalon and the pineal family, there is **no meaningful human safety dataset** — no long-term toxicology, no dose-finding, no immunogenicity surveillance in humans [S6][S8][S26][S30].
- **Immunogenicity** is the FDA's central named concern: synthetic peptides + impurities can provoke immune responses ranging from asymptomatic to **anaphylaxis** [S13][S5].
- **Gray-market product quality is documented to be poor.** In the closest hard analogue — **compounded/online semaglutide** — FDA-reported testing found peptide-related **impurities up to 33%**, strength as much as ~19% below label, and **trace metals** (boron, aluminum, chromium, nickel, etc.) promoting high-molecular-weight protein aggregates; a 2023 FDA HCP letter logged **352 adverse-event reports, 268 serious, 84 hospitalizations, 5 deaths** for compounded semaglutide [S29][S31]. An independent multifactor analysis of online semaglutide found measured purity as low as **7.7–14.37%** vs the "99%" claimed [S23]. The same quality failures (m-cresol contamination, endotoxin, mis-ID) are documented across gray-market research-peptide vials [S23][S31].
- **"99% purity" claims are misleading:** they typically come from HPLC measuring target peptide vs other *peptides* — saying nothing about residual solvents, heavy metals, or bacterial endotoxin, which matter most for injectables [S23].
- **Melanotan II** is the clearest example of *known* harm: priapism, rhabdomyolysis, renal failure, hypercortisolism, and melanoma case reports [S24].
- **Specific to this user (CFS, autism, skepticism):** none of the "fatigue/energy/longevity" peptide claims (Epitalon, MOTS-c, BPC-157 for "gut") rest on human RCTs in any fatigue or chronic-illness population; marketing toward ME/CFS-adjacent symptoms is **not evidence-based**, and injectable gray-market product carries real contamination/immunogenicity risk.

## Research Gaps

- **CAPTCHA/403-blocked primaries:** The thymosin-α-1 review (PMC7747025) and the BPC-157 *HSS Journal* systematic review (SagePub) returned CAPTCHA/403 on direct fetch; their key figures (n>11,000 safety; 544 screened / 36 included / 1 human) came from indexed summaries and should be confirmed against the PMC full text by the validator.
- **Exact FDA primary text for the April 2026 removal:** I relied on law-firm and trade summaries citing Federal Register 2026-07361; the validator should pull the FDA/Federal Register notice itself to confirm the precise 12-peptide list and the "does not authorize compounding" language verbatim.
- **The 7.7–14.37% online-semaglutide purity figure** (JMIR 2024 e65440) is striking and load-bearing — confirm against the published full text (PDF would not parse via fetch).
- **Pineal-peptide human trials:** I did not find any large, independent, Western, placebo-controlled RCT of Epitalon/epithalamin for any longevity, fatigue, or sleep endpoint. If the downstream pineal-peptide facet needs primary human data, that gap is the headline: it essentially does not exist outside the Khavinson group.
- **Count of FDA-approved peptides varies (≈80 vs ~119)** depending on whether diagnostics, insulins/analogs, and combination products are counted — present as a range, not a single number.
- **Vendor-blog sourcing:** GHK-Cu, sermorelin-stack, Selank/Semax, and Khavinson sections drew partly on vendor/clinic pages; these are flagged as marketing and should be down-weighted vs the PMC/FDA/WADA primaries where they conflict.
