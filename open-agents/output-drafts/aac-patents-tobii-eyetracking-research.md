## Executive Summary

Tobii AB holds one of the world's largest eye tracking patent portfolios, comprising **over 1,000 patents and patent applications worldwide**, including **over 200 granted US patents**. As of 2024, the company has approximately **887 total patents globally** with **488 granted** and a **53% grant share**. More than **77% of granted patents remain active**.

The patent portfolio covers virtually every aspect of eye tracking technology:
- **Hardware design** (illumination, sensors, optics, device manufacturing)
- **Software algorithms** (gaze estimation, calibration, glint detection, pupil tracking)
- **User experience** (gaze interaction, contextual actions, scrolling, dwell selection)
- **Integration** (screen-based, wearable, AR/VR, automotive)
- **AI/ML methods** (deep learning for gaze prediction, synthetic data generation)

**Key risk areas for AAC market entrants:**
1. Tobii's foundational patents on PCCR (Pupil Centre Corneal Reflection) eye tracking methods
2. Patents on adaptive illumination systems for eye tracking
3. Gaze-based UI interaction methods (dwell, scrolling, contextual actions)
4. Wearable and screen-based eye tracking device designs
5. Calibration methods for eye tracking systems
6. Tobii actively litigates patent infringement (successful lawsuit against Pupil Labs in 2025)

## Tobii Corporate & IP Structure

### Entity Overview

Tobii's intellectual property is held primarily under three entities:

| Entity | Role | Headquarters |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Tobii AB** | Current parent company; active patent assignee | Stockholm, Sweden |
| **Tobii Technology AB** | Former/legacy entity name; many older patents assigned here | Stockholm, Sweden |
| **DynaVox Systems, LLC** | Acquired by Tobii in May 2014 (from Chapter 11 bankruptcy); renamed Tobii Dynavox LLC | Pittsburgh, PA, USA |

### Company History

- **2001:** Tobii Technology AB founded; began developing eye tracking technology
- **1983-2014:** DynaVox Systems (founded as Sentient Systems Technology, Inc.) operated independently as AAC device maker
- **May 2014:** Tobii Technology acquired DynaVox from Chapter 11 bankruptcy; merged into Tobii Dynavox LLC
- **2019:** Tobii Dynavox separated from Tobii AB as independent company (Dynavox Group)
- **Present:** Tobii AB (eye tracking technology/licensing) and Tobii Dynavox (AAC products) operate as related but separate entities

### Sole Source Designation

Tobii Dynavox LLC is formally designated as the **sole source** of Tobii Dynavox products "due to the existence of patent and copyright." This sole source letter is used in healthcare funding procurement processes.

## Patent Litigation History

### Tobii AB v. Pupil Labs GmbH (Germany, 2025)

- **Court:** Munich Regional Court I
- **Case Number:** 7 O 15033/24
- **Ruling Date:** October 9, 2025
- **Patent at Issue:** EP 3,332,285 B1 (European Patent)
- **Outcome:** PARTIAL WIN for Tobii
- **Details:** Pupil Labs prohibited from offering/delivering "Neon" and "Pupil Invisible" eye-tracking systems and "Marker Mapper" software from Germany to German customers without stating these must not be used without Tobii permission. Pupil Labs must pay damages and render accounts to Tobii. Ruling subject to appeal.
- **Significance:** Demonstrates Tobii's willingness to litigate against eye tracking competitors and ability to win in court.

### Tobii Technology, Inc. v. Weinblatt (USA, 2020)

- **Context:** In April 2020, Weinblatt sent a letter to Tobii claiming that Tobii products (Pro Lab, Pro Spectrum, Pro Fusion, etc.) infringed his patents
- **Response:** Tobii filed a declaratory judgment action seeking noninfringement ruling
- **Significance:** Tobii is willing to proactively litigate to protect its freedom to operate

### University of Virginia Patent Foundation v. DynaVox Systems, LLC (2015)

- **Court:** U.S. District Court, Western District of Virginia
- **Case Number:** 3:2015cv00015
- **Filed:** March 27, 2015
- **Claim:** Breach of contract - failure to pay royalties for "ERICA" eye gaze system patents
- **Details:** DynaVox held exclusive license to UVA-developed eye-gaze computer apparatus patents. DynaVox's predecessor paid royalties 2005-2009. Lawsuit followed unsuccessful arbitration.
- **Significance:** Shows that DynaVox/Tobii Dynavox's eye tracking AAC technology has roots in university research with licensed IP obligations.

## IP Licensing Program & Strategy

### Portfolio Scale
- **1,000+ patents and patent applications worldwide**
- **200+ granted US patents**
- **887 total patents globally** (as of latest data)
- **488 granted** with **53% grant share**
- **77%+ of granted patents remain active**
- **USA is primary filing jurisdiction**, followed by China and European countries

### Technology Coverage Areas
Tobii's IP licensing program covers:

1. **Image Sensor Technology** - Design, control, and readout of data from image sensors for improved robustness, cost, and power-efficiency
2. **Screen-Based & Wearable Eye Tracking** - Integration, calibration, and system design for accurate, low-cost, robust solutions
3. **Illumination Methods** - Types, timing, direction, and frequency of illumination to maximize tracking performance while minimizing power consumption and cost
4. **Algorithms & Methods** - Robust, consistently accurate, and cost-efficient eye tracking computation
5. **User Experience Concepts** - Interaction paradigms, UI patterns, gaze-based controls

### Licensing Terms
- **Contact:** iplicensing@tobii.com
- **Terms:** Custom negotiated; no public standardized rates
- **End users** do not receive patent licenses unless separately agreed
- **SDK/Development licenses** available for non-commercial development; additional agreement required for commercial use
- **Restrictive EULA:** Tobii's end-user license explicitly limits how eye tracking data can be used

### Licensing Strategy Implications
Tobii explicitly positions its patent portfolio as a licensing asset. Any company building eye tracking technology (especially for AAC) should:
1. Conduct a freedom-to-operate analysis against Tobii's portfolio
2. Expect to need a patent license if using standard eye tracking approaches
3. Consider alternative technical approaches not covered by Tobii's patents
4. Budget for potential licensing costs or design-around engineering

### Strategic Recommendations for AAC Market Entry

1. **Camera-based eye tracking (no IR):** Apple's approach using front-facing camera + AI may circumvent many Tobii IR-based patents. Consider machine learning approaches to eye tracking that don't rely on IR illumination and corneal reflection.

2. **License from Tobii:** The most straightforward path if building IR-based eye tracking. Budget for this in product costs.

3. **Partner with existing eye tracker OEMs:** Use IRISBOND Hiru, Tobii PCEye, or other licensed eye trackers rather than building custom hardware. This shifts patent risk to the hardware partner.

4. **Focus on software differentiation:** Build AAC software that works with multiple eye trackers. Most AAC software patents (like Smartbox's Chat History) are narrower than hardware patents.

5. **Alternative input methods:** Consider head tracking, facial expression recognition, EMG-based input (like Control Bionics' NeuroNode), or switch scanning as primary or complementary access methods.

6. **Monitor Apple's built-in eye tracking:** iOS 18+ eye tracking could commoditize the eye tracking hardware market for AAC, potentially reducing dependence on Tobii hardware.

7. **Watch patent expiration dates:** Some foundational Tobii patents (e.g., US8025405 expires July 2028) are approaching expiration. Timing market entry accordingly could reduce risk.
