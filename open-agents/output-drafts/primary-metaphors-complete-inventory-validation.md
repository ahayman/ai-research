# Research Validation Report: Primary Metaphors Complete Inventory

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: Moderate

- **Confirmed**: 14 claims (64%)
- **Uncertain**: 6 claims (27%)
- **Invalid**: 2 claims (9%)

**Key Findings**:
The core inventory of primary metaphors is well-grounded in the literature. Grady's 1997 dissertation and Lakoff & Johnson's 1999 table are real and accurately characterized. The Lakoff & Narayanan (2025) book is a real publication. However, several specific claims have issues: the Forceville reference year is wrong (2013, not 2014), the Grady (1997b) publication venue is misidentified, some "canonical 24" entries may not actually appear individually labeled in the 1999 table, the THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS decomposition uses different labels than Grady's originals, several "extended" metaphors lack independently verifiable published sources, and the LOVE IS A JOURNEY compositional decomposition appears to be an original synthesis rather than a direct quote from any source.

**Systemic Issues**:
- The exact count of entries in the Lakoff & Johnson (1999) table is difficult to verify independently without the physical book. The research document's claim of 24 entries is plausible but cannot be confirmed with certainty; various secondary sources cite "about 23" or "approximately 24."
- Some "canonical" entries may be listed in the L&J 1999 table only as sub-entries or in running text rather than as individually labeled rows, making the claim that all 24 are individually enumerated in the table uncertain.
- Several of the "extended" and "debated" metaphors lack specific published attestation beyond the broad citation "Grady (1997)"; without access to the dissertation's full appendix, their inclusion cannot be independently verified at the level of individual entries.
- One reference date is wrong (Forceville 2014 should be 2013), and one publication venue is misidentified (Grady 1997b venue).

### Claim 9: "The THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS decomposition uses three primary metaphors: ORGANIZATION IS PHYSICAL STRUCTURE, PERSISTING IS REMAINING ERECT / VIABILITY IS ERECTNESS, and CAPACITY TO ENDURE IS STURDINESS"
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (5/10)
- **Original context**: Entry #11 note, Entry #26 note, Entry #32
- **Evidence for**: Grady (1997) does decompose THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS into more basic metaphors. The two core components confirmed across multiple sources are ORGANIZATION IS PHYSICAL STRUCTURE and PERSISTING IS REMAINING ERECT.
- **Evidence against**: Multiple independent sources consistently describe the decomposition as involving TWO primary metaphors (ORGANIZATION IS PHYSICAL STRUCTURE + PERSISTING IS REMAINING ERECT), not three. The "Notebooks on Language" blog entry on Grady 1997, the Semantic Scholar abstract, and the De Gruyter journal entry all describe two components. "CAPACITY TO ENDURE IS STURDINESS" may be related conceptual territory but does not appear as a separately named third component in the standard accounts of this decomposition.
- **Why uncertain**: Without access to the full text of Grady's dissertation (which may discuss additional sub-components in more detail than the journal article), it is possible that CAPACITY TO ENDURE IS STURDINESS is discussed as a related primary metaphor. But the standard published account (the Cognitive Linguistics journal article) describes two, not three, component metaphors.
- **What evidence actually supports**: THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS decomposes into two primary metaphors: ORGANIZATION IS PHYSICAL STRUCTURE and PERSISTING IS REMAINING ERECT.
- **Sources**: [Notebooks on Language](http://academiclogbook.blogspot.com/2011/09/joseph-e-grady-theories-are-buildings.html), [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/THEORIES-ARE-BUILDINGS-revisited-Grady/0c5fb6b4a20d2fd3fc1d8abdd7e32ee2b126fd14)

### Claim 10: "LOVE IS A JOURNEY decomposes into exactly 5 primary metaphors: PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS + INTIMACY IS CLOSENESS + DIFFICULTIES ARE BURDENS + STATES ARE LOCATIONS + CHANGE IS MOTION"
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (5/10)
- **Original context**: The "Key properties" section, line 23, lists this exact five-part decomposition
- **Evidence for**: All five of the listed primary metaphors are individually well-attested. The general principle that LOVE IS A JOURNEY is a complex metaphor composed of multiple primary metaphors is well-established in the literature. The Event Structure Metaphor (Lakoff 1993) includes several of these mappings. The MetaNet wiki lists ROMANTIC RELATIONSHIP IS A JOURNEY as inheriting from RELATIONSHIP-IS-ENCLOSURE and other source metaphors.
- **Evidence against**: No single published source was found that lists this exact five-part decomposition. The standard accounts of LOVE IS A JOURNEY emphasize the Event Structure Metaphor mappings (STATES ARE LOCATIONS, CHANGE IS MOTION, CAUSES ARE FORCES, ACTIONS ARE SELF-PROPELLED MOTIONS, PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS) rather than the specific combination listed here. INTIMACY IS CLOSENESS and DIFFICULTIES ARE BURDENS are not typically listed as part of the Event Structure Metaphor. Furthermore, the Event Structure Metaphor also includes ACTIONS ARE SELF-PROPELLED MOTIONS and CAUSES ARE PHYSICAL FORCES, which are absent from this five-part list.
- **Why uncertain**: The five-part decomposition appears to be an original synthesis by the research document's author rather than a direct citation from any published source. It is a reasonable composition, but presenting it as THE decomposition of LOVE IS A JOURNEY is misleading if no source explicitly provides this exact list.
- **What evidence actually supports**: LOVE IS A JOURNEY is composed of multiple primary metaphors drawn from the Event Structure Metaphor system and other primary metaphors, but the exact inventory and count of component metaphors varies by source and analytical purpose.
- **Sources**: [MetaNet: ROMANTIC RELATIONSHIP IS A JOURNEY](https://metaphor.icsi.berkeley.edu/pub/en/index.php/Metaphor:ROMANTIC_RELATIONSHIP_IS_A_JOURNEY), [Lakoff 1993: Contemporary Theory of Metaphor](https://terpconnect.umd.edu/~israel/lakoff-ConTheorMetaphor.pdf)

### Claim 22: "There are potentially 'hundreds' of primary metaphors if one counts every fine-grained experiential correlation"
- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Section "How Many Primary Metaphors Are There?" and "Debate About the Total Count"
- **Evidence for**: Lakoff & Johnson (1999) and Grady both emphasize the open-ended nature of the inventory. The Sketchplanations entry notes that people acquire "hundreds of primary metaphors."
