# Research Notes: TouchChat HD with WordPower - Comprehensive AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

TouchChat HD with WordPower is a full-featured Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) application developed by PRC-Saltillo for iOS devices. The app bundles the WordPower vocabulary system, created by speech-language pathologist Nancy Inman, with TouchChat's robust communication platform. It serves individuals with Autism, Down Syndrome, ALS, apraxia, stroke, and other conditions that affect natural speech.

PRC-Saltillo has a storied history dating back to 1966 when Barry Romich and Ed Prentke founded the Prentke Romich Company with a mission to help individuals with disabilities. Saltillo was created as a sister company in 1996-1998 by Dave Hershberger, who later became CEO and oversaw the merger into PRC-Saltillo in 2019. Today, the company is a worldwide developer of speech-generating devices (SGDs), market-leading apps, and several innovative AAC language systems.

The app is priced at $299.99 bundled with WordPower (or $149.99 without), positions itself as a more affordable alternative to competitors like Proloquo2Go, and offers extensive customization with nearly 40,000 SymbolStix symbols, 45+ button actions, head tracking, switch scanning, and multilingual support in English, Spanish, Canadian French, Arabic, Hebrew, and Mandarin Chinese. A major 2026.1 update introduced AI-powered features including AI Message Edit and AI Image Generation.

## Historical Context

### PRC-Saltillo Company Timeline

- 1966: Barry Romich (engineering student at Case Western Reserve) and Ed Prentke (engineer at Highland View Hospital) form the Prentke Romich Company (PRC) in Cleveland, Ohio.
- 1969: PRC produces its first communication device -- a typing system based on a discarded Teletype machine.
- 1984: Touch Talker becomes the first portable communication device from PRC.
- 1988: PRC enters the digitally recorded speech arena with IntroTalker.
- 1995: PRC launches Unity, the foundational vocabulary system for most PRC products.
- 1996-1998: Saltillo is created as a sister company by Dave Hershberger, then Director of R&D at PRC.
- 2019: Dave Hershberger named CEO; PRC and Saltillo merge to become PRC-Saltillo.
- 2025: Version 2025.2 released with expanded multilingual support.
- 2026: Version 2026.1 introduces AI-powered features.

### WordPower Origin Story

Nancy Inman developed WordPower while working at the Kennedy Krieger Institute. She was evaluating a young man with cerebral palsy who used a head stick to point to words on a low-tech board. She wanted to create a digital system that would give him the same communication capabilities on dynamic display devices, which were new to AAC at the time. The result was WordPower -- a word-based vocabulary featuring high-frequency words, color-coded by category, with keyboard and word prediction support.

## WordPower Vocabulary System

### Design Philosophy

1. Core Word Focus: High-frequency words prominently placed on the main page
2. Color Coding: Words organized by parts of speech (pronouns, verbs, adjectives, etc.)
3. Logical Next Words: Predictive word suggestions reduce keystrokes
4. Word Completion: Extends vocabulary reach with limited cells
5. Consistent Motor Planning: Core words stay in the same position across vocabulary levels
6. Core-Fringe Bridge: Category and activity pages include embedded core vocabulary

### Vocabulary Configurations

- WordPower 20: 20 cells -- motor disabilities, vision impairment, scanning access
- WordPower 25: 25 cells -- transitional users
- WordPower 42: 42 cells -- standard word-based (included with WordPower purchase)
- WordPower 42 Basic: 42 cells -- simplified with more picture support
- WordPower 48: 48 cells -- intermediate users
- WordPower 60: 60 cells -- expanded vocabulary
- WordPower 60 Basic: 60 cells -- early language learners
- WordPower 80: 80 cells -- core words + spelling + word prediction
- WordPower 108: 108 cells -- high-frequency words with word completion
- WordPower 108 w/ Keyboard: 108 cells -- plus integrated keyboard
- WordPower 140: 140 cells -- maximum vocabulary density

## Strengths

1. Extensive Customization: Fully editable pages, buttons, symbols, layouts
2. Clinically designed WordPower vocabulary by Nancy Inman
3. 11 vocabulary configurations (20-140 cells)
4. ~$100 cheaper than Proloquo2Go
5. Built-in Head Tracking (no extra hardware)
6. 6+ languages with code switching
7. Realize Language analytics integration
8. Compatible with PRC-Saltillo dedicated devices
9. Nearly 40,000 SymbolStix symbols
10. AI-powered features (2026.1)
11. Universal app (iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Mac M1+)
12. Professional training resources for SLPs, educators, parents

## Weaknesses

1. iOS only -- no Android version
2. $299.99 price point
3. Inconsistent layout across vocabulary levels (disrupts motor planning)
4. Camera roll clutter (daily logo save bug)
5. Software bugs after iOS updates
6. Sometimes requires full reinstall
7. Complex learning curve for customization
8. Realize Language requires paid subscription
9. Head tracking only on newer devices (iPad Pro 2018+, iPhone X+)
10. No web or cross-platform version

## User Reviews

### SLP Perspectives
- WordPower widely recognized as well-designed, research-informed vocabulary
- Consistent core word placement supports motor planning and language development
- Multiple grid sizes allow matching complexity to ability level
- Training resources and Nancy Inman's involvement add clinical credibility

### Parent Reviews
- Described as "extremely helpful" for nonverbal children
- Frustration with software bugs that interrupt communication
- Appreciation for customization flexibility
- Concern about layout inconsistencies between vocabulary levels
- Relief at lower price vs Proloquo2Go

### General Feedback
- Positive: powerful communication tool with extensive vocabulary
- Negative: stability issues after iOS updates can leave users unable to communicate
- Mixed: simpler interface than Proloquo2Go (pro for some, con for others)

## Competitor Comparison

### TouchChat HD w/ WordPower vs Proloquo2Go
- Price: $299.99 vs ~$399.99
- Both iOS only
- Vocabulary: WordPower (word-based) vs Crescendo (symbol + word)
- Symbols: SymbolStix (~40,000) vs SymbolStix + PCS
- TouchChat simpler interface; Proloquo2Go more feature-rich
- TouchChat has built-in head tracking; Proloquo2Go does not
- Both highly customizable
- Proloquo2Go stronger developmental progression emphasis

### Other Competitors
- TD Snap (Tobii Dynavox): integrated with eye tracking hardware
- LAMP Words for Life (PRC-Saltillo): sister app, LAMP methodology
- Proloquo (AssistiveWare): newer, more modern AAC
- GoTalk NOW (Attainment): simpler scene-based AAC
- Spoken AAC: emerging AI-focused competitor
