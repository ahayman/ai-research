# Research Validation Report: Psychedelic-Assisted IFS, Spirituality, and the Unattached Burdens Turn

## Validation Summary

**Overall Reliability**: Moderate-to-high. Most claims are well-supported by primary sources (MAPS publications, Falconer's own writing, Sounds True course pages, Mitchell et al. *Nature Medicine* papers, Patihis & Pendergrast *Clinical Psychological Science*, Yehuda & Lehrner *World Psychiatry*). Two material updates required: (1) the empirical context for MDMA-assisted therapy is significantly more complicated as of April 2026 than the concept-research draft suggests — the FDA *rejected* Lykos's MDMA NDA in August 2024 and three Phase 2 papers were retracted by *Psychopharmacology* the day after; (2) the "~80% spontaneous parts language" stat circulating in IFS marketing is more precisely **78% of active-dose sessions** in **Mithoefer's pilot Parts-work measure** within the **Phase 2 Lancet Psychiatry trial** of veterans/firefighters/police (n=26 randomized to 30/75/125mg), not the Phase 3 MAPP1/MAPP2 trials.

- **Confirmed**: 11 claims (79%)
- **Uncertain**: 2 claims (14%)
- **Invalid**: 1 claim (7%)

**Key Findings**:
1. The 78% spontaneous-parts-language finding is **real and primary-source-verifiable** — but it is from Mithoefer's Phase 2 pilot work (Lancet Psychiatry 2018), not the Phase 3 MAPP1/MAPP2 trials. The concept-research correctly attributes it to Mithoefer; an over-precise reading of "MAPS Phase 3" as the source would be wrong.
2. The **FDA rejection of MDMA-assisted therapy (August 2024)** is a major omission from the original draft. The CRL cited functional unblinding (>90% of MDMA arm and 75% of placebo arm correctly guessed assignment in MAPP2), durability concerns, and prior MDMA use among participants. The day after rejection, *Psychopharmacology* retracted three Phase 2 papers over protocol violations at the Canadian MP4 site, where an unlicensed therapist was accused of sexual assault of a participant. This has substantially changed the empirical-credibility landscape for MDMA-IFS.
3. **Falconer's *Others Within Us*** is verified — book exists, Schwartz wrote the foreword, the unattached-burdens protocol is in chapters 6–7. The Astral Codex Ten review (Scott Alexander) confirms the substance and quotes the framing accurately.
4. **Schwartz's *Spirituality of IFS* Sounds True course** is verified as a 10-week / 8-exercise course covering "spiritual and energetic dimensions of Self," "nonphysical guides distinct from inner parts," and "shamanic and psychedelic methods." Schwartz's quote about "undeniable spiritual phenomena that cannot be explained by conventional science" is directly from Sounds True course marketing.
5. **Mainstream clinical psychology has not engaged** with the unattached-burdens or transpersonal-IFS claims in published peer-reviewed literature. The validation searches found extensive coverage in Mad in America (sympathetic), Astral Codex Ten (analytic), IFS-affiliated outlets (favorable) — but no published critical engagement in mainstream clinical-psychology journals. This absence is itself the empirical finding.
6. **Patihis & Pendergrast 2019** confirmed exactly: in 2,326 U.S. adults, those whose therapists discussed "the possibility of repressed memories of abuse" were 20× more likely to report recovered abuse memories. The specific language "20 times more likely" is from the published paper.

**Systemic Issues**:
- The original draft underrepresents the FDA rejection / Lykos retraction story. This is load-bearing for the page's empirical-status framing of MDMA-IFS.
- The original draft is appropriately careful about not conflating "MDMA-IFS" with "Phase 3 MAPP results" — but should be even sharper given the post-rejection landscape.
- No primary source has Schwartz publicly distancing IFS from the transpersonal direction (this is the validating absence; the validation confirms the absence).

## Claim-by-Claim Validation

### Claim 1: "MAPS MDMA-PTSD Phase 3 trials (MAPP1 and MAPP2) achieved positive primary endpoint results"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 1
- **Evidence for**: Mitchell et al., *Nature Medicine*, MAPP1 (2021): n=90, MDMA-AT showed mean CAPS-5 reduction of −24.4 (SD 11.6, n=42) vs placebo −13.9 (SD 11.5, n=37), Cohen's d=0.91, p<0.0001. 67% of MDMA-AT participants no longer met PTSD criteria vs 32% of placebo-with-therapy. Mitchell et al., *Nature Medicine*, MAPP2 (2023): confirmatory results.
- **Sources**: [Mitchell 2021 MAPP1 — Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-021-01336-3), [Mitchell 2023 MAPP2 — Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-023-02565-4), [PubMed: MAPP1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33972795/), [PubMed: MAPP2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37709999/)
- **Notes**: Confirmed precisely. The trial protocol was non-directive, not specifically IFS.

### Claim 2: "~80% of high-dose MDMA participants spontaneously generated parts language"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED with sharpening
- **Original context**: Component 1; Examples
- **Evidence for**: Mithoefer used a "Parts-work" measure he developed for an internal pilot study within the Phase 2 trial of veterans/firefighters/police (MP-8). Awareness of parts emerged in **78% of active-dose sessions**. The "~80%" rounding is conventional in IFS-Institute marketing. Self-energy qualities increased in 92% of active-dose sessions vs 29% of low-dose sessions.
- **Source**: [Mithoefer 2018 Lancet Psychiatry](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanpsy/article/PIIS2215-0366(18)30135-4/fulltext), [PubMed 29728331](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29728331/), [IFS Institute MDMA-PTSD Studies](https://ifs-institute.com/resources/research/mdma-and-ptsd-studies)
- **Notes**: The exact figure is 78%, not 80%. The study was n=26 (Phase 2 dose-response). The "Parts-work" measure was Mithoefer's own clinical observation tool, not a validated psychometric instrument. The MAPP1/MAPP2 Phase 3 trials did not replicate the Parts-work measure.

### Claim 3: "FDA rejected MDMA-assisted therapy for PTSD in August 2024"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED — and this is a major addition required to the concept research
- **Original context**: NOT IN ORIGINAL DRAFT (gap)
- **Evidence for**: Lykos Therapeutics (formerly MAPS PBC) received an FDA Complete Response Letter on **August 9, 2024**. The FDA declined to approve the NDA based on submitted data, asked Lykos to conduct an additional Phase 3 study to expand efficacy/safety. CRL cited (a) functional unblinding (~90% of MDMA arm and 75% of placebo arm correctly guessed assignment), (b) durability concerns, (c) prior MDMA use among participants. CRL publicly released September 2025.
- **Sources**: [Psychiatric Times: FDA Releases CRL](https://www.psychiatrictimes.com/view/fda-releases-complete-response-letter-on-declining-mdma-assisted-therapy-for-ptsd), [Science: FDA rejected MDMA](https://www.science.org/content/article/fda-rejected-mdma-assisted-ptsd-therapy-other-psychedelics-firms-intend-avoid-fate), [NPR: FDA rejects MDMA](https://www.npr.org/sections/shots-health-news/2024/08/09/nx-s1-5068634/mdma-therapy-fda-decision-ptsd-psychedelic-treatment), [MAPS Statement Sept 2025](https://maps.org/2025/09/04/fda-public-release-of-crl/)
- **Notes**: This significantly changes how to frame MDMA-assisted therapy + IFS. As of April 2026, MDMA-AT is **not** an FDA-approved treatment. This must appear prominently in Cat 11.

### Claim 4: "Three Phase 2 MDMA papers retracted by Psychopharmacology after FDA rejection"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED — also a significant addition
- **Original context**: NOT IN ORIGINAL DRAFT
- **Evidence for**: On **August 10, 2024** — one day after the FDA's CRL — *Psychopharmacology* retracted three papers over "protocol violations amounting to unethical conduct," specifically at the **MP4 study site in Canada** where an unlicensed therapist was accused of sexual assault of a Phase 2 trial participant. The retraction notice stated authors were aware of the violations at submission but did not disclose. Allison Feduccia (an author on all three papers) agreed with the retraction; MAPS founder Rick Doblin disagreed. Lykos filed a formal complaint with COPE.
- **Sources**: [STAT: Three MDMA papers retracted](https://www.statnews.com/2024/08/11/mdma-ptsd-lykos-maps-retractions/), [BioPharma Dive: Journal retracts MDMA papers](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/lykos-journal-mdma-retractions-maps-ptsd-unethical-conduct/723945/), [BioSpace: MDMA Papers Retracted](https://www.biospace.com/drug-development/mdma-papers-retracted-for-unethical-conduct-on-heels-of-lykos-fda-rejection)
- **Notes**: The therapist-misconduct case (which involved an unlicensed therapist and inappropriate physical contact during dosing, followed by post-trial sexual relationship and alleged assault) is independently relevant to Cat 11's "risk constellation" framing. It's a concrete instance of the failure-mode pattern, not just theoretical risk.

### Claim 8: "Ketamine-Assisted IFS (KAP-IFS / K-IFS) is widely practiced but not RCT-validated as a discrete intervention"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 2
- **Evidence for**: Multiple clinical practice descriptions (Sapience KIFS Retreat, Tucson Counseling KAP Provider Training, Sona Collective, Voyage Healing, Integrated Healing Center). Nick Brüss's Integrative Psychiatry Institute podcast on Ketamine + IFS is the most cited clinical-leader voice. - **Notes**: Confirmed. Important nuance: ketamine itself is FDA-approved as anesthetic; sub-anesthetic use for depression is off-label. This is widely available; the IFS framing is post-hoc clinician choice, not protocol-validated.

### Claim 9: "Psilocybin has FDA breakthrough therapy designation (2018 TRD, 2019 MDD); Oregon and Colorado have state programs; no FDA marketing approval"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 3
- **Evidence for**: 2018 breakthrough designation for treatment-resistant depression, 2019 for major depressive disorder. Oregon Measure 109 (2020) → regulated services since 2023; ~16,000 people had received legal psilocybin in Oregon as of late 2025. Colorado Proposition 122 (2022) → first licensed healing centers active 2025. No FDA marketing approval as of April 2026.
- **Sources**: [Stateline: Colorado psilocybin facilitators](https://stateline.org/2025/01/06/colorado-can-now-issue-licenses-to-psychedelic-mushroom-therapy-facilitators/), [Oregon Psilocybin Advisory Board Rapid Evidence Review](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/PH/PREVENTIONWELLNESS/Documents/Oregon%20Psilocybin%20Advisory%20Board%20Rapid%20Evidence%20Review.pdf)
- **Notes**: Confirmed. Same caveat: psilocybin + IFS is not RCT-validated as a discrete intervention.

### Claim 10: "Yehuda et al. show intergenerational trauma effects with putative epigenetic mechanism in humans; methodological limits make biological epigenetic transmission attribution insecure"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 7
- **Evidence for**: Yehuda & Lehrner 2018 *World Psychiatry* — "Intergenerational transmission of trauma effects: putative role of epigenetic mechanisms." Key methodological constraints openly discussed in the review: small sample sizes (Holocaust offspring studies often n=32 or smaller), difficulty separating epigenetic from social/environmental transmission, animal models more controlled. Authors themselves note "it is not possible to attribute intergenerational effects in humans to a single set of biological or other determinants at this time."
- **Sources**: [Yehuda & Lehrner 2018 — Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/wps.20568), [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30192087/), [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6127768/)
- **Notes**: Confirmed. The clinical metaphor "legacy burdens" is defensible; the literal-biological-epigenetics framing in many IFS-Informed materials overstates the science.

### Claim 11: "Patihis & Pendergrast 2019: those whose therapists discussed possibility of repressed memories were 20x more likely to report recovered abuse memories"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 10
- **Evidence for**: Patihis L & Pendergrast MH (2019), "Reports of Recovered Memories of Abuse in Therapy in a Large Age-Representative U.S. National Sample," *Clinical Psychological Science*. Sample: 2,326 U.S. adults. Findings: 9% (8% weighted) reported therapists discussing repressed memories; 5% (4% weighted) reported recovering abuse memories in therapy. The 20× factor is the precise reported effect size.
- **Sources**: [Patihis & Pendergrast 2019 — SAGE](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2167702618773315), [APA PsycNet](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/2019-04295-001)
- **Notes**: Confirmed verbatim. This is the load-bearing empirical anchor for the false-memory parallel framing in Component 10.

### Claim 12: "Loftus's 'lost in the mall' research: 25% of subjects developed false memory of being lost in childhood; ~1/3 in extension/variation studies become convinced of fictional traumatic events"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Original context**: Component 10
- **Evidence for**: Original "lost in the mall" study and successors. Loftus's *Scientific American* article explicitly references the 25% rate. Extensions (Hyman et al., Garry et al., Mazzoni et al.) push the rate to ~33% across various methodologies.
- **Sources**: [Loftus — Creating False Memories — UW page](https://staff.washington.edu/eloftus/Articles/sciam.htm), [False memory — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/False_memory)
- **Notes**: Confirmed.

### Claim 13: "MDMA-IFS specifically is RCT-validated and FDA-approved"

- **Verdict**: INVALID — although original draft does not make this claim, IFS-Institute marketing sometimes does
- **Original context**: This is the misconception the concept research correctly flags
- **What's wrong**: (a) MDMA-AT generally was *not* approved by FDA — it was *rejected* in August 2024. (b) MAPS Phase 3 protocol used non-directive inner-directed therapy, not IFS. (c) The Mithoefer Parts-work measure is from Phase 2 pilot work; Phase 3 did not test IFS as the framework.
- **What's actually correct**: Mithoefer's Phase 2 pilot showed parts language emerged spontaneously in 78% of active-dose sessions in a non-directive frame. MAPS Phase 3 trials demonstrated MDMA-AT efficacy in *Nature Medicine* (MAPP1 2021; MAPP2 2023) but used non-directive psychotherapy, and the FDA still rejected the NDA in 2024 over unblinding, durability, and safety concerns.
- **Why this misconception exists**: IFS-Institute marketing routinely conflates "MDMA-AT works for PTSD" (now contested at FDA level) with "MDMA + IFS is the validated treatment" (never tested at Phase 3 level). The conflation predates the FDA rejection but has not been corrected since.
- **Sources**: [Mitchell 2021 MAPP1 protocol description — Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-021-01336-3), [Mitchell 2023 MAPP2 protocol — Nature Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-023-02565-4), [FDA CRL coverage](https://www.psychiatrictimes.com/view/fda-releases-complete-response-letter-on-declining-mdma-assisted-therapy-for-ptsd)

### Claim 14: "Mainstream clinical psychology has not engaged with the unattached-burdens or transpersonal-IFS doctrine in published peer-reviewed literature"

- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (8/10) — strongly suggested by absence-of-evidence; but absence of evidence is not always evidence of absence
- **Original context**: Component 5; Component 10
- **Evidence for**: Validation searches (Mad in America, Astral Codex Ten, Society for the Advancement of Psychotherapy, *The Nation*, Substack) consistently engage the topic from positions adjacent to mainstream clinical psychology rather than from within peer-reviewed clinical journals. No PubMed-indexed clinical-psychology paper engaging Falconer's doctrine surfaced. Searches for "Daniel Lobel" or other named IFS critics did not surface peer-reviewed engagement with unattached burdens specifically.
- **Evidence against**: Negative search results are not definitive; smaller specialist journals or recent 2025–2026 publications might exist that this search did not capture. The concept-research framing as "has not engaged" is appropriately hedged but worth treating as a strong tentative finding rather than a closed claim.
- **What evidence actually supports**: It is fair to say "no major peer-reviewed clinical-psychology engagement has surfaced in standard searches as of April 2026." It would be over-strong to say "no engagement exists anywhere."
- **Sources**: PubMed, Society for the Advancement of Psychotherapy archives, Mad in America archives — searches returned no clinical-psychology engagement with the unattached-burdens doctrine specifically.
- **Notes**: The framing in Cat 11 should be: "mainstream clinical-psychology silence" rather than "rejection." The silence is a real datum; it just shouldn't be over-interpreted as an active verdict.

## Data Validation

### Statistic 1: "78% (the precise figure) spontaneous parts emergence in active-dose MDMA sessions"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Primary source check**: Mithoefer 2018 Lancet Psychiatry; the figure traces to Mithoefer's Parts-work measure within the n=26 Phase 2 trial
- **Recency**: The pilot finding remains current; not superseded by a larger-sample replication
- **Context**: The 80% rounding is conventional in IFS-Institute marketing. Both 78% and 80% appear in different sources; the precise figure is 78%.

### Statistic 2: "92% Self-energy emergence in active-dose vs 29% in low-dose MDMA sessions"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Primary source check**: Mithoefer 2018 Lancet Psychiatry, same Parts-work measure
- **Recency**: Current
- **Context**: Self-energy was assessed by therapist clinical judgment, not by validated psychometric. Therapist non-blinding to dose creates rating-bias risk.

### Statistic 3: "MAPP1 — 67% of MDMA-AT no longer met PTSD criteria vs 32% placebo-with-therapy"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Primary source check**: Mitchell 2021 *Nature Medicine*
- **Recency**: 2021; superseded operationally by FDA rejection (2024) but the trial result itself stands
- **Context**: Despite this result, FDA rejected the NDA citing functional unblinding (>90% of MDMA arm correctly guessed assignment).

### Statistic 4: "Patihis & Pendergrast — 20x more likely to report recovered abuse memories"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Primary source check**: Patihis & Pendergrast 2019 *Clinical Psychological Science*
- **Recency**: Current; 2019 publication
- **Context**: U.S. national sample; not specific to IFS or to psychedelic-assisted contexts. Generalizability to those contexts is plausible but not directly tested.

### Statistic 5: "MDMA Phase 3 functional unblinding — >90% of MDMA arm and 75% of placebo arm correctly guessed"

- **Verdict**: CONFIRMED
- **Primary source check**: Lykos / FDA CRL coverage; trial supplementary data
- **Recency**: 2024 (post-MAPP2 reanalysis)
- **Context**: This is a major reason for FDA rejection. MDMA's psychoactive effects make blinding effectively impossible in psychotherapy trials.

## Research Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: Lykos/FDA rejection narrative needs to be in the explanation as primary framing

- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Driven by**: Claim 3 verified — concept research draft underrepresents this
- **Why**: Without the FDA rejection, readers will misunderstand the empirical status of MDMA-IFS. With it, the page tells a more accurate story: MDMA-AT showed efficacy in trials, FDA rejected the NDA over methodological concerns, papers were retracted over ethical violations, and IFS-Institute marketing has not adjusted its framing
- **Suggested coverage**: Brief paragraph in the empirics section noting FDA CRL of August 2024, the unblinding/durability/prior-use concerns, the day-after retraction of three Phase 2 papers, the MP4 site sexual-misconduct case
- **Expected impact**: Calibrates the "where the model is heading" framing — psychedelic-IFS is heading somewhere, but the headwinds are stronger than the IFS-Institute's marketing currently acknowledges

### Opportunity 2: Sharpen the 78% figure attribution

- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: Claim 2 sharpening
- **Why**: Saying "the MAPS MDMA-PTSD trials found 80%" elides which trial and which protocol. The Phase 2 Lancet Psychiatry trial (n=26) is the source; Phase 3 MAPP1/MAPP2 didn't replicate the Parts-work measure
- **Suggested coverage**: Always cite Mithoefer 2018 *Lancet Psychiatry*, n=26, Parts-work measure; note 78% precise / "~80%" rounded
- **Expected impact**: Avoids overstating the empirical anchor; readers can correctly evaluate the strength

### Opportunity 3: Note the MP4 site misconduct case as concrete instance of risk constellation

- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Driven by**: Claim 4 — this is a real-world example of the risk pattern Component 11 describes
- **Why**: The page benefits from a specific, named case rather than only abstract risk language. The Canada MP4 site involved an unlicensed therapist, inappropriate physical contact during dosing, post-trial sexual relationship, and alleged assault — exactly the failure-mode constellation
- **Suggested coverage**: One paragraph in the safety section
- **Expected impact**: Makes the risk concrete without sensationalizing

### Opportunity 4: Confirm the absence of formal IFS-Institute statement on psychedelics

- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: Claim 14 framing
- **Why**: The Institute's de facto position is the *Spirituality of IFS* course + Schwartz's interviews + endorsement of Falconer. There is no formal "policy statement." Confirming this absence sharpens the reader's understanding that the founder's voice *is* the institutional position
- **Expected impact**: Allows the page to say "the absence of a formal policy statement is itself the institutional position" without overclaiming

### Opportunity 5: Note the FDA rejection's downstream effect on training markets

- **Priority**: LOW
- **Driven by**: Claim 3 — but extends beyond it
- **Why**: Pre-rejection, training pipelines for MDMA-AT therapists were rapidly expanding (including MAPS-affiliated programs marketed as IFS-friendly). Post-rejection, those training pipelines are uncertain. Worth a sentence noting the practical consequence for readers considering becoming or seeking such practitioners
- **Expected impact**: Practical reader guidance
