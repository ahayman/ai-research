# Validation Report: Iran History & Geopolitics (Modern Era)

## Validation Methodology

Each of the five research files was read in full, and 3-4 independent web searches per file were conducted to verify key claims, dates, figures, and event sequences. Claims were cross-checked against Wikipedia, Britannica, primary government sources, major news outlets (Al Jazeera, NPR, CNN, Washington Post), think tanks (Brookings, CFR, CSIS), and human rights organizations (HRW, Amnesty International). Special attention was given to current events accuracy (2024-2026) and casualty/financial figures where precision matters.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

### Strengths Across All Files

1. **Chronological precision**: Dates are overwhelmingly accurate across hundreds of specific claims. This is the hallmark of careful research.

2. **Source diversity**: Each file draws on a mix of academic, governmental, journalistic, and think-tank sources, reducing single-source bias.

3. **Balanced framing**: The files generally present multiple perspectives on contested events (e.g., the October Surprise, Iran's role in Oct 7, the JCPOA debate) without adopting a single ideological lens.

4. **Current events integration**: The coverage of 2024-2026 events (Assad's fall, Hezbollah's devastation, the 2026 strikes) is remarkably accurate given how recent these events are.

5. **Technical accuracy**: Nuclear program details (centrifuge counts, enrichment levels, JCPOA provisions) are precisely documented.

### Recurring Concerns

1. **Casualty figure ranges**: Several files select specific numbers from wide ranges without always presenting the full spectrum. The 1988 prison massacres (2,800-30,000), Halabja (3,200-5,000+), and Hezbollah 2024 losses all have wider ranges than presented.

2. **Attribution certainty**: A few claims are presented as established fact when they remain contested (Neda's shooter, the grand bargain's provenance, specific financial flows).

3. **Slight pro-narrative framing**: While generally balanced, some sections adopt a slightly sympathetic framing toward the Iranian reform movement and a critical framing of U.S. policy decisions. This is a matter of degree and does not rise to the level of bias that would undermine credibility, but readers should be aware.

4. **Chemical weapons victim count inconsistency**: File 2 states "40,000 Iranians were affected by Iraqi chemical weapons attacks." Other authoritative sources cite 20,000 killed and 80,000-100,000 total affected. The "40,000" figure is unclear in its scope.
