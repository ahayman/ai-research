# Validation: Semantic Primitives and NSM

## Validation Summary

The document accurately presents the NSM framework, its history, and the 65 semantic primes inventory. The core claims about Wierzbicka's work, the cross-linguistic testing program, and the development timeline are all confirmed. The main area requiring correction is the count of "approximately 50 universal semantic molecules" — the actual number of truly universal molecules appears to be smaller (perhaps ~25), while the broader list of productive molecules (language-specific and universal) is much larger (~180 for English alone). The analysis of visual representability challenges is well-reasoned original synthesis.

### Semantic Molecules

| Claim | Rating | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| ~50 universal semantic molecules identified | UNCERTAIN (5/10) | **This is the document's weakest claim.** Independent verification reveals significant complexity. The Griffith University NSM portal mentions universal molecules but does not specify a count of ~50. Other sources suggest: (a) about 25 may be truly universal; (b) around 60-80 may be "near-universal"; (c) one comprehensive analysis found ~180 productive semantic molecules for English alone. The "~50 universal" figure appears to be an approximation that doesn't align cleanly with any specific published count. |

## Corrections Required

1. **Semantic molecules count**: Change "~50 universal semantic molecules" to something more nuanced, such as: "approximately 25-80 semantic molecules are considered universal or near-universal, with the exact count still under investigation. A broader inventory of approximately 180 productive semantic molecules has been catalogued for English, though most of these are language-specific." This better reflects the state of the research.

2. **Molecule categories**: The listed categories are accurate but the document should note that the universality of some social molecules (MAN, WOMAN, CHILD) is debated — the document does mention this in the Edge Cases section, which is good.
