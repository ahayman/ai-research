### Claim E3: Reports of $8,000 Tobii Devices Being Disabled During Updates
**UNCONFIRMED** -- broader pattern documented, specific incident not independently located

Multiple user reports (App Store reviews, community forums) describe TD Snap speech functionality being disabled after software updates for users without active subscriptions. Tobii dedicated AAC devices do cost in the $5,000-$15,000+ range, making the price point plausible. However, I could not locate the specific report of an "$8,000 device being disabled during an update" from any indexed source.

**Recommendation for grant use:** Cite the documented pattern of subscription-gating after updates rather than a single unverified anecdote. Example: "User reports document TD Snap speech functionality being disabled after software updates for users without active subscriptions, affecting devices that can cost thousands of dollars."

### Claim E4: Medicare Ended Telepractice Reimbursement for SLPs Effective December 31, 2024
**CONFIRMED**

The Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023 extended Medicare telehealth flexibilities for audiologists and SLPs through **December 31, 2024**. After that date, Medicare no longer directly reimburses SLPs for telehealth services. SLPs' remaining options: private pay or billing "incident to" a physician. ASHA documented this extensively in guidance materials for members.

**Sources:**
- [ASHA: Providing Telehealth Services Under Medicare](https://www.asha.org/practice/reimbursement/medicare/providing-telehealth-services-under-medicare/)

## LLM CLAIMS

### Claim L1: No Commercial AAC App Currently Uses LLMs
**CONTRADICTED**

Multiple commercial and freely available AAC apps incorporate LLM or generative AI features:
- **Spoken AAC** describes itself as using a "large language model" for context-aware predictive text (commercially available, iOS & Android)
- **Vocable AAC** (WillowTree/TELUS Digital) shipped OpenAI ChatGPT integration in **September 2023** ("Smart Assist" feature)
- **Cboard** uses OpenAI API via cboard-ai-engine for board content generation (open-source, free)
- **PRC-Saltillo** launched Apple Intelligence features in 2026.1 across LAMP, TouchChat, Unity AAC, and Dialogue AAC

The claim that "no commercial AAC app currently uses LLMs" is factually incorrect. A more accurate characterization: "LLM adoption in AAC remains nascent. While several apps have integrated AI features, none of the top 3 market-share leaders by dedicated device sales (Proloquo/AssistiveWare, TD Snap/Tobii Dynavox, Grid/Smartbox) have shipped LLM-powered core communication features as of early 2026. PRC-Saltillo's 2026.1 Apple Intelligence integration represents the first major platform to add LLM-adjacent features to established AAC apps."

### Claim L2: Spoken Uses a "Large Language Model"; Patent US11145313B2
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED**

- **Patent US11145313B2** is real: "System and method for assisting communication through predictive speech," assigned to Spoken Inc., published October 12, 2021. It covers context-based predictive communication using databases of words, phrases, and images mapped to context cues (time of day, location, recent usage, external speech).
- Spoken's Wikipedia page and marketing materials describe the app as using a "large language model." However, the patent predates modern transformer-based LLMs and describes a context-matching prediction system. Whether Spoken's current implementation uses a transformer-based LLM or a proprietary predictive model is unclear from public sources.

### Claim L8: On-Device LLM -- iPhone 17 Pro Achieves 136 tokens/sec
**CONFIRMED**

Benchmarks from **Cactus** (Y Combinator-backed startup) show the iPhone 17 Pro achieving **136 tokens/sec** for on-device LLM inference. Uses INT8 quantization and NPU acceleration. Sub-50ms time-to-first-token. Comparison: Mac M4 Pro = 173 tok/s, Galaxy S25 Ultra = 91 tok/s, Raspberry Pi 5 = 24 tok/s.

**Note for grant use:** This benchmark is from a specific framework (Cactus) with a specific model size (likely 600M-1B parameters, not frontier models). Performance will vary by model size and quantization approach.

**Sources:**
- [Argmax: iPhone 17 On-Device Inference Benchmarks](https://www.argmaxinc.com/blog/iphone-17-on-device-inference-benchmarks)

### Claim L10: SS-GEN Framework (AAAI 2025) -- 2B-7B Models Generate Social Stories
**CONFIRMED**

SS-GEN was presented as an **oral paper at AAAI 2025** (39th AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence). The framework uses LLMs with a constraint-driven strategy (STARSOW) to generate personalized Social Stories for children with ASD. Fine-tuned **2B and 7B parameter models** achieved comparable quality to expensive closed-source models. The 2B model generates simpler stories potentially more suitable for children with severe autism. Code available on GitHub.

### Claim A2: ~245-294K AAC-Eligible Autistic Students
**UNCONFIRMED** -- plausible derived estimate, no authoritative single source

The calculation appears to be: ~980,000 autistic students under IDEA (OSEP 2024 data) x 25-30% minimally verbal = 245,000-294,000. Both inputs are independently well-sourced:
- **~980,000** autistic students in IDEA (Disability Scoop, May 2024; OSEP Fast Facts 2024)
- **25-30%** of autistic children are minimally verbal (multiple peer-reviewed sources including Frontiers in Psychiatry, PMC)

However, no published source cites 245-294K as a specific estimate. Additionally, "AAC-eligible" may be broader than "minimally verbal" -- many verbal autistic individuals also benefit from AAC.

**Recommendation for grant use:** Present as a calculated estimate with methodology shown and both source citations, not as a published statistic. Example: "Based on ~980,000 autistic students under IDEA and research indicating 25-30% are minimally verbal, an estimated 245,000-294,000 autistic students may need AAC support."

### Claim A3: 5-6 Communication Acts Per School Day for AAC Users
**CONFIRMED**

Research from PMC ("Support for AAC Use in Preschool, and Growth in Language Skills, for Young Children with Developmental Disabilities") found teachers reported children using their formal AAC systems to communicate, on average, only **5 or 6 times in a typical school day**. The study also found formal AAC systems were infrequently used by teachers, other classroom adults, and peers.

**Sources:**

### Claim A4: 25-50% Implementation Fidelity for Autism Interventions
**CONFIRMED**

Multiple peer-reviewed sources confirm that only **25-50%** of evidence-based practices for autism are implemented with fidelity in school settings. Supporting data: STAR classroom fidelity averaged 57%, Structured Teaching 48%, range across programs 12-92%. The 25-50% figure is established in implementation science literature as a benchmark for real-world autism intervention delivery.

**Sources:**
- [PMC: Individual and Organizational Characteristics](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7308214/)
- [PMC: Pilot Study of Social Engagement Intervention](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6502245/)
- [PMC: Role of Treatment Fidelity](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3738205/)

**CONFIRMED**

Learning Policy Institute (2025): during the **2024-25 school year, 45 states** reported teacher shortages in special education -- the most commonly reported shortage area (followed by science at 41 states and math at 40). At minimum, **411,549 positions** were unfilled or filled by non-fully-certified teachers (~1 in 8 teaching positions nationally). U.S. Commission on Civil Rights also issued a 2025 report on this topic.
