# Research Notes: TD Snap by Tobii Dynavox - AAC Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

TD Snap is Tobii Dynavox's flagship augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) software platform, designed for people with speech and language disabilities including autism, cerebral palsy, ALS, aphasia, Rett syndrome, and spinal cord injuries. The software offers an unusually broad range of communication approaches through its multiple page set system, which includes six first-party page sets (Core First, Motor Plan, Express, Text, Scanning, and Aphasia) plus third-party options like PODD and Gateway available via in-app purchase.

What sets TD Snap apart in the AAC market is its native integration with Tobii Dynavox's proprietary eye gaze tracking hardware -- the world's leading eye tracking technology built on 15+ years of research using pupil-center corneal reflection (PCCR) with near-infrared illumination. The platform supports touch, eye gaze, and switch access methods across all page sets, and includes unique features like Google Assistant integration for smart home control, true bilingual code-switching, and data tracking through the myTobiiDynavox companion portal.

However, TD Snap's transition from a one-time $100 purchase to a $9.99/month subscription model in 2024 generated significant controversy in the AAC community. Families and speech-language pathologists have criticized the ongoing cost burden, especially given that many users depend on AAC for their entire lives. Despite this, professional reviews consistently praise TD Snap as one of the best AAC applications available, with a 4.43/5 App Store rating and strong endorsements from practicing SLPs.

## Historical Context

### DynaVox Origins (1983)
In 1983, Gary Kiliany, a Carnegie Mellon University graduate student volunteering at the Rehabilitation Institute of Pittsburgh, met a woman with cerebral palsy who could not communicate verbally. With guidance from Professor Mark Friedman, Kiliany created the "EyeTyper" -- a device that allowed her to compose and speak what she wanted to say. This invention laid the foundation for what would become DynaVox Systems, one of the pioneering companies in AAC technology.

### Tobii Technology Founded (2001)
Tobii Technology was founded in Sweden in 2001, initially selling eye tracking technology for gaze analysis in psychology, athletics, and market research. In 2005, Tobii released the world's first eye-controlled computer, enabling people with disabilities to communicate using their eyes as an input mechanism.

### Merger: Tobii Acquires DynaVox (2014)
In April 2014, DynaVox filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection. On May 22, 2014, competitor Tobii acquired DynaVox and integrated them into Tobii's Assisted Technology division. The combined company was renamed Tobii Dynavox LLC, creating the world's leading provider of assistive communication technology.

### Strategic Acquisitions
Tobii Dynavox further expanded through key acquisitions:
- **Acapela**: Belgium-based global provider of speech synthesis and digital voices
- **Rehadapt**: Germany-based global provider of medically certified mounting solutions for assistive technology

### TD Snap App Transition (2024)
In May 2024, TD Snap AAC was unpublished from the Apple App Store and replaced with a new version featuring a subscription-based model. The original paid app was renamed "TD Snap AAC Legacy" for existing purchasers.

## Page Sets Deep Dive

### Core First
- Symbol-supported page set for all stages of language development
- Grid sizes: 1x1, 1x2, 2x2, 2x3, 3x3, 3x4, 4x4, 5x5, 6x6, 7x7, 7x9, 8x10
- Seamless content continuity across grid sizes
- Words remain in predictable locations as users grow
- Path to literacy at any stage

### Motor Plan
- Symbol-supported with emphasis on consistent motor patterns
- Grid sizes: 30, 40, and 66
- Words grouped taxonomically by feature and function (not themes)
- Users learn distinct motor patterns for each word leading to automaticity
- Designed for both touch and eye gaze access
- Based on decades of language organization research

### Express
- Simplicity-focused with 18-button grid
- Theme-based navigation structure
- Large, easy-to-select buttons
- Built-in resources: book creation, photos, whiteboard, visual supports, PCS library
- Keyboard for letter play and spelling practice
- Minimal learning curve

### Text
- Designed for literate communicators
- No symbol dependency -- text labels only
- Phrases organized by topic with pre-stored messages
- Keyboard with word prediction and editing tools
- Access to smart assistants (Google, Alexa)
- For users transitioning from symbols to text-only

### Scanning
- Based on Core First, optimized for switch scanning access
- Organized into set groups for efficient scan patterns
- Auditory cues customizable for each grouping
- Feedback settings: zoom, highlighting, selection sound
- Timing settings and scan pattern customization
- For users with significant motor disabilities (e.g., Cerebral Palsy)

### Aphasia
- Built specifically for adults with aphasia
- Tools: Whiteboard, Rating Scales, Word Lists, Conversation Practice Templates, Photo Storage
- Supports language recovery alongside daily communication
- Features similar to therapy tools used by SLPs
- Customizable sizes, colors, and complexity levels

### PODD (Third-Party)
- Pragmatic Organization Dynamic Display by Gayle Porter
- Available via in-app purchase
- 15-cell layout representing common communication areas
- Language prediction features
- Designed for pre-school and school-aged learners
- Supports 1-4 keyword sentences

### Gateway (Third-Party)
- Created by Joan Bruno focused on core words
- Available via in-app purchase
- High frequency core and fringe words
- Home and School Core pages link to relevant vocabulary
- Theme pages for educational and social settings
- Stages-based design for language development levels

## Eye Gaze Technology

### How It Works
Tobii's eye tracking uses pupil-center corneal reflection (PCCR) technology:
1. The eye tracker sends out near-infrared (NIR) light toward the user's eyes
2. NIR light creates reflection patterns on the pupil and cornea
3. The corneal reflection (glint) is tracked relative to pupil center position
4. As eyes move, iris/pupil position changes relative to corneal reflection
5. This allows accurate estimation of the point of gaze

### Why Near-Infrared Light
- Invisible to the human eye -- no discomfort
- Minimal interference from ambient light
- Reliable illumination in different environments (indoor/outdoor)
- Enables stable tracking results

### Advanced Features
- Dynamic switching between bright and dark pupil tracking
- Works across all ethnicities, disabilities, eyewear, and lighting conditions
- Based on 15+ years of research and testing
- Fastest recovery time when eyes leave tracking box
- Precise gaze selection for quick screen target selection

## Controversies & Debates

### Subscription Model Backlash
The most significant controversy surrounding TD Snap is the 2024 transition from a one-time $100 purchase to a $9.99/month subscription model:

**Critics argue:**
- Annual cost of $119.88 creates ongoing financial burden for families
- Users who need AAC for life face unlimited cumulative costs (5 years = ~$600)
- Forced updates have disabled devices mid-use
- AT Act Programs often cannot obtain subscriptions
- Managed iPads using JAMF and similar systems don't work with the subscription model

**Tobii Dynavox's position:**
- Subscription enables continuous updates and improvements
- Enterprise option ($149.99 one-time) available for institutions
- Existing license card holders and pre-May 2024 purchasers grandfathered in
- 60-day free trial available

### Ecosystem Lock-In Concerns
- Tobii Dynavox explicitly states they cannot guarantee compatibility with non-approved hardware
- No technical support for hardware issues on third-party devices
- Custom page set configurations are platform-specific
- Proprietary eye tracking hardware creates dependency on Tobii ecosystem
- TD Pilot requires purchasing specific Tobii hardware case (~$10,500+) for iPad eye tracking
