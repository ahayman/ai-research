# AAC Voice Customization Research -- Validation Report

## Validation Summary

This report independently verifies 48 key claims across five AAC voice customization research files. Verification was conducted via web searches and cross-referencing official product pages, press releases, App Store listings, academic sources, and news articles.

**Overall assessment: The research is substantially accurate.** Most product feature claims, pricing data, and technical details are confirmed by primary sources. A few statistics lack traceable primary sourcing, and one claim about Apple Personal Voice phrase count in the original iOS 17 version requires a minor correction in the Spoken/Fluent research file.

---

## File 1: AssistiveWare Research (Proloquo2Go, Proloquo, Proloquo4Text)

### Claim 1.1: Proloquo2Go includes 100+ free Acapela Neural Voices
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare's product page states Proloquo2Go offers "over 100 free, natural-sounding voices" in English, Spanish, French, and Dutch. The App Store listing corroborates this. Version 8.7 (July 2025) added four new children's voices.

Sources:
- [Proloquo2Go Product Page](https://www.assistiveware.com/products/proloquo2go)
- [App Store Listing](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/proloquo2go-aac/id308368164)

### Claim 1.2: ExpressivePower adds emotional expressions and sounds to TTS
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare's support documentation confirms ExpressivePower allows users to create buttons with emotive expressions and sounds. Children's voices include variants of "dad/daddy" and "mom/mommy" in calling, angry, questioning, whining, and wheedling tones, plus play sounds (barking dog, fire truck siren). Available expressions vary by voice.

Sources:
- [ExpressivePower Support Page](https://www.assistiveware.com/support/proloquo2go/organize/buttons/expressivepower)
- [ExpressivePower Blog Post](https://assistiveware.com/blog/expressivepower)

### Claim 1.3: Acapela My-Own-Voice compatibility with AssistiveWare apps
**CONFIRMED**

Acapela's My-Own-Voice landing page for AssistiveWare confirms direct compatibility with Proloquo2Go, Proloquo4Text, Pictello, and simPODD.

Sources:
- [My-Own-Voice for AssistiveWare](https://mov.acapela-group.com/customer/1025/)

### Claim 1.4: Proloquo supports Apple Personal Voice (English only)
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare support pages confirm Personal Voice support in Proloquo2Go, Proloquo, Proloquo4Text, simPODD, and Pictello -- English only. Setup path matches the research: Settings > Language and Speech > select voice.

Sources:
- [Setup Personal Voice in Proloquo2Go](https://www.assistiveware.com/support/proloquo2go/speech/apple-personal)
- [Proloquo4Text Personal Voice](https://www.assistiveware.com/support/proloquo4text/adjust-speech-output/apple-personal-voice)

### Claim 1.5: Children's voices Josh and Ella took 2,500+ hours and ~1 year to develop
**CONFIRMED**

AssistiveWare's blog post "Creating Genuine Children's Voices" confirms the 2,500+ hours figure and approximately one-year development timeline. The voices were "recorded BY and FOR children." Acapela also confirms the American genuine children's voices Josh and Ella.

Sources:
- [Creating Genuine Children's Voices](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/creating-genuine-childrens-voices)
- [Acapela American Children's Voices](https://www.acapela-group.com/news/american-genuine-childrens-voices/?lang=en)

### Claim 1.6: African American English voices -- Tamira, Darius, Malik, Tiana
**CONFIRMED**

Acapela Group confirms the four AAE voices were developed collaboratively by Acapela, AssistiveWare, and PRC-Saltillo. Tamira was the first adult female AAE voice. All four are available across multiple AAC platforms.

Sources:
- [Acapela African American English Voices](https://www.acapela-group.com/news/african-american-english-digital-voice/)
- [Tamira Voice Release](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/tamira-voice-release-proloquo2go-7-6)

### Claim 1.7: Proloquo2Go priced at $249.99
**CONFIRMED**

App Store listing confirms $249.99 for iPhone/iPad. Mac version is $124.99.

Sources:
- [App Store Listing](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/proloquo2go-aac/id308368164)

### Claim 1.8: 60% of Proloquo2Go users are 11 years old or younger
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED**

This statistic appears in AssistiveWare's blog about children's voices but could not be independently verified through a third-party source. It is plausible given the app's focus on symbol-based AAC for children but originates from AssistiveWare's own data. No reason to doubt it, but it is self-reported.

Sources:
- [Creating Genuine Children's Voices](https://www.assistiveware.com/blog/creating-genuine-childrens-voices)

## File 4: Spoken AAC & Fluent AAC Research

### Claim 4.1: Spoken AAC ElevenLabs integration requires as little as 10 seconds of audio for cloning
**CONFIRMED**

Spoken's v1.9.3 release blog states: "Cloning through ElevenLabs only requires ten seconds of voice audio." Quality scales with more reference audio (5-10 minutes recommended for instant clones; 30 min to 2-3 hours for professional clones).

Sources:
- [Spoken v1.9.3 Release](https://spokenaac.com/blog/version-1.9.3/)
- [ElevenLabs Voice Cloning](https://elevenlabs.io/voice-cloning)

### Claim 4.2: Spoken AAC supports Audio Tags with ElevenLabs v3 model
**CONFIRMED**

Spoken's help documentation confirms Audio Tags are available with the Eleven v3 model selected. Tags are entered in square brackets (e.g., [sigh], [excited], [whispers]). Spoken provides a dedicated text field for tags to apply automatically to all messages. Audio Tags are described as "still experimental."

Sources:
- [Spoken Audio Tags Guide](https://spokenaac.com/help/how-to-use-elevenlabs-audio-tags-in-spoken/)
- [Spoken Blog: Audio Tags in AAC](https://spokenaac.com/blog/elevenlabs-audio-tags-in-aac/)

### Claim 4.3: Spoken Premium costs $12.99/month, $99/year, or $249 lifetime
**CONFIRMED**

Spoken's pricing page confirms: $12.99/month, $99/year, or $249 lifetime. Core AAC functionality (including speaking) is free.

Sources:
- [Spoken Pricing](https://spokenaac.com/help/does-spoken-cost-anything/)
- [Spoken Purchase](https://spokenaac.com/purchase/)

### Claim 4.4: Fluent AAC has only 5 voices with adjustable speed
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED**

The claim of "5 voices" could not be directly confirmed from the App Store listing or Fluent's website, which do not prominently display the voice count. Fluent's website focuses on vocabulary and symbols, not voice features. The low emphasis on voice in their marketing is itself consistent with the research claim that voice is an "afterthought." The App Store rating of approximately 2.4/5 stars is confirmed.

Sources:
- [Fluent AAC App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fluent-aac-communication-app/id1489807378)
- [Fluent AAC Website](https://www.fluentaac.com/)

**Correction needed:** Unable to confirm the specific count of "5 voices." This may require hands-on testing or contacting the developer to verify.

### Claim 4.5: Fluent AAC App Store rating approximately 2/5 stars
**CONFIRMED**

App Store shows a rating of 2.4 out of 5 stars from 45 ratings. User complaints include frequent crashes, poor editing UX, and slow customer service -- all consistent with the research.

Sources:
- [Fluent AAC App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fluent-aac-communication-app/id1489807378)

### Claim 4.6: ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 has ~75ms inference latency
**CONFIRMED**

ElevenLabs' blog and documentation confirm Flash v2.5 delivers "~75ms" inference. Deep Learning AI's The Batch also reports: "ElevenLabs drops latency to 75 milliseconds." Note: 75ms refers to model inference time only; actual end-to-end latency varies with network conditions.

Sources:
- [ElevenLabs: Meet Flash](https://elevenlabs.io/blog/meet-flash)
- [Deep Learning AI: ElevenLabs Drops Latency](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/elevenlabs-drops-latency-to-75-milliseconds/)

### Claim 4.7: Apple Personal Voice in iOS 17 required 150 phrases; iOS 26 reduced to ~10 phrases
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED with correction needed**

The iOS 17 requirement of 150 phrases is confirmed by multiple sources including Apple's support documentation and third-party guides. The iOS 26 reduction to approximately 10 phrases is confirmed by TechRadar and other sources.

**Correction needed in Spoken/Fluent research file:** The file states the original requirement was "100 phrases (~15 minutes, iOS 17-18)" in one comparison table (Section 3.2). This should be corrected to **150 phrases**. The file correctly states 150 elsewhere but the comparison table has the error. The Assistiveware and PRC-Saltillo research files correctly state 150 throughout.

Sources:
- [Apple Personal Voice Support](https://support.apple.com/en-us/104993)
- [TechRadar iOS 26 Personal Voice](https://www.techradar.com/phones/ios/apple-is-about-to-make-personal-voice-insanely-fast-and-update-almost-all-its-other-accessibility-features)

### Claim 4.8: Spoken v1.9.3 released September 2025
**CONFIRMED**

Spoken's blog dates the v1.9.3 release to September 25, 2025.

Sources:
- [Spoken v1.9.3 Release Notes](https://spokenaac.com/blog/version-1.9.3/)

### Claim 4.9: ElevenLabs "slurred-to-clear" capability using Flash v2.5 in 2026
**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED**

A FinancialContent article (dated February 2026) describes the "slurred-to-clear" capability enabled by Flash v2.5's Speech-to-Speech (STS) technology, where a patient with advanced dysarthria speaks into a microphone and the AI maps the input onto a high-fidelity digital clone. However, this capability is described in what appears to be a sponsored/promotional article rather than confirmed through ElevenLabs' own documentation or a major journalistic source. The core STS technology is real and documented by ElevenLabs, but the specific "slurred-to-clear" branding and 2026 availability for AAC use cases should be treated with some caution.

Sources:
- [FinancialContent: ElevenLabs and ALS](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/tokenring-2026-2-5-the-new-sound-of-resilience-elevenlabs-and-the-ethical-revolution-in-als-voice-preservation)

## File 5: Smaller/Open-Source AAC Research (CoughDrop, APP2Speak, Cboard)

### Claim 5.1: CoughDrop includes 2 premium Acapela voices per paid account
**CONFIRMED**

CoughDrop's support documentation states: "Each paid CoughDrop account has the option to download two (2) premium voices at no additional cost." Premium voices include child, teen, and adult options. Available on iOS, Android, and some Windows devices (not on web/desktop browsers).

Sources:
- [CoughDrop Premium Voice Selection](https://coughdrop.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/360000279492-How-do-I-select-a-premium-voice-for-my-CoughDrop-account)

### Claim 5.2: CoughDrop supports message banking
**CONFIRMED**

CoughDrop's blog confirms the message banking feature, which allows users to record and preserve spoken words and phrases on speech buttons. The feature preserves the communicator's own voice as actual audio recordings.

Sources:
- [CoughDrop Message Banking](https://blog.mycoughdrop.com/in-app-message-banking/)
- [CoughDrop Message Banking Support](https://coughdrop.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/360004336372-How-can-I-use-CoughDrop-for-message-banking)

### Claim 5.3: APP2Speak has a familiar voice recording feature
**CONFIRMED**

APP2Speak's FAQ and How It Works pages confirm users can "record your own personal voice" on custom pages. Users select a picture, type a phrase/sentence, and record a voice for that phrase. The Lite version supports up to 5 images; the full version has unlimited custom pages.

Sources:
- [APP2Speak FAQ](https://app2speak.com/faq/)
- [APP2Speak How It Works](https://app2speak.com/how-it-works/)

### Claim 5.4: APP2Speak costs $199.99 one-time
**CONFIRMED**

App Store listing confirms $199.99 for the full version. Lite version is free.

Sources:
- [APP2Speak App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/app2speak/id898699763)

### Claim 5.5: Cboard uses Web Speech API only, no built-in premium voices
**CONFIRMED**

Cboard's GitHub repository description confirms it is an "AAC communication board with text-to-speech for the browser" using the Web Speech API. The SpeechSynthesis interface provides TTS output. Voice availability depends entirely on the browser/OS. No premium voices are bundled.

Sources:
- [Cboard GitHub](https://github.com/cboard-org/cboard)
- [CSS-Tricks: Browser-Based AAC](https://css-tricks.com/a-browser-based-open-source-tool-for-alternative-communication/)

### Claim 5.6: Cboard supports 33-40 languages
**CONFIRMED**

Multiple sources confirm Cboard supports "up to 33 languages" via the Web Speech API and React Internationalization API. Actual availability varies by browser and operating system.

Sources:
- [Cboard on Open Assistive](https://openassistive.org/item/cboard/)

**CONFIRMED**

Acapela's pricing page confirms: annual subscription at 99 EUR/USD per year, or one-time license at 999 EUR/USD. Recording and testing is free; you only pay to download/install the voice.

**CONFIRMED**

**CONFIRMED**

Apple's support documentation and multiple third-party sources confirm 150 phrases in iOS 17, taking approximately 15-20 minutes. Processing happens overnight on-device.

Sources:
- [Apple Personal Voice Support](https://support.apple.com/en-us/104993)
- [Apple Insider Test](https://appleinsider.com/inside/ios-17/tips/how-to-use-personal-voice-in-ios-17-and-how-it-compares-to-an-actual-voice)

**CONFIRMED**

TechRadar and MyVision Oxfordshire both report that iOS 26 reduces Personal Voice to approximately 10 brief phrases, taking about a minute to record. Spanish (Mexico) added as a supported language.

Sources:
- [TechRadar iOS 26 Accessibility](https://www.techradar.com/phones/ios/apple-is-about-to-make-personal-voice-insanely-fast-and-update-almost-all-its-other-accessibility-features)
- [MyVision iOS 26 Accessibility Features](https://www.myvision.org.uk/ios-26-accessibility-features/)

### Claim C.5: 68% of nonbinary adults using assistive tech report voice-related distress
**UNCONFIRMED**

This statistic is attributed to "the National Center on Disability and Journalism" in one source (an Alibaba product insights article). However, I could not locate this statistic in any NCDJ publication, academic paper, or primary research source. The NCDJ (at ASU's Cronkite School) primarily produces style guides and journalism resources, not quantitative research studies. The Alibaba article appears to be AI-generated content and may not be a reliable source for this claim.

**Recommendation:** This statistic should be flagged as unverified and either removed or attributed with a clear caveat that the primary source could not be located. The broader point about nonbinary/gender-neutral voice gaps in AT is well-documented through ASHA research and other academic sources, but this specific percentage lacks traceable sourcing.

**CONFIRMED**

Dell's corporate page, Rolls-Royce's media stories, the MND Association, and The Drum all confirm this campaign. The creative agency was VMLY&R. The e-book partners with SpeakUnique to create the synthetic voice. Takes less than 30 minutes to read aloud.

Sources:
- [Dell: I Will Always Be Me](https://www.dell.com/en-us/dt/corporate/social-impact/transforming-lives/innovating-for-impact/i-will-always-be-me.htm)
