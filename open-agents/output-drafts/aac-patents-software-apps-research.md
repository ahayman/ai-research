## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY <a name="executive-summary"></a>

The AAC software patent landscape is dominated by a small number of players, with **Semantic Compaction Systems (SCS) / Prentke Romich Company (PRC)** holding the most aggressively enforced patent portfolio in the space. Their patents around dynamic keyboards, polysemous (multi-meaning) icon sequences, and semantic compaction (Minspeak) have been used in litigation against at least one competitor (Speak for Yourself) and are actively maintained and expanded.

**Key Findings:**

- **Semantic Compaction Systems / PRC-Saltillo** holds the most extensive and actively enforced AAC-specific patent portfolio (~15+ patents covering icon-based communication, dynamic keyboards, and semantic compaction)
- **Tobii AB** (parent of Tobii Dynavox) holds the **world's largest eye tracking patent portfolio** (1,000+ patents/applications, 200+ granted US patents) -- critical for eye-gaze AAC access
- **DynaVox Systems LLC** (now part of Tobii Dynavox) holds patents on speech generating devices, wearable SGDs, and eye gaze controllers
- **Microsoft** holds patents on AAC-related technologies including social cue proxies for SGDs and conversation throughput enhancement
- **Speak for Yourself LLC** holds a patent on their AAC language system (licensed from SCS/PRC after litigation)
- **Forbes AAC** claims "numerous patented innovations" for AAC devices (ProSlate, WinSlate)
- **Spoken Inc.** holds a patent on predictive speech communication using context-aware systems
- **AssistiveWare, Smartbox (Thinksmartbox), Therapy Box, CoughDrop, Avaz, Attainment Company (GoTalk)** -- No specific software patents found in public searches. These companies appear to rely primarily on **trade secrets, trademarks, and copyright** rather than patents.

**CRITICAL WARNING:** The SCS/PRC patent portfolio covering dynamic keyboards and icon sequencing is the single biggest patent risk for any company building an AAC app with symbol-based communication. Their willingness to litigate (as demonstrated in the Speak for Yourself case) makes this an area requiring careful legal analysis.

## 2. CRITICAL PATENT HOLDERS & THEIR PORTFOLIOS <a name="critical-patent-holders"></a>

### 2.1 Semantic Compaction Systems, Inc. (SCS) / Prentke Romich Company (PRC-Saltillo)

**RISK LEVEL: HIGHEST**

SCS is the single most important patent holder in the AAC software space. Founded by Bruce Baker, who invented Minspeak -- the only patented system for semantic compaction. SCS and PRC have a deep, decades-long partnership, with PRC licensing SCS technology for their devices.

**Key Technology Covered:** Polysemous (multi-meaning) icon sequences, dynamic keyboards, semantic compaction, symbol-based vocabulary encoding, word prediction using symbol chunks, tactile symbol sequences.

**Patent Law Firm:** Harness IP (formerly Harness, Dickey & Pierce) -- retained to "identify, prosecute and protect SCS patents from challenges both in the U.S. and abroad" and to "fend off others trying to take his valuable symbol sequencing technique, leading to expanded licensing efforts and decreased competition."

**PRC Device Patents (from prentrom.com/patents):**
- **Accent devices:** US 7,177,797 B1; US 7,194,404 B1; US 7,318,019 B1; US 8,577,279 B2; US 9,202,298 B2; US 9,208,594 B2; US 9,239,824 B2
- **PRiO and Via Pro devices:** US 9,202,298 B2; US 9,208,594 B2; US 9,239,824 B2

### 2.2 Tobii AB / Tobii Dynavox

**RISK LEVEL: HIGH (for eye tracking integration)**

Tobii holds the **world's largest eye tracking patent portfolio** comprising over 1,000 patents and patent applications worldwide, including over 200 granted US patents. This covers hardware design, algorithms, user experience concepts, calibration, illumination, and system design for both screen-based and wearable eye tracking.

**IP Licensing Contact:** iplicensing@tobii.com

### 2.3 DynaVox Systems LLC (subsidiary of Tobii Dynavox)

**RISK LEVEL: MEDIUM**

DynaVox has filed at least 4 patents specifically related to speech generating devices.

### 2.4 Microsoft Technology Licensing LLC

**RISK LEVEL: LOW-MEDIUM**

Microsoft holds patents related to AAC conversation enhancement but has not demonstrated aggressive enforcement in the AAC space.

### 2.5 Forbes AAC

**RISK LEVEL: LOW-MEDIUM**

Forbes AAC claims "numerous patented innovations" including patented device accessories (FlexABLE Handle, etc.) and proprietary technologies (Enable Eyes eye-tracking software, WinSlate Dashboard, CoreWord language system, SoundPOD speaker). Specific patent numbers not publicly listed.

### 2.6 Spoken Inc.

**RISK LEVEL: LOW**

Holds patent on predictive speech communication using context awareness. Relevant for text-based AAC apps with prediction features.

### PATENT 1: US4661916A - The Original Minspeak Patent
- **Title:** System for method for producing synthetic plural word messages
- **Patent Number:** US 4,661,916 A
- **Assignee:** Bruce R. Baker / Semantic Compaction Systems
- **Inventors:** Bruce R. Baker
- **Filing Date:** ~1984 (process initiated August 10, 1980)
- **Grant Date:** April 28, 1987
- **Status:** EXPIRED (20-year term from filing date)
- **Google Patents:** https://patents.google.com/patent/US4661916A/en
- **Description:** The foundational Minspeak patent. Covers a linguistic coding system with a keyboard using polysemic (many-meaning) symbols. A synthetic speech device allowing users to go directly from thought to speech. The keyboard utilizes polysemic symbols on respective keys; by designating a primary message theme key, stored plural word messages can be retrieved by actuating key combinations to vary the context of the polysemic symbols.
- **Related Products:** All Minspeak-based AAC devices (PRC TouchTalker, LightTalker, and successors)
- **Relevance to AAC:** HIGH -- foundational patent for icon-sequence-based AAC
- **Note:** While expired, continuation patents extend coverage of related concepts.

### Case 1: Semantic Compaction Systems & Prentke Romich v. Speak for Yourself LLC (2012)

**THE MOST IMPORTANT AAC PATENT CASE IN HISTORY**

**Parties:**
- Plaintiffs: Semantic Compaction Systems, Inc. and Prentke Romich Company, Inc.
- Defendant: Speak for Yourself LLC (founded by Heidi LoStracco and Renee Collender, two speech-language pathologists from New Jersey)

**Patents at Issue:**
- US 5,748,177 -- Dynamic keyboard and method for dynamically redefining keys on a keyboard
- US 5,920,303 -- Dynamic keyboard and method for dynamically redefining keys on a keyboard (continuation)

**Timeline:**
- **February 2012:** SCS/PRC file patent infringement lawsuit against Speak for Yourself
- **March-April 2012:** Story goes viral. Parents of 4-year-old Maya Nieder (who depends on SFY to communicate due to weak oral motor muscles) publicly advocate. Coverage by TIME, PCWorld, The Register, Techdirt, Huffington Post.
- **June 2012:** Apple removes Speak for Yourself from App Store based on takedown request from patent holders. Google also removes it. No injunction had been issued -- this was done voluntarily by Apple/Google based on the takedown notice alone.
- **October 2012:** Settlement reached.

**Settlement Terms:**
- SCS/PRC dropped ALL claims and takedown notices against SFY
- SFY agreed to **license** the two patents and certain copyrights from SCS
- SFY agreed to **pay** to be a license-holder (specific amount confidential)
- SCS/PRC agreed to **never again** issue takedown requests to stores hosting SFY
- SCS/PRC required to **contact previous takedown recipients and request reinstatement**
- Full terms confidential

**Key Takeaways for New Entrants:**
1. SCS/PRC **will** litigate against AAC software competitors
2. They successfully got an app removed from the App Store **without an injunction** -- just a takedown notice
3. Even after settlement, SFY had to become a **licensee** and pay for the privilege
4. The dynamic keyboard patents (now expired) were the key weapons
5. **However:** Both litigated patents (US5748177 and US5920303) have now expired (20-year terms from 1995 and 1997 filing dates respectively). New SCS patents (US8234589, US9229925, US9286884, etc.) cover different but related concepts.

## 6. RISK ASSESSMENT FOR NEW MARKET ENTRANTS <a name="risk-assessment"></a>

### HIGH RISK AREAS (Avoid or License)

1. **Polysemous Icon Sequencing / Semantic Compaction (Minspeak)**
   - Owner: Semantic Compaction Systems / PRC
   - Risk: Multiple active patents, proven willingness to litigate
   - Mitigation: Do NOT implement multi-meaning icon sequences where icons have different meanings based on combination/sequence. Use single-meaning icons/symbols instead.

2. **Dynamic Keyboard Redefinition for AAC**
   - Owner: Semantic Compaction Systems (patents now expired, but newer related patents exist)
   - Risk: While the core dynamic keyboard patents (US5748177, US5920303) have expired, newer patents (US8234589, US9229925, US9286884) cover related concepts including symbol previewing, dictionary databases, and tactile symbols.
   - Mitigation: Carefully analyze newer SCS patents before implementing dynamic symbol grids. Standard "folder navigation" UI may be distinguishable from patented dynamic keyboard approach.

3. **Eye Tracking Integration**
   - Owner: Tobii AB (1,000+ patents)
   - Risk: Tobii actively licenses eye tracking IP
   - Mitigation: Use Tobii's licensed SDK/hardware, or ensure any alternative eye tracking implementation doesn't infringe. Contact iplicensing@tobii.com.

### MEDIUM RISK AREAS (Proceed with Caution)

4. **Context-Aware Word/Phrase Prediction for AAC**
   - Owner: Spoken Inc. (US11145313B2)
   - Risk: Patent covers predicting words/phrases based on user context (location, situation)
   - Mitigation: Implement standard word prediction (based on usage frequency, n-gram models) rather than context/location-based prediction. If implementing context-aware features, review this patent carefully.

5. **Cloud/Network-Based AAC Content Delivery**
   - Patent: US20090300503A1 (network-based augmentative communication)
   - Risk: Covers server-client architecture for AAC content retrieval
   - Mitigation: If this patent was granted, review claims carefully. Many modern AAC apps use cloud-based content.

6. **Speech Generating Device Architecture**
   - Owner: DynaVox Systems LLC (US8004398B2)
   - Risk: Covers general SGD architecture with message construction, speech synthesis, and multi-modal output
   - Mitigation: This patent is broadly written. Review specific claims; many implementations may be distinguishable.

7. **Social Cue / Emotional Proxy Displays**
   - Owner: Microsoft (US9679497, US10262555B2)
   - Risk: Covers dual-display SGDs with emotional/social proxy displays
   - Mitigation: Avoid implementing a separate "conversation partner display" showing emotional proxies

### LOW RISK AREAS (Generally Safe)

8. **Standard Symbol Grid Communication**
   - Using a grid of symbols where each symbol has ONE fixed meaning (not polysemous/multi-meaning) appears to be outside the core SCS patent claims
   - Standard "tap a picture, it says a word" functionality is widespread and predates most patents

9. **Text-to-Speech AAC (Text-Based)**
   - Simple text input with TTS output is well-established technology
   - Word prediction based on standard frequency/language models is broadly available
   - No specific AAC patents found blocking this approach

10. **Open Board Format / OBF / OpenAAC Standards**
    - OpenAAC initiative promotes open standards for AAC
    - Using open formats for board/vocabulary sharing appears patent-free

11. **Standard Word Prediction**
    - T9-style and standard keyboard prediction patents (Nuance/Tegic) are separate from AAC
    - Basic word prediction in AAC apps using standard NLP is generally safe

## 7. OPEN SOURCE & PATENT-FREE ZONES <a name="open-source"></a>

### CoughDrop Open Source Code
- **Repository:** github.com/coughdrop/coughdrop (now under OpenAAC account)
- **License:** Originally open source; post-Forbes-AAC acquisition, new updates are proprietary
- **Patent Risk:** CoughDrop itself does not appear to hold patents, but using the code does not grant patent licenses from other patent holders (e.g., if any CoughDrop feature inadvertently infringes a third-party patent)

### OpenAAC Initiative
- **Website:** openaac.org
- **Purpose:** Getting high-quality AAC into as many hands as possible through open standards and social awareness
- **GitHub:** github.com/open-aac
- **Standards:** Open Board Format (OBF) for interoperable AAC boards
- **Patent Risk:** LOW -- promotes open standards, but does not provide patent indemnification

### PECS (Picture Exchange Communication System)
- **Note:** PECS is a registered trademark of Pyramid Educational Consultants, NOT a patented technology per se
- **Method:** PECS is a behavioral training methodology based on B.F. Skinner's Verbal Behavior
- **Risk:** Trademark only -- you cannot call your system "PECS" but you can implement picture-exchange communication methodology

## 8. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS <a name="strategic-recommendations"></a>

### For a Company Entering the AAC Space:

1. **MUST DO: Formal Patent Freedom-to-Operate (FTO) Analysis**
   - Commission a patent attorney to perform a full FTO analysis against all active SCS/PRC patents
   - Focus especially on: US8234589, US9229925, US9286884, US7177797, US7318019
   - Cost: $15,000-50,000+ but essential insurance

2. **DESIGN AROUND SCS Patents:**
   - Use single-meaning symbols (not polysemous/multi-meaning icon sequences)
   - If using folder/category navigation rather than "dynamic keyboard redefinition," document why this differs from patented approach
   - Avoid implementing "semantic compaction" or anything resembling Minspeak's icon-combination approach

3. **Eye Tracking:**
   - If integrating eye tracking, use Tobii's SDK (which includes patent licenses) OR
   - Use Apple's built-in accessibility features (head tracking, eye tracking in iOS) which Apple may cover under their own licenses
   - Do NOT build custom eye tracking without reviewing Tobii's 1,000+ patent portfolio

4. **Word Prediction:**
   - Standard NLP-based word prediction (frequency, n-gram, transformer-based) appears safe
   - Avoid context-aware prediction that determines user location/situation to predict words (Spoken Inc. patent US11145313B2)
   - If implementing AI/LLM-based prediction, this is likely novel enough to be distinguishable from existing patents

5. **Architecture:**
   - Cloud-based AAC content delivery is common but review US20090300503A1 if it was granted
   - Standard client-server architecture for syncing user data across devices should be distinguishable

6. **Monitor SCS/PRC:**
   - Set up patent monitoring alerts for new filings by Semantic Compaction Systems and Prentke Romich Company
   - Watch for any new litigation they initiate

7. **Consider Defensive Patent Filing:**
   - If developing novel AAC technology (AI-based prediction, novel UI paradigms, etc.), consider filing defensive patents
   - This provides bargaining chips in case of future patent disputes
