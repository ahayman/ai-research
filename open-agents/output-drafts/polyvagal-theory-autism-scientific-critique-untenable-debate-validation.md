### Claim 12: "In mammals, approximately 80% of cardiac vagal preganglionic neurons are located in the nucleus ambiguus (cats); ~60% in monkeys."
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (8/10) — directionally correct, species-variant
- **Evidence for**: Multiple sources (ScienceDirect ambiguus nucleus topic page, primary physiology literature) confirm the cat figure of ~80% NA. Monkey figure of ~60% is the commonly cited value (Cheng & Powley work). The general finding that NA dominates cardiac vagal control in mammals is robust.
- **Evidence against**: There is no single universal percentage — values are species-specific and methodology-dependent. Human values are typically inferred from comparative data because direct counts in humans are not feasible. "Most" or "majority" or "predominantly" is the safest phrasing.
- **Why uncertain**: The percentages are *real values from real species* but presenting them as if they apply uniformly across mammals (especially as a "human" figure) is precision creep. The conclusion that NA dominates cardiac vagal control in mammals is the load-bearing point and is well-supported.
- **Sources**: [ScienceDirect — Ambiguus Nucleus](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/ambiguus-nucleus)
- **Notes**: Downstream writing should explicitly cite species, e.g., "approximately 80% in cats, 60% in monkeys" rather than imply a universal human figure.

### Claim 16: "Porges's response argues critics ignore qualitative differences between NA and DMN projections (embryological origin, myelination, transcriptomic profile — citing Coverdell et al. 2019, Jalil et al. 2023, Strain et al. 2024)."
- **Verdict**: UNCERTAIN (7/10) — Porges's framing is verifiable; the cited supporting papers are real but their interpretation is contested
- **Evidence for**: Polyvagal Institute and Porges 2026 cite these papers explicitly. Coverdell et al. 2019 is a real paper on transcriptomic differentiation of vagal motor neurons. Strain et al. 2024 (optogenetic work) is real but its interpretation as PVT-supporting is the Polyvagal Institute's framing, not necessarily the original authors' framing.
- **Evidence against**: Critics would say transcriptomic differences between NA and DMN don't restore PVT's *functional* claims (that DMN mediates freeze, NA mediates social engagement). The qualitative-differences argument can establish NA ≠ DMN without restoring PVT's specific architecture.
- **Why uncertain**: The claim that these papers exist and Porges cites them is true. The claim that they actually rescue PVT is itself contested.
- **Sources**: [Polyvagal Institute](https://www.polyvagalinstitute.org/criticaldiscussionofpolyvagaltheory)
