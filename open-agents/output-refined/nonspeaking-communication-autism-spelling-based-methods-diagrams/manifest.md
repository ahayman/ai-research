## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Method Lineage
- **Type**: Timeline
- **Elements**: 3 sections (FC, RPM, S2C), 15+ events spanning 1977-2024
- **Key Insight**: FC, RPM, and S2C share the fundamental structure of a facilitator controlling the communication display; the central question is whether the lineage represents genuine evolution or rebranding
- **Accessibility**: Three-section timeline. FC (1977-1995): Crossley develops FC, Biklen imports, controlled studies debunk. RPM (~1991-2019): Soma develops for Tito, moves to US, empty systematic review. S2C (~2013-2024): Vosseller formalizes, I-ASC founded, Jaswal studies, Spellers documentary, Telepathy Tapes.

### Diagram 2: Session Process
- **Type**: Flowchart (annotated sequence)
- **Elements**: 6 observable steps (blue) with 6 facilitator decision points (amber)
- **Key Insight**: At every step of the process, the facilitator makes decisions that could influence the output -- from content presentation through board positioning, letter interpretation, and pacing cues
- **Accessibility**: Six sequential steps with parallel annotations. Step 1: content presentation (facilitator chooses content). Step 2: question posed (facilitator determines expected answer). Step 3: board held up (facilitator controls position). Step 4: pointing begins (facilitator interprets which letter). Step 5: letter announced (facilitator provides pacing cues). Step 6: message attributed (authorship question).

### Diagram 3: Evidence Standoff
- **Type**: Flowchart (dual interpretation)
- **Elements**: 5 observations, 5 proponent interpretations, 5 critic interpretations
- **Key Insight**: The same observable facts admit two radically different interpretations; this ambiguity is precisely why controlled authorship studies are needed
- **Accessibility**: Five observations each split to two interpretations. Pointing to letters: motor support (pro) vs. guided pointing (crit). Complex messages: hidden competence (pro) vs. facilitator authorship (crit). Eye-tracking: agency (pro) vs. expected behavior (crit). Empty reviews: not studied properly (pro) vs. cannot pass tests (crit). Independent typing: proof (pro) vs. needs verification (crit).

### Diagram 4: Jaswal Studies
- **Type**: Flowchart (claim-response)
- **Elements**: 2 study subgraphs with 3 and 2 claim-critique pairs respectively, status assessment
- **Key Insight**: The Jaswal studies are the most cited research by proponents but have received substantive published critiques; neither settles the authorship question
- **Accessibility**: Two study sections. 2020 eye-tracking (N=9): three claims (fixation, production patterns, agency) each paired with published critique. 2024 literacy (N=31): two claims (literacy patterns, no formal instruction) each paired with critiques about practice effects and task validity. Status: no controlled authorship test has ever been published.

### Diagram 5: Independence Continuum
- **Type**: Flowchart (horizontal progression)
- **Elements**: 5 stages from supported to independent, facilitator control annotations, verification note
- **Key Insight**: Authorship verification is only possible at stages where the facilitator does not control the communication display (Stages 4-5); the transition from unverifiable to verifiable stages has never been systematically documented
- **Accessibility**: Five stages left to right. Stage 1: stencil board (high facilitator control). Stage 2: laminated letterboard (high). Stage 3: board on table (moderate). Stage 4: keyboard/iPad (low). Stage 5: independent typing (none, verifiable). Key note: transition never documented with controlled testing.
