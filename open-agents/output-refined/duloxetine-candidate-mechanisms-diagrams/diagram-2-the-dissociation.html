Two effects. Opposite directions. Same drug, same person, same day.

This is the fact the whole page exists to explain, and it is the fact that rules out the easy explanations before any pharmacology is needed.

The Saturday
"The acute sensory overwhelm is worse... but I'm not crashing after the incident, which I normally would." And: "Its lack was very stark, not just to me but to my whole family. We all braced for it, but it never came."
What the shape rules out, before any pharmacology

NOT SEDATION

Sedation predicts the acute overwhelm would be milder. It was worse.

NOT EMOTIONAL BLUNTING

Blunting predicts both halves get quieter. One got louder.

NOT GLOBAL MUTING

A volume knob cannot turn two things in opposite directions at once.

NOT THE SHAPE EXPECTANCY PREDICTS

A hopeful person on a new drug reports the hard day feeling easier. He reported it worse, and the absence was independently anticipated by his family.

So the question is not "why did the episodes stop." It is narrower and stranger: why is the overwhelm DECOUPLED from the crash? A single mechanism cannot move two things in opposite directions, which is why every single-mechanism account on the candidate list fails on one half or the other.
