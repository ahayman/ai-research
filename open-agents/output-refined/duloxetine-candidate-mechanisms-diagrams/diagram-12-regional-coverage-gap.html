The three regions where both drugs have been measured show parity, so his hypothesis gets no support there. And every region the dissociation literature implicates — insula, anterior cingulate, prefrontal cortex — has never been measured for duloxetine at any dose, by anyone. [primary, verified] So the reason his hypothesis cannot be refuted is not that it is slippery. It is that duloxetine has been imaged in three brain regions in twenty years, and none of them is anywhere dissociation lives. He guessed this might not be answerable. He was right, and the reason is a fact about funding priorities rather than about his reasoning.
Why "slight difference, outsized effect" is not hand-waving
The amplifier is real, named and published.

The dorsal raphe holds the somatodendritic autoreceptors. Occupying the transporter there raises serotonin at those autoreceptors → raphe firing slows → serotonin release falls everywhere downstream. So a gradient at one small nucleus can change the sign of a drug's effect across the whole projection field, not merely its size. That is Kim 2017's own proposed explanation for why every SSRI takes weeks to work [primary, verified], and there is outcome-level support that the brake can win: at clinically relevant single doses in humans, cortical serotonin may go DOWN on initiation. [primary, verified — Finnema 2015] A second lab states his assumption almost verbatim as established preclinical fact: "regionally varying SERT activity and antidepressant drug concentration." [primary, verified — Baldinger 2014]

Three things against its own interest. He proposed it, which is exactly why it is not inflated.
The strict version, because he is owed it rather than a flattering one.
  1. As stated it is a schema with twenty-eight free parameters. Fourteen regions times two directions. Every result fits: if duloxetine helps it concentrated somewhere good; if it fails it did not. A story that cannot lose is not doing work, and that is why this candidate scores silent on his acute worsening rather than earning a tick for accommodating it. Scoring flexibility as fit is the move this page exists to refuse.
  2. The best regional data runs against the intuitive version. Escitalopram occupies the amygdala and subgenual cingulate MORE than the whole-brain average, not less. [primary, verified — Baldinger 2014] So "escitalopram failed because it never reached the emotional regions" is unavailable. He would need the opposite story, and a reason to pick it other than that it fits.
  3. The one comparison that favours it is an artifact. Duloxetine's midbrain 85% against escitalopram's 75% is a ten-point gap in the raphe-containing region pointing exactly the way he would want. It is PET against SPECT, two tracers, two labs, and the modality effect alone is several times the gap. It is the identical structure of the "escitalopram delivers less serotonin" claim on this page's dead list. The researcher nearly published it as support and stopped only on a deliberate check. That the hypothesis reproduces this project's signature error on first contact is real information about it.
What would settle it. A duloxetine [11C]DASB study reporting dorsal raphe and insula occupancy against Kim 2017's escitalopram data at matched plasma exposure. It names the region, the direction and the mechanism, and it predicts a number. The experiment exists as a design and has never been run. It costs roughly the price of a used car. That is a good place for a hypothesis to be, and it is a better place than candidate 4, which was hired for the same hole and has been tested twice and failed twice. [no evidence exists]
