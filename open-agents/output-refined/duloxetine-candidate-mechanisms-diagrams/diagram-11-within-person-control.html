Three warnings, here rather than underneath, because this is the strongest claim on the page
1. The best-matched pair is disputed inside this project's own records. The escitalopram figure is attributed to Arakawa 2016. The binding correction brief says that value is not in the primary and retires the comparison in both directions; the most recent research pass says it re-verified it and it is there. Two project documents disagree about whether the number was ever checked. [secondary — unverified] The argument below does not use it as load-bearing, and that is deliberate.
2. The curve-fit row is cross-modality. Escitalopram's curve is fitted from SPECT, midbrain; duloxetine's from PET, thalamus. Taken alone it is the same artifact class as the "escitalopram delivers less serotonin" entry in the graveyard. It is here as a convergence, not a proof.
3. What is within-person is the OUTCOME, not the exposure. He was never scanned. Every occupancy number above comes from other people's brains, and the 8.5-fold individual EC50 spread that makes his own body the best available control is the same spread that makes those borrowed figures loose when applied to him. The escitalopram side is also retrospective, unblinded, and about a year old. Indistinguishable is not identical.

What survives all three, and why it is enough. The claim that escitalopram dissociated him by delivering more serotonin is already dead on this page, and it died because this comparison cannot demonstrate a difference between these two drugs. That is the same finding read forwards: you cannot explain a difference in outcome by appealing to a comparison that cannot show a difference. [reasoning, not evidence]

So an occupancy-magnitude account now requires positing that duloxetine delivered substantially less engagement to him specifically than escitalopram did, despite every published estimate saying they match, and in exactly the direction that saves the theory. That rescue is available, because individual variation is real and large. It is also unmeasured, unmotivated, and it needs him to sit at opposite extremes of two different drugs' individual curves by coincidence.

The verdict, at the strength the evidence supports and no higher. Not "occupancy is definitively refuted." Occupancy is unsupported as the explanation, and left resting entirely on a rescue nobody can check. What makes it strong is not certainty. It is that the rival account has nowhere left to stand.

And it does not stand alone, which is what actually settles it

LEG 1 · HIS OWN CONTROL

Within-person. Imprecise. Two matched initiations, opposite outcomes, n=1, exposure imputed from other people's scans, escitalopram side retrospective.

LEG 2 · THE GATING LADDER

Between-person. Precise. A 3.7-point occupancy step, blind, placebo-controlled, prospective, planned in advance, flipped the human sensory filter from intact to disrupted. [primary, verified ×2]

THEY FAIL IN OPPOSITE DIRECTIONS

One is within-person and loose; the other is between-person and tight. They share nothing except their conclusion. That convergence, not either leg alone, is why this is a finding rather than a hypothesis.

Whose finding this is. He reasoned to the premise from first principles and wrote that duloxetine simply works differently from escitalopram, and that this alone was enough. An earlier research pass had reached the same place from the literature and recorded it as a hypothesis. His corrected history is what turned it into a demonstration, and the evidence needed to do that had been in the corpus the whole time, unassembled. What it establishes is negative and solid: serotonin transporter occupancy is not the variable. It does not say what is. [no evidence exists]
