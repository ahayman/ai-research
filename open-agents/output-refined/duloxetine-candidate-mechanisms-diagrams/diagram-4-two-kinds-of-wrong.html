Killed by heterogeneity

NOT DEAD

What happened
A trial found no average effect. A population did not respond.
Does individual variation rescue it?
Yes. A null average is a statement about a mean. You are not a mean.
Honest label
"Untested in anyone like you." Not "refuted."

The practical consequence, and it is the point. Almost nothing on this page died because a trial came out negative. The things that died, died of arithmetic. That is a much shorter list than eight passes of hypothesis-killing would suggest, and it is why "possibly valid" is the correct ceiling for nearly everything that remains. [reasoning, not evidence]
He was right and the project proved it by flip-flopping eight times. The failure mode was not a chain of increasing accuracy. It was alternating over-correction: each pass correctly diagnosed its predecessor's methodological sin, then committed the same sin in reverse. Recency is not reliability, and the ninth pass has no special claim either.
