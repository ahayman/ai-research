Transfer Map: Helps / Hurts / New

For English speakers learning German -- what to leverage, what to unlearn, what to build from scratch

Positive Transfer
English knowledge that directly helps
Cognate Vocabulary
house = Haus, water = Wasser, finger = Finger. Hundreds of transparent pairs in core vocabulary.
Impact
Modal + Bare Infinitive
I can swim = Ich kann schwimmen. Same syntax, direct mapping.
Impact
Strong Verb Ablaut Logic
sing/sang/sung = singen/sang/gesungen. The concept of vowel-changing verbs is already familiar.
Impact
Article Concept
a book / the book = ein Buch / das Buch. Article usage transfers, even though German articles are far more complex.
Impact
Basic SVO Main Clause
I see the dog = Ich sehe den Hund. Main clause SVO matches initially.
Impact
Phrasal Verb / Separable Verb
look up maps to aufschauen, give back maps to zuruckgeben.
Impact
Compound Word Concept
airport ~ Flughafen. English compounds scaffold understanding of German's more productive system.
Impact
Negative Transfer
English habits that actively mislead
SVO Overgeneralization
*weil ich habe keine Zeit → weil ich keine Zeit habe. English SVO habit invades German subordinate clauses and V2 contexts.
Damage
Gender Blindness
No intuition for der/die/das. English speakers cannot "feel" why Madchen (girl) is neuter or Tisch (table) is masculine.
Damage
Case Blindness
*Ich sehe der Hund → Ich sehe den Hund. English encodes roles via position, not morphology. Case markings feel invisible.
Damage
False Friends
Gift = poison (not gift). bekommen = receive (not become). aktuell = current (not actual). Maximum confidence, minimum accuracy.
Damage
Do-Support Habits
*Tust du sprechen Deutsch? → Sprichst du Deutsch? German uses simple inversion, not periphrastic "do."
Damage
Adjective Invariability
Expecting gut to stay "gut" everywhere. Reality: guter, gute, gutes, gutem, guten, depending on case/gender/determiner.
Damage
Verb-Final Resistance
Resisting ...dass ich das Buch gelesen habe. Placing verbs at the end feels "backwards" to English speakers.
Damage
Zero Transfer (New)
No English equivalent -- build from scratch
Modal Particles
doch, mal, halt, ja, schon, eben, wohl. Modify pragmatic force without affecting meaning. No English equivalent at all. "Komm doch mal her!" = untranslatable shade of urgency + casualness.
Novelty
Adjective Paradigm Choice
Strong, weak, or mixed? Depends on what precedes: no article = strong, definite = weak, indefinite = mixed. A three-way system with no English parallel.
Novelty
Gender Assignment Rules
-ung = feminine, -chen = neuter, -er = often masculine. Partial regularities that must be learned as an entirely new system.
Novelty
Sentence Bracket Processing
Hold verb frame open: "Ich habe gestern ein Buch gelesen." Must wait for clause-final verb to resolve meaning. Requires new parsing strategy.
Novelty
Genitive Case
des Mannes, der Frau. Plus genitive-governing prepositions: wegen, trotz, wahrend. No structural equivalent in modern English.
Novelty
Konjunktiv I (Indirect Speech)
Er sagte, er habe keine Zeit. A dedicated mood for reported speech that English handles with "that" clauses and tense backshift.
Novelty

The Same Feature Can Help AND Hurt

SVO helps in main clauses ↔ SVO hurts in subordinate clauses
"Ich sehe den Hund" (SVO helps) vs. "...weil ich den Hund sehe" (SVO habit produces errors)
Cognates accelerate vocabulary ↔ False friends create confident errors
The same mechanism (word similarity) that helps with "Haus/house" traps you with "Gift/poison"
Article concept transfers ↔ Article simplicity misleads
Knowing "the/a" helps understand articles exist, but expecting 2 forms when German has 16 causes constant errors

Lean Into Green

Use cognates and shared structures for rapid early progress. The green zone gives you a running start that learners of unrelated languages do not get.

Build Red Awareness

Actively monitor negative transfer zones. These produce the most persistent fossilized errors. Awareness alone prevents most damage.

Accept Blue as Genuinely New

Modal particles, adjective paradigm choice, and gender rules cannot be derived from English. Accept them as new systems requiring immersion and practice.
