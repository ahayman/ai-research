# Diagram Manifest: German-English Comparative Typology

## Topic
German-English Comparative Typology — Sister Languages, Divergent Paths

## Diagrams

### Diagram 1: Divergence Timeline from Proto-Germanic
- **File**: `diagram-1-divergence-timeline.html`
- **Type**: Dual-track timeline
- **Purpose**: Visualizes how Proto-Germanic split into two radically different typological profiles through 1,500 years of divergent evolution
- **Key elements**: Proto-Germanic ancestor node with full feature list; parallel English (innovative) and German (conservative) tracks; milestone events (Anglo-Saxon settlement, Viking contact, Norman Conquest, Consonant Shift, Luther's Bible); color-coded feature retention (green), loss (red), gain (blue), and change (yellow) tags at each milestone; modern endpoint profiles

### Diagram 2: High German Consonant Shift Sound Correspondence Map
- **File**: `diagram-2-consonant-shift-map.html`
- **Type**: Systematic correspondence table with interactive elements
- **Purpose**: Makes the three consonant shift patterns (p->pf/f, t->z/ss, k->ch) visual and memorable, enabling predictive vocabulary decoding
- **Key elements**: Three color-coded sections; position-dependent conditioning (initial/post-consonant -> affricate vs. medial/final -> fricative); 8-11 example pairs per section with highlighted shifted letters; decoder ring summary; interactive "try it yourself" section with hover-to-reveal answers

### Diagram 3: Feature Inventory — What English Lost / What German Kept
- **File**: `diagram-3-feature-inventory.html`
- **Type**: Comparative ledger diagram
- **Purpose**: Catalogs every major grammatical feature showing German retention vs. English loss, with compensatory innovation arrows
- **Key elements**: Proto-Germanic ancestor row; side-by-side comparison for 10 features (case, gender, V2, adjective declension, ablaut, separable verbs, subjunctive, articles, do-support, progressive); compensatory arrows showing case loss -> rigid SVO and V2 loss -> do-support; green/red/blue color coding

### Diagram 4: Cognate Vocabulary Heat Map
- **File**: `diagram-4-cognate-heat-map.html`
- **Type**: Tiered reliability gradient
- **Purpose**: Shows where cognates are dense and reliable vs. where they thin out and become misleading
- **Key elements**: Seven tiers from green (95%+ reliable: body parts) through yellow (75%+: nature, verbs) to red (low: abstract/institutional) to danger zone (false friends); specific example pairs at each tier; false friend danger zone with 12 flagged pairs showing German word, crossed-out English trap, and real meaning

### Diagram 5: Morphological Synthesis Spectrum
- **File**: `diagram-5-synthesis-spectrum.html`
- **Type**: Horizontal continuum with language cards
- **Purpose**: Places German precisely on the analytic-synthetic continuum relative to English, French, Arabic, Chinese, and other languages
- **Key elements**: Gradient spectrum bar from analytic to synthetic; positioned language markers (Chinese, English, French, German, Arabic, Latin, Turkish, Inuktitut); sample sentences showing information density per word; highlighted English-German gap with feature inventory; morphological type definitions (analytic, fusional, agglutinative, polysynthetic)

### Diagram 6: Transfer Map — Helps / Hurts / New
- **File**: `diagram-6-transfer-map.html`
- **Type**: Three-zone classification with cross-links
- **Purpose**: Practical map of where English knowledge helps, hinders, or is irrelevant when learning German
- **Key elements**: Green zone (7 positive transfer items with impact ratings), red zone (7 negative transfer items with damage ratings), blue zone (6 zero-transfer items with novelty ratings); cross-link section showing how the same feature can help AND hurt; learning strategy recommendations

## Design Notes
- All diagrams use a dark theme (background: #0a0e1a) for consistency
- Color coding is systematic: green = retained/positive, red = lost/negative, blue = gained/new, yellow/gold = German-specific or shifted
- All diagrams are self-contained HTML with no external dependencies
- Responsive design with mobile breakpoints at 768px or 900px
- Font: system-ui stack for cross-platform consistency
