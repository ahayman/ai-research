Why the Gradient Exists

The most reliable cognates come from the oldest vocabulary layer -- words already present in Proto-Germanic before the languages split. Body parts, kinship, numbers, and nature terms are ancient, stable, and shared. As you move toward abstract, institutional, and technical vocabulary, English systematically borrowed from French and Latin (post-1066) while German coined compounds from native Germanic roots. This is why "hospital" has no German cognate (it becomes Krankenhaus = "sick-house") while "hand" is identical in both languages. The older the concept, the more reliable the cognate.
