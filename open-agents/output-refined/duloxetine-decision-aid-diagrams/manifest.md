# Diagram Manifest: Decision Aid for the Appointment (Duloxetine, Category 12)

## The spine these diagrams serve

**The question eight passes never asked: what is the dose increase FOR?** He has answered it: *"the pain and fibromyalgia is important to me. It's just that the psychological effects were unexpected and something I also value."* **Both.**

That answer is what makes this a **genuine two-sided trade** rather than a formality. If only the pain mattered the arithmetic would be simple; if only the psychological effects mattered it would be simple the other way. Wanting both puts him in the one position where the evidence genuinely pulls two ways.

**§10 is absolute on this page: nothing here directs a dose.** Every diagram is framed as what to understand and what to raise. The page **prepares** the conversation; it does not pre-empt it.

## ⚠ Corrections these diagrams carry AGAINST the binding brief

The brief's §1 warns that each pass "correctly diagnoses its predecessor's methodological sin, then commits the same sin in reverse." **Two instances were found in the brief itself.**

### 1. The somnolence dose-step attribution (diagram 4 — material)

> **Brief:** *"Somnolence IS dose-dependent … and rises at the 20→60 step (2.6%→10.2%, 7.8%→14.3%)"*

**Both figures are 30→60 steps, not 20→60.**
- 2.6% → 10.2% = **NCT00849693**, MDD, children/adolescents, **30 vs 60mg**
- 7.8% → 14.3% = **NCT00960986**, MDD, Korean adults, **30 vs 60mg**
- **The only direct 20-vs-60 comparison, NCT00408876, found somnolence FLAT: 5.08% → 4.31%.**

**Why it matters:** the brief's version makes the case against a dose increase look stronger than the evidence supports, on the item the brief itself calls decisive. **The honest picture is mixed and does not resolve:** the direction is label-confirmed (FDA dose-dependence footnote), a real ~2–4× rise is documented **one step above** his, the single direct test **at** his step is null and underpowered (n=59), and the highest figure in the corpus (41%) is from his own diagnosis at the target dose in 29 people, not dose-stratified. All four facts are shown.

### 2. The Nicolini percentages (diagrams 1 and 3)

> **Brief:** *"Nicolini: 60–120mg −15.3/55% vs 20mg −14.7/45%"*

Supplement 1 §2.5 established these percentages are **internally inconsistent** (−14.7 as a 45% decrease implies baseline HAM-A ≈ 32.7; −15.3 as a 55% decrease implies ≈ 27.8; GAD registration trials enrol at ≈24–26, so **both implied baselines are too high and they disagree with each other**) and instructed explicitly: *"Do not put these percentages in front of the reader as facts."*

**These diagrams use the absolute changes (−14.7 vs −15.3) and the response/remission rates (60%/42% vs 65%/44%, placebo 42%/20%) instead**, and state the correction openly on diagram 3.

### 3. An omission restored, and it is the most decision-relevant of the three (diagrams 1 and 4)

The brief says *"20mg ≈ 60mg on every sleep AE"* and puts the emphasis on somnolence. **Both halves are off.** In NCT00408876, **fatigue rises 0% (placebo) → 0% (20mg) → 6.03% (60mg) → 8.93% (120mg)** — supplement 5 calls it *"the only clean monotonic rise across the 20→60 step."*

**Supportable form, now used on the page**: *of insomnia and somnolence, insomnia never rises across this range and somnolence sometimes does — but fatigue is the one that rises at his step.*

**Why it is the headline rather than a footnote**: fatigue is his primary disability, and his valued gain from the drug is **waking less tired**. So the single exception in the table lands precisely on what he is protecting. Promoted to the narrative and to the against column of diagram 1, with the construct caveat attached at the claim (the AE term "fatigue" is not his bone-weary exhaustion, and the equation is not made).

### 4. The 3× multiplier — a dose ratio is not a drive ratio (diagram 1, narrative, new Deep Dive callout)

> **Brief:** *"the Jensen/Oranje ladder says a 1.5× step flipped the filter. He is contemplating 3×."*

**This conflates a dose ratio with a drive ratio on a saturating curve.** Serotonergic engagement plateaus: he is already at **~72% SERT occupancy**, and 60mg is **~88%**, so the step is worth roughly **sixteen occupancy points, not a tripling** `[reasoning, not evidence — Emax, ED50 ≈ 7.9mg]`. Escitalopram 10mg is likewise already high on its own curve, so Jensen-to-Oranje is also a modest engagement step.

**What survives, and it is arguably stronger than the brief's version**: a *small* step in serotonergic engagement was enough to flip P50 gating once. **The concern is live precisely because the step was small.** What does not survive is the multiplier and the false precision it lends in either direction.

### 5. The 30mg option — accepted, with one refinement against the coordinator's compression (diagram 1, new Deep Dive section)

> **Coordinator:** *"20→30mg is the worst trade available: it pays the full price of destroying the clean 20mg experiment while buying a pain effect an adequately powered trial already failed to find."*

**The second half is well supported** (Arnold 2012: 30mg, n=155/153, **P=0.202**). **The first half is overstated and is corrected here.**

**30mg does NOT close the logical window the way 60mg does.** 30mg sits *below* the analgesic threshold, so the two models **still make different predictions there**: his predicts a slow benefit; the standard model predicts none. The discrimination survives in principle. What 30mg actually costs is the **clean baseline** — it resets the clock, adds a variable, and interrupts the slow accumulating trajectory his hypothesis needs him to read. **That is a real cost, and it is not the same cost as 60mg's.** Calling them identical is exactly the tidy overstatement this chain keeps having to walk back.

**Added, because the compression omits it**: there is a perfectly good reason to pass through 30mg that has nothing to do with its own evidence — **titrating for tolerability on the way to 60mg**, which is standard and sensible. **The distinction to carry into the room is 30mg as a WAYPOINT (reasonable) versus 30mg as a DESTINATION (not supported by the trial evidence).**

### 6. Candidate numbering — checked, no propagation found

Supplement 7 carries **two numbering schemes**: Part 1 uses the orchestrator's stale numbering (LC-NE = #1); **Part 3's table is FINAL** (#1 anxiolysis + sleep continuity, #5 tonic/phasic LC-NE, #6 the CFS-aggravation hypothesis). BINDING-CORRECTIONS §5 inherited the stale scheme and attributed the nap-test positive reading to *"candidate 1"*; it belongs to **candidate 5**, and **candidate 1 scores nap-failure ○ — it predicts nothing about napping.**

**No stale attribution reached these diagrams.** The nap material is written mechanistically throughout (*"if it lowered your arousal, latency should fall"*) and never invokes a candidate number, so the collision could not propagate. The only numbered reference anywhere in this build is **"candidate 6"** for the CFS-aggravation hypothesis (diagram 2 / Deep Dive), which is **correct under Part 3**.

### The pattern across all five substantive corrections

**Four of the five (somnolence step, fatigue omission, the 3× multiplier, the 30mg compression) all pushed in the same direction: they made the case against a dose increase look stronger than the evidence supports.** That is worth naming, because §1 predicts exactly this shape and §10 is absolute. **The corrections have been applied without letting them swing the page the other way. Neither column is favoured. No dose is directed.**

## Evidence absences carried (do not soften)

1. **Nobody has studied duloxetine in autistic adults** (one 3-patient case series)
2. **Nobody has studied the overwhelm→crash cycle pharmacologically**
3. **Nobody has studied whether prior dissociation predicts recurrence**
4. **Nobody has studied this decoupling in any human, for any drug**
5. **Nobody has measured what duloxetine, or any SNRI, does to daytime sleep propensity** — no MSLT study at any dose in any population
6. **Nobody has recorded an LC neuron on chronic duloxetine in an awake animal**
7. **No study validates a behavioural dozing-event diary against drug somnolence in any population** — the measurement design on diagram 6 is mechanistic reasoning, not a validated instrument
8. **No study has tested, as a psychophysical question, whether an ME/CFS patient can distinguish sleepiness from fatigue** — his exact problem is real and scientifically unaddressed; do not over-cite Watson as though it settled it
