The Questions

Six things worth asking, in rough order of how much they buy. None of them is "what dose should I take?" — that one you and your doctor work out together, and the rest of this deep dive is the preparation for it.

🖨️ This card is designed to print. Use your browser's print function. The answer boxes are there to fill in during the appointment.

Bring this to the appointment

Duloxetine 20mg · ~4 weeks in · dose discussion
1
"What is my transferrin saturation?"

You are on oral iron with ferritin around 70, which is correct — the IRLSSG threshold for oral iron is ferritin ≤75.

But the same guideline says NO IRON if transferrin saturation is above 45%, and ferritin cannot tell you that.

Worse: ferritin is an acute-phase reactant. With fibromyalgia and ME/CFS it can read falsely high, which means your true iron status may be worse than 70 suggests, not better.

What to actually ask for:
  • Morning, fasting — serum iron and TSAT swing substantially with time of day and food; a random afternoon draw can mislead
  • Ferritin + serum iron + TIBC + %TSAT together, not ferritin alone
  • "What is the number, and is it under 75?"
Why this question and not "did you order the test?": the real-world failure is acting on the result. Panels get ordered 92.1% of the time. Among patients with ferritin ≤75, only 50% actually received iron.
2
"When the time comes, will you write a compounded liquid?"

The taper problem exists at your current dose, right now. It is not created by going up. There is no capsule below 20mg, and the occupancy cliff is below where you already stand.

Ask now, while it is entirely hypothetical and the conversation costs nothing.

Either answer is worth having:
  • Yes → you have removed the largest obstacle from a decision you may never make
  • No, or "we'll just do every other day" → you have learned something important at the cheapest possible moment (and see the interaction card: alternate-day dosing is precisely wrong at a ~12h half-life)
3
"What is this increase for — the pain, or everything?"

The two effects have different dose curves. Pain has a threshold at ~40–60mg that you are underneath. Anxiety is essentially flat from 20mg upward.

Naming the target changes the answer. This is the question eight research passes never asked, and you have already answered it for yourself: both matter. Say so.

4
"Can we agree a trial period, with the reversal plan written down first?"

Reasonable on its face, and the time course supports it. Steady state arrives in ~3 days at a ~12h half-life, so a fortnight at the new dose with the first 3–5 days discarded as transition gives a readable window.

Ask for the reversal criteria to be written down BEFORE the change, because that substitutes for the blinding you cannot have. Without a threshold agreed in advance, every outcome reads as confirmation.

5
"Can I have the interaction list, in writing, to give to any other prescriber?"

MAOIs, linezolid and methylene blue are absolute contraindications — and they are given by anaesthetists and infectious-disease teams. In those rooms you may be the only person who knows you are on a serotonergic drug.

Tramadol is the one you are most likely to be offered, because it is routinely prescribed for fibromyalgia, and it is worse than additive. See the interaction card.

6
"I don't smoke — does that change what dose means for me?"

Smokers run 38.4% lower serum duloxetine and 53.6% lower dose-adjusted concentrations at the same dose. So you are already at the high-exposure end of whatever number is on the prescription.

This is not an argument in either direction. It is context for reading your own response, and for anyone comparing your dose to someone else's.

And one thing to say rather than ask
You are ~4 weeks in. At four weeks, between 2% and 14% of people for whom this drug does nothing would be seeing exactly what you are seeing, and unless chance has supplied a sensory overload you have no trigger-conditional data at all. The data arrives after the decision. Saying that out loud is more useful to a doctor than confidence would be, and it is the strongest thing you can bring: an accurate account of the size of your own uncertainty.
