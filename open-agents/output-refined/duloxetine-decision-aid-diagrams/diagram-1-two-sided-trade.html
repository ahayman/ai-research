The Two-Sided Trade

Both sides are real. Nothing here has a thumb on the scale. The question eight research passes never asked is what the increase is for — and the answer is both the pain and the psychological effects, which is exactly what makes this hard.

The case for

Going up

1Pain is why you're on it, and there's a threshold you're under
0-for-3 below 40mg across ~350 patients, twice adequately powered. Goldstein 2005: equal n≈114 arms — 60mg separated, 20mg didn't, so the "underpowered" excuse is gone. Cochrane's authors: "lower daily doses are not [efficacious]."
primary, verified
2The anxiety benefit likely survives the trip
Nicolini: HAM-A change −15.3 at 60–120mg vs −14.7 at 20mg; response 65% vs 60%; remission 44% vs 42%. Small gaps, pointing up, not down.
significance primary; magnitudes secondary20 vs 60 never tested
3No evidence this step damages sleep
In the only trial with a fixed 20mg arm: insomnia 10.2% → 8.6%, somnolence 5.1% → 4.3%, AE discontinuation 15.3% → 14.7%. The inflection is at 120mg, not 60.
registry, verifiedn=59 at 20mg
4POTS is dose-qualified, not a 60mg veto
The signal is real (2015 HRS Class III) but traces to 80–240 mg/day. Duloxetine's measured effect is small: HR +2.22 bpm across 42 studies / 8,504 patients. Direction for you is genuinely unknown.
primary, verifieddirection unknown
The case against

Going up

1The pain payoff is modest even when it works
Welsch 2018 (CD010292), fibromyalgia-specific: NNTB 11 (9–14). The review authors' own words: "no clinically relevant benefit." GRADE LOW. Lilly-sponsored. Eleven people take it so one gets meaningful relief.
primary, verifiedGRADE LOW
2Somnolence is dose-dependent, and lands on what you're protecting
FDA label: dose-dependent in fixed-dose studies; incidence 10–11% vs 3% (the "1.5%" in circulation is a discontinuation rate). 41% in the only ME/CFS trial. Your gain from this drug is waking less tired. Somnolence spends exactly that.
label, verifiedmagnitude at your step unmeasured
3FATIGUE is the one thing that rises at your exact step
Of the three sleep-adjacent measures: insomnia never rises across this range; somnolence sometimes does; fatigue is the one that rises at YOUR step. Same trial, same table: 0% (placebo) → 0% (20mg) → 6.03% (60mg) → 8.93% (120mg) — the only clean monotonic rise at 20→60. The AE term "fatigue" is not your bone-weary exhaustion, and pretending otherwise would be dishonest. But fatigue is your primary disability and your valued gain is waking less tired, so the single exception lands precisely on what you're protecting.
registry, verifiedconstruct mismatch
4The sensory-gating cost may scale
Jensen 2008: 10mg → gating INTACT. Oranje 2011: 15mg → gating DISRUPTED. Same lab, paradigm, design. One SMALL dose step flipped the filter — and it is live precisely because the step was small. "Transiently" is unsupported — every study is single-dose.
⚠ But not "3×." Serotonergic engagement saturates: you are already at ~72% occupancy and 60mg is ~88%, so it is a ~16-point step, not a tripling. Reading the ladder as a multiplier treats a saturating curve as a straight line. The concern survives; the multiplier doesn't.
human, dose-gradedsingle lab, n≈20chronicity unmeasured
5It closes the only clean test you'll ever have
The trap. Your model and the threshold model disagree now and agree at 60mg. Going up satisfies both and destroys the experiment permanently.
reasoning, not evidence

Three arguments that do not survive, and should not be carried into the room

  • "Going up makes coming down harder" — WEAK. The cliff is below 20mg (20→0 costs ~72 occupancy points with no capsule; 60→20 costs ~16 with capsules throughout). You're already on the cliff. But the taper question is strong: ask about a compounded liquid now.
  • "Emotional blunting is dose-dependent" — DEAD. Traces through two reviews to one 1990 case report. Aşçibaşi 2020 (n=98): doses RAISED, blunting FELL (P<0.001). This was called the strongest argument against an increase. It collapses.
  • "NE rises → sleep worsens at 60mg" — DEAD. Contradicted by outcome data; built on an n=2 occupancy point with a CI of −20% to +100%. Trust outcomes over occupancy.

⚠ The offer is probably not "20 or 60." It is probably 30 — and 30 has its own shape

  • The logical window survives at 30mg. Because 30mg is still below the analgesic threshold, your model and the standard model still predict different things there. So 30mg does not close the experiment the way 60mg does, and saying otherwise would be a tidy overstatement.
  • But the clean baseline does not survive. Any dose change resets the observation and adds a variable, and your model needs you to read a slow drift, which cannot be read across a discontinuity.
  • And 30mg is the only dose whose pain payoff has been directly tested at adequate power and found ABSENT. Arnold 2012: 30mg, n=155/153, P=0.202. So it pays a real information price for the least evidenced gain of the three.
  • The fair counterpoint: there is a perfectly good reason to pass through 30mg that has nothing to do with its own evidence — titrating for tolerability on the way to 60mg, which is standard and sensible. The distinction to carry into the room is 30mg as a WAYPOINT (reasonable) versus 30mg as a DESTINATION (not supported by the trial evidence). That is a question you can simply ask.
Both columns are coherent. Neither is obviously right.
Going up buys a real but modest shot at the thing it was prescribed for, and the anxiety benefit probably survives. Staying buys a clean answer to a question only you are asking, on a window that shuts permanently at 60mg. This page does not choose. That decision is yours and your doctor's.
