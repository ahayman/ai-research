What the distribution tells us

The largest quadrant in published clinician discourse is "concede critique + defend utility." This is intellectually honest — it accepts that the testable empirical claims didn't survive while preserving the working clinical practices. It also creates an interesting situation: the field is moving toward a position where PVT's anatomical claims are treated as historically interesting but not load-bearing, while the clinical concepts (safety, co-regulation, ladder-of-states) operate as useful metaphors. Whether this is intellectually honest "translation" or post-hoc rescue is the question Cat 11 (Practical Decision Aid) returns to.

What this means for you, the autistic adult navigating burnout/overwhelm: when you encounter a polyvagal-informed therapist, you can ask a clarifying question with diagnostic value — "Do you think the 2026 consensus paper raises valid scientific points?" A practitioner who says "yes, but the practices still work via mechanisms that don't require PVT" is in the green quadrant and likely intellectually careful. A practitioner who dismisses the critique as misunderstanding the theory and continues making strong mechanism claims is in the purple quadrant. The skeptic-reformer red quadrant suggests considering alternative frameworks (Cat 9: Neurovisceral Integration, predictive processing, allostatic load) where the science is less contested.
