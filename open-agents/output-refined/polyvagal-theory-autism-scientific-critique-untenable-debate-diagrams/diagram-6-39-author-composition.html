The 39-Author "Untenable" Consensus — Who Signed

The 2026 paper has 39 total co-authors: first-author Paul Grossman invited 39 experts in vagal physiology, comparative neuroanatomy, and vertebrate evolution; 38 of 39 invited accepted. Co-authors span seven research domains and eleven countries.

First author
Paul Grossman, PhD
Emeritus Research Director of Psychosomatic Medicine, University Hospital Basel, Switzerland
Compositional summary: The 38 invited co-authors represent neurology, cardiology, evolutionary biology, veterinary science, physiology, neuroanatomy, and behavioral studies. Many were previously cited in polyvagal-theory literature itself.

Why composition matters: this is not "one psychophysiologist's opinion." It's a 39-author consensus drawn from comparative physiology, vagal neuroanatomy, brainstem autonomic neuroscience, cardiology, animal behavior, and interoception research — precisely the fields whose findings PVT's claims rest on. The roster includes leading figures (Khalsa for interoception; Paterson, Paton, Spyer, Gourine for autonomic neuroscience; E.W. Taylor for vertebrate comparative physiology; Neuhuber for vagal anatomy; the Doody/Burghardt/Dinets reptile-ethology team for sociality claims). When 39 experts from these specialties reach a published consensus that the theory's core claims are untenable, it carries epistemic weight that a single dissenting voice does not.
