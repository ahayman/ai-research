Dorsal Vagal Freezing — PVT Claim vs. Neuhuber Counter-Evidence

The popular polyvagal account of autistic shutdown, dissociation, and chronic-illness collapse states depends on a specific mechanism claim: that the dorsal motor nucleus (DMV) mediates freeze. Direct neuroanatomy doesn't support this.

PVT's claim

Porges 1995/2011; widely adopted in trauma-informed therapy, somatic work, and popular polyvagal-informed parenting / chronic illness literature.

Mechanism: Freeze / shutdown / immobilization is mediated by the dorsal vagal complex — specifically DMV activation projecting via unmyelinated C-fibers.
Predicted physiology: Profound bradycardia (sometimes < 40 bpm), syncope risk, vasovagal-like collapse, sometimes accompanied by dissociation.
Evolutionary framing: A "phylogenetically ancient" unmyelinated pathway inherited from reptilian ancestors; the failsafe when both Social Engagement System and sympathetic mobilization have failed.
Clinical mapping: The "third zone" of the polyvagal ladder. Autistic shutdown, dissociative episodes in trauma, ME/CFS-spectrum collapse, "freeze" in PTSD — all attributed to dorsal vagal activation.

What Neuhuber & Berthoud 2022 actually found

Neuhuber W, Berthoud H-R. Functional anatomy of the vagus system: How does the polyvagal theory comply? Biological Psychology 174:108425 (PubMed 36100134).

Direct conclusion: "Evidence does not support a role of the 'dorsal vagal complex' in freezing as proposed by the PVT."
Anatomical finding: Mammalian freezing responses appear primarily mediated by the nucleus ambiguus (NA) — the same nucleus PVT assigns to social engagement.
Empirical observation: "Profound bradycardia is neither common nor frequent among humans during emotional freezing or psychological dissociation." The predicted physiology often isn't there.
Direct quote: The DMV "should not be linked to passive defensive behavior."

What this does and doesn't claim

Doesn't deny: the lived experience of shutdown/freeze states. The experience is real and clinically important. Autistic adults describing shutdown after high-demand weeks are reporting something true about their phenomenology.

Doesn't deny: that some bradycardia can accompany severe acute stress. It can — the vasovagal syncope mechanism is well-characterized and involves DMV in part.

Does deny: that the popular polyvagal mechanism story for chronic, clinical shutdown states is anatomically grounded. The "your dorsal vagus is taking over" framing is a clinical heuristic, not a direct neuroanatomical reading.

Does deny: that mammalian freezing is generically DMV-mediated. The bulk of evidence points to NA involvement, sometimes in coordination with limbic and brainstem circuits that have nothing to do with the PVT architecture.

The clinical implication: shutdown states need a different mechanism story. Neurovisceral Integration (vmPFC→amygdala→vagus), predictive-processing accounts of failed energy budgeting, allostatic load (chronic stress depleting regulatory capacity), and limbic-brainstem freeze circuits (in animal research) all offer better-supported frameworks. These alternatives don't deny that "shutdown" is a real, observable, clinically important state — they offer mechanism stories that survive contact with direct anatomy.
