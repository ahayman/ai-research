Options That Do Not Conflict, With Their Costs Attached

Duloxetine offers nothing for RLS and plausibly aggravates it. That asymmetry is real and decision-relevant. So are the costs of every alternative, which is why they are printed next to the benefits rather than in a footnote.

Non-drug options

OptionGradeNote
Iron repletion if ferritin ≤75 ESTABLISHED Guideline-level. Already started, correctly, unprompted. Will not report for ~12 weeks.
Bilateral high-frequency peroneal nerve stimulation AASM 2025 Conditionally recommended. The only non-drug intervention with a formal guideline recommendation.
Morning bright light SUGGESTIVE Whittom's n=8 showed reduced sensory symptoms (no effect on movements). Free, no interaction with duloxetine, independent support for circadian regulation.
Moderate exercise, sleep hygiene, relaxation Weak Guideline-endorsed, weak evidence. Caveat specific to this reader: "moderate exercise" carries PEM risk in ME/CFS. This generic advice does not transfer safely and should not be repeated uncritically.
Pneumatic compression, massage, warm/cool baths, acupuncture, CBT Limited Limited evidence, low risk. CBT for mild-moderate RLS is newly recommended in 2024/25 guidance.
Reducing aggravators Standard Alcohol, caffeine, nicotine, sedating antihistamines, dopamine-blocking antiemetics (metoclopramide, prochlorperazine).

Drug options compatible with duloxetine

OptionGradeWhat it buysWhat it costs
Pregabalin (or gabapentin / gabapentin enacarbil) AASM 2025: all three STRONG, moderate certainty 13 RCTs + 7 observational studies. Relieves symptoms in roughly two-thirds of patients. No serotonergic conflict, no CYP interaction (renally excreted). Additive drowsiness (documented in Gilron 2016). Class I impairment of processing speed, executive control and verbal fluency. Own withdrawal syndrome. "No CYP overlap" is not "no interaction."
Dopamine agonists Recommended AGAINST for standard use Historically first-line. Augmentation. The field has reversed its position.
Reducing the duloxetine dose CASE REPORTS Resolved the one on-point duloxetine-RLS case (60→80mg trigger, resolved at 40mg). n=1, and confounded by a simultaneous escitalopram cut. Would also trade away the effects he values.
Switching to bupropion RCT-BACKED for RLS 60-person RCT: does not exacerbate RLS. The natural experiment isolating serotonin, since it is noradrenergic-dopaminergic with no serotonergic action. Not an anxiolytic; often activating and can worsen anxiety, which is his primary reported benefit from duloxetine. No fibromyalgia indication. Right for depression + RLS; probably wrong for anxiety + fibromyalgia + RLS.
The honest, narrow claim. Pregabalin is a legitimate, guideline-supported option for this reader's RLS, where duloxetine offers nothing. Its fibromyalgia advantage over duloxetine is not demonstrable: the NNT confidence intervals overlap almost totally (pregabalin 9.7 [7.2–15] vs duloxetine 8 [4–21]), and the GRADE contrast of HIGH vs LOW is substantially an artifact of which Cochrane team wrote which review rather than a property of the drugs. Adding it to duloxetine has one positive RCT and one documented harm, additive drowsiness, which lands exactly on this reader's primary disability. That trade-off is his and his doctor's to make, and it should be made with the trial and the drowsiness both on the table, not as a clever untested idea.

Two corrections that matter here. First, "RLS doses sit below the cognitively-active range" is contradicted: the pivotal 52-week RLS trial used 300 mg/day, the usual effective RLS range is quoted as 150–450mg, and impairment has been demonstrated at 300mg. The ranges overlap; they do not sit apart. The defensible version is narrower: cognitive risk is dose-dependent, starting low may reduce it, and it has not been shown to eliminate it. Second, the pregabalin GAD meta-analysis was funded by Viatris, the pregabalin manufacturer, with one author employed by Viatris. That conflict must travel with the figure, in the same way Lilly's conflicts travel with duloxetine's.
