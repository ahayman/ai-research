## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: The Iron Decision Tree
- **Type**: Two-gate decision flow + linear threshold scale + six caveat panels
- **Elements**: TSAT gate (>45% = STOP) placed *first*, ferritin gate second with three outcomes (≤75 oral / ≤100 IV / >75 futile); a single scale showing the lab's deficiency flag (~15–30) against the IRLSSG line (75) with the reader marked at ~70; panels for acute-phase reactant, the weak-end-of-range caveat, ~12-week onset, hepcidin/alternate-day, the 92.1%/50% gap, and the never-studied question
- **Key Insight**: He was already inside the treatment zone and acted correctly. What he still needs is TSAT, which ferritin cannot substitute for. A ferritin of 40 is normal for anaemia and low for RLS.
- **Corrections carried**: "routinely skipped" is wrong (92.1% ordered; the failure is acting); iron cannot explain anything at the appointment
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc

### Diagram 2: Melatonin and RLS — Two Studies, Two Directions
- **Type**: Symmetric two-panel comparison with a central collision marker, plus a supporting-chain table
- **Elements**: Whittom 2010 panel (n=8, 3mg @19:00, NO placebo, SIT-PLM ↑) vs Kunz 2001 panel (n=9 PLMD, 3mg × 6wk, PLM ↓, well-being ↑ in 7/9); central "SAME 3mg DOSE" badge; table grading the mechanism (rat, in vitro, 1983), circadian correlation (n=7), case report (conference abstract), AGS non-listing, and the bright-light bonus
- **Key Insight**: The evidence points both ways and is weak both ways. Neither "melatonin is aggravating your RLS" nor "melatonin is irrelevant" is supportable.
- **Corrections carried**: all three prior passes wrong; Whittom's dose IS reported (3mg) and Whittom has NO placebo; Kunz was missed by every prior pass; a melatonin holiday now is confounded with duloxetine's two-week settling-in, so a negative result is more informative than a positive one

### Diagram 3: Three Variables in Motion, and a Fourth Proposed
- **Type**: Gantt-style overlapping timeline with vertical NOW and APPOINTMENT guides
- **Elements**: four lanes (duloxetine from −2wk with an attenuation zone; melatonin spanning the full width; iron from NOW with a 12-week silent period and a "reports" marker; proposed dose change as a dashed lane from the appointment); count callouts (3 in motion / 4 in motion); summary table
- **Key Insight**: Clean isolation is gone. If the dose changes now, four variables are moving and no result is interpretable.
- **Note**: The brief specified three variables in motion; the fourth (dose change) is marked as *proposed*, matching supplement 5 §4.7.

### Diagram 4: Duloxetine and PLMS — What Is Measured, What Is Extrapolated
- **Type**: Three-node extrapolation chain with an explicit break, a black "never measured" bar, two evidence panels, plus tables
- **Elements**: chain from measured venlafaxine/SSRI effect → Nicolas & Ruby's review sentence → the unsupported duloxetine claim, with a dashed/questioned link and a "THE CHAIN BREAKS HERE" marker; the full-width statement that duloxetine has never been measured for PLMS at any dose; Kolla verbatim panel; AMSP rate bars (mirtazapine 0.07% vs duloxetine 0.01%); evidence table including the confounded n=1; the four named breaks
- **Key Insight**: "Duloxetine raises PLMS" is dead. Monitor, do not predict.
- **Corrections carried**: the claim is extrapolated from venlafaxine with no duloxetine primary; Kolla says "uncommon" and "do not disrupt sleep"; the AMSP figure counts *severe new inpatient* RLS, so absence of severe new cases is not absence of aggravation; the n=1 is confounded by a simultaneous escitalopram cut

### Diagram 5: Augmentation Is the Wrong Word
- **Type**: Side-by-side definition panels + incidence table with a mislabel warning row
- **Elements**: augmentation panel (four defining features, dopamine-agonist attribution, its management algorithm) vs direct-exacerbation panel (marked "This is your box"); incidence table with DA-specific 6.0% (4.1–8.8), gabapentinoid 0.9% (0.2–3.3), a flagged row for the misquoted pooled 5.6%, and the 20–30% clinic-prevalence row
- **Key Insight**: Antidepressants do not cause augmentation; they cause direct exacerbation. Using the wrong word imports the wrong management algorithm.
- **⚠️ Deviation from the binding brief, deliberate**: the brief (§6) states "Augmentation is a DOPAMINE-AGONIST phenomenon (5.6–30%)". Validation supplement 1 §7.7 explicitly identifies that pair as mislabelled and names the brief as carrying it: the 5.6% is the **pooled rate across all drug types**, while the DA-specific figure is **6.0% (95% CI 4.1–8.8)**; the 20–30% is **cross-sectional clinic prevalence, not trial incidence**; the CIs overlap; the source paper's own title argues the incidence is overstated; and AASM's wording is "against the **standard** use," not "against long-term use." The diagram carries the corrected figures and flags the mislabel. **The brief's load-bearing point (the category error, and gabapentinoids ≈0.9% rather than zero) is preserved intact.**

### Diagram 6: Options That Do Not Conflict, With Their Costs Attached
- **Type**: Benefit/cost balance SVG + two option tables with a dedicated cost column
- **Elements**: pregabalin benefits panel (AASM STRONG, non-serotonergic, EMA GAD licence, Gilron's 68%/42%/39%/18%) vs costs panel (Gilron's additive drowsiness, Salinsky's Class I verbal-fluency hit, withdrawal syndrome); non-drug table including the PEM caveat on "moderate exercise"; drug table with costs beside every option
- **Key Insight**: Pregabalin is a genuine option with a genuine cost, not a clever unlock. Its cost lands on the reader's primary disability.
- **Corrections carried**: Gilron 2016 exists and is positive (the chain's largest factual error was calling it nonexistent) *and* documents additive drowsiness; "RLS doses sit below the cognitively-active range" is contradicted (pivotal trial 300mg; impairment shown at 300mg); the fibromyalgia NNT advantage is not demonstrable (CIs overlap; the GRADE contrast is a review-team artifact); the GAD meta-analysis is Viatris-funded

### Diagram 7: The Whole RLS Story, Sorted by What Kind of Claim It Is
- **Type**: Inverted-emphasis tier pyramid + full claim/tier table
- **Elements**: five tiers (n=1-about-you at top, then primary-verified, secondary-unverified, reasoning-not-evidence, no-evidence-exists as the widest bar); table assigning every load-bearing claim on the page to a tier with the reason
- **Key Insight**: The strongest evidence on this page is the reader's own observation, because the population evidence for duloxetine and RLS does not exist. That makes his observation the thing worth measuring carefully, not proof of a mechanism.
- **Purpose**: enforces §1 of the binding brief (inline provenance; caveats must not be appendix-filed) as a visual artifact

## Design Notes

- **Style**: matches the house pattern for this project (standalone light-mode HTML, `.container` card, `h1` + `.lead`, hand-authored SVG with `role="img"` + `<title>`/`<desc>`, `.caption` for sources, `.hedge` for the disconfirming note). - **Provenance tags are inline** in panel text and table cells (`primary, verified` / `secondary — unverified` / `reasoning, not evidence` / `no evidence exists`), never collected at the bottom.
- **No diagram lands on a verdict.** Each presents candidates or thresholds with what would falsify them, per §0 and §10 of the binding brief.
- **Nothing directs a dose or a supplement.** Framing throughout is what to understand and what to raise with his doctor.
