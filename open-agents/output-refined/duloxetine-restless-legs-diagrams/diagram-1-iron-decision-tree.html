The Iron Decision Tree

The RLS iron threshold sits far above the lab's deficiency flag. This is the single most common way iron gets missed in RLS, and it is why the useful question is "what is the number?" rather than "is it normal?"

Ferritin can read falsely high

Ferritin is an acute-phase reactant: inflammation pushes it up independently of actual iron stores. With fibromyalgia and ME/CFS, a reading of 70 may overstate true iron status, so the real number could be lower. primary, verified

And the reader sits at the weak end

The only trial at this ferritin (n=18, ferritin 15–75) found benefit at 12 weeks. At ~70 he is the least iron-deficient patient it enrolled, so the least likely to benefit. reasoning, not evidence

~12 weeks to act

The trial endpoint is 12 weeks; moving brain iron takes months. Iron was started this week. It cannot explain anything at an appointment two weeks away. primary, verified

Less often absorbs better

A dose raises hepcidin, which blocks the next dose. Guideline prefers once-daily to twice-daily; hepcidin work supports alternate-day. The n=18 trial used twice-daily, the older protocol. secondary — unverified

The real failure is not testing

Panels are ordered 92.1% of the time. But among patients with ferritin ≤75, only 50% got iron. The gap is acting on the result. secondary — unverified

The question nobody has asked

Does correcting iron reduce antidepressant-induced RLS specifically? Searched directly and repeatedly. Nothing. no evidence exists

What this diagram does not settle. The 92.1% / 50% figures come from a conference abstract in a single health system, which is weak. The iron evidence base at this exact ferritin is eighteen people. And whether iron helps a drug-induced RLS has never been studied, so the plausible assumption that repletion will buffer it is an assumption, not a finding. What is guideline-solid is only this: ferritin ≤75 puts you in the zone where oral iron is considered, and TSAT >45% is the stop sign.
