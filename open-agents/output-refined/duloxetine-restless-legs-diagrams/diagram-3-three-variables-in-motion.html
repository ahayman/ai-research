Three Variables in Motion, and a Fourth Proposed

Every one of these actions was sensible on its own. Taken together, without a measurement plan, they have removed the ability to attribute any change to any cause.

VariableStatusTime course, and what it means
Duloxetine2 weeks inEarly adverse effects characteristically attenuate over weeks, so the RLS may improve on its own. This is the confound that ruins every other experiment run right now.
MelatoninNightly, ongoing backgroundFast on and off (t½ ~40 min). Cheap to test, but it is the background variable, not the one that changed.
IronStarted this week~12 weeks to act. Silent at the appointment. Leave it alone and let it run.
Dose increaseProposed, ~2 weeks awayDays to weeks. The fastest-acting variable, arriving last, on top of three others.
The consequence, stated plainly. If the RLS improves over the next month, at least four explanations fit: duloxetine's early effects attenuating, melatonin doing whatever melatonin does, iron beginning to work early, or ordinary noise in a symptom that fluctuates anyway. If the dose changes now, no result will be interpretable. Not the restless legs, not the sleep, not the fatigue.
This is not a reason for despair and it is not a reason to stop any of them. It is a reason to decide, in advance, what will be measured and how the causes would be told apart. That decision is free, it is available now, and it is the only thing on this page that gets harder the longer it is left.
What this diagram assumes. The "early AEs attenuate over weeks" claim is a general property of antidepressant side effects, not a duloxetine-and-RLS measurement, because duloxetine has never been measured for periodic limb movements at any dose. The ~12-week iron figure is the endpoint of an eighteen-person trial, and some sources report improvement as early as 2–6 weeks. So the timeline's shape is sound and its exact widths are approximations.
