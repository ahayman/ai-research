What Died, and What Survived It

An earlier pass of this project found a diagnostic label that sounded like your description and handed it to you. The label is dead. The argument it was attached to never needed it, and stands.

Dead — do not resurrect

“You have non-fearful panic disorder”

“This is the reader's anxiety without negative thoughts, and it has a name.”
Cause of death 1 · Duration
DSM-5 defines a panic attack as an abrupt surge that reaches a peak within minutes, typically peaking around 10 minutes and lasting around 30. NFPD is a variant of panic disorder and still requires actual panic attacks.
Panic attackMinutes
Your episodesOne to two weeks
Roughly a thousand-fold difference. A different class of event.
Cause of death 2 · A different absence
Both descriptions are about something missing. They are not about the same missing thing.
NFPDAbsent fear, with somatic panic symptoms present
YouPresent severe anxiety, absent cognitions
You have the affect. NFPD patients do not. “No fear” and “no negative thoughts” are not the same absence.
Cause of death 3 · Population
NFPD is defined and measured in cardiology and emergency-department chest-pain presenters. Its prevalence figure has no established transfer to an autistic adult with sensory-triggered multi-week dissociative episodes.
Why it cannot come back
It died by measurement and category error, not by heterogeneity. Individual variation revives a mechanism whose average effect was null. It does not revive a label whose defining timescale is wrong by three orders of magnitude.
Survives intact

The CBT-mismatch argument

“If there is no negative cognition, cognitive restructuring has nothing to restructure.”
The validation pass's instruction, verbatim in substance
The downstream argument is good independent of NFPD. If there is no negative cognition, cognitive restructuring has nothing to restructure, so CBT's active ingredient is misaligned on mechanism. Keep that argument. Drop the diagnostic claim.
Why it never depended on the label. The argument runs entirely off your own report. It needs one premise, which is that your episodes arrive without negative thoughts, and you supplied that premise yourself. NFPD was decoration on top of it. Removing the decoration does not touch the structure underneath.
premise 1   your episodes have no negative cognitive content
premise 2   CBT's active ingredient is restructuring a negative cognition
therefore    CBT's active ingredient has no target in your episodes
note        no clause of this mentions panic, fear, cardiology, or a diagnosis
What it does not license. It does not follow that a non-cognitive intervention would work, and nothing here claims that. Nobody has studied the overwhelm-to-crash cycle pharmacologically in anyone. The argument is negative and narrow, and that is exactly why it holds.
If NFPD is mentioned at all, the figure has to be right too
The earlier pass's figure “20–40% of panic disorder in medical populations”
What the source actually says 32–41% of panic disorder patients seeking treatment for chest pain
The source Fleet RP, Martel JP, Lavoie KL, Dupuis G, Beitman BD. Non-fearful panic disorder: a variant of panic in medical patients? PMID 10906353. n=48 NFPD, 60 PD, 333 no-PD; emergency-department patients; 2-year follow-up.
The “20%” lower bound Appears nowhere in the source. It was invented in transit.
The correct framing A distant cousin in the literature, demonstrating that anxiety phenomena can lack their expected cognitive or affective features. Not a label for what you have.
The general rule this page is enforcing: a resonant label taken from an unrelated measurement literature and handed to a real person as a description of themselves is a specific, recurring failure. It happened here with NFPD and it was caught. The same move is available right now with “HAM-A somatic anxiety” and it is refused for the same reason. See diagram 3.

Provenance: the Fleet 2000 figure and quotation are primary, verified. The DSM-5 panic criterion is primary, verified. The three causes of death and the logical structure of the surviving argument are reasoning, not evidence, adjudicated by this project's validation pass and binding on this page.
