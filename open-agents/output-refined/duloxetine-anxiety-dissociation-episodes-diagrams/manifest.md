# Diagram Manifest: Anxiety, Dissociation & the Episode Pattern (Duloxetine, Category 5)

## The spine these diagrams serve

**20mg has real, direct, positive RCT evidence for anxiety, and it is the ONLY endpoint where his exact dose does.** Nicolini 2009 (PMID 18485261): genuinely fixed 20mg/day, n=84, 10 weeks, separating from placebo on HAM-A total AND the psychic factor at p=0.01–0.001. It did NOT separate on the somatic factor (p=0.07). Higher doses were **slightly better, not worse**.

**The decisive caveat: non-inferiority to 60mg was NEVER tested.** Hartford 2007 (161/162/164) + Nicolini (170/158/169) reconcile *exactly* to Allgulander 2008's pooled 331/320/333. The non-inferiority analysis pooled only the 60–120mg arms and excluded the 20mg arm entirely. **"Beat placebo" and "as good as 60mg" are different claims, and only the first was ever asked.**

**The placebo hypothesis does not collapse**: Nicolini's placebo arm dropped 11.6 HAM-A points with 42% response.

## THE GUARDRAIL — binding brief §8, enforced in diagram 3

**Duloxetine improves HAM-A *psychic* anxiety but not *somatic* anxiety, across all 8 RCTs (Li 2018, n=2,399). That is a real, dose-independent drug property.** BUT **"HAM-A somatic anxiety" is NOT his "anxiety without cognitive content." DO NOT MAP THEM.** Doing so would repeat exactly the non-fearful-panic-disorder false-match error this chain already made and retracted. HAM-A somatic = palpitations, sweating, GI symptoms — a list of *body locations*. His phenomenology = present anxiety, absent negative thoughts — a question about *propositional content*. **Different axis entirely. The words rhyme; the constructs don't.**

Diagram 3 makes the orthogonality visual with a 2×2, plots where he actually sits (the **psychic** side, which is the factor duloxetine *does* move), and carries the stop-bar in the middle of the diagram rather than at the bottom.

## What is deliberately NOT in these diagrams

Per binding brief §6 (dead list) and §8 (the Cat 5 guardrail):

- ❌ **Any mapping of "HAM-A somatic anxiety" onto his phenomenology.** Diagram 3 exists specifically to forbid it.
- ❌ **The NFPD diagnostic claim.** Diagram 5 kills it and shows the three causes of death. Only the CBT-mismatch argument survives.
- ❌ **The "20–40% in medical populations" NFPD figure.** Corrected to 32–41% in chest-pain presentations, Fleet 2000. The "20%" lower bound appears nowhere in the source.
- ❌ **"Near-parity with 60–120mg."** Diagram 2 shows why: no comparison was ever performed.
- ❌ **"All changes statistically significant vs placebo."** False; somatic was p=0.07.
- ❌ **"The placebo hypothesis collapses."** It does not. Diagram 1 gives the placebo arm its own panel.
- ❌ **The "45% / 55%" HAM-A percentages.** Internally inconsistent with the absolute values (implied baselines 32.7 and 27.8, which disagree with each other and are both too high for GAD registration trials). Diagram 1 states this and uses the absolutes plus response/remission instead. The substantive claim — higher doses slightly better, not worse — survives on those three comparisons alone.
- ❌ **The BNST sustained-threat duration story.** Off by ~1000×.
- ❌ **The propranolol / PTSD-prophylaxis parallel.** An anti-parallel; diagram 7 uses it as a ruled-out row rather than an explanation.
- ❌ **Any dose recommendation.** Per §10, nothing here directs a dose.
