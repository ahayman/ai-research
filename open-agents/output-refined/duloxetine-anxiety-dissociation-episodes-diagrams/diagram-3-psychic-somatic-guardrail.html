The Psychic/Somatic Split Is Real. It Is Not About You.

Duloxetine reliably improves one HAM-A factor and reliably does not improve the other. That is a genuine drug property with excellent evidence. It is also the single most tempting false match on this page, so the guardrail sits in the middle of the diagram rather than at the bottom.

The finding, and it is solid
Duloxetine improves the HAM-A psychic anxiety factor and produces no significant difference on the HAM-A somatic factor. This holds across every dose tested, and Nicolini's fixed 20mg arm shows the identical split (psychic p=0.01–0.001; somatic p=0.07). It is not a 20mg weakness. It is a dose-independent property of the drug.

What the two factors actually contain
HAM-A psychic factor duloxetine moves this
  • Anxious mood
  • Tension
  • Fears
  • Insomnia
  • Cognitive symptoms
  • Depressed mood
  • Behaviour at interview
HAM-A somatic factor duloxetine does not
  • Muscular symptoms
  • Sensory symptoms
  • Cardiovascular symptoms
  • Respiratory symptoms
  • Gastrointestinal symptoms
  • Genitourinary symptoms
  • Autonomic symptoms
In plain words: palpitations, sweating, churning stomach, tight chest, needing the toilet. It is a list of places in the body.
“HAM-A somatic anxiety” is NOT your “anxiety without cognitive content.” Do not map them.
The HAM-A split sorts anxiety by where in the body the symptom appears. Your description sorts your state by whether it has any propositional content. Those are two different questions, so a drug that moves one of them tells you nothing about the other. The words rhyme. The constructs do not.
Axis 1 — what HAM-A measures Where does the anxiety show up? In the mind (psychic) or in the body (somatic)?
Axis 2 — what you described Does the state come with negative thoughts attached, or does it arrive without content?
Axis 2: cognitive content
psychic · content present
Textbook worry. Racing thoughts about a specific fear, felt as dread.
somatic · content present
Palpitations plus “I am having a heart attack.” The classic panic presentation.
psychic · content absent
You are here. Severe anxiety and dissociation, arriving with no negative thoughts attached to it.
somatic · content absent
Body symptoms with neither fear nor thought. This is roughly where non-fearful panic disorder sits, and it is not where you sit.

Axis 1: where the symptom appears
All four cells are occupiable, which is what “orthogonal” means. You can have florid somatic anxiety with rich negative cognition, or neither, or either one without the other. The factor structure of a rating scale designed in 1959 has nothing to say about the second axis, because it never measured it. Note also where you actually land: on the psychic side, which is the factor duloxetine does move. Reading the somatic null as being about you gets the direction backwards as well as the axis.
Why this guardrail exists, specifically
This project already made this exact mistake once. An earlier pass found “non-fearful panic disorder” in the literature, noticed it sounded like your description, and handed it to you as a name for what you have. It had to be retracted. The failure's shape is to take a superficially resonant label out of an unrelated measurement literature and give it to a real person as a description of themselves. Mapping you onto the HAM-A somatic factor would be the same failure wearing a different label. Nobody has measured whether duloxetine's psychic/somatic split has anything at all to do with your episodes.

Provenance: the Li 2018 figures and the Nicolini factor results are primary, verified. The factor item lists are primary, verified (standard HAM-A structure). The orthogonality grid is reasoning, not evidence: it is an argument about constructs, not a measurement. Whether the split relates to your episodes: no evidence exists.
