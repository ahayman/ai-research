## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: The Five-Tier Evidence Framework (A–E) — ANCHOR DIAGRAM

- **Type**: Layered tier visual with horizontal bands
- **Elements**: Five tiers (A green, B blue, C amber, D orange, E red); each tier shows criteria left, examples right
- **Key Insight**: A portable decision tool. Tier A practices are mostly free, well-evidenced, and work regardless of whether PVT is correct. Tier E includes documented harm risks.
- **Accessibility**: Color is supplemented by tier letters (A–E) and explicit labels ("Do these now" / "Be skeptical" / "Documented harm").

### Diagram 2: What to Try First Decision Tree

- **Type**: Sequential decision flowchart with branching answers
- **Elements**: 6 sequential steps (sleep → sensory → demand → medical → Tier A → assess) plus default position
- **Key Insight**: For an autistic adult with overwhelm/burnout, the highest-leverage interventions are demand reduction and sensory accommodation. Most people skip these.

### Diagram 3: Polyvagal Framing: Helps vs. Substitutes

- **Type**: Two-branch decision tree from central question
- **Elements**: Central question; left branch (helpful uses) green; right branch (risky/substitute uses) red; seven-step operational check below
- **Key Insight**: The same polyvagal language can be scaffolding or substitute depending on context. The seven-step check distinguishes.

### Diagram 4: Practitioner-Evaluation Checklist

- **Type**: Four-column table (Question / Good Answer / Red Flag / Number)
- **Elements**: Eight questions covering license, scope, evidence citation, controversy awareness, COI disclosure, framework-vs-mechanism distinction, belief updating, integration approach
- **Key Insight**: Designed to be screenshot and brought to consultations.

### Diagram 5: The Full Intervention Landscape

- **Type**: Wide comparison table with tier color-coding
- **Elements**: 25 interventions × 6 columns (Tier, Intervention, Cost, Mechanism plausibility, Best use, Watch for)
- **Key Insight**: The decision-grade artifact of the chapter. Everything placed.

### Diagram 6: The Two-Week Starter Protocol

- **Type**: Calendar/schedule diagram with two-column week structure
- **Elements**: Week 1 (Foundations) and Week 2 (Add layers); each day-block is a specific Tier A practice; assess section at the bottom
- **Key Insight**: The reader could start tonight, at zero cost, with strong-evidence practices.

### Diagram 7: Symptoms That Should NOT Be Polyvagal-Self-Treated

- **Type**: Safety panel with three-column table (Symptom / Consider / Why polyvagal is wrong)
- **Elements**: Ten symptoms or symptom patterns with corresponding clinical considerations
- **Key Insight**: Wellness self-care is a complement to, not a substitute for, medical/mental-health workup.

### Diagram 8: Cross-Reference Map: How Cat 11 Ties Back to Cats 1–10

### Diagram 9: Single-Page Summary: Whole Deep Dive in One Image
