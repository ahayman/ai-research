## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Arnold 2015 — the negative primary, and where the evidence actually was
- **Type**: Two-panel SVG (left: the endpoint that failed; right: the table that informed) + horizontal bar comparison + full AE table
- **The spine**: The primary endpoint was NEGATIVE and that is the honest headline, presented first and unsoftened. Duloxetine is not a treatment for ME/CFS. But the same trial's AE table is the only direct ME/CFS evidence in existence, and eight passes cited the trial without opening it.
- **Load-bearing figures**: somnolence 12/29 = 41.38% vs 3/30 = 10.00%; insomnia 10/29 = 34.48% vs 4/30 = 13.33%; dizziness 9/29 = 31.03% vs 2/30 = 6.67%; lightheadedness 5/29 = 17.24% vs 3/30 = 10.00%; vivid dreams 4/29 = 13.79% vs 1/30 = 3.33%
- **Provenance**: retrieval route stated explicitly at the claim (ClinicalTrials.gov results posting for NCT00375973 via API; journal table not reachable, ScienceDirect/ResearchGate 403, no PMC deposit)
- **The caveats live at the claim**: n=59, single trial, secondary safety data, no multiplicity control, 3–6× his dose, unstratified, wide CI. "It is not proof."
- **The absence**: NO sleep outcome measured. The effect he values most is the one its only trial in his illness did not look for.

### Diagram 2: The volitional/intrusive tracker
- **Type**: Two-tier flow SVG (hijacked channel struck out on top, working channel below) + two-counter panel + read-out row + ranked discriminator table
- **The spine**: The channel is **hijacked, not buried**. His fatigue answers "am I wrecked?", not "am I sleepy?", so a sleepiness rating from him is not a noisy measurement of somnolence but a measurement of something else. The solution routes around introspection entirely.
- **The rule**: count (1) deliberate nap attempts that succeeded and (2) unintended dozes, separately, never merged. Benefit = (1)↑, (2)=0. Somnolence = (2)↑ whatever (1) does. Both can happen at once.
- **The re-grounding**: the instrument advice now rests on **Olson 1998 (PMID 9844851)** — *"The ESS cannot be used to demonstrate or exclude sleepiness as it is measured by MSLT"* — rather than on Watson's contested group dissociation. Advice right, mechanism story oversold.
- **Sangal 1992 (PMID 1555459)**: MSLT vs MWT r = 0.41, <17% shared variance, two factors.
- **⚠ The dose discriminator is marked UNAVAILABLE**: he is at one dose, so it only exists *after* the decision it would inform.
- **The honest limit**: "which is it?" is less decision-relevant than "does it cost me anything?" — countable; the label is not.

### Diagram 3: Gotts' reversal
- **Type**: 101-unit block chart (21 highlighted, the reader ringed) + two opposing causal-arrow chains
- **The good half, given its own box**: 21/101 = 20.8% did not nap at all across 14 days. He is in that fifth. **Common, not anomalous.** After a lifetime of it looking like a personal defect, that is worth knowing.
- **The reversal**: his arrow (nap-failure → poor sleep) against Gotts' finding (*"Napping, particularly in the afternoon is associated with poorer cognitive functioning and more daytime sleepiness in CFS"*). **Not napping may be PROTECTING his night sleep.**
- **The limit at the claim**: Gotts measured **reported napping, not nap ABILITY**. Cross-sectional; direction unresolvable; confounded by severity.
- **The nap-test panels**: the killer confound (improved sleep → less sleep drive → napping harder; Bonnet & Arand 13.4 → 3.8 min); the aborted test (MSLT allows 20 min, he quits at 10–20); the verdict (a positive is informative, a negative is not).

### Diagram 4: What actually rises at his dose step — REBUILT after a coordinator correction, and then again after checking the correction

- **Type**: Three-series grouped bar chart (insomnia / somnolence / **fatigue** at placebo/20/60/120) + per-row "across his step" verdict panels with Fisher p-values + two correction boxes + limits panels
- **All twelve cells recomputed from the registry JSON, not transcribed**: insomnia 3/117, 6/59, 10/116, 21/112; somnolence 0/117, 3/59, 5/116, 14/112; fatigue 0/117, 0/59, 7/116, 10/112. **Arm sizes recovered: placebo n=117, 20mg n=59, 60mg n=116, 120mg n=112.** The brief did not carry the arm sizes and they turned out to be decisive.

**Correction 1 (from the coordinator, confirmed against the registry).** The standing brief said *"somnolence rises at the 20→60 step (2.6%→10.2%, 7.8%→14.3%)"*. **Those are 30→60 steps from trials with no 20mg arm.** In the only trial with a fixed 20mg arm, **somnolence across 20→60 is 5.08%→4.31% (Fisher p = 1.00, flat)** and **insomnia falls (10.17%→8.62%, p = 0.78)**. **The only monotonic riser is fatigue: 0% → 0% → 6.03% → 8.93%.** That row was in this project's own published table underneath a spine written about somnolence. Printed, and unread.

**Correction 2 (found here, by testing correction 1 rather than repeating it).** **"Fatigue rises at his step" does not reach significance.** 0/59 vs 7/116 → **Fisher exact two-sided p = 0.0969**. Wilson 95% CIs: 20mg **0–6.11%**, 60mg **2.95–11.93%**. **They overlap.** The 20mg arm is 59 people with zero events; by the rule of three a true rate up to ~5.1% is compatible with observing zero. **Writing it as a headline would be a confident claim over a non-significant result — the exact failure this project documents three times in its source literature (Vgontzas p=0.07; Maloney CI 0.75–4.75; Maier & Seligman's unmeasured "3 days"). Committing it here would make four.** The diagram states this in its own hedge box rather than letting the new spine run unchecked.

- **What the diagram therefore asserts, in the supportable form**: (1) insomnia **falls** at his step, p=0.78; (2) somnolence is **flat** at his step, p=1.00; (3) fatigue is the **only mover** and the only row separating from placebo at 60mg, **0/117 vs 7/116, p = 0.0069 — solid**; (4) the **20→60 fatigue contrast is unresolved**, p=0.0969, in 59 people. Not "no", not "yes".
- **The irony that is the page's spine**: his disability is fatigue, his valued gain is waking less tired, and the one row with any signal at his step is the row he can least read. **That is the join to the tracker in diagram 2**: the fix is not to rate it harder, it is to count events.
- **Three limits at the claim**: CLBP not ME/CFS; a **spontaneously reported AE term, not a fatigue measurement** (the **0% placebo rate is the tell** — it cuts both ways, making 6% a higher bar than it looks *and* the row blunt); and **the 41% ME/CFS somnolence figure is a different kind of claim** (one arm, n=29, flexible 60–120mg, unstratified) that **says nothing about 20→60**. Conflating them is how the original error was made.
- **The 30→60 figures are kept but correctly relabelled** as "not his step, from trials with no 20mg arm": they support *"somnolence is dose-dependent above 30mg"*, not *"somnolence rises when he goes from 20 to 60"*.
- **The label is held to its actual scope**: somnolence *is* dose-dependent per FDA (10% vs 4%; 12% vs 3%), and the widely-quoted **1.5% is a DISCONTINUATION rate** understating incidence ~7×. But "dose-dependent across the licensed range" ≠ "rises at 20→60".
- **Where the inflection actually sits**: 60→120 on all three rows, converging with the label's own "no evidence that doses greater than 60 mg/day confer additional benefit".
- **The folk claim killed**: "low dose sedating, high dose activating" is not supported; duloxetine causes insomnia AND somnolence in the same population at once (34% and 41% simultaneously in 29 people).
- **§10 compliance is explicit in its own panel**: none of this directs a dose or argues against going up. The usable conclusion is about **measurement, not dose**.

### Diagram 5: The nap-failure retraction
- **Type**: Sleep-latency scale (0 to 20+ min) placing Watson's 8.9, Watson's controls at 10.0, Neu 2008's faster CFS group, Bonnet & Arand's insomnia patients at 13.4, and the reader off the right end + three-direction panels + the full 15-paper table
- **Two claims retracted explicitly, with cause of death**: (1) "his nap-failure is the ME/CFS MSLT finding lived" — MEASUREMENT: **Watson's twins fell asleep in 8.9 minutes**; (2) "the ESS-high/MSLT-normal dissociation is replicated" — **it is CONTRADICTED**.
- **The whole literature enumerated**, not selected: PubMed `("multiple sleep latency"[tiab]) AND ("chronic fatigue"[tiab] OR "myalgic encephalomyelitis"[tiab])` = 15 records. Points three ways. **Nothing in it describes a group that cannot nap.**
- **Two methodological catches**: Neu 2014 is circular (normal MSLT was an entry criterion); Spitzer's population is probably contaminated (HLA DQB1*0602 in 43%, neurology referral practice) **and that cuts both ways**.
- **The better fit, not oversold**: insomnia hyperarousal (Bonnet & Arand, longer MSLT latencies, 13.4 min baseline) — but he does not report insomnia, his sleep improved, and daytime nap-failure with decent night sleep is **not a described phenotype in either literature**.
- **Dressle & Riemann 2023**: the cross-diagnostic hyperarousal comparison is an **open question**, not a settled overlap.
- **The fifth evidence-absence**: no MSLT study of duloxetine or any SNRI exists.

### Diagram 6: The CCC quote with "crash" highlighted
- **Type**: Parallel-chain SVG (his sequence above, the CCC's sequence below, matched link-by-link) + two verbatim quote boxes with `.crash` and `.ol` highlight spans
- **The spine, delivered first**: sensory overload → crash → anxiety is **his exact sequence, written down in 2003, using his own word**. He rediscovered a documented clinical phenomenon independently. That validates the phenomenology completely.
- **Verbatim**: Criterion 5 with both footnote definitions; the "Overload Phenomena" body text; the HPA/ANS "trigger a 'crash'" passage; Criterion 6b.
- **The provenance discipline at the claim**: the CCC is **expert consensus and clinical description, not experimental evidence**. It delivers Claim 1 and is **silent on Claim 2**.
- **The GET guardrail, given its own box**: NICE NG206 1.11.14 and 1.12.32 verbatim. GET and the deconditioning model were withdrawn. **Nothing here is a psychological model of his illness**; the allostatic-load evidence points *away* from stress-reactivity and *toward* metabolic.
- **The criteria note**: IOM 2015/SEID requires onset *"not lifelong"*; he describes lifelong ME/CFS. CCC and ICC do not impose it. A note about criteria, not about whether he is ill.
- **The Chu correction**: sensory exposure was **never asked about as a trigger**; "sensory overload" appears as a *symptom*. Chu establishes emotional distress → PEM, not sensory exposure → PEM.

### Diagram 7: His hypothesis scored honestly
- **Type**: Three-claim chain (established / no evidence / speculation) + five-finding adversarial panel + "none of that is a refutation" bar + full scored table + trap box
- **The scoring**: Claim 1 ESTABLISHED as clinical description; Claim 2 NO EVIDENCE and **it is the load-bearing link**; Claim 3 SPECULATION.
- **The five adjacent findings**: (1) allostatic load never measured in FM (0 title-level papers; the one FM item studies allostatic *overload*, a different construct); (2) Maloney 2006 OR 1.9, CI 0.75–4.75, non-significant under a confident abstract; (3) Maloney 2009 r = −.26 — **longer illness, LOWER allostatic load, backwards from his model**; (4) Liang & Booker 2024 — metabolic RRR 2.00, **parasympathetic predicted nothing**, and his model runs through that limb; (5) Okifuji 2011 — **distress→pain NULL**, but the indirect distress→fatigue→pain path survives and is what makes the test possible.
- **The sixth item, a ruler not a finding**: human SIH is measured in minutes to ~90 minutes. Not one experiment measures weeks or months. Same error as the BNST duration story.
- **"AND NONE OF THAT IS A REFUTATION"** given its own green panel, at equal weight to the objections: unstudied ≠ false; his intersection is the population these studies exclude; `"autistic burnout" AND ("chronic fatigue"...)` = 1 result.
- **The base rate**: Bergenheim 16% achieving ≥50%, 13% mean over a decade. FM pain is largely stable, so a drift down at fixed 20mg would be genuinely surprising and therefore informative.
- **The trap box**: different predictions now, **identical predictions at 60mg**; the 20mg window is the only clean test that will ever exist and a dose increase closes it irreversibly. **Explicitly not an argument for staying at 20mg** — pain matters to him and he corrected that framing. The real trade is "how much is the answer worth, against months of pain?"
- **Pre-commitment requirement** and the breakthrough-crash durable-signature test.

### Diagram 8: The orthostatic picture
- **Type**: Five-row dose ladder (reboxetine 8mg → atomoxetine 40mg → duloxetine 80–240 → duloxetine 60–120 → duloxetine 20mg, marked NEVER MEASURED) + two-direction panels + active stand test table
- **Overturned back to REAL**: Mar 2014 (PMID 24227635) verbatim; Raj 2013 (PMID 23753844) verbatim; **2015 HRS Class III, Level B-R** — and the earlier pass missed it by searching the recommends-*for* list.
- **The dose qualifier is most of the story**: both sources say "high-dose" and "chronic"; the only duloxetine-specific study is **Vincent 2004 at 80–240 mg/day**, i.e. 4–12× his dose. **Not a 20mg problem; worth a conversation at 60mg.**
- **⚠ The largest remaining gap, stated at the claim**: Vincent 2004's full text was NOT retrieved; the attribution rests on Mar 2014's secondary characterisation.
- **DELETED**: "SSRIs preferred over SNRIs in POTS" — invalid, and Mar 2014's own data refute it (sertraline: standing HR not different, symptoms *worse*).
- **The direction is genuinely unknown**: NET inhibition worsens orthostatic tachycardia AND prevents vasovagal pre-syncope (29→35 min, p=0.001; OR 0.22; 9/18 vs 1/18). **Most ME/CFS OI is not POTS** (Garner & Baraniuk 6/39 met criteria, 72% symptomatic, *"orthostatic tachycardia did not account for OI symptoms in CFS"*; van Campen ~90% abnormal CBF including with normal HR/BP).
- **Mean small, tail real**: HR +2.22 bpm; 42 studies / 8,504 patients no sustained BP elevation; **Stevens 2008 at 20 mg/day, HR 110–120, positive dechallenge AND rechallenge**.
- **Three defeaters** against reading 30–40% NET as POTS risk, including the compartment argument (brain PET vs peripheral cardiac terminals) which is the strongest and which no earlier pass had.
- **⚠ Correction**: OR 0.22 is not a "78% risk reduction". Quote the OR.
- **The active stand test** with all three patterns and what each would mean.

### Diagram 9: Cortisol, the question is malformed
- **Type**: Two-literature panels (ME/CFS "low" vs insomnia "high", both failing on the same variable) + illustrative diurnal curve comparison (same total, different shape) + arousal-story ranking table
- **The spine**: he is **not backwards**; the question is malformed, and that is the finding. He asked "how much?"; the evidence says "the shape."
- **Powell 2013**: no significant difference on total cortisol output; only attenuated CAR increase, d = −0.34; verbatim *"total cortisol output... was rarely associated with fatigue in any population"* and *"outcomes reflecting within-day CHANGE... may be the most relevant"*.
- **Vgontzas 2001**: 24-h cortisol **P = 0.07, n=11** — not significant — under a title and conclusion asserting an increase. ACTH separated at 0.04.
- **The signature failure, third documented instance** in the source literature (with Maloney's OR and Maier & Seligman's "3 days").
- **The four-part answer** given in full.
- **The arousal-story ranking**: cortical > sleep-drive/arousal > autonomic > cortisol, and **his own phrasing is the version with the least support**. "Beats" means "is less badly supported".
- **What to ask for**: a diurnal salivary panel (waking, +30 min, evening).
- The curves are labelled as **illustrative of the finding, not plotted from its data**.

## Style compliance

- **Every load-bearing claim carries an inline provenance tag** (`.prov`, `.prov.sec`, `.prov.reason`, `.prov.none`), inside the sentence, never in a footer block. BINDING-CORRECTIONS §1.
- **Caveats sit at the claim**, not in an appendix. This is the chain's signature defect and every diagram places its limits adjacent to the number they limit.
- **Retractions are stated, not dropped** (diagrams 5 and 8 name what was wrong, who said it, and the cause of death).
- **No winner is declared.** Diagram 7 scores the reader's own hypothesis honestly in both directions and explicitly refuses to convert the verdict into a dose recommendation.
- **ME/CFS is treated as a real illness throughout.** Diagram 6 carries the NICE NG206 guardrail explicitly; no diagram contains a deconditioning, GET, or "it's stress" framing; diagram 7 notes the evidence points *away* from stress-reactivity and *toward* metabolic.
