Arnold 2015: The Negative Primary, and Where the Evidence Actually Was

One trial. That is the entire literature on duloxetine in ME/CFS. It failed on its primary endpoint, which is the honest headline. Eight research passes cited it for that failure. None of them opened its adverse-event table, which is where the useful material was.

Figure 1: The negative primary, and the table underneath it The trial failed, and that failure is the honest headline: duloxetine is not a treatment for ME/CFS and there is no evidence it is. But the same trial's adverse-event table is the only direct evidence in existence on what happens when you give duloxetine to people with ME/CFS, and it points at somnolence and at the orthostatic axis. The table was not reachable through the journal (ScienceDirect and ResearchGate both 403, no PMC deposit); it was retrieved from the ClinicalTrials.gov results posting for NCT00375973, where every figure resolves exactly against the arm sizes (12/29 = 41.38%, 10/29 = 34.48%, 9/29 = 31.03%, 5/29 = 17.24%, 4/29 = 13.79%; 3/30 = 10.00%, 4/30 = 13.33%, 2/30 = 6.67%, 1/30 = 3.33%). primary, verified
Grade this carefully, and the grading belongs at the claim rather than below it. n=59 randomised, single trial, secondary safety data, no multiplicity control, doses 3 to 6× his, not stratified by dose, and the confidence interval on 41% from n=29 is wide. It is not proof. It is the only direct evidence that exists on this exact question, and it was hiding in a trial every prior pass already had. reasoning, not evidence

Dizziness, in context

Roughly 11.0% in fibromyalgia, ~14.5% in breast cancer, ~16.1% in depression at 60mg. The ME/CFS cohort's 31% is roughly double, against a 6.7% placebo rate. That points squarely at the orthostatic axis, which is Figure 8. primary, verified

Insomnia and somnolence, together

34% insomnia and 41% somnolence, simultaneously, in the same 29 people. These are not opposite ends of one dose axis. They are two different effects happening to different people on the same drug, and the FDA label lists both among reactions with "a significant dose-dependent relationship in fixed-dose studies". primary, verified

What the trial cannot tell him

The 60–120mg dosing was flexible and unstratified, so it cannot say what 30mg or 60mg alone would do. And the entire literature is this one trial: nobody has studied duloxetine at 20mg in ME/CFS, or its autonomic effects in ME/CFS, or duloxetine in autistic adults. no evidence exists
