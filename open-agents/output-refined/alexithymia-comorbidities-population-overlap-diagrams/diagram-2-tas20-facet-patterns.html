Autism
Heterogeneous. High-cognitive subtype + comprehensive subtype both common. Bird & Cook 2013 hypothesis emphasises cognitive dimension.

DIF dominant. Frewen lineage. EOT variable; combat samples often higher.

Comprehensive elevation typical. Schimmenti / Caretti developmental synthesis.
Eating disorders
DIF specifically predicts poor prognosis. AN restricting subtype highest. Emotion-avoidance maintenance model.
Depression
DIF and DDF co-elevated. State + trait components. Genetic correlation r=0.65 with alexithymia.
Anxiety
Overlap with depression-alexithymia. Panic disorder shows somatic-amplification pattern.
SSD / Fibromyalgia / Chronic pain
Often comprehensive elevation. Important caveat: attenuates when controlling for negative affect (Aaron 2019).
ADHD
Emotion-recognition deficits prominent. Maps to broader emotion-dysregulation phenotype in adult ADHD.
Substance use
DIF dominant. Emotion-regulation-via-substance hypothesis. Cohen's d=0.83 in clinical samples.
Schizophrenia spectrum
EOT-dominant gradient (Type II profile). Rises across psychosis-risk gradient. Caveat: narrow research lineage.
FND / PNES
Heterogeneous; often comprehensive. Autonomic-symptom misattribution mechanism (PMC4173967).
First responders
Variable. Some samples show task-focused EOT + DDF pattern; others show DIF-dominant trauma profile.
Direction of contribution contested in this population.
The takeaways. DIF (Difficulty Identifying Feelings) is the most consistently elevated facet across populations. Eating disorders are the standout case where DIF specifically predicts poor prognosis. The schizophrenia-spectrum exception is the EOT-dominant Type II pattern that rises across the psychosis-risk gradient — but from a narrow research lineage (primarily Polish and Dutch groups; replicate with caution).

Practical use of this matrix. The dominant facet conditions which intervention components are most likely to fit. DIF-dominant profiles benefit most from emotion-identification work (granularity-building, affect-labeling, structured emotion vocabulary). Comprehensive-elevation profiles (CPTSD, FND, SSD) typically need broader phase-based protocols. EOT-dominant Type II profiles warrant attention to the cognitive-affective dissociation (feelings present but illegible).

Sources: Bird & Cook 2013 PMC3731793; Frewen 2008 PubMed 18404647; Nowakowski 2013 (eating disorders); Honkalampi 2022 PMC9790486 (SUD); Aaron 2019 PMC6688175 (chronic pain); van der Velde 2015 / Ozdemir 2025 PMC12050105 (psychosis); Demartini 2014 / PMC4173967 (FND).
