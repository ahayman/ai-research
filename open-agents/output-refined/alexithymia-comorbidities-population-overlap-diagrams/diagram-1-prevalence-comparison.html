The honest reading. Alexithymia is meaningfully prevalent across most major clinical populations, typically 3–6× the general-population baseline. The highest pooled rates are in autism (~50%), PTSD (53%), and SUD (42–67%). Eating disorders, depression, anxiety, ADHD, and FND/PNES sit in the 20–50% range. The schizophrenia-spectrum gradient is real but from a narrow research lineage.

What this category does NOT do. The mechanism question — does alexithymia cause the disorder, does the disorder cause alexithymia, or do they share substrate? — is treated in Cat 7 (alexithymia ↔ trauma bidirectional loop) for the trauma cluster and Cat 8 (autism × alexithymia × cumulative overwhelm) for the autism case. This diagram establishes the rates; the trauma and autism categories establish the mechanism architecture.
