Treatment-Matching Considerations by Comorbidity

Standard treatment is typically attenuated in high-alexithymia patients across populations. Where alexithymia-targeted approaches have evidence, this matrix shows the matchings.

Depression + alexithymia CBT, antidepressants CBT response attenuated; antidepressants address state component Behavioural activation (relatively more accessible than cognitive-restructuring); MBT and emotion-focused supplementation. Moderate evidence

Higher PTSD/anxiety/depression severity moderates greater benefit from EAET. RCT-supported
ADHD + alexithymia ADHD pharmacotherapy + standard psychosocial addresses some emotion-dysregulation indirectly DBT skills adapted for ADHD; externalised emotion-tracking (apps, journaling). Emerging
SUD + alexithymia 12-step, MI, CM worse retention, lower abstinence, higher relapse DBT-SUD; emotion-regulation-focused approaches (MBT, emotion-focused therapy). Moderate evidence
Schizophrenia spectrum Antipsychotics + psychosocial intervention not specifically alexithymia-targeted Cognitive remediation with emotion-recognition components (e.g., facial-affect-recognition training). Some benefit in social-cognitive impairment in schizophrenia. Moderate evidence
FND / PNES CBT-FND, physiotherapy specific alexithymia-targeted protocols not yet standardised Under-studied as a treatment population. Emerging interest in alexithymia-targeted protocols. The biopsychosocial integrative model treats co-occurring vulnerabilities (trauma, autism, alexithymia) as treatment-relevant. Limited / emerging
Anxiety + alexithymia Standard CBT, exposure attenuated; pace interoceptive exposure carefully for parsing-deficit profile ACT, MBT; pace interoceptive-exposure components (panic disorder). Moderate evidence

The treatment-matching pattern. Across populations, the consistent finding is that standard treatment is attenuated when alexithymia is high. The strongest RCT-supported alexithymia-targeted approaches are STAIR-NT (complex PTSD) and Lumley's EAET (chronic pain, fibromyalgia, MUS, IBS). For the other populations, moderate-evidence options exist but the literature is thinner than for the standard treatments.

The gap. For autism + alexithymia, schizophrenia spectrum, FND, ADHD, first responders, and SUD, the evidence base for alexithymia-targeted treatment is moderate to limited. This is one of the most-flagged research gaps in the Cameron et al. 2024 systematic review — most published treatment claims are subtype-blind, and very few RCTs explicitly test whether different alexithymia subtypes respond differentially to different therapies.
