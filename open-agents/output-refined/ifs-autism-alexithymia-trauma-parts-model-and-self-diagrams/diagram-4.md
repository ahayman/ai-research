# Diagram 4: Lifecycle of a Burden — How a Part Becomes Burdened and Unburdens

**Type**: Mermaid state diagram
**Purpose**: Show the IFS narrative arc — pre-burden → overwhelm → burden absorbed → role hardens → witnessing → unburdening → return to non-extreme function. Makes visible the model's claim that *parts are not the problem; the burdens they carry and the roles those burdens force them into are*.

```mermaid
stateDiagram-v2
    direction TB
    [*] --> NonExtreme : Original state

    NonExtreme : Part in non-extreme role
    NonExtreme : (e.g., a discerning evaluator,<br/>a part that loves doing things well)

    NonExtreme --> Overwhelm : Overwhelming experience<br/>exceeds capacity to integrate

    Overwhelm : Pain, image, or belief<br/>cannot be metabolized

    Overwhelm --> Burdened : Part absorbs the burden<br/>(painful belief, emotion,<br/>or somatic state)

    Burdened : Part now carries content<br/>not native to it
    Burdened : Often pushed out of awareness<br/>(exile) or forced into<br/>extreme protective role

    Burdened --> RoleHardens : System organizes<br/>around containing it

    RoleHardens : Manager strategies escalate<br/>Firefighter strategies activate<br/>when containment fails
    RoleHardens : Cycling continues, often<br/>for years or decades

    RoleHardens --> SelfApproach : Therapy enters<br/>(Self present, protectors<br/>permit access to exile)

    SelfApproach : 6 Fs sequence with protector
    SelfApproach : Protector consent obtained

    SelfApproach --> Witnessed : Self witnesses<br/>the exile's experience

    Witnessed : Exile feels seen
    Witnessed : (without retraumatization)

    Witnessed --> Unburdened : Burden released<br/>(imaginally to wind, water,<br/>fire, earth, or light)

    Unburdened : Part no longer<br/>carrying the content

    Unburdened --> NonExtreme : Part returns to<br/>non-extreme function

    note right of Burdened : This is where<br/>most lives are<br/>lived, often<br/>without knowing<br/>a different state<br/>is possible.
    note right of Unburdened : The model's claim:<br/>parts can return to their<br/>original valuable function<br/>once the burden is released.
```

**Caption**: The lifecycle of a burdened part in IFS. Every part begins in a non-extreme role — a part that knows how to evaluate, a part that knows how to rest, a part that knows how to feel. An overwhelming experience leaves the part holding content (a belief, an emotion, a sensation) that is not native to it. The system organizes around containing this burden — protectors take on extreme roles, exiles get sequestered — and the burdened state can persist for years or decades. The IFS therapeutic arc enters at "Self approaches": Self engages a protector with curiosity, gets permission to meet the exile, witnesses the exile's experience, and supports release of the burden. The model holds that parts return to their non-extreme function once unburdened — the perfectionist becomes a discerning evaluator, the binge-eating Firefighter becomes a part that knows how to rest. The note on the right names something honestly: most people live in the "burdened" state without knowing a different state is possible, and the IFS clinical claim is precisely that this state is reversible.
