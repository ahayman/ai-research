## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: The 65 Semantic Primes -- Category Map
- **Type**: Mermaid mindmap
- **Elements**: 65 primes in 15 categories, 3 color zones
- **Key Insight**: The complete inventory of meaning atoms, color-coded by visual representability. The ~20 "necessarily conventional" primes (red) in the Logical, Mental, and Speech categories represent the hardest design challenge.
- **Accessibility**: Central node "65 Semantic Primes" branches to 15 categories: Substantives (I, YOU, SOMEONE, PEOPLE, SOMETHING, BODY), Determiners (THIS, THE SAME, OTHER), Quantifiers (ONE, TWO, SOME, ALL, MUCH/MANY), Evaluators (GOOD, BAD), Descriptors (BIG, SMALL), Mental Predicates (THINK, KNOW, WANT, DON'T WANT, FEEL, SEE, HEAR), Speech (SAY, WORDS, TRUE), Actions (DO, HAPPEN, MOVE), Existence (EXIST, BE, HAVE), Life (LIVE, DIE), Time (WHEN, NOW, BEFORE, AFTER, durations), Space (WHERE, HERE, ABOVE, BELOW, FAR, NEAR, SIDE, INSIDE, TOUCH), Logical (NOT, MAYBE, CAN, BECAUSE, IF), Intensifier (VERY, MORE), Taxonomy (KIND OF, PART OF), Similarity (LIKE/AS/WAY).

### Diagram 2: From Primes to Complex Meaning -- The Explication Stack
- **Type**: Bottom-up flowchart
- **Elements**: 3 layers with example items, 1 traced explication
- **Key Insight**: NSM's three-layer architecture enables unlimited meaning construction from 65 atoms. The traced example shows HAPPY decomposing into SOMEONE + FEEL + SOMETHING + GOOD + HAPPEN + WANT + THINK + BECAUSE.
- **Accessibility**: Bottom layer (blue): 65 semantic primes listed by sample. Middle layer (purple): semantic molecules with examples (HANDS, MOUTH, EAT, WATER, FIRE, MAN, WOMAN, CHILD, ROUND, HARD). Top layer (green): complex concepts (HAPPY, ANGRY, AFRAID, JUSTICE, BREAKFAST, JEALOUSY). Upward arrows show composition. Side panel traces HAPPY explication step by step.

### Diagram 3: Visual Representability Spectrum
- **Type**: SVG three-zone spectrum
- **Elements**: 65 primes distributed across 3 zones with representation strategies
- **Key Insight**: ~25 primes are directly imageable, ~20 can be metaphorically grounded through image schemas, and ~20 require conventional symbols. The question marks in the red zone signal genuine open design problems.
- **Accessibility**: Three colored zones left to right. Green zone: spatial primes (ABOVE, BELOW, INSIDE, NEAR, FAR, etc.), substantives (I, YOU, BODY), descriptors (BIG, SMALL), senses (SEE, HEAR), life (LIVE, DIE), quantity (ONE, TWO). Amber zone: evaluators via UP-DOWN metaphor (GOOD, BAD), temporal via timeline metaphor (BEFORE, AFTER, NOW), body metaphor (FEEL), quantity metaphor (SOME, ALL, MUCH). Red zone: logical (NOT, IF, BECAUSE, MAYBE, CAN), mental (THINK, KNOW, WANT), speech (SAY, WORDS, TRUE), abstract relations (THE SAME, KIND OF, LIKE, VERY, MORE).

### Diagram 4: NSM Primes vs. Image Schemas -- Overlap Map
- **Type**: SVG Venn diagram
- **Elements**: 2 overlapping circles with labeled items in 3 zones
- **Key Insight**: NSM primes and image schemas are complementary, not competing. The overlap zone (INSIDE/CONTAINER, ABOVE/UP-DOWN, MOVE/PATH) is the "visual sweet spot." The non-overlapping zones provide complementary resources. A visual language needs both.
- **Accessibility**: Left circle (blue): NSM primes only -- abstract primes (IF, NOT, MAYBE, BECAUSE, CAN), mental/speech (THINK, KNOW, SAY, WORDS), taxonomy (KIND OF, LIKE), intensifiers (VERY, MORE), other (OTHER, HAVE, EXIST, BE, HAPPEN, DO, GOOD, BAD). Overlap zone (purple): INSIDE=CONTAINER, ABOVE/BELOW=UP-DOWN, NEAR/FAR=PROXIMITY, MOVE=PATH, PART OF=PART-WHOLE, TOUCH=CONTACT, BIG/SMALL=SCALE; metaphorical bridges: BECAUSE~FORCE, WANT~ATTRACTION, NOT~BLOCKAGE, IF~PATH-BRANCH. Right circle (green): image schemas only -- BALANCE, CYCLE, LINK, CENTER-PERIPHERY, SPLITTING/MERGING, FULL-EMPTY, FORCE, COUNTERFORCE, COMPULSION, RESTRAINT, ENABLEMENT, ITERATION, SURFACE, MASS-COUNT.

### Diagram 5: Three-Layer Architecture for a Visual Language
- **Type**: Bottom-up flowchart
- **Elements**: 4 layers with example items, 1 note of caution
- **Key Insight**: A proposed architecture combining image schemas (visual vocabulary) with NSM primes (semantic atoms), molecules (intermediate chunks), and open composition (unlimited expressiveness). This is a hypothesis, not a proven design.
- **Accessibility**: Layer 0 (green): image schema foundation -- CONTAINER, PATH, FORCE, BALANCE, UP-DOWN, LINK, SCALE, CYCLE. Layer 1 (blue): 65 semantic primes in 3 groups -- imageable (use schema icons directly), metaphor-bridged (use schemas via metaphor), conventional (learnable symbols). Layer 2 (purple): semantic molecules -- HANDS, MOUTH, EAT, WATER, FIRE, MAN, WOMAN, CHILD, ROUND, HARD. Layer 3 (amber): open composition examples -- HAPPY, ANGRY, JUSTICE, BREAKFAST. Italic note acknowledges this is hypothetical.
