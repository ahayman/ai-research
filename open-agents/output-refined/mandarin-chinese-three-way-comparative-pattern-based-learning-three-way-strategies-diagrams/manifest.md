## Diagram Inventory

### 1. Regularity Scorecard: Mandarin vs. English Head-to-Head
- **File**: `diagram-1-regularity-scorecard.html`
- **Type**: HTML table with color-coded winners and score bar
- **Content**: Ten regularity dimensions compared: verb forms, plurals, numbers, tones/stress, agreement, sound-symbol mapping, script entry, vocabulary transparency, gender, word order. Mandarin wins 7, English wins 2, 1 mixed. Score bar visualization.
- **Research Section**: Section 2 (The Regularity Scorecard)
- **Features**: Dark theme, winner color coding (cyan for Mandarin, amber for English, purple for mixed), score bar summary

### 2. Three-Way Transfer Matrix
- **File**: `diagram-2-transfer-matrix.html`
- **Type**: Four-card grid showing transfer patterns
- **Content**: English→Mandarin positive transfer (5 items), Traditional Chinese→Mandarin positive transfer (5 items), English→Mandarin traps/negative transfer (5 items), Traditional Chinese→Mandarin adjustments needed (5 items). Summary count bar.
- **Research Section**: Sections 3 (English Transfer) + 4 (Traditional Chinese Transfer)
- **Features**: Dark theme, color-coded cards (blue for English positive, gold for Traditional positive, red for traps, amber for adjustments)

### 3. Learning Curve Comparison: Mandarin vs. English
- **File**: `diagram-3-learning-curve-comparison.html`
- **Type**: SVG dual-curve graph with phase labels
- **Content**: English curve starts fast (26 letters) but decelerates due to irregularity drag. Mandarin curve starts slow (tones + characters) but accelerates as pattern recognition kicks in. Crossover point at ~9-12 months. Three learning phases labeled.
- **Research Section**: Sections 1 (Core Thesis) + 10 (Optimal Learning Sequence)
- **Features**: Dark theme, crossover annotation, phase description boxes

### 4. The "Unfamiliar ≠ Irregular" Matrix
- **File**: `diagram-4-unfamiliar-irregular-matrix.html`
- **Type**: SVG 2x2 quadrant diagram with plotted features
- **Content**: Four quadrants — Q1: Familiar+Regular (Easy: SVO, numbers), Q2: Unfamiliar+Regular (Learnable: tones, characters, classifiers — Mandarin's sweet spot), Q3: Familiar+Irregular (Frustrating: English irregular verbs, spelling, stress — English's weakness), Q4: Unfamiliar+Irregular (Hard: chengyu, broken phonetics).
- **Research Section**: Section 9 (The Unfamiliar ≠ Irregular Matrix)
- **Features**: Dark theme, quadrant color coding, feature pill labels, insight boxes

### 5. Radical-Based Character Learning Acceleration
- **File**: `diagram-5-radical-acceleration-curve.html`
- **Type**: HTML stage cards with multiplier badges + phonetic family example
- **Content**: Four stages of component knowledge: 10+10→~100 chars (x10), 30+50→~800 (x16), 50+100→~3,000/literacy (x20), 100+200→5,000+/mastery (x25+). Growth multipliers shown. 青 qīng phonetic family example showing how one component generates 6 characters via different radicals.
- **Research Section**: Section 6 (Character Learning Acceleration)
- **Features**: Dark theme, progressive color staging, radical-labeled family members

### 6. Three-Way Structural Comparison Master Grid
- **File**: `diagram-6-three-way-master-grid.html`

- **Content**: All 15 categories from the deep dive summarized in three columns (Mandarin Simplified / Traditional Chinese / English). Color-coded indicators showing where Mandarin and Traditional are identical (green "same"), where they differ (amber), and where English diverges from both (red).
- **Research Section**: Section 8 (Three-Way Master Comparison)
