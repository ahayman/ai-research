## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Sleep Adverse Events by Dose
- **Type**: Grouped bar chart (3 AEs × 4 dose arms) + full AE table + two hedge panels
- **Elements**: Insomnia / somnolence / fatigue at placebo, 20, 60, 120mg; a shaded danger band behind every 120mg bar; "HIS STEP" brackets spanning the 20 and 60mg bars in each group; "the inflection" callouts under 120mg
- **Key Insight**: The two middle bars are indistinguishable in every group. Insomnia actually falls 20→60. The inflection is at 120mg. The step he is considering sits on the flat part of every curve.
- **Corrections carried**: this is the only duloxetine trial with posted results containing a fixed 20mg arm, found after scanning all 135; the Wilson intervals for 20mg and 60mg overlap almost entirely, so even the 120mg spike is suggestive rather than decisive; differential dropout (24.1%) *flatters* 120mg; absence of dream/RLS terms is weak evidence of absence at a ≥1% threshold, not evidence of safety
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc giving all twelve values

### Diagram 2: What He Fears, and What the Data Shows
- **Type**: Two symmetric slope charts (insomnia left, somnolence right) + trial-by-trial table + three panels
- **Elements**: Green panel (insomnia: every slope flat or falling, 120mg spike dashed); red panel (somnolence: three rising slopes plus the one flat dashed green slope from the 20mg-arm trial); shared 0–20% axis
- **Key Insight**: He is watching the insomnia door; the traffic is at the somnolence door. Somnolence arrives dressed as the thing he is protecting (waking less tired), through a channel he has said he cannot read.
- **Corrections carried**: **the honest complication is drawn, not buried** — somnolence is flat 20→60 (5.08%→4.31%) in the only trial with a 20mg arm, and the three rising slopes are all 30→60 steps in trials with no 20mg arm, so "somnolence definitely rises at his step" is NOT supported; somnolence IS dose-dependent per the FDA label (10–11% vs 3%); the widely-quoted "1.5%" is a **discontinuation** rate
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc enumerating every slope

### Diagram 3: The Vivid-Dreams Graveyard
- **Type**: Three headstone panels + a 3×3 dissociation matrix + a black verdict band + claim table
- **Elements**: Each dead explanation with a "cause of death"; the Nicolas & Ruby matrix (trimipramine no-REM-change/recall-down; clomipramine REM-down/recall-unchanged; fluoxetine REM-down/recall-up) colour-coded to show every combination occurs; Vogel handled in a separate red panel
- **Key Insight**: Three drugs exhaust the possible combinations of REM effect and recall effect, so the relationship is not mechanistic. **Nobody knows why antidepressants intensify dreams.**
- **Corrections carried**: REM rebound is a DISCONTINUATION phenomenon and he is on a steady dose; Pace-Schott found recall DOWN while intensity UP; awakenings, not REM, explain recall; Vogel is never-replicated with a possibly inverted mechanism and must not carry the morning-fatigue story; what survives is 13.79% vs 3.33% and "he is in the 14%"
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc

### Diagram 4: The Only Dose Comparison That Exists
- **Type**: Two-arm comparison with pill timelines + three-box confound decomposition + collapse arrows + dose ladder
- **Elements**: Arm A (80mg QD, one morning pill, continuity improved) vs Arm B (60mg BID, morning + evening pill, continuity reduced), with the evening dose visually marked; three amber/red boxes for the three simultaneously-changing variables; a black band stating you cannot tell which caused it; a dose ladder marking 20/30/60mg QD as never tested
- **Key Insight**: The arm that hurt sleep continuity is the only one with an evening dose. Dose, schedule and evening-dosing change together, and n=6 cannot separate them. The regimen he would actually take was never tested.
- **Corrections carried**: nobody has ever measured duloxetine's sleep architecture at two doses in patients; the morning-vs-evening inference is reasoning, not evidence; there is no trial of morning vs evening dosing; the "morning dosing reduces insomnia ~60%" figure has no traceable primary and should be treated as fabricated
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc

### Diagram 5: The Nap Test — Informative for a Yes, Worthless for a No
- **Type**: Two asymmetry panels + opposing-arrow confound diagram + horizontal magnitude bars + 10-rule protocol table
- **Elements**: Green "informative" panel vs red "uninterpretable" panel; duloxetine box with a green arrow (arousal down → latency falls) and a red arrow (sleep improved → less drive → latency rises) colliding in an amber "these cancel" box; Bonnet & Arand's four measured latencies as bars (3.8 / 6.9 / 11.9 / 13.4 min)
- **Key Insight**: The drug pushes the outcome both ways at once, so a null is uninterpretable and must not read to him as a refutation. A five-minute walk moves latency more than any plausible drug effect.
- **Corrections carried**: the sleep-drive confound is **the killer** and is drawn, not filed; he has been running an aborted test (protocol allows 20 min, he quits at 10–20), so his lifelong binary may be a latency of 18 that has never been let run; the effort paradox is his specific risk; no MSLT study of duloxetine or any SNRI exists
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc

### Diagram 6: The Reversal — Not Napping May Be Protecting His Sleep
- **Type**: Two opposed causal-arrow panels + 101-dot prevalence grid + black verdict band + limits list
- **Elements**: His hypothesis (cannot nap → causes → poor sleep) in amber vs the Gotts direction (not napping → PROTECTS → night sleep) in indigo; a grid of 101 dots with 21 filled, bracketed and labelled; Gotts' verbatim conclusion in both panels
- **Key Insight**: His causal arrow may run backwards. The thing he wants to fix may be the thing currently working for him.
- **Corrections carried**: 21/101 (20.8%) never napped in 14 days, so he is in that fifth and it is common not anomalous; napping was associated with WORSE cognition and MORE sleepiness; **the three limits are stated at the claim** — Gotts measured reported napping not ABILITY (and his claim is about ability), it is cross-sectional so direction is unresolvable, and it is severity-confounded
- **Accessibility**: SVG role="img" with title and desc

## Design Notes

- **Self-contained**: no external CSS, JS, fonts or images. Inline `<style>` and hand-authored SVG only. No CDN dependencies.
- **Provenance inline**: every diagram carries its grades in the figure itself or in an adjacent panel, never only in a footer. This is BINDING-CORRECTIONS §1, which is doubly binding on this page because appendix-filed caveats are this chain's signature failure.
- **Caveats drawn, not filed**: where a finding has a disqualifying limit (Chalon's confound, the AIS non-replication, the somnolence 20→60 flatness, Gotts' reported-vs-ability gap), the limit appears in the same visual as the finding.
- **Colour semantics**: indigo = his own data / the strongest tier; green = supported or reassuring; amber = fragile, confounded or hedged; red = harmful, dead, or absent; black bands = the honest verdict.
- **Accessibility**: every SVG has `role="img"` with `<title>` and `<desc>`; the desc restates the quantitative content so the figure is usable without vision. Colour is never the sole carrier of meaning (labels and text duplicate every signal).
- **Responsive**: `viewBox` with `max-width: 100%; height: auto`; all tables sit in the page's `.table-scroll` wrapper when embedded.
