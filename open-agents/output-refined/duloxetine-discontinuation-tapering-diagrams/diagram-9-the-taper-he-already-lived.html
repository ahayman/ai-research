The Taper You Already Ran

Four identical 5mg reductions, every two weeks, coming off escitalopram a year ago. Three of them cost you nothing you could detect. The fourth cost you two weeks. Here is what those four identical steps were actually doing.

“Every 2 weeks I reduce the dose by 5mg… Each dosage reduction did nothing as far as I could tell. When I finally quit, though, (from 2 weeks at 5mg → 0mg), I lost 2 weeks of functionality.”

Why three steps were free and one was not

Each bar is one 5mg reduction. Identical in milligrams. This is what they cost in serotonin transporter occupancy.

30×

The last step is thirty times the first. Same five milligrams, thirty times the pharmacology. It is also 16× the second step and 6.5× the largest intermediate one. Your three painless reductions were sliding along the flat top of the curve, where five milligrams buys almost nothing in either direction. Your fourth fell off the steep bottom, where five milligrams is most of the drug's engagement with your brain.

This is the hyperbolic model's central prediction, and you confirmed it without knowing it existed. The model says a linear milligram taper gets exponentially harsher as it approaches zero, so the last steps should be the smallest. You ran that experiment on yourself, in even 5mg steps at half the label's pace, and it came back at thirty to one in the predicted direction. It also tells you what would not have helped: going slower. Slower steps of the same size still end at the same drop. The binding constraint is the size of the last step, not the interval between them.
One mismatch, stated rather than smoothed. The model ranks 10→5mg as the second-worst step at 9.2 points, and you report it as nothing. Either your detection threshold sits above roughly nine points, or the real curve is even more end-loaded than this model, which is what a published fit with a Hill coefficient below 1 predicts [primary, verified — Kim 2017, PMID 27557550]. Both readings leave the 30× ratio intact, so nothing here turns on which is right.

Provenance: the underlying occupancy measurements [primary, verified] — Klein 2006, PMID 16955282; single dose, midbrain, [123I]ADAM SPECT, n=5 per arm.
