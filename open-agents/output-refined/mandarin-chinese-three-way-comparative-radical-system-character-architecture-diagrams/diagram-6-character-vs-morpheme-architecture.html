Two systems for building meaning from sub-word parts: spatial (2D) vs sequential (1D)

Same Concept, Different Architecture
"electricity"
ZH
电 (diàn)
Traditional 電: 雨 rain + 申 lightning = electricity. Simplified 电 lost the rain radical.
EN
electricity
Greek elektron (amber) + Latin -icity (state/quality). Named for amber's static charge.
"computer"
ZH
电脑 (diànnǎo)
电 electric + 脑 brain = "electric brain". Two existing characters combined.
EN
computer
Latin computare (to reckon) + -er (agent). Root: com- (together) + putare (to think).
"telephone"
ZH
电话 (diànhuà)
电 electric + 话 speech = "electric speech". 话 contains 讠(speech radical).
EN
telephone
Greek tele (far) + phone (voice/sound). Two Greek roots compounded.
"psychology"
ZH
心理学 (xīnlǐxué)
心 heart/mind + 理 principle + 学 study = "heart-principle study".
EN
psychology
Greek psyche (soul/mind) + logos (study). Two Greek roots compounded.

Both systems use sub-word building blocks to create meaning. The key difference: Chinese encodes meaning and sound in separate visible components arranged in 2D space, while English concatenates morphemes in a 1D string.
