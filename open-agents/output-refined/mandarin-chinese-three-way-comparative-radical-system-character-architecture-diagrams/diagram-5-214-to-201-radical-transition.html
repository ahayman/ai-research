System Comparison
Traditional Kangxi System
214
Principal radicals. Designed for traditional (unsimplified) characters. Used in Taiwan, Hong Kong, Macau, and most international reference works. Standard for ~300 years.
Simplified PRC Standard
201 + 100
201 principal + 100 associated indexing components. Designed for simplified characters. National standard for PRC dictionaries since 2009. Used in Xinhua Zidian (11th ed+).
Types of Changes
Removed: Obsolete Radicals
Extremely rare Kangxi radicals that no longer head any characters in modern use were dropped from the principal list.
艮
gen (5 chars in Kangxi, rarely used today)
鬯
chang (sacrificial wine)
龠
yue (flute, ancient instrument)
鼓
gu (drum)

Radicals that were simplified alongside their characters now have distinct simplified forms used in PRC dictionaries.

Merged: Identical After Simplification
Some radicals that were distinct in traditional form became visually identical after simplification, so they were merged into a single entry.

Added: New Associated Components
100 "associated indexing components" were added to handle characters whose primary component doesn't match any of the 201 principal radicals.
龙
dragon (simplified standalone)
电
electricity (lost original radical)

Semantic lossSome simplified radicals lost their visual connection to meaning

Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Macau continue to use the 214 Kangxi radicals. The 201-radical system is specific to PRC simplified Chinese dictionaries.
