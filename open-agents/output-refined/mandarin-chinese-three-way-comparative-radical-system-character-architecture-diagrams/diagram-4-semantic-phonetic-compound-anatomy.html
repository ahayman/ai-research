Semantic-Phonetic Compound Anatomy

~80% of Chinese characters combine a meaning hint (semantic radical) with a sound hint (phonetic component)

The 青 (qing) Phonetic Family
青
qing (1st tone) — "blue-green, young"
Phonetic Component — shared by an entire character family
清
qīng
clear, pure, clean
氵 + 青 = 清
Water domain + qing sound = clear (water). Exact tone match.
请
qǐng
to request, please
讠 + 青 = 请
Speech domain + qing sound = to request (verbal act). Tone differs: 1st→3rd.
情
qing
emotion, feeling, affection
忄 + 青 = 情
Heart/mind domain + qing sound = emotion (of the heart). Tone differs: 1st→2nd.
晴
qing
sunny, clear sky
日 + 青 = 晴
Sun domain + qing sound = sunny (clear sky). Tone differs: 1st→2nd.
精
jīng
essence, refined, spirit
米 + 青 = 精
Rice domain + qing sound = essence (refined grain). Initial changed: q→j.
睛
jīng
eyeball, pupil
目 + 青 = 睛
Eye domain + qing sound = eyeball. Initial changed: q→j.

Sound changes over 2,000+ years explain why phonetic components often only approximate modern pronunciation. Characters were phonetically precise when first created in ancient Chinese.
