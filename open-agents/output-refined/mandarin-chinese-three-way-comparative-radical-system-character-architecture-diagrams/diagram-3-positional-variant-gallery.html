Why Do Radicals Change Shape?
Every Chinese character occupies the same uniform square, regardless of complexity. When a radical must share that square with another component, it compresses to make room. The most common transformation is left-side compression (the radical narrows to fit beside the phonetic component on the right). Bottom-position variants (like 灬) flatten horizontally. Right-side variants (like 刂) are rarer and typically involve vertical compression. These are not different radicals — they are the same radical wearing a "work uniform" for a particular position.
