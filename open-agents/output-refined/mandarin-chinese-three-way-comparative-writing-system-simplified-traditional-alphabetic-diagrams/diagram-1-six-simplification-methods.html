Six Methods of Character Simplification

How the PRC transformed 2,236 traditional characters (1956-1964)

Method 1
Cursive/Popular Variant Adoption
草书楷化 caoshu kaihua
Regularized forms already in common handwritten use, derived from centuries of cursive script tradition.
書 → 书
shu "book"
10→4 strokes -60%
東 → 东
dong "east"
8→5 strokes -38%
車 → 车
che "vehicle"
7→4 strokes -43%
Structural Consequence
Least disruptive -- formalized existing practice. Minimal semantic information lost since these forms were already widely recognized.
Method 2
Stroke Reduction
省略笔画 shenglue bihua
Removed strokes or simplified internal components while retaining recognizable overall structure.
龍 → 龙
long "dragon"
16→5 strokes -69%
門 → 门
men "door"
8→3 strokes -63%
馬 → 马
ma "horse"
10→3 strokes -70%
Structural Consequence
Pictographic transparency often lost. Traditional 龍 evokes a dragon; simplified 龙 is abstract. Traditional 馬 shows head, mane, legs; simplified 马 retains only a skeletal outline.
Method 3
Component Substitution
更换偏旁 genghuan pianpang
Replaced complex components with simpler ones, often using phonetic substitutes to maintain pronunciation cues.
燈 → 灯
deng "lamp"
16→6 strokes -63%
驚 → 惊
jing "surprise"
22→11 strokes -50%
遠 → 远
yuan "far"
13→7 strokes -46%
Structural Consequence
Phonetic component reliability may shift. In 灯, 丁 (ding) replaced 登 (deng) -- close but not exact match. Sometimes the substitution improves phonetic accuracy; sometimes it degrades it.
Method 4
Systematic Radical Simplification
简化偏旁 jianhua pianpang
Simplified recurring radicals across ALL characters containing them -- a cascading change affecting 1,754 characters.
言→讠 affects 语 说 认 话
"speech" radical
7→2 strokes -71%
金→钅 affects 钱 铁 银 锅
"metal" radical
8→5 strokes -38%
食→饣 affects 饭 饮 饿 饱
"food" radical
9→3 strokes -67%
Structural Consequence
Highest efficiency -- 54 simplified radicals cascaded to 1,754 characters. Semantic meaning is preserved (讠 still means "speech") but visual distinctiveness reduced. This method produced the most simplifications with the fewest rules.
Method 5
Character Merger
合并简化 hebing jianhua
Merged two or more distinct traditional characters into a single simplified form. The most controversial method.
髮+發 → 发
hair + develop
2 chars merged into 1
乾+幹+干 → 干
dry + do + shield
3 chars merged into 1
後+后 → 后
after + empress
2 chars merged into 1
Structural Consequence
Creates genuine ambiguity. In 头发发白 "hair turns white," 发 appears twice but represents different traditional characters (髮 and 發). Context must disambiguate. Automated simplified-to-traditional conversion becomes non-trivial.
Method 6
New Character Creations
另造新字 lingzao xinzi
Created entirely new simplified forms, sometimes with improved semantic logic through novel compositions.
態 → 态
tai "attitude"
14→8 strokes (太+心)
體 → 体
ti "body"
23→7 strokes (亻+本)
塵 → 尘
chen "dust"
14→6 strokes (小+土)
Structural Consequence
Sometimes improved semantic transparency: 尘 = 小 "small" + 土 "earth" = "dust" is arguably more intuitive than traditional 塵. But 体 = 亻"person" + 本 "root" for "body" is less transparent than 體's complex composition.
