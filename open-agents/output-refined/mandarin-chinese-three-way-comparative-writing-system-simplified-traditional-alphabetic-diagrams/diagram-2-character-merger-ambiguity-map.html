2:1 merger
Ambiguity example: 头发发白 vs. Traditional 頭髮發白 -- "hair turns white." Both 发 characters look identical but derive from different traditional characters with different tones and meanings.
理发 lifa = haircut (from 髮)  •  发展 fazhan = develop (from 發)

3:1 merger
Triple merger: Three distinct traditional characters collapsed into the simplest existing form. The character 干 originally meant "shield" but now carries all three meanings.
干净 ganjing = clean (from 乾)  •  干部 ganbu = cadre (from 幹)  •  干涉 ganshe = interfere (from 干)
Tone differences help in speech but are invisible in writing

台
4:1 merger
Quadruple merger: Four traditional characters with the same pronunciation but different semantic fields collapsed into one. Particularly notable because 台灣 (Taiwan) uses this character.
台风 taifeng = typhoon (from 颱)  •  台灯 taideng = desk lamp (from 檯)  •  舞台 wutai = stage (from 臺)
Traditional characters preserve semantic distinctions: 颱 has wind radical 風, 檯 has wood radical 木

后
2:1 merger
Same tone merger: Both traditional characters share the same tone, making even spoken context less helpful.
以后 yihou = afterward (from 後)  •  皇后 huanghou = empress (from 后)
Context resolves easily here -- "empress" appears only in specific historical/cultural contexts

面
2:1 merger
Semantic radical loss: Traditional 麵 contains the 麥 (wheat) radical, making the connection to grain/flour visually obvious. Simplified 面 loses this entirely.
面条 miantiao = noodles (from 麵)  •  面对 miandui = face/confront (from 面)
面包 "bread" (from 麵包) loses the visual wheat connection present in traditional form

里
2:1 merger
Common merger: The traditional character 裏 contains the "clothing" radical 衣, indicating something wrapped/inside. Simplified 里 uses the simpler existing character.
这里 zheli = here (from 裏)  •  公里 gongli = kilometer (from 里)
One of the most frequent mergers -- 里 appears in extremely common words
