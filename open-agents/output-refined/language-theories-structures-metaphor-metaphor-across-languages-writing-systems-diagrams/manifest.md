### 1. Cross-Cultural Time Conceptualization Map
- **File**: `diagram-1-cross-cultural-time-map.html`
- **Type**: Four-panel spatial-temporal comparison (SVG)
- **Content**: Four languages demonstrating four fundamentally different spatial-temporal metaphor systems side by side. Panel 1: English -- horizontal sagittal axis with future=ahead and past=behind, with human figure facing forward and example expressions. Panel 2: Mandarin Chinese -- dual-axis system showing both vertical (earlier=up via 上个月/下个月) and horizontal (前=past front, 后=future behind) mappings, citing Boroditsky 2001. Panel 3: Aymara -- reversed horizontal axis where past (nayra/eye/front) is visible in front and future (qhipa/back) is unseen behind, with gesture arrows showing speakers pointing backward for future events (Nunez & Sweetser 2006). Panel 4: Kuuk Thaayorre -- cardinal/solar axis with compass rose showing timeline flowing east-to-west following the sun's path, independent of speaker orientation (Boroditsky & Gaby). Center annotation: "All use space for time -- none use the same mapping."

### 2. Chinese Radical Semantic Web

### 6. Japanese Emotion Body Map
- **File**: `diagram-6-japanese-emotion-body-map.html`
- **Type**: Annotated human body diagram with four zones (SVG)
- **Content**: A human figure with four metaphorical loci marked and connected to detailed annotation panels. Atama/Head (頭, blue): rational thought, anger rising -- atama ni kuru "comes to head" = anger, atama ga yoi "good head" = intelligent. Kokoro/Heart-Mind (心, red): holistic emotion+thought unified in the chest -- kokoro ga itamu "heart hurts" = emotional pain, kokorozuyoi "strong heart" = reassured. Unlike English, kokoro unifies emotion and cognition. Hara/Belly (腹, gold): authentic intention, moral character -- hara ga tatsu "belly stands up" = angry, hara o waru "split belly" = speak frankly, hara ga kuroi "black belly" = scheming. No English equivalent. Ki/Breath (気, green): flowing vital energy for mood and temperament -- kibun, kimochi, genki. Non-localized. Mushi/Insect (虫, purple): imagined creature in the belly for instinctive reactions -- mushi ga sukanai "worm doesn't like it" = instinctive dislike. English comparison figure showing far less differentiated system (head=thought, heart=emotion, gut=instinct, spine=courage).

### 7. Four-Language Structural Comparison
- **File**: `diagram-7-four-language-structural-comparison.html`
- **Type**: Four-panel card comparison with synthesis (CSS/HTML)
- **Content**: Side-by-side comparison of how Chinese (isolating/logographic), Arabic (fusional/abjad), Indo-European/English (inflecting/alphabetic), and Japanese (agglutinating/multi-script) each build metaphors differently. Each card shows: the language's metaphorical machinery, three concrete examples with formulas and glosses, and a "unique" insight. Chinese: radical composition + free morpheme juxtaposition + classifier categorization. Arabic: trilateral root + wazn pattern system transforming meaning from within. English: etymological extension + derivational morphology + dead metaphor accumulation. Japanese: kanji semantics + 4,500 mimetic words + keigo social metaphor + dual readings. Synthesis bar: "All four can express any metaphor but use fundamentally different structural machinery." Bottom row: "How Each Language Builds UNDERSTANDING" -- Chinese 理解 "logic-untangle" (untangling), Arabic فهم F-H-M (root comprehension), English "comprehend" (grasping), Japanese 分かる "to divide" (separating).

### 8. Script Relativity Transparency Spectrum
- **File**: `diagram-8-script-relativity-transparency-spectrum.html`
- **Type**: Horizontal spectrum with brain processing comparison (SVG)
- **Content**: A horizontal gradient from Transparent (green, left) to Opaque (red, right) showing where different script types fall on the metaphorical visibility spectrum. Chinese (most transparent): 明 = sun+moon = bright, you can SEE the metaphor; 电脑 = electric+brain = computer; 休 = person+tree = rest. Arabic (partially transparent): K-T-B root connections recognizable to literate speakers; wazn patterns systematic but require morphological knowledge. Japanese (mixed): kanji radicals transparent, mimetic sound-symbolism partially visible, dual readings create two layers. English (most opaque): "comprehend" = Latin "seize" invisible without scholarly knowledge; "understand" = OE "stand among" completely dead; "obey" from "hear toward" invisible. Lower section: brain processing comparison with simplified diagrams -- logographic readers show greater left middle frontal gyrus and right hemisphere activation (holistic/visuospatial), alphabetic readers show greater left superior temporal activation (phonological/sequential). Implications: logographic = metaphor as visual gestalt; alphabetic = metaphor opaque to reader. Caveat box: script relativity hypothesis currently supported only at weak level -- channeling effects during reading confirmed, general cognitive effects unclear.
