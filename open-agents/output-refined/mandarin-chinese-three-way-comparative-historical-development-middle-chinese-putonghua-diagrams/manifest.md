# Diagram Manifest — Historical Development: From Middle Chinese to Putonghua

## Diagrams

### 1. Triple Timeline (diagram-1-triple-timeline.html)
- **Type**: Parallel timeline
- **Content**: Three parallel tracks showing Middle Chinese → Mandarin sound changes, Cantonese conservation, and English Old→Modern evolution. Highlights that Mandarin is the MOST innovative (most changed) Chinese variety, not the most conservative.
- **Dimensions**: 1100 x 720
- **Key data**: Sound inventory reduction (8 tones → 4, 36 initials → 21, 200+ finals → 35), entering tone loss, voiced obstruent loss, palatalization, -m → -n merger. Cantonese preservation of all features. English Great Vowel Shift parallel.

### 2. Standardization Milestones (diagram-2-standardization-milestones.html)
- **Type**: Annotated horizontal timeline
- **Content**: 10 key milestones from 1913 Pronunciation Conference through 2020 ~80% adoption, spanning Republic and PRC eras. Comprehension rate annotations along bottom.
- **Dimensions**: 1060 x 680
- **Key data**: 1913 conference, 1918 Zhuyin, 1932 Beijing standard pivot, 1949 split, 1956 definition, 1958 Pinyin, 1982 Constitution Art. 19, 1994 PSC test, 2000 Language Law, 2020 80.7% adoption.

### 3. Tone Reduction Cascade (diagram-3-tone-reduction-cascade.html)
- **Type**: Flow diagram (Sankey-style)
- **Content**: Middle Chinese 8 tone categories mapped to Mandarin 4 tones and Cantonese 6-9 tones. Shows the "chaotic redistribution" of entering-tone syllables across all four Mandarin tones. Character examples with Mandarin vs Cantonese pronunciations.
- **Dimensions**: 1060 x 750
- **Key data**: MC yin/yang splits for ping/shang/qu/ru, Mandarin T1-T4 mappings, Cantonese T1-T9, entering-tone examples (六 luk6, 十 sap6, 國 gwok3).

### 4. Three Pillars of Putonghua (diagram-4-three-pillars.html)
- **Type**: Architecture / column diagram
- **Content**: Three-column diagram showing Beijing Phonology, Northern Dialect Base, and Vernacular Literary Norms as supporting pillars of the Putonghua standard. Each pillar shows includes/excludes and key insights. English comparison at bottom.
- **Dimensions**: 1000 x 700
- **Key data**: 1956 State Council definition text, Beijing phonology ≠ Beijing dialect distinction, northern dialect coverage (~800M speakers), literary norm examples (Lu Xun, Mao Dun, Lao She).

### 5. Comprehension Growth Chart (diagram-5-comprehension-growth.html)
- **Type**: Line chart with annotations
- **Content**: Putonghua self-reported comprehension rates from 1956 to 2025 target, with policy milestone annotations and a subdued English standardization comparison line. Highlights stagnation period and acceleration after 2000.
- **Dimensions**: 1000 x 600
- **Key data**: ~54% (1960s), 53% (2000), ~70% (2010), 80.7% (2020), 85% (2025 target). Policy milestones: 1956 definition, 1958 Pinyin, 1982 Constitution, 1994 PSC, 2000 Language Law.

### 6. Cross-Strait Divergence (diagram-6-cross-strait-divergence.html)
- **Type**: Divergence/convergence timeline
- **Content**: 1932 shared standard splitting at 1949 into PRC Putonghua (top track) and Taiwan Guoyu (bottom track), with maximum divergence ~1970s-80s and reconvergence from 1990s. Four dimensions of divergence (writing, phonetic notation, vocabulary, grammar) rated by severity.
- **Dimensions**: 1060 x 720
- **Key data**: Writing system (HIGH), phonetic notation (HIGH), vocabulary (MODERATE, ~2,000+ differences), grammar (LOW). Vocabulary examples for both sides.
