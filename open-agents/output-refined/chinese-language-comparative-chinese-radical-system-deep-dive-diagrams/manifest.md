### Diagram 2: Semantic Field Clustering -- The Radical Periodic Table
- **File:** `diagram-2-semantic-field-clustering.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG grid of semantic domains
- **Description:** Eight major semantic domains (Water, Fire, Wood, Heart-Mind, Metal, Person, Mouth, Speech, Illness) each showing the radical, character count, and representative characters. ### Diagram 3: Positional Variants -- The Shape-Shifting Radicals
- **File:** `diagram-3-positional-variants.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG comparison grid with parsing algorithm
- **Description:** Eight radical transformations showing full form to positional variant (water to three dots, fire to four dots, heart to three strokes, etc.). English comparison: no visual parsing algorithm exists.
- **Key concepts:** positional variants, spatial grammar, visual parsing algorithm, semantic vs. phonetic position

### Diagram 4: The Qing Family -- Semantic-Phonetic Compound Network
- **File:** `diagram-4-qing-family-network.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG radial network diagram
- **Description:** Central phonetic component (qing) radiating to six derived characters, each with a different semantic radical that determines the meaning domain (water for clarity, speech for invitation, heart for emotion, sun for weather, eye for vision, rice for refinement). Shows phonetic drift in two cases (jing vs. qing). Includes phonetic reliability ranges (approximate, varying by study). English comparison: "clear," "please," "feeling" share nothing visible.
- **Key concepts:** semantic-phonetic compounds (xingshengzi), phonetic component families, phonetic drift, 80% rule

### Diagram 5: Learning Acceleration Curve -- Characters vs. Alphabet
- **File:** `diagram-5-learning-acceleration-curve.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG dual-curve comparison with data table
- **Description:** Two learning curves plotted: English/alphabetic (fast start, plateau) vs. Chinese characters (slow start, accelerating returns). Crossover point marked where character learning overtakes alphabetic in efficiency. Phase annotations show the "radical knowledge zone" where acceleration begins. Bottom table shows component knowledge progression (20 radicals to 500+ characters, 50 radicals to 1,500+, 100 radicals to literacy threshold).
- **Key concepts:** network effect, accelerating returns, component-based learning, crossover point
