## Diagrams

### Diagram 1: 3,300-Year Script Evolution Timeline
- **File:** `diagram-1-script-evolution-timeline.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG horizontal timeline with event cards
- **Description:** Seven script stages from oracle bone (~1250 BCE) through simplified (1956) plotted on a timeline with key characteristics for each stage. Four event cards highlight major historical moments (oracle bone discovery, Qin unification, May Fourth Movement, simplification reform). Notes the compositional principle present from the start (27% semantic-phonetic compounds in oracle bone script). Uses corrected oracle bone decipherment count (~1,200-1,500).
- **Key concepts:** script evolution, oracle bone, seal script, clerical script, regular script, dual-track (formal/speed), continuous compositional logic

### Diagram 2: The Divergence-Standardization Cycle
- **File:** `diagram-2-divergence-standardization-cycle.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG cyclical diagram with four historical instances
- **Description:** Central four-phase cycle (Divergence, Political Trigger, Standardization, Drift) with four concrete historical instances: Qin unification (221 BCE), Kangxi Dictionary (1716), May Fourth Movement (1919), PRC reform (1956). Cross-language comparison shows Chinese (centralized control) vs. French (institutional control) vs. English (organic evolution) vs. Arabic (religious authority).
- **Key concepts:** divergence-standardization cycle, centralized linguistic control, Academie Francaise, organic evolution, political constraint

### Diagram 3: Diglossia Across Four Languages
- **File:** `diagram-3-diglossia-comparison.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG four-column comparison
- **Description:** Chinese, Arabic, French, and English diglossia histories shown in parallel. Each column shows the high variety, low variety, and resolution status. Chinese and French resolved theirs by elevating the vernacular; Arabic's persists; English resolved organically. Includes the "variety-independent meaning encoding" demonstration (same sentence in five Chinese "dialects").
- **Key concepts:** diglossia, Classical Chinese (wenyanwen), vernacular (baihuawen), MSA, character-based unity, dialect vs. language

### Diagram 4: The Simplified/Traditional Split
- **File:** `diagram-4-simplification-methods.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG methods showcase with timeline and geographic split
- **Description:** Five simplification methods (stroke reduction, cursive adoption, component simplification, character merger, phonetic substitution) each with Traditional-to-Simplified example. Reform timeline (1956: 230 immediate + 285 phased; 1964: consolidated 2,236; 1977: second round rejected; 1986: retracted). Geographic split and cross-script reform comparison (Turkish, Korean, German, English).
- **Key concepts:** simplification methods, 1956 reform structure (corrected), Traditional/Simplified split, script reform comparison

### Diagram 5: Four-Language Historical Comparison Timeline
- **File:** `diagram-5-four-language-timeline.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG parallel four-track timeline
- **Description:** Chinese, English, French, and Arabic plotted on parallel horizontal tracks spanning 1250 BCE to present. Major events marked on each track with cross-cutting theme analysis (standardization, diglossia resolution, script reform). Highlights that Chinese writing predates English by ~1,700 years. Includes the dialect/language question and the political nature of classification.
- **Key concepts:** comparative historical linguistics, parallel development, political classification, universal pressures, divergent solutions
