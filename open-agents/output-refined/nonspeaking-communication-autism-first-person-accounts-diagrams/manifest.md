## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: The Authorship Verification Spectrum
- **Type**: Flowchart (graduated spectrum)
- **Elements**: 4 verification levels, 6 individuals placed, caveat note
- **Key Insight**: Position on the spectrum reflects evidentiary status of the METHOD, not a judgment about the PERSON
- **Accessibility**: Left (most verifiable): Zimmerman and Blasko using standard AAC independently. Center-left: Kedar, independent typing, no formal blinded study. Center-right: Higashida (Fein & Kamio 2014 authorship doubts) and Tito (failed blind tests per Strange Son). Right (least verifiable): Sequenzia using FC, no controlled verification.

### Diagram 2: Common Themes Across First-Person Accounts
- **Type**: Mind map
- **Elements**: 7 main branches with author-specific sub-nodes
- **Key Insight**: Convergence of themes across independent accounts using different methods is itself a form of evidence
- **Accessibility**: Central node: First-Person Accounts. Branches: Presumption of incompetence (all authors), Mind-body disconnect (Kedar, Higashida, Tito), Sensory intensity (Tito, Higashida), Transformative communication access (all), Critique of systems (Kedar on ABA, Zimmerman on education, Sequenzia on labels), Unique contributions (per author).

### Diagram 3: Timeline of Major Non-Speaking Autistic Authors
- **Type**: Timeline (Mermaid timeline)
- **Elements**: 4 sections, 15+ events spanning 1991-2025
- **Key Insight**: Non-speaking autistic voices have accelerated dramatically in public discourse over three decades
- **Accessibility**: Pioneers (1990s-2000s): Soma develops RPM, Tito publishes. First Wave (2007-2013): Higashida in Japan, Kedar publishes, English translation. Breakthrough (2014-2018): Zimmerman receives AAC, Savarese graduates Oberlin, Kedar's novel. Current Era (2020-2025): Documentaries, Forbes, policy visibility.

### Diagram 4: The Before/After Communication Access Divide
- **Type**: Flowchart (split comparison)
- **Elements**: 2 case studies (Zimmerman, Kedar), before/after/pivot sections, insight box
- **Key Insight**: What changed was not intelligence but access to a communication method that could reveal it
- **Accessibility**: Zimmerman: 18 years segregated, no AAC, no literacy --> Proloquo2Go at 18 --> degrees, Forbes, Board Chair. Kedar: ABA drills for known skills --> RPM then iPad --> published author at 16, novelist, advocate. Note on different verification levels.

### Diagram 5: External Presentation vs. Reported Internal Experience
- **Type**: Flowchart (dual-layer comparison)
- **Elements**: 7 paired behavior/experience items, caveat section
- **Key Insight**: The external presentation is described by all authors as deeply misleading about internal reality
- **Accessibility**: External: rocking, no eye contact, no response, echolalia, apparent inattention, stimming, test failure. Internal: self-regulation, perception overload, full comprehension with motor failure, language present with speech absent, active processing, purposeful movement, understanding trapped behind barriers. Caveat: verification varies; consistency is evidence, not proof.

### Diagram 6: The Evidential Weight Framework
- **Type**: Flowchart (decision tree)
- **Elements**: 4 method categories, 5 evaluation questions, 4 confidence levels, 4 appropriate responses
- **Key Insight**: Presented as a thinking tool for honest evaluation, not as a gatekeeping mechanism
- **Accessibility**: Decision tree from communication method (standard AAC, independent typing, supported method, FC) through verification questions to confidence levels (high, moderate, uncertain, low) with appropriate response at each level. Standard AAC without facilitator = highest confidence. FC with physical contact = lowest confidence. Each level includes guidance on how to cite and present the account.
