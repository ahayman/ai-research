## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Failure-Mode Taxonomy
- **Type**: Top-down flowchart with categorical hierarchy
- **Elements**: 1 root, 5 major categories, 15+ sub-patterns, 2 compound-risk endpoints
- **Key Insight**: The five failure-mode categories are not mutually exclusive; most failed sessions involve multiple patterns operating together. Premature unburdening and spiritual bypass compound into retraumatization risk; Self-energy unfalsifiability and Self-cannot-be-reached compound into therapy demoralization.
- **Accessibility**: Color-coded by category type (root, category, sub-pattern, compound risk)

### Diagram 2: Contraindications Matrix
- **Type**: Horizontal flowchart with red/amber/orange/green coding
- **Elements**: 1 root (presentation type), 11 clinical presentations across four severity levels
- **Key Insight**: IFS suitability is a gradient. Green presentations work well with pure IFS; amber needs adaptation or adjuncts; orange needs specialized training; red is contraindicated as primary modality.
- **Accessibility**: Color is supplemented with explicit labels (GREEN, AMBER, ORANGE, RED) and explanatory text

### Diagram 3: Window of Tolerance + IFS Pacing
- **Type**: Vertical zoned flowchart (5 zones)
- **Elements**: Hyperarousal, upper edge, window, lower edge, hypoarousal — each with markers and IFS pacing instructions
- **Key Insight**: IFS work — particularly unburdening — is only safe within the window. At the edges, slow down. Outside, stop inward work and orient. Pure IFS does not explicitly teach this; it comes from sensorimotor, TIST, or SE.
- **Accessibility**: Each zone has both somatic markers and corresponding pacing decisions

### Diagram 4: Markers Checklist
- **Type**: Tree-structured checklist (1 header, 4 columns, 4-5 markers per column)
- **Elements**: Rushed unburdening (5 markers); Performed Self (4 markers); Spiritual bypass / merciful manager (5 markers); Scripted but empty unburdening (5 markers)
- **Key Insight**: Designed to be screenshot-portable. A client can bring this to a session and use it to discuss what is happening, rather than waiting until the failure mode has compounded.
- **Accessibility**: Color-coded by failure-mode category

### Diagram 5: What Good IFS Practice Looks Like
- **Type**: Mind map (radial)
- **Elements**: 1 root, 7 major branches, 20+ specific behaviors
- **Key Insight**: The shape of good practice is integration of pacing, contraindication awareness, willingness to refer out, and humility about the model's universals — not perfect protocol execution.
- **Accessibility**: Mind map is keyboard-navigable; structure is hierarchical for screen readers
