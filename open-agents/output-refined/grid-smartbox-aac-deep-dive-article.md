# Grid by Smartbox: The Complete AAC Platform That Goes Beyond Communication

For over 100,000 people worldwide, the simple act of speaking is not simple at all. Whether due to ALS, cerebral palsy, autism, stroke, or other conditions that affect speech and mobility, these individuals rely on technology to give them a voice. Among the tools serving this community, Grid by Smartbox stands out as one of the most comprehensive AAC platforms available -- not just enabling communication, but offering users genuine independence through environment control, computer access, and accessible entertainment. It is a platform that transforms a communication device into a gateway to the entire digital world.

But Grid is also a product of contradictions. Its Windows version is a powerhouse of accessibility features, while its iPad counterpart feels comparatively stripped-down. Its eye gaze technology is among the best in the industry, yet its App Store reviews reveal persistent bugs and a frustrating British English bias. This deep dive examines everything Grid offers, where it excels, where it falls short, and who it serves best.

## The Company Behind Grid: Smartbox's Journey

### From Family Startup to Global Force

Smartbox Assistive Technology began in 2006 as a small family venture. Founded by Paul, Alyson, Barney, and Dougal Hawes, the company set out with a focused mission: provide alternative communication solutions to people in the UK who cannot communicate through speech. What started as a family operation has since grown into one of the top four global AAC solution providers.

The company's path to its current position has been anything but straightforward. In 2018, Swedish AAC giant Tobii Dynavox acquired Smartbox, creating what would have been a dominant force in the assistive technology market. However, the UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) intervened, determining that the merger would reduce competition in the UK AAC market to unacceptable levels. Tobii Dynavox was forced to divest.

In October 2020, CareTech Holdings stepped in, acquiring a majority stake in Smartbox for up to £10.6 million -- comprising an initial purchase price of £7.05 million plus earn-outs of up to £3.55 million over two years. This gave Smartbox the stability and resources to continue growing, but the most transformative change was still to come.

### The Verdane Partnership and Ambitious Growth Targets

In 2024, Verdane, a European specialist growth buyout investment firm, became Smartbox's majority shareholder. CareTech retained a minority stake, and Three Hills Capital Partners invested alongside. This partnership was designed specifically to accelerate international access to AAC technology, and the ambition is striking: **Smartbox is targeting a three-fold increase in the number of people it serves by 2030**.

Today, Smartbox employs approximately 750 people, with UK headquarters in Bristol and Malvern and a global presence spanning the United States, Germany, France, the Netherlands, and Australia. Under CEO Dougal Hawes -- the last of the founding family members still leading the company -- Smartbox continues to push the boundaries of what AAC technology can achieve.

## How Grid Works: Communication That Adapts to Every User

At its core, Grid is a communication platform that speaks on behalf of users who cannot speak for themselves. It achieves this through two complementary approaches: symbol-based communication for those who think in pictures and pre-linguistic communicators, and text-based communication for literate users who type what they want to say.

### Symbol Communication: Super Core

The heart of Grid's symbol-based approach is **Super Core**, a research-based vocabulary system developed by Smartbox's speech and language therapy team. Super Core organizes over 3,500 words and phrases into colour-coded grids where each word consistently occupies the same position, enabling users to develop motor memory -- a critical factor for communication speed.

Super Core comes in multiple configurations:

- **Super Core 50**: The full vocabulary with a 50-cell grid layout, offering the broadest range of expression for experienced users
- **Super Core 30**: A simplified 30-cell layout that reduces visual complexity while maintaining comprehensive vocabulary
- **Super Core Learning**: Introductory grids designed for those just beginning their AAC journey, gradually building vocabulary and confidence

The colour coding system groups words by grammatical type -- verbs in one colour, nouns in another, adjectives in a third -- creating a visual grammar system that supports language development alongside communication.

### Text Communication Options

For literate users, Grid provides several text-based communication systems:

- **Alpha Core**: Designed for people whose physical abilities may change over time (particularly relevant for progressive conditions like ALS), providing quick access to predictive text and common phrases
- **Beeline**: An extensive text-based vocabulary system praised by users for its depth
- **Vocab for Life**: Targeted at young adults and college-age users with age-appropriate vocabulary
- **Fast Talker** and **Text Talker**: Streamlined keyboard-to-speech options with varying levels of prediction support

All text input benefits from **SwiftKey-powered word prediction**, significantly reducing the number of selections needed to construct sentences. A built-in **Word Finder** tool helps clinicians and caregivers locate any word within the system and demonstrates the navigation path to reach it -- invaluable for teaching communication partners how to model language.

## Eye Gaze: Where Grid Truly Leads

If there is one area where Grid and its parent ecosystem outshine virtually all competitors, it is eye gaze technology. For users who cannot touch a screen -- people with severe motor impairments from conditions like ALS, locked-in syndrome, or advanced cerebral palsy -- controlling a device with their eyes is not just a feature. It is their entire interface to the world.

Smartbox offers three distinct eye gaze camera systems, each designed to integrate seamlessly with their Grid Pad hardware:

### Evo: The In-House Innovation

Evo represents Smartbox's commitment to owning the full technology stack. Built entirely in-house, Evo uses powerful machine learning algorithms trained on real-world scenarios rather than laboratory conditions. The key technical innovation is its dedicated **Neural Processing Unit (NPU)**, which means all eye tracking computation happens directly on the Evo device rather than burdening the communication device's processor. This architectural choice delivers faster, more responsive performance and better battery life.

### Lumin-i: The Precision Workhorse

The Lumin-i camera operates at **60 Hz tracking frequency**, meaning it samples eye position 60 times per second for incredibly responsive cursor movement. It operates effectively at distances of **45 to 85 centimetres**, providing flexibility for different mounting positions and user postures. The camera is housed in an impact-resistant, IP-rated module -- critical for a device that may be mounted on a wheelchair and subjected to the rigours of daily life.

### Vida: The Third-Party Powerhouse

Powered by **IntelliGaze 11** software, Vida offers a responsive and dynamic eye gaze experience compatible with both Grid Pad 13 and Grid Pad 16. As of December 2025, Smartbox has also added compatibility with **eyetuitive** as a supported third-party eye tracker, complete with snap-to-item functionality.

### Interaction Modes

All three cameras support multiple interaction modes:

- **Dwell selection**: The user holds their gaze on a target for a configurable time to activate it -- the most common mode for AAC users
- **Blink to click**: The user moves a pointer with their gaze and blinks to confirm selection
- **Mouse pointer mode**: Full computer cursor control using eye movement alone, enabling access to any Windows application

## Environment Control: Independence Beyond Words

This is where Grid transcends typical AAC software and becomes something much more: a **complete independence platform**. Through Grid 3 on Windows, users can control their physical environment -- lights, televisions, doors, beds, heaters -- directly from the same device they use to communicate.

### How It Works

Grid Pad devices come with two built-in transmitters:

1. **GEWA infrared transmitter**: Sends IR signals to control TVs, set-top boxes, and any other IR-compatible device. Smartbox maintains a library of pre-recorded IR command files for common devices.
2. **Radio control transmitter**: For devices that use radio frequency signals.

Additional transmitters can be added:

- **EasyWave transmitter**: For controlling EasyWave-compatible devices
- **Z-Wave transmitter**: For integration with the Z-Wave smart home ecosystem, connecting to thousands of compatible smart home products

### Smart Home Integration

Beyond the built-in transmitters, Grid 3 integrates with two smart home control platforms:

- **Servus**: A dedicated environment control system designed for people with disabilities
- **Nous**: A broader smart home control platform that brings modern IoT device control to Grid users

The practical impact is profound. A person with ALS who can only move their eyes can use Grid to have a conversation with their family, then switch to controlling the television, adjust the room lighting, and send a text message -- all without anyone else touching a single button.

**Critical caveat**: Environment control is available **only in Grid 3 for Windows**. Grid for iPad does not support environment control of any kind.

## Accessible Apps: Entertainment and Social Connection

Grid 3 includes purpose-built accessible versions of popular applications, designed to be fully operable through any access method -- including eye gaze and switch scanning. This is not simply running a standard app; these are custom interfaces rebuilt for accessibility.

### Entertainment
- **YouTube** and **YouTube Kids**: Browse, search, and watch videos with accessible controls
- **Netflix**: Browse the home page, view by category, search for content, with titles and descriptions read aloud
- **Spotify**: Stream over 50 million songs using a free or premium account, accessible via any input method
- **Disney+**: Accessible streaming interface

### Social Media and Communication
- **Facebook**: Post, read, and interact on social media
- **Instagram**: Browse and interact accessibly
- **WhatsApp**: Send and receive instant messages
- **Email**: Accessible email client
- **SMS**: Text messaging directly from the device

### Creative Expression
- **Mind's Eye**: Described as the world's first AI art expression tool for people with disabilities, developed in partnership with the MND Association. Users type what's on their mind, select from over 100 style preferences, and the AI generates images they can save and share.

### Full Computer Access

Beyond dedicated apps, Grid 3 provides complete Windows computer control through an accessible toolbar that sits beside the screen. Users get on-screen keyboards with SwiftKey prediction, mouse pointer tools, and shortcuts -- enabling them to use Microsoft Word, Excel, web browsers, and virtually any Windows application through alternative access methods.

**Again, this is Grid 3 only.** Grid for iPad cannot control other iPad applications.

## Grid Pad Hardware: Purpose-Built for AAC

While Grid software can run on standard Windows computers and iPads, Smartbox's purpose-built Grid Pad devices represent the optimal experience. These are not consumer tablets repurposed for AAC -- they are engineered from the ground up for the demands of assisted communication.

### Grid Pad 13

- **Display**: 13-inch high-definition, ultra-durable, daylight-readable with anti-glare coating
- **Second Screen**: 6.5-inch full-colour daylight-bright display for quick-access information
- **Programmable Smart Buttons**: Physical buttons for frequently used commands
- **Durability**: Fully enclosed aluminium chassis, drop-tested to 1 metre, IP54 rated (splash and dust resistant)
- **Built-in Transmitters**: GEWA infrared and radio control, with optional EasyWave/Z-Wave
- **Charging**: 60W USB-C
- **Eye Gaze**: Compatible with all three camera systems via purpose-built mounting modules

### Grid Pad 16

- **Display**: 16-inch WQXGA HDR with 100% sRGB colour gamut
- **Enhanced Visuals**: Superior contrast and colour accuracy for users who need larger, clearer targets
- **Second Screen**: 6.5-inch companion display
- **Same Durability and Integration**: All the same environmental control and eye gaze capabilities as the 13

The second screen on both models is a particularly thoughtful feature -- it allows quick access to information or controls without navigating away from the main communication interface.

## Grid 3 vs Grid for iPad: A Tale of Two Platforms

This is perhaps the most important comparison for prospective users, and the gap between the two platforms is significant.

**Grid 3** is the full-featured platform. It supports multiple user profiles (essential for shared devices in schools or clinics), direct eye gaze camera integration, comprehensive environment control, full Windows computer access, and a suite of accessible entertainment and social media apps.

**Grid for iPad** is essentially a communication-focused subset. It provides symbol and text-based communication with a solid vocabulary selection, touch access, and Dropbox cloud sync with Grid 3. But it lacks environment control, computer access, accessible apps, multiple user profiles, and direct eye gaze support (relying instead on iOS's built-in accessibility features). It supports only a single user profile.

The iPad version does have merits: it is more portable, more affordable, and sufficient for users whose primary need is straightforward communication. The availability of a monthly subscription and a 30-day free trial lowers the barrier to entry. And cloud sync means work done on the iPad carries over to Grid 3.

## Pricing

Grid's pricing structure varies by platform and purchase model:

- **Grid for iPad (Subscription)**: Approximately £9.99/month ($9.99/month USD), with a 30-day free trial
- **Grid for iPad (Lifetime)**: Approximately £250-300 one-time purchase
- **Grid 3 (Windows)**: Included with Grid Pad devices; also available as a standalone software license
- **Grid Pad Hardware**: Typically funded through healthcare programmes, disability insurance, or government assistive technology schemes rather than purchased out-of-pocket

Smartbox periodically runs promotions -- during AAC Awareness Month in October 2025, Grid for iPad was available at 50% off.

## Strengths: Where Grid Excels

1. **Unmatched eye gaze ecosystem**: Three camera options with purpose-built hardware integration
2. **True environment control**: IR, radio, Z-Wave, and smart home integration
3. **Complete computer access**: Full Windows control through Grid 3
4. **Accessible entertainment and social media**: Purpose-built Netflix, YouTube, Spotify, and social platform interfaces
5. **Research-backed vocabularies**: Super Core with 3,500+ words and phrases
6. **Rugged, purpose-built hardware**: Grid Pad devices drop-tested, splash-proof, daylight-readable
7. **Mind's Eye AI art**: Creative expression tool for people with disabilities
8. **Massive language support**: 200 voices across 32 languages

## Weaknesses: Where Grid Falls Short

1. **British English bias**: UK terminology even in US English version
2. **Audio and stability bugs**: Sound stopping after updates, crashes when speaking
3. **Dramatic platform gap**: Grid for iPad lacks most advanced features
4. **No mobile phone or watch support**: No iPhone/Apple Watch backup option
5. **Complex initial setup**: Steep learning curve requiring clinical support
6. **Reported crashes**: Freezing when typing, crashes when pressing speak buttons
7. **Customer support responsiveness**: Some users report difficulty reaching support

## User Reviews: The Community Verdict

Grid for iPad carries an approximate **3.7 out of 5 star** rating on the Apple App Store, reflecting a mixed but generally positive reception.

**What users love:**
> "Grid is my favorite AAC app. It has been great with motor planning and it fits my needs."

Users consistently praise the depth of vocabulary options, the effectiveness of motor planning through consistent symbol placement, and the extensiveness of the Beeline text-based vocabulary.

**What frustrates users:**
> "I downloaded the US English version but it still uses many UK words like 'crisps' for chips."

The most common complaints centre on the British English vocabulary issue, audio bugs following updates, app crashes, and the inability to use Grid on iPhone or Apple Watch as a backup communication method.

## Recent Updates (2025)

- **Super Core vocabulary refresh** (October 2025): Extended maths and English grids, new "silly words" grid, reorganised sex and sexual health grids, easier personalisation, Find Word bug fix.
- **eyetuitive compatibility** (December 2025): Full support for eyetuitive eye tracker with snap-to-item feature.
- **Whiteboard feature**: New drawing and writing tool for touch access, designed for aphasia users.

## Who Is Grid Best For?

**Grid 3 (Windows) is ideal for:**
- Users who need eye gaze access (ALS/MND, locked-in syndrome, severe CP)
- Users who want environment control and smart home integration
- Users who need full computer access for work, education, or entertainment
- Clinical and school settings where multiple profiles are needed

**Grid for iPad is best for:**
- Users who primarily need touch-based symbol or text communication
- Users seeking a more affordable entry point to Grid's vocabulary system
- Users who want portability and simplicity over advanced features
- Users who also use Grid 3 and want cloud-synced content on a secondary device

## Key Takeaways

- Grid is one of the most comprehensive AAC platforms available, particularly when paired with Grid Pad hardware
- The eye gaze ecosystem (Evo, Lumin-i, Vida cameras) is industry-leading
- Environment control and computer access transform Grid into a complete independence platform
- The gap between Grid 3 and Grid for iPad is substantial
- British English defaults and app stability issues are legitimate concerns
- With Verdane's investment and a target of tripling users by 2030, Grid is positioning for significant global growth

## Conclusion

Grid by Smartbox occupies a unique position in the AAC landscape. No other platform offers the same combination of communication, environment control, computer access, and accessible entertainment in a single ecosystem. For users with severe motor impairments who need eye gaze access and full computer control, Grid 3 with a Grid Pad device is arguably the most capable AAC solution available.

But that capability comes with caveats. The iPad version is a shadow of the Windows experience. Bugs and British English defaults frustrate users. The setup complexity demands clinical support. And the cost of the full ecosystem is substantial.

As Smartbox pursues its ambitious growth targets under Verdane's partnership, the question is whether the company can maintain the depth that makes Grid exceptional while broadening its accessibility. The platform's 100,000-plus current users testify to its value. Whether Smartbox can reach three times that number by 2030 will depend on addressing the rough edges that currently hold it back.
