Lumley's EAET — Evidence Summary

Emotional Awareness and Expression Therapy. Real, manualized therapy by Mark Lumley and Howard Schubiner (Wayne State). Multiple chronic-pain RCTs.

What is EAET?

Scaffolded explicit-emotion-naming therapy — not pure body-focused interoceptive training. Combines three core components:

Psychoeducation
Linking past stress/trauma → present somatic symptoms.
Emotional awareness training
Identifying stuck or avoided emotions related to current and past stressors.
Expressive techniques
Verbal expression, written expression, role-play, addressing avoidance.
2010s onward Multiple chronic-pain RCTs
Fibromyalgia, IBS, medically unexplained symptoms (MUS)
Across populations, EAET shows clinically meaningful pain-severity reductions, often in head-to-head comparison to active controls or wait-list. PMC4724386 (2015 musculoskeletal pain RCT).
2021 EAET for somatic symptom disorder — mediation finding
Frontiers Psychology 2021 / PMC7928393
"Impoverished Emotional Experience" facet of EPS-25 (overlapping with affective alexithymia) moderates EAET outcomes. Increased capacity to be aware of emotion (without intrusive flooding) was the active mediator of somatic symptom reduction.
2024 EAET vs CBT for chronic pain in older veterans — RCT
PubMed 38869899 — Yarns BC, Lumley MA et al.
Head-to-head against CBT in older male veterans with chronic pain. EAET showed pain-severity reductions; the severity-moderation finding (see paradox diagram) emerged from this and earlier work.

Key implication for subtype-matching: EAET is the cleanest example of scaffolded explicit-emotion-naming as a working therapy in chronic-pain + alexithymia populations. It is NOT pure body-focused interoceptive training, so it does not cleanly test the parsing-deficit caution. The Lumley severity-moderation finding (Diagram 7) is a real complication for the parsing-deficit-matched-therapy theory.
