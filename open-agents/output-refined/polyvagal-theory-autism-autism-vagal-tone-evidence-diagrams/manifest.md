## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Cheng 2020 Effect Sizes
- **Type**: Custom HTML bar chart (no Mermaid dependency)
- **Key elements**: 4 effect-size bars, scale axis, Hedges' g labels, p-values, highlighted largest effect (RSA reactivity to social stress, g = −0.72)
- **Key insight**: Reactivity effects are larger than baseline effects; the social-stress RSA reactivity is the single largest finding and most clinically meaningful.
- **Accessibility**: All bars labeled with both numeric values and verbal descriptors; legend explains color coding; takeaway block summarizes the chart in prose.

### Diagram 2: Cheng vs Kushki Tension
- **Type**: Two-sided comparison panel
- **Key elements**: Two side-cards (red border for Cheng, blue border for Kushki) with verdict statements, methodology rows, strengths, limits. Center reconciliation block with four explanatory points.
- **Key insight**: The disagreement is real but partial. Both papers are right within their scope. Heterogeneity + study composition + power + comorbidity confounds reconcile them.
- **Accessibility**: Tabular data structure works for screen readers; color is supplemented by text labels and structural cues.

### Diagram 3: Heterogeneity Subgroups
- **Type**: Distribution visualization with subgroup callouts (SVG)
- **Key elements**: Three overlapping distribution curves (hypo-arousal autistic, controls, hyper-arousal autistic) with subgroup descriptor cards beneath; pooled autism mean marked.
- **Key insight**: "Autistic autonomic profile" is a population statement; individual autistic people fall into distinct subgroups with opposite physiological patterns.
- **Accessibility**: Subgroup descriptions repeated as text below the SVG; verbal axis labels; takeaway prose-summary.

### Diagram 4: RSA Reactivity Time-Series
- **Type**: SVG line chart showing autonomic response across three phases
- **Key elements**: Y-axis (RSA amplitude), X-axis (time across baseline/stressor/recovery), neurotypical line (grey, large drop), autistic line (red, smaller drop), reactivity brackets, phase labels.
- **Key insight**: The g = −0.72 effect describes a blunted vagal-withdrawal response to social stress. Two interpretations remain plausible.
- **Accessibility**: Phase descriptors below chart; verbal interpretations in cards beneath.

### Diagram 5: Defensive-State Hypothesis
- **Type**: Vertical causal pathway with evidence/critique blocks
- **Key elements**: PVT claim node → symptom-row (4 cells: auditory, food/gut, tactile, visual) → intervention node → SSP commercial node; evidence-for and evidence-against cards; neurodiversity-paradigm critique block.
- **Key insight**: The defensive-state hypothesis is the original clinical motivation for SSP. It's testable, has preliminary support, and has methodological + interpretive problems.
- **Accessibility**: Pathway flows top-to-bottom; arrows are decorative; all content is in text blocks.

### Diagram 6: Adult vs Child Comparison
- **Type**: Side-by-side comparison panel
- **Key elements**: Pediatric column (blue header, 5 study rows) vs Adult column (red header, 4 study rows); pattern-summary block; takeaway.
- **Key insight**: Adult and pediatric HRV findings differ in shape, not just magnitude. Reactivity differences are the most stable adult finding.
- **Accessibility**: Tabular data structure; pattern block summarizes in prose.

### Diagram 7: Confounds Map
- **Type**: Hub-and-spoke layout with 12 confound cards
- **Key elements**: Center hub node, 12 confound cards each with title, magnitude badge (LARGE/MEDIUM/VARIES), and 1-2 sentence explanation.
- **Key insight**: Many things lower HRV other than autism. Individual HRV readings need to be interpreted in light of these confounds.
- **Accessibility**: Magnitude badges have both color and text; cards are independently readable; legend row defines magnitude categories.

### Diagram 8: Interpretation Gap (PVT vs NVI vs PP vs Allostatic Load)
- **Type**: Hub-and-four-quadrants comparison
- **Key elements**: Center node (the data), 4 framework cards (PVT, NVI, predictive processing, allostatic load), gap-banner emphasizing the central point.
- **Key insight**: Four frameworks predict the same correlations. The data doesn't discriminate. Citing Cheng as PVT evidence is a category error.
- **Accessibility**: Each framework card is independently readable; central insight is stated in two places (banner + takeaway).

### Diagram 9: Self-Assessment Decision Tree
- **Type**: Vertical decision flowchart (5 questions)
- **Key elements**: Start node → 5 question nodes, each with yes/no branches → endpoint interpretation block.
- **Key insight**: User-actionable framework. Walks through medication, comorbidity, methodology, fitness, and state-factor confounds before treating a low reading as meaningful.
- **Accessibility**: Tree structure flows top-to-bottom; yes/no branches are color-coded but also text-labeled; endpoint block provides full interpretation in prose.
