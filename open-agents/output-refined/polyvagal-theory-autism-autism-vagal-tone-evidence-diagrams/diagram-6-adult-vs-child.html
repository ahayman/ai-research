Adult vs Child Autistic HRV — Why the Developmental Scope Matters

Most cited HRV/autism research is pediatric. The adult literature is sparser and shows somewhat different patterns. For an adult navigating this evidence, the distinction is load-bearing.

Pediatric findings

Bulk of the autism-HRV literature. Cheng 2020 meta-analysis heavily weighted toward children.

Cheng 2020 (meta-analysis) · 34 studies, mostly pediatric
Baseline parasympathetic HRV g = −0.52; RSA reactivity to social stress g = −0.72. Strong group-level effects.
Bal & Porges 2010 · children, J Autism Dev Disord
Lower amplitude RSA + faster HR. Within-autism, higher RSA → faster emotion recognition.
Kushki 2022 · pediatric data + theory
Group-level baseline RSA within typical range; small subgroup outside CI. Pushback on "autistic ANS dysfunction" frame.
Panju 2015 · children, PMC4676885
High-anxiety ASD subgroup shows reduced electrodermal activity vs low-anxiety ASD + controls. Subgroups stratify the autonomic signal.
Pace 2025 child cohort · n = 22 ASD, 22 controls
Parasympathetic under-activation at rest with phasic sympathetic activation. The "expected" autism profile.

Adult findings

Far fewer studies. Baseline differences less consistent; reactivity differences more consistent.

Thapa et al. 2019 · n = 55 ASD adults, Autism Research
Significant reduction in resting log-HF and RMSSD. Adult parasympathetic activity reduced vs controls.
Dijkhuis et al. 2019 · n = 47 ASD / 27 TD adults analyzed, J Autism Dev Disord
No baseline HR/HRV difference; significantly reduced HRV reactivity during public-speaking task (d = 0.6, p = 0.023). Self-reported emotional awareness no different.
Pace 2025 adult cohort · n = 21 ASD adults, 21 controls
Reversed pattern: higher tonic sympathetic, lower phasic sympathetic activity. Authors propose adaptive compensation across development, possibly involving atypical locus coeruleus.
Bellato et al. 2021 · response-conflict task, ADHD/autism, J Atten Disord
HRV indices and task performance differently associated with ADHD vs autism — supports distinct adult autonomic-cognitive profiles.

What the adult pattern suggests

  • Baseline differences become less consistent in adulthood. Some studies (Thapa) find them; others (Dijkhuis) don't.
  • Reactivity differences remain consistent. Even when adult baseline numbers look ordinary, response to social demand shows a real difference.
  • The autonomic profile changes shape, not just magnitude. Pace 2025's "reversed pattern" hint suggests compensation or developmental restructuring of autonomic regulation.
  • Adult literature is heavily male-weighted and English-speaking-context-weighted. Generalization to autistic women, gender-diverse adults, and non-Western contexts is uncertain.
For the adult reader: The pediatric-to-adult transition is not a simple extension. The autonomic profile of an autistic 8-year-old in 2010s research doesn't deterministically predict the autonomic profile of an autistic 40-year-old in 2026. Reactivity to social demand is the most stable adult finding — even when baseline HRV is "normal," social demand can be physiologically more taxing than for a neurotypical peer. This matches the lived-experience reporting from autistic adults around social-energy depletion and overwhelm.
