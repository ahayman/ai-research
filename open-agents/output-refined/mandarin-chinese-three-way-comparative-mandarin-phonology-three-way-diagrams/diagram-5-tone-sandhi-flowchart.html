Mandarin Tone Sandhi Rules

Decision flowcharts for the three major sandhi processes

Rule 1 Third-Tone Sandhi (Most Important)
Tone 3 + Tone 3?
-->
First T3 becomes T2 (rising)
Obligatory. Always applies.
Tone 3 + Tone 1, 2, or 4?
-->
T3 becomes "half-third"
Falls low but does not rise. Only dipping portion.
Tone 3 alone / phrase-final?
-->
Full Tone 3
Complete dip-and-rise contour (214).
你好ni hao (T3+T3)--> ni(T2) hao(T3)
可以ke yi (T3+T3)--> ke(T2) yi(T3)
老虎lao hu (T3+T3)--> lao(T2) hu(T3)
Three+ Consecutive Tone 3s: Structure Matters

When 3+ syllables are all Tone 3, syntactic grouping determines which change:

Pattern A: First two grouped [AB]+C
[ T2 T2 ] T3 e.g., [展览]馆 [zhan(T2) lan(T2)] guan(T3)
Pattern B: Last two grouped A+[BC]
T3 [ T2 T3 ] e.g., 我[可以] wo(T3) [ke(T2) yi(T3)]
Rule 2 Yi (一) "One" Sandhi
一 before Tone 4?
-->
一 becomes Tone 2 (rising)
yi(T1) --> yi(T2)
一 before Tone 1, 2, or 3?
-->
一 becomes Tone 4 (falling)
yi(T1) --> yi(T4)
一 in counting / isolation?
-->
一 stays Tone 1 (high level)
Citation form: yi(T1)
一样yi yang (before T4)--> yi(T2) yang
一天yi tian (before T1)--> yi(T4) tian
一起yi qi (before T3)--> yi(T4) qi
第一di yi (counting)--> di yi(T1)
Rule 3 Bu (不) Negation Sandhi
不 before Tone 4?
-->
不 becomes Tone 2 (rising)
bu(T4) --> bu(T2)
不 before Tone 1, 2, or 3?
-->
不 stays Tone 4 (falling)
No change from citation form.
不是bu shi (before T4)--> bu(T2) shi
不对bu dui (before T4)--> bu(T2) dui
不吃bu chi (before T1)--> bu(T4) chi
不好bu hao (before T3)--> bu(T4) hao
Pattern: Both 一 and 不 change to Tone 2 before Tone 4. This is a dissimilation effect — two falling tones in a row are avoided. The 一 rule is more complex because it also changes to Tone 4 before non-falling tones.
