Beijing Dialect

Erhua is pervasive. Applied to many words where standard Putonghua would not require it. A defining marker of Beijing identity.

哪儿 nar "where" | 一块儿 yikuar "together" | 好玩儿 haowar "fun"

Standard Putonghua (PRC)

Official standard includes a defined subset of erhua words. More restrained than Beijing dialect. Used in broadcasting, education, formal speech.

一点儿 yidianr "a little" | 这儿 zher "here" | 花儿 huar "flower"

Southern Mandarin

Minimal erhua usage. Speakers in Sichuan, Guangdong, etc. may recognize it but rarely produce it naturally. Often sounds artificial when attempted.

Prefer 一点 yidian and 这里 zheli

Taiwan Mandarin (Guoyu)

Erhua is virtually absent. Strongly associated with "mainland" speech. Taiwanese speakers systematically use non-erhua alternatives.

这里 zheli not zher | 那里 nali not nar

What Erhua Does to Syllable Finals

-n final
nasal absorbed
-->
vowel + r
e.g., men --> mer (the /n/ disappears)
-ng final
nasal dropped
-->
nasalized V + r
e.g., deng --> der (nasalization remains on vowel)
-i final
vowel modified
-->
schwa + r
e.g., yi dianr (the /i/ centralizes)
open vowel
simple addition
-->
vowel + r
e.g., hua --> huar (r-coloring added directly)
