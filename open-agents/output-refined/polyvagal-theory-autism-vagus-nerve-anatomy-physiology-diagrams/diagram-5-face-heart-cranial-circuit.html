The Face-Heart Cranial Nerve Circuit

Several cranial nerves (V, VII, IX, X, XI) cluster around the medulla and pons and together innervate face, middle ear, voice, swallowing, and heart. This anatomical clustering is uncontested. The polyvagal "social engagement system" is one interpretive layer placed on top of it.

CN Name Origin (brainstem) Innervates (functional summary)
V Trigeminal Pons (motor + sensory nuclei) Face sensation; muscles of mastication; tensor tympani in the middle ear
VII Facial Pons (facial nucleus) Muscles of facial expression; stapedius in the middle ear; taste anterior tongue; parasympathetic to lacrimal/salivary glands
IX Glossopharyngeal Medulla (nucleus ambiguus + NTS + inferior salivatory) Stylopharyngeus (swallow); taste posterior tongue; carotid sinus/body afferents; parasympathetic to parotid
X Vagus Medulla (DMN + NA + NTS) Larynx, pharynx (NA); heart (NA, vagal brake); lungs, esophagus, gut (mixed)
XI Accessory (spinal) Spinal cord C1–C5 (anterior horn) Sternocleidomastoid, trapezius (head orientation toward sound and gaze)
The interpretive line. The anatomical clustering of these nerves is uncontested neuroanatomy. Whether the cluster operates as a unified, coordinated "social engagement system" — with the myelinated cardiac vagus as its central regulator — is the interpretive claim distinctive to polyvagal theory. That construct is covered in Category 1 (Foundations) and contested in Category 3 (the Critique). For this anatomy chapter, we note the clustering as observation, not as endorsement of any specific functional claim.
Figure 5. The cranial nerves of the "face-heart" cluster share brainstem real estate and overlapping nuclei. CN V and VII innervate middle ear muscles (tensor tympani, stapedius), part of the acoustic reflex that modulates auditory input. CN IX and X share the nucleus ambiguus for some motor output. CN XI's spinal component orients head and shoulders. This is the anatomical substrate for any later interpretation about coordinated social/expressive behavior.
