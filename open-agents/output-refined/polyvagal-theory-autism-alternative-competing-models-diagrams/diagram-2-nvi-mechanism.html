Neurovisceral Integration — How vmPFC Controls Vagal Tone

Thayer & Lane's NVI model: the ventromedial prefrontal cortex tonically inhibits the amygdala, which gates autonomic output via brainstem projections to the vagus. Higher tonic vmPFC inhibition produces higher HRV. Same HRV predictions as polyvagal theory — without phylogenetic claims about vagal nuclei.

How NVI explains autism HRV findings. Cheng 2020's meta-analysis (g = -0.52 baseline, g = -0.72 RSA reactivity to social stress) is exactly what NVI predicts for any population with reduced vmPFC top-down regulation. Autistic individuals show atypical vmPFC structure, atypical amygdala reactivity, and atypical resting connectivity in regulatory networks. No phylogenetic claims needed.
Compared to polyvagal theory. NVI makes the same HRV-emotion regulation predictions PVT makes, but skips the contested anatomical claims (mammalian-only myelinated vagus, three-tier ladder, dorsal vagal freezing). NVI has > 10× the citation impact of PVT in autonomic-neuroscience literature. If the question is "what is the established framework for explaining the HRV-autism link?", the answer is NVI, not PVT.
