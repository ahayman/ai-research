Duloxetine is 9× more selective for serotonin than for norepinephrine

The name says "serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor," which sounds balanced. The binding data says otherwise, and it says so explicitly rather than by inference.

Panel A — What the paper reports: Ki, in nanomolar
The trap: Ki is an inverse measure. A lower number means a tighter grip. So the short bar above is the strong binding, and the long bar is the weak one. This reads backwards, and it has been reported backwards: a search summary this project encountered early inverted it, which would have flipped the conclusion of the entire deep dive.
Panel B — What it means: relative grip strength (1 / Ki)

In a living animal the serotonin preference is larger, not smaller, than the binding assay suggests. Duloxetine is genuinely the more balanced of the two common SNRIs, because venlafaxine's ratio is 30. But "more balanced than venlafaxine" and "balanced" are different claims. [primary, verified]
