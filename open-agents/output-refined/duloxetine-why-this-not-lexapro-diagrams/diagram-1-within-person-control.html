The Within-Person Control

Two drugs. One person. One year apart. Serotonin transporter engagement that nobody can tell apart, and outcomes that are not remotely alike. This is the closest thing to a controlled experiment in the project, and he ran it on himself without knowing.

Why the striatal row is the best comparison this project has ever had

✓ same tracer — [11C]DASB ✓ same modality — PET ✓ same region — striatum ✓ same n — 10 vs 10 ✓ same dosing — multiple-dose ✗ different lab — Vienna vs London ✗ different population — depressed vs healthy

Five matched axes, two unmatched, and it requires no inference at all: the two numbers are printed in the same column of the same table. No prior pass read that table. 73 ± 4 against 75 ± 8 does not become distinguishable under any handling of the two mismatches, and the direction of the trivial 2-point gap is meaningless at those standard deviations. reasoning, not evidence

⚠ Two numbers this diagram refuses to use, and both were in the brief that commissioned it.
  • “Matched within 2.5 occupancy points.” That compares 69.2% (modelled from a SPECT scan of the midbrain in Vienna) against 71.7% (modelled from a PET scan of the thalamus in Chiba). Cross-modality, cross-region, cross-laboratory. You cannot establish a 2.5-point sameness inside a 23-point noise band any more than you can extract a 10-point difference from it, and the second is exactly why the old “less serotonin” story is dead. The blade cuts both ways and only one edge had been noticed.
  • “Within ~1 point on tracer-matched thalamic data (71.3% vs ~70%).” This revives a dead-list item. It traces to Arakawa 2016 (PMID 27082864), whose abstract names no brain region and gives no escitalopram-10mg value at all. Its only figure is “Escitalopram and sertraline showed high occupancies of 69.1–77.9% at 4h”, spanning three drug groups at four subjects each. Taking the bottom of that range and calling it escitalopram 10mg is the precise inference this project retired in both directions. primary, verified — abstract retrieved this pass
The finding never needed the precision, and it is stronger without it. What the evidence supports is not “the two exposures were equal to within a point.” It is “nobody can show they differed, in any region where anyone has looked, and his outcomes differed categorically anyway.” An argument built on a precise match invites an attack on the precision. An argument built on an overlap nobody can resolve cannot be attacked that way, because it claims less and it needs less.

What this establishes, and the one tension worth naming

It establishes his premise as fact rather than conjecture. He reasoned from first principles that the two drugs “simply work differently, and that alone is enough to explain the variation.” The evidence for it was sitting in the corpus unassembled, and it took his corrected history to make it visible.

Why within-person is the right design here, and it is not a consolation prize. The 8.5-fold between-person spread in concentration-response is exactly what stops population comparisons from speaking about him. His own body is the only control that does not suffer from it.

The caveats that travel: the escitalopram side is retrospective and about a year old, he was not blind and neither was anyone else, and nobody has ever scanned him, so these are population figures applied to a man whom this page's own best-evidenced candidate says population figures do not describe. That is a real tension and it is worth naming rather than hiding. It does not dissolve the argument, because the argument needs only that no defensible difference exists, and individual variation makes a defensible difference less available rather than more. reasoning, not evidence
