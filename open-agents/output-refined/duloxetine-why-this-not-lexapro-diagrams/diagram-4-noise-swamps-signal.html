Two things this diagram kills, and one it leaves standing

Dead: "he needed less serotonin." There is no measurable gap to explain.

Also dead: "actually they are at parity, so the hypothesis is refuted." That claim needed a precise escitalopram-10mg value which does not exist in its primary source. Retired in both directions.

Standing: the ranges overlap, and that is enough. If both drugs plausibly put him in the same occupancy territory and only one dissociated him, then SERT occupancy is not the explanatory variable. This conclusion needs no precision at all, which is exactly why it survives.
