The Graveyard: Three Explanations and How Each Died

Every previous answer to "why did this work when Lexapro didn't" is buried here. What matters is how each one died, because only one kind of death is permanent.

The distinction that makes this list short enough to be useful

Killed by heterogeneity → NOT DEADA null population average is perfectly compatible with a large effect in one person. These stay on the candidate list. This is the reader's own argument, and it is correct.
Killed by logic or measurement → GENUINELY DEADNo amount of individual variation revives a mechanism built on an artifact, contradicted by the drug's own chemistry, or predicting the opposite of what he reports.

All three below died the second way. None is waiting quietly for the right subgroup.

DEAD
Explanation 1

"Duloxetine adds norepinephrine. That's the difference."

The one the old page title assumed. Retitled because of this.

Killed by: the drug's own binding constants.
  • SERT Ki 0.8 nM vs NET Ki 7.5 nM, ratio 9 (Bymaster 2001, PMID 11750180) primary, verified
  • Duloxetine is 9× more selective for serotonin, not for norepinephrine.
  • At 20mg the imaging agrees: ~75% SERT, ~30% NET.
Not a heterogeneity death. The molecule binds what it binds.
DEAD
Explanation 2

"So maybe he needed LESS serotonin, not more norepinephrine."

Duloxetine 71% vs escitalopram 80%. The numbers seemed to cooperate.

Killed by: a cross-study artifact, on every axis at once.
  • PET vs SPECT. DASB vs ADAM. Thalamus vs midbrain. Single-dose vs 10-day. n≈3 vs n=6. Chiba vs Vienna.
  • Midbrain reads highest, thalamus lowest, so the artifact ran in the hypothesis's own favour.
  • Claimed effect: 10 points. Escitalopram 10mg's own between-study range: 59–88% primary, verified
The noise is 3× the signal. Nothing left to explain.
DEAD
Explanation 3

"20mg is sub-therapeutic on both transporters anyway."

Told a man reporting benefit that his dose was doing nothing.

Killed by: two compounding errors plus a threshold that was never a threshold.
  • Single-dose number applied to a steady-state patient. 71.3% is single-dose thalamic; steady-state striatal is 75.2%.
  • Cross-region comparison. Meyer's line is striatal; the 78% figure averages in the midbrain.
  • Meyer's 80% is anchored to approved minimum doses, not to who responds. Never validated for anxiety or pain. One of its "five SSRIs" is venlafaxine.
  • It failed when tested in anxiety: n=27 at 78% occupancy, no correlation with improvement primary, verified
Wrong number, wrong region, wrong line.

The correction is buried here too, and this is the instructive one

A later pass announced it had found a properly matched comparison showing the two drugs at parity, and used it to declare the hypothesis "not unsupported but refuted." That parity claim is also dead. The study behind it (Arakawa 2016, PMID 27082864) names no brain region in its primary abstract and gives no escitalopram-10mg-specific value, only a 69.1–77.9% range spanning three drug groups at four subjects each. Assigning the bottom of that range to escitalopram 10mg was an inference, and it was the inference that produced the tidy parity. primary, verified

The pass that made this claim flagged both problems in its own open-items list, then ran the claim as a headline anyway. That is this project's signature failure in miniature: caveats filed in an appendix while the headline spends unearned certainty.

The comparison is retired in BOTH directions. Not because it reverses, and not because it shows parity, but because no defensible number exists on either side.

What walks out of the graveyard

Escitalopram 10mg's measured range (59–88%) entirely contains duloxetine 20mg's (71–78%). So the two are indistinguishable given the noise. That does not resurrect any explanation. It replaces the question with a better one:

If both drugs put him at roughly comparable serotonin transporter occupancy and only one dissociated him, then SERT occupancy is not the explanatory variable. Something else is.

That reframe stands on the overlap of the ranges, not on any false-precision parity claim. It is the only thing on this page that three dead explanations agree on.
