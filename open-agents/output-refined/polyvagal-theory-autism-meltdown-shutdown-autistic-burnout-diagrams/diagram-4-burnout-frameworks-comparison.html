Autistic Burnout — Two Frameworks, Different Evidence Bases

Burnout is the strongest empirical construct of the three states in this chapter — but the empirical literature was built using an allostatic load framework, not a polyvagal framework. The popular polyvagal reframing as "chronic dorsal vagal state" is post-hoc, lacks direct measurement, and inherits the same anatomy critique as shutdown. Treatment implications differ accordingly.

Allostatic Load Framework EMPIRICAL — the framework Raymaker's literature uses

Core mechanism

Cumulative chronic stress + barriers to recovery + loss of access to coping mechanisms = systemic dysregulation. Wear-and-tear from sustained physiological/neuroendocrine load over weeks to years (McEwen 1998).

Operational definition

Chronic exhaustion + loss of skills + reduced tolerance to stimulus. Duration typically 3+ months. Caused by "life stressors that add to the cumulative load," "expectations and demands without adequate supports," and "barriers to support that create an inability to obtain relief."

Raymaker et al. 2020, Autism in Adulthood 2(2):132–143 (n=19 interviews + 19 public sources)
Empirical literature

Phung et al. 2021 (n=8 youth); Higgins et al. 2021 (Grounded Delphi); Arnold et al. 2023 (AASPIRE measure dev); Mantzalas et al. 2022/2024 (Conceptual Model integrating Job Demands–Resources, Conservation of Resources); Bougoure et al. 2026 (AASPIRE n=379, ω=0.98, AUC=0.92); Ali, Mandy & Happé 2025 (systematic review: 48 studies, ~4,000 people).

What the data fits

Cross-comorbidity with PTSS r=0.70, depression r=0.61, anxiety r=0.67 (Higgins/Arnold 2025). Masking as chronic stressor (mitochondrial-AL framework, PMC9732262). Strong relief-on-recognition effects in later-diagnosed adults (Ali 2026).

Treatment implications

Demand reduction. Recovery time (weeks to months). Sensory accommodation. Removal of chronic stressors. Late-diagnosis identity work. Cumulative-load-aware pacing. Cannot be "regulated out of" with acute interventions.

Polyvagal Reframing POPULAR — post-hoc, lacks direct measurement

Core mechanism (claimed)

"Chronic dorsal vagal state." Ventral vagus offline for weeks or months. The person is "stuck at the bottom of the ladder" — chronic immobilisation circuit dominance.

Operational definition

Borrowed phenomenology (chronic exhaustion, skill loss) reframed under polyvagal language. No distinct operational definition exists in peer-reviewed literature — the reframing is mostly in coaching, clinical-practitioner content, and popular psychology.

Empirical literature

None direct. No measurement of "chronic dorsal vagal state" in autistic burnout. No psychometrically-validated instrument measuring this construct. The reframing is theoretical and follows the popularity of polyvagal language in trauma-informed circles.

Mechanism critique

Inherits Neuhuber & Berthoud (2022) critique: emotional freezing appears NA-mediated, not DMV-mediated. So even if "chronic dorsal vagal" existed as a measurable state, the proposed mechanism (DMV over-recruitment) is anatomically contested.

Treatment implications (as deployed)

"Ventral vagal activation": breathwork, humming, cold exposure, polyvagal-informed therapy. The breath/somatic practices may help via independent mechanisms (slow breathing, attention training, social safety) — but they don't address chronic over-demand, accommodation gaps, or comorbidities, which is what the load model says is needed.

Recommended frame for autistic burnout: allostatic load. The framework Raymaker's empirical work actually uses. The framework the AASPIRE measure validates. The framework that fits the data. Polyvagal reframing of burnout is post-hoc and inherits known mechanism problems.
