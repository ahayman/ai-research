# APP2Speak: The Photo-Based AAC App Giving Adults Their Voice Back

Imagine waking up from surgery and being unable to speak. Your thoughts are clear, your needs are urgent, but the words simply will not come. For millions of adults living with conditions like stroke, ALS, Parkinson's disease, and traumatic brain injury, this is not a hypothetical scenario -- it is daily reality. While the AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) market has long been dominated by symbol-based systems designed with children in mind, one speech-language pathologist decided that adults deserved something better: a communication tool built around real photographs, personal memories, and the dignity of grown-up life.

APP2Speak is that tool. Created by Gina Baldwin, an ASHA-certified SLP with over four decades of clinical experience, APP2Speak takes a radically different approach to AAC. Instead of cartoon icons and abstract symbols, users communicate by touching real photographs -- of their family, their home, their favorite meals, their daily world. At $199.99 with no subscription fees, it undercuts major competitors while offering something they often lack: a deeply personal, adult-centered communication experience.

## The Story Behind APP2Speak

### A Clinician's Frustration Becomes Innovation

Gina Baldwin, M.S., CCC-SLP, did not set out to build an app. For over 40 years, she worked in the trenches of speech-language pathology -- hospitals, rehabilitation centers, long-term care facilities -- helping adults who had lost their ability to speak. She created countless speech boards, tried dozens of devices and apps, and watched her patients struggle with tools that were not designed for them.

> "After decades of working with adults who had lost their speech, the pattern was impossible to ignore. The tools were often too childish, too complex, or too focused on therapy or school instead of daily life." -- Gina Baldwin, Creator of APP2Speak

The problems were consistent across the industry. Most AAC devices cost hundreds or thousands of dollars. Symbol-based systems used cartoon-like icons that felt infantilizing to adult users. Interfaces were cluttered with features designed for speech therapy sessions rather than everyday conversation. And perhaps most critically, the tools available treated adult communication needs as an afterthought in a market oriented primarily toward children.

Baldwin's solution was straightforward but revolutionary in its simplicity: build an AAC app around real photographs. After an exhaustive search for the ideal product confirmed that nothing like it existed, she founded Cabson Consulting LLC and began developing APP2Speak. The app launched for iPhone and Android in 2020, bringing decades of clinical insight into a single, photo-driven communication platform.

## How APP2Speak Works

APP2Speak provides three distinct communication modes, each designed for different situations and skill levels. Together, they form a comprehensive system that grows with the user.

### Mode 1: Preset Communication Pages

Out of the box, APP2Speak comes preprogrammed with **80 real-life photographs** organized across **4 themed pages**. These cover essential daily communication needs -- food, hygiene, emotions, common requests -- and each photo is paired with a title word and voice output. Users simply touch a photo to have the associated word or phrase spoken aloud through the device's speakers.

The preset pages are designed for immediate use with zero setup time. Photos can be turned on or off, rearranged within pages, and displayed in two different size options to accommodate visual preferences or low-vision needs. This means a patient can begin communicating basic needs within minutes of downloading the app.

### Mode 2: Custom Communication Pages

This is where APP2Speak truly distinguishes itself. Users can create an **unlimited number of custom pages** populated with their own photographs. Images can be added from three sources:

- **Personal photo library** on the device
- **Internet images** saved to the device
- **In-app camera** for taking photos directly within APP2Speak

Each photo can be associated with one or more custom phrases. A photo of a user's daughter might trigger "I want to talk to Sarah," while a photo of their living room could say "Please help me to the couch." This level of personalization means every APP2Speak installation becomes a unique reflection of its user's life, relationships, and daily needs.

No special training is required to create custom content. Family members, caregivers, or the users themselves can set up new pages in minutes. Custom pages can even be shared with other APP2Speak users via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, allowing speech-language pathologists to create and distribute curated page sets to their clients.

### Mode 3: Text-to-Speech

For users who can type or have some literacy skills, the text-to-speech page provides a freeform typing interface. Users type any message and the app speaks it aloud. Frequently used words and phrases are saved to a history for quick access, enabling more fluid, spontaneous conversation.

The text-to-speech engine offers both male and female voice options with adjustable speech rate, so users can find a voice that feels natural to them. For an even more personal touch, family members can record their own voice -- meaning a user's spouse, child, or friend can literally become their voice through the app.

## Why Photos Matter: The Case for Photo-Based AAC

### The Problem with Symbols

Traditional AAC systems overwhelmingly rely on symbol sets like PCS (Picture Communication Symbols) -- stylized line drawings that represent words and concepts. While these systems have decades of research behind them and work well for many children, they present significant challenges for adults with acquired communication disorders:

**Cognitive load**: Adults with aphasia or brain injuries may struggle to interpret abstract symbols, especially when the connection between symbol and meaning is arbitrary. A line drawing of a house looks nothing like *their* house.

**Dignity concerns**: Many adults find cartoon-like symbols infantilizing. Being handed a communication board covered in clip-art-style icons can feel demeaning for someone who, just weeks earlier, was having normal conversations.

**Learning curve**: Symbol systems often require memorizing the meaning of hundreds of abstract icons -- a significant cognitive demand for someone already dealing with communication and possibly cognitive impairments.

### The Photo-Based Alternative

APP2Speak's approach addresses each of these challenges directly:

**Immediate recognition**: A photograph of a glass of water is instantly recognizable. There is no learning curve, no abstraction to decode. The user sees exactly what they mean.

**Personalization**: When the photo on screen is of *their* kitchen, *their* family member, *their* favorite restaurant, the communication experience becomes deeply meaningful and emotionally resonant.

**Research backing**: Studies on Visual Scene Displays (VSDs) published in PMC demonstrate that adults with acquired communication disorders particularly benefit from personalized photo displays. Research (PMC6436972) shows that realistic, scenic representations of daily life activities are significantly more suitable for adults with aphasia than abstract symbols.

**Adult dignity**: Real photographs inherently respect the user's maturity. There is nothing childish about a photo of your family or your morning coffee.

### Limitations to Acknowledge

The photo-based approach is not without its trade-offs. Abstract concepts like "tomorrow," "maybe," or "because" are difficult to represent with photographs. Photo-based systems also lack the structured linguistic organization found in systems like LAMP (Language Acquisition through Motor Planning) or Unity, which are designed to build complex sentences and support language development over time. For users who need to construct novel, grammatically complete utterances, a photo-based system may need to be supplemented with text-to-speech or paired with other tools.

## Accessibility: From Bluetooth Switches to Eye Tracking

One of APP2Speak's most important features for its target audience is its deep integration with iOS accessibility technologies. Many of the conditions that cause speech loss also affect motor function, making standard touchscreen interaction difficult or impossible.

### Supported Accessibility Features

- **Bluetooth Switches**: External switch devices connect via Bluetooth, allowing users with severe motor limitations to navigate and select items using one or two switches. This is critical for users with advanced ALS or quadriplegia.

- **Touch Accommodations**: iOS touch accommodation settings (hold duration, ignore repeat, tap assistance) work natively with APP2Speak, helping users who have tremors or limited fine motor control.

- **Low Vision Support**: Two display size options for photos, plus compatibility with iOS low-vision settings, ensure that users with visual impairments can still use the app effectively.

- **Apple Eye Tracking (iOS 18+)**: Perhaps the most transformative accessibility feature, APP2Speak works seamlessly with the eye-tracking technology Apple introduced in iOS 18. Users can navigate the entire app and select photos using only their eye movements -- no hands, no switches, no physical contact with the device at all.

- **Page Lock**: A simple but essential feature that prevents accidental edits or navigation during active use, especially important for users with motor control challenges.

### The Control Bionics Partnership

For users with progressive diseases like ALS, APP2Speak has partnered with Control Bionics, whose Trilogy device offers three access modalities that adapt as the disease progresses:

1. **Touch control** in early stages when finger movement is still possible
2. **Eye-gaze tracking** in intermediate stages when hand function declines
3. **NeuroNode EMG control** in advanced stages, detecting the faintest muscle signals to drive communication

This partnership means that a user who begins with APP2Speak using touch can continue using the same familiar app and vocabulary as their condition advances, without needing to learn an entirely new system at each stage.

## Pricing and Market Position

### The Affordability Advantage

At **$199.99** as a one-time purchase with no subscription fees and all future updates included, APP2Speak is positioned as one of the most affordable comprehensive AAC apps on the market. A free Lite version is available for initial evaluation, and a 14-day free trial of the full version helps users commit with confidence. Virtual demonstrations and consultations are also offered at no charge.

For context, dedicated AAC hardware devices can cost $5,000 to $15,000 or more. Even among app-based solutions, APP2Speak's $199.99 price point undercuts Proloquo2Go ($249.99) by $50 and TouchChat HD ($299.99) by $100. The absence of recurring subscription fees -- increasingly common in the AAC market as platforms like TD Snap move to annual models -- makes the long-term cost even more attractive.

### Who Is APP2Speak For?

APP2Speak's primary audience is **adults who have lost their speech** due to acquired conditions. The app is specifically designed for and marketed to people living with:

- **Stroke and aphasia** -- the largest user group, often dealing with sudden speech loss
- **ALS** -- progressive motor neuron disease that gradually affects speech, then all movement
- **Parkinson's disease** -- which can cause severe speech difficulties (dysarthria) in later stages
- **Traumatic brain injury** -- where speech and language abilities may be disrupted
- **Head and neck cancer** -- particularly after surgeries affecting the throat or vocal cords
- **Autism** -- nonverbal adults who benefit from photo-based rather than symbol-based communication
- **Down syndrome** -- individuals who may find photos more intuitive than abstract symbols

## Strengths and Weaknesses

### What APP2Speak Does Well

**Personalization depth**: No other major AAC app centers its entire communication model on personal photographs. The ability to use photos of your actual family, your actual home, your actual daily objects creates a communication experience that is inherently more meaningful and easier to use than any symbol set.

**Adult-first design**: APP2Speak was built from the ground up for adults. There are no childish graphics, no therapy-oriented interfaces, and no features designed for classroom use that get in the way of real-world communication.

**Clinical credibility**: Designed by an SLP with 40+ years of experience, recognized by ASHA, featured by Easter Seals -- APP2Speak carries strong professional endorsements.

**Accessibility breadth**: Support for Bluetooth switches, touch accommodations, low vision, and Apple Eye Tracking covers the full spectrum of motor impairments common in the target population.

**Price**: At $199.99 with no subscriptions, it is the most affordable full-featured AAC app purpose-built for adults.

**Offline operation**: The app works without an internet connection, which is essential in hospitals, care facilities, and rural areas where connectivity is unreliable.

**Voice personalization**: The ability for family members to record their voice adds an emotional dimension that text-to-speech alone cannot match.

### Where APP2Speak Falls Short

**Smaller ecosystem**: Compared to Proloquo2Go or TouchChat, APP2Speak has a smaller user base, fewer online tutorials, fewer third-party resources, and a smaller community of experienced users and clinicians.

**Limited clinical research**: While the app is endorsed by individual SLPs and recognized by ASHA, there are no independent clinical trials or outcome studies specifically evaluating APP2Speak's effectiveness. Competitors like Proloquo2Go have substantially more research backing.

**No linguistic framework**: APP2Speak does not include a structured language system like LAMP or Unity. For users who need to build complex, grammatically correct sentences or who are developing language skills (rather than restoring lost ones), the app's photo-and-phrase model may be insufficient.

**English only**: The app is currently available only in English, limiting its usefulness for multilingual populations.

**Single-developer risk**: Cabson Consulting LLC appears to be a small operation. The long-term viability and development velocity of APP2Speak depends heavily on its founder, which introduces sustainability concerns.

**No cloud backup**: There is no documented cloud synchronization feature for custom content. If a user's device is lost, stolen, or broken, their carefully curated custom pages may be lost as well.

**Abstract concepts**: The photo-based model struggles with abstract vocabulary -- words like "maybe," "later," "because," or emotional states that do not photograph easily. The text-to-speech mode partially compensates, but users who cannot type are left with limited options for expressing nuanced thoughts.

## Recent Developments and Future Outlook

APP2Speak continues to evolve. The integration with Apple's iOS 18 Eye Tracking in 2024 was a significant milestone, opening the app to users who had previously been unable to use touchscreen-based AAC. The Control Bionics partnership extends that accessibility even further into the progressive disease community.

The app maintains an active presence at ATIA conferences and continues to publish blog content covering user stories, AAC best practices, and feature updates. Version 1.50, the most recent release, was published in June 2024.

Looking ahead, the AAC landscape is being reshaped by advances in AI-powered speech generation, personalized voice banking, and brain-computer interfaces. APP2Speak's photo-based approach positions it well to incorporate these technologies -- imagining a future where AI can automatically suggest photos, generate personalized visual scenes, or integrate with voice banking to produce speech that sounds like the user did before their condition.

For now, APP2Speak fills an important and underserved niche: affordable, dignified, deeply personal communication for adults who have lost their voice. In Gina Baldwin's words, the mission is simple -- "helping to provide a voice to those in need."
