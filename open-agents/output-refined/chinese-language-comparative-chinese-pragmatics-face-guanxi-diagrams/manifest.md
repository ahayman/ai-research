## Diagrams

### Diagram 1: The Dual Face System -- Mianzi vs. Lian
- **File:** `diagram-1-dual-face-system.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG comparative architecture diagram
- **Description:** Two overlapping ellipses show the mianzi (social prestige) and lian (moral integrity) dimensions of Chinese face, with their distinct properties (orientation, valence, gain/loss triggers, recovery difficulty) and overlap zone. Right side shows English "face" as a single unified concept for direct comparison. Highlights that high mianzi + low lian = corrupt official; high lian + low mianzi = virtuous poor person.
- **Key concepts:** mianzi, lian, face, Brown and Levinson, Goffman, social vs. moral evaluation

### Diagram 2: Guanxi Network Architecture
- **File:** `diagram-2-guanxi-network.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG concentric ring diagram with network comparison
- **Description:** Concentric rings of decreasing obligation (family, close friends, classmates/colleagues, acquaintances, strangers) centered on ego, with intermediary (zhongjianren) bridges and reciprocity arrows. Contrasted with English flat networking graph. Shows structural differences: hierarchical vs. flat, morally binding vs. loosely transactional, multigenerational vs. short-term.
- **Key concepts:** guanxi, reciprocity (huhui), trust (xinren), hierarchy, intermediary (zhongjianren), English networking

### Diagram 4: Sentence-Final Particles -- The Pragmatic Stance Grid
- **File:** `diagram-4-sentence-final-particles.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG grid/matrix diagram
- **Description:** Seven major sentence-final particles (ma, ba, ne, le, a, ma-flat, la) mapped across three dimensions: epistemic stance (uncertain to certain), illocutionary force (asking to asserting), and interpersonal warmth (neutral to insistent). Each row shows the particle, example, pragmatic force, dimensional ratings, and English equivalent. Demonstrates how the same base sentence (ni qu) produces seven distinct meanings through particle choice.
- **Key concepts:** sentence-final particles (yuqici), epistemic stance, illocutionary force, interpersonal dimension, pragmatic slot

### Diagram 5: The Ritual Refusal Sequence
- **File:** `diagram-5-ritual-refusal-sequence.html`
- **Type:** Custom SVG parallel flowchart
- **Description:** Three-column comparison of Chinese (2-3 round ritual), Arabic (similar pattern), and English (direct acceptance) gift-giving sequences. Each step shows the actual phrases used. Annotations explain the face-saving function at each step. Bottom section shows the same pattern applies to bill-paying, invitations, and compliment responses.
- **Key concepts:** ritual refusal, keqi (politeness), gift-giving, bill-paying fight (zhengzhe maidan), face preservation
