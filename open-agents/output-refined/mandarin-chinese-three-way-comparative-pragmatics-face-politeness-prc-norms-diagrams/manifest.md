# Diagram Manifest: Pragmatics — Face, Politeness & PRC Social Norms

## Diagrams

### diagram-1-dual-face-system.html
**Title:** The Dual Face System: Two Independent Dimensions
**Description:** Side-by-side comparison of mianzi (social face / prestige) and lian (moral face / integrity), with key expressions, severity scale, and three-way comparison (PRC Mandarin vs Classical Chinese vs English). Shows how Chinese splits "face" into two independent axes while English treats it as unitary.
**Embed height:** 750px

### diagram-2-address-term-evolution.html
**Title:** Address Term Evolution: 1949 to Present
**Description:** Timeline showing how PRC political history reshaped the entire Chinese address system across five eras (Pre-1949, Mao Era, Reform Era, Contemporary, Digital Age). Tracks the rise and fall of specific terms (tongzhi, xiaojie, meinv, etc.) with a PRC vs Taiwan divergence comparison panel.
**Embed height:** 900px

### diagram-3-politeness-strategy-comparison.html
**Title:** Politeness Strategy Comparison: Chinese vs English
**Description:** Parallel comparison of Chinese involvement/warmth-oriented politeness (Gu Yueguo's Limao framework) versus English autonomy/restraint-oriented politeness (Brown & Levinson's Face Theory). Includes dimension comparison table and Hall's high-context/low-context spectrum.
**Embed height:** 850px

### diagram-4-refusal-ritual-sequences.html
**Title:** Refusal Ritual Sequences: Chinese vs English
**Description:** Side-by-side walkthrough of a Chinese three-round gift refusal ritual versus English single-round acceptance, with face-saving function annotations at each step. Includes three ritual domain scenarios (gifts, bills, compliments) and cross-cultural collision points.
**Embed height:** 900px

### diagram-5-guanxi-concentric-circles.html
**Title:** Guanxi: Concentric Circles of Obligation
**Description:** SVG visualization of Fei Xiaotong's differential mode of association (chaxu geju) showing four rings radiating from self (family → close circle → acquaintances → strangers) with decreasing obligation levels. Includes three-component GRX model (ganqing, renqing, xinren) and linguistic markers of guanxi.
