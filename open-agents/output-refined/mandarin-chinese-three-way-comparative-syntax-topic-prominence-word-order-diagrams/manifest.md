# Diagram Manifest: Syntax — Topic-Prominence, Word Order & Discourse Structure

## Diagram 1: Topic-Comment vs. Subject-Predicate Architecture
- **File**: `diagram-1-topic-comment-vs-subject-predicate.html`
- **Type**: Side-by-side structural comparison with typology grid
- **Content**: Li & Thompson (1976) four-way typology grid; parsed sentence examples showing topic-comment (Mandarin) vs. subject-predicate (English) organization; three-way comparison strip
- **Key Insight**: In English, the subject must have a grammatical relationship with the verb. In Mandarin, the topic merely establishes the frame — it can be semantically detached from the predicate entirely.

## Diagram 2: Topic Chain Visualization
- **File**: `diagram-2-topic-chain-visualization.html`
- **Type**: Multi-clause flow diagram with reference counting
- **Content**: 4-clause narrative shown in Mandarin (with zero anaphora markers), English (with required pronouns), and Classical Chinese (maximal compression); reference count comparison bars
- **Key Insight**: Mandarin states the topic once and carries it through subsequent clauses via zero anaphora, while English fills every subject slot with a pronoun.

## Diagram 3: Zero Anaphora Three-Way Comparison
- **File**: `diagram-3-zero-anaphora-comparison.html`
- **Type**: Slot-by-slot reference analysis with donut charts and explicitness spectrum
- **Content**: 5-sentence narrative analyzed slot-by-slot across all three language varieties; donut charts showing reference strategy distribution; reference explicitness spectrum
- **Key Insight**: Classical Chinese is the most implicit (67% zeros), Mandarin is moderate (56% zeros), and English is the most explicit (64% pronouns, 0% zeros).

## Diagram 4: Word Order Flexibility Map
- **File**: `diagram-4-word-order-flexibility-map.html`
- **Type**: Central-node diagram with radiating word order variations
- **Content**: SVO as the central/unmarked order, with four branches: OSV (topicalization), SOV (ba construction), OVS (bei passive), V-copy (manner/degree); each with trigger conditions, examples, and language availability badges
- **Key Insight**: Each word order variation is triggered by specific pragmatic conditions. The ba construction is uniquely Mandarin, absent from both Classical Chinese and English.

## Diagram 5: Information Structure Slots — The Mandarin Sentence Template
- **File**: `diagram-5-information-structure-slots.html`
- **Type**: Linear slot template comparison
- **Content**: Full Mandarin positional template (Topic-Time-Place-Subject-Adverb-PrepPhrase-VERB-Complement-Object-Particle) vs. English template; information zone brackets; fully filled example sentence; key contrast callout
- **Key Insight**: Mandarin and English sentence templates are mirror images — Mandarin puts context (time, place, manner) BEFORE the verb; English puts it AFTER.
