Sample Weekly Self-Directed Practice Schedule

A starting structure to adapt. Total time commitment: 5–10 minutes most days, 25–35 minutes once a week. Calibrate volume so the cumulative load goes down, not up.

Daily
~5 min
Morning
~1 min
Body-state check-inPause. Note three things the body is doing right now (tight / loose / heavy / hot / restless / numb / fine). No labelling required.
Lunch
~1 min
Behaviour pulseWhat have I been doing? What have I been avoiding? Quick note in app or paper.
Evening
~3 min
Four-column entrySituation · behaviour · body-state · best-guess label (or "I don't know"). One row per significant event of the day.
Mon, Wed, Fri
+5–8 min
Anytime
Granularity drill (10–15 min)Pick a recent emotional event. List 5 candidate emotion-words from the feelings wheel. Pick the closest. Pick the next-closest. Note how they differ.
Tue, Thu
+5–8 min
Anytime
Affect-labelling practice (5–10 min)Three labelling reps in moments where state is detectable. Approximate labels are valid. The act of labelling — not precision — is the regulator.
Saturday
25–35 min
Block 1
10–15 min
Expressive writing — alexithymia-modifiedPick a stressful event from the week. Write what happened, what you did, what you wish had happened, what you would tell yourself today. Vocabulary list at your elbow. Imprecise language is fine.
Block 2
10–15 min
Pattern reviewRead back the four-column entries from the week. What recurs? Which contexts produced which body-states / behaviours / avoidance? Which labels (or "I don't know"s) cluster together?
Block 3
5 min
Externalisation reviewLook at biometric data (HR / sleep / etc.). Where did body data diverge from "I felt fine"? Those gaps are signal.
Sunday
10–20 min
Anytime
Psychoeducation reading (10–20 min)Read a section of How Emotions Are Made / Atlas of the Heart / chosen psychoeducation material. Or a relevant deep-dive page. No journaling required; reading itself is the practice.

Calibration principles

  • Adapt the volume. If the schedule is producing load rather than relief, cut it back. Less practice that you actually do beats more practice that becomes another source of overwhelm.
  • Sensory environment first. If you're sensory-overloaded, you cannot read body or behaviour reliably. Manage the environment before adding inner work.
  • Special-interest scaffolding. If you have a topic of expertise, use its categorical structure as scaffolding for emotion-vocabulary work.
  • Imprecision is data. "I don't know" entries are valuable; they map the regions where parsing fails most reliably.
  • Patterns over weeks. A single entry is noise. Patterns over weeks are signal. Don't expect insight from any one session.
Calibration caveat For an autistic + alexithymic user with cumulative-overwhelm risk (Cat 8): calibrate the practice volume so the cumulative load goes down, not up. If the practice is producing load rather than relief, cut it back. The goal is to give yourself information about your own life, not to add another demand.
