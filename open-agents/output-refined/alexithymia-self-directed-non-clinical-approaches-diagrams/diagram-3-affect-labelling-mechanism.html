Lieberman 2007 — RVLPFC → mPFC → Amygdala Pathway

"Putting Feelings Into Words" — affect-labelling reduces amygdala reactivity. The mechanism that makes "name it to tame it" more than a slogan.

STIMULUS
↓
AMYGDALA
Affective stimulus arrives — emotional face, charged image, distressing memory, lived event. Amygdala activates: rapid valence-and-threat detection.
LABELLING
TASK
↓
RVLPFC
The act of putting the affect into words — picking an emotion-label, even an approximate one — engages right ventrolateral prefrontal cortex (RVLPFC). This is the verbal-categorisation step.
RVLPFC
↓
mPFC
RVLPFC modulates medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC), the region most consistently associated with self-referential and emotion-related cognition.
mPFC
↓
AMYGDALA
DOWNREGULATION
Amygdala reactivity is reduced. The labelling has done regulatory work — even when the labelling task is implicit and the participant was not asked to use the label to regulate the emotion. The act of mapping a state onto a verbal category is itself a regulator.
Lieberman, M. D., Eisenberger, N. I., Crockett, M. J., Tom, S. M., Pfeifer, J. H., & Way, B. M. (2007) "Putting Feelings Into Words: Affect Labeling Disrupts Amygdala Activity in Response to Affective Stimuli."
Psychological Science 18(5):421–428.
Subsequent work: Lieberman et al. (2011); Burklund, Creswell, Irwin & Lieberman (2014); Torre & Lieberman (2018) review in Emotion Review.

Why this matters for alexithymia

If you can find the precise label

  • "frustrated" not just "upset"
  • "exposed" not just "uncomfortable"
  • "betrayed" not just "angry"

Stronger pathway engagement; more regulatory benefit.

If only an approximate label is available

  • "something pulling-toward"
  • "something pressing-out"
  • "something tight, somewhere"
  • "chest heavy, not sure why"

Some pathway engagement still appears to occur. The act of labelling — not the precision — is the regulator. Don't insist on precision; insist on the act.

Honest hedge The Lieberman effect is a real, replicated lab finding. Whether it scales into clinically meaningful symptom reduction at the population level is more uncertain. Frame as: there is a real underlying mechanism; the practice is well-supported as a regulatory technique; the magnitude of clinical benefit is moderate, not transformative.
