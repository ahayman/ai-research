The External-to-Internal Scaffold

Why most self-directed alexithymia tools work for parsing-deficit profiles. They build vocabulary first (external, learned, taught) and map it to internal states later — the opposite of body-focused interoceptive training.

Body-First / Internal-First

Body-focused interoceptive training

1
Detect internal signal (HR, breath, sensation)
2
Interpret what the signal means
3
Label the resulting state
For parsing-deficit alexithymia: step 1 returns confused or uninterpretable data. The whole sequence stalls or produces miscalibration. Cat 10 documents how this can backfire as iatrogenic harm.
External-First / Vocabulary-First

Granularity / affect-labelling / journaling / apps

1
Build conceptual vocabulary (external, learned, taught)
2
Build labelling fluency on that vocabulary (external practice)
3
Map labels onto internal states gradually, scaffolded by the labels
For parsing-deficit alexithymia: this is the structurally correct order. You cannot ask someone whose internal channel is unreliable to do detection-first. Give them the labels and structures first; let those find their referents.
The structural principle Most self-directed alexithymia tools — granularity-building, affect-labelling, expressive writing, structured journaling, apps, psychoeducation, externalisation, vocabulary tools — share this architecture. They differ in which slice of the scaffold they emphasise (vocabulary vs. labelling vs. context-recognition vs. proxy-data), but all build from the outside in. This is what makes them well-suited to parsing-deficit profiles where internal-only access is unreliable.

Which tools sit where on the scaffold

  • Vocabulary layer (Step 1): Plutchik wheel, feelings wheel, Atlas of the Heart, How Emotions Are Made, Emotion Thesaurus.
  • Labelling-practice layer (Step 2): affect-labelling drills, structured journaling sentence-starters, app-based emotion check-ins (MT-ALEX, Animi, AlexiLearn).
  • Mapping-to-internal layer (Step 3): granularity-building over time, Pennebaker-style expressive writing with scaffolding, integration of body-state proxies, externalisation work (parts-drawing, sand tray).
