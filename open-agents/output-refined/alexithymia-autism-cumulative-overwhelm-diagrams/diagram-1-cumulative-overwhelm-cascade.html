The Cumulative Overwhelm Cascade — 6 Steps With Per-Link Evidence Weights

Each link in the cascade has empirical support of varying strength. The whole chain as a unified causal mechanism is a plausible synthesis, not yet validated by a single longitudinal study. The substrate is empirical; the unification is theoretical.

1

Chronic sensory + social load + masking demands

Daily cumulative environmental load: sensory mismatch (lights, sounds, textures), interpersonal-mismatch demands (double empathy problem), masking effort (CAT-Q-measured camouflaging across Masking, Assimilation, Compensation factors).

Empirical support: STRONG

Cassidy 2018, 2020, 2023 (camouflaging-suicidality); Hull 2019 (CAT-Q); Pearson & Rose 2021, 2023 (masking conceptual analysis); Co-twin 2025 (PMC12659362); Scheeren 2025 EMA. Botha & Frost 2020 minority stress framework.

2

Alexithymia interoceptive failure → can't detect mounting overwhelm

Multidomain interoception failure (cardiac, respiratory, muscular, taste — not just affective) means the person cannot track rising activation. Body whispers; the alexithymic system shouts only when extreme.

Empirical support: STRONG (alexithymia) Caveat: alexithymia, not autism per se

Murphy/Brewer/Bird 2018 (PMC5824617); Brewer/Cook/Bird 2016 (PMC5098957); Shah/Bird 2016 (PMC4962768) — "alexithymia, not autism"; Brewer/Murphy/Bird 2021 (PMC8522807).

3

Late-recognition → "I didn't know I was at the edge until I went over"

The state is only detected when extreme. Early warning signs are missed. Internal experience of "the meltdown came out of nowhere."

Empirical support: MODERATE Strong clinical-writing consensus

Interoception Sensory Questionnaire data (74% of autistic adults report significant interoceptive confusion unless extreme); Mahler 2022 interoception intervention (PMC9045986); Quattrocki & Friston 2014 predictive-coding framework.

4

Meltdown / shutdown / dissociation as the breaking point

Sympathetic mobilization (meltdown) or dorsal-vagal collapse (shutdown). Phenomenologically experienced as terrifying internal disasters; "stuck on the blue screen of death," "survival mode," "going inside myself."

Phenomenology: STRONG Polyvagal mechanism: theoretical

Phung et al. 2021 BIMS framework (PMC8595127); Paris et al. 2025 metaphor analysis (DOI 10.1089/aut.2024.0193); Schauer & Elbert 2010 defense cascade.

5

Repeated unsupported breakdowns as themselves traumagenic events

Each meltdown/shutdown leaves a residue of shame, self-criticism, and post-event rumination. Without external validation or integration, the breakdown itself becomes encoded as a traumatic memory — "an internal disaster I cannot predict."

Clinical-writing consensus Direct longitudinal test ABSENT

Inferential from Rumball 2020 (mental-health events as non-DSM-5 trauma source). Clinical writing: Neff, Price, Hendrickx, Reframing Autism, Trauma Geek lineage. NOT directly tested longitudinally.

6

Resulting CPTSD-pattern symptoms — without a discrete Criterion-A stressor

Full PTSD/CPTSD symptom profile: hyperarousal, avoidance, intrusion, negative self-concept, emotional dysregulation, interpersonal difficulties — but no single identifiable life-threat event to point to.

Empirical: STRONG (the endpoint) Cascade-as-mechanism untested

Rumball 2020 (DOI 10.1002/aur.2306) — >40% of trauma-exposed autistic adults meet PTSD criteria from non-Criterion-A events. Higgins/Arnold 2025: r = 0.70 between autistic burnout (ABSI) and PTSS (PCL-5).

The honest framing Each LINK has empirical support of varying strength. The WHOLE CHAIN as a unified causal mechanism is a plausible synthesis with empirically anchored components and no validated longitudinal test. "The substrate is empirical; the unification is theoretical." Hold the cascade as a candidate model that fits the available evidence, not as established mechanism.
