Counter-Evidence and Complications That Must Be Honoured

No honest account of the cumulative-overwhelm picture can ignore these three findings. Each pushes against an over-simple version of the cascade. The cascade is still useful as a candidate model — but only after these complications are folded in.

1. Shah, Hall, Catmur, Bird (2016) — "Alexithymia, not autism"

Cortex 81:215–220. PMC4962768.

The earlier "autism interoception deficit" findings actually load on co-occurring alexithymia, not on autism per se. About 50% of autistic adults are alexithymic; for that subset, the multidomain interoception failure mechanism applies. For autistic adults without alexithymia, the interoception story is different.

What this means for the cascade Frame link 3 as "co-occurring alexithymia produces interoceptive failure in many autistic people," NOT "autism produces interoceptive failure." The cascade applies to the autistic-AND-alexithymic subset, not to all autistic adults.
What is still claimed Bird/Murphy multidomain interoception failure: STILL claimed for ALEXITHYMIA. Cardiac, respiratory, muscular, taste — all impaired. Murphy/Brewer/Bird 2018 (PMC5824617) is a strengthening, not a walk-back. The autism-specific claim is what was walked back.

2. 2026 dissociation-bullying study

S305065652600012X.

Pathological dissociation in autistic adults was associated with bullying victimization rather than with autistic traits per se. Trauma exposure tracks the dissociation; autism alone does not.

What this means for the cascade Dissociation in autistic adults follows trauma exposure (bullying, interpersonal trauma, chronic invalidation), not autism intrinsically. The cascade should not claim "autism causes dissociation" — it should claim "trauma exposure (including the cumulative-overwhelm pattern) tracks the dissociation, and autism + alexithymia raise the probability of being unable to integrate that exposure."
What is still consistent with the cascade The cascade does NOT require "autism causes dissociation" — it claims autism + alexithymia + cumulative load → late-recognition → meltdown/shutdown/dissociation as a context-and-load-driven outcome. The 2026 finding is consistent with that framing.

3. PMC8964964 — ANS heterogeneity in autism

"Autism Spectrum Disorder in Children Is Not Associated With Abnormal Autonomic Nervous System Function: Hypothesis and Theory."

Challenges the universal-ANS-dysfunction-in-autism narrative. Methodological and population heterogeneity confound the picture; not all autistic people show ANS dysfunction; studies finding it may draw from samples with high alexithymia or trauma comorbidity.

What this means for the cascade Polyvagal-informed framing of "chronic dorsal-vagal shutdown" in autistic adults without discrete trauma is a clinical-writing extension of polyvagal theory, not a directly tested empirical finding. Not all autistic adults are in chronic dorsal-vagal collapse; HRV findings are real but heterogeneous.
What remains supported Reduced HRV and lower parasympathetic tone in autistic adults under social stress is replicated. Affective reactivity is elevated. Interoceptive accuracy moderates the cardiac-emotion processing relationship. The polyvagal mechanism remains a plausible framework; the universal-claim version of it is not supported.

Plus: Kupferstein 2018 ABA-PTSS finding — methodologically contested

Advances in Autism, DOI 10.1108/AIA-08-2017-0016. Critical response: PMC9114057.

Kupferstein reported PTSS in 46% of ABA-exposed respondents. The specific quantitative claim is methodologically criticized in PMC9114057 and elsewhere. The broader observation that instructed masking can be traumatizing has substantial clinical-writing support, but the specific Kupferstein percentage should be presented cautiously.

How to present this honestly "Some autistic adults report PTSS-pattern symptoms following ABA exposure (Kupferstein 2018, contested); the broader pattern (instructed masking can be traumatizing) has clinical-writing support but lacks rigorous quantitative confirmation." Do not cite the 46% figure as established. Do not deny the broader pattern.
The integration principle None of these complications collapses the cumulative-overwhelm cascade. They sharpen its scope. The honest version of the cascade applies to: autistic adults who are also alexithymic, exposed to chronic environmental load, with measurable masking-stress cost, and where dissociation tracks trauma exposure rather than autism per se. That's the population the literature has actually documented PTSD-pattern symptoms in. The cascade is a candidate explanation for that population, not a universal claim about all autistic adults.
