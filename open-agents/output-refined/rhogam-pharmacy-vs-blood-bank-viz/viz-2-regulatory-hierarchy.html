FDA / CBER Regulation

RhIG is regulated by the Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research (CBER) under 21 CFR Part 640 Subpart J - Immune Globulin (Human).

This classifies RhIG as a plasma derivative, NOT a blood component under Part 606.

Key Point: Storage at 2-8°C required. Never expose above 45°C during processing.

AABB Standards

Standard 5.30: "The transfusion service SHALL have a policy for Rh Immune Globulin prophylaxis."

Standard 5.30.2: Requires D antigen testing before administration.

Standard 5.30.3: Ensures appropriate dosing.

Key Point: The word "SHALL" makes this a mandatory requirement for AABB-accredited facilities. The transfusion service, not pharmacy, is explicitly responsible.

College of American Pathologists

CAP's Transfusion Medicine (TRM) Checklist includes requirements for RhIG testing and administration.

Surveys focus on laboratory procedures including weak D testing and FMH quantification.

Key Point: CAP checklist TRM.40780 addresses weak D testing requirements for newborns.

Joint Commission

JC classifies RhIG as a "blood derivative" which can be surveyed under:

• Immunohematology Standards (Blood Bank), OR
• Medication Management Standards (Pharmacy)

Key Point: Official JC position: "No, this is NOT a mandatory practice" - either approach is acceptable. Some surveyors have incorrectly stated pharmacy is required.

State Regulations

State requirements vary significantly by jurisdiction.

Some state pharmacy boards may require oversight of all medications including blood derivatives.

Key Point: Check your specific state's pharmacy board and health department requirements for guidance.

Hospital Policies

Institutions have flexibility in determining whether blood bank or pharmacy manages RhIG inventory.

Recommended: Blood bank management with Type & Screen verification before dispensing.

Key Point: Regardless of storage location, Type and Screen must be completed before release, and blood bank should verify patient eligibility.
