# Diagram Manifest: Classifiers & Measure Words

## Diagrams

### Diagram 1: Classifier Slot in Noun Phrase Syntax
- **File**: `diagram-1-classifier-slot-syntax.html`
- **Description**: Shows the mandatory [Dem + Num + CL + N] template in Mandarin, contrasting with English's direct [Num + N] and Cantonese's unique bare [CL + N] for definiteness. Includes grammatical/ungrammatical examples.
- **Key Insight**: The classifier occupies a syntactic slot that English fills with nothing (for count nouns) and that Cantonese can use alone for definiteness marking.

### Diagram 2: Shape/Dimension-Based Classifier System
- **File**: `diagram-2-shape-dimension-system.html`
- **Description**: Visual catalog of 9 major shape-based classifiers organized by physical dimension (1D flexible, 1D rigid, 2D flat, 3D round, handled, chunks, slices, bound volumes, bundles). Each card shows the classifier character, pinyin, semantic logic, and example nouns.
- **Key Insight**: Classifiers encode embodied cognition — how humans physically perceive and interact with objects.

### Diagram 3: Animacy Hierarchy in Classifier Selection
- **File**: `diagram-3-animacy-hierarchy.html`
- **Description**: Four-level hierarchy (humans → higher animals → lower animals → inanimate objects) showing which classifiers are required at each level and whether the general 个 gè is acceptable. Includes ERP neural evidence box.
- **Key Insight**: Animacy is a fine-grained gradient, not a binary; higher animals neurologically resist 个 replacement.

### Diagram 4: Top 20 Most Common Classifiers Reference Card
- **File**: `diagram-4-top-classifiers-reference.html`
- **Description**: Ranked table of the 20 most frequent classifiers with character, pinyin, semantic category (color-coded), primary domain, example phrase, and relative frequency bar.
- **Key Insight**: 个 alone accounts for ~87-94% of all classifier tokens; the remaining 19 cover nearly all other daily needs.

### Diagram 5: 个 Gè Domain Expansion
- **File**: `diagram-5-ge-domain-expansion.html`
- **Description**: Three-zone visualization (accepted / encroaching / blocked) showing which nouns 个 can replace, the prototypicality gradient effect, and the four factors driving generalization.
- **Key Insight**: 量词个化 (classifier ge-ification) is one of the most active grammatical changes in contemporary Mandarin.

### Diagram 6: Mandarin Obligatory vs. English Optional Comparison
- **File**: `diagram-6-mandarin-vs-english-comparison.html`
- **Description**: Feature comparison matrix, parallel examples (count/mass/collective nouns), and overlap zone showing where English measure words parallel Mandarin's mensural classifiers. Highlights the true typological divide.
- **Key Insight**: English and Mandarin overlap in mensural measures but diverge completely in sortal classifiers — the mandatory encoding of shape, animacy, and function.
