# Proloquo: Inside AssistiveWare's Next-Generation AAC Revolution

What happens when you take over a decade of real-world usage data from more than 10,000 AAC users and pour 100 person-years of development into building something entirely new? You get Proloquo -- AssistiveWare's bold, research-driven reimagining of augmentative and alternative communication that earned the first-ever App Store Cultural Impact Award given to an AAC app.

Launched in 2022, Proloquo is not an update to Proloquo2Go. It is a fundamentally different product built on a fundamentally different philosophy: that consistency, motor planning, and an integrated coaching ecosystem matter more than unlimited customization. With over 16,000 pre-planned words -- four times more than any competing AAC system -- and innovative features like Thinking Space and Focus Mode, Proloquo represents a generational leap in how nonspeaking individuals communicate. But the app is not without its trade-offs, and understanding what it does differently (and why) is essential for families, clinicians, and educators making one of the most consequential technology decisions in a person's life.

## The Origin Story: Why AssistiveWare Built a Second AAC App

AssistiveWare's journey began in 2000 when founder David Niemeijer created the company in Amsterdam after his friend Giesbert became paralyzed in a car accident and needed accessible computer technology. Through the early 2000s, the company developed Mac OS X accessibility tools before making history in 2009 with the release of Proloquo2Go -- widely recognized as the first full-featured AAC app for iOS.

Proloquo2Go became a transformative product, helping catalyze the shift from dedicated AAC devices costing $8,000-$15,000 to consumer tablets running specialized software. By 2013, AssistiveWare had also released Proloquo4Text for text-based AAC users. But as the company gathered anonymous usage data from tens of thousands of users across both products, a realization emerged: the next generation of AAC needed to be built from the ground up.

> "Over a hundred person-years of work went into creating Proloquo and Proloquo Coach." -- AssistiveWare

The key insight was that Proloquo2Go's greatest strength -- its near-infinite customizability -- was also a limitation. When buttons can be moved, deleted, and rearranged freely, users cannot develop the consistent motor plans that enable fast, fluent communication. AssistiveWare made the deliberate decision to build a new app around a different philosophy rather than compromise either approach. The result was two products serving two different sets of needs, coexisting in the market rather than replacing each other.

## Crescendo Evolution: 16,000 Words That Never Move

At the heart of Proloquo is its Crescendo Evolution vocabulary system -- a research-based vocabulary architecture that represents arguably the most ambitious pre-planned word set in AAC history.

### The Research Foundation

Crescendo Evolution was built by analyzing anonymous word usage data from over 10,000 AAC users across both Proloquo2Go (symbol-based) and Proloquo4Text (text-based). This real-world data was then combined with extensive vocabulary research to identify the words needed for full participation in educational curriculum from early childhood through late elementary school and beyond.

The result: over 16,000 pre-planned words, including 4,500+ graphic symbols for general conversation and 5,000+ essential curriculum words. This is more than four times the vocabulary of any competing AAC system.

### Motor Planning by Design

What makes Crescendo Evolution truly distinctive is not just its size but its permanence. Words in Proloquo never move. They are always in the same location, reached through the same path of taps. There is no "back button" in the traditional sense -- every word is accessed through a consistent, repeatable motor plan.

This design choice is grounded in AAC research showing that motor planning -- the ability to navigate to a word through muscle memory rather than visual scanning -- is one of the strongest predictors of communication speed and fluency. When you always know that "happy" is in the same place, you stop searching for it and start reaching for it.

The home screen presents 48 symbol buttons and 12 text-only buttons organized across 9 tabs. The two top rows remain static across all views, providing one-tap access to the most frequently used words. Over 90% of curriculum words come preprogrammed, freeing parents and educators to focus on adding only the personalized vocabulary specific to each individual user.

### The Customization Trade-Off

This stability comes at a deliberate cost: users cannot move or remove preprogrammed vocabulary. You can add new words, but the base vocabulary is locked in place. AssistiveWare argues this is essential for motor planning integrity. Critics argue it limits the ability to tailor the system for users with unique communication needs or preferences.

This philosophical divide -- consistency versus flexibility -- is the central tension that separates Proloquo from Proloquo2Go and drives much of the debate in the AAC community about which approach better serves users.

## Thinking Space: Communication Beyond Sentences

One of Proloquo's most innovative features is Thinking Space -- a flexible whiteboard area that breaks free from the linear sentence bar found in traditional AAC apps.

In Thinking Space, users can drag symbols from the vocabulary grid into an open canvas, then cluster, rearrange, and connect them freely. This enables forms of expression that go beyond constructing grammatical sentences: brainstorming, concept mapping, showing relationships between ideas, and exploratory communication.

For emergent communicators -- including very young children just beginning their AAC journey -- Thinking Space removes the pressure of building "correct" sentences. A child can place symbols for "dog," "park," and "happy" on the canvas to communicate a feeling or memory without needing to construct "I was happy at the park with the dog."

The contents of Thinking Space can be saved as photos, printed, or shared with communication partners, making it a powerful tool for both real-time interaction and reflective communication.

## Focus Mode: Guiding Without Gatekeeping

Focus Mode addresses one of the thorniest challenges in AAC education: how to direct a learner's attention to target vocabulary without restricting their access to the full word set.

Traditional approaches to teaching AAC often involve shrinking the grid, hiding buttons, or removing words to reduce overwhelm. But these strategies come at a cost -- they limit the user's communicative freedom and can create prompt dependency, where the user only communicates what they have been explicitly set up to say.

Proloquo's Focus Mode takes a different approach. It uses light and motion to gently highlight target words, creating visual pathways that invite exploration without building walls. The full vocabulary remains accessible at all times. If a student navigates away from the tab containing the focus words, the visual cues follow them, gently guiding attention back without forcing compliance.

This design reflects a broader philosophy in modern AAC practice: that access to robust vocabulary should never be sacrificed for instructional convenience, and that the best way to learn language is through exposure to a rich communicative environment.

## Proloquo Coach: The Team Behind the Communicator

Perhaps the most significant systemic innovation in Proloquo is not in the communication app itself but in its companion: Proloquo Coach.

Available free to all Proloquo subscribers, Coach is a separate app designed primarily for parents but also valuable for educators and speech-language pathologists. It runs on both iPhone and iPad (unlike Proloquo itself, which requires an iPad) and provides structured, self-paced guidance on how to support an AAC user effectively.

Coach includes topic-based training modules like "Starting Proloquo With Your Child" and "Learn About AAC," a usage tracking dashboard with weekly graphs showing how frequently the main Proloquo app is being used, and a direct support channel to AssistiveWare's team. The coaching approach is positive and encouraging, designed to build confidence in communication partners who may feel overwhelmed by AAC technology.

This matters because AAC success depends heavily on the environment around the user. Research consistently shows that communication partner training -- teaching the people in an AAC user's life how to model, respond to, and encourage AAC use -- is one of the strongest predictors of successful AAC outcomes. Proloquo Coach attempts to democratize this training, making it accessible to any family member with a smartphone rather than requiring paid professional consultation.

## Voice Technology: Sounding Like Yourself

Proloquo was the first AAC app to incorporate Acapela Neural Voices -- speech synthesis powered by deep neural network technology that produces significantly more natural, smoother-sounding output compared to traditional concatenative synthesis. The voices include multiple regional English accents, allowing users to select a voice that reflects their identity and geographic context.

The app also supports Apple's system voices and, most notably, Apple's Personal Voice feature introduced in iOS 17. Personal Voice allows users to create a synthesized voice that sounds like their own natural speech by reading 150 phrases to train the model. For people with progressive conditions like ALS or Parkinson's who may lose their natural voice over time, this feature represents a profound form of identity preservation -- continuing to communicate in your own voice even after the ability to produce natural speech has diminished.

## Team Sharing and Cloud Sync

Proloquo's cloud-based architecture enables what may be the most seamless team collaboration experience in the AAC market. When a family purchases a Proloquo subscription, every member of the AAC user's team -- parents, teachers, speech-language pathologists, grandparents -- can download both Proloquo and Proloquo Coach for free and sync automatically.

All vocabulary data is stored in the cloud and syncs instantly across devices. When a teacher adds a new word at school, it appears on the home iPad within moments. Role-based permissions allow the team to control who can use the vocabulary, who can edit it, and who can manage team membership.

This stands in sharp contrast to Proloquo2Go, where sharing vocabulary between devices required manual backup and restore procedures through iTunes, Dropbox, or WiFi -- a process that was technically challenging and often resulted in teams working with inconsistent vocabulary setups across settings.

## The Proloquo2Go Comparison: Two Philosophies, One Company

Understanding Proloquo requires understanding how it relates to the product it was born from. Proloquo and Proloquo2Go are not competitors in the traditional sense -- they are complementary products reflecting fundamentally different design philosophies.

**Proloquo2Go** is the right choice for users who need extensive grid size options (from 9 to 144 buttons), multiple language support, iPhone compatibility, advanced alternative access methods for users with significant motor or vision impairments, or deep customization of vocabulary layout.

**Proloquo** is the right choice for users who can independently operate an iPad, benefit from strong motor planning with consistent word placement, want the guided coaching experience of Proloquo Coach, need seamless cloud-synced team collaboration, or are served by the English language.

### The Pricing Debate

The pricing models also differ significantly. Proloquo2Go charges a one-time fee of $249.99 -- you pay once and own the app indefinitely. Proloquo uses a subscription model: free to download with a 6-month trial, then $99.99 per year.

This is a contentious topic in the AAC community. Subscription advocates point out that the initial cost is lower and that ongoing revenue supports continuous development, Proloquo Coach, and cloud infrastructure. Critics argue that AAC is a lifelong need and that subscriptions create indefinite financial burden -- a particularly painful consideration for families already managing the considerable expenses associated with disability.

## The 2023 App Store Cultural Impact Award

In November 2023, Apple selected Proloquo for its App Store Award for Cultural Impact -- an honor recognizing apps that have made "a lasting impact on people's lives and communities." Proloquo was the first AAC app ever to receive this distinction.

The award brought unprecedented mainstream visibility to AAC technology. For a field that often operates in the margins of the tech industry, Apple's recognition signaled that communication access is not a niche concern but a cultural imperative.

2. **Massive Vocabulary**: 16,000+ pre-planned words, including 5,000+ curriculum words
3. **Motor Planning Excellence**: Words that never move build reliable muscle memory
4. **Thinking Space**: A genuine innovation for non-linear, exploratory communication

## Weaknesses and Limitations

1. **English Only**: No multilingual support -- a significant gap in diverse communities
2. **iPad Only**: The main app does not run on iPhone, limiting portability
3. **Limited Customization**: Cannot move or remove preprogrammed vocabulary
4. **Subscription Model**: $99.99/year adds up; costs exceed Proloquo2Go after year 3
5. **Single Grid Size**: Only one layout option (48+12), unlike Proloquo2Go's 23 options
6. **Learning Curve**: Users transitioning from other AAC systems report difficulty adapting
7. **Narrower Accessibility**: Not designed for users with significant motor or vision impairments
8. **Voice Quality Concerns**: Some recent updates criticized for voice changes
9. **Platform Lock-In**: No Android, no macOS, no Windows -- Apple ecosystem only
10. **Font and Contrast**: Limited options for adjusting text size and visual contrast

## User Reviews and Community Reception

Proloquo holds a 4.1 out of 5 rating on the App Store -- solid but notably below Proloquo2Go's 4.8/5 rating, which reflects the mature, well-understood nature of the older product.

Positive reviewers consistently highlight the app's ease of use ("less overwhelming than other apps"), the strength of the research-based vocabulary design, and the value of Proloquo Coach for family engagement. Full-time AAC users praise the personalization options including voice selection, display preferences, and the history and typing functions.

Critical reviewers point to the navigation learning curve, frustrations with customization limitations ("very difficult and frustrating"), and specific accessibility gaps like the inability to adjust font size or icon dimensions. Some users have reported quality issues with recent voice updates, describing a "grating breathy quality" in certain voice packs.

The subscription pricing model generates the most heated debate, with passionate arguments on both sides about the appropriate way to fund assistive technology in an era of cloud services and continuous development.

## Who Should Consider Proloquo?

**Ideal candidates:**
- Nonspeaking children (as young as 2) who can independently interact with an iPad
- Adults who need symbol-based AAC communication
- People with autism, stroke, physical trauma, or progressive conditions
- Families wanting structured coaching support through the AAC journey
- Educational teams wanting seamless cloud-synced vocabulary collaboration
- Users who benefit from strong motor planning and consistent placement
- AAC beginners who benefit from a massive pre-built vocabulary

**Consider alternatives if you need:**
- Multilingual or non-English communication
- iPhone-only AAC access
- Extensive grid size customization
- Advanced alternative access methods for significant motor/vision impairments
- One-time purchase pricing without ongoing subscription

## Looking Ahead

Proloquo continues to receive regular updates. The 4.0 release in 2024 brought major enhancements including the full Crescendo Evolution vocabulary and the Thinking Space whiteboard. As of early 2025, development continues with Focus Mode refinements and bug fixes.

AssistiveWare has not publicly announced plans for additional language support or Android development, though these remain among the most-requested features from the community. The company maintains both Proloquo and Proloquo2Go as active products with independent development roadmaps, suggesting a long-term commitment to serving diverse user needs through complementary approaches rather than a single unified solution.

## Conclusion

Proloquo represents a bold bet: that the future of AAC lies not in infinite flexibility but in intelligent constraint. By fixing vocabulary in place, Proloquo enables the motor planning that makes communication fast and fluent. By including Proloquo Coach, it acknowledges that an AAC device is only as good as the support system around it. By cloud-syncing across teams, it ensures that the person at the center of the system always has the same words in the same places, no matter where they are.

The trade-offs are real. English-only support, iPad-only availability, limited customization, and subscription pricing are meaningful limitations that will make Proloquo wrong for some users. But for those it serves well -- English-speaking iPad users who benefit from consistency and motor planning, supported by engaged families and teams -- Proloquo is the most research-informed, thoughtfully designed AAC product available today.

In a field where the right communication tool can mean the difference between isolation and connection, that matters enormously.
