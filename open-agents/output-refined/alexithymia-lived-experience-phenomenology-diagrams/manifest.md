# Diagrams Manifest — Lived Experience and Phenomenology (Cat 12)

## Overview

Seven standalone HTML diagrams supporting Cat 12 of the alexithymia deep dive. All are self-contained pages with inline CSS, no external dependencies, designed to be embedded via `<iframe>` in the explainer page and also navigable as standalone pages.

## Diagram inventory

### Diagram 1: The Substitution Patterns
- **File**: `diagram-1-substitution-patterns.html`
- **Purpose**: Show the four common patterns that appear when emotion-words are unavailable: somatic, behavioural, cognitive, numerical/situational substitution.
- **Key content**: Side-by-side examples of what the alexithymic user says vs what the substitution carries. Frame is "substitutions are information, not evasion."
- **Embed height**: ~1100px

### Diagram 2: Type-Specific Phenomenology
- **File**: `diagram-2-type-specific-phenomenology.html`
- **Purpose**: Three-column comparison of Type I "affectless" / Type II "feelings present but illegible" / hypervigilant-with-mislabelling lived experience. Includes calibration anchors for the user.
- **Key content**: Inside-the-head sound, body, behaviour, mappings, and honest take per type. Closing note that combinations are common.
- **Embed height**: ~1300px

### Diagram 3: Calsius 2015 Lived Body Axes
- **File**: `diagram-3-calsius-lived-body.html`
- **Purpose**: Hyper-presence of the lived body vs alienation from the lived body — the two themes from the Calsius IPA study with attention to the finding that they often co-exist.
- **Key content**: First-person quotes for each axis, phenomenological texture, and explicit epistemic note about IPA methodology.
- **Embed height**: ~1100px

### Diagram 4: Therapist-Side vs Patient-Side Mismatch
- **File**: `diagram-4-therapist-patient-mismatch.html`
- **Purpose**: Show the documented mismatch between therapist reports of alexithymic clients ("bored, closed off, intellectualizing") and patient reports of the same sessions ("confused, ashamed, trying hard, in something intense"). Bridge into the failed-therapy-patient cycle.
- **Key content**: Two columns of report types with mismatch indicator between, plus explicit locus question (traditional vs parsing-deficit framing).
- **Embed height**: ~900px

### Diagram 5: Autistic First-Person Themes
- **File**: `diagram-5-autistic-first-person-themes.html`
- **Purpose**: Six recurring themes from autistic-affirming alexithymia literature, plus three compensatory routes, plus the "different emotional language" reframe.
- **Key content**: Late recognition, in-state confusion, intellectual-self gap, pattern-recognition asymmetry, intensity-without-category, late-life recognition. Compensatory routes: pattern recognition, special-interest scaffolding, external rules. Honest sourcing note.
- **Embed height**: ~1500px

### Diagram 6: The "Failed Therapy Patient" Cycle
- **File**: `diagram-6-failed-therapy-patient-cycle.html`
- **Purpose**: Eight-step iatrogenic cycle showing how alexithymic patients accumulate the "failed therapy patient" narrative across multiple therapeutic relationships, with explicit corrective frame.
- **Key content**: Steps 1-8 with the cycle indication that it repeats with next clinician; what compounds; corrective frame naming the pattern as iatrogenic, not personal verdict.
- **Embed height**: ~1400px

### Diagram 7: Deficit Frame vs Different-Language Reframe
- **File**: `diagram-7-deficit-vs-different-language.html`
- **Purpose**: Side-by-side comparison of the standard deficit frame and the autistic-affirming different-language reframe, with explicit "both can be held" synthesis.
- **Key content**: Implicit norm, what each says, therapy goal, internal experience, where each works. Mottron-tradition lineage. Honest caveat that the reframe complements rather than fully replaces the deficit framing.
- **Embed height**: ~1200px

## Style notes

- All diagrams use the consistent palette: indigo (#6366F1) for primary, red (#DC2626) for problems/risks, amber (#F59E0B) for caveats/synthesis, green (#10B981) for affirming/sources, blue (#2563EB) for therapist-side or comparison.
- All diagrams have inline CSS, no external CSS dependencies, no JavaScript.
- All diagrams are mobile-responsive (grids collapse to single column on narrow screens).
- All diagrams use system-ui font stack to match the parent page.
- All diagrams include source attribution where citations are central.
