The Interaction Map, Graded by Real Risk

A pharmacy checker flags every one of these at roughly the same severity. That is functionally identical to flagging nothing, because a list where everything is urgent teaches you to stop reading it. Graded by what the evidence actually shows, the list sorts into four very different things.

The framing problem

The serotonin syndrome literature is genuinely bimodal: a handful of combinations that have killed people, and a long tail of reflexive warnings with no cases behind them. Under-warning fails you. So does over-warning, and it is the more likely failure, because over-warning feels responsible while it quietly trains you to ignore the three items that matter.
Tier 1 — Genuinely dangerous: absolute contraindications Label contraindications. Real deaths.
MAOIsphenelzine, tranylcypromine, isocarboxazid, selegiline
Contraindicated. 5 days off duloxetine before starting an MAOI; 14 days off an MAOI before starting duloxetine. The washout is long and asymmetric.

Linezolidantibiotic
Reversible MAOI. Avoid. Given by infectious-disease teams treating resistant Gram-positive infection, who are not thinking about your antidepressant.
[primary, verified]
Methylene blueintravenous
Potent reversible MAO-A inhibitor. Avoid. FDA safety communication, 2011. Given by anaesthetists, intraoperatively — when you are unconscious and cannot tell them.
[primary, verified]
Tramadollikely to be offered for fibromyalgia
Triple hazard, and this one is live rather than theoretical. Serotonergic in its own right; duloxetine's CYP2D6 inhibition blocks its conversion to the analgesic metabolite, so it works less while the serotonergic parent lingers; plus it lowers the seizure threshold. See Diagram 3.

Tier 2 — Real and situational Genuine, but conditional on circumstance
Fluvoxamine
Raises duloxetine AUC ~6-fold; apparent clearance down ~77%. A 20mg dose behaves like roughly 120mg. The single largest interaction on the drug.
[primary, verified]
Ciprofloxacin, enoxacin
Potent CYP1A2 inhibitors, and CYP1A2 is duloxetine's primary metabolic route. Avoid per label. Ciprofloxacin is an extremely ordinary antibiotic. Tell any antibiotic prescriber.
[primary, verified]
Another antidepressantespecially an SSRI
Genuine additive risk. After MAOIs, the most common real-world cause of serotonin toxicity. Relevant during cross-tapers.
[secondary — unverified]
Dextromethorphanat supratherapeutic doses
SRI + NMDA antagonist at high doses, with documented cases. A single OTC cough-syrup dose is a materially different exposure from recreational use, but it is in products bought without thinking.
[secondary — unverified]
Tier 3 — Moot for you: you already told us Padding the page with these would be the reflex this map is correcting
St John's wort
You do not take it. Worth one line only because the mechanism is interesting: it is additively serotonergic and a CYP1A2 inducer, so it would lower duloxetine levels — producing withdrawal symptoms while you were still taking the drug.
[secondary — unverified]
Alcohol
You rarely drink. Substantial alcohol use is the main amplifier of duloxetine's hepatotoxicity, so the principal amplifier is largely off the table for you.
[secondary — unverified]
Smoking
You do not smoke — but this one inverts into something that does apply. Smokers run 38.4% lower serum / 53.6% lower dose-adjusted duloxetine. Read from where you stand: you sit 38–54% HIGHER in exposure than a smoker on an identical prescription. Not a hazard; a calibration. See Diagram 4.
[primary, verified]
Tier 4 — Over-warned, and saying so matters Reflexive flagging is as unhelpful as none
Triptansmigraine
Of the 29 FDA case reports behind the 2006 alert: 7/29 met Sternbach criteria, 0/29 met Hunter criteria (the gold standard). A 14-year cohort of 19,017 co-prescribed patients over 30,928 person-years found 2 definite and 5 possible cases: 0.6–2.3 per 10,000 person-years. The American Headache Society recommends against restricting the combination. Known to be wrong for over a decade. Untreated migraine is a real cost paid for a 1-in-10,000 hazard.
[primary, verified]
5-HTP
ZERO documented human cases, alone or combined with antidepressants, after decades of OTC use. The mechanism is not silly — it is a direct serotonin precursor bypassing tryptophan hydroxylase. But the case count is the case count.
[primary, verified]
Ondansetron
A 5-HT3 antagonist. Despite an FDA warning, the mechanism runs the wrong way. This flag can be safely ignored.
[secondary — unverified]
Lithium
"Unlikely to cause significant serotonin toxicity even when combined with other serotonergic medications." Routinely flagged; rarely a real problem.
[secondary — unverified]
L-tryptophan, SAMe
Theoretical. Tryptophan is rate-limited by tryptophan hydroxylase and was historically combined with TCAs deliberately and safely. SAMe's interaction literature is explicitly characterised as "very limited."
[secondary — unverified]

The shape, not the list

Serotonin syndrome on duloxetine monotherapy is very rare — it exists as a case report, which is to say possible and vanishingly uncommon. Almost all of the risk that gets flagged at you is not risk. Almost all of the risk that is real arrives through a door this page cannot watch: an anaesthetist reaching for methylene blue, an infection team reaching for linezolid, a pain clinic reaching for tramadol, a walk-in centre reaching for ciprofloxacin. That asymmetry is the thing to act on, and the action is unglamorous: the information has to live somewhere outside your head, because the situations that matter most are the ones where you cannot explain yourself.
