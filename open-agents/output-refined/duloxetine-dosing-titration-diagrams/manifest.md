## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: Two Effects. Two Dose Curves. One Appointment. — THE CENTRAL IMAGE
- **Type**: Two dose-response plots side by side, each with "YOU ARE HERE" at 20mg, plus a five-row dose table
- **Elements**: Left panel, pain: a measured null through 30mg (red), an uncertainty band marked "THE STEP," a plateau from 40mg (green) drawn *low* to encode NNTB 11, and a footer carrying Goldstein's equal-arm design. Right panel, psychological: a measured 20mg point above placebo, a **dashed** segment to 60mg labelled "NEVER COMPARED," a flat 60→120 line, and a red escalation arrow reading 30.0% → 30.5%
- **Key Insight**: The two curves have different shapes and from 20mg they point in different directions. Going up buys a modest threshold-gated pain effect and buys nothing measurable on the axis he reports benefiting from.
- **Design decision**: the plateau is drawn low rather than high. An NNTB of 11 is a real effect and a small one, and a chart that draws the post-threshold plateau near the top of the axis would overstate it. The visual encodes the size, not just the existence.
- **Corrections carried**: NNTB **11** (Welsch 2018, fibromyalgia-specific), not NNTB 8; Cochrane's authors' conclusion quoted verbatim; the Sultan disagreement presented rather than resolved

### Diagram 2: Every Pain Trial, Plotted by Dose and Outcome
- **Type**: Single dose axis with every trial placed on it, three shaded verdict zones, plus a correction table
- **Elements**: the 0-for-3 zone below 40mg; Goldstein and Russell at 20mg; Arnold 2012 at 30mg with "POWERED" called out; the Japanese 40mg trial as the lowest positive; the 60/120mg cluster; Murakami and Chappell in amber as *misread* rather than failed; a flat bar spanning 60→120
- **Key Insight**: Nothing, then a step, then a plateau. The shape is a threshold, not a slope.
- **Corrections carried, in both directions**: Murakami is not a pain failure (responder rates and 6/7 pain secondaries separated); Chappell is not a 60mg trial (84% blindly escalated, mean 113.4mg); Cochrane's "no dose effect from 40mg to 120mg" *starts at 40mg* and is not about 20mg; the ~193 census refutes "~66"

### Diagram 3: The Gain Was Time, Not Milligrams
- **Type**: Left-to-right randomization flow, a black "half a percentage point" bar, the authors' verbatim sentence, and a forest plot of Dold's CI
- **Elements**: the 60mg → non-remission → randomize → stay/double flow ending in 30.0% and 30.5%; the full authors' sentence with its usually-dropped first half highlighted; Dold's CI drawn to scale (−0.20 to 0.12) with a note to read the interval rather than the p-value; the side-effect-dropout asymmetry
- **Key Insight**: Doubling the dose in people who had demonstrably not responded bought half a percentage point, and both arms kept improving, so the improvement was time rather than dose.
- **Corrections carried**: cite **Kornstein**, not Dunner (the publisher page reverses the author order — the second such trap caught in this project); Furukawa and Jakubovski **contradict each other** and **both exclude duloxetine**, so the "flat above minimum doses" background claim is not applied to his drug; venlafaxine, the other SNRI, is the drug in Furukawa's dataset that looked *least* flat; prophylactic escalation has never been studied in anyone

### Diagram 4: "Beats Placebo" and "As Good as 60mg" Are Different Claims
- **Type**: Nicolini's four arms above, Allgulander's pooled arms below, with the 20mg arm struck through in the pooled row; plus a claim-status table
- **Elements**: arm sizes as large numerals; every pooled column marked "reconciles exactly"; the excluded 20mg arm in dashed red; a non-inferiority arrow spanning only 60–120mg vs venlafaxine
- **Key Insight**: "20mg is as good as 60mg" is untested rather than true, and so is its opposite. The absence cuts both ways.
- **Corrections carried**: the **45%/55% percentages are internally inconsistent** with the absolute changes and must not be presented as facts (supplement 1 said so explicitly and the binding brief propagated them anyway — see Deviations below); the somatic null is **weak evidence of absence**, not a demonstrated failure, and saying so runs against this page's own convenience; the Li 2018 guardrail that HAM-A somatic ≠ his "anxiety without cognitive content"

### Diagram 5: The Only Trial That Ever Compared 20mg to 60mg on Sleep
- **Type**: Grouped bar chart (3 AEs × 4 arms) with "20 ≈ 60" brackets, plus the full nine-row AE table
- **Elements**: insomnia, somnolence and fatigue bars at placebo/20/60/120 with exact percentages; green brackets marking 20 ≈ 60 on insomnia and somnolence; an amber dashed bracket marking fatigue as the exception; a "THE INFLECTION IS AT 120mg" callout; twin footers separating the defensible claim from the indefensible one
- **Key Insight**: There is no evidence the 20→60 step damages sleep, and the harm sits at 120mg. That is the claim. "60mg helps your sleep" is not.
- **⚠ Deviation from the brief, deliberate and flagged on the diagram**: the brief states "**20mg ≈ 60mg on every sleep measure**." That is **not quite right and the exception is decision-relevant.** It holds for insomnia, somnolence, sleep disorder and discontinuation. **Fatigue rises 0% → 6.03% across the 20→60 step** — supplement 5 itself calls it "the only clean monotonic rise across the 20→60 step" — and fatigue is his primary disability. The diagram carries the corrected version and names the exception rather than rounding it away. The brief's load-bearing point (the inflection is at 120mg, not at 60mg) is preserved intact.
- **Corrections carried**: the Athens Insomnia result is a secondary endpoint of a trial that **failed its primary**, uncorrected across six comparisons, and **does not replicate** (p=0.329, p=0.590); the Wilson intervals for 20mg and 60mg overlap almost entirely; the 120mg differential dropout *flatters* 120mg; the absent RLS/dreams terms are weak evidence of absence and must not be reported as "no RLS in 404 patients"

### Diagram 6: You Are Braced for the Wrong Thing
- **Type**: Two symmetric columns (insomnia green, somnolence red) with per-trial step rows, plus a five-trial table and a first-person quote block
- **Elements**: three insomnia rows all flat or falling below 120mg with the 120mg exception in red; three somnolence rows all rising at 30→60; a black ME/CFS panel carrying 41%; a footer correcting the 1.5% figure
- **Key Insight**: Insomnia is what he fears and it does not move until 120mg. Somnolence is what moves, and it lands on the fatigue he is trying to protect.
- **Why the quote block is there**: his own words about the kinds of exhaustion establish that somnolence may be **undetectable to him by feel**, because the detection channel is the one his ME/CFS saturates. That is what makes this reframe his rather than generic, and it is why the instrument advice is *count events, don't rate feelings*.
- **Corrections carried**: the 30→60 somnolence rise comes from adolescents and Korean adults and was **flat** in the fixed-dose trial, so the honest statement is "rises in two trials and did not in a third"; Zhou 2023's model-based curves **partly disagree** with the within-trial finding and the within-trial randomized comparison is the stronger design on that point; the folk "low dose sedating, high dose activating" is unsupported (insomnia and somnolence occur simultaneously in the same population); no morning-vs-evening trial exists and the "~60%" claim is unsourced

### Diagram 7: A 1.5× Step Flipped the Sensory Filter. You Are Contemplating 3×.
- **Type**: A two-rung ladder (10mg intact / 15mg disrupted) with the flip arrow, beside his proposed 20→60mg step, plus the five-study Copenhagen table
- **Elements**: green "P50 GATING: INTACT" rung above a red "P50 GATING: DISRUPTED" rung with "a 1.5× step" between them; his 20mg panel carrying his own verbatim report; a dashed 60mg panel with "Acute sensory cost: ?"; a full-width red correction bar retracting "transiently"
- **Key Insight**: The only human dose comparison that exists flipped the sensory filter at 1.5×. He is contemplating 3×. This is not a reason not to go up; it is a cost he is entitled to know is on the table.
- **Why it is on this page rather than in Cat 4**: it is the strongest mechanistic argument against 60mg in the whole project and it concerns the thing he most fears, so filing it in a mechanism chapter would be exactly the appendix-burial the binding brief §1 forbids.
- **Corrections carried**: **"transiently" is unsupported** — every study is a single acute dose and chronicity has never been measured; the cross-study move is flagged rather than smuggled; Mann 2008 is the **mirror image** (depletion breaks PPI, spares P50), which argues for an inverted-U rather than one dial and is stated on the diagram; single lab, no replication, escitalopram not duloxetine, n≈20 healthy men, and the P50-to-festival leap is unbridged

### Diagram 8: The Cliff Is Below You, Not Above You
- **Type**: Hyperbolic occupancy curve plotted from the fitted Emax model, with capsule/no-capsule markers, plus a six-row dose table
- **Elements**: the curve through 0/5/10/20/30/60/120mg; a red "NO CAPSULE EXISTS IN HERE" zone below 20mg; solid markers where a capsule exists and dashed hollow markers where one does not; a green ramp arrow (60→20, ~16 points) and a red cliff arrow (20→0, ~72 points); "YOU ARE HERE" at 20mg ≈ 72%
- **Key Insight**: Going up adds a well-provisioned ramp in front of a cliff whose height does not change. The taper argument against going up does not work, and the taper *question* is worth asking anyway.
- **Corrections carried**: everything below 20mg is **modelled, not measured** (the model was fitted to the 20 and 60mg points, so its agreement there is not independent confirmation); Takano's ED50 rests on n≈3/dose, single-dose, thalamus only; nobody has ever given a person 5mg of duloxetine in a PET scanner; the counterweight (more drug means more to remove) is stated; **alternate-day dosing is precisely wrong** at a ~12h half-life
- **Consistency**: matches the conclusion already reached in the Cat 11 discontinuation page, which this page cross-references rather than re-deriving

### Diagram 9: The Trap
- **Type**: 2×2 prediction matrix (two models × two doses) resolving into a YES/NO distinguishability row, plus his hypothesis graded in three claims
- **Elements**: green "AT 20mg · NOW" column against a dark red "AT 60mg" column; each model's prediction in each cell; a green YES and a red "NO. NOT EVER."; a full-width dark-red bar carrying the permanence; a green separating-observation panel
- **Key Insight**: At 20mg the models are distinguishable. At 60mg they are identical. If pain improves at 60mg he will never know which cause it was, because both will have been applied at once and there is no way to unmix them.
- **What it deliberately refuses to do**: recommend staying. A dedicated panel states that information is not the only thing worth having and that buying relief at the cost of an experiment is a rational trade.
- **Corrections carried**: his hypothesis's **claim 1 is established** (Canadian Consensus Criteria, verbatim, using his own word "crash"), **claim 2 has no evidence and is the load-bearing limb**, claim 3 is speculation; the **three places adjacent evidence leans against it** (Maloney's inverse allostatic correlation; the metabolic-vs-parasympathetic limb finding, where his model runs entirely through the limb that fails; Okifuji's null direct path); it survives by absence of research rather than by logic, and the one-result PubMed search establishes why

### Diagram 10: What Each Step Actually Buys, and What It Costs
- **Type**: Three-column ledger with a green BUYS band and a red COSTS band, plus a nine-axis comparison table
- **Elements**: 20→30 / 20→60 / 60→120 columns; "Nothing" in large type for the two steps that buy nothing; NNTB 11 with Cochrane's "no clinically relevant benefit" attached directly beneath it; the irreversibility flagged in each column
- **Key Insight**: Only one of the three steps buys anything, and it is also the one that closes the experiment.
- **The structural finding**: the 20→30mg step is the compromise most likely to be offered and it is **the worst trade on the board** — it pays the experiment's full price and buys a pain effect an adequately powered trial has already failed to find.
- **⚠ Self-correcting caveat carried on the diagram**: the 1.5×/3× proportional comparisons to the Copenhagen ladder are **dose ratios, not drive ratios**. Duloxetine's SERT occupancy is hyperbolic and already ~72% at 20mg, so 20→60mg is roughly a 16-occupancy-point step, emphatically not a tripling of serotonergic drive. Whether the P50 effect tracks dose or occupancy is unknown. The diagram uses the dose ratio because that is what the Copenhagen studies varied, and it says so.
- **Also carried**: the Abanades 8.5-fold individual EC50 spread, because every cost row here is a population average and the population curve describes no individual in it

### Diagram 11: The Arguments That Died, and How
- **Type**: The two-causes-of-death frame (heterogeneity vs logic) above four corpse panels, plus an eleven-row status table
- **Elements**: green "KILLED BY HETEROGENEITY → NOT DEAD" against red "KILLED BY LOGIC → DEAD"; four corpses each with a black banner naming what the claim used to be; an amber footer stating that the arguments pointing toward caution collapsed too
- **Key Insight**: Two of the dead arguments were this project's headline reasons to stay at 20mg. A caution built on an untested premise is still an untested premise, and it does not become sound by pointing in the safe direction.
- **Purpose**: enforces §0 (the heterogeneity/logic distinction that shortens the candidate list) and §6 (the DEAD list) as a visual artifact, on the page the binding brief identifies as having the most corpses
- **Corrections carried**: the blunting chain roots to a single 1990 case report via a qualitative study that never measured dose, and Aşçibaşi found blunting *fell* when doses were raised; "dead" means dead *as an argument*, not that the opposite is true, and blunting remains real, common (~46%) and reversible on dose reduction; **Goodwin 2017 found no difference by ANY agent**, which is not comforting and is carried anyway

## Deviations from the binding brief, deliberate and flagged

Two, both in the direction of the brief's own sources:

1. **"20mg ≈ 60mg on every sleep measure" (§4, and repeated in the Cat 9 brief) is overstated.** Supplement 5's own text says "20mg and 60mg are indistinguishable on **insomnia, somnolence, sleep disorder and discontinuation**" and separately identifies **fatigue (0% → 6.03%) as "the only clean monotonic rise across the 20→60 step."** Diagrams 5 and 10 carry the exception explicitly, because fatigue is the reader's primary disability and the exception lands precisely on the thing he is trying to protect. The brief's load-bearing claim (the inflection is at 120mg) is preserved.

2. **The Nicolini "−15.3/55% vs −14.7/45%" figures (§4, and repeated in the Cat 9 brief) should not be presented with the percentages.** Supplement 1 §2.5 states explicitly: *"Do not put these percentages in front of the reader as facts. Use the absolute changes and the response/remission rates; drop the percentages until someone reads the primary paper."* The percentages are internally inconsistent with the absolute changes (they imply baselines of ≈32.7 and ≈27.8 against a typical GAD enrolment of ≈24–26, and they disagree with each other). Diagram 4 and the explanation use the **absolute changes and the response/remission rates**, and name the inconsistency. This is the brief propagating a figure its own source told it to drop, which is exactly the failure mode §1 describes.

A third item is noted rather than deviated from: the brief's "**SOMNOLENCE does** [rise 20→60] (2.6%→10.2%, 0%→6.8%, 7.8%→14.3% at that step)" is accurate but the three figures are all **30→60** steps from two other trials. In the fixed-dose 20/60/120 trial, somnolence was **flat across 20→60** (5.08% → 4.31%). Diagrams 5, 6 and 10 state both, because "somnolence rises at 30→60 in two trials and was flat 20→60 in a third" is what the data says and it is still decision-relevant.

## Design Notes

- **Style**: matches the house pattern for this project (standalone light-mode HTML, `.container` card, `h1` + `.lead`, hand-authored SVG with `role="img"` + `<title>`/`<desc>`, `.caption` for the framing note, `.hedge` for the disconfirming note). - **Every diagram carries a `.hedge` box** naming what it does not settle, per §1 of the binding brief. On this page that is doubly binding, because this is the page attached to a decision with a date on it.
- **Provenance tags are inline** in panel text and table cells (`primary, verified` / `secondary — unverified` / `reasoning, not evidence` / `no evidence exists`), never collected at the bottom.
- **No diagram directs a dose.** Diagrams 1, 9 and 10 each carry an explicit statement that the weighing is the reader's, per §10. Diagram 9 carries a dedicated panel refusing to recommend staying, because that is the recommendation the trap material would most easily smuggle.
- **The trade is presented as two-sided throughout**, per §2 and the reader's explicit correction: *"the pain and fibromyalgia is important to me. It's just that the psychological effects were unexpected and something I also value."* No diagram treats the pain benefit as expendable.
