Two Effects. Two Dose Curves. One Appointment.

The dose conversation will proceed as though there is one dial with one answer. There are two curves, they have different shapes, and from 20mg they point in different directions. This is the whole decision.

(you)		 Measurably absent. Goldstein's 20mg arm (n≈114) failed against an equal-sized 60mg arm that worked. Beats placebo. Nicolini's fixed 20mg arm (n=84): HAM-A total and psychic factor positive. Somatic factor missed at p=0.07. primary, verified
30mg Still absent. Arnold 2012, n=155/153, adequately powered, pain P=0.202. 30mg does not rescue it. Never tested as a fixed dose against 20 or 60. primary, verified
40mg Appears. The lowest dose with a positive pain trial. Not tested. secondary — unverified
60mg Works, modestly. Multiple positive trials across two indications. NNTB 11 (9–14), fibromyalgia-specific. Beats placebo. Never compared to 20mg. primary, verified
120mg No better than 60mg. Cochrane and Lilly's own pooled analysis agree. No better than 60mg. Kornstein: 30.0% vs 30.5% in non-remitters randomized to stay or double. primary, verified
Cochrane's own authors, in their formal conclusion, verbatim: "There is adequate amounts of moderate quality evidence from eight studies performed by the manufacturers of duloxetine that doses of 60 mg and 120 mg daily are efficacious for treating pain in diabetic peripheral neuropathy but lower daily doses are not." No pass of this project quoted that sentence for seven passes, while arguing about whether the dose curve was real.
What this diagram does not settle. The exact location of the step is not known: 30mg fails, 40mg separates in one trial, so it is somewhere in between, and "40 to 60mg" is a summary rather than a measurement reasoning, not evidence. The plateau height is contested: Cochrane's fibromyalgia-specific review says NNTB 11 and "no clinically relevant benefit," while Sultan 2008, the one genuinely independent analysis, concluded "the dose of duloxetine made little difference to the result" — though that statement pools all doses including 120mg and rests on 66 patients at 20mg primary, verified. The right-hand curve is built from depression trials (Kornstein, Dold) plus one anxiety trial (Nicolini), and nobody has ever studied duloxetine's dose-response for the specific thing you report: the decoupling of acute overwhelm from the crash that follows it no evidence exists.
