An honesty note about the summary sentence. "20mg is the same as 60mg on every sleep measure" is not quite right, and the exception matters. It is true for insomnia, somnolence, sleep disorder and discontinuation. Fatigue is the exception: 0% to 6.03% across the step. Your primary disability is fatigue and your current valued gain is waking less tired, so the one row that moves is the one that lands where you would least want it. This diagram states that rather than rounding it away.
What this diagram does not settle, starting with the thing that looks most like a finding. The same trial measured the Athens Insomnia Scale and 60mg beat placebo (p=0.045) while 120mg did not, and 60mg beat 120mg (p=0.012). Do not use it. These are secondary endpoints in a trial that failed its primary (no dose beat placebo on 24-hour average pain); there is no multiplicity correction across six pairwise comparisons, so those p-values would very likely not survive one; the 20mg arm is n=59, half the others; the 120mg arm lost 27 of 112 to adverse events, so its completers are enriched for people who tolerated it, which flatters 120mg and means the real 120mg harm is plausibly worse than shown; and it does not replicate — two other trials measured the same instrument at the same dose and returned p=0.329 and p=0.590 primary, verified. Also: rough Wilson 95% intervals from these counts put insomnia at 20mg ≈ 4.8–20.5% and at 60mg ≈ 4.8–15.1% reasoning, not evidence — my calculation. Those intervals overlap almost entirely, and the 120mg interval (12.6–26.9%) barely clears 60mg's, so even the 120mg spike is suggestive rather than decisive within this single trial. Population is chronic low back pain, not fibromyalgia and not ME/CFS. Lilly-sponsored. And abnormal dreams and restless legs do not appear in this trial's AE table at all, but with n=112 a single case is 0.89% and would fall below the reporting threshold, so that is weak evidence of absence and must not be reported as "duloxetine caused no RLS in 404 patients" no evidence exists.
