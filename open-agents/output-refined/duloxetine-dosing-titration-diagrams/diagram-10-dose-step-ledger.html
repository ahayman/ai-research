The asymmetry worth noticing, because it is the only structural finding in this ledger. The 20→30mg step is the one that gets offered as a compromise, on the intuition that it splits the difference. It does not split anything. It pays the experiment's full price, incurs a proportional serotonergic step the same size as the one that flipped the sensory filter, and buys a pain effect that a properly powered trial has already looked for and failed to find. Whatever the right answer is, "30mg as a compromise" is the one option the evidence positively argues against, and it is also the one most likely to be proposed.
What this ledger does not settle. The 1.5× and 3× comparisons to the Copenhagen ladder are proportional analogies across different drugs with different occupancy curves reasoning, not evidence. Duloxetine's SERT occupancy is hyperbolic and already near-saturated at 20mg (~72%), so a 3× dose step is emphatically not a 3× step in serotonergic drive: 60mg is roughly 88%, so the occupancy step is about 16 points, not a tripling. The dose ratio and the drive ratio are different quantities and this diagram uses the dose ratio because that is what the Copenhagen studies varied. Whether the P50 effect tracks dose or occupancy is unknown, and nobody has measured either for duloxetine no evidence exists. Also: the pain figure of NNTB 11 is disputed by the one genuinely independent analysis in this literature (Sultan 2008: "the dose of duloxetine made little difference to the result"), and that disagreement is real rather than resolvable primary, verified. Finally, every "cost" row here is a population average, and the individual EC50 spread across ten people fitted individually was 8.5-fold — same drug, same dose, same protocol primary, verified. The population curve does not describe any individual in it, including you.
