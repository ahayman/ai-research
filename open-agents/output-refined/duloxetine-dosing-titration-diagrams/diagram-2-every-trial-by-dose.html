Every Pain Trial, Plotted by Dose and Outcome

Low-dose duloxetine has not been neglected for pain. It has been tested, three times, in two conditions, in about 350 patients, and it failed on the pain primary every time. Here is the whole literature on one axis.

Chappell 2008
PMID 20428412		 60→120mg Responder-enriched blind escalation; "supportive," not pivotal ✗ P=0.053 "60mg failed both co-primaries" is FALSE. 98 of 117 (83.8%) were blindly escalated to 120mg at week 13; mean dose among them 113.4mg. Its design is a dose escalation, so it cannot be evidence against dose-response.

Cochrane says three different things about dose and they must not be merged. (1) "no significant difference nor a dose effect in the RR of improvement with increasing doses of duloxetine from 40 mg to 120 mg" — flat above 40mg, and note the range starts at 40mg, so it says nothing about your dose. (2) "no dose effect exists from 60 mg to 120 mg" — flat above 60mg. (3) "doses of 60 mg and 120 mg daily are efficacious… but lower daily doses are not" — a threshold below 60mg. Statement (1) is the one usually quoted to argue that dose does not matter. It begins at 40mg. It is not about 20mg.
What this diagram does not settle. Every trial here except one is manufacturer-sponsored primary, verified. The one genuinely independent analysis, Sultan 2008 (funded by the Oxford Pain Research Trust, no author stock holdings), concluded that "the dose of duloxetine made little difference to the result" — which cuts directly against this diagram primary, verified. Weigh it honestly: that statement pools all doses including 120mg, and rests on only 66 patients at 20mg. Cochrane's contrary statement is a formal conclusion drawn from eight studies. Two independent syntheses disagree, and the disagreement is real. Also note the census correction: ~193 patients were randomized to 20mg in duloxetine pain trials (Goldstein ≈114 + Russell 79, both verified per-arm), not the "66" that circulates primary, verified. Sultan's "66" sits inside a ≥50%-pain-relief subsection and its meaning is unresolved; do not try to explain it, because the refutation does not depend on the explanation.
