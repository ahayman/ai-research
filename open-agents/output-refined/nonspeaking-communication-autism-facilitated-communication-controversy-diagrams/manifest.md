## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: FC Timeline
- **Type**: Timeline (Mermaid timeline)
- **Elements**: 4 sections, 20+ events spanning 1977-2024
- **Key Insight**: The FC controversy spans nearly five decades, with scientific consensus established by the mid-1990s but the practice persisting through rebranding and media amplification
- **Accessibility**: Four-section timeline. Origins (1977-1990): Crossley develops FC, Biklen imports to US. Scientific Response (1991-1995): controlled studies, false allegations, PBS documentary, ASHA position. Continued Evidence (2001-2014): Mostert reviews, Boynton confession, Schlosser ISAAC review. Modern Era (2018-2024): ASHA update, AAIDD position, Spellers documentary, Telepathy Tapes podcast.

### Diagram 2: The Ideomotor Effect
- **Type**: Flowchart (circular feedback loop)
- **Elements**: 7 sequential nodes forming a cycle, Ouija board parallel subgraph, key insight note
- **Key Insight**: The ideomotor effect is unconscious -- facilitators are sincere in their belief that messages originate from the individual; this is the same mechanism that produces Ouija board movements
- **Accessibility**: Seven-step cycle: facilitator expectation, unconscious motor activation, subtle physical guidance, directed pointing, message appears, attribution error, social reinforcement (loops back to expectation). Parallel: Ouija board experiments show gibberish when visual feedback removed.

### Diagram 3: Controlled Study Design
- **Type**: Flowchart (experimental design)
- **Elements**: 2 condition subgraphs, results node, critique and response nodes
- **Key Insight**: Across 50+ studies, correct responses appear only when the facilitator has the information; under blinding, rates drop to 0-1.8% (chance)
- **Accessibility**: Two conditions. Condition A: facilitator knows answer, FC produces correct response. Condition B: facilitator blinded, FC produces facilitator's information or random. Pattern consistent across 50+ studies. Proponent critique: conditions artificial. Critic response: any genuine authorship should produce some correct responses under blinding.

### Diagram 4: The False Allegations Pipeline
- **Type**: Flowchart (consequence chain)
- **Elements**: Sequential flow with critical branch point, 2 case study subgraphs
- **Key Insight**: FC-produced abuse allegations led to approximately 60 documented cases of family separation and criminal charges by 1995; in every case subjected to controlled testing, facilitator authorship was demonstrated
- **Accessibility**: Flow from facilitator training through FC sessions, abuse allegations, authorities report, family separation, to branch point: testing conducted (leads to charges dismissed) or not (leads to prosecution risk). Boynton case: confessional published. Wendrow case: 80 days jailed, $3.75M settlement.

### Diagram 5: Both Sides of the Debate
- **Type**: Flowchart (two-column comparison)
- **Elements**: 6 arguments per side, central reconciliation assessment
- **Key Insight**: The empirical evidence weighs heavily on the scientific consensus side; the ethical arguments from proponents are not trivially dismissible but do not override the need for controlled evidence
- **Accessibility**: Left column (scientific consensus): 50+ controlled studies, 5 systematic reviews, false allegations, professional unanimity, ideomotor explanation, evidence-based AAC support. Right column (proponent): presuming competence, least dangerous assumption, ecological validity critique, individual cases, disability rights, absence of evidence argument. Center: perspectives currently irreconcilable.
