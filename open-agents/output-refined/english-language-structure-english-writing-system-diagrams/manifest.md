# English Writing System Diagrams

## Diagram Manifest

### diagram-1-four-layers-spelling-irregularity.html
- **Title**: The Four Layers of English Spelling Irregularity
- **Type**: Stratified geological diagram (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Shows the four historical layers that created English spelling irregularity -- the Great Vowel Shift freeze, French scribal conventions, Renaissance etymological respelling, and foreign borrowing preservation -- as geological strata, with example words for each layer showing the specific historical cause of their spelling opacity.
- **Key Insight**: English spelling irregularity is not random; it is stratified sediment from identifiable historical events.

### diagram-2-morphophonemic-regularity.html
- **Title**: Morphophonemic Regularity: The Hidden Logic
- **Type**: Paired-word comparison diagram (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Displays morpheme families (sign/signal, bomb/bombard, damn/damnation, muscle/muscular, hymn/hymnal) where silent letters in base forms come alive in derived forms, plus vowel-shift pairs (nation/national, serene/serenity, photograph/photography) where spelling holds constant across pronunciation changes. Color-coded to distinguish silent consonants, alive consonants, and vowel shifts.
- **Key Insight**: Chomsky & Halle's (1968) argument that English spelling preserves morpheme identity at a deeper level than surface pronunciation.

### diagram-3-twenty-six-to-forty-four-mapping.html
- **Title**: The 26-to-44 Mapping Problem
- **Type**: Multi-section reference diagram (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Visualizes the fundamental mismatch between 26 letters and ~44 phonemes, showing one-sound-many-spellings (e.g., /iː/ has 7+ spellings), one-spelling-many-sounds (e.g., letter "a" has 5+ sounds), the notorious "ough" six-way pronunciation split, the four bridging strategies (digraphs, magic-e, consonant doubling, positional rules), and actual predictability statistics (consonants ~93%, vowels ~55%, overall ~75-80%, with morphology ~85-90%).
- **Key Insight**: The system is not chaos -- it is a 26-slot alphabet doing 44 phonemes' worth of work through systematic (if complex) strategies.

### diagram-4-spelling-reform-timeline.html
- **Title**: Spelling Reform Timeline: A History of Failure
- **Type**: Vertical timeline (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Chronicles every major spelling reform attempt from Franklin (1768) through modern proposals, showing proposed changes, backers, sample reformed spellings, and outcomes. Includes the five structural reasons reform always fails (dialect problem, morphophonemic loss, transition cost, social symbolism, coordination problem). Webster's partial success highlighted as the sole exception.
- **Key Insight**: The recurring pattern -- proposal, enthusiasm, failure -- reveals that the obstacles are structural, not accidental.

### diagram-5-dual-route-reading-model.html
- **Title**: Dual-Route Reading Model for English
- **Type**: Flowchart with evidence panel (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Shows the two parallel routes English readers must maintain: sublexical (grapheme-phoneme conversion for regular/novel words) and lexical (whole-word recognition for familiar/irregular words). Includes the double dissociation evidence (surface vs. phonological dyslexia) and cross-linguistic acquisition data (Seymour et al. 2003) showing English children at ~34% accuracy vs. ~95% in transparent orthographies.
- **Key Insight**: English's deep orthography forces readers to build and maintain two independent processing systems -- this is the measurable cognitive cost of the writing system.
