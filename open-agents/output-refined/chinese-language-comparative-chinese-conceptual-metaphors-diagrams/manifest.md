# Chinese Conceptual Metaphors -- Diagram Manifest

## Diagram Inventory

### 1. The Dual-Axis Time Model -- Chinese vs. English
- **File**: `diagram-1-dual-axis-time-model.html`
- **Type**: SVG spatial comparison diagram
- **Content**: Side-by-side comparison showing English uses only horizontal time axis (front/back) while Chinese uses both horizontal (primary, ~64%) and vertical (additional, ~36%). Includes Boroditsky (2001) replication controversy callout and corpus frequency data from Chen (2007).
- **Research Section**: Section 1 (Vertical Time: The Boroditsky Hypothesis)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, color-coded axes, controversy annotation

### 2. The Heart-Mind Unification -- Chinese 心 vs. English Head/Heart Split
- **File**: `diagram-2-heart-mind-unification.html`
- **Type**: SVG conceptual comparison diagram
- **Content**: Chinese side shows single unified circle containing both cognitive characters (想, 思, 忆, 悟) and emotional characters (情, 怒, 恐, 悲, 愁, 恨) sharing the heart radical. English side shows two separated circles (Mind/Head vs. Heart). Cross-linguistic note on Arabic qalb parallel.
- **Research Section**: Section 2 (The Heart-Mind Unification: 心 Xin)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, cognition/emotion color coding, radical evidence

### 3. The Body-Organ Emotion Map -- Chinese vs. English
- **File**: `diagram-3-body-organ-emotion-map.html`
- **Type**: SVG body diagram with organ annotations and comparison table
- **Content**: Simplified body outline with seven labeled organs mapped to emotions (heart/joy, liver/anger, spleen/worry, lungs/grief, kidneys/fear, gallbladder/courage, intestines/deep feeling). TCM five-element color coding. English comparison showing near-exclusive heart concentration. Granularity comparison table.
- **Research Section**: Section 3 (Body-Based Metaphors: The Internal Organ System)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, TCM element colors, organ-to-emotion mapping

### 4. Compound Ideograph Gallery -- Characters That Visually Encode Metaphor
- **File**: `diagram-4-compound-ideograph-gallery.html`
- **Type**: HTML card grid with visual decomposition
- **Content**: Nine compound ideographs decomposed into components with metaphorical logic: 明 (sun+moon=bright), 休 (person+tree=rest), 好 (woman+child=good), 信 (person+speech=trust), 森 (tree x3=forest), 安 (roof+woman=peace), 家 (roof+pig=home), 从 (person+person=follow), 炎 (fire+fire=scorching). Folk etymology caveat. English comparison box.
- **Research Section**: Section 4 (Characters That Visually Encode Metaphor)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, component color coding, etymology caveat

### 5. The Universal-to-Culture-Specific Metaphor Spectrum
- **File**: `diagram-5-metaphor-universality-spectrum.html`
- **Type**: SVG horizontal spectrum with positioned metaphors and comparison table
- **Content**: Gradient spectrum from Universal (ANGER IS HEAT, HAPPINESS IS UP) through Widespread (TIME IS SPACE with axis variation) to Culture-Specific (GALLBLADDER IS COURAGE, SPLEEN IS TEMPERAMENT, HEART-MIND UNITY). Source motivation axis (body vs. cultural model). Detailed Chinese-English comparison table for computer, train, happy, sad, angry, worried, brave.
- **Research Section**: Section 5 (Universal vs. Culture-Specific Metaphors)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, three-zone spectrum, embodiment source annotations

### 6. Three Levels of Metaphorical Encoding -- Unique to Chinese
- **File**: `diagram-6-three-levels-metaphorical-encoding.html`
- **Type**: SVG layered architecture diagram
- **Content**: Three stacked layers for Chinese (graphemic, lexical, cognitive) vs. two for English (lexical and cognitive only, with graphemic marked as "structurally impossible"). Example threading through all three levels with 想 (xin radical visible in character). English column shows the absent graphemic layer.
- **Research Section**: Section 6 (The Interaction of Metaphor, Writing, and Cognition)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, layer hierarchy, missing-layer notation

### 7. Heart (心) Compound Word Families -- Metaphorical Productivity
- **File**: `diagram-7-heart-compound-families.html`
- **Type**: SVG radial/cluster diagram
- **Content**: Center 心 radiating to eight compound words grouped by valence: positive (开心 open=happy, 放心 release=relieved, 热心 hot=enthusiastic), negative (伤心 wound=sad, 担心 carry=worried, 恶心 bad=disgusted), cognitive (小心 small=careful, 用心 use=diligent, 心眼 heart-eye=shrewd). English comparison showing limited heart productivity.
- **Research Section**: Section 5 (Metaphorical Compound Words)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, valence color coding, pattern insight box
