The Polyvagal-Informed Therapy Landscape

Five major clinical applications of polyvagal vocabulary, colour-coded by how deeply each depends on PVT being biologically correct.

Polyvagal Theory (Porges 1994)

Three-state autonomic hierarchy · ventral / sympathetic / dorsal · the source of the vocabulary used by all modalities below

Rhythm of Regulation (Dana)

Canonical clinical application

Founded: 2018 onwards (Norton trilogy: 2018, 2020, 2021).

What it is: The translation of PVT into a teachable clinical curriculum (befriend, map, navigate, shape). The polyvagal ladder, glimmers, autonomic mapping.

PVT-dependence: Maximum vocabulary use. Practices (paced breathing, body awareness, co-regulation) work for other reasons; the ladder is psychological scaffolding.

Polyvagal-Informed EMDR (Kase)

Vocabulary added to EMDR protocol

Founded: 2023 (Norton).

What it is: Adds polyvagal orientation to the eight-phase EMDR Standard Protocol without modifying the protocol itself. Pacing, presence, autonomic mapping.

PVT-dependence: Low. EMDR's existing evidence base (Shapiro 1989 onwards) does the therapeutic heavy lifting. Polyvagal layer is case-conceptualisation framing.

Somatic Experiencing (Levine)

Independent origin; vocabulary integrated

Founded: 1970s (Levine's writings); Levine-Porges first meeting 1978.

What it is: Body-tracking, titration, pendulation, discharge. Brom 2017 RCT (n=63): d=0.94-1.26 for PTSD symptoms.

PVT-dependence: Low. Core techniques predate PVT. SE's evidence base would survive PVT being wrong.

Sensorimotor Psychotherapy (Ogden)

Independent origin; vocabulary integrated

Founded: 1970s onwards; Hakomi Institute co-founded 1981; SP Institute formalised 2000s; Trauma and the Body 2006.

What it is: Mindfulness, body-tracking, phase-oriented trauma treatment. Hakomi lineage.

PVT-dependence: Low. The somatic-therapy tradition predates PVT by decades. PVT vocabulary was added to an already-complete framework.

Internal Family Systems (Schwartz)

Independent origin; vocabulary cross-mapped

Founded: 1980s onwards (Schwartz, eating-disorder work, Institute for Juvenile Research, Chicago).

What it is: Parts-based therapy. Self-led therapeutic stance. Eight C's (calm, curiosity, compassion, clarity, confidence, courage, creativity, connectedness).

PVT-dependence: Low. The IFS-PVT mapping (Self ↔ ventral vagal) is vocabulary alignment, not mechanism. See the IFS deep dive elsewhere in this project for the full evaluation.

SSP / "Activate-the-Vagal-Complex" Claims

Mechanism-load-bearing

Founded: Listening Project Protocol 2014; SSP launched 2016 (Unyte).

What it is: Filtered music intervention. Marketed on specific mechanism: acoustic filter recruits middle-ear muscles → activates ventral vagal complex → social engagement system.

PVT-dependence: HIGH. The mechanism claim is load-bearing. If PVT's specific anatomical predictions don't hold (see Category 3), the mechanism story falls. See Category 6 for evidence audit.

How to read this map
Vocabulary-only: The modality has an independent origin and an evidence base that doesn't depend on PVT being biologically correct. Polyvagal language is layered on top of an already-complete framework. This is the dominant pattern.
Vocabulary-augmented: The modality has its own evidence base; polyvagal vocabulary is added as orientation without modifying the underlying protocol.
Mechanism-dependent: The intervention rests its specific claims on the polyvagal mechanism being correct. If the mechanism story is contested (see Cat 3), the claim is at risk.
