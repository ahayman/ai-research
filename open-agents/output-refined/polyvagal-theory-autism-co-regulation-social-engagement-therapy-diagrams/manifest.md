## Diagram Descriptions

### Diagram 1: The Polyvagal-Informed Therapy Landscape
- **Type**: Custom landscape/hub map
- **Elements**: Central PVT node; six modality cards (Dana, Kase EMDR, SE, Sensorimotor, IFS, SSP/mechanism claims) colour-coded by dependence
- **Key Insight**: Most clinical applications use PVT vocabulary on top of independently-grounded practices; only mechanism-load-bearing claims (SSP, "activate ventral vagal" marketing) genuinely depend on PVT.
- **Accessibility**: Cards organised in clear grid with colour-coding plus textual labels; legend explains the three categories.

### Diagram 2: Dana's Rhythm of Regulation Framework
- **Type**: Hybrid (ladder visual + four-verb curriculum)
- **Elements**: Three-rung polyvagal ladder (ventral/sympathetic/dorsal) + four numbered cards (Befriend/Map/Navigate/Shape) + session arc + book pills (2018, 2020, 2021)
- **Key Insight**: The canonical clinical operationalisation of PVT; structured curriculum with named tools and a clear session structure.
- **Accessibility**: Ladder uses both colour and text labels; verbs are numbered for sequential reading.

### Diagram 3: Which Therapy Practices Actually Require PVT? (ANCHOR)
- **Type**: Two-column comparison
- **Elements**: 8 practices in left column (works regardless of PVT) with founding-source attribution; 4 claims in right column (specifically requires PVT) with mechanism-load-bearing flags
- **Key Insight**: The chapter's central analytical contribution. Most therapy practices wrapped in polyvagal language have independent evidence bases.
- **Accessibility**: Strong colour contrast (green/red); textual labels; each practice has founder/year attribution.

### Diagram 4: Co-Regulation — Attachment Theory Roots vs Polyvagal Language
- **Type**: Timeline (1958-2018)
- **Elements**: 9 timeline events colour-coded by lineage (attachment-theory in blue, PVT in amber/orange)
- **Key Insight**: Co-regulation has 25+ years of theoretical and empirical development before PVT entered the conversation.
- **Accessibility**: Linear chronological layout; colour-coded with text legend; each event has explicit "pre-PVT" or "PVT" tag.

### Diagram 5: Polyvagal-Informed EMDR — What's Added to Standard EMDR
- **Type**: Side-by-side comparison (8 phases)
- **Elements**: Eight phase rows showing standard EMDR (left column, blue) and Kase's polyvagal layer (right column, amber)
- **Key Insight**: The protocol mechanism is unchanged; the polyvagal layer is consistently additive (vocabulary + pacing).
- **Accessibility**: Numbered phases for clear sequential reading; colour-coded columns with explicit headers.

### Diagram 6: SE & Sensorimotor Independent Evidence Bases
- **Type**: Dual timeline (SE on left, SP on right)
- **Elements**: Parallel timelines showing each modality's development from 1970s to 2017; Brom 2017 RCT callout for SE
- **Key Insight**: Both modalities developed substantively complete frameworks before integrating PVT vocabulary.
- **Accessibility**: Two columns with consistent structure; meet-with-Porges events highlighted with arrows.

### Diagram 7: Low Resting RSA as Transdiagnostic Biomarker
- **Type**: Hub-and-spoke (radial)
- **Elements**: Central "Low Resting RSA" hub + 7 condition-specific spoke cards with effect sizes/study details
- **Key Insight**: RSA's transdiagnostic nature undermines PVT-specificity claims; the marker is non-specific to any one condition.
- **Accessibility**: Grid layout adapts for small screens; each spoke has both effect-size data and qualitative description.

### Diagram 8: Treatment-Selection Map for Autistic Adults
- **Type**: Decision matrix (4 presentations × multiple options each)
- **Elements**: 4 presentation rows (anxiety, depression, PTSD, autism core features) with treatment options coded by category
- **Key Insight**: Polyvagal-informed therapy fits as adjunct/wraparound, not primary treatment; gold-standard treatments take priority for specific conditions.
- **Accessibility**: Strong colour-coding with category legend; consistent row structure; mobile-responsive.

### Diagram 9: Scaffolding vs Mechanism: The Conceptual Distinction
- **Type**: Two-column conceptual diagram
- **Elements**: Left column "Scaffolding" with 5 uses + recommendation; right column "Mechanism" with 5 claims + recommendation
- **Key Insight**: The single most useful analytical distinction the chapter offers. Polyvagal language can be both clinically helpful (as scaffolding) and scientifically risky (as mechanism claim).
- **Accessibility**: Mirror-image structure; colour contrast green/red; explicit "what to do" guidance for each mode.
