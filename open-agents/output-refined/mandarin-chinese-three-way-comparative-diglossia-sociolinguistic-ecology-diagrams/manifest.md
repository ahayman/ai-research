# Diglossia & Sociolinguistic Ecology -- Diagram Manifest

## Diagram Inventory

### 1. Ferguson's Diglossia Model Applied to China
- **File**: `diagram-1-fergusons-diglossia-china.html`
- **Type**: Side-by-side model comparison with historical timeline
- **Content**: Ferguson's (1959) classic H/L diglossia model alongside Tsou's (1980) triglossic expansion for China (Supreme/High/Low). Shows functional domains for each tier. Historical timeline tracking the shift from literary/vernacular diglossia to national/regional diglossia. Insight box on the monoglossic trajectory.
- **Research Section**: Section 2 (Ferguson's Diglossia Model)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, color-coded tiers (S=red, H=amber, L=indigo), four-phase timeline

### 2. Chinese Language Family Tree
- **File**: `diagram-2-chinese-language-family-tree.html`
- **Type**: Horizontal bar chart with speaker data
- **Content**: All ten major Chinese language groups with percentage of speakers, approximate absolute numbers, and geographic regions. Mandarin highlighted as the dominant group (65.7%). Summary statistics: 10 groups, ~300 varieties, 0% cross-group mutual intelligibility. Note comparing Mandarin-Cantonese unintelligibility to Portuguese-Romanian.
- **Research Section**: Section 3 (The Chinese Language Family)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, proportional bars, Mandarin highlighted row

### 3. Code-Switching Triggers Map
- **File**: `diagram-3-code-switching-triggers.html`
- **Type**: Multi-section trigger analysis with spectrum bars
- **Content**: Three trigger categories: (1) Domain-based switching showing Putonghua/Mixed/Dialect balance across six domains (education, government, workplace, marketplace, social media, family) using stacked spectrum bars. (2) Interlocutor-based triggers: four cards showing how different conversation partners trigger different language choices. (3) Identity signals: what Putonghua vs. dialect choice communicates socially.
- **Research Section**: Section 5 (Code-Switching)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, stacked spectrum bars, trigger cards, identity signal tags

### 4. Regional Mandarin Continuum
- **File**: `diagram-4-regional-mandarin-continuum.html`
- **Type**: Ranked list with intelligibility meters
- **Content**: Eight Mandarin sub-groups ranked by mutual intelligibility with Beijing standard. Each row shows name, Chinese characters, speaker count, intelligibility bar (green/amber/red), and key distinguishing features. Beijing Mandarin flagged as basis for Putonghua. Insight on Southwestern Mandarin's 260M speakers.
- **Research Section**: Section 4 (Regional Mandarin)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, color-coded intelligibility bars, Beijing highlight badge

### 5. Dialect Endangerment Funnel
- **File**: `diagram-5-dialect-endangerment-funnel.html`
- **Type**: Progressive funnel with case studies
- **Content**: Six-stage funnel showing progressive domain contraction (full vitality → education lost → media lost → workplace shifts → intergenerational break → passive knowledge only). Three case study cards: Shanghainese (critical: 26% under-18 fluency), Cantonese (under pressure: protests), Hokkien/Taiwan (reviving: 2019 national language status). Pressure factors grid (6 items) and counter-currents grid (4 items).
- **Research Section**: Section 6 (Dialect Endangerment)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, gradient funnel, status-coded case cards, factor grids

### 6. Three-Way Sociolinguistic Comparison
- **File**: `diagram-6-three-way-sociolinguistic-comparison.html`
- **Type**: Three-column comparison matrix with analogy
- **Content**: Eleven dimensions comparing Mainland China's diglossia, English global varieties, and Taiwan's trilingualism: ecology type, standard variety, internal diversity, mutual intelligibility, policy direction, legal framework, education policy, endangerment risk, political symbolism, writing system role, code-switching. Romance-language analogy box explaining why Chinese internal diversity is fundamentally different from English variety diversity.
- **Research Section**: Sections 7-8 (Taiwan, English Comparison)
- **Features**: Dark/light mode, color-coded columns (China=red, English=blue, Taiwan=green), highlighted key rows, analogy comparison box
