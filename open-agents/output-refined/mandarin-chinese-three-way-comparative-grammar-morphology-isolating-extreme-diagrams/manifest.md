# Diagram Manifest: Grammar & Morphology — The Isolating Extreme

## Parent Topic
Contemporary Mandarin Chinese — Three-Way Structural Comparison

## Category
Grammar & Morphology: The Isolating Extreme

## Diagrams

### Diagram 1: Morphological Typology Spectrum
- **File**: `diagram-1-morphological-typology-spectrum.html`
- **Type**: Spectrum/positioning diagram (SVG)
- **Description**: Horizontal spectrum from isolating → agglutinating → fusional → polysynthetic, with Mandarin, English, Turkish, Latin, and Mohawk positioned. Includes Greenberg synthesis index values and example boxes showing "I didn't eat" across types. Shows Mandarin and Taiwan Mandarin at the same extreme isolating position.

### Diagram 2: Aspect Markers vs. Tense System
- **File**: `diagram-2-aspect-vs-tense-system.html`
- **Type**: Side-by-side comparison (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Mandarin's 3-4 optional aspect particles (了/着/过/在) compared against English's 12 obligatory tense-aspect forms displayed in a 3x4 grid. Highlights that 了 is NOT past tense. Includes Taiwan Mandarin's 有 (you) as perfective marker.

### Diagram 3: 把 ba and 被 bei Constructions
- **File**: `diagram-3-ba-bei-constructions.html`
- **Type**: Slot diagrams with examples (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Architectural slot diagrams showing how 把 (disposal/active) and 被 (passive) rearrange word order. Shows the standard SVO → 把 SOV and 被 patient-fronting transformations. Includes semantic constraints, mirror relationship, and English passive comparison.

### Diagram 4: Three De Particles
- **File**: `diagram-4-three-de-particles.html`
- **Type**: Decision flow + triple card comparison (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Decision flow showing which de to use based on what's being modified, followed by three detailed cards for 的 (attributive/noun), 地 (adverbial/verb), and 得 (complement/degree). Includes the 地 vs 得 distinction and English equivalents.

### Diagram 5: Serial Verb Constructions & Resultative Compounds
- **File**: `diagram-5-serial-verb-resultative-compounds.html`
- **Type**: Visual sentence diagrams + compound gallery (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: Part A shows four types of serial verb constructions (purpose, instrumental, directional, consecutive) with color-coded verb chains. Part B shows a gallery of 8 resultative V-V compounds with action-result decomposition, plus the potential form (得/不 insertion).

### Diagram 6: Missing Grammar Comparison
- **File**: `diagram-6-missing-grammar-comparison.html`
- **Type**: Feature comparison table (HTML/CSS)
- **Description**: 12-row table comparing grammatical features present in English but absent from Mandarin (verb conjugation, tense, agreement, plurals, articles, gender, case, comparatives, passive morphology, infinitives, relative pronouns, copula). Each row shows English form, Mandarin status, and what Mandarin uses instead.
