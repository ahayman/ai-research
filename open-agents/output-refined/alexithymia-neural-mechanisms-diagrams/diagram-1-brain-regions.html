The Neuroanatomical Inventory in Alexithymia

Regions implicated in alexithymia, their network role, and replication strength. The anterior insula is the most replicated single locus; the supragenual ACC is the second. Other regions are involved but evidence is mixed-to-suggestive rather than well-replicated.

Region Network role Finding in alexithymia Replication Key citation
Anterior insula (AI) Salience network; interoceptive integration Reduced grey matter; reduced activation during emotional stimuli; lesion >15% AI volume produces acquired alexithymia Well-replicated van der Velde 2013; Xu 2018; Hogeveen 2016
Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) — supragenual Salience network; appraisal & conflict-monitoring Hyperactivation during emotional stimuli (interpreted as compensatory); reduced GABA in some studies Replicated van der Velde 2013
Amygdala Affective salience & threat detection Reduced activation to emotional faces in some studies; null or increased in others; reduced amygdala–ACC coupling (Pohl 2018) Mixed Reker 2010; Kano 2003; Pohl 2018
Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) Valuation; somatic-marker integration Reduced activation during reward/decision tasks; reduced grey matter in VBM Suggestive Bagby 2010; selective IGT vs GDT impairment (Zhang 2017)
Medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) Default-mode network; self-referential & conceptual processing Altered self-evaluation activation; consistent with Barrett constructed-emotion frame Suggestive Barrett 2017
Lateral PFC (left) Verbal/symbolic system; affect-labelling pathway Implicated in the language route of the multiroute model; affect-labelling reduces amygdala reactivity via RVLPFC→mPFC→amygdala Indirect Lieberman 2007; Hobson 2019
Legend. Well-replicated = multiple meta-analyses or independent confirmations across populations and methods. Replicated = several studies, generally consistent. Mixed = significant inconsistency across studies. Suggestive = small literature, plausible but not definitive. Indirect = mechanism inferred from related findings rather than directly tested in alexithymia. Treat the AI as the strongest single causal anchor (Hogeveen 2016 lesion study) and treat the rest as parts of a multi-system mechanism rather than as separate well-established findings.
