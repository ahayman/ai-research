The Garfinkel Three-Dimensional Interoception Model

Garfinkel, Seth, Barrett, Suzuki & Critchley (2015). "Knowing your own heart: Distinguishing interoceptive accuracy from interoceptive awareness." Biological Psychology 104:65–74. The architectural foundation for separating detection from labelling failures.

Interoceptive ACCURACY

IAcc
Objective behavioural performance on a task — e.g., counting heartbeats accurately during a fixed interval. The signal-detection layer.
DETECTION LAYER

Interoceptive SENSIBILITY

ISen
Self-reported tendency to attend to body signals. Captured by questionnaires like MAIA. The attentional disposition layer.
ATTENTION LAYER

Interoceptive AWARENESS

IAw
Metacognitive correspondence between accuracy and confidence — does the person know whether they are accurate?
METACOGNITIVE LAYER
The three dimensions DISSOCIATE empirically. A person can have any combination of high or low values across them. This means that "interoception" in the alexithymia literature has been doing too much work — what a paper means by "impaired interoception" depends on which of the three dimensions the paradigm measures.

Three resulting alexithymia profiles

Profile A: Detection-deficit

Low IAcc + Low ISen

Reduced upstream signal. Body signals are weak or absent. The afferent channel doesn’t deliver adequate information to the rest of the system.

Plausible candidate for interoceptive amplification training.

Profile B: Parsing-deficit

Normal/high IAcc + IAw low

Signals detected but the metacognitive labelling step fails. The body is generating data but the system isn’t converting it into conscious affective categorisation.

Body-scan training without concept-scaffolding may amplify already-loud signals without resolving the labelling failure.

Profile C: Hypervigilance + mislabelling

High ISen + Low IAcc + Low IAw

Lots of body attention, poor accuracy, poor metacognition. The somatic-symptom-disorder pattern. Hypervigilant to body signals but interprets them inaccurately or catastrophically.

Open-ended interoceptive training most likely to backfire here.
Architectural significance. The Garfinkel three-dimensional model is the substrate for the differential-therapy thesis the user takes into Categories 7 and 8. It is what makes the parsing-deficit reading of alexithymia mechanistically coherent rather than rhetorically convenient. The three dimensions correspond to clinically distinct profiles — and the same intervention (e.g., body-scan meditation) can help one profile, do nothing for a second, and amplify symptoms in a third.

Source: Garfinkel SN, Seth AK, Barrett AB, Suzuki K, Critchley HD. (2015) Biological Psychology 104:65–74.
